# THROAT — review verdicts + real-user lore (the honesty layer)

## MusicRadar (Computer Music) AVOX 2 review — Throat
- Physical-modelling of the vocal tract: adjust **breathiness, perceived throat size, glottal
  waveform**.
- Verdict: **"tricky to get realistic results from"**, and **"you can't push it too far or the
  voice will sound false"** — BUT the reviewer notes that artificiality "may sometimes serve
  creative purposes." Called **"a powerful creative tool."**
- **Rig read-through:** for this drone/doom/"recorded-wrong" rig, the criticism IS the feature.
  The "false" / unnatural zone everyone else avoids is exactly the target. Don't aim for a
  believable re-voice — push past it for the impossible-tract timbre.

## KVR forum thread (kvraudio.com/forum/viewtopic.php?t=163740) — real users on Throat
- Divisive plugin. One user: **"I think I'm one of the only people in the world that likes
  throat"** — used it to add **subtle growl** to a vocal recorded with a poor mic choice.
- Consensus best-practice: **use it subtly — "a little tweak goes a long way"**; pushed to
  extremes most users find it "unnatural and odd" (again, a plus for this rig, not a minus).
- A recurring complaint targets the **OLDER AVOX-2-era Throat**, which **handled formants but
  had no built-in pitch-shift** — users said "get Melodyne instead" for pitch+formant work.
  **NOTE / correction:** the **modern THROAT Evo (what's in Auto-Tune Unlimited) DID add a Pitch
  control** (confirmed in the voice-actors walkthrough capture). So that specific complaint is
  partly obsolete for the installed version.
- Gender-swap reality check: don't expect miracles from formants alone; if you need a convincing
  register change the performer should sing in that register (users cite natural performance >
  processing).

## Honesty flags
- The MusicRadar review is the **2008 AVOX 2** generation, not Evo — control feel verdicts carry
  over but exact UI differs (Evo has the 5-point graphical display + Pitch + Level Matching).
- No drone/doom/shoegaze/experimental artist credit found for Throat. Relevance is by capability
  + the reviewers' own "great for false/creative, bad for natural" framing.
