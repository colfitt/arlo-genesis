# Vocodist — modern vocoder + Auto-Tune, deep sound-design controls (distilled)

A vocoder = a **modulator** (usually a voice) shapes filters that process a **carrier**
(usually a synth). Vocodist bundles its own carrier synth AND can take an external carrier,
plus Auto-Tune pitch correction — so it's a one-stop "talking-instrument" / robot-voice / synth-
texture box. Only available in the Auto-Tune Unlimited subscription.

## Vocoder filter bank (the sound-design core)
- **Number of bands** — adjustable (few bands = coarse, vintage/robotic; many bands =
  intelligible, smooth).
- **Min / Max band frequency** — set the frequency window the bands span.
- **4 filter shapes** + **Q** of the filter response.
- **Stretch** and **Slide** — reshape/shift the band layout (formant-style sweeps; Slide is
  X-Y/automatable).
- Filter models derived from **20 different vintage hardware vocoders**.

## Carrier / internal synth
- Built-in **MIDI-playable synth**, up to **8 voices**, **dual oscillators** per voice
  (sawtooth or variable-pulse). Play the carrier from a MIDI controller.
- Synth can **track the incoming voice pitch** (so it follows your sung/played melody without
  MIDI), OR
- **External side-chain carrier input** — feed any sound (a drone, a pad, a banjo, hardware
  synth) as the carrier and vocode the voice/instrument onto it.
- **Oscillator scale-transpose** for instant 3-part harmonies from the carrier.

## Envelope / analysis
- Per-band level, band shift up/down, attack/release of the envelope follower, a **Smear**
  control, and a **dice/randomize** button for instant variation.

## Output FX (extra texture)
- **Saturation**, **Ring Modulation**, **Chorus** on the vocoder output; a **Noise** section
  (white/pink, with sensitivity/balance/level/colour) for the unpitched fricative "shh/ff"
  detail that makes vocoders intelligible — or, cranked, a noise bed.
- **X-Y pad**, MIDI-CC-mappable, assignable to e.g. Filter Slide or Vocal Envelope for
  performable morphs.
- 125+ artist presets (P-Thugg, Buddy Ross).

## Why it matters for the rig
- Feed a **sustained drone / pad / hardware-synth chord** as the external carrier and a
  banjo or voice as the modulator → robotic, vowel-shaped synthetic texture that follows the
  played line. Slow attack/release + few bands + the chorus/sat/ring-mod FX = evolving,
  metallic, "machine-choir" walls rather than clean talkbox.
- Reviewers: "fabulously creative," easier to dial than most vocoders despite the parameter
  count, strong sound quality.
