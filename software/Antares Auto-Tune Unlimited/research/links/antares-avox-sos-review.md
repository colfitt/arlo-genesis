# The AVOX suite, module by module — controls + creative/extreme uses (distilled)

The value of AVOX for a *sound-design* (not pop-vocal) rig is that each module pushes
beyond "natural" into special-effect / texture territory. Per-module:

## MUTATOR — the headline sound-design tool (ring-mod + throat mangle)
Three sections (confirmed by the Wavy Wayne walkthrough transcript):
- **Voice Design**: Pitch Shift (transpose ±24 semitones / ±2 octaves), **Throat Length**
  (lengthen/shorten the modelled throat — shifts formants down/up accordingly), **Throat
  Width** (narrow/widen the throat — also moves formants). Input-range control "could be for a
  vocal or an instrument."
- **Mutation**: a **Mutation** control choosing from **24 different mutations** built on
  *pitch-tracking ring modulation* — this is the ring-mod/metallic/inharmonic mangle. A
  **Mutant Mix** control blends processed vs unprocessed.
- **Alienize**: on/off; **tempo-syncable** to the host; a **Dialect** control sets the length of
  the speech segments that get *time-reversed* and blended back in → chopped, backwards,
  glitchy "alien language" texture.
- Verdict: "from subtle voice changing through to sci-fi special effects" — Darth-Vader (pitch
  + mutation), full alien (everything up), monster growls. The standout for the degraded /
  inhuman aesthetic.

## THROAT — physical-modelling vocal-tract reshaper (creative formants)
- Neutralises the source's own vocal tract, then lets you **stretch / shorten / widen /
  constrict** a modelled tract; a **graphical 5-point throat-shaping display** (vocal cords →
  throat → mouth → lips) for hands-on formant sculpting; **glottal waveform** edit; **breathiness**
  (variable-frequency noise add).
- Crucial for the rig: controls go **"well beyond the limits of physical human anatomy"** —
  impossible-tract formant timbres, not just gender shifts. Pairs with pitch-shift to keep
  formants where you want (or deliberately wrong).

## CHOIR — one voice → up to 32 unison voices (the choir/drone wall)
- **Choir Size**: 4 / 8 / 16 / **32** distinct unison voices from one mono source.
- **Pitch Variation**, **Timing Variation**, **Vibrato Variation** — how loosely each clone
  detunes / drifts / wobbles (small = tight double; large = lush, swimmy, almost chorused).
- **Stereo Spread** — fans the voices across the field (the "3-D sheen").
- Verdict: at 16/32 with moderate variation it's a convincing ensemble; pushed, it's a
  shimmering detuned wall. Multiple instances on different held notes = a stacked choral bed.
  (Mixed reviews warn it can sound "computer-like" if variation is mis-set.)

## DUO — automatic double-tracker
- Generates ONE simulated double of the lead (vs Choir's many), with pitch/timing/vibrato
  variance + pan. The tight-double / two-part-widening tool; lighter than Choir.

## HARMONY ENGINE (4x) — 4-voice harmony/choir generator (works on instruments)
- **Four** independent Harmony Voices, each with its own **interval**, **vocal timbre/character**,
  **vibrato** (rate/onset/depth), **pan**, **volume**, **formant**, mute.
- **Interval range ±2 octaves** (16vb down → 16va up).
- **Control modes**: Fixed Interval · Scale Interval (set key+scale like Auto-Tune) · Chord
  Degrees · Chord Name (with inversions) · Chord-by-MIDI (real-time chords from a controller) ·
  **MIDI Omni** (play the 4 voices like a synth) · MIDI Channel (one voice per channel, absolute
  control). → you can literally *play* harmonies/clusters from the 61SL MkIII.
- Built-in **Choir** function multiplies *each* of the 4 voices into 2/4/8/16 unison voices
  → effectively a big choral engine from one input.
- **Throat Modelling** on each voice (character), a **Freeze** function (Formant-Only or
  Formant+Pitch) to lock/sustain, Spread/Register spacing.
- Reviewer calls it "the real highlight." Explicitly harmonizes **a single vocal OR monophonic
  instrument** — so banjo/synth lead → instant 4-part (or massive choir-multiplied) bed.

## PUNCH — vocal compressor (simple, transient-add)
## WARM — tube/tape colour: "Velvet" (smooth preamp) + "Crunch" (valve-amp saturation),
  Input/Drive/Omnitube. "One of the nicest tube emulations around" — usable as a saturation
  stage on any source.
## ARTICULATOR — a **vocoder** needing two inputs (Control = modulator, Audio = carrier);
  formant/amplitude/EQ controls + built-in noise generator. (Talkbox/vocoder-style; Vocodist
  is the deeper modern vocoder.)
## ASPIRE — adds/subtracts **breath/aspiration** noise, frequency-targeted (breathy or
  de-breathed). Subtle on its own; a texture-add when pushed.
