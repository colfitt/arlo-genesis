# The vocal-chain / clean-up utilities — what they do + the skip verdict for this rig

Auto-Tune Unlimited bundles a full "AI-powered vocal chain" + a denoiser. In a drone/doom/shoegaze
rig these are the **corrective / pop-polish** tools — competent, but they duplicate (or work against)
what the rig already does. Decisive verdicts below. (Official Antares pages are JS-rendered and
return only a title to a fetcher — content here is triangulated from search result summaries +
Recording Magazine's Vocal Reverb review; LABELLED where thin.)

## Auto-Tune Vocal Compressor — AI-assisted vocal compressor
Voice-aware compressor; placed after EQ in the chain so it works on a tonally-balanced signal.
"Musical" pop-vocal dynamics control.
- THIS RIG: **SKIP (corrective).** It's lead-vocal glue. A sustained drone doesn't want its
  dynamics evened out, and the rig has plenty of dynamics tools. No creative angle.

## Auto-Tune Vocal EQ — AI-assisted, voice-specific EQ
Shapes tonal balance with "voice-specific technology" — auto-suggests corrective EQ moves for a
vocal. A surgical/tonal-balance box.
- THIS RIG: **SKIP (corrective).** Voice-tuned corrective EQ; irrelevant to banjo/synth/drone
  sound-design, and Logic's stock EQ (or any rig EQ) covers tone shaping with no vocal-only bias.

## Vocal De-Esser — AI sibilance reducer
Targets harsh "s"/"sh" sibilance using AI detection without dulling overall brightness.
- THIS RIG: **SKIP (corrective, vocal-only).** Pure de-essing. Only conceivably useful if a hard-
  tuned vocal take gets spitty — and even then it's a fix, not a creative move. (The installed
  de-esser; note the AVOX-4 marketing "Sybil" module is the same job and is NOT installed here.)

## Auto-Tune SoundSoap — noise removal / restoration
Denoiser/restoration: strips hum, hiss, broadband noise, clicks. ("SoundSoap" lineage = Acoustica's
restoration tool, folded into the Antares chain.)
- THIS RIG: **mostly SKIP — one off-label angle.** As intended (clean up a noisy take) it's
  irrelevant; the aesthetic *wants* noise. BUT: aggressively over-denoising a busy/lo-fi source can
  "scrub the life out of it" into a hollow, artifacted, gated-sounding husk — a degraded "recorded-
  wrong" texture. Niche, experimental, and RC-20/SketchCassette do degradation more directly.
  (Thin sourcing on creative SoundSoap use — labelled speculative.)

## Auto-Tune Vocal Reverb — vocal-dedicated AI reverb (the one with a creative angle)
"World's first vocal-dedicated reverb." **3 algorithms (Hall, Plate, Room)**, **2 delays (Mono,
Ping-Pong)**, **5 pre-verb FX (Pitch, Throat, Tube, De-Esser, Reverse)**, **5 post-verb FX (Auto-EQ,
Tone Shaper, Compressor, Gate, Width)**, plus **Reverb Assist** (AI "describe the vibe" preset gen)
and the headline **Auto-EQ** that dynamically ducks the reverb tail around the vocal's fundamental
to stop mud. Recording Mag: "all three algorithms have lovely depth… Plate does that metallic
ping/splash… Auto-EQ adds a lovely smoothness."
- THIS RIG: **mostly SKIP vs Valhalla, BUT situationally interesting.** For a tail/wall you'd reach
  **Valhalla VintageVerb/Room** every time — deeper, more characterful, the rig's reverb axis.
  HOWEVER Vocal Reverb has two things Valhalla doesn't: a **Reverse** pre-verb effect (backwards-
  swell into the tail = shoegaze/post-rock reverse-reverb without manual reversing) and **Pitch +
  Throat** pre-verb effects (pitch-shimmer / formant-warp baked INTO the reverb path). Niche
  reach-for: when you want a reverse or pitch-shifted reverb on a vocal/banjo in one box. Otherwise
  redundant — keep Valhalla as the default and don't add this to the chain.

## Overlap summary (skip-because-the-rig-already-has-it)
- Vocal Compressor / Vocal EQ / Vocal De-Esser → generic corrective vocal-chain → rig has stock +
  better tools; **skip**.
- SoundSoap → denoise → opposite of the aesthetic; **skip** (one perverse over-scrub angle only).
- Vocal Reverb → **Valhalla VintageVerb/Room wins** for tails; Vocal Reverb only earns a slot for
  its Reverse / Pitch / Throat pre-verb tricks. Vocal Prep app = front-end chain assembler for the
  above — irrelevant to instrument-sound-design.
