# MUTATOR — spec confirmation + the odd/even rule + verdicts (NEW corroboration)

Builds on the existing Wavy Wayne transcript and SoS review; adds independent confirmation of
the **odd/even Mutation** rule and the MusicRadar special-effect verdict.

## Three sections (confirmed across all sources)
1. **Voice Design** — Shift Pitch (±24 semitones / ±2 oct), **Throat Length**, **Throat Width**
   (throat modelling + pitch shifting to craft the base voice; "add a layer of robotic, synth-
   like vocals to a track"). Throat Length/Width go from "more natural" to "more alien."
2. **Mutate** — **24 varieties** of mutation via **pitch-tracking ring modulation** (+ formant
   control) → "mangle the voice into strange creatures." **Mutant Mix** = wet/dry blend.
3. **Alienize** — chops the audio into small segments and **plays each segment in reverse** →
   "alien language"; **Dialect** sets segment length; **tempo-syncable** to the host.

## THE ODD/EVEN MUTATION RULE (independently confirmed)
Antares' own product page states it outright (matches the Jamie Blake video citing the manual):
- **ODD-numbered Mutation values → INHARMONIC / atonal effect** → gritty, growly, "less musical."
- **EVEN-numbered Mutation values → more MELODIC / harmonic / pleasant.**
This is the single most actionable Mutator dialing tip: pick odd for destroyed/metallic doom
textures, even for broken-but-musical synth-double layers.

## MusicRadar (Computer Music) AVOX 2 verdict on Mutator (23 Jul 2008)
- "A pitch-shifter combined with throat length/width controls… ring modulation where pitch tracks
  the input signal, progressing toward **robotic extremes**."
- Alienize/Dialect "systematically reverse segments of audio, creating subtle, **granular
  effects** — or what sounds like a whole new language."
- Verdict: perfect for "voices that sound like something from another planet." **"It's not very
  good at natural alterations, but we assume that it's not meant to be!"** Practical use for
  conventional vocals "somewhat limited."
- **Rig read-through:** the "not good at natural / robotic extremes / granular" critique is the
  endorsement for a degrade/destroy rig. This is a pure sound-design instrument, not a corrector.

## Stated creative reach (Antares + retailer copy)
- "From subtle voice changing through to sci-fi special effects": Darth-Vader (Pitch + Mutation),
  full alien (everything up), monster/demon (Pitch + Throat + a Mutation).
- Marketed for **film/TV post, electronic music, film scores, and experimental soundscapes** —
  the closest the official copy comes to acknowledging non-pop, experimental/texture use.
