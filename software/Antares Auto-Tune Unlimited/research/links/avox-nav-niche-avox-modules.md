# The smaller AVOX modules (ARTICULATOR / ASPIRE / PUNCH / WARM) — what they actually do

These are the AVOX modules that the existing SoS-review file touches only briefly. Here is the
per-module "job + reach-for-in-this-rig" detail, distilled from the official AVOX product copy.
(NOTE: the AVOX-4 marketing roster also lists **Sybil** = variable-frequency de-esser, but Sybil
is NOT in this machine's CONTENTS.md — the installed de-esser is the standalone **Vocal De-Esser**.)

## ARTICULATOR — digital talkbox / 2-input vocoder
"A digital talkbox perfect for talking guitars, singing synths, whispering voices, and a wide range
of special effects." It's Antares' vocoder-style module: needs TWO sources — a **Control** signal
(the modulator, normally a voice) and an **Audio** signal (the carrier). The voice's
formant/articulation is imprinted on the carrier → talking-instrument. Has its own noise generator
+ formant/amplitude/EQ controls.
- THIS RIG: **situational** creative tool. It's the OLD/simpler vocoder — **Vocodist is the deeper,
  20-model modern one**, so for a "machine choir on a drone carrier" you'd normally reach Vocodist.
  Articulator's niche = quick talkbox flavour (a banjo or synth "saying" the contour of a played
  voice) when you want something lighter than Vocodist. Overlap: redundant-with-Vocodist; pick one.

## ASPIRE — aspiration (breath) noise processor
"The world's first tool for modifying a voice's breathiness independently of its harmonic content."
It isolates ONLY the breathy/aspiration element and lets you boost or cut it without touching the
tonal/harmonic part — i.e. add breath to a stiff take, or de-breathe a noisy one.
- THIS RIG: **mostly skip / niche.** It's a corrective/subtlety tool built for pop-vocal polish.
  Off-label angle: pushed hard to ADD breath-noise it becomes an airy hiss/texture layer you could
  bury under a drone — but that's a stretch, and RC-20/SketchCassette already supply noise/air
  character. No headline use here.

## PUNCH — vocal impact enhancer (transient/limiter hybrid)
"Gives your vocal more dynamic impact so it cuts through a dense mix." Operationally it's **like a
limiter but voiced to sound like gentle compression** — evens out dynamics while adding front-end
punch/presence. A specialised vocal dynamics box.
- THIS RIG: **skip (corrective).** Pure mix-glue/loudness for a lead vocal in a dense pop mix —
  the opposite of a sustained, smeared drone wall. Any generic comp/limiter (or just not
  compressing) serves the aesthetic better.

## WARM — tube saturation generator (the one keeper of this group)
"Warms up vocals with Antares' analog tube modeling." Modes/controls: **Velvet** (smooth preamp
warmth) and **Crunch** (valve-amp-style saturation), with **Input / Drive / Omnitube**. SoS called
it "one of the nicest tube emulations around." Works on ANY source, not just vocals.
- THIS RIG: **GOLD-ish / situational saturation stage.** A clean, usable tube colour you can drop on
  a banjo, baritone, synth pad, or the whole drone bus for grit/harmonic thickening. Caveat: the rig
  already has **Decapitator** (and SketchCassette/RC-20 for dirtier lo-fi). WARM is the *clean,
  polite* tube flavour — reach for it when you want gentle valve warmth rather than Decapitator's
  aggression. Note Mic Mod's Tube Saturation control is literally the same WARM engine built in.
