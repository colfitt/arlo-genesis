# Auto-Tune Slice — turn a recorded phrase into a playable, tuned, sliced instrument

## What it is
Slice is a **sampler + synthesizer hybrid** with Auto-Tune baked into the engine — "the only sampler
with Auto-Tune in its DNA." Built "vocals-first" but works on ANY pitched source. It's the one
Auto-Tune product that is an INSTRUMENT (you play it from MIDI), not an insert effect. (Installed as
`Auto-Tune Slice.component/.vst3`.)

## How it works
1. Load a sample — from the 200+ built-in vocal library (Bon Iver / Aaron Dessner content) OR
   **drop in your own audio** (a recorded banjo phrase, a vocal take, a synth line, field recording).
2. An advanced **transient-detection** algorithm auto-slices the sample into segments and
   **auto-maps the slices across your MIDI keyboard** — play slices in order to replay the phrase, or
   rearrange them to build new phrases/patterns. A **Chromatic mode** instead maps one slice
   chromatically (classic one-shot pitched sampler).
3. Auto-Tune is integrated, so slices can be pitch-corrected / pitch-bent without leaving the plugin.
4. **14 onboard effects** including **vintage tube saturation, Chorus, Ring Modulation, Vocal
   Doubling**, plus Auto-Tune pitch-bending.

## Reach-for in THIS rig (the creative-resampling angle)
**SITUATIONAL-to-GOLD for resampling / found-sound chopping.** This is the bundle's hidden gem for a
sound-design rig because it sidesteps the bundle's biggest limit — the mono pitch-tracking insert
plugins (Auto-Tune/Mutator/Throat) can't handle chords or messy input, but Slice just *chops and
maps whatever you feed it*:
- Record a banjo roll, a baritone phrase, or a vocal fragment → drop into Slice → transient-slice it
  → now it's a **playable, tuned chop-instrument** triggered from the 61SL MkIII / Digitakt / MPC.
- Rearrange slices into stuttered, glitchy, or reversed phrases for the "recorded-wrong" aesthetic.
- The onboard **Ring Mod + tube sat + doubling** overlap conceptually with Mutator/WARM/Duo but are
  one-window-fast for quick mangle while you're playing the chop kit.
- Pairs with the rig: chop a hardware/pedalboard-recorded part, then smear with Valhalla, degrade
  with RC-20/SketchCassette. A strong companion to the user's existing MPC/sampler workflow.
Caveat: it leans on its bundled-vocal library for the "intended" workflow; the rig value is entirely
in **importing your own sources**. Drum-instrument sample chopping is better served by the MPC/
Kontakt, but Slice's Auto-Tune-native pitch handling is unique for *tuned melodic* chops.
