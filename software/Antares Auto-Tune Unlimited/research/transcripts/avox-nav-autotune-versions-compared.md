# Auto-Tune versions compared — distilled (which engine to open, and why)

Official-style walkthrough of the FIVE Auto-Tune editions in the bundle. Distilled to prose; the
useful payload for the navigator is the per-edition "what's it for + what it drops."

## Access — the simple, set-and-forget auto-tuner
Uncluttered UI, basic tuning controls only. Explicitly "the go-to to achieve the classic Auto-Tune
effect" when paired with Auto-Key. Retune is just three presets — **Slow / Medium / Fast = 65 / 25 /
3 ms**. Click the keyboard to add/remove scale notes; Humanize relaxes tuning on long sustains. Use
it across many similar sources (e.g. background vocals) or when you want a no-drama tuner that "just
works." (Lightest CPU.)
→ RIG NOTE: 3 ms "Fast" is its hardest snap — usable for a quick robotic effect, but for the real
RS-0 control you want Pro/Artist/EFX+. Access = the CPU-saver, not the creative box.

## Artist — full Auto mode, no Graph
"Every feature of Auto-Tune Pro EXCEPT Graph mode." Input tracking, formant control, Flex-Tune,
vibrato creation, MIDI control, expanded scale presets, show/hide advanced tab. The daily-driver
"unless you need the precision Graph offers."
→ RIG NOTE: since Graph is the single creative reason to use Pro here, Artist is skippable — it's
Pro-minus-the-fun-part.

## Hybrid — ultra-low-latency, Avid DSP only
For Pro Tools HDX / Carbon / VENUE live consoles. Has the Auto-Tune 5 algorithm; latency ~0.001 ms.
**Feature-wise close to Artist BUT DSP-intensive processes like FORMANT CONTROL are left out** to
hit the speed. For live tracking/monitoring.
→ RIG NOTE: not in this machine's AU/VST3 CONTENTS (it's a DSP-platform build). Irrelevant to a
Logic/Ableton native rig — confirms why it's not on the roster.

## Pro (Pro X) — everything + Graph
Full Auto mode + the most extensive pitch-Graph facilities of any product. Workflow: start in Auto,
"graph that data and continue," node-object editing, or full manual mode for "every syllable and
utterance." The flagship.
→ RIG NOTE: **the one to open.** Graph/manual = draw a mono banjo/synth line to wrong targets for a
designed inhuman melody. Nothing below Pro can do this.

## EFX+ — "Auto-Tune EFFECTS, plus"
The "+" is a **pattern-generator / sequencer that re-pitches the signal through preset melodic
patterns according to the current key & scale** (this is the Auto-Motion engine). Auto-Tune section
is simple/sleek like Access but the same engine underneath. **Four effect slots** + effects presets.
Host's own quote: "use it to spice up a track momentarily or to COMPLETELY RESHAPE a sound."
→ RIG NOTE: the **creative wildcard.** Feed it a held drone/banjo note and the pattern generator
spits out a stepped, rhythmic, robotic re-pitched melody automatically — instant generative
machine-arpeggio texture, no playing required. The fun second engine after Pro.

## Presenter's own verdict
"You could just stick with Auto-Tune Pro and possibly EFX+." → confirms the two-engine reach-for:
**Pro X for designed/Graph work, EFX+ for the pattern-generator effect.** Access only as the quick
CPU-light tuner; Artist and Hybrid are redundant in this rig.
