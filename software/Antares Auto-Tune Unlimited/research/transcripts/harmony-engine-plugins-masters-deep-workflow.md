Plugins Masters (Leroy, Audio Camp / Sherman Studio, Bordeaux FR) · Create vocal harmonies with Harmony Engine · n.d.

A real engineer building harmonies on a song ("Air Fan") in **Pro Tools** — deeper, more practical interval/chord choices than the manual-tour videos. Distilled:

**Setup**
- Duplicate the lead vocal, harmonize the duplicate (keeps a clean lead). Insert Harmony Engine — **picks the stereo instance** ("I'm going for the stereo one") so panning is available.
- Sees the channel strips: original + harmony voices, each with its own interval/octave (3rd, 5th, etc.).

**Scale Interval workflow (the bread-and-butter)**
- Harmony Source = **Scale Interval** ("it's on scale… that's just going to give me single notes / follow the scale of the song"). Sets **Key/Root = F# and Scale = minor** — the song's tonality. This forces every voice to stay diatonic.
- Then *auditions intervals per voice* by ear, soloing each: tries a voice on the **5th**, then drops it to a **6th** ("sounds kind of nice"), then tries the **3rd** and takes it **down an octave**. Settles on a **low 3rd (down an octave) + a higher voice** as the pair.
- **Pans** the two voices hard-ish: "pan one to the right and one to the left." Then un-solos and blends with the lead.

**Chord Degrees workflow (chord changes that follow the song)**
- Switches Harmony Source to **Chord Degree**, still in F# minor. The chord buttons appear: chord I, I with a 7th, II, II with a 7th, etc.
- Builds his own progression by **assigning each chord to a Harmony Preset button and renaming it as the actual chord name** (he prefers reading "F#, G#7, B major" over "ii7, IV7" — easier than scale-degree labels). Assigns F#, then ii-with-7 = G#7, then IV-with-7 = B major, etc.
- Then **automates Chord Degree** in Pro Tools: opens the automation lane (select the box at top → choose "chord degree" → add it), and draws the chord changes across the song so the harmony follows the progression. Shows a full pre-built map: F#, F#7, G dim, D dim, G dim7, A major, A7, B, B7, C — a complete diatonic-plus-borrowed chord set.

**Mix finishing on the harmonies**
- A "little bit of reverb" on the voices; "you can put delays on these things and pan them the way you want — be really creative."
- **EQ on the Harmony Engine output**: rolled off the **bass**, cut some **boxy** lower-mids, added a **little high** so the harmonies "cut through really nice" and don't clutter the lead.

Rig takeaways: (1) Scale Interval + set key/scale + audition each voice's interval by soloing (5th/6th/3rd, octave-down) is the fast way to a tasteful, diatonic stack — directly usable on a banjo/baritone lead. (2) Assign chords to the **Harmony Preset buttons, then automate them** = a song-length chord-following choral bed without MIDI. (3) Always EQ the harmony bus (HPF + de-box + air) so the wall sits under the lead. (4) Use the **stereo** instance and hard-pan voices for width.
