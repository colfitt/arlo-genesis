# MUTATOR — 3 practical sound-design tricks (NEW capture, goes past Wavy Wayne)

Where the existing Wavy Wayne capture is a static control tour, THIS is an applied,
settings-and-listening session on a real lead vocal — the most useful Mutator workflow
source captured. Auto-captions, distilled to prose. Goes section by section as creative
LAYERS, not a full re-voice.

## Routing (important and NEW)
- Mutator works as an **insert OR a send/return effect**. In the demo it's on a **return** —
  lead vocal is **sent to Return A** where Mutator lives → you blend the mutated layer under
  the dry vocal with the send/return level instead of committing the whole track. This is the
  key "texture layer, not replacement" move.

## Trick 1 — Voice Design as a synth-lead layer
Goal: a "really high synth-lead" doubling layer that tracks the vocal's pitch.
- Use the **Voice Design** section: **Shift Pitch** up, then sculpt **Throat Length** + **Throat
  Width**.
- **Throat Length on STRETCH** → "rubbery… nice gooey soft sound."
- **Throat Width WIDE** → "a little more grit… brighter."
- **Throat Width NARROW** → "more soft and kind of round." (Presenter picks NARROW here.)
- Result: a pitched layer that "follows the pitch really nicely… just as if you played a lead
  sound on an analog synth." Add a touch of reverb on the return, blend under the dry vocal.
- Rig translation: this is exactly the **re-voiced sustained-lead layer** move — pitch-shifted,
  throat-sculpted, sitting under the source. Works on any monophonic source, not just voice.

## Trick 2 — Mutate (the ring-mod) for bendy analog-synth grit
- **Mutate = a PITCH-TRACKING ring modulator**: it listens to the incoming pitch and moves the
  modulation signal relative to that pitch (so it stays musical-ish instead of fixed-frequency
  ring-mod clang).
- **THE RULE (from the manual, confirmed on Antares' own product page):**
  - **ODD-numbered Mutation values = INHARMONIC / atonal / "less musical"** → use these for
    "gritty, growly stuff. Super cool for that."
  - **EVEN-numbered Mutation values = more HARMONIC / pleasant.**
- Presenter picks an **even** Mutation + a little **Pitch Shift** → "a cool bendy synth sound…
  sounds like an old analog synth." Blend under the dry vocal.
- Rig translation: odd Mutation = the destroyed/metallic/growl flavour for doom/drone; even =
  the more melodic "broken-synth" double. Mutant Mix sets wet/dry of just this stage.

## Trick 3 — Alienize as a real-time reverse-chop / reverse slap-delay
- **Alienize takes little chunks of audio and plays them back IN REVERSE, in real time** (works
  live off a mic input too).
- **Dialect = chunk length.** Slow/long → audible backwards "alien language" segments. **Go fast
  → almost a slap-delay, but reversed** → a glitchy granular reverse texture.
- GOTCHA worth knowing: even if you pull **Mutant Mix wet DOWN**, the **Alienize section still
  plays back the reversed audio** — so you can blend between the pitch-shifted/mutated reverse
  playback and the un-pitch-shifted reverse chop independently. (Mutant Mix is the Mutate stage's
  wet; Alienize has its own path.)
- Add reverb, check in the mix → "really interesting texture… playing back the reverse chunks
  so quickly."

## Closing technique tips
- **Automate the SEND** to Mutator at different song points for varied texture (not always-on).
- "Barely scratched the surface" — presenter treats it as open-ended sound design.

## Net-new vs. existing captures
- Send/return routing + blend-under-dry workflow (not in Wavy Wayne).
- Concrete Voice-Design feel map: STRETCH = gooey/rubbery, WIDE = grittier/brighter, NARROW =
  soft/round.
- The **odd=inharmonic/growl, even=harmonic/pleasant** Mutation rule (also on the product page).
- Alienize **fast = reverse slap-delay**; Mutant-Mix-down still passes Alienize reverse.
