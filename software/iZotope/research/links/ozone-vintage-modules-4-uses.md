izotope.com (Learn) · "4 Uses for Ozone's Vintage Modules" · iZotope

The four Vintage modules (Advanced only) model classic hardware, giving "idealized"
character without the noise/maintenance of the real boxes. Each is also usable as a
standalone insert plugin — i.e. you can drop just Vintage Tape on a bus, not only the
master.

## 1. Vintage EQ — models Pultec EQP-1A + MEQ-5 tube EQs
- "Broad stroke" tonal moves with the classic Pultec curve and tube color, no inherent
  noise. Limited bands = faster, more intuitive decisions.
- Bands: Low / Low-mid / High-mid / High.

## 2. Vintage Compressor — hybrid of beloved hardware compressors
- The "mixbus glue" module. True FEEDBACK compression (hard to do in digital), with
  variable release that depends on how much you're compressing.
- Three modes to enhance Transients / Body / or a Blend of both.
- Has a sidechain filter.

## 3. Vintage Tape — models a Studer A810 two-track deck
- Adds tape non-linearities: saturation, compression, phase rotation = "grit" + gentle
  transient control. No real-tape hiss/wow/flutter.
- Concrete example settings from the article:
  - Speed: 30 IPS (preserves top end, cleans low frequencies, enhances kick thump)
  - Input Drive: 4 dB
  - Bias: -0.5 (shifts distortion toward the highs, pushes the midrange)
  - Harmonics: 2.0 (adds density / even-order harmonics)
  - Low Emphasis: 0.5 ; High Emphasis: 2.5

## 4. Vintage Limiter — models the Fairchild 670 feedback tube limiter
- "Thick, vibey" limiting: soft knee, program-dependent attack/release, subtle tube
  harmonics. A character contrast to the transparent Maximizer — glues + adds weight
  while reducing the level demand. Subtle change to transients vs Maximizer.

### Rig note (drone/doom/shoegaze)
Vintage Tape + Vintage Limiter are the two to reach for to "glue without killing grit."
The Tape's harmonic lift + the Fairchild-style limiter's soft-knee weight add the analog
density that degraded/sustained material likes, instead of the surgical/clean Maximizer.
