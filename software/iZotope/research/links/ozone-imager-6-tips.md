The Imager is per-band stereo width + a mono->stereo "Stereoize" generator. (A free
standalone Ozone Imager also exists; same engine.) The Width control is a mid/side
balance: 0 to +100% raises the Side up to +6 dB; 0 to -100% fades Side to -inf (mono).

## The 6 tips
1. **Stereoize mono instruments** — turn mono into stereo with no reverb/doubling. Push
   Width up, adjust the Time slider to taste. Mode II = more natural, handles wider
   settings (50-60% suggested). Mode I = colorful phasing effect.
2. **Width on backing vocals** — Width + Stereoize to widen BVs/doubles behind the lead,
   adds excitement in choruses.
3. **Width on FX returns** — put Imager on reverb/delay buses, Width up, Stereoize OFF.
   Gives a "larger-than-life space around the mix." (Best place for a wall: widen the
   reverb/delay return, not the dry source.)
4. **Width on instrument buses** — widen non-essential "ear-candy" groups. Widened
   elements LOSE level in mono, so never widen mix fundamentals.
5. **Width on the master** — subtle only (~25%), and check on a good stereo system.
   "Less is more."
6. **Automate Width** — narrow during verses/bridges, push width in final choruses to
   reward stereo listeners.

### Rig note
Low end should stay centered/mono — keep band 1 (sub) at 0% width. Widen the upper bands
of a guitar/synth WALL, or better, widen its reverb send. Watch the correlation meter:
going hard-negative = phase-cancellation / mono-fold problems.
