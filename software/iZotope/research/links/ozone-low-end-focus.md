Low End Focus = a bass-shaping tool for muddiness/clarity that EQ + compression can't
easily fix. Works 20 Hz – 300 Hz where kick/bass/low-mids fight. Ideal for taming a
boomy/mud-heavy DRONE low end.

## Modes
- **Smooth**: subtle, preserves the recording — clarifies 808s/bass synths, warmth without
  aggressive color. (Use this on a sustained drone bass so it cleans mud without killing
  the rumble.)
- **Punchy**: aggressive, exaggerated — accentuates percussive transients (kick, snare
  bottom). (Less useful for sustained drones; good if there's a slow pulse you want to pop.)

## Controls
- **Contrast** (-100% to +100%): how pronounced the effect is. The module preserves the
  prominent spectral content and reduces the less-prominent (the "mud" between notes).
  Moving Contrast down shifts emphasis lower in the spectrum (tone-shaping, not just
  cleanup).
- **Frequency Range markers**: drag to narrow onto a specific instrument/problem band
  inside 20-300 Hz.
- **Stereo vs Mid-Side**: process the Side independently. Low content (kick/bass) lives in
  the center, so M/S lets you clean the sides without touching the mono sub.

## Other Ozone 9 "tricks" worth knowing (Black Ghost Audio)
- **Master Rebalance**: nudge vocal/bass/drum level on a stereo bounce with no stems
  (light moves only).
- **Match EQ**: 8000+ bands; match a problem range to a reference, save snapshots. Target a
  specific deficiency (e.g. low-mid) rather than broadband matching.
- **Tonal Balance Control**: meter your mix vs genre target curves; talks to the EQ across
  the session.
