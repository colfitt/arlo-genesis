Master Assistant = the AI starting-point engine. It analyzes a passage and BUILDS a
custom module chain you then edit. The fastest way to a "loud + glued + roughly balanced"
bounce.

## Launch + flow
- Click the circular Master Assistant button at the top of the Ozone window.
- Two paths: **Auto-Master** (one-click, minimal input) or **Custom** (you set genre +
  loudness + which modules before it analyzes).
- Set **Analysis Time** to your loudest section (the chorus/peak of the wall); seconds
  or bars. Hit play to run the analysis pass.

## Targets
- Ozone suggests a **genre target** from 34 options (Pop, Hip-Hop, EDM, Rock, Orchestral...)
  built from "chart-topping hits." For drone/ambient there's no exact genre — pick the
  CLOSEST (Orchestral / "All-Purpose") or, better, use a custom reference.
- **Custom reference track:** press the "+" in Targets, upload your own file (e.g. a
  reference drone record you like the tone of). MA matches its tonal balance, width,
  dynamics, and loudness. NOTE: custom references do NOT generate a Vocal Balance target
  (no Master Rebalance added).

## Sub-targets / macro knobs (what MA exposes)
- **Tonal Balance** (drives EQs + processors), **Vocal Balance** (Master Rebalance; check-
  mark if no change needed), **Dynamic Match**, **Width Match**, **Clarity & Stabilizer**
  (Stabilizer = unwanted-resonance controller), and **Loudness** (one slider hitting
  multiple processors; integrated LUFS).
- Use **Gain Match** so the louder MA output doesn't fool your ear.

## Intent: Modern vs Vintage
- MA (and the older versions) lets you choose **Modern** (transparent modules) or
  **Vintage** (colored: Vintage EQ/Comp/Tape/Limiter) and Low/Med/High intensity. For
  degraded music, choose **Vintage + Low intensity** — it seeds the chain with the
  character modules instead of clinical ones.

## After
- "Enter the Ozone" / module view to fine-tune. **Relearn** re-runs analysis.

### Honest note for this rig
MA is trained on commercial masters, so it WILL try to make a drone louder/brighter/wider
than intended. Treat it as a sketch: run it, then pull the Maximizer back, bypass anything
that flattens the dynamics, and keep only the modules that help (often just Vintage Tape +
a gentle EQ).
