izotope.com (Learn) · "10 steps to a quick master in Ozone" · iZotope

iZotope's own end-to-end quick-master workflow. The skeleton to adapt (lighter) for a
drone bounce.

1. **Prep** — calibrate monitoring; export the mix as 24-bit/32-bit-float WAV at native
   sample rate.
2. **Master Assistant** — insert Ozone, click the Master Assistant (globe) button. Use
   Custom to control which modules analyze; pick a genre target (e.g. All-Purpose),
   analysis time ~30 s, enable True Peak + Maximizer; example loudness target -9 dB
   (use -14 for streaming/ambient instead).
3. **Level-matched audition** — Gain Match on; solo/bypass each module level-matched vs
   the original mix.
4. **Preset exploration** — audition per-module presets as starting points.
5. **EQ + Dynamics** — trim Equalizer curves (reduce excess shelf boosts); Dynamic EQ on
   mid-channel only to preserve imaging; tune Clarity.
6. **Bass control / Low End Focus** — add sustain + punch to the lows, control peaks.
7. **Impact** — tighten the lows.
8. **Stabilizer** — tame resonances/pumping; preset "Harshness Tamer."
9. **Maximizer** — transparent limiting (IRC modes); adjust from MA defaults.
10. **Codec preview + export** — preview at 320 kbps MP3; export lossless WAV
   (44.1 kHz/24-bit); test across systems; revise.

### Rig adaptation
Steps 6-9 (Bass/Impact/Stabilizer/Maximizer) are where over-mastering creeps in for drone.
Keep them light; the Maximizer should barely move (≤2-3 dB GR). The real keepers for this
aesthetic are MA-as-sketch (step 2), the EQ trim (step 5), and Low End Focus (step 6).
