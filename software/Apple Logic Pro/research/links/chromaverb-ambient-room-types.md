ChromaVerb is the reach-for stock reverb for wide ambient washes. This collates the room types and the copyable ambient/drone settings.

## Ambient-friendly room types (algorithms)
- **Synth Hall** — super-wide, the go-to for pads/walls.
- **Reflective Hall** — soft characteristic, beautiful for long washes; crank Decay to ~**14 s**.
- **Dense Room** / **Strange Room** — wide, deep, well-suited to **modulated** reverberation under synth pads.
- **Bloomy** — rich, dense.
- **Greek Wash** is a factory *preset* (sits on a hall algorithm) — Decay **30 s**, Width **100%**.

## Copyable settings
**Long ambient wash**
- Room Type: **Reflective Hall**
- Decay: **14 s** (up to 30 s for pads)
- Width: **100%**
- Dry **0** / Wet **100** (hear only the wet) — use on a send.
- Lift the high end in the **Damping EQ** to make it shimmer.

**Modulated pad reverb**
- Algorithm: **Dense** or **Strange Room**
- Mod Speed: **3.90 Hz**
- Mod Depth: **40**
- → deep, rich Lexicon-224-style modulation.

**Infinite drone**
- Use the **Freeze** control (unique to ChromaVerb) to hold a near-infinite reverb tail. Automate Freeze on/off; the tail sustains forever from the moment it's engaged ("frozen" ambient shimmer, à la the Kate Bush "Running Up That Hill" backdrop per the tutorial).

## Notes
ChromaVerb also has a built-in damping EQ and an output EQ — sculpt these per-layer when stacking multiple reverbs so they don't mud up (same principle as the 4-bus drone fan-out).
