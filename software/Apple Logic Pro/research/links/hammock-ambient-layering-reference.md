Hammock are an ambient/post-rock/drone duo whose "wall of layered guitars" method is the *aesthetic* this rig chases. Captured as a TECHNIQUE reference — NOT a Logic attribution.

## The layering method (the stealable part)
- An average Hammock song = **12+ guitar parts**, layered and mixed for depth/texture.
- "To cut through the wall of sound by the end of a song, the main part will be **doubled or tripled**." — Marc Byrd.
- Goal stated repeatedly: a *signature sound* — "we just want to sound like ourselves... like Hammock."
- Hardware they spotlight: **Strymon TimeLine** delay and **EHX Cathedral** reverb, both credited (on their IG) for their core "TEXTURE!!" sound. (Both overlap this rig's TimeLine/Big Sky time-section.)

## HONESTY FLAG
- No source found states which DAW Hammock track in. The defensible use here is **technique transfer only**: the 12-track double/triple-the-lead wall is directly reproducible in Logic via Track Stacks (Summing) + the shimmer/IR/4-bus recipes captured elsewhere in this folder.
- Do not claim Hammock "use Logic." Claim: "Hammock's documented 12+-layer guitar-wall method is the model; Logic's Track Stacks + send-bus reverbs are how you build it in this rig."

## Rig fit
This is the clearest real-artist blueprint for the rig's "sustained walls / banjo-as-lead" aim: track many passes of banjo/baritone through the board, double/triple the lead, summing-stack them, and fan into the parallel reverb buses.
