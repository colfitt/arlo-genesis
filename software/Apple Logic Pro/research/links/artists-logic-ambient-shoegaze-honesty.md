THE HONEST HEADLINE: Logic Pro is a hugely popular DAW, but in the **specific** drone/ambient/shoegaze/experimental world it is *not* the dominant tool, and clean, sourced "this artist makes their ambient records in Logic" claims are genuinely scarce. Most of that scene leans **Ableton Live** (clip/looping workflow) and **Max/MSP / Pure Data** for generative work. The big "famous Logic users" listicles are real but skew pop/EDM/hip-hop and are mostly unsourced. Below: what's actually supportable, flagged by certainty.

---

## Genre-relevant, with a real source

**Tim Hecker (ambient/drone)** — *partial / forum-sourced.*
- Source: ilovefuzz.com thread (http://www.ilovefuzz.com/viewtopic.php?f=149&t=48136). User "Ev_O)))": "Hecker uses Logic to compile his end results while doing a lot of work in MAX and Audiomulch," and recalls him favouring an Eventide H8000; another user notes his sources are field recordings + granular synthesis.
- HONESTY: this is **forum hearsay, not an official interview**. The supportable claim is narrow: Logic appears to be his *final assembly/compile* stage, with the actual sound design happening in Max/MSP + Audiomulch + hardware. Do NOT state "Tim Hecker produces in Logic" flatly — state "reportedly uses Logic to compile, sound-designs elsewhere."

**Ben Frost (drone/experimental)** — *Ableton, not Logic.* Same thread: "Frost generally uses Ableton... to process guitar and field recordings," with heavy compression and low-mid removal. Listed here only to mark the boundary (a name people assume; he's an Ableton case).

**Jonny Greenwood / Radiohead (art-rock, ambient-adjacent)** — *Max/MSP, not Logic.*
- Source: Cycling '74 mini-interview (https://cycling74.com/articles/mini-interview-jonny-greenwood) — Greenwood learned **Max/MSP** (recommended via a friend of Nigel Godrich). Radiohead's studio DAW history is more ProTools/Logic-mixed at various points, but the *ambient/generative* Greenwood work is Max-based, not a Logic story. Included as a boundary note, not a Logic attribution.
