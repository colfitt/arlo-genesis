soundonsound.com · Sound On Sound · "Arturia V Collection 7" review (Synthi V section) · 2019

On the Synthi V specifically:
- Emulates the EMS Synthi AKS. The reviewer's caveat — "which Synthi AKS?" — because real
  units vary wildly. The soft version is "far more predictable," with better tuning stability;
  spans "simple monosynth leads and basses to 4-voice polyphonic sounds to the most complex
  of effects."
- PIN MATRIX: five pin types (vs. three on originals), each a consistent attenuation —
  pins at 100%, 75%, 50%, or 25% — plus GROUPING pins that "affect multiple destinations using
  a single controller or modulator." (Color-coded by strength.)
- A VINTAGE / MODERN switch toggles between modelled original instability and predictable
  modern behaviour. (Vintage = drift/instability = drone gold; Modern = stable.)
- Arturia ADDED beyond the original: oscillator sync, Sample & Hold, FIVE function generators
  producing "complex contours," joystick animation with looping (a vector-style move), a 7×4
  modulation matrix, DUAL sequencers (a 256-event and a 32-step), and three effects slots with
  ten effect types.
- Best-at verdict: "evolving electronic timbres, harsh rhythmic effects" — i.e. exactly drone,
  texture, and noise rather than clean emulation duty.
