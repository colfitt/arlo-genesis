idmmag.com · IDM Mag · "Exploring the weird and wonderful Arturia Buchla Easel V" · (n.d.)

Synthesis approach:
- Additive, not subtractive — builds tone via "FM and wavefolding basic waveforms" instead of
  starting harmonically rich and filtering down. "Forces the user to think outside the box,
  and leaves a huge sonic palette to be explored."

Sequencer / rhythm:
- 5-step sequencer (also 4 or 3). The 5-step default "forces you to usually make interesting
  polyrhythmic stuff" — called "an absolute powerhouse for techno bass and rhythms," i.e. great
  for self-running rhythmic-pulse beds under a drone.

Modulation / patching:
- Banana-jack system: route "any of the modulation sources to any of the parameters." Use
  "random modulation using the white output jacks" (the random voltage source's four white
  outputs) for evolving textures.

FM / tonal character:
- "The Buchla Easel is fantastic, it does a really nice FM sound." Switching the Modulation
  Oscillator into LFO mode turns "your FM sound into a high-pitched squelch" (i.e. crossing
  from audio-rate FM grit into slow wobble).

Workflow tip: "hit record and just play around with the patching and cherry-pick the best
results" — the canonical generative-Buchla capture workflow.
