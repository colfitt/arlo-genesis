CZ V = Casio CZ-1 / CZ-101 phase-distortion (PD), the '80s answer to FM — but
warmer and easier to program than DX7. Strong for glassy/animated pads.

## How PD works (for the texture)
- Carrier scans a sine-wave period from a table; a **Modulator** + **DCW (Digital
  Controlled Waveshaping)** dynamically changes the scan rate to distort the
  carrier's phase → richer harmonics than a plain sine.
- Each oscillator can be modulated independently → layered, intricate tones; a
  **ring-modulator** adds harmonically rich (clangy/bell-ish) content.

## Ambient/glassy-pad approach (Arturia)
- "Dry PD sounds can be very full and alive" — use **fast subtle vibrato** +
  **slow LFO sweeping of the phase distortion (DCW)** for movement.
- Then push it over the top with Arturia's added **Delay, Chorus, Phaser,
  Reverb** FX. The UI makes PD programming approachable ("child's play").

## Community packs proving the ambient angle
- **"Silent Ocean"** (Vicious Antelope): 30 atmospheric pads for CZ V — shimmering
  pads, animated textures, analog-style colors.
- **"Cosmo"** (Reverb Machine): 80 presets incl. ambient pads with full FX chains
  + macro mapping (e.g. "Symmetry Arps," "Gestation Period").

→ Reach for CZ V when you want a glassy/animated pad that's warmer and less
brittle than DX7 V; sweep the DCW slowly under a banjo wall.
