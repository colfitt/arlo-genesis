Practical guide notes:
- PATCH MATRIX: the software replaces buying physical pins (real EMS pins cost £1–59 each) —
  you click a cell to place a pin and choose its colour/strength. Read the manual to learn the
  grid (rows = sources, columns = destinations).
- JOYSTICK: assign components to X and Y; e.g. filter on X + oscillator pitch on Y → "crazy
  sound upon movement of the joystick on each axis." The drone hand-controller.
- Onboard FX via dropdowns: delay, reverb, chorus.
- The "added function" (function-generator) section makes evolving soundscapes that "can
  automatically alter over time based on your configuration" — the hands-free drone engine.
- Recommended approach (very on-brand for our aesthetic): explore presets first, then learn the
  matrix — "an approach inspired by Brian Eno's methodology" of studying the synth's behaviour
  by ear.
