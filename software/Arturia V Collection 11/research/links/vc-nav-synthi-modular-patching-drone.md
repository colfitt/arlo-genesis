PATCHING (both Synthi V and Modular V3):
- Drag one patch point to another to make a cable; valid destinations highlight
  in RED. Synthi uses the iconic PIN MATRIX (drop pins to route); Modular V uses
  patch cords across a Moog-modular-style rack.

SYNTHI V — drone/noise sound-design tricks (from Arturia's own designers):
- FEEDBACK patches — route output back into the matrix for self-oscillating chaos.
- Use RING MOD in unconventional ways for metallic/inharmonic drones.
- OVERDRIVE the filter and the VCAs for grit.
- Randomly add pins to the matrix to find happy accidents (between musical and SFX).
- THE "BIRD PATCH" trick: NO oscillators at all — the sound is the SELF-RESONATING
  FILTER. (A pure-filter drone source — ideal degraded/ambient texture seed.)
- The joystick + matrix = hands-on evolving modulation.

MODULAR V3 — for big generative/ambient builds:
- Full Moog-modular: multiple VCOs, filters, the 960-style sequencer, plenty of
  mod sources. Patch slow free-running LFOs onto pitch/cutoff for evolving walls.
- ~3hr LearningModular course exists (Modular V3) covering FM/AM + advanced
  patching — the deep-dive resource if Modular becomes a drone workhorse.

Both are top-tier DRONE/NOISE engines in the bundle alongside Synthi (smaller,
faster) and Buchla (generative). Synthi = fast chaos; Modular = deliberate builds.
