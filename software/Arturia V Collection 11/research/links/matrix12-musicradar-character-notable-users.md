The character + notable-users anchor for the Matrix-12 (1985 original, £4595 — pro-only).

## ★ The defining mod fact
**Up to SIX modulators per parameter.** "It's possible to make patches that are impossible to
do with an old modular analogue synth." Five envelopes + five LFOs **per voice**; DADSR
envelopes with multiple trigger modes and **freerun options** (LFOs/envs that aren't reset by
note-on → free-running, never-aligned cycles = the evolving-texture engine). Tracking
generators give pitch→filter / pitch→mod-depth shaping.

## Filter character
The 15 modes are "a mixture of Bandpass, Hi-Pass, Lo-Pass and Phase in differing combinations
which work to radical effect" — incl. dramatic combos like **3-notch + 1-lowpass.** Overall
sound: "**fat and warm but with a real clarity and edge; not as raw as earlier Obie's but
substantial and characterful.**" Equally good at evolving pads, searing leads, percussive
stabs, articulate bells/plucks and crystalline timbres.

## Notable users (triangulated; NOT ambient-patch-credits)
Studio/film: "a Matrix-12 or Xpander lurking in the studio of many a Hollywood sound
designer." Named across sources: **Vangelis, Steve Roach** (ambient/drone — the closest anchor
to this rig), **The Orb** (ambient house), **Brad Fiedel** (Terminator score), Tangerine-adjacent
film work, Prince, Toto, Herbie Hancock, Depeche Mode, Vince Clarke, Trent Reznor (NIN),
Michael Brecker (the single-note-harmony EWI player — see Glen Darcey transcript), Larry Fast.

## Honest flag
**No specific shoegaze/doom/slowcore artist is documented using a Matrix-12.** Steve Roach
(deep ambient/drone) and The Orb are the genuinely relevant names; everything else is
"prized for evolving textures" by reputation, not a verified shoegaze patch credit. Relevance
to this rig is by **technique** (deep slow modulation → wall), not by named-act lineage.
