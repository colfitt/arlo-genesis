What CS-80 V4 adds + the full feature list (vendor reference; the "what V4 added" answer).

**Core engine (faithful CS-80):**
- **Dual-layer architecture** — two complete parallel chains, each with own VCO, VCF,
  VCA, and Touch Response. Independent timbral control across both voices.
- Two oscillators, each with its **own LFO for pulse-width modulation**.
- **Sub-oscillator** — deep bass or textural movement (global).
- **Ring modulator** — "plucky, metallic, lazer-gun timbres."
- **Four filters** — multi-mode high-pass + low-pass in series, each with its own
  envelope.
- **Ribbon controller** — pitch-bend from any touch point.
- **Touch Response** — velocity + aftertouch set independently per oscillator;
  **polyphonic aftertouch** supported. Portamento + sustain.

**V4 enhancements (what's new vs V3):**
- **New effects engine** — THREE effects slots, **15 effect types** (incl. MultiBand
  Compressor, Tape Delay, chorus, reverb, etc.), in series OR parallel.
- **Advanced modulation** — three multi-segment envelope generators with customizable
  curves; a **Modulation Mixer** for complex routings (e.g. sidechain-style).
- Dedicated keyboard-control section: Velocity, Aftertouch, Mod Wheel, Keyboard
  Tracking — each a routable mod source.
- **Resizable GUI**, **MPE** compatibility, **MTS-ESP** microtuning, TAE analog
  emulation.
- **200 factory presets** across Bass, Leads, **Pads**, Sequences, Keys, **Strings**.

**Rig takeaway:** the V4 3-slot / 15-type effects engine means you can build the
Vangelis chain (tape delay + chorus + reverb) INSIDE the plugin, then still send to
Valhalla/EchoBoy externally. The Mod Mixer + multi-segment envelopes are the new path
to slow evolving pads without an external LFO. Browse Pads/Strings categories first.
