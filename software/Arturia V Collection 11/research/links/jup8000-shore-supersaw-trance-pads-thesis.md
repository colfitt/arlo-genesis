Companion academic study to Szabo — focuses specifically on **how the supersaw is used to
build PADS** (the use-case that matters for a wall-of-sound rig).

## What is a pad (per the paper)
"Pads are simply **sustained legato chords**, frequently used to fill out areas of the
frequency range." In trance they live in breakdown sections. Studied real tracks; the common
threads across them = **stacked/legato sustained chords made of multiple sawtooth waves**, the
supersaw being the iconic vehicle.

## Method / variables studied
Waveform type, multiple oscillators, **detune**, and **phase** — the same four levers Szabo
isolates. The paper runs detune tests at **doubled / halved / linear** detune amounts vs the
real curve and rates them (ITU-R impairment scale), confirming the stock non-linear detune
curve is what "sounds right" — over-detuning (doubled) degrades the iconic character.

## Practical pad takeaways (for Jup-8000 V)
- Build pads as **sustained legato CHORDS**, not single notes — the supersaw's width × chord
  voicing is the wall.
- Keep detune near the **stock curve** (don't crank past the sweet spot — doubled detune
  measurably worsens the classic sound; for ambient, even less than stock).
- Mentions an **EQ/HPF + reverb** finishing stage on trance pads (Stereoklang EQ guide cited) —
  i.e. the iconic sound is supersaw → EQ → big reverb, exactly the degrade-then-drench chain.
