soundonsound.com · Sound On Sound review · 2025

FLAGSHIP NEW SYNTH = Jup-8000 V (JP-8000 virtual-analogue). Great for trance/Goa
but versatile: "lovely pads, leads and basses." Caveat: a non-random, constant
phase offset between the dual oscillators can cause cancellation across voices.

Pure LoFi = the real novelty — sample-based + analogue-modelling + wavetable.
You can "replay the samples and oscillator waveforms through emulations of the
signal paths of early digital samplers such as the Fairlight and Emulator."
Imports unlimited-length WAV/AIFF samples. Downside: sounds share a "common
underlying character."

SEM V → V3: redesigned UI, expanded modulation, keeps the "Oberheim-y character."
New-to-collection: Synthx V and MiniBrute V (MiniBrute V offers eight-voice
polyphony). Augmented gained Mallets + Yangtze; all seven Augmented instruments
improved morphing/modulation/arpeggiation/effects/UIs.

Pricing: full VC11 €699 (~€15/instrument). Intro €199 = 10 instruments.
Notes: minor Pure LoFi bugs; convoluted upgrade pricing; no dedicated drum
machine; the Augmented instruments "deserve much more than a casual glance" for
texture work.
