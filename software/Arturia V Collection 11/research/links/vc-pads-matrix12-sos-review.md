Why the Matrix-12 V is the deepest-modulating pad machine in the bundle (Oberheim
Matrix-12 / Xpander emulation).

**Modulation matrix:** **40 routing slots** (the original had 20), **27 sources →
47 destinations.** Spreadsheet-style assignment. This is the single biggest reason to
reach for it for *complex evolving* pads — you can stack many slow modulators onto
cutoff, pan, oscillator pitch, FM amount, etc., each from a different source.

**Filters:** **15 different filter types** (the legendary Xpander/Matrix-12 multimode
filter). "The flexibility of its filter section is amazing." Supports audio-rate filter
modulation for aggressive texture.

**Modulation generators (per voice):**
- **5 five-stage envelopes (DADSR-ish contours)**
- **5 LFOs** including noise + sample-and-hold waveforms
- 1 dedicated vibrato generator
- **3 tracking generators** (reshape any function — e.g. bend a mod curve)
- **4 ramp generators**

**Multi mode:** layer up to **6 different Single patches** across keyboard zones at once
— treat each as a separate synth. Enables "rotating" and quasi-random voicing, and
"quite extreme effects" via independent per-voice modulation.

**Pad design advantage:** extensive routing + 5 envs + 5 LFOs per voice = pads with
multiple independent evolving layers, each driven by a different mod source. The richest,
most complex bed in the collection — but the slowest to program.

**Rig takeaway:** Matrix-12 V = the "set up many slow LFOs/envs and let it churn" choice
for a deep, living wall. Use the 15-mode filter for character; route 2–3 slow LFOs (at
different rates) to cutoff/pan/PWM for an ever-shifting drone. Matrix-12 = complexity;
Prophet-VS = vector morph; CS-80 = expressive brass-pad. Pick by how much you want to
program vs play.
