Brief notes on the vintage keys/organs for lo-fi character work under the aesthetic.

## CP-70 V (Yamaha CP-70 electro-acoustic grand, 1976) — the felt-ish lo-fi piano
- Modeled with a **mix of samples + circuit modeling**: raw CP-70 sound +
  modeled output stage, preamps, EQ, tremolo.
- Front panel: volume, 3-band EQ (bass/mid/treble), tremolo (depth + speed).
- **Advanced mode**: velocity curve, tuning, AR envelope of the samples, and
  **output noise level** — push the noise + soften the EQ for a dusty/felt vibe.
- **Pedal rig: 5 slots, 13 effects + convolution reverb** built in.
- Character: "introspective, long envelopes melt together in ambient
  soundscapes" — the standout key here for drone/ambient beds. Run it into
  EchoBoy/Valhalla for a lo-fi felt-piano wall.

## The combo organs (dirty/wheezy lo-fi)
- **Farfisa V** + **VOX Continental V2** = the cheap, reedy, slightly out-of-tune
  '60s combo-organ sound — inherently lo-fi; good for a sustained drone bed or a
  buzzy organ pad. Add their built-in/external amp + spring reverb for grit.
- **B-3 V2** (Hammond + Leslie) — the full tonewheel/rotary sound; less "lo-fi"
  but the Leslie + overdrive can get gnarly for a swirling drone.

## The electric pianos / clav (texture layers)
- **Stage-73 V2** (Rhodes) — softer chordal bed; **Wurli V3** — barkier, hollow
  layer; **Clavinet V** — percussive/rhythmic, good for a dry plucky texture
  under banjo. In real projects these stack: Stage-73 chords + Wurli soft layer +
  Clavinet rhythmic riffs.
- All have modeled imperfections (velocity curves, mechanical noise, pickup/amp
  modeling) → lean into the noise floor + amp/cab + tremolo for the dirty,
  "recorded-wrong" character rather than a clean studio key.

> Verdict for THIS rig: these are *supporting* lo-fi colors, not flagships. The
> CP-70 (ambient/felt) and the Farfisa/VOX combo organs (dirty drone bed) are the
> two most aesthetic-relevant. Everything else = reach for when you want a real
> playable key with built-in tape/amp grit.
