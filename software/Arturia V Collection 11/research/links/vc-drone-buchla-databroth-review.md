West-Coast synthesis fundamentals, as framed by this review:
- The defining trait vs. Moog/"East Coast": "using lowpass gates, instead of filters,
  usually some sort of wave shaping." LPGs (not resonant filters) + wavefolding give the
  distinctly plucky/organic, percussive tonal quality.
- Intentionally inverted/counterintuitive logic: on the Easel "attack and decay times are
  long on the bottom, and short up top" — designed to push you to explore by feel rather
  than build predictable patches.

Generative / gesture tools on the front panel:
- Arpeggiator, a clock pulser, and a five-step voltage sequencer (settable to 3 or 4 steps).
  The "odd" default of 5 steps is deliberate Buchla aesthetic — encourages polyrhythm.
- "Gravity" tab = Arturia's added experimental gestural modulator: an XY controller with a
  bouncing ball; you place walls, portals, and gravity / anti-gravity objects so the ball's
  path modulates parameters — organic physics-based evolution with no manual sequencing.

Verdict for our use: not a conventional sound-design box; it shines "for bridging new sonic
ideas" — experimental textures, generative evolving material, rather than emulating real
instruments. Exactly the drone/texture-generator role.
