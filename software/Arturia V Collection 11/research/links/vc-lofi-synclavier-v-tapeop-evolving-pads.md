The Synclavier emulation — additive+FM, built for long evolving ambient pads.

## Architecture (additive ⨉ FM)
- **12 partials** (orig had 4). Each partial: an **additive oscillator (up to 24
  harmonics)** as the FM **carrier**, modulated by **another additive waveform
  (up to 24 harmonics)** — not just a sine. Two complementary synthesis methods
  in one voice → "timbral complexity... unique and very powerful even in 2016."

## Timbre Slices (orig "Timbre Frames") — the drone/evolution engine
- **Sequence up to 50 Timbre Slices** = 50 snapshots of the 12-partial timbre.
- **Parameter crossfades up to 30 s between slices**, over a total of **up to 5
  minutes** → long, continuously-morphing ambient pads and cinematic beds. This
  is the headline feature for drone/textural work.

## Evolving-pad workflow
1. Build one partial's additive waveform + envelope.
2. Use it as the FM carrier; modulate with a 24-harmonic additive waveform.
3. Add a second envelope between modulator↔carrier for time-based FM evolution.
4. Stack across the 12 partials.
5. Sequence through multiple Timbre Slices with long crossfades.
→ continuously shifting, "organic" textures that subtractive analog can't do.

## Why it fits the aesthetic
"Especially ideal for extended pads and slow cinematic, ambient soundscapes."
The additive-FM hybrid makes "entirely new but still 'real' sounds." Deep, but
the Timbre-Slice sequencer is the fast path to a 2–5 minute evolving drone bed.
