A producer's real reach-for list — character-driven (pick the tool for the
emotional/textural identity, not the spec):

- CMI V (Fairlight) — opening textures + glassy/'80s character: "raw,
  unmistakably '80s character — glassy, sharp, and slightly alien." Grounds a
  track in retro digital warmth.
- EMULATOR II V — emotional, lo-fi FOUNDATION work: "imperfect texture,"
  "subtle pitch drift," "grainy depth." Ideal for sample-based emotional passages
  — exactly the degraded/"recorded-wrong" aesthetic.
- DX7 V — reliable FM bass + access to the original presets.
- SOLINA V — synth strings + atmospheric sweeps; "thick, lush" with "analog
  shimmer." The string-machine pad for nostalgic / shoegaze beds.
- VOCODER V — voice processing + formant design; scales "from Kraftwerk to
  Burial" — i.e. usable for dark, degraded vocal-texture sound design, not just
  robot voices.

PHILOSOPHY: these are "living instruments," not museum pieces — choose by the
character a section needs. The CMI/Emulator pairing = the lo-fi sampling core;
Solina = the string wash; DX7 = the FM anchor.
