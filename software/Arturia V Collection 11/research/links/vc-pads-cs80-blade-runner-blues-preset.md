A free, copyable CS-80 V patch recreating Vangelis's "Blade Runner Blues" — "one of the
most expressive patches Vangelis ever did on the CS-80."

**Maker:** Paul Schilling (synthesist/sound designer).
**What it recreates:** the Blade Runner Blues lead/pad CS-80 patch.
**How:** built from a default patch with "many hours of micro-tweaking every possible
parameter" + extensive A/B vs the original — NOT from preset buttons. Available via the
article's Google Drive link (a bank-file format was added later for users who hit import
issues). **Requires CS-80 V3 or later** (so V4 is fine).

**The load-bearing design notes (copyable principles):**
- **Sensitive, fine-tuned velocity AND aftertouch response — with DIFFERENT settings for
  each of the two synth lines (Voice I vs Voice II).** This per-voice touch asymmetry is
  what makes the patch breathe; reproduce by setting Touch Response independently on the
  two channels.
- The patch is fundamentally an **expressive-performance** sound, not a static preset —
  it lives on how you play velocity/AT and the ribbon.

**To complete the full Vangelis bed (Schilling's additions):**
- A pad background using Roland VP-03 strings to emulate the VP-330 → in this rig =
  **Solina V2 / Mellotron V**.
- Rhodes piano flourishes → **Stage-73 V**.

**Rig takeaway:** the actionable lesson is the per-voice velocity/aftertouch asymmetry —
set Voice I and Voice II to respond differently to touch, and the CS-80 wall stops sounding
like a static synth pad and starts sounding orchestral/alive under the banjo.
