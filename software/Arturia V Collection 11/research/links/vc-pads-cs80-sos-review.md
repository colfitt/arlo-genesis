Authoritative architecture breakdown of the Arturia CS-80 emulation.

**Dual-voice (dual-layer) architecture** — the heart of the synth. Each of the two
oscillators has its OWN dedicated filters and contour generators, arranged in two rows
named **"Voice I" and "Voice II."** A **Mix** fader balances the two. Building a pad =
treat them as two stacked sub-synths (detune one, octave-shift one, brighten one).

**Oscillators/waveforms:** four basic waveforms per oscillator — triangle, sawtooth,
pulse, noise. Quirk: the "sawtooth" is actually a **ramp (rising sawtooth)**. Two
waveform-selector switches let you combine **pulse+saw or pulse+triangle** per voice.
Six footage (octave) options per voice. The "squared sawtooth" character is what makes
this THE synth for stringy-brass / brassy-string tones.

**Filters:** each voice has a resonant **high-pass AND a resonant low-pass** in series,
with selectable **12 or 24 dB/oct** slope. (The HP/LP pair per voice = the 4 filters.)

**Contour generators are NOT standard ADSR** — they're the unusual CS-80 **IL/AL/ADR**
shape: **Initial Level, Attack Level, Attack Time, Decay Time, Release Time.** This is
why pads breathe differently here — you set a starting level and an attack-peak level,
not just times.

**Ring modulator:** modulation speed + amount, with its own Attack/Decay envelope —
the metallic/sci-fi 1970s textures.

**Sub-oscillator = a global LFO** (one per patch, affects BOTH voices): routable to
pitch (VCO), filter cutoff (VCF), and amp gain (VCA). The pad "movement" knob.

**The iconic sound:** the review confirms the emulation nails "the slow, swelling solo
brass patch so beloved of Vangelis on Chariots of Fire and, in particular, Blade
Runner" — near-identical to hardware when settings are matched.

**Rig takeaway:** for walls, the two independent voices + HP/LP-per-voice + the global
sub-osc LFO give you a self-modulating pad before you add any external FX. Use the
IL/AL/ADR contour for slow swells; ring mod for degraded metallic edges into Decapitator.
