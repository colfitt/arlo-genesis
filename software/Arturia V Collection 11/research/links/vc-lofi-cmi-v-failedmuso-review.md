The Fairlight CMI Series IIx emulation — its gritty sampling + Page R + additive.

## Sampling / import (NOT live sampling)
- CMI V has **no live sampling** — you **import WAV files** into any of the 10
  sound slots (folder icon on each slot, or the Browse tab for the factory
  library). It also reads **original CMI .VC files** (some final Series II
  revisions are flaky), so vintage Fairlight libraries found online can be
  "brought back to life."
- Tape Op: "the entire original Fairlight CMI sample library" is included, "can
  easily import your own sounds," **up to 30 s at 24-bit/44.1 kHz** with optional
  downsampling for a "more authentic '80s sound."

## The gritty lo-fi controls (Edit tab) — the whole point
- **Sample rate**: adjustable down from 44.1 kHz to as low as **~2 kHz**. (The
  classic Fairlight character lives around **14 kHz / 8-bit**.) Right-click drag
  for big sweeps; you watch the waveform visibly degrade.
- **Bit depth**: variable; classic = **8-bit**. You can go down to **6 bits**
  and still have a usable (dirty) sound; **1-bit** = on/off square chaos.
- There's already "a certain gritty digital texture" to many sounds **even
  before** you lower rate/depth — the emulation bakes in the converter grunge.
- Also per-slot: **Filter Low (high-pass)** + **Filter High (low-pass)** (note:
  inverted naming logic), **Gain** (push for clipping/square-wave distortion),
  **Reverse**, **Invert (phase)**, loop + BF (backwards-forwards) loop mode.

## Page R sequencer
- Modernized: **8 tracks** (orig 6), **32 steps** (orig 16), light-pen-style
  "draw" interface, pattern-based.
- (CMI overall: 10 tracks, 32-note polyphony per track, full mixer + one effect
  per track + an aux-bus effect, e.g. reverb on the aux.)

## Additive / Time Synth / Spectral Synth
- **Time Synth**: draw up to **32 harmonics**/wavetables with per-harmonic
  amplitude envelopes; convert a sample → wavetable via **FFT analysis** ("Convert
  to Synthesis" → Analyze) and resynthesize it.
- **Spectral Synth** (new, not on originals): a higher-level, easier additive
  approach over 32 harmonics with evolution slots.
- Cross-synthesis: FFT-analyze a sample into harmonics + envelopes, then morph.

## Named factory presets
Orch5, Orch2, Sararr, Swannee, Kotobend, Cello 2 (the iconic '80s stabs/hits).

## Verdict
"The best way to get your hands on a really good CMI recreation outside of
getting the real thing." Factory patches lean '80s, but it rewards creative
sample mangling.
