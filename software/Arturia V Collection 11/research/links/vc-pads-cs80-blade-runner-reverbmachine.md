reverbmachine.com · "Vangelis' Blade Runner Synth Sounds" (Tears in Rain deconstruction) · undated (~2020)

The single best deconstruction of the Blade Runner CS-80 sound, focused on "Tears in
Rain" (Roy Batty's final monologue). Vangelis improvised cues live to footage; his
tape rig was noisy so he leaned on dbx noise reduction and minimal overdubs — natural,
unprocessed performances were the priority.

**The three layers of "Tears in Rain":**
- **Melody** = Fender Rhodes (with onboard graphic-EQ mods) + chorus for lushness.
  Recreation: Arturia **Stage-73 V** "Clean Mark V" preset.
- **String/choir chords** = Roland **VP-330** lush string-synth/choir (marketed as a
  vocoder but used as a string machine). Vangelis likely rode the VP-330 tone fader
  live during the take. → in this rig, **Solina V2** or **Mellotron V** fills this slot.
- **Brass swells** = Yamaha **CS-80** (he owned FOUR of them). This is the signature.

**Why the CS-80 brass sounds alive:** velocity + **polyphonic aftertouch** make the
brightness and volume of *each note* respond to touch. On the original recording you
can hear the brass shift brightness/intensity note-to-note — "an almost orchestral
quality." The **ribbon controller** does the dramatic descending pitch slides you hear
near the end of the cue (in the plugin: pitch-bend / the on-screen ribbon).

**Reverb = Lexicon 224** (Vangelis's choice from 1980 on): a huge, cavernous wash that
drenches the otherwise-dry CS-80 and makes it epic. The author recreates it with
**Valhalla VintageVerb on a send** (not an insert) — mirroring '80s studio practice —
plus a Space-Echo-style delay routed across tracks for ambience.

**Full modern recreation chain (author's):**
- Rhodes → Arturia Stage-73 V "Clean Mark V"
- Brass → Arturia **CS-80 V** (custom patch; free preset offered in the article)
- High strings → Arturia **Prophet-5 V** — LFO + mod wheel applying vibrato to **only
  one of the two oscillators** (the classic Vangelis detune-shimmer trick)
- Reverb → Valhalla VintageVerb on a send + tape/Space-Echo delay

**Honest caveat:** the article does NOT publish the raw CS-80 V patch parameters
(cutoff/resonance/envelope numbers) — it ships them as a free downloadable preset and
emphasizes expressive real-time performance over static settings. For copyable numbers,
see the Jon Audio CS-80 V4 transcripts (classic-pad + tips), which DO give exact values.

**Rig relevance:** this is the blueprint for the user's "lush evolving wall under
banjo" — a CS-80 brass-pad + a string-machine bed (Solina/Mellotron) + a big send
reverb (Valhalla, already owned) + tape delay (EchoBoy, already owned). Ribbon/AT for
the slow swells; keep performances live and a touch noisy to match the aesthetic.
