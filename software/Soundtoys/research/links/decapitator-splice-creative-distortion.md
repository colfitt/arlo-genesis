# Decapitator — creative distortion recipes

## Controls (concise)
- **DRIVE** raises gain + distortion. **PUNISH** = +20 dB instant.
- **MIX** = parallel compression-style blend (dry + saturated).
- **TONE** = gentle tilt EQ (dark boosts lows / bright boosts highs).
- **LOW CUT** removes lows *before* the saturation circuit (stops flabby low-end
  distortion). **HIGH CUT** removes highs *after* saturation (kills fizz).
- **STEEP** = high-cut slope 6 dB → 30 dB per octave.
- **THUMP** = a few dB of LF boost *at* the low-cut frequency, *before* the circuit
  → punch.

## Concrete instrument recipes
- **Drums:** Style **P**, Drive 6+, minimal everything else → "crunchy" punch
  without squashing.
- **Bass:** **Punish on**, Low Cut about midway so the distortion doesn't get
  flabby.
- **Electric guitar (DI):** Style **N**, Drive ~9, Tone toward bright, High Cut
  raised slightly to remove the "cheap and digital" fizz.

## Principle
Running Decapitator across *multiple* tracks (same/similar style) gives a whole
mix unified "character and edge" — a console-color move, not per-instrument
surgery.
