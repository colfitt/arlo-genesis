Four concrete copyable bass/808 recipes (low-end is one of Decapitator's strongest jobs —
harmonics make a sub read on small speakers without raw volume).

## Subtle warmth (thicken without obvious distortion)
- **Style:** A (Ampex) or N (Neve) — smoother, warmer.
- **Drive:** 1-3, raised gradually while listening.
- **Tone:** small nudges for clarity (bright) or low-end weight (dark).
- **Mix:** 100%.

## Attack enhancement (define the transient)
- **Style:** E (EMI) — more aggressive, defined transient.
- **Drive:** raised slightly until the initial attack becomes more prominent.
- **Tone:** slight upper-mid / bright nudge to enhance attack + clarity.
- **Mix:** 100%.

## Aggressive distortion
- **Style:** T (triode) or P (pentode) for pronounced distortion.
- **Drive:** increased significantly; **Punish ON** for the extra gain/intensity.
- **High Cut:** used to manage harsh highs; **Low Cut:** tighten the low end if it gets muddy.
- **Mix:** blend to taste so the distortion doesn't overwhelm.

## Parallel processing
Route the 808/bass to an aux with **extreme Decapitator settings**, then blend the distorted
aux back with the original — character + grit without sacrificing the clean low-end integrity.

Cross-check (the Splice article agrees): a common bass move is **Punish ON + Low Cut ~midway**
to add musical distortion while preventing flabby low end.
