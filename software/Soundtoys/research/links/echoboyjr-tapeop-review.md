tapeop.com · Tape Op review, John Baccigaluppi · (orig. price $99)

The definitive "why reach for Jr" review from a working engineer.

## The workflow argument (the whole point of Jr)
- The reviewer **prefers the original EchoBoy's sound** but found it **slower to dial
  in than Pro Tools' stock delay** — too many options for a quick job. Jr solves
  exactly that: "the **best of both worlds**" — keeps the analog EchoBoy sound but
  with simplified controls so setup is fast.
- Despite being stripped down, Jr "**still sounds great and has tons of analog
  vibe**."
- Verdict: it became his "**new go-to delay plug-in for all jobs, simple to complex**."
  Closing line: "**It tastes great, and it's less filling for sure!**"

## Style note relevant to this rig
- The **Ambient** style is called out as getting into a "**Daniel Lanois zone**"
  (warm, diffused, atmospheric) — directly on-aesthetic for ambient/post-rock walls.
- Memory = bucket-brigade emulation; Transmitter = resonant CB-radio style (called
  out by name).

## Controls confirmed
- All params on the main panel, no hidden menus: Mix, Echo Time, Feedback, Low Cut,
  High Cut, Input, Output, Saturation. Time syncs to DAW in note values or ms. Three
  stereo modes (Normal / Wide / Ping-Pong).

## When to reach for Jr (reviewer's framing)
- Jr suits anyone needing **quick, straightforward delays** without EchoBoy's
  extensive tweak options — i.e. when the decision is "what flavor of analog echo,"
  not "design a custom tape circuit."
