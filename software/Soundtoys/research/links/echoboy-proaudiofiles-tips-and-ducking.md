## Ducking a vocal/lead delay (EchoBoy has no internal sidechain → do it externally)
- EchoBoy doesn't duck itself. Standard move: put EchoBoy on a **send/return**, then put a **compressor
  with an external sidechain** after it, keyed from the dry vocal/lead. 2–4 dB of gain reduction while the
  part plays → the delay tucks under; at the end of each phrase the key drops and the delay **blooms into
  the gap.** (musictech.com / puremix technique-of-the-week.)
- In Logic this = a bus send to an EchoBoy aux, Compressor in sidechain mode keyed from the lead bus. In
  Ableton this = the auto-ducking-delay return-track recipe (Compressor with sidechain on the return).

## Delay throws
- Bus the lead to an EchoBoy send (often **1/8 or 1/16**), **automate the send up** on the specific word/
  phrase you want to echo → classic "throw." Pick a tape/Memory-Man style so the throw contrasts the dry.

## EchoBoy as saturator/tape (recurring power tip across sources)
- Time + Feedback at 0 → the styles become saturation colors. If you don't own a tape plugin, EchoBoy is
  a credible **tape simulator** for warming vocals/busses. (Watch the Width/Diffusion/Wobble phasing
  caveat — see magneticmag capture.)

## Filter default (Brett's saved default — the "Abbey Road" delay setting)
- HPF ~**500 Hz @ 12 dB/oct** + LPF ~**8 kHz @ 6 dB/oct** on the delay → pushes echoes into the midrange
  so they don't mud the mix or mask the lead. He's used it as his EchoBoy default for ~10 years.
