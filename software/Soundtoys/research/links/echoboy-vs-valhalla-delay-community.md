KVR Audio (Effects Forum) · "Best delay ATM? EchoBoy or Valhalla Delay?" + related threads · community

Distilled community consensus on EchoBoy vs ValhallaDelay (and the wider "why EchoBoy" question).
Cross-referenced with Produce Like A Pro's delay roundup and the Gearspace "EchoBoy vs ValhallaDelay"
thread (both 403 to direct fetch, summarized via search snippets).

## Why people reach for EchoBoy
- The **30 modeled styles** (EchoPlex, Space Echo, Memory Man, DM-2, TelRay oil-can) + Low/High Cut +
  Saturation + Input/Output let you sculpt repeats "crystal clear, warbly, warm, dull, brash, etc."
- The **Saturation section** is the headline: "instantly add classic tape vibe to your delays." Many users
  **skip the delay entirely** (Feedback + Time at 0) and use EchoBoy purely as a **color/saturation unit**
  — "the saturation and filters sound fantastic combined with the unique timbres of the 30 styles."
- "You could likely make do using only EchoBoy" — it's a delay + chorus + reverb + saturator in one.
- More options than first appears: hidden Tweak dropdowns reveal extra knobs (user *an-electric-heart*).

## Where Valhalla wins
- **ValhallaDelay** = also a great character box (set delay 0 ms / no feedback, turn up Drive/Age) and a
  **20-second delay buffer → great looper** (user *gentleclockdivider*). More modes (tape, BBD, 80s
  digital, hi-fi/lo-fi), more intuitive, faster.
- **Valhalla H-Delay** (Waves H-Delay framing): dead-simple, **zero added latency** (good for tracking/
  cue mixes), 14 modes. EchoBoy by contrast "has a lot of features you'll want to get comfortable with."

## The honest summary
- Both are quality. **EchoBoy = deeper vintage-hardware emulation + the strongest saturation/character
  shaping**; Valhalla = easier, more modes, better looping, lower friction. Replika XT and Color Copy
  named as character-delay alternatives (Replika = crystal-clear *or* very dirty; Color Copy = dark/dirty).

## Rig implication (drone/tape)
- For *character + tape generation-loss decay + the saturation-only color trick*, EchoBoy is the pick.
  For *long-buffer looping/freeze-style ambient*, ValhallaDelay's looper edges it. The user owns both
  worlds (Valhalla reverbs in software; long-buffer/freeze on the hardware side via TimeLine/Big Sky).
