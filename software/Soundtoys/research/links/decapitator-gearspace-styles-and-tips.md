# Decapitator — Gearspace community tips & favorite styles

## Style mapping (community-corroborated; matches the manual)
- **A** = Ampex 350 tape preamp — the Sun Studios / Motown-50s tape color. Smooth,
  tube-driven, least instrument-specific, "sounds good on anything."
- **E** = Chandler/EMI TG channel — full bass + silky highs.
- **N** = Neve 1057 preamp — full low-end without losing mids.
- **T** = Thermionic Culture Vulture, **Triode** ("T for Triode").
- **P** = Thermionic Culture Vulture, **Pentode** — less harmonically rich, raw.

## Concrete community recipes
- **Subtle warm/glue:** Decapitator at **10–20% Mix with Drive at 2 or less.** The
  "tone control that injects magic" — warm/brighten without obvious distortion.
- **The "fizz to taste" trick:** **N mode, Drive 7, Bright up to full 10** → a
  controllable fizz you can blend in with Mix. (Bright-then-blend.)
- **Distorted guitars:** into Decapitator on **N mode, Drive till it's just
  crackling, then Steep + High Cut till the gross fizz is gone**, then Bright/Dark
  to taste.
- **Subtle saturator via external gain-staging:** put a gain plugin (e.g. Sonalksis
  FreeG) *before* Decapitator to lower input, then another *after* to make up gain
  — lets you sit Decapitator in a gentle window. (A manual version of Auto.)

## Recurring community consensus
"Decapitator really shines when it is NOT used for distortion — the key is
subtlety: warm or brighten without distorting, think of it as a tone control that
also injects magic." The most-repeated piece of advice on the plugin.
