# Decapitator — notable users & techniques (sourced, flagged)

## Strong, sourced names
- **Tchad Blake** (Radiohead, Black Keys, Tom Waits) — quoted on Soundtoys' own
  page: **"I like it on everything."** The most aesthetically-aligned name for a
  dark/saturated/"recorded-wrong" rig; also a documented EchoBoy user. *(Verified
  quote on vendor page.)*
- **Andrew Scheps** (Adele 21, RHCP, Metallica, Black Sabbath; famous
  in-the-box mixer) — uses Decapitator "constantly — on drums, guitars, synths,
  even vocals when he wants grit … sometimes subtle saturation, sometimes full-on
  mangling." *(From multiple Scheps interview write-ups; he is openly an
  almost-entirely-ITB mixer, so plugin saturation is core to his sound — high
  confidence, paraphrased not a direct quote.)*
- **Fabrice "Fab" Dupont** — "instavibe-in-a-box" *(vendor-page quote).*
- **Ryan Hewitt** (RHCP, Avett Brothers) — "Watch out! This thing will tear your
  head off!" *(vendor-page quote).*
- **Pete Weiss** (Tape Op reviewer / engineer) — uses it on "nearly every mix" to
  *sand down* harsh top-end on vocals while preserving transients with the Mix
  knob. *(From his Tape Op review — a real working-engineer workflow.)*
- **Bassy Bob** (Notorious B.I.G., Mary J. Blige) — uses Decapitator **in place of
  EQ in the vocal chain** (after de-esser + comp): "it adds harmonics to the vocal,
  and that equates to more high or low frequency detail, which sounds really musical
  inside the track." Saturation-as-EQ. *(Soundtoys "in the mix with Bassy Bob" page +
  SonicScoop.)*
- **Mike Shinoda** (Linkin Park) — used Decapitator on a 2020 "CoronaJam"
  Hybrid-Theory-style demo livestream. *(web/search snippet.)*
- **Qmillion** (mixer, SonicScoop "3 Techniques") — on **bass** for compression,
  sustain, and low-end body + light "pushing the amp slightly" distortion; runs it
  **parallel (Mix knob) on dirty synth leads.** *(SonicScoop page 403 on direct
  fetch — distilled from snippets; flagged.)*

## Soundtoys preset-author workflows (numeric presets are download-only)
- **Trevor Wright** (drums) — "Decapitator on drums is a no-brainer — it's the
  **first insert on the drum bus** of my template." Stood out **on drum samples to
  fatten them and help them translate on small speakers.** Recommends **E** style
  ("beefing things up while keeping the top-end nice"). Source:
  https://www.soundtoys.com/trevor-wright-drums-preset/
- **Matty Amendola** (bass) — uses **Punish mode** on a "Bass Hi" preset so "bass
  fills / finger movement cut through without muddying the low end"; keeps **a second
  bypassed Decapitator ready to blow something up** and **automates a Decapitator on
  the bass bus through the track.** Source: https://www.soundtoys.com/matty-amendola-bass/

No *named* drone/doom/shoegaze artist saying "I use Decapitator" surfaced. As with

invented.

## Technique threads (genre-aligned, from blogs/forums, not artist-attributed)
- Shoegaze chain convention: **distortion FIRST**, then modulation, then time/
  reverb — so Decapitator sits early as harmonic texture that layers under chorus/
  reverb to "blur note definition" and build the wall (output.com shoegaze guide).
- Decapitator → Valhalla as the saturate-then-wash move for ambient/shoegaze tails.
