Sound On Sound · review of Sound Toys EchoBoy · (review)

Pro-magazine framing of EchoBoy's character and the parameters that make it more than a delay.

## Character
- ~32 style presets including device emulations: Space Echo, TelRay, DM-2, Echoplex, Memory Man — but it
  "adds a creamy analogue warmth in the process" rather than being a sterile clone. Authentic character.

## The tape/generation-loss engine
- Style Editor's **Decay** works *cumulatively*: "each echo will get more and more filtered off, allowing
  you to emulate the generation loss that is such a prominent characteristic of tape delays." (Apply a cut
  to the High-frequency EQ's Decay knob → tails darken with each repeat.)
- Per-band three-band EQ (low + high shelf, mid peak with adjustable Q), **saturation and wobble are
  independently adjustable for both the main delayed signal AND the feedback path.**

## Wobble
- Varispeeds the wet signal → alters pitch + delay time together; sine/square/random-walk/S&H waveforms;
  simulates tape wow & flutter, but also does "rich chorus effects as well as swept flanges."

## Prime Numbers
- A switch that prevents phase coherence between repeats → reduces resonant build-up (cleaner long tails).

## Rhythm mode
- 16-step multitap with preset patterns: pick number of repeats, pattern length, decay shapes quickly.

## Groove & Feel
- Shuffle/Swing for the pattern; **Feel** = "Rushin' and Draggin'" — shift echoes earlier/later vs the
  input (negative or positive pre-delay), for an organic, off-grid feel.
