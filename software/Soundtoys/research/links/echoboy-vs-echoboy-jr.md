The owner has BOTH EchoBoy.component and EchoBoyJr.component installed (per CONTENTS.md). When to use which.

## What EchoBoy Jr. IS
- A stripped-down EchoBoy: **all controls on one panel, no hidden/nested Tweak menus.** Front-panel knobs:
  Mix, Echo Time, Feedback, Low Cut, High Cut, Input, Output, Saturation.
- A curated **7 styles** (the "most desirable" from EchoBoy): **Studio Tape, Plex, Space, Cheap Tape,
  Memory, Ambient, Transmitter.** Single-echo only.

## What you LOSE vs full EchoBoy
- The Style Editor (per-band EQ + **Decay** = the tape generation-loss engine), **Diffusion/Size/
  Loop-Post**, **Wobble** (wow/flutter), **Groove/Feel**, **Accent/Warp**, **Prime Numbers**,
  the **Dual-echo / Rhythm** 16-tap modes, and all the deep saturation routing.
- Jr. is **single-echo** (one delay line, no Dual/Rhythm multitap) — **but it DOES keep a 3-way
  Stereo Mode (Normal / Wide / Ping-Pong)**, so it is *not* mono-only. *(Correction 2026-06-13: the
  prior "mono-in single-delay, no ping-pong" claim was wrong per the EchoBoy Jr. v5 manual — see
  `EchoBoyJr-UsageGuide.md`.)*

## When to reach for which (this rig)
- **EchoBoy Jr.** = fast slapback / quick tape-warm echo / "stamp a Memory-Man or Space color and move
  on." Lower friction, lighter. Good as a quick coloring delay on a banjo/baritone insert.
- **Full EchoBoy** = anything *ambient/drone/evolving*: you need the Style Editor's Decay for generation
  loss, Loop-position Diffusion for the wash, Wobble for tape warble, Rhythm mode for evolving patterns,
  Prime Numbers for clean self-feeding clouds, and Dual/Ping-Pong for wide stereo tails. **For the
  drone/shoegaze tape aesthetic, use the full EchoBoy** — Jr. can't do the deep tape/ambient mangling.

## Note
- EchoBoy Jr. has been given away free by Soundtoys at times; both share the same engine/sound, Jr. just
  hides the depth. (Tape Op review: Jr. "sounds great, faster and easier, fewer options.")
