Equipboard · Soundtoys.com artist pages · Gearspace/Aliada · 2020–2021

Real, attributable uses — flagged by certainty. EchoBoy is a ubiquitous pro plugin, so this is genuine,
not invented; but most are general "they use it" data points rather than drone/shoegaze-specific.

## Strong / quoted
- **Greg Wells** (producer — Katy Perry, Adele, Dua Lipa). On his Soundtoys artist page:
  *"I'd be dead in the water without the Memory Man preset in EchoBoy."* Also cites the Katy Perry track
  **"By the Grace of God"** as showcasing EchoBoy prominently — the delay feedback intensifies at the end
  of the verses as it transitions into the chorus (= the automated-feedback bloom, the same trick the
  6-Tips video demos). Source: https://www.soundtoys.com/artist/greg-wells/
- **Manny Marroquin** (mix engineer — Rihanna, John Mayer, Kanye). Quoted:
  *"I also love the Valhalla reverb, the [Soundtoys] EchoBoy."* — note the EchoBoy + Valhalla pairing,
  exactly the in-the-box chain this rig leans on. Source: equipboard.com/pros/manny-marroquin
- **Jack Antonoff** (Bleachers; Taylor Swift, Lana Del Rey, Lorde). Per Sound On Sound / Equipboard:
  he tends to use Waves R-Verb + Valhalla Plate, **Soundtoys EchoBoy for delay**, and FilterFreak/PanMan/
  Crystallizer for extreme effects. (Relevant: Antonoff's productions lean retro/lo-fi/saturated.)
  Sources: equipboard.com/pros/jack_antonoff ; soundonsound.com/people/jack-antonoff

## Documented (listed in their plugin sets)
- **Andrew Scheps** (Adele, Red Hot Chili Peppers, Black Sabbath): uses EchoBoy "for building longer
  delays in individual mixes as needed," alongside H-Delay and FabFilter Timeless.
  Source: gearspace.com/board/interviews/1362076-interview-andrew-scheps.html
- **Tchad Blake** (Radiohead, Wilco, Black Keys, Tom Waits / The Latin Playboys): EchoBoy listed among
  his plugins as a "versatile delay plugin with character" for "creative echo effects." Tchad Blake is
  the most aesthetically aligned name here — his dark, saturated, lo-fi, "wrong"-sounding mixes are
  close cousins of the degraded/tape aesthetic this rig chases. Source: aliada.io/blog/tchad-blake
