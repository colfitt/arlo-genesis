## Community sentiment (workflow-first)
- Recurring take: users reach for **Jr more than the full EchoBoy** specifically
  because of the **simple one-panel layout** — minimal controls = faster decisions /
  better workflow. (Matches the Tape Op reviewer's conclusion.)
- **CPU:** people ask whether Jr is lighter than full EchoBoy; no hard published
  number surfaced. Jr is the stripped engine (no Rhythm/16-tap, no Diffusion-loop, no
  Wobble, no Style Editor), so it is reasonably lighter and quicker to instantiate at
  scale — but treat "much lighter CPU" as **inferred, not benchmarked**. Both share
  the same core sound.

## Availability / packaging (matters for "do I even have it")
- EchoBoy Jr. is **not sold individually** — it ships in the **Soundtoys 5 Bundle**
  (alongside Devil-Loc, Little MicroShift, Little PrimalTap, Little Radiator).
  (sinesquares "definitive guide" footnote.)
- Soundtoys has **given Jr away free** more than once — most recently the **Dec 2025
  "12 Days of Soundtoys" holiday giveaway** (free ~Dec 8–11, 2025). So a lot of people
  own Jr without owning full EchoBoy. (rekkerd.org / offtheknob.com / synthanatomy.)
- Original standalone-era price was ~$99 (Tape Op).

## Net
- Jr's value proposition in the community is **speed + the genuine EchoBoy analog
  sound**, not features. If you already own the full EchoBoy (this rig does), Jr earns
  its insert slot purely as the **fast/low-friction** option, not a sonic upgrade.
