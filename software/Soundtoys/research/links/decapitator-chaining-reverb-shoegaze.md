# Decapitator — chain placement into reverb/delay (shoegaze/ambient/drone)

## Where it sits in the chain
- **Shoegaze/wall convention: distortion FIRST → modulation → time/reverb.** Put
  Decapitator EARLY, before the chorus/flanger/vibrato and before the Valhalla
  wash. The saturation adds harmonic complexity that *layers* and blurs note
  definition rather than biting — "distortion as texture."
- **Saturate, THEN wash:** Decapitator → Valhalla (VintageVerb / Supermassive /
  Shimmer). The harmonics generated upstream get smeared into the tail, giving the
  saturated-atmosphere quality of shoegaze/ambient — instead of trying to dirty a
  clean reverb tail after the fact.
- **Saturating a reverb RETURN:** alternatively put Decapitator (low Drive, low
  Mix, A/E/N) *on the reverb aux's output* to grime up the tail itself — adds
  analog grit/age to an otherwise pristine Valhalla wash. Watch aliasing here
  (reverb tails are full-spectrum) — High Cut + Steep.
- **After a delay:** Decapitator post-delay adds overtones/analog character and
  tames the "too crisp" digital-repeat sound (per the AudioPluginNews tip) — the
  in-the-box analog-delay-feel move that complements the hardware Generation Loss.

## Drone-specific
- Valhalla Supermassive's slow-attack modes (Andromeda) turn even percussive input
  into a long drone; feeding that with a Decapitator-saturated source gives a
  thick, harmonically-rich sustained wall. Saturate the source, not (only) the
  tail, to keep the drone dense without making the reverb itself harsh.
