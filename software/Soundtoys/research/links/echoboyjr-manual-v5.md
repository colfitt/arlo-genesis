## Design intent (verbatim framing)
- "We designed EchoBoy Jr. as a **simplified version of EchoBoy** that still provides
  the necessary controls and parameters to emulate many classic and contemporary
  delay devices. Our main goal was to **strip away the complex tweak options** in
  EchoBoy and provide you with a delay that is simple to use but still offers the
  exceptional sound that EchoBoy has become known for."
- The Soundtoys creed restated in this manual too: "echoes sound better when the
  repeat does NOT sound exactly like the input."

## Full control panel (everything on ONE page — no nested menus)
- **Mix** — dry/wet; 12 o'clock = 50/50; past 12 = dry drops out. In-line → use Mix;
  on a bus/send → 100% wet + use send returns.
- **Echo Time** — knob, or **type into the LCD readout**, or browse via up/down menu
  arrows. Selectable by the four red buttons: **Time** (ms), **Note**, **Dot**, **Trip**.
  Note/Dot/Trip = tempo-synced divisions **1/2 down to 1/64**, auto-syncing to DAW
  tempo. (Short-time creative ranges: 0–10 ms = flanging, 10–50 ms = chorus/doubling,
  100–200 ms = slapback.)
- **Feedback** — number of repeats; high/near-max = "runaway" self-oscillation (the
  RE-201 Space trick). **Warning in the manual: high Feedback can boost output level
  significantly** — be careful at volume.
- **Stereo Mode** (3 radio buttons, left side): **Normal** (centered, somewhat narrow)
  · **Wide** (slight L/R delay offset → much wider image) · **Ping Pong** (repeats
  bounce L↔R).
- **Glide** (button): ON = old-tape/analog behavior — adjusting delay time pitch-
  shifts the audio (creative pitch jumps between note values); OFF = retime with no
  pitch artifact (easier to dial time with Feedback up).
- **Echo Styles** (center): turn the Style knob or click the name. **7 styles** (see
  below).
- **Low Cut / High Cut** — 2-band tone on the echoes; High Cut "darkens" the delay
  (mimics analog HF roll-off), Low Cut clears bass; "effect depends greatly on the
  Echo Style selected." Used to shape distance/blend.
- **Input / Output Level** — replicate true analog input-section response; **affect the
  echo signal ONLY, dry unchanged**. LEDs: **yellow = 6 dB below clipping**, **red =
  max level / possible audible clipping** (optional as a sound). Input drives tone.
- **Saturation** — "quick and easy" tube/tape compression + emphasis + subtle
  distortion on the delay; lets Jr emulate many analog devices. Result depends on the
  style; "in some cases the Saturation changes may seem fairly subtle." Example given:
  on **Studio Tape**, more Saturation = odd-harmonic distortion in low/mid + HF
  compression → "a bit of automatic de-essing on the echo during louder vocal
  passages."

## The 7 styles (manual descriptions, verbatim sense)
- **Studio Tape** — "Subtle distortion and high frequency compression modeled after
  the **ATR-102 running at 15 ips**, typical of the sound of tracking to studio tape."
  (Note: full EchoBoy splits this into Master Tape @30ips + Studio Tape @15ips — Jr's
  single "Studio Tape" is the 15 ips flavor.)
- **Plex** — "modeled after the **EchoPlex EP-3** solid state tape echo." A true classic.
- **Space** — "modeled after the **Roland RE-201 Space Echo** we have here in the lab.
  Warm and gritty... staple sound of dub and reggae... self-oscillation achievable...
  utilizing the 'Feedback' control."
- **Cheap Tape** — "A Soundtoys original, modeled after... a variety of vintage
  consumer-grade tape stock. **Bright and very compressed** sounding."
- **Memory** — "**Warm low-bandwidth chorus echo**, modeled after the **Electro-Harmonix
  Memory Man** delay pedal."
- **Ambient** — "A combination of the **Distortion and Diffusion modes in EchoBoy**.
  Great for **long feedback loops and solo instruments**."
- **Transmitter** — "Similar to a **CB Radio** type response... very distorted and
  resonant in mid-frequencies. **Good for adding echo grit to synth sounds.**"

## What's explicitly NOT here (vs full EchoBoy)
The manual's whole pitch is "strip away the complex tweak options." Absent: the Style
Editor / per-band EQ + Decay (generation-loss engine), Diffusion/Size/Loop-vs-Post,
Wobble (wow/flutter), Prime Numbers, Feel/Accent/Groove/Warp, Dual + Rhythm (16-tap)
modes, and the full 30-style bank (Jr = 7 curated). Jr keeps the same core engine and
the ms/Note-Dot-Trip timing, Feedback self-oscillation, and the Saturation/Input
character — just on one panel.
