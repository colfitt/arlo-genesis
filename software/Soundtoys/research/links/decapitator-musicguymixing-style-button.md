# Decapitator — the five Style buttons and the best use for each

Authoritative-style mapping (corroborated across sources): the five Style buttons
model the saturation character of five different pieces of analog hardware.

- **A — Ampex 350 tape preamp.** "Ultra smooth tube-driven colorful sound." The
  least instrument-specific — "just sounds good on anything you want to impart
  classic analog tape color to." Suggested: **Drive 1–3, low wet/dry favoring
  dry.** Good for warming digital recordings/samples and vocals. *(This is the
  tape-flavored one — most aligned with a degraded-tape rig as an "always-on tape
  color" stage.)*
- **E — Chandler/EMI TG channel.** Balanced full bass + silky highs. Best on the
  **drum bus / mix busses** — a light "kiss" (low drive, low mix) that lifts
  kick/snare low-end while brightening cymbals. The classic Abbey Road console
  color.
- **N — Neve 1057 preamp.** Full low-end without sacrificing mids. The pick for
  **guitars, especially top-heavy/harsh sources** — designed to *tame* excessive
  brightness, smooth fizz, fix overly bright tracks. (Forum favorite for distorted
  guitar: N mode, drive till it just crackles, then high-cut the gross fizz out.)
- **T — Thermionic Culture Vulture, Triode setting.** "Warm punchiness." Fills out
  thin snares; adds fatness/roundness; vocal grit. Versatile across most sources.
- **P — Thermionic Culture Vulture, Pentode setting.** Less harmonically rich,
  more raw distortion. Best on **bass (synth + electric)** — adds mid/low-mid
  harmonics to make sub-heavy bass cut through. Also a "crunchy" drum pick.

The Culture Vulture (T/P) is itself a dedicated hardware distortion box — so T and
P are the most "this is a distortion unit" styles; A/E/N are more
console/preamp-color styles.
