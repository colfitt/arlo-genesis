The Pro Audio Files (Thomas Brett) · "6 Tips for Using Soundtoys EchoBoy" · 2020-05-17

Thomas Brett ("EchoBoy has been my delay of choice for the last 10 years"). Walks through five
electric-guitar parts in one track, each using EchoBoy differently. Cleaned/de-duplicated auto-captions.

## Tip 1 — "The Edge" sound (Memory Man style)
- For U2/The Edge-style 80s delay, just pick the **Memory Man** style — instantly gives the high-end
  roll-off + saturation of the real EHX pedal. No deep tweaking needed.
- Turn up the **Width** knob — it's "the closest thing to the Chorus knob on the Memory Man pedal" that
  EchoBoy offers. Recreates the pedal's built-in chorus.
- **Counter-intuitive:** when emulating a *distinct* classic unit (Memory Man, etc.), he leaves the
  Low/High Cut filters OFF to stay faithful to the unit's own voicing — even though he normally filters
  delays heavily. Let the style do the filtering.

## Tip 2 — Feedback to fill the gaps
- Feedback = number of repeats. Set it to "rhythmically fit the song." On a guitar part with gaps, dial
  feedback so the repeats fill the holes and die out exactly as the player comes back in.
- Mix knob ~**50%** is his go-to "amount of delay I'm after" when running EchoBoy inline (not on a send).

## Tip 3 — Filtering & the Abbey Road trick (Studio Tape style)
- **Studio Tape** style = multitrack tape machine: more saturated, compressed, mid-focused than clean
  digital. Bumped Width + Feedback for width and rhythmic match.
- Full-spectrum delay "gets in the way." His default Low/High Cut, analyzed with Q-Clone, happens to
  match the **Abbey Road reverb trick**: HPF ~**500 Hz / 12 dB** slope + LPF ~**8 kHz / 6 dB** slope.
  Pushes delays/reverbs into the midrange so they don't mud the mix or eat vocal clarity. He saved this
  as his EchoBoy default 10 years ago and still uses it.

## Tip 4 — EchoBoy as a saturator / distortion unit
- "Most people aren't aware of this": set **Echo Time = 0 and Feedback = 0**, then the different delay
  *styles* become saturation/distortion colors. Used the **Telephone** style here for a vintage
  telephonic distorted lead — EchoBoy as a distortion plugin.
- If you don't own a tape-machine plugin, use EchoBoy this way as a **tape simulator** to warm/saturate
  vocals.

## Tip 5 — Slapback (Telephone style)
- Slapback = short echo time **100–200 ms** + short feedback (Elvis/Lennon 50s–60s vocal trick). Here he
  pushed feedback *longer* than typical to get a "metallic spring-reverb" tail, still on Telephone style.

## Tip 6 — Ping-Pong for width
- Ping-Pong = two delays, one panned L one R, taking turns feeding back. His setting: **dotted-16th on
  the left, straight-16th on the right**, Memory Man style, lots of feedback. Pulls the ears wide.
- Note: when going stereo he then *did* filter the low end out — excess lows into the (ping-pong) tail
  caused muddy build-up.

## Bonus — Automate Feedback to infinity (the drone/synth-pad trick)
- For the last note of a solo he **automated the Feedback to infinite ("runaway")** so the note feeds
  back endlessly into a "weird psychedelic outer-space sound." Feeding almost any sound into infinite
  feedback yields glitchy, evolving textures "which can even be used like synth pads for your songs."
  (= the in-the-box equivalent of holding a tape-delay's feedback/oscillation for a sustained wall.)
