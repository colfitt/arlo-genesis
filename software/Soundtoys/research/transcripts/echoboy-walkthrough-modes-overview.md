## Signal flow & filters (important for tone-shaping)
- **Mix:** all-the-way wet on a return/send track; wet/dry blend when on an individual insert. "No real
  rules — as long as it sounds cool."
- **Low Cut is BEFORE the delay circuit; High Cut is AFTER it.** That ordering matters:
  - Low Cut in front = clean up the lows feeding *into* the delay → lets you "make massive clouds of
    feedback" without low-end mud building up.
  - High Cut after = roll the highs off the repeats so feedback isn't harsh.
- **Psychoacoustic tip:** high-cutting the repeats makes them feel like they're "moving away from you,"
  creating a sense of motion/depth — a darker tail recedes. (Directly useful for ambient depth.)

## Tempo / groove
- Middle section has the red MIDI light switch → locks to host tempo. Echo Time = ms, seconds, or note
  values (incl. dotted/triplet).
- **Swing / Shuffle / Rush / Drag** = nonlinear spacing between repeats. Swing = bluesy; Shuffle = more
  downbeat-oriented; the Feel (rush/drag) moves the first hit forward or back ("like dropping a coin on
  a table").

## The four modes
- **Single Echo** — your standard delay; can be stereoized with **Width** + **L/R Offset** (make a mono
  delay, or widen it).
- **Dual Echo** — two independent mono delays → polyrhythms. "Classic 16th on one side, 8th on the
  other" used all the time on drums/percussion.
- **Ping-Pong** — Ping waits for Pong, Pong waits for Ping → "pulls your ears really, really wide."
- **Rhythm** — like a drum machine; each green line is a tap/delay circuit; add/subtract/level taps.
  **Tip:** run a hi-hat through Rhythm mode for drum programming — "you end up with stuff you'd never get"
  drawing or performing it normally.

## The big idea: EchoBoy is ALSO a chorus and a reverb
- "There's chorus and there's reverb inside EchoBoy." When building an effects rack and you wish you had
  a reverb or chorus in your serial chain — **EchoBoy is your reverb and EchoBoy is your chorus.** Don't
  think of it as one or the other.
- **Chorus:** copy the signal, delay 20–50 ms, modulate the delay time, no feedback → thickening. Pan dry
  one way and wet the other = "a wicked widener" that stays natural and "cleans out the center." Lots of
  presets in the Chorus folder; great choruses (incl. CE-1).
- **Reverb:** EchoBoy has "a wonderful kind of metallic reverb sound" hiding in it. Throw it in the rack
  and wet/dry-mix in just the amount of reverb you want.
- Takeaway: "a very powerful, multifaceted tool — you should know it inside and out."
