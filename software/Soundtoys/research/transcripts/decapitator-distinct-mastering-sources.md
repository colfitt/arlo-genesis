Controls quick reference (from the bottom up): the **style buttons** model different
hardware — first is an Ampex, second an "EMT" [he means the EMI/Chandler TG], third an
"NE" [Neve], the **T** is the Thermionic Culture Vulture **even harmonics**, the **P** is
the Culture Vulture **pentode / odd harmonics**. Then: **Auto** (auto-gains the drive —
note the output drops when you raise drive), **Punish** (adds a lot more saturation),
**Low Cut** (filters lows before the saturation), **Thump** (adds low-end presence at the
low-cut point), **Tone** (dark/bright character), **High Cut** (cuts highs AFTER the
saturation — kills fizz), **Steep** (makes the high cut steeper), **Mix** (parallel — if
transients are getting smashed, drop to ~50% to get them back), and the **Attitude VU
meter** (not the output — it's how much "attitude"/aggression you're driving in).

**BASS (stutter-house track, bass that isn't in-your-face):** Solo the bass, drive it up.
Audition styles — the **E ("EMT")** style "just really works with this bass." Find the
parts that need emphasis, drive it, set the **low cut** so you don't saturate the low end,
and turn up **Thump** for weight (the bassline wasn't super-subby, so this re-adds body
instead of needing a layered sub an octave down). It's running ~100% wet; pull **mix** down
if you don't want full wet. There isn't much fizz, but you can high-cut + **Steep** if
needed. Result: the bass gets thickness and sticks out in the mix.

**PAD BUS (a square + two pads):** Saturating musical/tonal material — **be careful with
odd harmonics (P) on musical tones.** He picks **A** (Ampex), doesn't want all the low end,
wants it brighter, so with Auto on he raises **tone** toward bright (then tames it a touch).
It adds air/fizzle and a sense of cleanliness, letting the bass breathe better.

**DRUM BUS:** Throw Decapitator on, full drive, find a good style, then **parallel** it —
but first set the **low cut** so it doesn't touch the low end, then blend (mix down) so it
"helps my drums stick out without doing much." Saturate → low-cut → parallel.

**Auto-gain note:** Auto is great for gain-staging/level-matching on the way out — but **if
you actually want more output level out of the unit, turn Auto OFF**, otherwise it keeps
pulling the output down as you drive.
