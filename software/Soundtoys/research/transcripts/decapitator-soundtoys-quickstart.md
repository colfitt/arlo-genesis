Decapitator models the behavior of classic hardware preamps, consoles, and tube circuits
with the convenience of switching between models and adding insane amounts of gain.

- **Drive** operates like an input control — more drive = more saturation. Use it to subtly
  thicken a sound or push it for heavy, aggressive distortion.
- **Punish** — "If you want to really obliterate your sound source, slam that punish button
  to add an **extra 20 dB of gain**."
- **Auto** (enabled by default) automatically compensates for volume changes within the
  plug-in and turns down the output. Useful when A/Bing with the unprocessed sound. Flip it
  off to manually adjust output.
- **Tone, Low Cut, and Thump shape the sound BEFORE it hits the saturation stage.** Tone is
  a gentle sloping EQ from dark to bright. Low Cut removes unwanted lows before they distort,
  keeping things clean and focused. Thump adds a volume bump at the specified low-cut
  frequency for "the fat sound of analog tape."
- **High Cut** removes harsh/fizzy highs introduced by aggressive distortion.
- **Steep** increases the high-cut slope to **30 dB/oct**. **Concrete tip: with Steep ON, try
  setting High Cut between 4 kHz and 5 kHz — great for emulating a guitar amplifier speaker
  cabinet, or a guitar directed straight to the board (DI/direct-to-board sound).**
- **Mix** blends dry and processed for **parallel saturation** — perfect for retaining
  transients and clarity on things like drums and vocals.
- The **Style** buttons (bottom) switch between five saturation algorithms. Choose a style,
  then really drive Decapitator to bring out the character of that algorithm.
