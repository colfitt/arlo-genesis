# Loscil — the "only eight tracks" rack-switching live architecture

Loscil (Scott Morgan), ambient/drone elder statesman (Kranky). His live-set
architecture is a near-perfect description of how to perform ambient within Lite's
exact 8-track ceiling — even though his specific instruments are Suite.

## The Lite-shaped technique (sourced quote)
- "I can switch between racks on the fly... I can just trigger a new chain. It's
  something I've become quite reliant on in the live context because it allows me to
  have only eight tracks."
- = nest many sounds as CHAINS inside ONE Instrument Rack on ONE track; use the
  Chain Selector (or key/scene mapping) to switch which chain is active. One track
  holds a whole set's worth of voices.
- → This is fully Lite-loadable: Instrument Racks, chains, and the chain selector are
  all in Lite. It's arguably THE answer to "how do I perform a varied ambient set on
  8 tracks." Build: Track 1 = Instrument Rack with N Drift/Simpler chains, switch live.

## Hardware integration (maps to this rig)
- Routes hardware synths (Deckard's Dream, Novation Peak, Moog Minitaur) OUT into
  Ableton for "modulation and post-processing via LFOs, EQs, and compression."
  → Same pattern as feeding VG-800 / OP-1 / Digitakt into Live for stock-FX
  post-processing. (His LFO device is M4L — Lite swap = device-internal LFOs.)

## NOT Lite (flagged honestly)
- **Granulator** ("I use that religiously") — Suite. **Sampler** ("my desert island
  tool") — Suite (Lite has Simpler, not full Sampler). **Glue Compressor** — Suite
  (Lite has Compressor). Visual control = custom Max for Live patches → Push 3 → OSC
  → Resolume — all Suite/M4L.

## Verdict
Borrow the ARCHITECTURE (rack-chain switching to live within 8 tracks), not his exact
devices. Substitute Drift/Simpler for Granulator/Sampler, Compressor for Glue Comp.

HIGH — direct quote and interview. The "this is the Lite-performance answer" framing
is my mapping (high confidence: the architecture uses only Lite-present features).
