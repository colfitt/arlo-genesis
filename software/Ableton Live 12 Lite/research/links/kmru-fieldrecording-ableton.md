# KMRU — field-recording ambient/drone in Ableton (grid OFF)

Nairobi/Berlin ambient & drone artist (Editions Mego / OFNOT). Builds long-form
drones from field recordings inside Live.

## Workflow (sourced)
- Turns OFF the metronome/grid and warp; composes intuitively, not to a click —
  "I would not know what a recording's 'time' is, so I'd just approach composing
  more intuitively."
- Three uses of field recordings: (1) as finished compositions/performances,
  (2) as samples/instruments (resample + edit), (3) as raw texture for synthesis.
- Goes through banks of field recordings in Live looking for a building-block sound,
  then layers.
- Uses **Ableton Push** to arrange/structure/layer (Push not required; technique
  transfers to mouse/61SL).
- Granular: **Grain Scanner (Amazing Noises) Max for Live** device for spectral/
  texture work; also processes in Max/MSP sometimes.

## Lite mapping
- The CORE (drag field recordings onto audio tracks, warp OFF, stack/layer, intuitive
  arrangement) is fully Lite-safe. Maps directly to dropping OP-1 Field / Octatrack /
  phone field recordings into Live and building walls.
- The GRANULAR layer (Grain Scanner, Max/MSP) is **NOT Lite** (needs M4L). Lite
  substitute for texture: Simpler in granular-ish loop modes + Erosion + Redux +
  Reverb freeze-style long tails. Honest: it won't match true granular.
