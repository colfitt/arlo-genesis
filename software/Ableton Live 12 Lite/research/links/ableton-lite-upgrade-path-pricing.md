# Lite → Standard/Suite upgrade path & pricing

## How the upgrade works
- Register your **Live Lite serial** to your ableton.com account → the webshop
  then shows **discounted upgrade pricing** calculated from your registered
  license. You don't re-buy the full price.
- **You keep your license, preferences, and projects** when upgrading. Lite sets
  open unchanged in the higher edition (Lite is a strict subset).

## Concrete prices (2026)
- **Lite → Live 12 Standard: ~$189** (the discounted upgrade; full Standard is
  much more).
- Lite → Suite: more (Suite is the top tier; exact upgrade price shown in-account).
- **Registered Focusrite OR Novation customers save 20%** off the Standard
  upgrade — relevant here: the rig owns a **Novation 61SL MkIII**, so the user
  likely qualifies for the 20%-off Standard upgrade.
- Ableton seasonal sales (e.g. Spring) discount upgrades further: ~15% off
  Standard, ~25% off Suite have been seen — worth waiting for a sale.

## What each upgrade actually buys (for THIS rig)
- **Standard ($189, or ~20% less via Novation):** unlimited tracks (kills the
  8-track cap), 12 sends (vs 2), 256 I/O, audio-to-MIDI, audio slicing,
  **Comping** (take lanes), device/clip **modulation**, Complex/Complex-Pro warp,
  more effects/instruments. This is the upgrade that removes every Lite ceiling
  that bites a performer EXCEPT Max for Live.
- **Suite:** everything in Standard PLUS **Max for Live** (the whole generative/
  ambient M4L device world), Sampler, Wavetable, Operator, Granulator III,
  Convolution + Hybrid Reverb, Stem Separation, the full ~70GB+ library.

## Verdict framing
- If the only pain is the **8-track / 2-send cap** → **Standard** fully solves it
  (and the Novation discount applies).
- If you want the **M4L generative-ambient ecosystem** + the deep synths
  (Wavetable/Operator) natively → only **Suite** delivers, but the rig already
  owns Arturia/NI/Soundtoys/Valhalla as plugins, which cover most of that ground
  inside Lite already.
