# Caterina Barbieri — modular into Ableton, multi-layered delay lines

Ambient/minimalist-electronic artist. Live rig = modular synth → Ableton for
processing. Lite-relevant because her processing leans on STOCK devices that exist
in Lite.

## Setup (sourced quotes)
- "My current live rig is my modular setup going into Ableton for further
  processing, especially multi layered delay lines."
- "long-time fan of Ableton's ping pong delay and reverb."
- Make Noise modules "incredibly expanded the possibilities of sonic
  investigation."
- Also uses SuperCollider for synthesis / alternative tunings (out of scope for Lite).

## Why this matters for a hardware-front Lite rig
This is the cleanest "hardware → Ableton stock FX" template that maps onto Lite:
- Ableton's **Delay** device (stock, IN Lite) has a Ping Pong mode → her core sound.
- **Reverb** (stock, IN Lite).
- The technique = run an external source (her modular = your VG-800 / banjo / OP-1)
  into an audio track, stack multiple Delay devices with different times/feedback
  for "multi-layered delay lines," then Reverb. All Lite-loadable.

## Certainty
HIGH — direct Ableton interview, her own quotes. The specific recipe (stacked
Delays + Reverb on an external feed) is a faithful, Lite-safe reconstruction of
