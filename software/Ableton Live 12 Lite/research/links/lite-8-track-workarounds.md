# Living within Lite's 8-track / 16-scene ceiling (template strategy)

The binding Lite constraints for an ambient/drone build: 8 tracks, 16 scenes, 2
returns. These are the community-standard workarounds — the backbone of any Lite
performance/looping template.

## Core moves
1. **Resample/bounce to consolidate.** Build a dense sub-section on several tracks,
   then resample (record those tracks to a new audio track) and delete the sources.
   A whole drone wall collapses to 1 track, freeing 7. This is THE Lite move for
   walls.
2. **Commit groups (freeze + flatten).** Render a Group to a single audio clip,
   replace the group → reclaims slots.
3. **One track does many jobs.** A single Simpler/Drum Rack patch, or a stacked
   Instrument Rack (multiple chains under one device), reads as "many parts" on one
   track.
4. **Scene re-use for arrangement.** Reuse the same 8 tracks across sections by
   swapping clips per scene (verse/build/wall) — 16 scenes = plenty for an
   ambient piece that evolves slowly.
5. **Print external hardware early.** Don't keep 4 live hardware feeds on 4 tracks —
   record the OP-1 / Digitakt / VG-800 pass to audio, then free that input track.

## Why this fits the rig
A hardware-front drone build rarely needs many SIMULTANEOUS Live tracks — it needs
a couple of live inputs + a return for delay + a return for reverb + a bounce track.
8 tracks + 2 returns is workable IF you resample aggressively. When a piece outgrows
Lite (lots of automation/arrangement), that's the "bounce stems and finish in Logic"
handoff.

## Certainty
HIGH — universal Lite community practice; resampling + group-commit are documented
Live features present in Lite.
