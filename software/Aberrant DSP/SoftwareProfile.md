# Aberrant DSP (suite)

**Summary** — Lo-fi/character effects. **Installed: SketchCassette II only**
(ShapeShifter not installed).

> ⚠️ **Partial install.** Only `SketchCassette II` is on disk per `CONTENTS.md`.

## Why I have it
**SketchCassette II** is the in-the-box degrade centerpiece — the recallable,
automatable tape-cassette sound that complements the hardware tape pedals (Big
Time / Generation Loss). The software half of the "recorded-wrong" aesthetic.

## Signature uses
- **Per-track / per-stem** degrade (never the master): worn banjo/baritone leads,
  drone beds, texture auxes.
- **Tempo-synced Wow** as a rhythmic pitch-slur; **automated Dropouts** that hit
  only on cue; **gain-compensated Saturation** ridden to swell grit.
- **NR Comp** as brightness/parallel-gloss; **ghost-tape layer** hack for an
  overall wet blend (there's no global dry/wet).
- Division of labor: hardware tape pedals **on the way in**, SketchCassette
  **after the fact on stems** — don't double-degrade the same wow/flutter.

## Notes
- **Don't put it on the 2-bus** (the universal warning). Per-track/aux only.
- **Latency** as a send/parallel or with many instances — prefer it as an
  insert; freeze/commit before overdubbing.
- **No global dry/wet, no hiss envelope**; Hiss defaults OFF in II; double-click
  a param to reset. AU/VST3/AAX (no VST2).
- Deep usage research → `research/SketchCassette-II-UsageGuide.md` (6 transcripts
  + 6 links).
