Ambient/experimental user perspective — exactly the target aesthetic. Covers SketchCassette II + ShapeShifter (also Aberrant) + Digitalis.

## SketchCassette II — how this ambient user works it
- Core philosophy: tape character WITHOUT the obvious degradation. "Used subtly, it adds that certain je ne sais quoi associated with analogue warmth."
- Tape formulation Type I–IV with quality grades; the "New → Worn" (Age) slider drives big tonal shifts.
- Praises saturation's gain compensation as good standard practice.
- Wow & Flutter with tempo-sync used for RHYTHMIC effects (not just wobble).

## Specific techniques worth stealing
1. **Automate Dropout** in the DAW so dropouts "only occur as needed" — sparse, intentional, impactful moments rather than constant random dropout. (Pairs with Logic automation lanes.)
2. **Ghost-tape layering hack** (their workaround for the missing global dry/wet + no hiss envelope): run a SECOND track with SketchCassette at EXTREME settings, low in the mix, to simulate ghost tape artifacts under the main sound. Great for drone beds.

## Limitations they flag
- No overall dry/wet knob (only the two Mix knobs = Tape filter + NR Comp). No hiss envelope control.
- Workaround = the parallel/duplicate-track approach above.

## ShapeShifter (sibling plugin, also Aberrant — worth noting for the rig)
- "Rapidly becoming my first port of call on drums, but it makes just about any signal, even a full mix, sound 'better.'"
- Key = UPWARDS compression (boosts quiet signals rather than limiting peaks) → "can emphasise delicate sounds that might otherwise be masked." Useful for surfacing detail in sustained drone beds before/after SketchCassette degrades them.
