(Official Celemony overview of the Melodyne 5 interface and core concept. Distilled from auto-subs.)

---

**The core idea.** With Melodyne you "look directly into the recorded music." You see where notes begin and end, their pitch, and how loud or quiet they are. The middle of the window is the **Note Editor** — the content of your audio files shown as notes arranged by pitch and position in time, **regardless of whether the source is mono or polyphonic**.

**Layout.**
- **Top left**: info panes added via tabs (cmd-click to show several at once).
- **Studio edition** adds a **Track pane** (bring multiple tracks on screen) and a **Sound Editor** (work on the sound/overtones of the recording itself, not just pitch/time).
- **The core of Melodyne are the Tools** — they govern **pitch, formants, dynamics and timing**, with dedicated access down to the finest detail.

**The three pitch tools work independently of one another** — so you don't lose the vibrato when you alter the pitch. Corrected intonation "then sounds perfect but not artificial." (This is the central Melodyne pitch model: pitch-center, pitch-modulation/vibrato, and pitch-drift are three separate dimensions.)

**Timing tools.** Several tools for the attack, for how notes change over time, and to move/shorten/lengthen notes — with **automatic length adjustment of neighboring notes** and portamento taken into account (demoed on a bass). Timing changes "always sound as they would if a musician had played them that way."

**Navigation / selection.**
- Grids support both timing and pitch.
- Zoom by dragging or double-clicking the scroll bars, or with **alt-cmd** / **cmd +/-**.
- The **Options menu** chooses which details are shown with the notes.
- Note selection: **rubber-banding**, **cmd-click**, **shift-click**; special functions like the **"snake"** or selecting particular pitches via the Ruler.

**Copy/paste & Auto Stretch.** Copy and paste individual notes or entire passages within a track or **track to track**. Copies can even **adjust to tempo variations** — switch **Auto Stretch on** for this. (Relevant for adapting a sample/riff to a new tempo or chord context.)

**Shortcuts are remappable.** Preferences → Shortcuts: choose a category, click the command, press the new combo (he uses alt-shift-F as an example); search box by the gear icon; gear icon saves/loads custom sets or restores factory defaults.
