Valhalla's own "which plugin for which job" guide. Most useful for the "which Valhalla" question
— from the vendor, so it's honest about Shimmer/Supermassive being better than VintageVerb for
some ambient jobs.

## Ambient / drones / pads / shimmer
- **ValhallaShimmer = the primary ambient pick.** "The pitch shifting in the feedback loop and
  the lush cascaded diffusors create huge ambient washes from any audio source."
- For evolving pitch clusters: **cascade several instances of Shimmer in series.**
- **VintageVerb also works well:** "The Hall algorithms ... are also perfect for replicating
  that late-70s and 80s ambient sound." (So VintageVerb = vintage-flavored ambient; Shimmer =
  pitched/octave shimmer washes.)

## Vocals
- **VintageVerb.** Tip: "adjust the Diffusion until the time response on sibilance is smooth
  (i.e. without obvious echo patterns)."

## Drums
- **VintageVerb** dominates. Don Gunn (guest): "I use Ambience when I want the impression of the
  space without *hearing* the space ... Dirty Plate and Dirty Hall for bigger, splashier sounds."
- **ValhallaPlate** = "super smooth and dense drum reverbs."

## Large spaces / vintage character
- **VintageVerb** for "expensive"-sounding mixes; the Smooth algorithms (inspired by vintage
  digital) give natural decay.

## Budget / free
- **ValhallaSupermassive (free)** handles ambient, experimental, and general reverb effectively.

## Takeaway for the rig (only VintageVerb + Room installed)
- VintageVerb = the colored vintage-ambient + character workhorse.
- For octave-shimmer washes specifically, the vendor points at Shimmer (not installed) — the
  free **Supermassive** is the honest substitute (huge delays/feedback, ambient/experimental).
