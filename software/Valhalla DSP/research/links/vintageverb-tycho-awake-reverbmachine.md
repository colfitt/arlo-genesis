THE gold-standard sourced artist use. Tycho's "Awake" synth sound is built on TWO VintageVerb
INSERTS (not sends) — directly applicable to the saturated-wall aesthetic. Copyable values below.

## Tycho's two VintageVerb instances (inserts, in series)
1. **Reverb 1 — "less dry" base:** preset **Fat Plate**, **Mix 15%**. Just takes the bone-dry
   edge off the source.
2. **Reverb 2 — the lush wall:** preset **Large RHall**, **70s color mode**, **Mix 30%**,
   "cranked up the modulation amount and depth." This is the swirling, chorused, vintage-noisy
   layer that defines the sound.

## Signal chain (reverb sits EARLY, delay AFTER)
`input → compression → heavy EQ → reverb → delay → heavy compression → EQ → output`
- Note the order: **delay comes AFTER the reverb** (unconventional). RM: this makes "an even
  bigger sound as our big modulated reverb is now getting repeated by the delay effect." The
  delay smears the already-huge modulated tail = wall.
- Heavy compression + EQ AFTER the time effects keeps the wash cohesive instead of muddy. This
  is the key to "saturated wall without mud."

## Why it matters for THIS rig
- Two cheap inserts (one subtle plate at 15%, one cranked-mod 70s RHall at 30%) → instant Tycho
  wall. Maps cleanly to: light Plate first, then big modulated Hall in 1970s color.
- "Reverb → delay" with delay repeating the modulated tail = a recipe to try with EchoBoy AFTER
  VintageVerb (the opposite of the usual delay-then-reverb), then bus-compress to glue.
