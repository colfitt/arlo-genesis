tapeop.com · Tape Op · "ValhallaDelay Review: Behind the Gear with Co-Designer" (Don Gunn + Sean Costello)

The designers on how they actually use it.

## Design intent
~4-year development to make "the most accurate digital representation of the tape echoes"
they loved — especially the **Roland RE-201 Space Echo** — analysing distortion, feedback
character, noise, signal path.

## The diffusion-as-reverb confession
Co-designer **Don Gunn**: they debated whether to even include the Diffusion controls but
kept them because **"we liked the reverbs we could make in ValhallaDelay more than most of
our usual suspects."** The Amount + Size params turn delays into distinctive reverbs — the
designers consider ValhallaDelay a legitimate reverb, not just a delay.

## Ghost mode
"Perfect for creating scary-movie voices and reverbs you've never heard anywhere else" —
frequency-shifting for unconventional, enharmonic textures. (The drone/horror-leaning,
unstable-tonality wall = directly on-aesthetic for doom/drone.)

## How Don Gunn uses it (workflow)
- **10–12+ aux tracks of ValhallaDelay simultaneously per mix** — micro-shifted delays
  and pitches on vocals, reverbs on drums, slap-back on snare. (CPU is a non-issue, like
  the other Valhalla plugins.)
- Emphasis on **automating parameters** for a constantly-evolving output (delay time,
  feedback, mod — the wall keeps moving).

## Limitation the designer himself names
- **No tap-tempo / click-free tempo change** — he wishes it existed. Relevant if you want
  to ride delay time live without artifacts.

$50, with free future updates promised (and delivered — Quartz mode etc. came later free).
