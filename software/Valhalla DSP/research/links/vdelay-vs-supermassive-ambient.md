When is paid ValhallaDelay worth it over the FREE Supermassive (and Shimmer)?

## What free Supermassive already does
Supermassive is a **free combined delay+reverb** "designed from the ground up for massive
delays and reverbs" — huge slow-blooming ambiences, cosmic echoes, feedback networks that
"blur into evolving clouds," modulated galaxy-scale tails. It's explicitly the go-to free
tool for ambient/cinematic/shoegaze walls and has genuine **infinite feedback / Freeze**.
For guitar: shorter delay + moderate feedback + a little mod depth + wide stereo = instant
sparkle. (The sibling agent covers Supermassive in full.) $0.

## What paid ValhallaDelay adds over Supermassive (the buy case)
- **Accurate tape/BBD/Digital character** — Supermassive is "not interested in reality";
  ValhallaDelay's Tape (RE-201/RE-301), BBD, and Digital modes give *authentic degraded
  delay character* (wow/flutter tied to delay time, splice noise, bit-depth crunch). This
  is the tape/lo-fi aesthetic Supermassive does NOT do.
- **Tempo sync** (ms/notes/dotted/triplet) — Supermassive's delay timing is mode-preset;
  ValhallaDelay syncs to project tempo. The rhythmic-delay job.
- **Ghost frequency-shift + Pitch-in-feedback + RevPitch** as discrete, dialable modes,
  plus **Ducking / Pitch Duck** (adaptive shimmer that gets out of the source's way).
- **Diffusion → reverb** AND a real delay in one — so it's a character delay first, a
  reverb second, vs Supermassive being a reverb/delay-wash first.

## The verdict for this rig
- **For pure infinite ambient WALLS** (no rhythm, no tape character) → free Supermassive
  is enough; don't buy.
- **For tape/BBD/lo-fi character delays, tempo-synced dub/echo, the Ghost metallic drift,
  or the Pitch-Duck adaptive shimmer under a played source** → ValhallaDelay earns its $50.
  It's the *character delay* this rig lacks in the box (the hardware analog is the tape/
  echo pedals + Big Sky/Microcosm); Supermassive is the *free wall*.
