## VintageVerb vs Room — when to reach for which (relevant since both are installed)
- **Funkybot's Evil Twin:** Use **VintageVerb for colored, synthetic, lush, modulated** sounds (the "80s" vibe); use **Room for transparent, realistic ambience**. It's about reverb CHARACTER, not instrument.
- Technically: VintageVerb = single processor, 19+ modes emulating specific 70s/80s/now hardware, era color modes, max decay 70 s. Room = dual processor (independent Early/Late reflections), 12 size-focused algorithms, separate hi/low multipliers + crossover, more granular and "deeper/larger/more ambient" psychoacoustic spaces, max decay 100 s.
- **dubguy99:** Stack both — VintageVerb as the primary texture, a small amount of Room underneath for extra stereo image and depth.
- **Sean Costello (developer):** VintageVerb "does ambient. It has several takes on the classic 'Concert Hall' reverb that was used in tons of old-school 70s/80s ambient music. Lots of modulation on tap."

## VintageVerb ambient/drone tips
- **Chaotic Chamber** = rich ambient textures; its saturation "glues the source and reverb together" (Delta Sign).
- **Size & Attack are crucial** — Attack balances early vs late reflections (notably in Ambience mode); raise Attack for a slow swell into the wall.
- **Smooth Plate in NOW color** with lower HighShelf damping stays bright despite the dark reputation (Winstontaneous) — a clean lush plate option.
- For "subtle, not-so-crazy soundscapes," VintageVerb is the pick over Supermassive/Übermod/Shimmer; it's the colorful-but-controllable algorithmic workhorse.
- General ambient consensus: crank **Size**, push **Decay** long, add **Modulation** for an evolving chorused tail, and roll the **high cut** down to sit the wash behind the source.
