# ValhallaRoom vs VintageVerb — division of labor (the contrast)

The whole reason both are installed: they are complementary, not redundant. (VintageVerb's own guide already covers VVV in depth — this file captures the *contrast* and when to reach for Room.)

## Philosophy
- **VintageVerb** = device emulation. Loosely models specific hardware reverbs across **three eras (1970s sparse/dark/thick · 1980s vibrant/bright · Now neutral)**. "Sounds like a record very easily." Reach for it to **add color/character**. Single processor. Max decay 70 s. The Lexicon-flavored halls live here.
- **Room** = a **psychoacoustic / algorithmic** approach to *realistic spaces*. **"Discreet / anonymous"** — adds depth and space without a vintage stamp. **Dual processor (Early + Late)** for independent control of reflections vs tail. Max decay **100 s**. 12 algorithms from tight ambience → vast modulated space.

## When to use which (for this rig)
- **Reach for Room** when you want believable **depth/placement** (the Early engine + Depth knob), a **transparent bed** that glues a mix without coloring it, frequency-shaped tails (3-band decay mult), or **independent early/late control**. It's the natural-space foundation.
- **Reach for VintageVerb** when you want **vintage color, era-degraded character, lush 70s/80s halls**, the obvious "record" sound. It's the character layer.
- **Both at once (the move):** **Room a few % for stereo image + depth** under **VintageVerb's colored texture** — already noted in the VintageVerb guide (§5). Or invert it: a transparent Room as the realistic wall, VintageVerb on a parallel send for the degraded color sheen.

## Honest community verdict
Mixed. Many own both and reach for **VintageVerb more often** ("by a long stretch") because it flatters material fast. But Room wins for anonymous depth, dark-ambient algorithm variety, and the dual-engine control. "Choice comes down to personal taste and mixing circumstances." One real Room limitation flagged repeatedly: **plates are not its strong point** (use a convolution reverb + plate IRs, or VintageVerb's plate, for that).

## Control differences worth knowing
- VintageVerb damping = high shelf + bass-frequency multiplier (2-band-ish).
- **Room = full 3-band decay**: high & low **Mult + crossover** points (finer than VVV) — more surgical tail-shaping.
- Room's **Depth (early/late) and Space** controls have no VVV equivalent.
- Neither has a true **Freeze**; for held-infinite drones use **Big Sky's Freeze or the free Valhalla Supermassive** (max feedback). Room's Decay tops out at 100 s (longer than VVV's 70 s) so "near-frozen" is a touch easier in Room.
