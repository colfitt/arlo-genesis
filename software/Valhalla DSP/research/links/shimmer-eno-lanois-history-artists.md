The provenance of the shimmer effect and the documented artist references.

## The original effect (pre-plugin)
- The Eno/Lanois shimmer = a **feedback loop with a pitch shifter set to +1 octave plus a
  reverb with a fairly long decay**. The output of the reverb feeds a +12 pitch shifter,
  which feeds back in, so the tail keeps climbing octaves = "swelling octave overtones."
- This is what Shimmer reproduces in one box: **Shift +12, Feedback 0.5+** = the classic
  sound "as heard on Eno/Lanois productions for U2 and others."

## Documented songs / records
- **U2 — "With or Without You"**: the "**infinite guitar**" tone (the climbing octave
  pad behind the guitar). HIGH confidence (designer's own analysis).
- **U2 — "4th of July"** (instrumental): the effect "more prominently featured."
- The effect predates its famous use; Eno/Lanois were using it before **U2's *The
  Unforgettable Fire* (1984)** brought it to a wide audience.

## Artist references (from product page / Costello)
- Named on the Valhalla product page as the "shimmer" lineage: **U2, Brian Eno, Daniel
  Lanois, Coldplay.** HIGH for Eno/Lanois/U2 (the documented origin); Coldplay = listed
  by Valhalla (MEDIUM — marketing-level, plausible given Eno produced them).

## Honest flag for this rig's aesthetic
The documented users are 80s art-rock / stadium-ambient, NOT doom/shoegaze/slowcore.
There is **no sourced drone/doom/shoegaze artist using ValhallaShimmer specifically** —
the ambient-wall reputation is real and word-of-mouth (the effect itself is all over
shoegaze/ambient, but Shimmer-the-plugin credits in that scene aren't documented). None
invented. The transferable fact: the *technique* (+octave-in-feedback) is the foundation
of the shimmer wall this rig wants.
