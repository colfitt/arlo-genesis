Copyable ambient/drone settings & recipes — VintageVerb
Synthesized from the sourced files in this folder (modes post, PluginDrop, FaderPro, Tycho/RM,
KVR threads, iZotope). Each recipe flags its origin. Values are starting points — tune to taste.

## Recipe A — "Tycho saturated wall" (sourced: reverbmachine.com, 2017)
Two VintageVerb INSERTS in series:
- #1 Fat Plate, Mix 15% (de-dry).
- #2 Large RHall, **70s color**, Mix 30%, Mod amount + depth CRANKED.
- Then delay AFTER (EchoBoy), then bus compression + EQ to glue. The reverb is repeated by the
  delay = bigger wall.

## Recipe B — "Clean enormous wash / almost-frozen drone"
- Mode **Cathedral** (or **Random Space** for zero pitch artifacts), Size large, **Decay ~40–70 s**
  (70 s = the ceiling; no true freeze — see gotchas), PreDelay 20–60 ms, **NOW** color for clarity.
- LowCut the wet (~120–200 Hz) so the drone doesn't boom; HiCut/Damping to push the tail into fog.
- 100% wet on a send. (Modes post + FaderPro.)

## Recipe C — "Lush chorused shoegaze pad"
- Mode **Chorus Space** (or **Bright Hall**), **1980s** color, big Size, long Decay, **Mod Rate +
  Depth up** — PluginDrop: "modulation is unusually musical ... no obvious pitch wobble," so push
  it. This is VintageVerb's nearest-to-shimmer without owning Shimmer.

## Recipe D — "Tape-warble / recorded-wrong drone"
- One of the **Chaotic** modes (Chaotic Hall / Chamber / Neutral) for wow-&-flutter + subtle
  saturation, or **Sanctuary** (1970s German-box emulation) in **1970s** color. Long Decay. Pairs
  with SketchCassette / RC-20 on the return. (Modes post.)

## Recipe E — "Reverse swell" (shoegaze intro)
- **Nonlin** mode, reverse/gated character, Size = swell length, Attack to taste. Or bounce-reverse
  trick (iZotope #6). "Lush chorusing modulation on tap, for your shoegazing needs" — Valhalla's own
  Nonlin description.

## Recipe F — "Saturated doom return"
- VintageVerb on a SEND, 100% wet, big Hall/Cathedral → **Decapitator/RC-20 AFTER it on the return**
  → "raw and bright, a unique layer of air" (iZotope #3). LowCut before the saturator. The core
  doom/wall move. Optionally EQ after to tame (fixes no-post-reverb-EQ gotcha).

## Fundamentals to bake into all of the above
- **PreDelay** = clarity (keep dry attack / drone pitch readable). 100% wet on sends.
- **Damping** shapes tail tone over time; **EQ (HiCut/LowCut)** sets the wet's static balance —
  two different jobs (FaderPro).
- 70s = dark/lo-fi/noisy, 80s = bright/rich-mod, NOW = clean. Vintage character lives in 70s/80s.
- Restraint: carve with EQ/predelay/damping, don't just crank Mix ("ambient's worst cliché" — KVR).
