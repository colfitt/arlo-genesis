# Room vs VintageVerb — "which do I reach for?" (real user verdicts)

The settings facet's `room-vs-vintageverb.md` has the *philosophy* contrast. This file
captures the **lived "which do I actually grab"** consensus, with quotes.

## The honest community split
- **Most owners reach for VintageVerb more often** — "by a long stretch" — because it
  "sounds like a record very easily" and flatters synths/material fast. VintageVerb is the
  better *all-rounder* and the go-to for color, 80s lushness, and slick synth verbs.
- **Room is the specialist they reach for on purpose:** for **"transparent and realistic"**
  space, "putting things into a physical space," and — notably — several users find Room
  **"deeper, larger and more 'ambient' than VintageVerb."** So the "VVV for ambient"
  reflex isn't universal: a real minority prefers **Room** for the *biggest, deepest*
  ambient beds specifically because it's uncolored.

## What users say Room is uniquely good at (the reach-for triggers)
- *"The magic of Room is in the detailed Early & Late Reflection controls (including Size
  & Modulation for both) and the variety you can get by adjusting the ER/LR balance."* →
  reach for Room when you need **independent early/late control or the Depth crossfade.**
- *"Notably better than other premium reverbs on vocals when properly dialed in"* — i.e.
  it rewards effort; VintageVerb rewards speed.
- **Dark ambient algorithm variety** — the Dark/Alien modes give Room an edge for *dark*
  ambient even where VVV wins on lush.

## The "use both" verdict (recurring, multiple users)
- *"VVV as the main reverb for texture and colour and a few % Room for better stereo image
  and depth."* → the canonical both-at-once routing: **VintageVerb = color layer, Room =
  depth/stereo layer.** (Confirms the draft.)
- Inverse is equally endorsed: **Room as the believable wall, VintageVerb on a parallel
  send for the degraded color sheen.**
- Practical closer that comes up a lot: *"Just use them and see what you like — then roll
  with it."* The choice is taste + material, not a rule.

## One-line decision rule for the rig
- Want a **believable, deep, uncolored** wall, or **surgical early/late/Depth** control →
  **Room.** Want **vintage color, 70s/80s degrade, fast 'record' sound** → **VintageVerb.**
  Want the best wall → **both**, Room for depth under VVV's color.
