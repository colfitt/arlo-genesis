Practical workflow thread. Strongest takeaway = choose by SOUND CHARACTER, not by instrument.

## Choose by character (Funkybot's Evil Twin)
- **VintageVerb** = "more colored, less realistic, slicker (modulated)." Reach for 80s/lush vibes.
- **Room** = "more realistic, natural ambience. Transparent." Reach for authentic space.

## Concrete technique
- **VintageVerb** (Winstontaneous): learn how **Size & Attack** behave per algorithm. Try
  **Nonlin in Ambience mode** where Attack balances early/late reflections. **Smooth Plate in
  NOW** with lower HighShelf Damping = bright modern plate.
- **Room** (Winstontaneous): separate Early/Late Reflection controls each have own Size & Mod;
  Depth slider balances ER/LR ratio; Dark and Aliens modes = deeper tones / different mod.

## Wet/dry
- (Sound Mechanics) Some run reverb 100% wet on a send and tune decay/time per source rather
  than blending — "sounds more authentic to a real space."

## Stacking (dubguy99) — directly useful for walls
- Run BOTH at once: "VVV as the main reverb for texture and colour and a few % Room for better
  stereo image and depth." → VintageVerb for color, a touch of Room underneath for width/depth.

## Mindset (Neon Breath)
- Use tools against their design on purpose for "happy accidents" — e.g. Nonlin/reverse for drones.
