soundonsound.com · Sound on Sound · ValhallaDelay review (+ MusicRadar)

How reviewers actually drive each mode — the character-and-creative-use angle.

## Tape mode
"Crank up the Feedback in Tape mode and you get a gorgeously dirty yet controllable slab
of recirculating sound that can be manipulated to your heart's content by changing the
delay time and other controls." → the **self-oscillating dirty drone** move: high feedback
+ ride the delay time. Wow/Flutter colour it.

## BBD & Digital
Recreate "the dirty sound of a bucket-brigade chip" and "the slightly crunchy flavour of
old-school digital delay lines" — lo-fi character with no hardware.

## Ghost mode (the standout)
"Small amounts of **Shift and Detune** add a distinctive, appealing character to the
repeats which helps them stand out without sounding noticeably effected." Push it and "the
echoes become increasingly **enharmonic and metallic** as they decay" — evolving tonality,
not obvious processing. → the on-aesthetic move: a little FREQ Shift/Detune for character;
a lot for a metallic spectral drift.

## Pitch / RevPitch
"Rocket-taking-off swooshes, seasick wobbles, vinyl slowdowns" via recirculation control,
and they're "pretty much **impossible to make sound broken in a bad way**" — i.e. safe to
abuse for drone/doom texture.

## Diffusion
Subtle echo-softening → **full reverb simulation**; at extreme settings with minimal Size
it generates "a neat flanging sound."

## Routing
PingPong / Ratio (proportional/golden) / Quad extend past hardware delays.

## Verdict
Reviewers treat ValhallaDelay as both a top-tier tape/character delay AND a hidden reverb;
the Ghost + Diffusion combination is repeatedly singled out as what makes it special for
ambient/creative work.
