Authoritative, plugin-agnostic creative-reverb techniques — all directly applicable to VintageVerb
in the drone/wall rig. (VintageVerb specifics added in [brackets].)

1. **Insert as wash** — Mix >50% or 100% wet to "fade a sound into the background." Long decay +
   sustained source = ambient pad. [Cathedral / Random Space / Chorus Space, big Size, 70s color.]
2. **Return-channel processing** — put VintageVerb on a send/aux so dry and wet are separate; now
   you can EQ the wet alone and stack effects on just the reverb. [Fixes the no-post-EQ gotcha —
   EQ the return, not the insert.]
3. **Saturate / distort the reverb return** — drive the wet to make it "more raw and bright ...
   a unique layer of air." [→ Decapitator or RC-20 AFTER VintageVerb on the return = saturated
   doom/shoegaze wall. The core "saturated wall" move.]
4. **Gated reverb w/ sidechain** — gate on the reverb return keyed by an external input; shape
   hold/release for rhythmic tails. [Pairs with Nonlin's gated modes for stutter-y beds.]
5. **Pre-delay for separation** — raise PreDelay so the reverb "function[s] as its own sound in
   the groove," keeping the dry attack readable. [Essential to keep drone pitch legible under a
   70 s tail.]
6. **Reverse reverb transitions** — reverse audio → reverb → bounce → reverse again for swelling
   intros. [Or use VintageVerb's Nonlin reverse mode directly for the shoegaze pre-verb swell.]

## Chain-order notes for the rig
- **Delay → reverb** (classic): EchoBoy then VintageVerb = echoes get smeared into the space; the
  usual, controlled ambient depth.
- **Reverb → delay** (Tycho): VintageVerb's big modulated tail gets repeated by EchoBoy = even
  bigger wall (see vintageverb-tycho-awake). Then bus-compress + EQ to keep it cohesive, not mud.
- **Reverb → reverb**: small Plate/Room first (Mix low) → big Cathedral/Chorus Space after, OR run
  VintageVerb (color/effect) + a touch of ValhallaRoom (tight natural space) in parallel/series.
