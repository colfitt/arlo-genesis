The official framing of ValhallaDelay's ambient/drone/shimmer uses (the wall-maker angle).

## Pitch mode = shimmer-delay
"Perfect for micro-shifting vocals, adding harmonies to synths, **creating vast shimmering
soundscapes, or shrieking sounds forever spiraling up or down.**" → Pitch +12 in the
feedback = an octave-climbing shimmer DELAY (the Shimmer-reverb effect, but on the delay
engine; spirals up forever with high feedback). Negative = spiraling down. The shimmer
move lives here too: **Shift +12, Feedback 0.5+** for the climbing wall.

## Ghost mode
Tape delay + frequency shifting + diffusion = ethereal/clangorous; "these sounds can split
your skull in two." The enharmonic, metallic, inharmonic drift (FREQ Shift/Detune) = the
unstable-tonality drone the doom/horror aesthetic wants.

## RevPitch mode
"Big, spacy feedback delays that turn into **psychedelic washes of sound**" (reverse +
pitch). Swooshing reverse swells in the wall.

## Diffusion = delay → reverb
"Any delay Mode can be transformed into a smeared delay, or a **massive reverb**." Works
"as an amazingly smooth and lush reverb."

## Ducking
The "**adaptive shimmer**" — ducking + pitch shifting + diffusion makes the echo/shimmer
level track the input volume, so the wash kicks in after the source quiets (see
vdelay-pitch-duck-adaptive-shimmer.md).

## ★ Frippertronics / 20-second loops
Delay range extends to **20 seconds** → "long Frippertronic loops" (Robert Fripp / Eno
tape-loop ambient technique) in the box. The defining infinite-tape-loop ambient move,
no two tape machines required.

## Age = clean→dusty
The Age control sweeps from "clean and shiny" to "warped and dusty" — the lo-fi/degrade
knob baked into the repeats.
