The Shimmer-vs-the-rest-of-Valhalla-for-ambient verdict, including a Costello quote.

## The consensus ranking for ambient
- **ValhallaShimmer = the consensus choice for classic ambient.** Sean Costello (in-
  thread): Shimmer is **"built from the ground up for ambient work. Not good for
  'realistic' reverbs, and never intended to make real reverbs."** Praised for sounding
  "really good when cascading multiple stereo instances."
- **ValhallaÜberMod** = close second for ambient — Costello: "can do ambient reverbs, as
  well as sparse multitap delays, diffuse delays, and sounds that blur the space between
  delays and reverb." Described as "luxurious and wild," chunkier atmospheres. (Not owned
  here; noted for context.)
- **VintageVerb** = for 70s/80s "old school" ambient (halls + modulation). **Room** =
  subtler, more realistic spaces than Shimmer's "outlandish character."
- Non-Valhalla ambient names dropped: Eventide **Blackhole** (= the H90 algorithm this
  rig already owns), Zynaptiq Adaptiverb (latency/$ concerns), and **free CloudSeed**
  (praised for stability — another no-cost shimmer/algo-verb option alongside Supermassive).

## The paid-Shimmer-vs-free question (for this rig)
This thread is why the existing VintageVerb/Room guides say **paid Shimmer is "optional":**
free **Supermassive** + free **CloudSeed** both make octave-shimmer / infinite-feedback
walls at $0. Shimmer's specific edge = its dedicated pitch-in-feedback engine (cleaner,
smoother Reverse modes; the cascade-instances chord trick; one-knob shimmer), plus near-
zero CPU for stacking many instances. Worth buying ONLY if you want shimmer as a daily,
recallable, low-CPU workhorse and find Supermassive's interface/sound less controllable.
