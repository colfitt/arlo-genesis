Authoritative breakdown of all 20 VintageVerb modes by the man who coded them. The
single most useful "which mode" reference. Highlights tagged for the drone/ambient rig.

## The 20 modes (sonic character → best for)

- **Concert Hall** — huge spatial image, high echo density, lush chorusing modulation.
  Models late-70s/early-80s hall algorithms. THE big-ambient-hall pick.
- **Bright Hall** — brighter initial sound, deeper/lusher modulation.
- **Plate** — highly diffuse, bright initial sound, high echo density (early-80s plate).
- **Room** — medium diffusion/early echo density, darker (early-80s room).
- **Chamber** — transparent and dense, highly diffuse, high echo density. Clean/less colored.
- **Random Space** — "DEEP and WIDE reverbs, with a slow attack," uses delay randomization.
  → Long washes WITHOUT pitch artifacts. Key drone mode when you want size but not chorus.
- **Chorus Space** — same as Random Space but with "lush chorused modulation" instead of
  randomization. → Ambient textures / shimmery washes. The lush-pad pick.
- **Ambience** — time-varying randomized early reflections + full reverb tail. "Adding air
  to drums, vocals." Impression of space without obviously hearing the space.
- **Sanctuary** — discrete early reflections, dense late reverb, detuned modulation. Emulates
  a 1970s German digital reverberator. Vintage/lo-fi character.
- **Dirty Hall** — lusher and denser than Concert Hall, "warmth and grit." Synth verbs in
  retro contexts. Most faithful to late-70s/early-80s digital HW.
- **Dirty Plate** — high echo density, wide stereo image, "metallic sheen." Drums/character.
- **Smooth Plate** — smooth decay at all settings, most transparent/naturalistic. Modern.
- **Smooth Room** — smooth and transparent room reverb, low coloration.
- **Smooth Random** — transparent decays, tighter initial attack, smoother decay. "Jack of
  all trades." (Smooth modes = modern update of classic Lexicon algorithms.)
- **Nonlin** — gated, reverse, and nonlinear reverbs with Size/Attack control. Experimental
  atmospherics — reverse swells for shoegaze.
- **Chaotic Hall** — chaotic take on Concert Hall, subtle wow & flutter chorusing. Lush/warm.
- **Chaotic Chamber** — smooth + chaotic, wow & flutter modulation, subtle saturation.
- **Chaotic Neutral** — chaotic modulation with fairly colorless algorithm architecture.
  (Chaotic trio = "longer synth verbs that still sit well in the mix." The tape-warble verbs.)
- **Cathedral** (added 2021) — "BIG yet clear," long open decays, ensemble modulation,
  realistic HF rolloff. The clean-but-enormous wash. Top long-decay drone pick.
- **Palace** — more realistic/open than other room algos, "old school charm." Rooms of all sizes.

## Quick map for the rig

- **Clean enormous wash:** Cathedral, Random Space (no pitch artifacts).
- **Lush chorused pad:** Chorus Space, Concert Hall, Bright Hall.
- **Tape-warble / "recorded-wrong" drone:** the three Chaotic modes (wow & flutter), Sanctuary.
- **Vintage grit:** Dirty Hall, Dirty Plate (most faithful to 70s/80s hardware).
- **Reverse/gated swells (shoegaze):** Nonlin.

## Color modes (cross-cutting, per mode)
- **1970s** = dark, noisy, lo-fi (10 kHz ceiling, internal downsampling).
- **1980s** = brighter, classic-digital.
- **NOW** = clean, full-bandwidth, colorless.
Vintage character comes from 1970s/1980s color; Sanctuary + Dirty modes lean vintage; Smooth +
Chaotic Neutral + Palace lean modern.

Tip: hovering over the current Mode shows a brief description of its sonic personality in-UI.
