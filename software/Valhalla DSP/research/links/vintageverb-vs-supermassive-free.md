## The lane split
- **VintageVerb (owned)** = lush, musically *conventional* hall/plate/chamber spaces with vintage
  color + modulation. The "real-space + vintage-character" reverb. Max Decay 70 s, **no freeze**.
- **Supermassive (FREE, not installed)** = feedback delay networks (each delay up to 2 s) → "huge,
  feedback-based spaces that lean more delay-meets-reverb than classic halls/plates." Long evolving
  tails, modulated echoes, "drones that drift forever," and a genuine **infinite/freeze** (set
  Feedback to max — see Valhalla's "Infinite Supermassive" post). MusicRadar: "the perfect tool for
  textures, beds and drone effects."

## Recommendation for THIS rig
**Install Supermassive (it's free).** It fills VintageVerb's two biggest gaps for drone/doom/ambient:
1. **True infinite/freeze** held drones (VintageVerb caps at 70 s, no freeze).
2. **Delay-into-reverb feedback washes** that drift and self-evolve (VintageVerb is more "fixed space").

Division of labour:
- **VintageVerb** = the colored vintage space, the chorused shoegaze pad, the saturated-wall inserts
  (Tycho), anything where you want a *believable but characterful* room/hall/plate.
- **Supermassive** = the held infinite bed, the cascading feedback drone, the "drifts forever" pad.
- Stack them: Supermassive for the infinite underbed → VintageVerb (or Room) for the front-of-space
  color and air. Best of both, $0 extra.

(Shimmer — also not installed, paid — is the dedicated octave-shimmer tool; Supermassive + VintageVerb
modulation cover most shimmer-adjacent needs for free/owned.)
