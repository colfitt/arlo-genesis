## Mode families (Sean Costello's own groupings + "best for")

**DIRTY modes** — Dirty Hall, Dirty Plate
- Most faithful to late-70s / early-80s digital hardware reverbs (Lexicon 224XL, AMS RMX-16 era). High echo density, warm and gritty.
- BEST FOR: "adding depth and character to synthesizer sounds." Vintage character / grit.

**SMOOTH modes** — Smooth Plate, Smooth Room, Smooth Random
- A modern update of the "classic" Lexicon algorithms. Careful, unobtrusive filtering; output taps shaped for a natural exponential decay. Smooth Plate = "the most transparent and naturalistic reverb in VintageVerb."
- BEST FOR: "expensive"-sounding vocals and realistic rooms. (Tip from forums: Smooth Plate in NOW color with lower HighShelf damping can still be bright despite the "dark" reputation.)

**CHAOTIC modes** — Chaotic Hall, Chaotic Chamber, Chaotic Neutral
- Offshoot of the tape-mode work in ValhallaDelay. "Chaotic" modulation waveforms close to the wow/flutter of worn-out tape loops; saturation based on the pre-emphasis / nonlinearity / de-emphasis of tape decks (higher internal drive than the other modes). Digital quantization noise is replaced by tape modulation noise → a broader spread of frequencies as the reverb decays. Counterintuitively this gives CLARITY: the saturation reduces ringing in the tail, and tape chorusing makes fewer random pitch shifts than standard random-walk modulation.
- Chaotic Hall ≈ Dirty Hall structure + chaotic elements. Chaotic Chamber ≈ Smooth-based + signal-dependent diffusion for a clearer tail. Chaotic Neutral = chaotic modulation on a fairly colorless architecture ("sounds like the input").
- BEST FOR: "longer synth verbs that still sit well in the mix." Works on pretty much any input. **This is the family for tape-flavored ambient drone tails.**

**RANDOM / CHORUS SPACE** — Random Space, Chorus Space
- "DEEP and WIDE reverbs, with a slow attack." Random Space uses internal delay randomization (instead of chorusing); Chorus Space is the same but with lush chorused modulation.
- BEST FOR: deep, wide, slowly-blooming ambient beds. Chorus Space = the shimmery/chorused wide wash.

**REALISTIC / BIG modes** — Cathedral, Palace
- Cathedral (2021, FV-1-derived): "BIG yet clear, for long, open reverb decays." Palace (2023): realistic, open decay from small rooms up to concert halls and giant cathedrals — "more realistic and open" than other room algos.
- BEST FOR: huge, clear, open spaces; cathedral-sized ambient without mud.

**HALL / PLATE / ROOM / CHAMBER (the classics)**
- Concert Hall: hall algorithms of the late 70s/early 80s. Huge spatial image, echo density adjustable sparse→dense, lush chorusing modulation. The classic "old-school ambient" hall.
- Bright Hall: like Concert Hall, brighter, deeper modulation.
- Plate: early-80s plate. Fast diffuse attack, bright initial sound, high echo density, lush chorused modulation.
- Room: early-80s room, medium diffusion, darker.
- Chamber: transparent, dense, high diffusion, minimal coloration.

**NEWEST (4.0.0, 2023)** — Chamber1979, Hall1984
- Chamber1979: inspired by a renowned late-70s digital reverb chamber. "Lush," fewer random pitch shifts, an "aperture filter" adds brightness even at low sample rates. Versatile across small rooms → huge cathedrals.
- Hall1984: tribute to early-80s digital hall hardware but also does "dark 70s digital halls, brighter 80s halls, and crystal-clear modern reverbs." Open and spacious, less metallic, far greater control over reverb time, distributed modulation. Good for "short room sounds, big halls, cathedrals, and GIANT synth reverbs."

**SANCTUARY** — 1970s German-hardware-inspired. Discrete early reflections, rapidly-building echo density, detuned modulation, vintage bit-reduction artifacts. Dense late reverb. A massive, near-infinite bloom that ambient/electronic producers reach for.

**NONLIN** — gated, reverse, and nonlinear reverbs via Size/Attack interpolation. (Forum consensus: excels on drums/percussion — snares, toms.)

**AMBIENCE** — time-varying randomized early reflections + a full reverb tail; adds subtle "air." Attack balances early/late reflections. Good for short, reflective room sound on drums/vocals.

## Quick "which mode for the drone/ambient rig" map
- **Tape-warm drone tail:** Chaotic Hall / Chaotic Chamber (1970s color).
- **Deep wide slow bloom:** Random Space / Chorus Space (Chorus Space = chorused/shimmer-ish).
- **Old-school lush ambient hall:** Concert Hall or Bright Hall, mod up.
- **Synth/character grit:** Dirty Hall / Dirty Plate.
- **Huge clean cathedral wall:** Cathedral or Palace; Hall1984 for "giant synth reverbs."
- **Near-infinite bloom pad:** Sanctuary, long decay, big size.
- **Gated/reverse hits:** Nonlin.
