## Framing
$50, "a massive amount of product" — ~10 modes = "the equivalent of having five or so
different delay units from different manufacturers in one box." All Valhalla units share
a family sound (his style). A delay that lets you "impart, enhance, or at least not lose
character."

## DIFFUSION — the headline feature
"The number one thing… oh my god when you turn that Diffusion knob." Valhalla's diffusion
is "the OTT of diffusion models" — far from practical/realistic but a huge commitment:
**your delay will just launch itself into being a fully-fledged reverb.** Love-it-or-
hate-it, not much in between. This is THE reason to reach for ValhallaDelay over a plain
delay for ambient — it crosses the line from echo → wash → reverb on one knob. (Pairs
with DIFF Size: bigger Size = more reverb-like.)

## Modes & the trade-off
~10 modes selected by the MODE control (Tape, HiFi, BBD, Digital, Ghost, Pitch, RevPitch,
etc.). Caveat he flags: **the modes gate the available controls** — e.g. Ducking only
exists in certain modes, so if you're happy with a mode that lacks Ducking you can't add
it. "Good because it focuses you; the limitation is real." Pick the mode for the
character, accept its control set.

## STYLE / routing (the delay-line topology)
- **Single** — one delay time, L/R share it (with Spread for stereo offset). The default;
  "whatever you learn here applies to the rest."
- **Dual** — two completely independent delay lines (DELAY L / DELAY R), no cross-feedback.
- **Ratio** — sets the LEFT delay with the DELAY knob; the RIGHT = a *ratio* of it (RATIO
  knob). At 100% they're identical; under 100% the right comes first; you can **invert**
  (flip which side leads) for stereo control. A handy way to find the second delay when
  you don't know where to put it in Dual — set the long one (e.g. 405 ms) then ratio the
  shorter against it. (Golden-ratio 61.8% = the pseudo-reverb trick — see primary blog.)
- **PingPong** — slightly unusual: lets you set TWO different delay times and ping-pongs
  them anyway. (He's "never been a massive fan of ping-pongs.")
- **Quad** — FOUR taps (tap = a delay). A button sets where feedback comes from:
  - **Repeat** mode: plays taps 1-2-3-4 then repeats from tap 4 (multi-head tape echo).
  - **Swell** mode: takes the SUM of all four taps and sends THAT back around → "a delay
    or reverb effect that goes *whoa* as it grows." This is the **bloom/swell** move — the
    same blooming behaviour as in the reverb plugins. ★ For ambient: Quad + Swell +
    rising feedback = a self-building wall. "If your delays feel too regular/square,
    multi-taps are the way to complexify" instead of buying more drippy plugins.

## Practical notes
- Feedback up + the right settings = the wall builds. He keeps things mostly in Single
  while teaching.
- Use the modes' baked-in character rather than reaching for separate lo-fi plugins.
