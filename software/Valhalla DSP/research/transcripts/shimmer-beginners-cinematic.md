## Two halves of Shimmer
1. **The reverb side** — you can BYPASS the pitch shifter (Pitch Mode = Bypass) and use
   Shimmer as a plain diffusion-based reverb. It's "a diffusion-based reverb," so the
   Diffusion knob is where the reverb magic happens (it's not a delay-network reverb like
   Room — the smooth wash comes from diffusion).
2. **The shimmery side** — re-enable the pitch shifter for the climbing/falling shimmer.

## Recommended starting points (presenter's defaults)
- **Mix**: "I like to keep the mix knob at least 60%" for a cinematic wet sound (higher
  than you'd run a normal reverb; this is meant to be a wash, not a subtle space). Run
  lower on a send.
- **Color**: two modes, **Dark and Bright** — "I like Dark a bit" (warmer, less aliasing
  in the climbing tail). Use Low Cut + High Cut to further shape.
- **Diffusion** is the core reverb-shaping control here.

## Shift knob = the shimmer pitch
- Set in **semitones**. At 0 = no shimmer (same pitch as input). **−12** = an octave
  below (much darker/lower shimmer). **+12** = an octave above = the classic high
  shimmer/"sparkle."
- **Feedback** determines how much of the shimmer is recirculated — high Feedback makes
  the shimmer climb (or fall) further and sustain longer.

## Pitch Mode
- **Single** = shimmer at one pitch (the Shift value).
- **Dual** = shimmer at two pitches at once — the positive AND negative of the Shift
  value (e.g. Shift 12 in Dual = +12 up AND −12 down simultaneously). Fuller, organ-/
  octaves-stacked texture.

## Beginner cinematic recipe
Mix ~60% · Pitch Mode Single · Shift +12 · Feedback fairly high · Diffusion high · Color
Dark · trim with Low/High Cut = a big cinematic octave-up shimmer wash. Switch Pitch Mode
to Bypass to use the same plugin as a clean diffusion reverb.
