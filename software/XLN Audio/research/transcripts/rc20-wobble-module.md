## Distilled
Wobble both **adds life** and creates a **massive stereo spread**. In the demo it's on a Rhodes
("roads") in a mix. The Wobble module = **two wobble types, Flutter and Wow**:
- **Flutter** = faster; speed set by the **right Rate knob**.
- **Wow** = slower; speed set by the **left Rate knob**.
- The **Stereo** button creates **two wobbles, one per speaker** = a huge stereo spread.
- Combine Wow + Flutter with the **slider** for interesting results.
- **Lower the Mix knob** to reduce the wobble's wet signal → "a nice **chorus effect**."
- The **Flux** slider adds "a lot of wild non-linear fluctuations" → life and texture.

## Cleaned transcript
Adding wobble to elements in your mix can both add life and a massive stereo spread. Here
we've added the RC-20 Retro Color on the Rhodes in this mix. The wobble module consists of two
types of wobble: flutter and wow. The flutter is faster — control the speed with the right
rate knob. The wow is slower — control it with the left rate knob. The stereo button creates
two wobbles, one for each speaker, which creates a huge stereo spread. You can combine the wow
and flutter with the slider to create interesting results. Lower the mix knob to lower the wet
signal of the wobble module, which creates a nice chorus effect. The flux slider has a lot of
wild non-linear fluctuations which can really add life and texture to your tracks. Now it's
time to put your spaghetti hat on and wobble away.
