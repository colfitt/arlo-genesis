XLN Audio · Get huge Stoner Doom drums in the style of Sleep and Electric Wizard (feat. Otto) · 2023-02-15

Hey guys, it's Otto. I had a video come out recently which was called "if Electric Wizard sounded like Sleep" — so I took their song Funeralopolis and "sleepified" it with a little bit of Holy Mountain magic and a little bit of Jerusalem magic. I downloaded XLN Audio's Addictive Drums and gave them a shot and I loved them.

The thing about my virtual drum workflow is that I like to layer drums together — I do this all the time. This was a thing that was hammered into my head when I went to audio school: when you layer things together they sound more realistic, so I still go by that. Peter from XLN Audio suggested I dig into the United Heavy, Black Velvet, and Retroplex drum kits. I opened those up, played around with them, and was flabbergasted. I took all the suggestions Peter had and put them all into one drum kit and it sounds huge.

Let's look at the session. We've got quite a lot of drums here, color-coded as this purple thing. We're going to the beginning — we've got three layers of drums. (Plays.) Kind of this dry Holy Mountain vibe — that's what I was going for in the beginning.

**Layer 1 — "drums lo-fi."** I selected a kit and with an EQ I did some adjustments — took out some highs, took out some lows. Another thing I did was add a compressor (FabFilter Pro-C 2). The reason I used it was to dig out some of those very quiet hits you can't hear well in the mix — digging those out. Let's open up Addictive Drums 2. I took out the bottom sound of the snare — I didn't want that high-mid buzz sound, so I took that out. Mostly I played around with the snare, did some EQ'ing, and I played with the room sound — I wanted a little bit of room for the start. The kit I used was the **United Heavy** kit, rumbling floor. Another thing I did with the snare was adjust the pitch — lowering the pitch to make it have a big smacking sound. Then there's the kick — I played around with the **Pork Pie Custom kick**, I'm really liking how that sounds. (Solos it.)

**Layer 2 — "drums sub."** This is basically one of these drum kits made into its own track to emphasize the low end. The way I usually do this is with a signal generator — I'll take a low tone and adjust it so it comes in every time the kick is there, to get very low rumbleage. This time I just chose a drum kit — United Heavy again — and the main thing I've done is adjustments with the EQ: took out all the high end and emphasized the low end. (FabFilter Pro-Q 3 is a great EQ because it has a spectrum analyzer — you can see all the low-end info and choose what to boost by looking and listening. You don't want to just look, you've got to listen.)

**Layer 3 — "drums vintage compressed."** Again Addictive Drums, with the **Retroplex** kit. I've kind of put that in the background and compressed the living hell out of it. (Plays.) Yeah, the very flabby-sounding kick — I love it. That's the beginning.

Then we get into the heavy territory. Here we still use the same tracks from the intro but there are two more additional tracks. The first is called **"drums punchy"** — exactly what it is, a drum kit with punchier transients, more dry. (Plays — very punchy.) Lastly we have **"drums roomy,"** a roomy-sounding kit — I've boosted the room a lot, adjusted the overheads quite a bit, again tuned down the snare, did a little EQ'ing (if not in the AD2 plugin itself, then with Pro-Q 3 or the stock Pro Tools EQ).

One last thing that comes to mind: I'll turn down the hi-hat mic quite a bit on all of these kits so it's not in your face so much.

One thing to note when you're doing sludgy kind of drums: when you listen to your references, you might notice that lots of bands don't have a lot of high end on their overheads. It's good to have references so you're not making an ultra-bright mix. (There's nothing wrong with bright — there's all kinds of stoner rock and sludge out there — but references are great so you don't over-brighten.)

(The rest of the video covers the bass and guitars: bass via Guitar Rig 5 Bass Pro amp duplicated, plus the Native Instruments DRIVER plugin for a low-fi broken-speaker distortion; guitars via Guitar Rig 5 Hot Plex amps with the Sleep heavy tone, duplicated and maxed, again with DRIVER, plus blended Marshall/White amps and a lo-fi panned center track; vocals doubled with delay, reverb, de-esser, and the Waves H-Comp pushed hard for nasty distorted vibes.)

XLN really does their drum stuff right — the UI is dead simple, it's fun, there's all kinds of presets and lo-fi stuff to play around with. I'll be using them a lot in the future.
