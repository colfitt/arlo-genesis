## Framing
Compares three lo-fi/vinyl tools on a guitar stem (from collaborator Rebecca, with glue comp + SplitEQ) and a drum group: **Waves Retro-Fi** (~$200, on sale ~$30), **XLN RC-20 Retro Color** (~$99), and the **free iZotope Vinyl**. Deliberately excludes Waves Factory Cassette and SketchCassette II, framing RC-20 and Retro-Fi as VINYL-leaning tools (pitch warble + noisy crackle) vs cassette/tape-saturation tools. All three placed on the MASTER track for easy A/B in this test.

## RC-20 navigation
"Sleek minimalist design, everything titled down at the bottom." Modules light up in their own colors as you turn them on: noise, wobble, distortion, digital, space, magnetic. Input gain, output gain, combined with EQ + width + tone knobs; presets at the top. Verdict vs Retro-Fi: RC-20's modules are "not hard to find but not as color-coded" as Retro-Fi (whose parameter names are labeled and color-coded — pink = adjustable, yellow = the important mix knobs, blue = input/output).

## Presets
RC-20 has cassette / noise / digital / space / VINYL preset categories. Retro-Fi has many ARTIST presets. The presenter jumps straight to RC-20's **vinyl presets** to compare against Retro-Fi's vinyl presets.

## Noise & wear (the core lo-fi character)
- **RC-20 Noise** — a level, plus **Tone** (darker/brighter), plus **Follow** (plays with the guitar) or **Duck** (ducks under it). Several noise presets ("not as many [as Retro-Fi] but still quite a lot").
- **RC-20 Digital** — bit-crushing; "dial in just where that bit crusher performs" (the focus/frequency control).
- **RC-20 Magnetic** — "the instrument dropping out as if the needle was skipping."
- **RC-20 Wobble** — a rate, a mix knob, stereo/mono.
- (Retro-Fi by contrast has explicit threshold gate/duck on its noise, warble A / wow B, device/era selectors. iZotope Vinyl has mechanical+electrical noise blend, dust, scratch, warp, year selectors, RPM 33/45/78.)

## Other effects / bonus features
- **Retro-Fi** has bonus FX RC-20 lacks: a Squash + Ringer (distortion + ring mod) section, high-pass/low-pass + mono, and a "Space" tool with echo/DELAY + REVERB (spring or plate reverb, ping-pong delay, BPM-synced).
- **RC-20** has distortion (with type selector + EQ + tone + width, mono at full-left) and **Space (reverb)** — but **NO delay**. (This is the honest gap vs Retro-Fi for this presenter.)
- iZotope Vinyl (free): mechanical/electrical noise, dust/scratch/warp, a fun spin-down option that "works great with melodic instruments," and a lo-fi/bit-crush option (no mix control on its crusher).

## Takeaway for the rig
RC-20 = the deepest, most flexible CHARACTER suite of the three (6 modules, per-module focus filters, Flux, the Magnitude macro), but it has no built-in delay and its reverb (Space) is decent-not-flagship. Retro-Fi wins on built-in delay/reverb + ring-mod; iZotope Vinyl is the free vinyl-only option with the spin-down trick.
