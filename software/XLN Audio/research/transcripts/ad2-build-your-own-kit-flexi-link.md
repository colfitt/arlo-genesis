XLN Audio · Addictive Drums 2 Spotlight: Build Your Own Kit · 2015-12-17

In this Spotlight video we're going to look at how you can build your own kit within Addictive Drums 2. First, head over to the **Kit page**. This is filled with slots that contain all the individual kit pieces — one for the kick, one for the snare, one for the hi-hat, and so forth.

Up in the top-left corner of the slots you find an **L (as in Load)**. This brings up the kit-piece browser where you can see what's selected right now. If you click on another kit piece, it instantly loads and you can hear how it sits in your mix. At the bottom you can scroll right or left using the arrows to find more options.

Now a really cool feature: the **kick and snare slots have a Link icon** up in the top-right corner. By clicking and dragging it to one of the **Flexi slots** to the right, I can link the two and create really cool combinations (layered kick or snare).

Say I want to change what's in the slot, though. Besides the Load button, you can click the **name at the bottom of the slot** to access a drop-down menu and find what you're looking for. There — that's nicer.

What's also cool is that you can **link your kick or snare slot to multiple slots** like this — in fact, you can link to whatever slot you want. And if you want to try out different kit pieces for a slot quickly, you can just use the **up/down arrows** to toggle through all the kit pieces available (that goes for all slots).

But here's what's cool: if I switch out the snare, the **linked Flexi slots stay unaffected, but the linking stays intact**. This is a great way to mix and match and quickly try out sound combinations without having to redo the link setup.

That's it for building your own kit.
