Home Music Studio 1 (Dave Maxi) · How To Use Addictive Drums 2 · 2016-07-27

How to use Addictive Drums 2 to build high-quality, professional, realistic acoustic drum tracks. This is my virtual instrument of choice for drums and it leans toward a realistic acoustic sound. Supports VST/AU/AAX, Mac and PC, works with virtually any DAW. (Demo in Reaper; I've also used it in Pro Tools, Cubase, Live, Logic, Sonar.) You can download a fully functional **demo** from xlnaudio.com — the engine is identical, just limited to a few drum kits. Presets sync with your online account (cloud), so tweaks survive a system move/upgrade.

**Inserting / multi-out routing.** Insert as a virtual instrument on a new track. Your DAW will ask if you want to use the **multiple MIDI/audio outputs** and assign them to individual tracks — **select Yes** for full functionality. (This is the only step that's a bit unique per DAW.) You then get individual tracks: Master, Kick, Snare, Hi-hat, four Toms, three **Flexi** tracks, Overhead, Room, and a **Bus** track — all stereo even if the element is mono (so a kick can be panned L/R). Overheads and Room are stereo (two mono mics summed to stereo: left OH / right OH; stereo room mics).

**Gallery / Explore views.** Gallery gives an overview of available kits (broken down by style). Explore lets you preview — click Play to hear a sample of a kit (samples if you don't own it; presets if you do). Even in Explore you can make quick adjustments to a preset (e.g. pull a preset's snare down a bit) right away.

**Kit view.** Shows all kit pieces in the preset (cymbal, ride, toms, kick, snare, hat, block of wood, plus Flexi 1/2/3). Each piece outputs to its own track in the mixer view. **Flexi tracks are the layering/parallel trick:** want parallel compression on a kick? Add the same kick (or a different one) into a Flexi track, then **drag the little Link handle onto the kick** so both trigger together — now compression on the Flexi vs. the main kick lets you do parallel compression and sound-blending. You can build your own kit from whatever pieces you have installed; the toms let you pick a whole set or a low-tuned set, etc. (There are ~80 ADpaks on xlnaudio.com — metal kits, machine/sampled kits, R&B, techno — plus individual single kit pieces you can buy.)

**Edit view — per-piece control (like a fully miked kit).** Kick has two mics (beater side, front side); snare has top + bottom plus a **snare-buzz** control; hi-hat (and all pieces) have controls for their level **in the overheads and in the room**. You can **Pan** each piece L/R and — rarely seen elsewhere — control the **Width** of each track (e.g. keep the snare centered but tighten its width inside the overheads). Per drum: a **Response** control (sensitivity / which velocity tones come through), **Pitch** for each drum (and you can **offset** the pitch differently in the overheads vs. the room vs. the original — early reflections in a real room cause pitch differences, so this adds realism). **Tone Designer** sets decay/release/timing of the attack — tighter attack + fast decay, or level it out for a more natural open-sounding kick. There's also a **Volume Envelope**.

**Per-channel FX chain.** Down in the effect section each channel has: **Noise** (different preamp/noise types), **Compression**, **Distortion** (a little grit in a kick helps it pop out of a mix), **EQ** (bands + high-pass/low-pass), **Shape** (tone shaping), and **Saturation** (with pre/post options in the chain).

**Master FX bays + sends.** Two separate **effects bays** that run simultaneously, each switchable between a **Delay** side and a **Reverb** side (room / hall / plate / ambient room). You control the EQ, width, and panning of the reverb, and the **Send 1 / Send 2** amount per individual channel into those bays.

**Beats (drag-and-drop MIDI).** Pre-built beats are ready to roll — find one you like and **drag the MIDI track into your DAW** (turn your DAW's snap on so beats line up). Buy MIDI beats individually too; each preset comes with lots of beats. Within a beat you get variations — straight beat, hat closed/open, ghost notes. On a selected beat you also get realism controls: **Velocity** (with a randomize amount so hits aren't machine-gun equal), **Timing** (loosen/"sloppy" it for realism), and **Accents** (accent the 8th vs. the 16th note) — all adjusted on the fly. Or just insert a MIDI item in your DAW and program your own in the piano roll.

**Separate-outputs setup (the payoff).** In Kit view, in the mixer section, each channel has a **little drop-down arrow**. Click it to choose the output mode: **pre-fader separate out**, **post-fader**, **post-fader + Master**, etc. I select **pre-fader** so scrolling through presets doesn't change my per-track volumes (use **post-fader** instead if you want preset mixer volumes to carry through to the individual track outputs). Do this for kick, snare, all pieces, plus overheads, room, and bus. The **Bus**: the little send knob determines which tracks feed it — another way to do parallel compression on the whole kit (here it's just kick + snare). Once routed, each piece sends signal to its own DAW track, so you can use any DAW EQ/effects/sidechaining. You can even **disable the AD2 effect inserts** per track to get a fully dry signal out and process it exactly as if you'd recorded the samples yourself in a top studio.

Bottom line: stay in Gallery view for fast results, or use multiple outputs for massive control. Either way you can build amazing-quality drum tracks.
