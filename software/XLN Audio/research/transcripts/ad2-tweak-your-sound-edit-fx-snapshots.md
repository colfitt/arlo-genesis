XLN Audio · Addictive Drums 2 Spotlight: Tweak Your Sound · 2015-12-17

In this Spotlight video we're going to look at how you can tweak your sound within Addictive Drums 2. Click **Edit** up in the menu to go to the Edit page. In the middle here we have the versatile **Channel Insert** — one for every channel on the mixer: one each for the close mics (kick, snare, hi-hat, and so forth), and Overhead, Room, Bus, and Master also have their separate ones.

I want to tweak the room a bit, but first let's **take a Snapshot** of our current settings — click down here to take a snapshot. Now all your current settings are saved in that snapshot. This is a great feature to easily compare settings when you tweak your sound and try stuff out.

Now let's push up the Room fader and give that a listen. There — now it's nice and lush, maybe a bit heavy in the mids though, so let's cut a bit there in the EQ. There — drier but still nice and roomy. Let's also cut the attack a bit with a **shaper**, and maybe compensate by boosting the sustain a bit. That's nice. The **M button** down here mutes the room. (Listens with room tweaked.)

Let's head over to the **Master channel** — all the way out to the right. Let's boost the upper mids to add a bit more definition — that's better. Maybe also increase the overall attack using the shaper. That's nice.

Now let's take another snapshot of where we're at. This is what's so powerful — now I can toggle between **Snapshot 1** (takes us back to where we started) and **Snapshot 2** (all the tweaking done). (Compares 1 vs 2 — much better.)

Another way of tweaking your sound is the **Tone Designer**. It's a great tool that lets you shape the tone of a kit piece over time. To demonstrate, I've got this **14×5 Sonor snare** selected — it's rich, punchy, and delivers loads of tone. Say I want to use this Sonor snare but tame it a bit — it's got a very upfront tone and ring. This is where Tone Designer is truly your friend. Hit Play to listen to the snare in the mix. If I **pull the END value down**, you can hear the tone tapering off — the punch is still there but the ring and tone are gone. Now the opposite direction: start from the neutral position, and if I instead **push the END value up**, we boost the tone and can truly unleash the snare in the mix.

In a song like this one it can be really effective to add **Reverb to the snare**. Head over to the **Effects** page up in the menu where we find our effects modules. The two modules look the same — we can activate the second one here. There's a **Delay** section, a **Reverb** section, and an **EQ**. There's also a **slider that seamlessly mixes the delay and reverb**: all the way to the right is just reverb, but as you slide to the left you get more and more delay, and all the way left you have just delay in your mix.

Let's spice up this snare. Hit Play. First, add some reverb to the **close-mic channel** (send) — that's a bit too much, back it off. Then add some to the **overhead** too, and the **room**. Really nice and lush.

So there you have it — a lot of great ways to tweak your sound in Addictive Drums 2.
