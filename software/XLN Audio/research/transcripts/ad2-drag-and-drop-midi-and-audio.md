XLN Audio · Addictive Drums 2 Spotlight: Drag And Drop (Audio & MIDI) · 2015-12-17

In this Spotlight video we're going to look at how to drag and drop from Addictive Drums 2 to your host. The song you hear in the background lacks drums at the moment, so let's fix that.

**MIDI drag-and-drop.** I'm hearing some type of Tom groove for this one, and I think I know where to find it — the **Rock & Roll MIDI pak**. (Listens.) That's the one. Now I want this beat in my song: I just **grab the name down here, drag it onto my timeline, and drop it**. Couldn't be easier. Let's duplicate this one.

Now here's a new section of the song and I need something else — still powerful, with a featured crash. Browsing through my MIDI paks, the **Loud MIDI pak** might have what I need. I want to hear it in the correct tempo, so I'll **enable Sync Tempo** down here. Let's find something with a crash — look under "Songs." This one's perfect. I drag and drop it at the transition of the song, then duplicate it to see how it sits with the rest. Perfect.

**Audio drag-and-drop (one-shots).** You can also drag and drop **audio** from AD2 into your host. Open AD2 again, and if I click the kick down here, you can see the **Audio Recorder** down here reacts — it just recorded that sound. Now I can **drag that as a WAV file onto my timeline** and I've got it sampled. So I can lay out a nice four-on-the-floor beat with it. Now I want a snare too — click this one, same thing, it's been recorded, drag it onto the timeline, put it on the twos and fours, and duplicate. A really nice solid four-on-the-floor groove.

**Loops.** The drag-and-drop functionality is also great for working with loops. I have a loop in mind for this song. First I **adjust the loop to my tempo**, then I **play two bars by clicking Play** up here — now that's been recorded and I can drop that WAV file. I cut it up a little so I get two even bars (delete, duplicate). Then we can play around — cut it up even more, duplicate, and listen. Really cool, all done in seconds.

The drag-and-drop functionality truly opens up a world of possibilities, regardless of whether you prefer to work with MIDI, pure samples, or loops.
