Morsy Music · XLN Audio - DS-10 Drum Shaper to tighten drums & more! · 2024-06-04

One of my favorite plugins for tightening up drums: the DS-10 Drum Shaper by XLN Audio. I use this on EVERYTHING. On this track I wanted to tighten the drums because the arrangement was really busy — I use a lot of percussion and hi-hats, and I wanted it really tight so the bass can shine and the kick can poke through.

OPEN HI-HAT: When you have an open hi-hat that takes up a lot of mud, you want to make it nice and tight.
TAMBOURINE: Tambourines are notorious for taking up space. Nice and tight.
The simple move: take the Sustain and pull it all the way DOWN to "100%" (i.e. fully cutting the sustain/tail) — specifically on the tambourine. On the hi-hat top I also took Sustain to ~100% down, then added a little Attack, which brings the transient up so it pokes through a little more. Same idea with Mojo.
Because these elements are so busy, I was trying NOT to add more — I was trying to take AWAY. That's why you don't see much Attack and you see Sustain all the way down. (You CAN add Attack for more transient, or Mojo to fatten it up — but adding Attack made it hard / sometimes harsh, and I wasn't going for that here. I was just trying to tighten.)

BASS: I'm also using it on bass. This bassline was recorded with a lot of sidechain on it — the sidechain envelope is curving in, coming in late, and dragging. I used Drum Shaper to add transients EARLIER and tighten so it's not draggy. I added it to the bass GROUP. I added Attack, still tightened with Sustain, and added some Mojo Tightness. Look at the top of the transients — they're going UPWARD instead of just downward (this is visual — always use your ears). It's almost fixing all that sidechain, adding the front-end back a bit while tightening it.

Bottom line: I use this a lot. When you accumulate it across the kit you get your drums so much tighter. I was using the SPL De-Verb (De-Verb Plus) in the past — turn the reverb-reduction dial all the way up and it does pretty much the same "tighten" thing — it was my favorite at the time until Drum Shaper came along.
