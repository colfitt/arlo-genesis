# Shoegaze / post-rock drum approach — applied to AD2

Genre-craft (not AD2-specific), but every move maps onto AD2's mixer + the rig's
degrade chain.

## Pattern / performance
- **Tempo 90-120 BPM**, steady and understated. Kick on 1 & 3, snare on 2 & 4, hats
  consistent 8ths. "Less about complex rhythms, more about mood." Repetitive,
  foundational — leave the busy stuff out. (For motorik post-rock: lock the hat 8ths,
  keep the kick hypnotic.)
- **Ghost notes + cymbal swells** for movement/transitions — AD2's velocity layers
  and the Beats "Edit Play" / humanize handle these.

## Processing (do in AD2 or downstream)
- **Reverb:** Hall or Plate, **decay 1-3 s**. Apply to individual elements (snare,
  toms) AND the whole kit as a send. → AD2's **Delerb (Hall/Plate, Swirl on)** does
  this in-box, or send the dry room out to **VintageVerb**.
- **Delay:** subtle, **tempo-synced on snare or hats, minimal feedback** → AD2
  Delerb delay side, low feedback.
- **EQ:** roll off **below 50 Hz**, boost **~200 Hz** (warmth) and **~10 kHz**
  (cymbal shimmer). Note: for the degraded version you'll often kill the 10 kHz
  instead and let LPF/tape eat the top.
- **Compression:** **gentle**, slower attack / medium release — smooth dynamics
  without killing the organic feel.

## The key shoegaze MIX move
- **Drums TUCKED into the mix**, blending under walls of guitar — not up front. This
  is the opposite of pop drum mixing. Bury the kit, let the room/wash define it.
- **Layer real acoustic (AD2) with electronic samples** for hybrid texture — pairs
  perfectly with the owner's MPC/Digitakt one-shots layered under AD2.

## Rig translation (degraded shoegaze kit)
1. AD2 = **Boutique Mallets** or **Vintage Dead**, Delerb Hall + Swirl for the wash.
2. Multi-out the **room/OH** stereo pair → **SketchCassette II** (wow/flutter) +
   light **Lossy** for the degraded recorded-wrong top.
3. Close mics → gentle DS-10 to keep a little thump, then a touch of **Decapitator**
   (low drive) for body.
4. Whole bus low and buried; LPF the cymbals ~12-14 kHz so they don't pierce the
   guitar wall.
