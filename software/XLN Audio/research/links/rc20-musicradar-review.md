MusicRadar · staff review

The most precise spec-level review (exact ranges, algorithm counts) — useful as a reference
to back up the looser tutorial language.

- **Noise:** "**16 looped** mechanical, electrical, and electronic noise samples" — vinyl,
  tape, VHS, 8-bit computer. Controls: **Tone, Follow, Duck**, plus repositioning (POST).
- **Wobble:** separate **Wow (0.1–4 Hz)** and **Flutter (6–20 Hz)** LFOs; stereo-chorus
  capability.
- **Distort:** **six saturation algorithms** — valve, transformer, broken speaker, + three
  waveshapers — with a frequency-targeting **Focus Filter**.
- **Digital:** sample-rate + bit-depth reduction, a **Smooth** control, optional
  frequency-range cutting.
- **Space:** "a **12-note resonatory reverb**" with Decay, PreDelay, and Focus Filter —
  "though reviewers question its necessity."
- **Magnetic:** tape degradation via dual LFOs (**Wear** and **Flutter**) with random
  dropouts; mono or stereo.
- **Magnitude:** "Scales the six amount knobs and all controls in the master section from 0%
  to 100% of their set values, **macro style**."
- **Flux:** "introduces randomized variation **unique to each module**" — e.g. random
  overdrive with Distort, pre-delay modulation with Space. (Concrete proof Flux differs per
  module, not a global wow/flutter.)
- **Master section:** Input/output gains, **Width** (stereo imaging), Cut Filter, Tone EQ.
- **Verdict:** "Makes anything sound old. Simple, stunningly effective processors… one of the
  finest effects plugins we've come across in a long time" — for aging sources *and full
  mixes* (i.e. master-bus use is sanctioned here).
- **Weaknesses:** "**Space module seems a little superfluous**" — the reverb underwhelms;
  bypassed modules disappear visually (minor UI gripe).
