WHAT IT IS — DS-10 shapes TRANSIENTS: the short, non-harmonic "attack" spike at the start of a waveform (drum hits, but also vocals, guitars, etc.). It lets you enhance or reduce the attack and the sustain WITHOUT affecting the rest of the waveform. First plugin in XLN's "Addictive FX" line. AU / VST / VST3 / AAX, 64-bit; macOS 10.13+ / Win 7+. Low CPU.

CORE CONTROLS
- **Attack** — increases or reduces the volume of the ATTACK (transient) portion. Push up = more smack/click/punch; pull down = soften the attack (tame harsh percussion, soften ambient hits).
- **Sustain** — increases or reduces the volume of the SUSTAIN (post-transient/tail) portion. Pull DOWN = drier, tighter, less room/ring (de-verb-like). Push UP = more body, more tail, more room, parallel-compression-like fatness.

THREE MODES (top tabs) — each is "individually tuned/optimized" under the hood for its source, AND each changes what the Mojo knob targets:
- **Kick** → Mojo = **Tightness** (low-end). Goes from boomy → controlled, tight punch (or back). Counteracts low-end frequency build-up from long Sustain settings.
- **Snare** → Mojo = **Body** (low-mid, typical snare frequencies). Add/remove midrange body.
- **Bus** → Mojo = **Presence** (high-frequency). Increase to cut through better; decrease to soften/tame harsh high end. (NOTE: ProducerSpot's copy calls the Bus/high-freq Mojo "Snap" rather than "Presence"; MusicRadar + the XLN UI per the Thomann video call it "Presence." Same control — high-frequency focus.)
- Modes are a STARTING POINT, not a rule — you can run any mode on any source (multiple sources confirm).

MOJO — a separate processor from the main Attack/Sustain engine. Shapes the attack and punch in CURATED FREQUENCY BANDS while leaving others unaffected. "Adds flavor and color just where you need it." Dovetails with Attack/Sustain; its tighter settings are "brilliantly effective on kicks and snares" (MusicRadar).

THREE ALGORITHMS (change the overall decay/release style — per MusicRadar this has a MORE obvious impact than the Mode setting):
- **Natural** — the default. Newly developed, transparent, artifact-free even when pushed into extreme territory. Cleaner, punchier, LOUDER transients than Classic; most noticeable on kicks. Recommended for nearly all material.
- **Classic** — the same algorithm from Addictive Drums 2. Sharp, "raw," more aggressive with a pronounced PUMPING effect. Sounds lacklustre vs Natural on kicks but its positive-Sustain settings nicely emphasise snare tails. Use for vibe/pump.
- **Smooth** — extra-long release time = "buttery smooth, parallel-compression-like results." Slightly gentler initial sustain than Natural's otherwise-fast response. Great on complex sources (full kits, drum loops).

OTHER
- **Soft Clipping** — toggle; adds light saturation when you push into the non-linear zone (e.g. heavy Attack boosts). Used as a gentle harmonic/limiting character (Thomann video).
- **Metering** — visual transient display; "use your ears, not eyes" caveat from users (Morsy video).
- Known LIMITATIONS (MusicRadar): no dedicated input-gain control for gain-staging; no multiband mode (Mojo is the only frequency-aware element).

The full step-by-step manual PDF is reachable from inside the plugin: menu icon (top-right of the DS-10 UI) → manual.
