Elektronauts · "Reels vs Echomelt vs RC-20 (lo-fi plugin opinions)" · General Discussion

The cleanest "which lo-fi tool when" thread — RC-20's edge vs where users reach elsewhere.

## RC-20's edge
- **Magnitude slider is the headline win:** "moving all parameters at once" eliminates complex
  automation. A user describes putting RC-20 **on the master bus** and automating the single
  Magnitude parameter to morph **lo-fi → hi-fi** across a section. (The build/transition move.)
- **One-box breadth:** "if you only have one" lo-fi plugin, "**RC-20 covers a lot of ground**"
  depending on need.

## When users reach elsewhere
- **For cassette-specific realism:** they pull **Klevgrand DAW Cassette** or **Wavesfactory
  Cassette**, and rate **SketchCassette II** worth considering. (Same verdict as the KVR
  cassette thread: RC-20 isn't the *authentic-tape* tool.)
- **For precision bitcrushing:** **Plogue chipcrusher** over RC-20's Digital module — users
  find most stock bitcrushers (RC-20 included) limited beyond basic bit/sample-rate control.

## Takeaway
RC-20 = **workflow efficiency + all-in-one versatility + the Magnitude macro**. Supplement it
with specialized tools for (a) authentic cassette character and (b) deep bitcrush. Maps
directly to this rig: RC-20 for overt one-knob lo-fi morphs, SketchCassette for believable
tape, the hardware (Generation Loss / Big Time) for played-in real-time degradation.
