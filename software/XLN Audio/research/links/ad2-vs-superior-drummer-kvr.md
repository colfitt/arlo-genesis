# AD2 vs Superior Drummer 3 — honest pros/cons (thread skews pro-SD3)

## Where AD2 LOSES vs SD3 (cited limitations — relevant truths)
- **Shallower sampling depth.** el-bo: "they just weren't sampled anywhere deeply
  enough" — fewer velocity layers/round-robins than SD3. Matters for exposed,
  realistic performances; matters LESS for buried/degraded shoegaze drums.
- **Fixed instrument slots.** EvilDragon: AD2 has a **fixed number of instrument
  slots**; SD3 is unlimited. You can't endlessly add pieces to an AD2 kit.
- **Compressed samples.** AD2 uses sample **compression** (smaller library, lighter
  disk) vs SD3's uncompressed — a fidelity-vs-footprint tradeoff.
- **Cymbals can be harsh/overbearing**, with limited room-sound control on the base
  kit (from the parallel Gearspace SD3-vs-AD2 thread). BFD is the cymbal king.
- General consensus: heavy users move to SD3 and "haven't used AD2 once since."

## Where AD2 WINS (the reason to keep reaching for it)
- **Speed / "program drums on the fly."** Lighter, faster to load, fast preset and
  Beats browsing — the go-to for sketching a part quickly.
- **Smaller footprint** (compressed library, lower disk/RAM than SD3's huge install).
- **Keyboard-drummer friendly** — "keyboard drummers will get great results with AD2
  or EZdrummer" (vs the deep e-drum/MIDI editing SD3 rewards).
- **Per-channel FX are immediate** (EQ/comp/dist/transient/tape on every drum).

## Takeaway for THIS rig
For doom/shoegaze/slowcore where drums are slow, simple, and intentionally
degraded/buried, AD2's "shallow sampling" and harsh cymbals are largely irrelevant —
you're going to soften, wash, and dirty everything anyway. AD2's **speed + per-channel
character FX + small footprint** are the actual wins. SD3's depth would be wasted
effort here.
