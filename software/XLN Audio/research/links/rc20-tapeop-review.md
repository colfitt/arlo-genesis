Tape Op Magazine · gear review #142 · (RC-20 launch-era review)

The most credible editorial review for this rig's audience.

- **Module map:** "Digital is a bit crusher, and Magnetic simulates tape machine magic."
  Plus Wow/Flutter, "Air Pressure Distortion," Reverb/Resonator (Space), Tape Wear and
  Dropouts.
- **Noise:** standard (Vinyl Crackle, Hum) *and* creative options — "**Big Muff Noise, VHS
  Noise, DC Noise**, etc." Each module has **Follow** and **Duck** options plus a **Flux**
  setting "for authenticity."
- **Magnitude** = "a 'global' amount control for the entire plug-in" — adjusts all six big
  knobs and the master controls (In, Out, EQ) at once. Lets you ride "from a dry, unprocessed
  sound to **full blast wobbly distorted mayhem**." (This is the build/automation hook.)
- **Key observation (why it's forgiving):** "regardless of which parameters I turned up to
  inadvisable levels, the RC-20 still came out sounding **musical and retro**" — because XLN
  enabled **default master low/high cuts**. (Implication: it self-protects against harshness;
  the trade-off is it's hard to make it sound *broken/ugly* without disabling those cuts.)
- **Only complaint:** "**lack of an auto-gain feature**" — output level jumps as you push it,
  so ride In/Out yourself. The reviewer accepts this as fitting the creative design.
- **Verdict:** "fun, powerful," ideal when "other plug-ins are too technical or complex."
