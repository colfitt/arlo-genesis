AudioBlob · review

Source-by-source "what each module is good ON," plus the clearest CPU statement.

- **Noise** — vinyl crackle / VHS hiss / tape static with **Follow/Duck**; "effective on
  **drum loops**."
- **Wobble** — "**Small amounts give a subtle lo-fi vibe, while extreme settings turn synths
  into seasick nightmares**." (Use small on a drone bed; extreme on a single synth layer.)
- **Distort** — warm saturation → aggressive fuzz; recommended on **bass to add grit without
  muddiness** (pairs with the Focus filter to keep the low fundamental clean).
- **Digital** — bitcrush + sample-rate reduction; "**12-bit drum machine character on
  hi-hats**."
- **Magnetic (Tape)** — wear + dropouts; "**instant 'cassette demo tape' vibes**" on vocals.
- **CPU:** "**RC-20 barely moved the CPU meter even with multiple instances**" on a mid-range
  laptop. VST/AU/AAX 64-bit, Win+Mac, minimal footprint.
- **Presets:** cover drum buses, synth leads, vocals, strings, pads; modular toggles let you
  keep one module from a preset and drop the rest.
- **Only real knock:** "Using other plugins combined can achieve same results" — i.e. it's a
  convenience/all-in-one, not a unique algorithm you can't get elsewhere.
