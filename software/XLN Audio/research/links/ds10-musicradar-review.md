The most authoritative hands-on assessment found. Key usage takeaways:

ALGORITHMS matter more than Mode:
- "The choice of algorithm has a more obvious impact on transient shaping than the Mode setting." So audition Classic/Natural/Smooth FIRST, then pick a mode.
- **Natural** — "very transparent, enabling more careful emphasis of the tail with the Sustain control." Delivers "cleaner, punchier, louder transients than Classic." Most noticeable improvement is on KICKS.
- **Smooth** — shares Natural's "fast response, but with a slightly gentler initial sustain"; also cleaner than Classic. (Parallel-comp-like long release.)
- **Classic** — "quite aggressive and 'pumping'," colored. Sounds "positively lacklustre in comparison" on kicks — BUT "positive Sustain settings really emphasise tails of snares." So Classic = the snare-tail / pumping-vibe algorithm.

MOJO usage:
- "The Mojo knob introduces another layer of frequency-specific shaping that dovetails nicely with Attack and Sustain, and its tighter settings are brilliantly effective on kicks and snares."
- Kick **Tightness**: "brilliantly effective on kicks, counteracting some of the frequency build-up that results from really long Sustain settings." (i.e. push Sustain for body, then use Mojo Tightness to clamp the resulting low-end mud — a key combo.)
- Bus **Presence**: works "as an enhancer or harshness tamer on overheads." Also succeeds on non-drum material — "acoustic guitar, piano and electric bass."

VERDICT / LIMITATIONS:
- Feature-rich, great-sounding, very effective. Natural/Smooth beat Classic for clean punch, especially kicks.
- Criticisms: NO input-gain control for gain-staging; NO multiband operation (Mojo is the only frequency-aware element). Otherwise well-regarded.
