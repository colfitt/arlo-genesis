Internet Tattoo · "The 7 Best Plugins to Achieve That Lo-Fi Sound" · 2020

Best single articulation of RC-20's *character* vs the realistic-tape camp — the "which tool"
decision in plain language.

- **Positioning:** RC-20 is "the multi-effect plugin to rule them all (or some of them, at the
  very least)." Ubiquitous in tutorials and live sessions.
- **vs Wavesfactory Cassette:** RC-20 is "**not subtle — or even realistic**," whereas Cassette
  is "a little more faithful to real tape machines."
- **vs SketchCassette II:** SketchCassette targets "distorted magic" / "bedroom pop"; RC-20 is
  the broader, more versatile box.
- **vs Bad Tape:** the author got "tired of the **Vinyl 1 preset** on RC-20" and switched to
  Bad Tape when wanting something "pronounced and kinda wild." (Watch the over-used factory
  presets — see Pitfalls.)
- **What RC-20 is best at:** a "**cartoony rendition of a bad tape or a degraded vinyl**, which
  might often be what you want." Easy: "you don't need years of sound engineering school."

**Rig read:** when you want *obvious, exaggerated, cartoon* degradation (the overt lo-fi
statement), reach RC-20. When you want it to sound like a real worn cassette nobody clocks as
a plugin, reach SketchCassette / the hardware tape.
