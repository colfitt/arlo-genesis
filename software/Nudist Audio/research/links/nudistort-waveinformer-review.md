The most detailed independent review. Describes Nudistort as "not just another
'vintage tube warmth' saturation unit" but "a monster. A beautifully designed one."
Character over fidelity; "behaves almost like a collaborator."

## Distort section controls (PRIMARY, from hands-on review)
- **Mix** — wet/dry blend.
- **Drive** — input gain, but does NOT follow a traditional distortion curve:
  "higher values don't always mean 'more distorted'"; subtle settings can yield
  the strangest results.
- **Type** — four distinct algorithms (A/B/C/D), each a very different flavor.
- **Mode (T/P)** — T = more traditional; P = untamed, unpredictable, expressive.
- **Grain** — alters micro-timing / feel of the distortion; interacts uniquely with
  each Type (badly named — NOT a granular effect).
- **Damping** — controls/tames transient harshness.

## Delay section (a real second engine, not an afterthought)
- **Mix** (note: even 100% still passes some dry), **Time** (keep <90ms for
  tone-shaping; higher for harmonic expansion), **Abnormality** (clean delay →
  "harmonically recombined chaos"), **Feedback** (warning: high feedback + high
  abnormality "can quickly spiral"), **Type** (4 algorithms), **Sync**.
- Verdict: "If you want a classic delay, go get EchoBoy and call it a day. But for
  unpredictable delay magic, Nudistort is a different beast entirely."

## Global / advanced controls
- **Input/Output Gain**, **Tone** (pre-distortion tilt EQ), **Mu-law**
  (telecoms-inspired companding algorithm), **Buzz** (amp-like noise).
- **L/R Offset** (stereo delay), **Wobble** (tape-style pitch mod),
  **Mod Amount/Frequency** (stereo modulation depth/speed),
  **Noodle** (pitch-following sine wave with blend modes),
  **Species** (harmonic resampling keyed to a musical note — manual tip: set the
  Species note to the tonic or dominant of the key you're playing in).

## How the reviewer uses it (sources, by name)
- **Drums** — great on individual hits or across the drum buss for aggressive
  transients, glitchy textures, raw energy; on drum *rooms* it makes everything
  "more explode-y."
- **Bass** — "monstrous density and harmonics."
- **Guitar** — "fizzy, scorched leads to mangled power chords"; mentions processing
  each guitar string separately via a SubSix Hexaphonic Pickup (i.e. per-string
  distortion — directly relevant to a GK-5/hexaphonic rig).
- **Vocals** — "alien-like formants or ripping distortion"; recommends putting
  Nudistort on **Reverb or Delay sends** for inhuman effects.
- **Synths/Pads** — "ambient detuning to dense, dystopian soundscapes."
- **Subtle use** — "a few percentage points of Nudistort for subtle thickness."

## Chain placement / parallel
- Insert directly OR on aux sends; the Mix blend enables subtle parallel use on a
  buss or send rather than total replacement. Works on individual tracks and busses.

## Presets / learning
- Ships with presets from Kenny Beats, Andrew Huang and other producers — used as
  learning starting points. Manual recommended reading (esp. for Species).

## Limits
- "Doesn't aim for fidelity"; not for anyone wanting clean/linear behavior; needs a
  different creative mindset than traditional saturation.
