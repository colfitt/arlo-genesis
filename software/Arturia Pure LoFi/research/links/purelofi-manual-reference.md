Arturia · "Pure LoFi User Manual" v1.0.0 EN · 148 pp · 2025 (authoritative reference)

> **CRITICAL CORRECTION:** Pure LoFi is an **Arturia** software *instrument* (synth/sampler), part of **V Collection 11** — it is NOT a Cableguys plugin (the repo's Cableguys SoftwareProfile.md and the task brief both mis-file it). It is also NOT an insert effect like RC-20/SketchCassette — it generates pre-degraded sound you PLAY. Built on the Pigments engine.

## 1. What it is (manual §1.1–1.4)
"Pure LoFi is a new frontier for Arturia: instead of capturing a specific sound, it tries to capture a vibe." Lofi = a production style/philosophy across dreampop, hip-hop, vaporwave, chiptunes, sleepcore, grunge, ambient electronica. Two sound engines (each = Realistic Instrument / Creative Sampler / LoFi Oscillator) + a Noise engine, all routed through a multi-mode filter. The **analog** lo-fi side = the **LoFi Processor** at the top (reel-to-reel, cassette, and tape-instrument like the Mellotron — "as in our Tape Mello-Fi effect plug-in" — plus vacuum-tube/valve circuitry and vintage loudspeakers). The **digital** lo-fi side = the per-engine **Hardware Modes** (Fairlight CMI, Emulator, MPC, etc.). Up to 16-voice poly, mono legato/retrigger.

## 2. Signal flow (authoritative order)
**Sound Engines (×2) + Noise engine → [per-engine EQ → per-engine Hardware mode] → multi-mode Filter → LoFi Processor → Effects (×4) → output.** (Per the manual + the official sound-design page: "modelling of old digital units on the single sound sources, and the tape emulation on the master output.")

## 3. LoFi Processor (manual §3.2) — the centerpiece
Three sub-areas: **coloration modes**, the big **LoFi Amount** knob, and **six LoFi parameters** (right side, usually hidden under the Arturia logo — appear when the mouse is in the upper area).
- **6 Coloration modes** (floppy-disk icons; up/down arrows or six buttons): **Golden Age** (warm, subtle saturation + gentle compression), **Velvet Frost** (cool/airy, cold tape, soft HF grain), **Vintage Glow** (slightly overdriven, classic analog bite), **Dim Memories** (washed-out/degraded, dusty nostalgia, softened transients), **Cathodic Tube** (bright/noisy, raw edge, tube buzz), **Fuzzy Line** (harsh, midrange-heavy, aggressive lo-fi roughness). Each mode changes what the six params do under the hood (distortion type/range of Drive, the speaker IRs, tape wear/head calibration, etc.). "Turn knobs and listen."
- **LoFi Amount** = master scale over the six params; preserves their proportional differences — the main "more or less" macro.
- **6 LoFi parameters:** **Drive** (distortion/overdrive; can add noise modulation on filter cutoff depending on mode), **Wear** (tape condition; can add noise mod on cutoff), **Tone** ("tilt" EQ — low+high shelves skewing toward bass/mid/treble), **Wobble** (wow & flutter together: flutter = faster pitch variation/"bad vibrato"; wow = slower, "seasick"), **Speaker** (vintage speaker/cab IR + tone/filter EQ), **Vintage** (amount of analog variance and drift between voices). Each knob is itself a macro over several internal params.

## 4. Sound engines (manual §3.3–3.6)
Common per-engine controls: **Octave / Coarse / Fine** tuning, **Pan**, **Level** (into the filter), an **EQ** (low + high shelf = HP/LP, **upstream of and independent from the main filter**), and the **Hardware mode**.
- **Realistic Instrument (§3.4):** multisamples — bells & mallets, brass & winds, guitar & bass, piano & keys, strings, synths. **Start** knob = sample start time (e.g. shave off the piano attack transient).
- **Creative Sampler (§3.5):** up to **5 sample slots**, each with tuning, looping, forward/backward playback, gain, pan. Trigger modes: round-robin, random, selected. Deep sample edit (trim + loop markers). Can import your own samples (≤16 s recommended).
- **LoFi Oscillator (§3.6):** waveform browser in 5 categories — **Analog, Basic, Digital, Harmonic, Natural** — generated by analog modeling or wavetable. Main control = **Timbre** (changes harmonic content: waveshaping on analog waves, wavetable-pointer position on wavetable sounds, harmonic blend on Harmonic waves). "An ideal target for modulation."

## 5. Hardware mode (manual §3.3.3) — per engine, independent
Emulates the converters/frequency response of six classics: **Fairlight CMI, Emulator II, Casio SK-1, E-mu SP-1200, Akai S900, Akai MPC60**, plus three Arturia Custom (increasing bit-crush + downsampling): **Deteriorate, Damage, Crush.** (Simpler than the standalone Bitcrusher effect for mimicking iconic sampler rates/bit depths.) Each engine has its own — combine different modes across engines for layered textures.

## 6. Noise engine (manual §3.7)
Two modes: **Texture** (loops continuously while a note is held — wind, rain, vinyl noise) and **Transient** (one-shot bursts for the attack — drum hits, scrapes). Browser is different per mode; can import your own. Controls: **Tune** (±36 st), **Level** (upstream of the filter), **Stereo** (Texture only — widens) / **Pan** (Transient only). Keyboard-tracking toggle (Transient).

## 7. Filter (manual §3.8–3.9)
Three buttons route any combination of Engine 1 / Engine 2 / Noise through the filter. Three types:
- **Clean** = the Pigments multi-filter (LP/HP/BP/notch, sharp resonance).
- **Analog** = modeled on the Oberheim SEM, warm/musical with soft saturation.
- **LoFi (downsampling)** = from Pigments 6; resamples at a lower rate for a grungy quality. **Frequency = the downsampling sample rate** (NOT a cutoff). **Pre** and **Post** = separate LP filters before/after downsampling (each on/off + knob). **Envelope amount** scales filter-env modulation of the downsampling frequency. **Jitter** introduces timing variation between downsampling cycles. Distortion "stays consistent" — it doesn't build up as more notes play. Dedicated **filter ADSR** + the **amp/VCA ADSR**.

## 8. Effects (manual §4) — four slots, 18 FX
Insert or send (Spatial FX has a Send/Insert toggle), per-effect On/Off + Dry/Wet + tempo sync, copy/swap, effect presets. The 18: **Reverb, Delay, Tape Echo, PS Delay, Compressor, Multiband, Multi Filter, Parametric EQ, Distortion, Bitcrusher, Trance Gate, Super Unison, Chorus, Chorus JUN-6, Flanger, BL-20 Flanger, Phaser, Panner.**

## 9. Modulators (manual §5) — drag-and-drop, 3 mod slots
**Envelope** (ADSR + scale/curves/retrigger), **Function** (multi-breakpoint complex envelope/LFO with presets, draw tools, rate + tempo sync, polarity/smooth), **Random** (scale, rate, sync, retrigger), **Voice Modulator** (new — sends a different value each time a voice triggers; count + mode + polarity + randomize), **Mod Sequencer** (step modulation with swing). Plus MIDI modulators (mod wheel, note, velocity, release velocity, aftertouch, MPE slide) and **four Macros**. **Limitation (per Molten review): you cannot modulate a modulator's amount — a feature Pigments 6 has but Pure LoFi lacks.**

## 10. Formats / price / specs
**Standalone, VST, VST3, AU, AAX.** Mac: macOS 11+, quad-core 3.4 GHz, 4 GB RAM. ~£149 standalone (also in V Collection 11 Pro). **250+ presets.** Resizable GUI, NKS, MPE, NTS-ESP microtuning. In-app tutorials (Side Panel → Tutorials), though community notes the built-in tutorial lags the shipping feature set slightly.
