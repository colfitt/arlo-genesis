MATTHS (Woody Piano Shack) · "ARTURIA Pure LoFi Is Here! Walkthrough, Demo and Features V Collection 11" · 2025-04-24 · 27:18

> First-impressions walkthrough — the strongest for the ambient/drone-player angle. Arturia Pure LoFi instrument, V Collection 11.

**Framing.** "A synthesizer they've come at from a whole new perspective — lo-fi sonic imperfection, defined through warmth, grain, imperfection, character." Lo-fi engines mimic vintage digital degradation; LoFi Processors add tape character; wrapped together with acoustic sample playback. Explicitly aimed at the world of **tape loops, the crossover between old samplers and found sounds, organic lo-fi music production**.

**Signal flow (stated).** Two oscillators (each = realistic instrument / creative sampler / lofi oscillator) + a noise oscillator → filter → effects. At the top is the **LoFi amount** as a master control, with Drive, Wear, Tone, Wobble, Speaker, Vintage, and six coloration "flavors" (algorithms). **"All of the lofi stuff comes before the effects; the effects are at the end."**

**Walking the first preset apart.** A felt piano (Realistic) + Creative Sampler + Noise Texture ("forest rain," with stereo enhancement) + Analog filter, all at once, into reverb / tape echo / bit-crush. He isolates each layer. The filter can be assigned per source — **engine 1, engine 2, or the noise — so you can route only some things through it** (e.g. keep the noise out of the filter). Each engine also has its **own EQ** ("you can craft that") and its **own Hardware mode**, and independent tuning.

**Drone/ambient-relevant moves:**
- "It's one of those synths that's very sound-designy and allows you to explore. **It makes me want to play slow.** Allow time for things to happen — you don't have to fill everything with notes; the noise texture fills those spaces."
- Modulate **Wear** from the **mod wheel** → "make things sound duller... sounds really tired now, in a good way."
- On a bass patch, "on the decay of the sound you can really hear the quality of the sampler degradation coming back." Turning engine **levels up drives into the filter** → extra saturation/distortion.
- Notch out resonance build-up with the parametric EQ effect; brighten with it too.
- "Really broken and wobbly and gorgeous... it's like a Mellotron vibe."

**The killer ambient trick (LoFi amount as an envelope target).** He drags the **main amp ADSR onto the master LoFi amount**: "If you don't have the sustain on, you just get it on the initial hit — so if you want the initial transient to have the lo-fi feel and then it clears up, you can do that. Or bring in a little so it backs off after the initial transient." (Lo-fi that decays into clarity, or the reverse.)

**Other notes.** Hardware modes listed (CMI, EMU, SK1, SP1200, Weary 900, MPC, + Deteriorate/Damage/Crush). Noise: "warn vinyl / tape wheel" textures, switchable to transient. Filter types Clean/Analog/LoFi; "the LoFi filter sounds like the new filter from the latest Pigments — you've got Jitter in there as well." Creative Sampler loop points: change start point + loop length, loop forward/back/fade, per-sample gain (separate from level — drives distortion). Trance Gate from the JUP-8000. Verdict: "a very welcome addition... people are going to absolutely love this one... brilliant for ambient."
