The Sound Test Room · "Arturia Pure LoFi - Low Fidelity Synthesizer - Walkthrough & Demo" · 2025-04-26 · 42:20

> Arturia Pure LoFi instrument, V Collection 11. Hands-on demo strong on the drag-and-drop modulation workflow and the "clean too, not just extreme" point.

**First impressions.** "I absolutely love this... it seriously appeals to my sense of nostalgia." Starts on a preset called **"Dry Landscape."** Key point made early: **"Although it'll do extreme lo-fi, you can actually make it sound very clean as well."** (Lo-fi is opt-in via the LoFi amount.)

**Architecture (restated).** Two synth engines / oscillators, each one of three instruments — Realistic, LoFi oscillator, or Creative Sampler. Each has loads of presets (e.g. Creative Sampler → Voices, or Guitar & Bass → acoustic guitar; Realistic → upright piano). Tons of effects, "incredible modulation."

**Building from a default patch.** Loads a default ("all" → D). Filter on (many types — "noisy filter or clean"). The **noise texture**: turn off both oscillators, switch on noise → ambient bed under your playing; categories include **natural** (audition, double-click to load — e.g. "frying vegetables"), with level + tuning. **Hardware modes** (the down-sampling/down-grading based on vintage hardware) demoed on the clean LoFi oscillator → Fairlight CMI, EMU, SK1, SP1200, MPC + two Arturia customs ("a very clean oscillator is a good example because you really hear the aliasing").

**The LoFi processor page.** "This is a cathodic tube — these are different algorithms you can apply whenever you like. Turn the LoFi amount right up and go through the different algorithms." Further control via **Drive, Wear, Tone, Vintage, Speaker, Wobble.** Important UI tell: **"If you notice an underline under a knob, that means there's movement added in the modulators."** (Visual cue that a parameter is being modulated.)

**Drag-and-drop modulation (the headline workflow).** Advanced → effects all have presets too (e.g. reverb = Hall, and you can modulate it). "Modulating anything is really easy." Open a **Function** (an LFO — the wavy line at the top shows what it's doing), slow the rate, then **pick it up and drop it onto a destination** (e.g. cutoff). "There are two now applied — the one originally there and the one I just set up." You can apply multiple modulations per function/envelope and swap a slot between Function / Random / Voice Modulator. **Random** = pushes out random pulses. Four **Macros** to assign anywhere.

**Transient engine.** The noise engine's Transient mode = sharper, shorter bursts, good for kick drums / percussion (crash, Foley). "Transient sounds are really nice for drums and stuff."

**Saving presets.** Save preset as → User bank → set author, type (e.g. Ambient/Atmospheric), characteristics, e.g. "lofi keys."

**Verdict.** "Massive potential... I've turned the lo-fi off here, there's no degradation going on — really good selection of filters (low/band/high pass, notch), brilliant selection of modulation sources. It's brilliant for ambient. Hours of fun sound design — and it's not difficult now, it's really easy."
