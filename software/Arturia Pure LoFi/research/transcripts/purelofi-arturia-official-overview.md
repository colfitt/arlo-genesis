> NOTE: Pure LoFi is an **Arturia** software *instrument* (synth/sampler), part of V Collection 11 — NOT a Cableguys plugin and NOT an effect you put on a track. This is the authoritative vendor walkthrough.

Pure LoFi captures the imperfections, noise, warmth and character of iconic vintage hardware. It's designed around **three independent sound sources: two main engines + one noise generator**, combined freely — from mellow keys to rich hybrid textures. Arturia's **Hardware mode** and **LoFi Processor** imprint legendary sampler and tape-machine imperfections directly at the source.

**Three sound sources.** The two main engines each independently set to one of three modes:
- **Realistic Instrument** — rich acoustic instruments: Rhodes, upright piano, mallets, brass, wind, guitars, more.
- **Creative Sampler** — experimental textures; multiple samples played unpredictably via mapping modes (round-robin, random, selected).
- **LoFi Oscillator** — a wavetable-based oscillator offering dynamic synthetic lo-fi character.

**Per-engine controls.** Each engine has Coarse/Octave/Fine tuning. Realistic Instrument adds a **Start** control (adjusts sample playback position — for dynamic expression or to remove the natural attack). LoFi Oscillator's **Timbre** morphs through waveform harmonics, shaping brightness/complexity. Pan and Level for stereo position/amplitude. Each engine has an **EQ (highpass + lowpass) placed BEFORE Hardware mode** — it reshapes the sound before it reaches Hardware mode, **significantly influencing the lo-fi coloration**.

**Noise generator, two modes.** **Texture** = continuous ambient noise (rain, vinyl crackle, tape hiss) for atmospheric backgrounds. **Transient** = short percussive noise to enhance attack and add definition. Adjust Tune, Level, and Stereo width (Texture) / Pan (Transient).

**Hardware mode** authentically emulates vintage digital samplers, imprinting the iconic coloration at the engine level: **CMI** (Fairlight's noisy digital textures), **EMU** (Emulator II's crunchy aliasing), **SK1** (raw low fidelity), **SP1200** (gritty 12-bit character that defined early hip-hop), **Weary 900** (Akai S900) and **MPC** (warm, unstable digital sampled tones), plus Arturia originals **Deteriorate / Damage / Crush** (subtle warmth → extreme distortion). **Each engine selects its own Hardware mode independently — enabling layered lo-fi complexity.**

**Filter, three types.** **Clean** (precise digital filtering, sharp resonance). **Analog** (warm, musical, soft saturation). **LoFi** (aggressive downsampling, aliasing, digital artifacts for extreme lo-fi textures). All enhanced by a dedicated filter ADSR envelope; can use MIDI velocity and keyboard tracking.

**LoFi Processor (after the filter)** adds tape-inspired warmth/texture. Six coloration modes: **Golden Age, Velvet Frost, Vintage Glow** (shades of warmth/subtle saturation) → **Dim Memories, Cathodic Tube, Fuzzy Line** (more aggressive, degraded). Beneath the Arturia logo are the six fine-control parameters: **Drive** (saturation intensity), **Wobble** (pitch instability + drift), **Wear** (degradation: noise + artifacts), **Speaker** (vintage playback-system emulation), **Tone** (overall brightness/warmth), **Vintage** (subtly varies voice playback to recreate analog imperfections).

**Vibrato** modulator routes expressive modulation to pitch, amplitude or filter cutoff (gentle vibrato, rhythmic tremolo, subtle filter movement).

**Effects: four FX slots** — parametric EQ, phaser, chorus, reverb, delay, etc. Main-panel knobs give quick adjustments; deeper editing in the Advanced panel.

**Modulators (Advanced panel):** ADSR, Function (multi-segment envelope for precise/evolving modulation), Random (unpredictable variation — organic living textures), Mod Sequencer (rhythmic step modulation), Voice Modulator (per-voice values, the analog-hardware feel). Routable to nearly any parameter via drag-and-drop. Plus keyboard/MIDI modulation (velocity, aftertouch, polyphonic/MPE, mod wheel) and four **Macros** (one knob → many params, e.g. cutoff + reverb amount together).

**Creative Sampler** supports your own custom samples with mapping modes (random, round-robin); playback params include loop, reverse, fade.

**Closing tips (from Arturia):**
1. The **EQ sits before Hardware mode** — it significantly shapes how sounds are colored; use it creatively to influence the lo-fi artifacts.
2. **Modulating engine params (Start, Timbre, Pan) adds expressive variation and realism.**
3. **Combining different Hardware modes across the two engines** yields intricate layered textures and unique results.
