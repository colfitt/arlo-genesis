Molten Music Technology (Robin Vincent) · "Arturia Pure Lofi Review" · 2025-04-24 · 59:50

> The deepest aesthetic read + several rig-relevant nuggets. Arturia Pure LoFi instrument, V Collection 11.

**The core thesis (matters for how to file this tool).** "When I first booted it up I dismissed it as yet another contextual cinematic sample-based sound source... but that would be a mistake, because **Pure LoFi is actually a synthesizer.** It's not just a sample-based library — it has waveforms inside, simplicity inside, layers of sound, and upon that Arturia have built extraordinary levels of degradation. You can have as much or as little as you like — dial it fully in to pull itself apart in every vintage-overdriven, speaker-cracked, crumbly way, **or just use it as a synth, because it's good at that too.**"

**Structure (built up from scratch).** Front end = two oscillators + a noise oscillator + a filter + effects, then "shovels of degradation you throw on top."
- **LoFi Oscillator**: simple waveforms — organ, CS-80, DX7, Farfisa, Juno, Jupiter, sines/squares, a bunch of digital oscillators. "This is what I mean about it having an extraordinary synthesizer basis."
- **Realistic Instrument**: pianos, plucked instruments, CZ synths, brass. Two engines, so you can stack two realistic instruments, two samples, or blend oscillators/wavetables.
- **Creative Sampler**: import your own samples AND explore the realistic-instrument bank in a sample-based way (sample-level edits). Per round-robin layer: tune, loop on/off, play mode, gain, set the start point; choose what each round-robin slot's sample is (piano / bell / glock / guitar / your own sample) → "you can create your own instruments in here and then cover them full of lo-fi potential."

**LoFi amount knob.** As you turn it, it introduces the six parameters: overdrive, **Wear and tear**, pulls the **Tone** apart, **tape Wobble**, **Speaker** brokenness, general **Vintage** goo-ness. Six knobs set how much each is pulled in by the macro.

**Coloration modes (the floppy discs).** Velvet Frost / Vintage Glow / Dim Memories / Cathodic Tube / Fuzzy Line. **Key behavior:** "As you change these textures, the settings stay the same. It's not moving from preset to preset — it's just changing the **nature** of what you're applying. The amount stays the same." (Swap character without losing your dialed-in values.)

**Standout wish (confirms scope).** "**I'd really like this [LoFi Processor] section to be available as a plugin, as an effects plugin by itself. I think that would be a lovely thing.**" → confirms the LoFi Processor is NOT separately available as an insert effect; it only exists inside the instrument.

**Filter / noise tricks.**
- Filter section is taken from the new Pigments — Analog filter + LoFi filter (essentially bit-crushing).
- **Noise routing trick:** "One nice feature on the filter is that you can leave the noise out — because noise is often felt more in the high frequencies, **you can still filter your bass and leave the noise frying those eggs in the background.**" (Filter the tonal layer, keep the texture unfiltered.)

**Hardware modes** = a separate level of deterioration "outside the main LoFi": CMI / EMU / SK1 / SP1200 / Weary 900 / MPC + Arturia Deteriorate / Damage / Crush. "I'm thoroughly enjoying using a **single oscillator, a single sound source, and then layering on different amounts of lo-fi loveliness and wobble** just to create these simple textures. It's gorgeous."

**Advanced panel.** Two detailed envelopes (amp + filter), expression (vibrato, glide, velocity), four effects (insert or send, with presets). "The visual information is sublime... almost all of this instrument comes from Pigments." Three modulators (Envelope, Function with lots of presets, Random, Voice Modulator [from Pigments 6], Mod Sequencer), all drag-and-drop onto any parameter — including the **main LoFi knob** and the individual six knobs. Voice Modulator gives a different value per note (great with the arpeggiator). Keyboard tracking, velocity, release velocity, aftertouch, MPE; four Macros (one knob → many params; they also appear in the preset library for auditioning).

**Honest limitation flagged.** "**You can't modulate a modulator** — you can't drop a modulator onto an amount knob that's there for something else. That's odd considering it's a feature in Pigments 6." (A real workflow ceiling vs Pigments.)

**Verdict / vibe.** "There's something in that sense of deterioration, that sense things are not all as they should be... it has a life to it, something otherworldly, ethereal. It's a ghost that's found a home inside a machine on its edge, falling apart, held together with masking tape... a sense of grieving within it that these sounds shouldn't really be there — the result of things going wrong, as opposed to things being intentionally pushed in that direction." Bottom line: **it's a synthesizer** — start small with waveforms/samples, build, then break.
