## Iridium (hardware) — the committed front-end
- Strymon's own framing: "walk into the studio with your ideal tone already dialled in" → **commit while tracking**, latency-free analog path, DI straight to interface in stereo. Place LAST in chain (print amp+cab) or before time FX (so delays/verbs land post-cab).
- Reviewer consensus (TapeOp): Iridium "did a much better job than a plug-in on every source" for its core amps; "feels more like a real amp than a real amp." Breakup smooth/dynamic; pedals & fuzzes take to it well → ideal as the committed DI for tracking rhythm in the same space as bass/drums.
- Trade-off: fewer amps, fixed models, you print the decision.

## Neural DSP plugins — the flexible, deep-feature option
- Way more amp options + full pre/post FX chain + custom IR loading, cheaper per voice, **non-destructive** (change amp/IR after the take without reamping each track).
- Caveat: slight monitoring **latency** when run as a DAW insert. (Secret Weapons reviewer runs Nolly **standalone**, routes guitar in via the interface, and prints the wet audio to the DAW to dodge plugin latency — or reamps DI tracks at mix.)
- "Besides high-gain, the tonal quality is not that different" between the better plugins and hardware (cross-source verdict). Reach for Neural when you want the *specific* modelled amp + its FX, or flexibility to revise at mix.

## Practical split for a hybrid rig
- **Iridium** = the print-it DI you commit to during tracking (esp. tracking with the band/loops).
- **Neural plugin** = the reamp/mix-stage amp where you want the exact amp voice + onboard ambient FX and the freedom to change your mind.
