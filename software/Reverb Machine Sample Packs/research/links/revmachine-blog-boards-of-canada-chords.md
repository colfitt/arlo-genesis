# Boards of Canada Chord Theory — the fifths-and-detune nostalgia method

## Primary device: perfect-fifth dyads
Two-note chords spanning 7 semitones, no third → "neither major nor minor," ambiguous,
less emotional pull. This open ambiguity is the BoC (and a lot of drone) signature.

## Voicing strategies
- Root-position fifth = standard.
- Fourth in bass → sus2 sound (G5/C = Csus2).
- Stacked fifths (two dyads) = richer drone-chord.
- Inverted fifths for smoother voice-leading between chords.

## Structure
**Odd-length phrases** to dodge obvious loops — "Pete Standing Alone" uses 6+5+5 bars
instead of a clean 4 or 8. (Same anti-repetition instinct as Eno's incommensurable loops.)

## Synth implementation (copyable)
1. **Monophonic auto-fifth**: osc 1 at root, osc 2 tuned **+7 semitones** → one key plays
   the fifth automatically.
2. **Polyphonic fifths patch**: each played note auto-harmonised up a fifth → two keys make
   a four-note chord.

## Pitch drift / tape character
BoC recordings sit **20–50 cents flat/sharp** of concert pitch for the warm, degraded feel.

## Rig application
- Auto-fifth osc-detune works on any owned synth (Arturia, Massive X). For the user's
  banjo-as-lead drone, tune a held drone string up a fifth and let the two ring.
- The **±20–50 cent global detune** maps to: SketchCassette II / RC-20 wow-flutter, the CB
  tape pedals, or simply nudging Logic Varispeed slightly off. Reverb Machine's own
  **Chroma Campfire** preset pack productises this BoC sound for Arturia Pigments/Analog Lab
  (loadable here — even includes a patch named "Reach for the Drone").
