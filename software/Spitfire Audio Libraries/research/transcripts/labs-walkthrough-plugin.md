[Short first-impression walkthrough of the 2024 "new LABS plugin" (the LABS+ era redesign — the dark UI with the built-in in-plugin browser). Useful for understanding the plugin's browser/store layout and the free-vs-LABS+ split that preceded the 2025 Splice INSTRUMENT rebrand.]

## The new plugin (2024)
What arrived was not just another instrument but a whole new plugin. UI looks like the old LABS except it's no longer white — a dark "pop art" aesthetic.

## The in-plugin browser/store
- A little button in the corner opens a built-in browser with a whole plethora of sounds you can download from inside the plugin.
- Sounds are split into **free** (download for free now) and **LABS+** (premium subscription) content.
- Auditioning: hover over any sound and press the play button to hear a demo — no need to load it first.
- Content is **categorized by instrument type** — Brass, Choir & Vocal, Drums, Experimental, Strings, etc.
- The new interface makes it easier to know what you're loading, because some patch names ("Bell Swarm") don't tell you much on their own — now there's a preview.

## Why LABS sounds the way it does
"What I like about LABS is that their sounds are always so rich — you can hear they actually sampled real instruments most of the time, it's not just synthesized." Examples shown: Epic Choir (from original LABS), a BBC SO trumpet, Expressive Strings, Firewood Piano.

## The subscription (LABS+, 2024 pricing as quoted)
Clicking Upgrade goes to a subscription page: **$12/month** or **$99/year** for the premium (LABS+) content. His honest note: if it's too expensive, the FREE stuff already comes with enough sounds to work with — you don't need the subscription.

## Note (context, post-dates this video)
In late 2025 this plugin/ecosystem was folded into **Splice INSTRUMENT**. The standalone LABS plugin keeps working "well into 2026," existing installed packs keep loading, and free monthly drops moved to INSTRUMENT. (See research/links/labs-splice-instrument-rebrand.md.)
