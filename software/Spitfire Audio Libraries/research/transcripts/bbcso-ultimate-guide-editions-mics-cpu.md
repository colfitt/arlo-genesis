[A concert composer's year-plus review of BBC SO Pro. Best single source for the edition tier breakdown (Discover / Core / Pro), the mic-vs-mix tradeoff, disk/SSD realities, and the CC mapping. Frames BBC SO as "very natural, a little bit dark, nothing exciting" — i.e. the realistic, restrained orchestra, which is exactly the ghost-layer brief. Pro owner.]

## What it is / overall character

"A very realistic library for live instruments… there's nothing added to the instrument." Great for printing a believable mockup for real players. Caveat he flags: "BBC is a little bit dark, it's nothing exciting — but it's much more natural, very impressively natural." If you want something more "exciting/hyped," look elsewhere. (For this rig the dark/natural/un-hyped character is the *feature*.)

## Edition tiers (the clearest breakdown)

- **Discover (free):** "basically nothing — 33 instruments, ONE mixing signal (one mix), 47 techniques (long + short notes only)." Sections are grouped — no solos. No percussion. "Quite limiting, but it's free."
- **Core (~$500 CAD):** "a very big step from Discover." All the **articulations** (same set as Pro). Violins 1, Violins 2, Violas, Cello, Basses; solo woodwinds + a-sections; piccolo (only one); horns/trumpets/trombones/tuba with all articulations; **percussion (detailed), harp, tuned + non-pitched perc.** Missing: English horn, bass clarinet, contrabassoon, and — the dealbreaker for him — **no solo strings (no leaders)**. Still **one mic signal only** (no mic control).
- **Pro (~$1000 CAD):** adds **20 signals / mic positions**, the extended low instruments, the **soloists/leaders**, and more techniques. The mic control is the main reason to go Pro: lets you pull a soloist forward on a close mic with the section wider behind, instead of being "stuck with the big room mix." Educational/student discount ~50%; frequent sales (Black Friday).

## Mic / mix workflow (Pro)

You build your own custom mix from the individual mics, OR use the presets:
- **Mix 1** — "always very good… especially in a concert setting." The default balanced mix.
- **Mix 2** — a different combination of the same mics.
- Or pull all the mix faders down and **focus on the Close mic** alone for an intimate, in-front sound (vs. the wide room mix). "There's quite a big difference" between Close and the full wide mix. Even Mix 1's reverb "at its slowest is already pretty wide." (For this rig: the Close mic = the dry, controllable starting point to degrade downstream; the wide mixes bake in too much Maida Vale room.)

## The FX panel (per-instrument controls)

In the UI's FX square: **Reverb, Release, Tightness, Variation** (some don't apply to every instrument — e.g. brass/horns only expose Reverb). He **dials Reverb all the way down** ("I like them as dry as possible, especially when using many instruments") and adds reverb later on his section busses instead. (Same dry-source-then-add-your-own-space logic this rig uses.)

## CC mapping (confirmed, with a clarification)

- **CC1 = Expression**, but here Expression is just **plain volume** ("associated with the volume, not dynamics — sounds weird, but that's what it is").
- The **mod wheel** is the line he actually rides — "more control over the dynamics of your track" (the dynamic-layer crossfade). [Note: across Spitfire's own docs/clips the mod wheel = CC1 = Dynamics; this reviewer's labelling of "CC1 = expression/volume" conflicts slightly — treat the mod-wheel-drives-dynamics behaviour as canonical.]

## Disk / CPU / streaming

- Pro is **~600+ GB** ("pretty crazy… had to buy a new hard drive just for this; it needs to be SSD so instruments load faster"). The size comes from every mic position being stored individually — "it multiplies the size."
- Round robins ("the Robins") are "very very good"; the **legato sound is amazing**; multiple dynamic layers.
- Settings expose **maximum voice / preload size / stream buffer size** ("don't need to touch unless your computer is having a hard time") and master tuning.
