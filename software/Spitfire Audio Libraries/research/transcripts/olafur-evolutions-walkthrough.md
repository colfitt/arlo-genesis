Spitfire Audio (Paul Thompson) · "Spitfire Walkthrough - Olafur Arnalds Evolutions" · 2015-11-23 (27:07)

The official walkthrough for the FIRST Ólafur Arnalds Evolutions library (the original quartet, NOT confirmed owned — installed library is Cells). The most thorough Evo Grid tutorial: the 16 categories, every preset, mic positions, individual instruments, the warped section, and the per-note "re-orchestration" workflow.

---

Spitfire's first collaboration with Ólafur Arnalds (BAFTA-winning Broadchurch score). The exciting thing about the collaboration: designing an Evo Grid that has the essence of his string sound. Made with two of his regular collaborators, Viktor and Sebastian. A unique take on the Evo style. A globe-trotting enterprise — Iceland, Berlin, Finland.

**16 categories**, separated into **subtle / thrills / episodic / dissonance**.

The individual evolutions, split up:
- **Subtle**: sul tasto long; long wave; multi-waves; sul tasto / sul tasto sul pont trem; sul tasto trem.
- **Thrills**: trem var-speed medium-to-fast; trills; hectic octave trills; sul pont trem.
- **Episodic** ones.
- **Dissonance**: slow pitch-bend; pitching episodic; wow effect; sul pont octave harmonics.

**The interface.** Main Evo Grid; **curated presets**; and the **individual evolutions** (per-instrument — each instrument recorded separately) and the standalone individual instruments. Main mic section: the mix; close mics; **room valve mic** (not a distant room — more "players with air," like a tree); and a **ribbon** sound (slightly more distant, softer top). The **warp section** has the stretched ambient versions.

**Controls.** Usual Evo Grid effects controls; ADSR; the V/Dynamics (variation / expression). You drive the **Dynamics** to shape the swell.

**Walking the subtle evolutions** (individual, then combined):
- #1 sul tasto — beautiful, gentle, atmospheric.
- #2 long wave.
- #3 multi-waves — very inspiring.
- #4 sul tasto or.
- #5 sul tasto sul pont — wonderful, evocative.

**Randomize within subtle.** Each roll of the dice up top gives a different combination of pegs. If one peg doesn't fit your track, it's easy to move it around — refer back to the list of individual evolutions, pick what you want ("tweak it to your taste"), or just keep rolling the dice until you get something you love.

**Thrills** — #6 sul tasto trem thrill; #7 sul tasto trem var-speed (great); #8 medium-to-fast trem; #9 hectic octave trills.

**Mic comparison (mid-walkthrough).** The mix → close mics (immediacy) → valve sound (more air, the feel of the room) → ribbon (softening of the top, more ambience). Mix and match within the three mics, or use the standard default mix.

**Episodic** — #10 vibrato episodic (expands textures Ólafur designed for Broadchurch); #11 sul pont episodic; #12 trem episodic.

**Dissonance** — #13 slow pitch-bend; #14 pitching episodic ("just dissonant enough"); #15 wow effect; #16 sul pont octave harmonics.

**Multi-category randomize.** Roll a couple of thrills, then randomize episodic, then randomize dissonance — you get a ton of usable, inspirational material. The keyboard keys are color-coded so you can see where the different styles of evolution are happening as you play. (E.g. a little cluster of dissonance at the top of the keyboard, thrills lower down.)

**Curated presets** — "blue episodic," "ebb and flow," "iced trems" — load to leap straight in.

**Individual instruments.** Presented in the BML codebase. No grid for these — just the individual evolution across the instrument range. Usual BML controls plus a **Reverb control** — a **modeled vintage plate** (the same model that's in the Evo Grid). You can go from really dry/delicate to fully wet "vintage plate" sound. The Ensemble patch covers the full keyboard range — achingly beautiful. (Demo: a cello with the reverb right down and then stereo ribbons added.) Per-instrument grids also exist — e.g. the cello with all its evolutions on the pegboard across the cello's range; randomize within subtle for beautiful textures.

**Warped section.** Familiar from previous Evo Grids: the **ambient stretch** sound and the **Tri XX super-ambient** sound. Randomize with "any." Lovely warped textures with full dynamic control.

**The signature workflow.** "What I love doing with the Evo Grid: I'll write a cue, get something together, then sit playing around with the pegs and just see what happens if I change just one. It's fabulous to get one note you want to add extra significance to — you just dial into where it is on the grid (maybe a B3 or the C next to it) and change that one peg. It's almost like *re-orchestrating* each time you change one of those pegs."
