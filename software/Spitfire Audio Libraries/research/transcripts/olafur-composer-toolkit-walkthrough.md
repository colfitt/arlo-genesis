The official walkthrough for Ólafur Arnalds Composer Toolkit (NOT confirmed owned — installed library is Cells). The centerpiece is his felt grand piano; also organic warps, synth embellishments, tempo-locked sounds, and Sonic Warps. Strong window into Ólafur's outboard-pedal / "warped" aesthetic and how it's productized.

---

The Composer Toolkit is a collection of sounds and textures — the kinds of things Ólafur likes to make his music with.

**The felt grand piano (centerpiece).** Different from Spitfire's earlier practice-felt pianos — this is a felt *grand*, miked and produced by Ólafur in his own specific way. You get the characteristics from his music — you can clearly hear the pedal moving, and all of that is controllable. Set up to be as easy to use as possible, but you can dive in and control everything individually.

- The mixed patch: very beautiful, dynamic but gentle and soft, with real sound character.
- Two things are heard at once: (1) the **mix of the two mics**, and (2) an **ambience element** on its own — a verby sound in its own right, but blended in it's lovely.
- The two mics individually: the **KM84** (tight, crisp), and the **Coles ribbon** (tons of character). In the individual section you get the Coles ribbons, the KM84 on its own, and the mix-blend on its own.
- Front-panel controls: easy-tweak layout, with the spanner for detailed controls. **Auto-pedal volume** can be disabled to manually control sustain-pedal noise. **Pedal Dynamics** is by default controlled by key velocity; **Pedal Volume** is the actual level of that pedal noise. Disable the auto-link on the spanner page and control it manually. There's a **Reverb** — go from the basic sound all the way to a real distant sound. Velocity can also be driven from CC1 (mod wheel).

**Composer toolkit folder** — three sections: organic warps, synth embellishments, and tempo-(long-)locked.

**Organic warps** (Mercury engine — core controls to alter the sound). All warped with various outboard pedals:
- "Evo ambience" — lovely, inspirational.
- "Ólafur's chamber" — two signals you can balance to taste; add the delay.
- "Plate space piano."
- "Prime crusher" — a tiny bit of amp distortion in there.
- Command-click any section to edit its parameters.

**Synth embellishments** ("my second favorite after the piano — really wonderful synth programming"). A varied bag — "Basic 60," "Self Entrance" (a favorite), a useful sub that goes right down. These are designed to slot into pieces — sit behind things nicely or double up parts underneath, like the felt grand's background ambient/sound-design layer.

**Tempo-locked sounds.** Automatically pick up the sequencer tempo. Examples: "a bit more resonant," "time machine," "time to delay stab." Useful rhythmic things locked to your project tempo.

**Sonic Warps.** Takes all the organic material and re-warps it again through outboard + in-the-box techniques. Key point Paul makes: these are *not* overproduced — they don't suffer from "keyboard workstation preset syndrome" (sounds incredible on load but won't fit a track because too much is going on). Instead they're useful colors with character that fit into productions easily.

**Mercury synth engine.** Command-click to get into the controls; click-and-move gives visual feedback of the level. Lots of room to alter these sounds, make them your own, and do your own sound design.
