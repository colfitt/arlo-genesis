[Official short walking the BBC SO UI — uses the free Discover edition but the layout is identical across editions. Covers: the orchestral-layout preset picker (recorded in-situ panning), the preset browser/star/load flow, the three bottom-left controllers (Expression / Dynamics / Reverb), the techniques panel on the right, key switches and how to relocate them. ## Selecting instruments — the orchestral-layout screen

By default it loads to Violins 1. To change instrument, click another area of the **orchestral-layout screen** in the middle. The layout is arranged "as we recorded it" — players are placed in-situ, in the positions they sat on the soundstage. E.g. trumpets were recorded slightly to the right, horns on the opposite side, "so you'll hear the sound slightly towards that side" — a true spatial representation of each instrument in that space. (Implication for this rig: the panning is baked from the real seating; recenter via the plugin's pan control if you want a mono-collapsed ghost.)

You can also change presets from the **top bar**: preview by pressing the play buttons on the left; **star** items to collect them in a separate view; **double-click** (or click once then **Load** at the bottom) to load.

## The three bottom-left controllers

1. **Expression** — "adjusts the level of the technique" (a level/volume control for the current articulation).
2. **Dynamics** — "the most important fader on the interface as it crossfades between different dynamic layers, from very soft to the louder layers." This is the soft↔loud morph.
3. **Reverb** — click and dial in to taste.

## Techniques panel (right side)

Available techniques depend on the loaded preset. Piccolo example: **Long** (sustain) and **Staccatissimo**. Click a technique to switch. Violins 1 shown with four mic/signal "pieces" at the bottom and more techniques on the right.

## Key switches

Techniques can also be changed via **key switches**, which by default sit at the very bottom of the keyboard (out of the visible range — extend the keyboard view to see them). You can **relocate** the key switches by clicking the keyboard-icon button up top and dragging right, which moves them up the keyboard. Some presets (e.g. Violins 1) have many techniques and therefore more key switches than others (e.g. piccolo).

## Other

A collapse icon shrinks the interface. Additional settings live at the top; for those, "worth checking out the user manual."
