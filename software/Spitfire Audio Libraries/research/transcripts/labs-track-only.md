## Setup
LABS is a free Spitfire library with soft piano, strings, a drum set, keys, guitars, experimental sounds, etc. The challenge: all sounds come ONLY from LABS; other plugins (delays, reverbs, EQ) are allowed for processing. Target vibe: cinematic instrumental, sync-licensing-friendly. In Logic: create a Software Instrument track → Spitfire Audio → LABS. (Note: BBC Symphony Orchestra Discover is also free.)

**Workflow trick — open many instances at once:** he opens ~10 instances of LABS up front so he can audition and layer fast without stopping to instantiate each time.

## The plugin controls (as he describes them)
The interface is the same for every LABS instrument and is "really straightforward":
- A **reverb control** (knob).
- A **volume/expression** control.
- The **mod wheel** drives the expression — moving it "makes it bigger" (crossfades into louder/fuller dynamic layers). On Soft Piano you hear it move from soft to fuller.
- A dropdown picks the instrument/preset. Categories: Piano, Drums, Keys, Synths, Guitars, Strings, Experimental.

## The build (layering order)
1. **Soft Piano** as the foundation — chord progression A minor / F major 7 / C major / E minor 7. Starts the figure on bar 2 to "lead things up." Colors the track yellow (Logic: Shift+Option+C colors tracks). Duplicates the part rather than looping (personal taste). 4 bars of just the progression to open.
2. **Electric piano (Keys)** for an arpeggio with reverb — adds motion.
3. **Strings** — "Sul tasto" articulation (player bows close to the fretboard) for a soft, airy tone; also a **harmonic violin**. Likes the **scratchiness** of the more aggressive string articulation — "now we're talking."
4. **Cello** ("Super Chisel / sul tasto" style) at the beginning; mutes the harmonic violin where it doesn't fit.
5. Keeps adding **layers** — "gotta get more layers, more layers"; short strings (violin/viola duo feel), a **granular piano** ("doesn't even sound like a piano") to grow into the section.
6. **Choir** added later for lift (no brass/horns in LABS at the time — he checks and there are none).
7. **Synth pads** for extra color/texture, used at the beginning to "add a little more color."
8. **Dulcimers** — "I forgot how much I love the dulcimers on here."

## The arrangement principle — "it's gotta go somewhere"
His repeated mantra: a static loop "needs to go somewhere." The whole method is building tension by ADDING and SUBTRACTING layers and automating, so the piece evolves rather than loops.

## Key processing moves (the "growing in" technique)
- **Automate EQ to grow layers in:** "I'm going to grow that in, we're gonna EQ it." He automates an EQ to **take out all the highs** at the start of a layer and open them up over time — so a part fades/blooms in by un-muffling rather than just a volume fade. This is the core trick for making LABS layers "arrive."
- Wants certain parts to "sound kind of muted" — dark/lo-fi voicing via EQ.
- **Output "Movement" plugin** on the piano for rhythmic motion/modulation ("if you're familiar with Movement by Output, it's dope") — throws the piano through it to generate evolving rhythmic movement.
- Rhythm choices: prefers **dotted eighths** over triplets for an arp delay-style feel.

## Takeaway for this rig
The method is pure layering-and-evolution: stack a handful of LABS patches (piano + multiple string articulations + cello + granular piano + choir + synth pad), keep none of them static, and use automated EQ (highs out → highs in) plus a motion plugin to make the wall breathe and build. Swap his "cinematic sync" target for "drone/shoegaze bed" and the workflow is identical — and everything routes out to your Valhalla/EchoBoy/Decapitator chain instead of his stock FX.
