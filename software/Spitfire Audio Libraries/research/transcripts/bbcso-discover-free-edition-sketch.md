## What Discover is, and the "sketch" framing

The free edition. He mixed the demo "with a few of the LABS sounds for some extra texture." Pitch: even if you own detailed orchestral libraries, Discover is worth having because it's free → everyone has it → trivial **collaboration** (no need to match sample libraries with another composer), and sketches translate later to a fuller library by swapping sounds/articulations. "If you're not doing an orchestral composition, this can totally be used just as a really good string library for pop or dance tracks." (← endorses the off-label "string-texture-in-a-non-orchestral-track" use this rig wants.)

## Footprint — extremely light

A full template with every instrument loaded = **2.24 GB RAM** ("by modern standards, minuscule — a fraction of one patch in some libraries"). Download ~**200 MB**. Loads fast. Instances are aware of each other and show a combined memory readout.

## Discover's limits (stated plainly)

- **Single dynamics layer** — so the Dynamics control acts "more as a volume control"; not the realistic soft→loud morph of Core/Pro. "More than enough" at this price.
- **Limited articulations** per instrument — "the basics." Strings: he notes **no legato patches** ("to be expected for free"). Mostly Long + a short/staccatissimo per instrument.
- **No mic positions** — "there's some reverb inherent in the samples (recorded in a hall), but no mic choice." A Reverb send dial is the only space control (no settings, just send amount).
- **NOT multitimbral** — "you can't load multiple instruments within one instance; load a new instance per track." (Contrast: Core/Pro support the one-instance multitimbral trick.)
- Per-instance: refresh, voice readout, set MIDI channel, tuning, pan, volume, velocity curve (exponential±, linear, shelf, compressed, full-range), voice/preload/buffer settings, resizable UI, a **lock** to prevent accidental changes.

## CC mapping (confirmed)

- **Dynamics** → mod wheel by default.
- **Expression** → **CC11** by default.
- Both remappable: right-click → learn CC automation, move your control. (He remapped Expression to CC14.)

## Instrument layout (Discover contents)

Orchestral-layout picker mirrors a real seating plan. Strings: Violins 1, Violins 2, Violas, Cellos, Basses (the keyboard's playable range shifts per instrument). Back row woodwinds: piccolo, flutes, oboes, clarinets, bassoons. Brass: horns (far left), trumpets→tuba (right, lower register). Percussion (back): tuned perc + harp + celeste + tuned timpani hits; untuned (cymbals, snare, etc.); a second tuned-perc group (glockenspiel, xylophone, marimba, tubular bells). All **grouped sections — no solo instruments** in Discover.

## His preferred workflow (Cubase, but transferable)

He avoids key switches; instead loads **a separate instance per articulation**, foldered by section (Strings/Brass/Woodwind/Perc), plus **LABS folders** at the bottom for "extra textures, esoteric weirdness, quite nice to mix with the traditional orchestral samples." Selecting two channels at once lets him play e.g. Violins-1 + Violins-2 (or violas + cellos) together as an ad-hoc ensemble across the keyboard.
