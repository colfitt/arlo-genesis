## The premise

Not orchestration-style layering (flute + violin); this is **sample-orchestration** — combining different articulations of the *same* instrument to create more variety/control, essentially building "quasi-new articulations."

## Technique 1 — Attack + sustain = a new articulation

A sustained French horn has a soft attack. To get a more aggressive start, layer a **Marcato** (or, for shorter/sharper, **Staccatissimo**) sample on the front, going into the sustain. With the mod wheel drawn for dynamic movement in the sustain, you get "sustain with a marcato attack" — a new articulation. He frequently **bounces both to audio** for more control: once they're audio you can see exactly how they line up, put a fade on the short, and **crossfade** into the sustain, controlling the relative volume.

## Technique 2 — Adding edge to legato runs

A cello **legato** is smooth/lush, but for more edge per note, **blend Shorts** under the legato. Alternatively, **tremolando** is frequently used in runs — "it has a certain blurry effect that works really well." Blending these gives a much more controlled sound because you're choosing the bowing/style rather than relying on the straight legato. The BBC SO **runs** are "really really good" but may not always be the sound you want — blend to taste.

## Technique 3 — Leader vs. section (Pro only)

On Violins 1 (Pro), add the **violin leader** (the solo "leader" player) against the 16-violin section and balance the two. You get the swooshy lyrical section sound vs. the individual edge — "occasionally you'll just hear one instrument come to the fore" in a live section. Works best of all with **shorts**. (This requires the Pro leaders — Core/Discover don't have them.)

## The recurring meta-tip — bounce to audio

"I bounce a lot of things to audio — gives me more control, I can manipulate it more." When layering/blending you need the layers to sound like one instrument/section, which means very fine level adjustments; sometimes MIDI blending works, other times you "need to bounce it to audio to get that additional layer of control." (For this rig: bounce the layered sul-tasto/flautando bed to audio, then degrade — same offline-wall workflow used with Cells/LABS.)
