## The two-control performance model (the whole point of the plugin)

There are essentially only two controls you use to play it:
- **Dynamics** — preset to the **mod wheel (CC1)**. A very smooth crossfade between recorded dynamic layers (soft→loud). Can be shown as a slider or controlled from the UI.
- **Vibrato** — a smooth crossfade for vibrato amount, also resettable / bring-up-able on the UI.

"Simply controlling those two things — the dynamics and the vibrato control — is incredibly easy to get a very musical performance out of the sound." Legato is monophonic; for polyphonic playing you select the **Longs** instead.

## Strings — the textural articulation list (what serves the ghost-layer aesthetic)

Walking the first violins, then the same set repeats across Violins 2, Violas, Celli, Basses (each is a different section, different players, but they blend):

- **Long** (sustain) — the polyphonic workhorse.
- **Con Sordino / muted Long** — "a muted version of that long."
- **Flautando** — "our characteristic flautando sound, a very very light gentle sound." (← key ethereal patch.)
- **Shorts** — a great selection.

Second articulation page = "the more esoteric sounds":
- **Tremolo** (major and minor trills).
- **Sul Tasto** — "this gives us the sound of the strings playing over the fingerboard, and it gives you a very warm and very quiet sound." (← the #1 ghost-string patch.)
- **Harmonics** (long) + a **short version of harmonics**.
- **Bartók snap pizzicato** (strong).
- An alternative long with a strong Bartók/Marcato attack.
- **Tremolo sul ponticello** (on the bridge) — "really crisp and quite nasal."
- **Tremolo con sordino** (muted tremolo).
- **Long sul ponticello** (on the bridge).
- **Short pizzicato** — "very very tight, short off the bow, with the mutes on."

Per-section colour notes that matter for the dark/soft aesthetic:
- **Violas:** legato is "a beautiful, more mellow, almost more throaty sound."
- **Cellos:** "incredibly passionate, beautifully cohesive but very agile." Longs + muted version; harmonics + super-strong Bartók at the bottom.
- **Basses:** "the bass long flautando sound is particularly beautiful and haunting." Bass pizzicato; strongest col legno (wood of the bow on the string) and strongest Bartók pizzicato of all sections.

## Brass — quiet/choral drone options

Solo + section patches: solo + 4 horns, solo + 3 trumpets, tenor trombone + 3 tenor trombones, 2 bass trombones, tuba.
- Solo horn: "build really fabulous-sounding chords using this single horn, lovely quiet choral stuff." (← soft brass beds.)
- 3 tenor trombones played as a 3-part chord = effectively 9 trombones "but playing very quietly… that beautiful rich sound." Bass trombones "even sound great with this very quiet choral sound, really down low."
- **Cuivré** ("queer a") — very brassy/nasal; note the tight embouchure actually *reduces physical volume* vs. a full open fortissimo. Different from **Sforzando** (strong push then drops back).
- **Multitongue** sections with a **Variation** control (number of tongues: double / triple / quad as you push Variation up; and how the note ends — short when soft, accented when harder).
- **Flutter**, **Trills**, **Staccatissimo**, **Marcato**.

## Woodwind — soft/breathy options

Solo + a3 (three-player) for flute, oboe, clarinet, bassoon; piccolo on top.
- Flute: agile; **Longs**, **Staccatissimo**, **Tenuto** (gently accented longer short), **Marcato**, **Flutter** ("works particularly beautifully on the flute"), **Multitongue**.
- Piccolo: **Rips and Falls** (with a Variation that gives a longer articulation).
- Bassoon: "very expressive," loves **staccato** and **flutter**; bassoons-a3 = "lovely chorus-like richness" down in the bass.

## Percussion (Core)

Tuned + untuned: vibraphone; timpani (mod-controllable rolls, soft mallet heads + rolls, hot-rod hits, two damped types); harp (incl. **bisbigliando** tremolo + glissandi via Direction + Variation controls); marimba, celeste (with damped/longer-ring versions), xylophone, glockenspiel, tubular bells (+ damped/rolled). Untuned: anvil, second bass drum, cymbals, "toys" (castanets, woodblock, cowbells, guiros), triangle. Hover the mouse over a key to see which articulation that key plays.

## Edition split stated at the end

- **Core = 44 of the 56 Pro instruments + a single mix position.** You are "missing a few of the lower instruments — bass clarinets, contrabassoons, those extended low-end sections — those are in Pro."
- **Pro adds:** all the microphone positions (the extra control), the few extended instruments, and the **leaders of the string sections** (solo "leader" players).
- **Buying Core also gets you Discover free.** Discover = "super super light edition for a laptop… do great sketches with it, still sounds great."
