# Spitfire Resonate — Computer Music review

**Verdict.** "If you need a great resource for interesting and otherworldly sounds, Resonate will provide you with a hearty resource." Price £199.

**What it is.** Built with Dame **Evelyn Glennie**. NOT a normal percussion library — it focuses on the **reverberations and resonances that FOLLOW the strike**, not the hit itself. Four unconventional sources used as **resonators**:
- **Barrel** (scraped with implements)
- **Timpani** (played non-traditionally)
- **Water Tank** (struck and resonated)
- **Thundersheet** (dramatic textures)

Emphasis on "long and unwinding sonic identity" with harmonic overtones sustained after the initial strike.

**Specs.** 45 articulations across the four instruments. **7 mic perspectives including contact mics and UNDERWATER placements.** 32,229 audio files; 23.05 GB compressed.

**Real-time controls.** Reverb, compression, filtering, and a **"Stretch" effect for timing manipulation** — all manipulable live for evolving sounds.

**Warp / after-effects.** 5 individual FX chains add character per sample, ranging from "gritty, textural, yet organic" to "endlessly resonating reverb." Six after-effects total.

**Evo Grid.** Present — "lays samples across the keyboard, in a sometimes randomised way," enabling experimental sonic combinations.

**Target use.** Explicitly for **media composers, film scoring, post-production / sound design** — "if you want a percussion library, this is NOT what you need." Runs in **Spitfire's dedicated plugin player** (not Kontakt). Price elsewhere listed $249.
