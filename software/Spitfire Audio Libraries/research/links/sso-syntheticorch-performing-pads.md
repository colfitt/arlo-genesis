# Performing SSO strings as pads — soft releases, swell attacks, the relevant CCs

Concrete playing technique for held/atmospheric writing (this tutorial is for the older SSO
patch architecture; CC numbers may differ on the relaunched/Curated patches — verify on disk).

**Soft releases for pad/chord work:** "**Soft releases are great for playing chord progressions
or 'pad'-style phrases.**" Toggle the **Release slider** (Advanced Configuration) between abrupt
and smooth decay — mapped to **CC17** by default. Smooth/long release = chords bleed into each
other = a continuous bed rather than separated notes.

**Swell attacks (built-in crescendo):** default sustains have "**a crescendo attack**" that
"build[s] slowly, ~a second to reach full dynamic, giving you a **swell**." For smoother pads,
**avoid high-velocity triggers** (they fire the hard attack) — play soft so every note swells in
under the hall. This is the SSO equivalent of riding dynamics low for the ghost timbre.

**Legato speed:** **Speed slider = CC16** — automate per-phrase ("full speed for some intervals,
pull all the way down for others") so slow drifting lines don't sound mechanical.

**Rig application:** soft velocity + long release (CC17 smooth) + the auto-swell attack =
overlapping, never-re-attacking held chords that the Lyndhurst hall fuses into a wall. Stagger
section/note entrances so swells never line up → motion without a new transient (the same
no-re-attack evolution trick used on BBC SO, but here the hall does the blending for free).
