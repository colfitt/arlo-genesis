## Recording sources
- **Strings**: SinfoniaNord recorded all string performances at the **Hof Cultural Center in Akureyri, Iceland** (the ensemble specializes in film/TV/music orchestral recordings). Cells bridges "the organic fragility of the SinfoniaNord orchestra and the grit of Ólafur's vintage Korg and Juno synthesisers."
- **Synths**: vintage Korg and Juno analogue synths, signal paths curated by Ólafur.
- Download size: **29.79 GB**. Runs in the **Spitfire Audio app + dedicated plugin** (AU, VST2, VST3, AAX) — *proprietary player, NOT Kontakt*. Uses **eDNA** interface technology. **200+ presets.**

## Content list (preset/instrument families)
**Strings / Full Band** — Cells Motion Clouds; Scatter Cirrus Intensity; Octaves Living Cells (Sparse/Medium); Shimmer variations (Sparse/Medium); Parallel Motion Shimmer; Full Band combinations with interval options (unison, 2nds…7ths); glissando and two-note variations.

**Synth tools** — three dedicated Cells synth modules (Cells 1/2/3) with interval-based variations; a vintage analogue synth toolkit created and performed by Ólafur; Bass instruments (Long Straight, Long Sparse/Medium unison); Synth Bass (Sub 60 II, Sub Stab, Short Stab, Short Sub 20); One Shots (Back Stab, Swell 60, Ratatattat variations, Single Hits).

**Sound design** — Mallet Pads (Swarm Moving, Attach Organ, Revenv II, Soft Organ, Organic/Inorganic Moves, Ps Waves); Organic Warps (Air Ensemble, Granular Strings, Scattered Bows, String Abstraction/Waves, Submerged/Synth Strings); Sonic Warps (Bass Drone/Dist, Ps Pad Filtered, Detuned, Old Tape variations).

## The Cell Generator — modes
- **Scale Mode** — provides harmonic movement aligned with the composer's chosen tonality; introduces unexpected harmonic layers by adding notes that fit the key.
- **Played Mode** — detects the user's key in real time and uses a degree of randomness to introduce unexpected-yet-complementary harmonies; controls which intervals the engine offers.

"Cells creates a space where unpredictability, decision making and harmony converge… frees composers from the daunting prospect of the blank canvas."

## Ólafur's quote — the capture workflow (KEY)
> "[Cells] constantly surprises me with little melodies that get created within the random intervals, often inspiring new melodies that I then take over to other instruments."

→ i.e. the intended workflow is *capture the surprise, then re-voice it elsewhere*: record the MIDI/audio that Cells generates, lift the melodic fragments you like, and play them on another instrument (banjo, baritone, felt-piano sample). Because output is deterministic per played note (per the Soundbed walkthrough), a recorded MIDI take plays back identically.
