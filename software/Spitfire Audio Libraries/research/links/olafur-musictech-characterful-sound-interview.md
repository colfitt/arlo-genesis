MusicTech · interview: "Ólafur Arnalds on making characterful sounds, fusing piano and synths and collaborating with Bonobo"

The core source for Ólafur's actual recording/processing aesthetic — directly copyable technique.

## Sound-design philosophy
Composition and production are inseparable: "Why play this piano instead of that piano? Then zoom in further: why this mic and not that mic?" He treats "all sound design as much as melodies," whether classical or electronic.

## Felt piano & sampling
He approached Spitfire because he "couldn't find a sampled piano that I really liked" and wanted to capture his rare felt grand. That collaboration became Stratus. He sees the libraries not as proprietary but as a tool to "keep me evolving and looking for new things."

## String recording — tone at the source (not EQ)
- **Sul tasto** (bow toward the neck) for "a hollow and more harmonic-sounding tone."
- Lower instrument registers (G-strings for violins, C-strings for violas) → reduces "frequencies you will have to unavoidably carve out in the mix."
- Mics: **Neumann KM84** on strings; original **AKG C12** as main string mics. (Matches the Cells/felt-piano mic story: KM84 = tight/crisp, ribbon = soft top.)

## Synth & outboard chain (copyable)
- **Primary synth: Korg PS-3100** — used for "pretty much" all characteristic synth lines on *Some Kind of Peace*.
- **Secondary: Roland Juno** — select leads and sub-bass.
- **Roland (Space) Tape Echo** for vitality.
- **Studer console** for hands-on feedback routing between effects (Space Echo, reverb).
- **Tape machines** "mostly for effects," and on solo-piano recordings "to add a little instability."
- "Touching buttons with your hand" vs a mouse creates "a very different process" cognitively.

## The Stratus → synth move (KEY rig idea)
He applies his **Stratus piano software to the Korg PS-3100** to generate patterns, "this texture that turned out to be very unique." → Generative MIDI doesn't have to drive a piano; route it to a synth/pad for evolving texture.

## DAW
- **Pro Tools** for recording/engineering; **Ableton Live** for creative sound manipulation.
- No soft-synths; editing is in-the-box, but creative sound-shaping stays on hardware where possible.

## Space
"I'm addicted to spaces… I can't write just anywhere." He positions gear to require *movement* rather than keeping everything within reach — too much control "made me too closed off."
