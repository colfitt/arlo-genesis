# Notable users & the Spitfire ambient aesthetic — sourced (certainty flagged)

## Confirmed / well-sourced
- **Ólafur Arnalds** — direct, named **collaborator**: Spitfire built Cells, (Chamber) Evolutions, Composer Toolkit, Stratus and a free LABS instrument *with* him at his Reykjavik studio. His whole signature — "reimagining strings and pianos with innovative effects, loops, and beats… ambient to electronic" — IS the textural-Spitfire sound. **High certainty** (Spitfire's own artist pages). The user OWNS Cells = literally Arnalds' generative-strings engine.
- **Hans Zimmer / Bleeding Fingers Music** — co-created **Fractured Strings** (and it featured in BBC's *Frozen Planet II*). **High certainty** (vendor + SOS review).
- **Dame Evelyn Glennie** — co-created **Resonate**. **High certainty** (vendor).
- **Ben Foskett** — contemporary concert composer who orchestrated the **48 Evos** in Symphonic Strings Evolutions. **High certainty** (vendor).

## The aesthetic lineage (general, not a per-library citation)
Spitfire's textural/evolving range sits squarely in the **modern-classical / ambient-drone** world associated with **Max Richter, Nils Frahm, Hildur Guðnadóttir, Ben Frost, Jóhann Jóhannsson** — the "Icelandic/Bedroom Community" and post-classical scene. Equipboard lists Spitfire-adjacent tools for several of these composers. **Medium certainty** as a *scene* association; **low certainty** that any specific one uses a specific *owned* library — treat as aesthetic context, not a citation.

No hard source found tying classic drone/shoegaze acts (Stars of the Lid, Hammock, Grouper, etc.) to these *specific* Spitfire libraries.
