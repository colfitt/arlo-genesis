# LABS technique — the concrete how-to bits

## Hidden parameters behind the reverb dial
The circular reverb dial is more than reverb. **Click the large button in the middle of the circular dial** to reveal other parameters baked into the sampler instrument — commonly **Attack and/or Release**, and on textural patches **Variation / Decay / Sustain**. This is the "hidden page" most people miss:
- Crank **Release** for long trails (the held-drone "let go and it keeps ringing" effect).
- Long **Attack** so notes bloom/breathe in instead of stabbing.
- **Variation** dial controls intensity/randomness of the texture.
(Confirmed in the drones-pads transcript: Arctic Swells exposes Release/Sustain/Decay/Attack/Variation this way.)

## Dynamics control = brightness on some patches
The Dynamics slider (mod wheel by default) normally crossfades velocity layers, but **on some instruments it adopts a different function — e.g. cutoff/brightness**. So riding the mod wheel can open/close a filter rather than (or as well as) swelling dynamics. Check per patch; either way it's your primary "make it move" control on a sustained note.

## Reverb is subtle by design
Even at 100%, the built-in reverb is subtle/unintrusive (true of BBC SO Discover too). It's for gentle space — for the big drone-wall tails you still send to Valhalla/Supermassive after LABS.

## Layering strategy (multi-instance)
The author's real workflow: run **multiple instances of the plugin — one track per section / per texture — each with its own bespoke line, then group/buss them into sub-mixes.** That's the same multi-instance approach the "track-only" composer uses (10 instances open at once) and the "drones-pads" composer uses (3 stacked LABS instruments split by keyrange). The pattern across every good LABS tutorial: **one patch per layer, never one patch doing everything.**

## Building evolving textures
Sustained chords on textural patches (e.g. "Wind and Birdsong") evolve on their own; nudge the Variation dial for intensity. Combine with: long attack/release, mod-wheel rides, and slightly different settings on two copies of the same patch so they drift in and out of phase (the "orbit" trick from labs-drones-pads.md).

## Ambient-roundup verdict
LABS is repeatedly listed among the best free plugins for ambient/drone music — "the quality is astounding for the price (free)." Iconic picks for ambient: Soft Piano, Ambient Guitars, Cinematic Pads, the string + vocal textures.
