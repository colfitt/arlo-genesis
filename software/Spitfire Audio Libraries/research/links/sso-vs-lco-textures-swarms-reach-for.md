# SSO vs the Spitfire designed-texture orchestras (reach-for differentiation)

Clarifies how SSO differs from Spitfire's OWN ambient orchestral tools — and corrects a naming
trap on the owner's shelf.

**LCO Textures** (Spitfire's flagship orchestral-texture lib — NOT owned, but the clearest
"designed" pole): Evo-Grid engine; "long instrument performances that **slowly change and
evolve over time until they loop**"; four ensembles (Ethereal/Mercurial/Quantum/Astral);
recorded in a **hangar with a 10-second tail**; players "introducing vibrating objects,
quarter-tones." "Beautifully three-dimensional… **intimate and vast at the same time.**"
"Stretched" mic path = samples at **2× length** for "ethereal, pad-like effects." Designed
specifically for texture, NOT realistic playback.

**Spitfire Swarms** (vendor) — pointillist *ensemble* engine: 9 harps / 9 marimbas / 18
mandolins-charangos-ukuleles; "**discrete short notes coalesce in the ear to give the
impression of longer continuous tones**" → "haunting tremolos, epic glissandi"; warm tape
character; recorded at Lyndhurst (4 mics). NOTE: this is a *plucked/struck* swarm, distinct
from the owner's **"Orchestral Swarm" (= Spitfire Swarm STRINGS / human micro-detune string
divisi shimmer)** per the Navigator — don't conflate the two Swarm products.

**The core distinction, stated plainly:**
- **SSO = realistic, comprehensive orchestra** — you write notes/chords; realism is the goal;
  evolution is something YOU create (soft attacks, CC/dynamic drift, staggered entrances,
  bounce-to-wall). No built-in evolve/loop engine.
- **LCO Textures / Swarms / SSEvo / Cells / Fractured = DESIGNED texture engines** — the
  evolution/shimmer/loop is built in; they're "alive" out of the box and don't aim to sound
  like a literal orchestra.

**Reach-for heuristic:** want a believable REAL orchestra ghost (and love the wet Lyndhurst
hall as the space) → **SSO**. Want an alive evolving bed that needn't read as an orchestra →
the designed engines (owner already owns the relevant ones). SSO is the only one of these that
sounds like an *actual symphony* — that's both its reason to exist on the shelf and its
overkill warning.
