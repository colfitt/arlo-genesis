Spitfire Audio (Composer mag) · "Ólafur Arnalds: The Meaning of Stratus"

Development history + design philosophy of Stratus, from Spitfire's own publication.

## Development
- Built with collaborator/programmer **Halldór Eldjárn**. "A full two years on and off developing for Stratus." First functioning version appeared on the 2016 track **'Doria'** (from *Island Songs*).
- Experimental process: "We would have ideas like, 'I want the pianos to be a form of a physical delay effect with variable feedback' and then while trying to achieve that we would accidentally happen upon something else."
- The installation version connected two self-playing pianos to a server/website with a democratic voting system so people worldwide could play together.

## Why
- "My way-over-the-top way of getting over a writer's block I had been suffering from." Aim: "bring together creativity and technology, the composer and the listener."

## Philosophy (the design rule for the whole Cells/Evolutions family)
> "I think of technology as a tool… First and foremost all extra technology you bring into the creative process needs to serve a purpose, spark ideas and have a function."

→ Useful framing for the rig: the generative engine is a *idea-sparker / writer's-block tool*, not the finished part. Capture what it gives you, then commit.
