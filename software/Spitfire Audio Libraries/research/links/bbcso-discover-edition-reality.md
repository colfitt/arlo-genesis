# BBC SO Discover (free edition) — EXACT reality vs Core/Pro

Owner's installed BBC SO edition is **unconfirmed** (drive offline). This is the
free-tier baseline so you know the *floor* of what you have if it turns out to be
Discover.

## Discover — hard specs (Spitfire product page + manual triangulation)
- **34 instruments** (incl. a Steinway Model D concert grand). Sections: Vln 1, Vln 2,
  Violas, Celli, Basses; Piccolo, Flutes, Oboes, Clarinets, Bassoons; Horns, Trumpets,
  Tenor + Bass Trombones, Tuba; piano.
- **47 techniques total** across the whole library — but **only ~2 core artics per
  instrument**: a **Long (sustain, vib)** + **shorts** (Spiccato / Staccato), plus
  **Pizz** and **Trem** on strings. Woodwinds/brass = **Long + Staccato** only.
- **ONE mix signal only** (a single pre-baked stereo mix — NO mic mixer, NO close/dry
  vs ambient/balcony choice).
- **~200–360 MB** install (240 MB lightweight package). Trivially small — fine on the
  system drive, doesn't *need* the offline `PlaySomeGodDamnMusic` drive.
- Native **AU/VST/VST3/AAX/standalone** → loads in Logic with no Kontakt.

## What Discover CAN do for THIS rig (drone/doom/ambient ghost)
- The **Long (sustain)** patches on strings/brass at **soft mod-wheel** ARE the
  ghost-layer. Hold a triad/cluster, ride CC1 down low, and you have a believable faint
  real-orchestra bed — exactly the "ghost under banjo" job. Discover's strings-Long is
  genuinely usable for sustained walls.
- Because it's a **single dry-ish Maida Vale mix**, it takes external reverb well —
  send straight into Valhalla Room/VintageVerb + EchoBoy + Decapitator (see degrade
  capture). You lose the *built-in* dry-vs-ambient mic blend, but you're adding your
  own space anyway, so for this workflow the single mix is **not a real loss**.

## What Discover CANNOT do (the limits that matter for ambient texture)
- **No legato** (the coveted Spitfire legato is Core/Pro only).
- **No textural/contemporary artics** — this is the big one: **no Super Sul Tasto, no
  Flautando, no Con Sordino, no Harmonics, no Sul Tasto.** Those are the patches the
  navigator calls the single most useful BBC SO sounds for ethereal texture, and they
  live in **Core/Pro only**. Discover gives you Long-vib sustains, which are warmer/
  more "normal orchestra" than the breathy super-soft colours.
- **No mic mixer** — can't pull up Ambient/Balcony/Sides for distance, can't isolate
  Close for max dryness. You get the one mix and shape it externally.

## Practical read
If the installed edition is **Discover**: you have a real-orchestra sustain bed for the
ghost job, but the *characterful* super-sul-tasto/flautando ghost colours are NOT
present — for those reach instead for **LABS Frozen Strings** (free, and it has the
standout super-sul-tasto six-player ensemble) or the owned Cells/SSEvo/Swarm. If the
edition is **Core or Pro**, the full textural artic set below (separate capture) is
available. **Worth confirming on disk when the drive is mounted** — it changes which
patches the ghost-layer recipe can name.
