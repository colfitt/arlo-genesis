# Spitfire Symphonic Strings Evolutions (SSEvo) — the evolving-wall workhorse

**Format.** Runs in **Kontakt 5 / free Kontakt Player** (NCW). ~30 GB (needs ~2× during install). Recorded at AIR Studios Hall, London, with **60 players** (16+14 violins, 12 violas, 10 cellos, 8 basses).

**The "Evos" concept.** Instead of static articulations, the samples **change over time** — "the strings stir and slowly awaken, ebbing and flowing as the Evos play." Each individual Evo runs **up to a minute before looping**. This is the literal evolving-string-wall engine.

**48 Evos in three flavours:**
- **Traditional (20)** — playable, musical movement.
- **Episodic (16)** — more eventful/gestural.
- **Extreme (12)** — the darkest/most textural; "dark and foreboding soundscapes" that build to crescendos.

**The Evo Grid ("The Grid").** A pegboard where:
- **Y-axis = Note Centre** (perfect-fourth intervals, D#2–A#6)
- **X-axis = which Evo / articulation**
Assign different Evos to different keyboard ranges; per-Evo volume/pan; route individual Evos to onboard FX (**reverb, delay, tape saturation, ADSR envelope**). **Randomise** ≈ 1.5×10²⁰ combinations. **18 curated presets** as starting points. An **Individual Evolution mode** for single-Evo control with velocity-response refinement.

**Mics.** Close, **Decca Tree**, ambient, wide outriggers. Two folders: **Main Mics** (full mixing) and **Stereo Mics** (pre-mixed Broad/Medium/Fine for lower CPU/RAM).

**Use / verdict.** For **underscoring and mood-setting**, not full scoring — pair with a normal-articulation library for melody. "One hell of a trick"… arguably "a one-trick pony" — but the trick IS evolving string textures + atmospheric layering. **For drone/doom: this is the symphony-sized evolving string drone.**
