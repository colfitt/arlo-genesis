# "Close mics + your own reverb" — the make-it-your-own workflow (BBC SO named)

The forum's dominant pro workflow for dry-leaning libraries, with BBC SO called out by
name — this is the exact technical justification for the rig's degrade angle.

## The named BBC SO technique
- VI-C, on BBCSO specifically: "BBCSO can sound dryish with the right mics… if you have
  a good reverb plugin (or Berlin Studio), you can **activate close mics on every
  instrument to normalize the sound, remove as much room as possible, put them on equal
  footing, and apply your own reverb manually.**" → That's the whole move: **kill the
  hall, keep Close, add YOUR space.**
- General pro consensus: "Many professionals use **only close mic positions** and manage
  reverb through a small number of plugins — sounds better and lets you blend libraries
  from different developers." Same principle that lets BBC SO sit under banjo/baritone
  in one coherent invented space.

## The layered-reverb recipe (adapt to Valhalla, not literal)
Forum's 3-stage convolution approach (translate to the owner's algos):
1. **Short reverb / body** per-instrument (small ER) → Valhalla Room, short.
2. **Positioning ER** to place it in 3D (stage IR) → can fold into the same Valhalla Room.
3. **Shared tail** all sends into one hall → VintageVerb (long, degraded) on a bus.
"Very dry libraries go through the stage IR first (positioning/ER) then the hall." For
this rig the "hall" is deliberately a *wrong* space — VintageVerb on a dark/lo-fi mode,
not a realistic concert hall.

## Why dry is the asset (the "make it your own" thesis)
- Dry source = no committed baked space → total control. The forum repeatedly: a dry lib
  "requires reverb to shine" but that's a *feature* when you want a specific invented
  space, not Maida Vale's.
- Jóhannsson-sound thread (the on-aesthetic citation): his string beds are "mostly slow
  legatos and sustains for a **pad-like quality**," using "delicate **flautando** and
  **sul tasto**" with **cross-fading between articulations for a 'woozy' vibe**, then
  **tape saturation** (Studer A800 / J37 / Satin) for "subtle compression, wow/flutter,
  high/low rolloff," plus dry strings + a chosen IR (e.g. Altiverb Kaufman Astoria) "to
  bring them to life." → For the rig: BBC SO sul-tasto/flautando sustains, crossfade
  artics, **EchoBoy/Decapitator for the tape/wow-flutter + saturation**, Valhalla for
  the space. This is the closest documented analogue to exactly what this rig does.
