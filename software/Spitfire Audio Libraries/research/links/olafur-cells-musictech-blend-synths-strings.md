The single best technical breakdown of how Cells works — the generative concept, the two modes, and the eDNA Sound Bays for blending synths with strings.

## The generative concept
Cells generates musical "cells" — micro-expressions that shift constantly when you hold a note or chord. "These micro expressions can encompass different intervals but will never stray from the harmonies you're working with." The randomization mirrors Ólafur's collaborative studio technique of asking live musicians to produce variations in pitch, timbre, and expression.

## Two operating modes
- **Scale mode (key-locked / "Standard")** — automatically detects your harmonic center and constrains variations to your chosen key. Ideal when you know your scale beforehand. You can manually select intervals using on-screen circles (one octave) if your key is unknown.
- **Played mode (flexible)** — analyzes the notes you play in real time, generates texture variations matching the detected key, and allows breaking from strict harmonic constraints.

## Shaping the output
- **Variation cards** — select preset cards with musical-notation images ("Intensity," "Motion," "Cirrus," "Clouds") to determine the cell types.
- **Out-of-scale toggle** — allows chromatic notes outside your working key (for tension).
- **Reverb dry/wet** — adjust presence via the center grey button or the FX tab.

## eDNA engine — blending synths & strings
Access via Mode menu → Synth for the alternative interface:
- **Two Sound Bays** — load string samples into Bay A, synthesis into Bay B.
- **Crossfader** — mix the two sources together.
- **Oscillate Mixer** — enable and set a multiplier (e.g. 16x) for slow timbral shifts between sources.
- **Vertical sliders** — control how much each sound feeds the final mix.
- **Dual effects bays** — each source (A and B) has dedicated effect processing.

## Practical use
Use Cells for lush swells, emotive passages, and dramatic moments where strings emerge in the foreground. (The article does not document a MIDI/audio capture workflow or mic positions.)
