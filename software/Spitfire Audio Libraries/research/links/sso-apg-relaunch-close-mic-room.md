# Relaunched SSO — the close-mic-is-still-roomy proof + Curated Ensembles for instant pads

The single clearest confirmation of the wet-hall point, and the relaunch feature map.

**★ Even the close mic is roomy (the load-bearing differentiator vs BBC SO):**
> "**Even the close-mic signal alone gives a sense of a large room in the early reflections it
> captures.**"
This is the opposite of BBC SO's genuinely dry/gritty Close mic. On SSO there is no "dry mic
to isolate and degrade." The reviewer frames the whole library as a "**symphonic sound** rather
than intimate or dry."

**Curated Ensembles (formerly "Masse") = instant pads/sustains:** pre-orchestrated, pre-mixed
tutti sections (strings / brass / winds / all-three Tutti) — "**over 100 unique players layered
to give up to 300+ musicians in a single patch.**" Sustains/shorts/stabs. For this rig, the
**Curated Ensembles sustains = the fastest route to a huge held orchestral WALL** (one patch,
one held chord, instant Lyndhurst-drenched bed) before you even touch individual sections. A
**Legacy folder** preserves the original uncurated patches (incl. the "Nutcracker" preset).

**Performance Legato** — new patches that auto-select articulation by dynamic/speed of playing.

**Mic positions:** ships Close / Tree / Ambient by default; many instruments add **Outriggers**,
strings add a **Leader** spot. Reviewer "frequently preferred the more open, wide sound of the
**Outriggers** over the default Tree." (For a vast ambient bed, Outriggers/Ambient lean is on-
aesthetic; for a touch more focus, Close — but it's still roomy.)

**Format/size:** runs in the **free Kontakt Player** (latest Kontakt also fine); ~368 GB
(per the FAQ). Strings 16-14-12-10-8. Piano sits "in its natural position in the orchestra,"
easy to blend — i.e. a contextual orchestral piano, not a solo piano.
