# SSO textural articulation reference + the wet-close-mic confirmation

Differentiates from the existing `spitfire-nav-symphony-orchestra-textures.md` by pulling the
specific ambient-relevant detail.

**The textural sustain set (strings) — the patches that matter for ghost/pad work:**
- **Super Sul Tasto** — "ultra-soft, ghostly" (bow far over the fingerboard; the #1 ethereal
  colour, same name as BBC SO's best patch but here drenched in Lyndhurst).
- **Flautando (long)** — "gorgeous breathy flautandos."
- **Long CS Blend** — a baked mix of con sordino + unmuted sustains (cohesive muted bed).
- **Harmonics (long + short)** — glassy/ethereal top.
- "Luxuriant and expansive **three-dynamic sustains**" + non-vib → molto-vib control, plus
  trems/trills across all sections.

**Soft releases = pad writing:** reviewer notes the **soft releases are "great for playing
chord progressions or 'pad'-style phrases."** Confirms SSO is comfortable as a held-chord pad
engine, not just a notes-in-a-mockup orchestra.

**Woodwind/brass longs as beds (in the hall):**
- Alto flute, clarinet, bassoon legatos "**absolutely beautiful in the big hall.**"
- French horn sustains "warm" with wide dynamic range → quiet horn chords = soft choral pad.
- Brass: crossfadeable from "warm quiet pads to blaring dynamics" — keep the crossfade low.

**Percussion as TEXTURE (not rhythm):** "exquisite marimba," "soft, mournful-sounding tubular
bells," vibraphone — all "resonating within the hall's reverb." The Lyndhurst tail turns
tuned percussion into shimmering sustained textures (let the hall do the sustain).

**The wet hall is integral / not removable:** "steeped in AIR Lyndhurst's sumptuous reverb"
— "captured through the recordings themselves rather than as a removable effect." Four mic
positions (Close / Tree / Ambient / Outriggers; strings add Leader) give *indirect* control
of how wet, but never dry.
