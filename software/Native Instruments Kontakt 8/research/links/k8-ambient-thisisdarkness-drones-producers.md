thisisdarkness.com · Michael Barnett · "Dark Ambient 101: Drones" · 2018-04-07

A roundup where actual dark-ambient artists describe how they build drones. The
recurring answer = a SAMPLER (mostly Kontakt) + field recordings + time-stretch.
This is the sourced "notable users" backbone for the textural/drone aesthetic.

## Named producers + their drone methods
- **protoU** — "Sometimes I layer sounds on top of each other... it's mostly
  **Kontakt**, and **morphing all the different field recordings** I make." →
  Kontakt as the field-recording morphing/layering engine.
- **Mebitek** — uses "Native Instruments and **Kontakt**"; technique =
  "**time-stretching on a single sound, delay, distortion and reverb**." (One
  source, stretched + dirtied = a drone.)
- **Aegri Somnia** — "**Reaktor** enables you to create **random calculated
  drones**" (generative drone via NI Reaktor — in Komplete).
- **Skadi** — Spectrasonics Omnisphere 2 for sound design with custom samples.
- **Stuzha** — "**Paul's Extreme Sound Stretch** on field recordings like wind";
  also moving toward "more live instruments" for genuine sounds.
- **Seesar** — acoustic drone sources: **bowing cymbals**, shaking objects,
  rolling/scraping, "dragging a rubber ball over a large drum head." (Then sampled
  in.)

## The distilled drone method (consensus across artists)
1. Capture an imperfect acoustic/field source (bowed cymbal, wind, scrape).
2. **Time-stretch it extremely** (PaulStretch / Kontakt time-machine) so a short
   sound becomes a sustained bed.
3. **Layer/morph several** in Kontakt — crossfade between field recordings.
4. **Delay + distortion + reverb** to smear into a continuous drone.
5. Optional: **Reaktor** for randomized/generative drone motion.

### Why this matters for the rig
This is the dark-ambient blueprint and it maps 1:1 onto owned gear: bow the banjo
or a cymbal → record → drop into Kontakt → stretch + morph multiple takes →
delay/dist/reverb (EchoBoy/Decapitator/Valhalla). PaulStretch (free) is the
canonical extreme-stretch tool the genre leans on, complementing Kontakt's own
time-machine. Reaktor (owned via Komplete) = the generative-drone option.
