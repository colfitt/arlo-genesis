# Turning a short sample into a lush sustained pad/drone

Transforms a brief one-shot (their example: a bottle hit) into an evolving pad — the recipe for the rig's "sample anything → wall" workflow.

**Steps:**
1. **Set root key** to the sample's actual recorded pitch so keyboard notes track correctly (no unwanted transpose).
2. **Pick a stable mid-section** away from the attack/beginning so the tone is consistent ("away from this beginning section so we get a nice consistent tone").
3. **Set loop start/end markers.** Snapping to grid puts start/end on a **zero crossing** to reduce clicks.
4. **Crossfade** between loop boundaries to mask the restart seam.
5. **Amp envelope:** long **attack (1–2 s)** and **release (2–3 s)** with sustain maxed → gradual fades that hide the loop mechanism and give a pad feel.
6. **Effects:** ping-pong delay + reverb to diffuse and disguise loop boundaries.
7. **Movement:** filter envelope, FM, or a second oscillator/LFO sweeping at a *different* rate than the amp envelope → evolving (not static) texture.

**Why it matters for drone/ambient:** the combination of (long crossfaded loop) + (slow modulation at a rate decoupled from the amp env) is exactly what kills the "obviously looped" static feel — you get a living, breathing sustained tone from a 2-second recording. Stack two slightly **detuned** layers for thickness/beating.
