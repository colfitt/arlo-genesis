# BITE — recipe + range reference (going past the nav one-liner)

Concrete Bite settings pulled and triangulated across the NI blog and the MusicRadar review (and cross-checked vs the official walkthrough + manual).

## Verified ranges (two sources agree)
- **Freq / sample rate:** 0.1 kHz → 44.1 kHz (manual says 0.1; MusicRadar quotes the working range as 100 Hz–44.1 kHz).
- **Bits:** 2 → 16 bit.
- **Jitter** fluctuates sample rate **per channel** (stereo) → noise artifacts.

## Named recipes (NI blog)
- **Lo-fi drums:** sample rate down to **12 kHz**, Pre/Post filters opened to ~**22.1 kHz** (let the aliasing through).
- **Max noise:** bit depth down to **6 bit**.
- **Dubstep bass texture:** sample rate to **1.47 kHz**.
- **★ Tape-disintegrate sweep:** automate sample rate **44.1 kHz → 300 Hz** over a passage → progressive fall-apart (transition/riser; "this wall is collapsing" doom move). Daft-Punk-style atmospheric transition cited.

## Character (MusicRadar)
- "Filthy device" → "old-school hip-hop flavour" to "full-on chiptune digitalia."
- Weakness: no modulation controls (unlike Freak's sidechain).

## Rig application (drone/doom/shoegaze)
- **Insert on a sustained wall / banjo lead** → degraded-tape edge; use the **Post filter** to roll off harsh aliasing so grit reads as warmth, not fizz (Pre/Post at MIDDLE = vintage-sampler tuning).
- **Freq-sweep automation under a held drone** = the signature "tape disintegrating" arc.
- **Mix knob (equal-power) for parallel blend** — keep a crushed layer under the clean drone instead of replacing it.
- Bite is the ONLY confirmed-installed unit of these six (Bite.component/.vst/.vst3 on disk) and is also a free standalone NI plugin.
