# RAUM — the three algorithms & their controls (official manual)

"Each of the algorithms can be used to create anything from tight ambiences to otherworldly
soundscapes, while maintaining the basic character of its paradigm."

## Grounded (rooms) & Airy (halls) — SAME six controls
- **Diffusion** — texture of the reflections; higher = softer/smeared early reflections.
- **Size** — swell + reflection pattern; small spaces → large.
- **Density** — toggle **Sparse / Dense**. Sparse = dispersed, clearly discernible reflections
  (grainier). Dense = uniform smooth tail.
- **Decay** — reverb time / tail length.
- **Damp** — bright→dark via HF attenuation in the tail.
- **Modulation** — amount of movement, lush → detuned (see Parker interview for the >70% dissonant zone).

Grounded = strong early reflections + dense tail; tiny ambience → large chamber.
Airy = naturally dispersed early reflections, smooth tail; medium chamber → cathedral & beyond.

## Cosmic (abstract spaces) — only FOUR controls
- **Density** — same Sparse/Dense toggle.
- **Size** — spatial impression.
- **Rate** — speed of movement, slow → fast.
- **Modulation** — amount of movement.
- (No Diffusion/Decay/Damp knobs — Cosmic's "decay" is governed by the predelay Feedback path.)
- ★ KEY: *"Since the algorithm is placed inside the predelay's feedback path, it interacts more
  closely with all parameters related to predelay."* → On Cosmic, the **Predelay + Feedback**
  controls (separate Predelay/Filters section) are the real decay/drone controls. Each echo
  through the cosmic all-pass cascade gets more blurred/diffuse.

## Granular trick (NI guidance)
Density = **Sparse** + Size at **high** values → reverb takes on a **granular** quality. This is
the doom/shoegaze "almost-granular cloud" setting (works on Cosmic and on Grounded/Airy Sparse).
