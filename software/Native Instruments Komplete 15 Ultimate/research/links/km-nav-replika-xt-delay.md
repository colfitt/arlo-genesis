# Replika XT — five-mode delay (the ambient/dub delay)

Five modeled delay types, each after a revered hardware delay:
- **Modern** — clean, precise, controllable repeats (digital).
- **Vintage Digital** — warm, crunchy repeats modeled on early samplers/digital delays.
- **Analogue** — dark, hazy **bucket-brigade pedal** sound (each repeat darker — perfect for dub-style decaying echoes).
- **Tape** — organic tape-echo character (wow/flutter, saturation — the lo-fi pick).
- **Diffusion** — "entirely its own": **swirling reverberant echoes** that blur into reverb-like wash. The ambient/textural mode — makes spaces no physical room gives you.

## Engine
- **Pattern controls** for rhythmic/multi-tap repeats; **per-delay stereo panning**; **ducking** (keeps percussion/transients tight under the echoes).
- **Seven additional time-based + pitch effects** that enhance or run **independently** of the delay (so it's also a standalone FX rack).
- High feedback + modulation → self-oscillating, "hyper-modulated echoes."
- Artist presets from **Richard Devine** and **Funkstörung** ship with it (experimental/IDM pedigree).

## Rig recipes
- **Dub-doom decay:** Analogue mode, long time, high feedback, roll off highs each pass → echoes dissolve into the drone.
- **Shoegaze wash:** Diffusion mode → smears repeats into a reverb-adjacent cloud behind a guitar/banjo.
- **Tape lo-fi slap:** Tape mode short, with the inherent flutter, into a wall.
- **Freeze-style infinite:** crank feedback to self-oscillation under a held note for a building wall.
- Stack Replika XT (Diffusion) → RAUM (Cosmic) for delay-into-infinite-reverb chains.

Note: base **Replika** (free) = the simpler single-mode version; **Replika XT** (Ultimate-included) = the full five-mode + 7-FX version.
