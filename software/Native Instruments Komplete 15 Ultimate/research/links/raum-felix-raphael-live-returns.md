# RAUM in practice — Felix Raphael (real artist, ambient/emotional electronic, album DO YOU)

The single best documented real-world RAUM workflow. Treats RAUM as a **playable instrument on
returns**, not an insert. Concrete numbers below.

## Two dedicated return buses (NOT inserts)
- **"Room" return** — intimate presence. Mix **10–35%**, **short Decay**, Pre-delay **20–60 ms**
  (preserves articulation/transient pocket).
- **"Atmos/Drones" return** — expansive swells. **Long Decay**, used with Freeze.
- Both EQ'd: **Low cut 150–200 Hz, High cut 8–12 kHz** to prevent mud/harshness.
- Returns-only = CPU headroom + independent depth control from the dry source. ★ This is the
  send-vs-insert answer for the rig: run RAUM on a Logic aux/return, Mix 100% on the bus.

## Freeze as a performance instrument
Captures a **vocal consonant or chord tail** into Freeze on the Atmos/Drones bus, then plays over
the sustained pad. Shapes it live by **riding Size** (the only live param under Freeze) — "without
clicks or noise," especially effective **before drops**. → For the rig: freeze a banjo chord/drone,
solo over the infinite bed, ride Size.

## Algorithms mapped to emotional arc (his words)
- **Grounded** = "intimacy and honesty for fragile phrases."
- **Airy** = "opens into hope, letting phrases bloom."
- **Cosmic** = "bends time and place for those surreal moments."

## Restraint / performance-safety
- Maps controls to **limited ranges** so "adrenaline doesn't push you into swampy territory on
  stage." (Good template for automation lanes: clamp the range.)
- Aesthetic: "the boldest move is no move. Let the space speak… resist chasing every swell with
  more reverb." (Counterpoint to wall-of-sound maximalism — negative space.)
