NI's own shoegaze guide; recommends Guitar Rig 7 Pro as the software way to access the amps/effects "to experiment with and layer up" without hardware limits.

## Core philosophy
"In shoegaze, the effects ARE the music" — simple notes/chords are transformed into expansive landscapes by effects.

## Techniques (directly applicable in GR7)
- **Reverb & delay**: push **feedback and wet levels higher** so tails sustain and notes/chords **stack into wall-like textures**; use to blend guitars, drums, vocals into one soundscape.
- **Distortion at the END of the chain**: positioning distortion *after* the long reverb/delay tails **compresses/crushes them together** → the characteristic crashing, corrupted, "almost cracking from intensity" tone. (Note: this is the shoegaze-specific inversion of the usual "distortion first" order — see Ali Jamieson + Decapitator guide for the standard order; both are valid, different sounds.)
- **Modulation** (chorus/tremolo/flanger/phaser) for shimmering dynamics; **pitch-bend** (Kevin Shields glide) for wavering leads.
- **Foundation** = fuzz/distortion + drawn-out delay + reverb = a wall of powerful noise.

## Warning
Dense layering risks mud — requires "delicate EQing and correct balancing of elements." (In GR7: use per-module EQ, the Solid EQ, and Color's Bass Saver to keep the low end clear.)
