# Replika XT — MusicRadar review (independent practical observations)

## Modes in use
- **Tape** — "good and flexible tape-style sonics."
- **Analogue** — "the essence of those early delay units, with **convincing pitch changes when the
  delay timing is tweaked on the fly**." → ride the Time knob live for tape/BBD pitch-warble wall.
- **Diffusion** — effective reverb emulator, "conjuring up some **wonderfully long, evocative
  spaces**." Confirms the delay-as-reverb-wash works for genuinely long ambient tails.

## ★ The shimmer/evolving trick — FX inside the feedback loop
All seven mod effects sound "excellent." Crucially: **Filter, Frequency Shifter, and Pitch
Shifter can operate WITHIN the delay feedback loop** → "echo effects that evolve in very
interesting ways." This is the route to:
- **Shimmer delay** = Pitch Shifter +12 semitones inside the feedback loop (each repeat climbs an
  octave → rising shimmer cloud, like a shimmer reverb but on the delay).
- **Spiralling drift** = Freq Shifter inboard (each repeat detunes further → inharmonic wall).
- **Self-filtering dub** = Filter inboard (each repeat darker/resonant).

## Ducking (reviewer's favourite)
In Single mode, ducking "can be set so that the **delay fades up from its initial low level as the
feedback progresses**" → the echoes swell in behind the source instead of clashing. Ideal for
banjo/baritone plucks: play clean, wash blooms in the gaps.

## Pattern
Shuffle / **Feel (Rush↔Lag)** / Accent = "quick but powerful overall adjustment of the delay's
rhythm." (Feel Lag = drag the repeats behind the beat for a slack, slowcore feel.)

## Verdict
"A mixture of regular, panning and modulated delays alongside an interesting array of spatialising,
pitched and oddball effects" — "plenty to offer sound designers and creative producers alike."
