# Mod Pack review — Phasis / Choral / Flair (distilled)

Verdict: "A trio of slick, creative effects stocked with lots of creative presets, all at a very reasonable price." No star number quoted.

## PHASIS
- Notch-amount + feedback/resonance controls; sync-able LFO with rate + mod depth.
- **Ultra mode** "extends the Rate and Centre ranges into the audible frequency range, to create effects more akin to Phase Modulation synthesis." (Corroborates the phaser→FM-synth behavior.)

## CHORAL
- Four modes, here framed by their hardware lineage: **Synth (Juno-style), Ensemble (string-synth), Dimension (BBD-style), Universal (plugin-style/modern).**
- Controls: Width, Rate, Delay, Feedback. (This review does NOT mention Scatter — but the official walkthrough and manual both confirm Scatter as the unique reverb-like feedback routing.)

## FLAIR
- "Four separate comb filters, referred to here as **Voices**. These voices can be added at a variety of preset harmonic intervals, effectively allowing the plugin to act as a **chord generator**."
- Three modes: Standard, Thru Zero, Scan (arpeggiator-like).

## Weakness flagged
- "At their best when used with NI controller hardware" — i.e. tuned for Maschine/Komplete Kontrol hands-on; not a sound limitation but a workflow note. (Relevant: this rig's 61SL MkIII is NKS, so parameter pages still map — see km-nav-61sl-mkiii-nks-reality.md.)

## Rig takeaway
- Confirms Flair = chord generator (Voices at harmonic intervals) → tuned-resonator/harmonic-wall move.
- Confirms Phasis Ultra = phase-modulation-synthesis territory for turning drones/noise into tones.
