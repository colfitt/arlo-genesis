# The Novation 61SL MkIII + Komplete Kontrol — the honest reality

The single most important integration fact for THIS user's rig. Triangulated across Novation's own support article, NI's community forum, and the Ableton/VI-Control comparison threads.

## What the 61SL MkIII DOES get (NKS via Komplete Kontrol)
The SL MkIII is an **NKS-compatible third-party controller** (Novation × NI partnership). Through the Komplete Kontrol plugin/standalone it gets:
- **Unified preset browsing** of 2,000+ NKS instruments/effects — Browse Up/Down = prev/next preset; Shift+Browse = prev/next KK plugin slot.
- **8-knob parameter control** mapped per page — the **NKS pre-mapped parameter pages** appear on the SL's screens; Page+Browse = next page of mapped params.
- Load instruments/effects, tweak, manage presets with hardware controls — its **five LCD screens** show param names/values.
- Setup: load **Template 1 MIDI** on the SL (left-most rotary), then KK talks to it.
- It also does generic **InControl / HUI** for any DAW (Logic transport etc.) outside KK.

## What it does NOT get (the catch)
- **NO NI light guide.** The on-key light guide is **100% two-way comms between Komplete Kontrol software and NI's OWN hardware** — NI **has not licensed the tech** to any other manufacturer. The SL MkIII has its **own Fatar-keybed RGB key LEDs** (actually taller than NI's), and those show **Novation's own scale/zone feedback** — but the **KK Smart-Play light guide (which keys are in-scale / which trigger chords) does NOT drive the SL's LEDs automatically.**
- Workaround: you can **build custom light-guide templates inside Komplete Kontrol**; some third-party content (even non-NKS) shows the guide when loaded inside KK — but it's manual, not automatic.

## Net for the rig
- **Run instruments inside the Komplete Kontrol plugin** and the 61SL MkIII is a genuinely good NKS controller: tag browser + 8 mapped knobs across the whole 150+ bundle, on its screens.
- **Smart Play (scales/chords/arp) still works** — it's a software MIDI feature in KK, independent of which keyboard plays (so the SL benefits even without the hardware light guide).
- The **only thing you lose vs an NI S-series** is the automatic on-key light guide. For a drone/pad player that's minor — you're holding sustained chords, not sight-reading scale highlights.
- **Verdict (forums):** "probably the best keyboard controller available if you're willing to live without full [auto light-guide] NKS support."
