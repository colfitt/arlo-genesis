Pluginoise · "NI Guitar Rig vs Neural DSP Amps — Which Is Best?"

The clearest head-to-head for deciding when to reach for GR vs Neural DSP Archetypes (both owned in this rig).

## Sound quality & feel
- **Neural DSP wins on amp-tone realism** — "takes the crown for pure amp tone realism"; Archetypes nail the details, "feel alive, reacting to every pick scrape and volume knob adjustment," "like sitting in a studio with a boutique amp." Strongest advantage is **high-gain**.
- **Guitar Rig prioritizes versatility** — broader sonic territory; some amp models "a bit polite." Honest concession: "**besides high-gain tones, the tonal quality is not that different.**"

## When to reach for each
- **Neural DSP** — when you know your target tone (specific artist/amp), want authentic pro recordings, high-gain, tone realism is the priority. Does one thing really well.
- **Guitar Rig** — "a chameleon that can handle any genre," experimental sound design, genre-hopping, **building elaborate modular signal chains**. The creative FX playground.

## Guitar Rig's all-in-one strengths
26 amps + 115 stompboxes/effects; modular elaborate chains; new lo-fi effects (Tape Wobble, Noise Machine); custom IR loading; **one GR license costs less than one Neural DSP Archetype**.

## CPU
Neural DSP is lighter/easier to run; Guitar Rig "can be more demanding, especially with complex signal chains."

## Verdict
"Guitar Rig 7 Pro shines as the versatile all-in-one solution, while Neural DSP is for those moments when tone realism and attention to detail matter most." (Cross-source note: another comparison adds "Guitar Rig isn't that bad once you pair it with a 3rd-party cab sim / IRs" — and GR7's own IR loader closes much of that gap.)
