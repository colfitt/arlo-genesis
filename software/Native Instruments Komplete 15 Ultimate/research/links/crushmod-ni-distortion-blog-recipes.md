# NI distortion blog — Dirt + Bite concrete recipes (distilled)

## DIRT — bass tone-shaping recipe (from the article)
- Turn up Drive on the **first** distortion circuit to **~40%**.
- Add the **second** circuit at **~20%** Drive to boost lower harmonics.
- Blend **50/50 wet/dry** with the Mix knob.

## Wavefolding vs saturation
- Wavefolding is "even more extreme" than waveshaping: "the signal isn't clipped when it reaches the threshold, but is instead flipped back around and added to the original signal. The result is chaotic, and good for extreme sound design."

## Parallel distortion blend targets
- Drums: ~**50/50** wet/dry.
- Vocals: ~**80% dry / 20% wet** (keep it subtle).
- General: "pull back the Mix until it's blended with your dry signal."

## Placement rule (and how to break it)
- "Place distortion AFTER EQ" and "BEFORE any time-based effects such as chorus, delay, or reverb."
- "However, this rule was made to be broken. **Try distorting a heavily reverbed signal for a chaotic effect.**" ← directly useful for doom/drone (distort the wet reverb tail of a wall).

## BITE — lo-fi/ambient recipe
- Increase **Jitter** for erratic sample-rate variation → noise in the higher frequencies.
- Then add **Saturation (~6 dB)** for warmth.

## Rig takeaway
- Dirt parallel 50/50 (or 80/20 on a lead/banjo) keeps degrade musical inside a wall.
- The "distort a heavily-reverbed signal" inversion = put Dirt/Bite/Freak AFTER RAUM on an aux for a collapsing, chaotic tail — a deliberate break of the usual degrade→reverb order.
