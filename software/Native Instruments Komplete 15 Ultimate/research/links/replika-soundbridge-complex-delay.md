# Replika XT — building complex/ambient delays (tutorial, distilled)

Two worked examples (light on exact numbers — FLAG: tutorial gives note-values not percentages).

## Ex 1 — Diffusion depth-and-space (the ambient move)
- Mode = **Diffusion**, **Size = slightly larger**, **Time = short 1/32**, a **Filter** modulation
  effect applied. Result = "nice depth and space" — a short diffuse delay reads as ambience, not
  as distinct echoes. (Short time + big Diffusion Size = the reverb-wash trick.)

## Ex 2 — distortion-driven uplifter (sound-design)
- Mode = **Analogue + distortion**, **very short double delay with MAXIMUM feedback**, plus a
  **Frequency Shifter** FX with the shift parameter automated → rising "uplifter." Shows the FX-
  section riding on top of self-oscillating short feedback.

## Interface notes
- Delay-type selector, a **grid** for visual dual-delay positioning, mix/time/feedback, the FX
  section, and ping-pong switching. Confirms FX (filter, freq-shifter) are the primary sculpting
  tools layered over the delay.
