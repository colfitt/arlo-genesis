Authoritative reference on the Performers — Massive X's signature modulation feature and the key to never-repeating evolving motion (when several run at unrelated rates). Distilled.

## What they are
- **Three Performers** (P1, P2, P3) — "specialized sequencers for modulation" that apply complex, rhythmical movement to parameters, as an alternative to host automation. They bake intricate modulation INTO the patch.

## Patterns / steps
- Each Performer supports **up to 12 patterns**, selectable via the **Remote Octave** feature (trigger snapshots by MIDI key) or by mouse in the footer.
- Grid quantization: steps default to 24 (bipolar -24..+24, unipolar 0..+24).

## Editing modes
- **Basic** (default): set time signature + rhythmic resolution from quarter notes down to 32nd divisions.
- **Custom**: custom time signatures with up to EIGHT sections, each with its own beats-per-bar, beat length, and subdivisions. Switch between modes without data loss.

## Painting tools (7 brushes)
Edit (individual points joined by lines), Line, Step (static values), Ramp A (descending), Ramp B (ascending), Triangle, Sine. Shapes snap to the chosen division/note value.

## Rate / level
- **Rate** is host-tempo-relative: center = host tempo, far-left = 1/8 speed, far-right = 8× faster.
- **Level** slider sets output intensity 0–100%.

## Why they matter for evolving textures
Unlike envelopes (note-triggered, one-shot) or LFOs (simple free-running shapes), Performers combine rhythmic precision with pattern sequencing — synchronized, evolving modulation locked to tempo. **Running several Performers at unrelated Rates (and/or different bar lengths via Custom mode) produces long, polyrhythmic, effectively non-repeating motion** — exactly the "constantly evolving wall" behaviour. Use the 12 patterns + Remote Octave to switch the whole feel mid-performance.
