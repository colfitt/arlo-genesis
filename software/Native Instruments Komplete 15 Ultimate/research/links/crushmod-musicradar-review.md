# Crush Pack review — MusicRadar (distilled)

## BITE (digital distortion)
- Bit-depth + sample-rate reduction: **100 Hz–44.1 kHz, 2–16 bit.**
- **Jitter** randomly fluctuates the sample rate per channel.
- Pre/Post low-pass filters manage aliasing creatively.
- Character: "filthy device" → "old-school hip-hop flavour" or "full-on chiptune digitalia."
- Weakness: lacks modulation controls (no sidechain like Freak).

## DIRT (diode-clipping distortion)
- Dual diode stages (A/B), series or parallel. **Amount transitions from saturation to wavefolding in its second half**, "dramatically increasing crunch."
- Character: **"earthy analogue sound"** — "presence-enhancing bite at low levels to screaming serration when pushed into the wavefolding range."
- Weakness: would benefit from envelope-follower modulation.
- Best on bass, guitar, drum buses, vocals, synths.

## FREAK (ring mod / frequency shifter)
- Diode-ring circuit, three modes. Type morphs **Amplitude (tremolo/metallic sidebands) → Ring (sideband replacement) → Frequency Shifting (linear offset).** Feedback + Harmonics routing.
- **"The jewel in the Crush Pack crown."** Generates "inharmonic noises, sci-fi burbles, detuned sweeps, crazy frequency wanderings." Radio mode = evocative retro/extraterrestrial AM broadcast.

## Verdict
"Must-have creative signal-processing pack... at a ridiculously low price. Don't miss it!" (£59). No explicit star number quoted.

## Rig takeaway
- Confirms Bite range (100 Hz–44.1 kHz, 2–16 bit) and Dirt's saturation→wavefold split at the Amount midpoint.
- Freak = the standout for the inharmonic/sci-fi/detuned doom textures this rig wants.
