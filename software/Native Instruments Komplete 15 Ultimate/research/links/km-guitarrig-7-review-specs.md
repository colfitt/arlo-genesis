Guitar World / Sound on Sound / Gearnews — GR7 Pro reviews (authoritative spec + verdict reference)

## What's in the box (totals)
- **26 guitar/bass amps** with matching cabinets, **115 stompboxes + rack effects**, 100+ new presets, **VST3 / AU / AAX + standalone**.

## The 4 new ICM amps (and what they model)
- **Super-Fast 100** — Soldano SLO-100-style high-gain lead; wide usable gain, keeps note definition dimed.
- **Reverb Delight** — Fender Deluxe / mid-60s workhorse clean.
- **AC Box XV** — Vox AC-style 60s clean→gritty breakup.
- **Bass Rage** — Ampeg-style 300W all-tube bass.
ICM = Intelligent Circuit Modeling (machine-learning), praised for "feel and response to your playing dynamics" — "a significant improvement" over the older Fender/Vox models.

## New stompboxes / effects (5)
- **Screamer/Skreamer Deluxe** (Tube-Screamer-style, 3 modes: Classic/LED/EQ), **Chainsaw** (80s Swedish-death-metal buzz distortion), **Seattle Fuzz** (grunge fuzz + diode clipping), **IVP Stomp** (rack-preamp, EQ before drive), **Kolor** (saturation/10 algorithms).
- New **lo-fi** modules (the v7 theme): **Tape Wobble, Noise Machine, Vintage Vibrato** (+ Color/Kolor).

## New utilities
- **Cabinet IR Loader** — blend up to 4 IRs, per-IR level/pan/EQ, **Auto-align** phase; ships with 3 Sigma Audio, Bogren Digital, cabIR, Eminence, Lancaster IRs; loads custom IRs.
- **iZotope Ozone Maximizer** module (Dynamics) — IRC-LL peak limiting in-rack.
- **Loop Machine Pro** (returned, MIDI-controllable), new **Signal Flow sidebar**, per-parameter hover help.

## Verdicts (consensus)
- "Still the ultimate suite of software guitar tools" / "still king of the hill for amp modelling" — best and easiest **all-in-one** guitar-effects suite. Excels at "humanising sterile computer recordings"; great as a live rack AND a DAW plugin for experimental work; "new lo-fi sounds are great."
- **Caveats**: not an essential upgrade from v6; the OLDER (pre-ICM) amp models can feel "a bit polite"; won't satisfy "tone-modelling OCD" purists chasing exact single-amp emulation (that's where Neural DSP wins). Honest cross-source line: "besides high-gain tones, the tonal quality is not that different" from Neural.
