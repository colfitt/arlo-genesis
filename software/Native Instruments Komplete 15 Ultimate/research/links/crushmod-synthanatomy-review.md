# Crush Pack review — Bite / Dirt / Freak (distilled)

A sound-design-leaning review of the trio.

## BITE
- "You can mimic the sound character of old hardware samplers that give each sound a nice noisy warmth." Produces classic lo-fi effects easily.
- Particularly effective on drum machines → "dirty industrial sound design."
- Controls noted: resampling freq, bit depth, Crunch, stereo clock jittering, stereo dither, filtering, saturation.

## DIRT
- Two circuit-modeled diode stages (A/B), three modes (I/II/III), drive + amount, tilt, parallel blend.
- Key verdict: **"DIRT does not completely destroy the audio signal"** — "new harmonics created with this effect fit perfectly into the mix without making the signal useless." A musical saturator→distortion, not a wrecking ball.

## FREAK
- Identified as **"the most versatile plugin"** of the three.
- Makes sounds **"more metallic, smoker [smokier] but also crazier"** — not traditional saturation. The amp→ring→freq-shift morph = "discovering many different sound colours."
- Best for versatile modulation; less for classic distortion.

## Rig takeaway
- Dirt's "doesn't destroy / harmonics sit in the mix" character = good for adding teeth to a drone layer that still has to sit in a wall.
- Freak = the inharmonic/metallic specialist (matches its role as the doom/sci-fi degrade unit).
- Bite's industrial-drum-machine strength = degraded sampled-hardware percussion (MPC/Digitakt material).
