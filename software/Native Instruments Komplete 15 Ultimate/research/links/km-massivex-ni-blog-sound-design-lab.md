NI's round-up of pro sound-designers' Massive X techniques — several directly about evolving/endless soundscapes. Quoted/attributed tips below.

- **John Valasis** — creates "evolving soundscapes and SFX" that "could play endlessly without repeating themselves" using the extensive modulator connectivity. Technique: run multiple **Performer lanes at different time signatures** for polyrhythmic motion inside a sustained sound.
- **Carlo De Gregorio** (Tracker module) — a "negative exponential curve ramping up, attached to velocity and assigned to filter cutoff" makes a dampened instrument that only opens at high velocity → dynamic pad responsiveness.
- **Richard Devine** — highlights "Phase Modulation, the wavetable modes and algorithms (Bend, Mirror, Gorilla, etc.)" as critical timbre-shapers. Layer the two oscillators with phase modulation between them for continuously-evolving pad textures.
- **Torsten Fassbender (noise)** — the "two complex noise voices" are rich textural bases; try the various included noise tables as starting points for ambient patches.
- **Routing / cross-mod** — the new routing system lets you "connect nearly anything to anything." Route LFOs into other LFOs sequentially for cascading depth; feed wavetable oscillators through multiple filter types for layered harmonic complexity.
- **Effects for atmosphere** — ARC Noise: "modulating the delay settings to get really wonky, dubby delay effects." The Chorus has "ten different variations" for textural diversity on pads.

## Takeaway for drone/ambient
The endorsed recipe for an endless evolving wall: (1) two wavetable oscillators + PM between them + noise voices; (2) several Performers at DIFFERENT time signatures (the unrelated rates = no repeat); (3) Trackers mapping velocity/keytrack to cutoff for response; (4) modulated delay/chorus for atmosphere.
