[A full ambient-swell recipe. CRUCIAL for this rig because it shows GR used exactly as the user's tools are split: GR = the clean amp foundation, EchoBoy = the delays, Valhalla = the reverbs. Settings are explicit. (GR6, but the clean-amp role is identical in GR7; GR7 could also do the delays/reverbs in-box.)]

## The signal chain (all on ONE track, in series)
Strat (neck pickup) → **EQ** (HPF ~153 Hz, LPF ~6 kHz, to clean up the source) → **Guitar Rig clean preset** (amp simulator, kept clean "so I have more room to sculpt afterwards") → 3 delays → 2 reverbs → 3–4 pitch shifters. Volume swells done **manually in the DAW** (clip fades), not a swell pedal, "for more control and flexibility."

## The role split (directly mirrors this rig)
- **Guitar Rig = the clean amp foundation only.** "Mostly I prefer a clean preset." Nothing fancy — it's the tone/cab bed under everything.
- **EchoBoy (Soundtoys) = all the delays.** Three of them:
  1. Single echo (no extra stereo), **mix ~45%**, slight low cut, unlinked from tempo, **~300 ms**, **feedback ~50%**, + a little **saturation** "for a rough edge."
  2. Second echo, slower, low cut + high cut "so it sits in the background," **~500 ms**, feedback cranked.
  3. **Ping-pong**, unlinked, left **424 ms** / right **233 ms**, feedback cranked, stronger low cut on the sides + high cut to blend.
- **Valhalla = the reverbs.** Two:
  1. **VintageVerb** — large + lots of **modulation** (chorus-y), mix ~40% (because it's on the track not an aux), no pre-delay, **decay ~2.8 s** (short, because the delays already give the long tail; also softens the chord attack).
  2. **Room** set to **Hall**, less modulation for natural ambience, wet ~40%, no pre-delay, shorter decay.

## Pitch-shimmer layering (placed AFTER delays, BEFORE reverbs)
- Octave **down** shifter: blend dry up + wet down ~−6.7 dB, boost ~+2/2.5 dB = extra bass body.
- Octave **up** shifter: ~−6/−7 dB so the highs aren't intrusive/unnatural.
- **+2 octaves** shifter: very subtle, ~−20 dB, "just a detail."
This builds the shimmer/wall by stacking pitched copies of the already-delayed signal. (In GR7 the in-box equivalent = **Replika Shimmer** / **Pitch Pedal** / **Harmonic Synthesizer**, or the Psych Delay's pitch.)

## Chord/voicing rule for walls
Keep chords SIMPLE and spaced — root, fifth, third (sometimes 9th or 4th). Heavy delay + reverb turns full chords into "an undefined blob and wall of music"; sparse voicings stay defined. Pitch shifters multiply every note, so keep the bass transparent. (Critical guidance for banjo/baritone drone walls.)
