I'm Calum, one half of Clouds, showing my creative process while making a sketch.

I start with a kick drum as a basis to set down rhythms — using Massive X for the low end (I'll change the preset slightly later; I just want the transient kick down). Take the decay right down, attack at zero, roll off the low frequencies of the transient kick so it doesn't interfere with the low end. A bit of reverb on the high transient kick to give it space so it sits in the mix. Now there's a kick = a metronome to start adding things to.

I start designing rhythm sounds using Massive X and MOLEKULAR. You can see this is just loads and loads of switchers — they're all on random LFO modulation going through all sorts of different parameters on Massive X. I run Massive X as a sound source and have MOLEKULAR after it. Usually I'll load TWO instances of MOLEKULAR: one I'm cycling through presets on, then another after that capturing the sound in its looper. I open a new audio track and route the Massive X / MOLEKULAR track into it, capturing the audio so I can save and use it. I do this a lot even when not arranging — just sound designing.

That bit at the end was nice — a subtle rhythmic loop. Add delay using REPLIKA XT for more rhythm. I use a lot of delays; on most tracks I stick delays on things — it's instant techno.

Hi-hats from BATTERY (a sharp transient hi-hat layered with a more traditional 909-style closed hat), a little delay/reverb on the sharp transient for space. Quarter-note shaker via the same Massive X + MOLEKULAR rhythmic-loop process — you get really different sounds by just changing the noise oscillator in Massive X. Group them and bring down sustain with TRANSIENT MASTER.

More sound design for effects/atmosphere and noisy things throughout the track — same patch as the rhythmic percussion, taken straight from Massive X; capture all those changes as audio. Add movement to the frequency on DRIVER. Bass: cycle through Komplete Kontrol presets, tweak, add distortion (BITE for crispy high-end distortion). Melodies from layering presets (FORM was great). Layer a plucky Massive X patch with a sharp transient (another envelope on the pitch with a really sharp decay; reverb and delay off). Then arrange by muting/unmuting and consolidating audio.
