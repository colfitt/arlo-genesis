# PHASIS — official NI walkthrough (distilled)

Phasis = phaser that goes from classic to FM-synthesis territory via ULTRA mode. **NOT confirmed installed on disk.**

## Context
"Traditionally phaser effects have been used by acts such as Tangerine Dream and Radiohead to create wide, psychedelic synth and guitar parts." Ultra mode opens new possibilities.

## Core moves
- On Massive's **"Airborne Memories"** pad: "by itself the sound is really static, but adding Phasis gives the part a rich modular movement that not only moves the signal into the sides more, but also has a taming effect on those full-on mids." (Phaser as both widener AND mid-tamer — easier to mix.)
- Standard controls: **Rate, Feedback, Amount, Mix.**
- **Notches:** "set the number of notches in the frequency spectrum up to 12." **Large number of notches + high Feedback = a vocal-like / vowel quality.**

## ULTRA mode (the extreme)
- Best shown on **white noise** (here from Monark): "Ultra mode allows us to add new harmonic content into the signal."
- **Turn up Feedback, Notches and Rate** and "Phasis blurs the lines between processing and synthesis, turning the white-noise bursts into a tone." (Phaser-as-synthesizer.)
- **Gradually sweep the Rate down** → "the sound changes from frequency modulation to phasing as the rate lowers." (FM at audio-rate Rate, classic phasing at low Rate — one continuous sweep.)
- Set a Rate in the audio region, then **sweep Center** to tailor the resonant tone.
- **Mod Mix toward Spread** = a cleaner effect (distributes modulation between Center and Spread).
- (Per manual/specs: ULTRA raises max modulation speed from 8.37 kHz to 477.3 kHz; up to 12 notch pairs.)

## Rig relevance (drone/doom/shoegaze)
- **Static pad → motion + mid-tame:** Phasis on a held drone adds slow modular movement and pulls down honky mids in one move (mix-friendly).
- **Vowel wall:** max Notches + high Feedback on a sustained source = formant/vocal-like resonance.
- **ULTRA on noise/drone:** drive a white-noise or granular bed into a *tone* (phaser-as-synth) — then sweep Rate down to settle from FM grit into slow phasing as a long evolving transition.
- Slow Rate + moderate Feedback = the seasick psychedelic pad movement under a wall.
