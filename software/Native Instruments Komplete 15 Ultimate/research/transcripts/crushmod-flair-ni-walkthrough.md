# FLAIR — official NI walkthrough (distilled)

Flair = a flanger that does "exciting resonator, harmonization and tape-style flanging." The most distinctive Mod Pack unit. **NOT confirmed installed on disk (Mod Pack referenced; only Bite verified).**

## Core moves shown
- On a Razor chord preset ("Make It Bright"): adding Flair instantly makes the sound more intense.
- **Width move:** Rate down a little + Amount and Mix all the way up = "a really strong stereo-width-enhancing modulation effect that's not too obvious but still really pushes the sound into the sides." (The subtle shoegaze widener.)
- **Feedback up** = "that characteristic metallic flanging feel."
- **Pitch knob** "adjusts the fundamental frequency of the first flanger voice in semitones" — used to TUNE the effect.
- **Low feedback = slurred sound; high feedback = ethereal, dreamy quality.**
- **Pitch down + Rate up high** = "dramatic sci-fi effects."

## Modes & Voices (where it gets interesting)
- **Voices knob fades from 1 to 4 flanger voices.**
- **Standard mode:** voices stack as a chord (the default so far).
- **Thru Zero mode:** "phase cancellation with inverted feedback... quite drastic flanging where the signal can disappear completely."
- **Scan mode:** "rather than playing all the voices simultaneously, the voices are 'scanned' between, creating an arpeggiator-style effect" — harmonic relationship set by the **Chord** control.
- **Chord** affects the other modes too, "especially with non-harmonic material like a drum loop."
- **Resonator move:** "try this with almost no modulation for a resonator effect" — Voices + Chord + high feedback + near-zero Rate/Amount = a tuned comb resonator (a chord rings out of any source).
- (Per manual: Detune ±60 cents per voice; 24 chord settings.)

## Rig relevance (shoegaze/drone)
- **Subtle widener:** Standard, Rate low, Amount+Mix high, low feedback → push a banjo/guitar/pad to the sides without obvious whoosh.
- **Tuned resonator wall:** near-zero modulation + 4 Voices + a Chord + high feedback → a chord rings out of a drone/noise; Pitch sets the root. This is the doom/drone "harmonic jet-plane" move.
- **Thru Zero** for dramatic disappearing-signal flanging swells into a chorus/reverb.
- Chord control on **non-harmonic material** (drum loops, noise, granular textures) imposes a pitched ring — useful on degraded/sampled hardware.
