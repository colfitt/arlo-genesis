# BITE — official NI walkthrough (distilled)

The definitive control-by-control tour of Bite. Bite is the bitcrusher/resampler element of Crush Pack; it is ALSO available free/standalone and ships inside Komplete (the only Crush-Pack unit confirmed installed on this rig's disk).

## Layout
Two sections. **Left = resampler** (affects the *frequency/sample-rate* of the sound). **Right = bit resolution** (affects *bit depth*).

## Resampler section (left)
- **Sample rate (Freq):** drop it to degrade. Demo reduces it on an "urban beat" → vintage-sampler grit. Range 0.1 kHz → 44.1 kHz.
- **Jitter:** "makes the resampling clock imprecise, which results in noise artifacts." This is the broken-tape/clock-instability control — turn up for unstable, noisy resampling.
- **Pre filter:** low-pass *before* resampling. "Reduces the amount of high-end content that is allowed through and could potentially produce aliasing." Tame fizz at the source.
- **Post filter:** low-pass *after* resampling+quantization. "Reduces aliasing in the output" — kills the ringing/metallic top end the crush creates.
- **Filters track the resampling frequency.** "In the middle position the filters are perfectly tuned according to the set resampling frequency to sound like a vintage sampler." (The default middle = the authentic-sampler sweet spot.)
- A little **saturation** can be added in the resampler stage.

## Bit-reduction section (right)
- **Bits/Depth:** lower it → "the sound is breaking up... very low bit resolution." 2–16 bits.
- **DC mode:** "DC gives the sample sustain" vs. letting the signal break up / fade to silence. (Sustains buzz vs. allows decay to silence.)
- **Dither:** subtly adds noise to mask quantization distortion — but "in this example the amount is cranked up for creative purposes" (i.e. usable as a grit/noise generator).
- **Crunch:** "simulates a recording signal with very bad levels — as you have a low signal it feels like you've recorded with a lower bitrate. The parameter is continuous, so this is similar to a stepless bit-depth control." (Smooth, zipper-free way to dial resolution.)
- **Expand:** "suppresses low-level artifacts — used to clean up dirty signals." (Quantization-distribution control: clean up the tail.)

## Key takeaway
"The real beauty of the plugin unfolds when combining the resampler and bit-crusher sections" — the character comes from the interaction, not either alone. Demoed across drums, synth bass (Atari → industrial beast), and guitar ("crush your guitar in a way no amp allows").

## Rig relevance (drone/doom/shoegaze)
- Middle-position Pre/Post filters = the "warm vintage sampler" baseline; only open them past tracking when you WANT the harsh aliasing top.
- Jitter is the unique move here — clock instability on a held drone = wobbling, broken-tape pitch noise.
- Crunch = the smooth, automatable lo-fi-recording knob (no zipper) — ideal for slow degrade arcs.
- DC mode lets you choose: sustained buzz under a drone vs. letting crushed tails fade.
