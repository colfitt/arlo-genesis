# RAUM full hands-on walkthrough (auto-sub, distilled)

A complete knob-by-knob run on a pad (NI "Pads of [Union]" expansion), then presets. Confirms
manual facts independently and adds practical technique.

## Confirms (independent of manual)
- **Freeze is on GROUNDED and AIRY only — "cosmic doesn't have this."** (Matches manual; the
  nav's "Cosmic + Freeze" pairing is wrong.)
- Freeze: get Mix to 100% so you hear it indefinitely, hit Freeze, stop transport → the frozen
  tone "will continue forever… a lovely drone in the background." You can then ADD feedback/
  predelay on top of the frozen bed for evolving texture.
- Two separate level knobs: **Mix** = dry/wet (how much algorithm) and **Reverb** = how intense
  the reverb is. (On a send, Mix to 100%.)
- Low/High cut = "exactly what it says." Damp = "use it as a filter essentially" (HF rolloff of tail).

## Technique gold
- **Metallic / tonal texture:** turn the **Predelay Feedback up** — "the feedback loop is now
  actually generating a sound which you otherwise didn't have… progressively more intense." Low
  predelay + feedback = metallic comb ring on a pad/banjo. He gets a "nice metallic sound thanks
  to the feedback and the pre-delay."
- **Hans-Zimmer pitch-bend reverb:** *"that classic Hans Zimmer sort of pitch-bending reverb… is
  essentially just modulate the size — just move it and it'll sound awesome."* → **automate Size**
  for the rising/bending swell. On Airy it's "all nice and smooth, no clicks or pops" — Airy is the
  algorithm for clean Size-automation sweeps; Grounded is grittier/smaller.
- **Predelay Sync rhythms:** straight / dotted / triplet / quintuplet / sextuplet, 16th/8th/quarter.
  Triplet predelay used to lock the generated echo to a track's groove. You can **automate Size
  AFTER** the sound is in — unlike a normal delay, RAUM's "delay" (predelay+feedback) keeps
  responding to Size moves post-hoc.
- Algorithm size-ranges (his framing): Grounded = rooms→halls; Airy = halls→arenas; **Cosmic =
  "massive, absolutely colossal spaces… ridiculous"** (the abstract/soundscape extreme).

## Presets named (worth knowing, in the "Large" category he favours)
- **Pluck Wash** — long lush wash for plucks (banjo-relevant).
- **Orbital** — long decay; he removes damp + low to open it up; toggles Sparse/Dense to hear
  density difference (Dense = crowded tail, Sparse = dispersed).
- **Echoes Yellow Memories** — "interesting," vocal/ad-lib texture wash.
- Others mentioned: **Vocal Echoes**, **Walls**, **Table-Tennis**, **Synth Particles**.
