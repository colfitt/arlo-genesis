AudioSEX · "Plugins to emulate the effects of lossy encoding?"

The key "honest niche" thread — maps Lossy against its alternatives AND against the
free DIY method (actually encoding to low-bitrate MP3 and back). Establishes that
Lossy is the *realtime, tweakable, automatable* version of a thing you can also do
crudely for free.

## On Lossy specifically
- One user's UI gripe ("their UI puts me off — not saying it's bad"), but another:
  "had good MP3 results with this." So it works; taste-dependent.
- Positioned vs **Lese Codec (free)**, which one user calls "the best one I've ever
  heard in terms of musicality," with **Goodhertz Lossy "a close second."** (i.e.
  Lossy is top-tier musically, but a free rival is competitive.)

## Alternatives map (for the comparison section)
**Free / budget:**
- **Codec by Lese** (free) — "best in terms of musicality"; Lossy a close second.
- **Unfiltered Audio LO-FI-AF** — recreates "MP3 chirps" / "MP3ification."
- **MAIM by Wilder Garden Audio** (free) — codec/degrade (also surfaced on KVR).
- **DtBlkFX** (free, 32-bit) — FFT-based artifacts.
- **LAME VST** — an *actual* MP3 encoder as a plugin (GitHub).
**Paid:**
- **iZotope Ozone Codec Preview** — real codec simulation w/ adjustable bitrate
  (more of a checker than a creative effect — owner already has Ozone).
- **Aberrant DSP Digitalis** ($24) — versatile lossy/degrade emulation; the
  most-cited direct Lossy alternative (also on KVR).
- **ADPTR Streamliner** — transcode/codec-preview-based.

## The DIY method (the honest baseline)
Several users: skip the plugin and just **encode the bounce to MP3 (V0, or 192/320
kbps) and re-import** to the DAW — "sounds like I want it." The catch they all
flag: it's **destructive and offline** — "prevents real-time A/B comparison during
mixing." → This is exactly the gap Lossy fills: realtime, automatable, recallable,
and tweakable far past what a real encoder gives you (packet loss, phase jitter,
verb, slow speed for smear). The DIY route is more "authentic" to a single bitrate
but can't be ridden/automated or pushed to extremes.
