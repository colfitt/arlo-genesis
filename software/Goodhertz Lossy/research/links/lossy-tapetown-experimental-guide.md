The most usage-focused blog source — frames Lossy squarely for experimental/lo-fi/
vaporwave/IDM and gives concrete preset+recipe pointers. Also the source of the
artist-usage examples (treat those as MEDIUM until cross-checked — see
lossy-artists-sourced.md; Lorde & Jane Remover are corroborated, Omar Apollo is NOT).

## What it is
Emulates the sound of low-bitrate digital audio — artifacts from MP3, broken
cellphones, and streaming video. A creative tool, NOT a real codec. Mimics "the
crunchy artifacts of low-bitrate MP3s or bad internet streams." Affordable, light
on CPU. Lo-fi hip-hop, vaporwave, and experimental genres suit its "glitchy digital
crunch." Honest caveat from the article: a *limited* preset library — it leans on
simplicity and niche appeal rather than a deep factory bank.

## Named presets / recipes it calls out
- **"Low-Fi" preset + high jitter** → MP3-like artifacts on vocals for a glitchy,
  retro-digital vocal sound (experimental-pop aesthetic).
- **"Bad Connection" preset** → on synth pads for an internet-streamed/degraded,
  streaming-quality texture (vaporwave).
- **"Internet Noise" setting on an empty track** → atmospheric lo-fi ambience floor
  layered under the mix (a noise-bed trick).
- **"Dial-Up Disaster" preset** → degraded textures with quirky digital character.
- **Heavy bit reduction on drum loops** → crunchy, compressed beats for experimental
  hip-hop / IDM.
- **High jitter on SFX** → unsettling digital noise for cinematic glitch transitions.
