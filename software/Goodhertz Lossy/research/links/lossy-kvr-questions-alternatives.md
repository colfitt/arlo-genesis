KVR Audio · "Goodhertz Lossy - some questions & any alternatives?" (Effects Forum)

Short thread (4 posts). Most useful for: (1) the OP's own overlap question — he
already owns RC-20, D16 Decimator, and Speakerbox and asks whether Lossy is "kinda
unique" despite them (answer in practice: yes — none of those do codec/MP3
artifacts), and (2) the budget alternative + licensing notes.

## Alternatives offered
- **Aberrant DSP Digitalis** — "an excellent alternative" at **$24, no copy
  protection** (the recurring budget-Lossy recommendation).
- **MAIM by Wilder Garden Audio** — a **free** option.
- **Plogue Chipcrusher** — "my personal fav bit crusher / sample-rate reducer"
  (note: bitcrush ≠ codec artifacts — different axis, the owner already covers
  bitcrush with NI Bite).

## Licensing / copy protection (gotcha note)
Goodhertz: first load asks you to log into your goodhertz.com account, **but there
is offline authorization** — email support a challenge code, they email back a
response code, fast turnaround. (Account login on first use; offline path exists.)

## Broader Goodhertz-thread sentiment (related threads)
- Lossy is "a special-interest effect that emulates the chirping side effects of
  MP3 encoding — you could get a similar sound from spectral tools, but this is
  spot on."
- "A love/hate thing — most of the time I don't like it, but when I do, I love it."
  → Reinforces it as a *specialized character* tool, not an everyday insert.
