## Origin story (official)
Lossy began when producer **Tyler Duncan** asked Goodhertz for a drum fill that
sounded "**like the year 2001 — a low-bitrate digital MP3 ripped from KaZaA**." The
result: a plugin that delivers "artifacts of heavily compressed audio in a highly-
tweakable realtime plugin." Official aesthetic targets: "streaming music on a 56k
modem, an MP3 ripped from a CD-R," low-quality 2007-era YouTube uploads, broken
cellphones, "the underwater cathedral."

## Official creative-appeal note
"The Goodhertz plugins in general are a lot of fun to use — very easy to use — and
really nice to look at and inspiring." Lossy's pitch: those degradation artifacts
are "infinitely desirable," not flaws to avoid.

## Automation (from goodhertz.com/faq, cross-cited)
**Any** parameter in a Goodhertz plugin — even on/off switches — can be automated
"freely and smoothly without clicks, pops, or zipper noises... push them, pull them,
LFO them." → This is why the **bitrate/Loss-Amount automation arc** (and master-bus
automation) is the headline Lossy move: you can ride Loss Amount or Loss Mode from
clean to destroyed across a section with zero zipper artifacts. (Confirmed in the
ad-libs transcript: a rap engineer automating it on the master fader.)

## Artist usage — VERIFIED vs UNVERIFIED
**VERIFIED (two independent sources each):**
- **Lorde — "Shapeshifter"** — Lossy used to **crunch up the background ambiance**.
  Source: Tape Notes podcast Ep. 158 (with producer **Jim-E Stack**), echoed by the
  Tapetown writeup. The closest documented use to this rig's aesthetic: degrading a
  *texture/ambience layer under the main vocal*, not the lead.
- **Jane Remover — *Frailty*** — Lossy used on the **master bus** / for texture &
  depth throughout the record. Source: Equipboard + multiple gear blogs. The
  hyperpop/shoegaze-adjacent, "whole-mix degrade" use — directly relevant to
  master-bus codec-crush.

**UNVERIFIED (single source only — DO NOT assert):**
- **Omar Apollo — "Evergreen"** (vocal filtering) and a guitar-chain-distortion
  example — appear only in the Tapetown blog with no corroboration. Flag, don't cite
  as fact.

**Honest gap:** no verifiable *named drone/doom/shoegaze* artist credit for Lossy.
Relevance to the rig is by **technique** (master-bus crush à la Jane Remover; under-
the-drone ambience crush à la the Lorde example) + the codec aesthetic itself, NOT a
documented genre-peer endorsement.
