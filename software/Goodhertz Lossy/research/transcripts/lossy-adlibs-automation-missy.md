Missy (mixing tutorials) · "How To Mix PRO ADLIBS With Only ONE PLUGIN — GoodHertz LOSSY Lo-Fi Rap Vocals Effects" · 2023-09

The single most practical Lossy tutorial found: a full session walkthrough (in Pro
Tools) of using Lossy as a vocal/ad-lib character processor, leaning hard on
parameter **automation**. Genre is rap, but every technique transfers directly to
the degraded/"recorded-wrong" aesthetic — the core move is automating a clean signal
into a degraded one for contrast and momentum.

## What Lossy is (his framing)
"This plugin is kind of like modeling data compression with MP3." His analogy:
when you download a type-beat off YouTube and run it through a YouTube-to-MP3
converter, you lose a little clarity/detail and pick up digital artifacts —
"sometimes you feel like you're losing a little bit of quality." Lossy takes that
same idea and says those digital artifacts "are not really bad — they can actually
be used to your advantage." He praises Goodhertz plugins specifically for being
*fun and fast* to use ("like playing with an Xbox 360 controller, just clicking
buttons and moving stuff around") — versus the older McDSP FutzBox, which he
abandoned because it "was too complicated in the process of being creative."

## The controls, in his words
- **Reverb** — built-in, "really gritty nasty sound that complements the rest of
  the settings." You pick **pre or post** (before/after the loss processing), the
  **amount**, and the **Decay**. He automates the verb amount a lot.
- **Filter** — "how you're going to be shaping your ad-libs." Normal filter +
  pickable **slope**: "the more aggressive the slope, the more it really cuts —
  kind of gives you a more abrupt, jagged, edgy sound." (Octave-per-slope.)
- **Loss type** — Standard, Inverse, Phase Jitter, Packet Repeat, Packet Loss,
  etc. "It's degrading the sound but it's using MP3 compression... when you take a
  song down to MP3 you lose a little clarity, a little detail."
- **Amount** — the MP3 data-compression amount ("how much you're getting").
- **Speed** — "how quickly do you want the data compression to be working... if you
  want something a little bit smaller, you start to hear the FX and the pops and
  the clicks in between." (= slower Loss Speed → more audible glitch artifacts.)
- **Gate / Gate Threshold** — his favorite for ad-libs: "it helps tighten up the
  ad-libs, makes them more jagged, more like a jack-in-the-box peekaboo." Praises
  Goodhertz for including a gate ("they know music") because on a degrade plugin it
  makes the effect "more surprising," creeping up from behind. (= mutes the
  artifact/noise tail in the gaps.)
- **Stereo modes** — Stereo / Joint / Mono: "dictates how the loss is working —
  only in the mid channel, summed down both channels, or independent L/R lo-fi-ing."
  Independent-stereo loss gave him "a little VHS-type stutter sound... so dope."
- **Gain** — output level.

## The signature workflows (all automation-driven)
1. **Clean → dirty contrast on ad-libs.** Keep one pass of ad-libs clean, then
   "bring the Lassie in" on the answer pass — automate Lossy on/amount so the
   effect drops in. "Give them a clean floor, now I'm automating Lossy to give a
   dirty floor." Creates variety + a "TikTok moment" (the listener's favorite
   part). Clean-the-whole-way = no favorite part.
2. **Mid-word transition.** Automate Lossy to come in *partway through a single
   word/ad-lib* ("I'ma" clean, "pop" dirty within the same ad-lib) for a
   surprising in-line degrade. He deliberately rides the automation "a little
   wacky / human," not robotic.
3. **Lossy as a reverb throw.** Instead of automated sends, he puts Lossy on a
   dedicated throw track with "hella reverb" (high verb amount) and drags vocal
   words to it — a gritty, degraded reverb throw instead of a clean one.
4. **Lyric-aware wetting.** He listens to the lyric: on an important/emotional
   word he keeps it clear; on a repeated word ("truth," second hook) he "wets it
   up" with Lossy because "we don't need to hear it clearly again."
5. **Master-fader automation for a VHS vibe.** He runs Lossy on the **master fader**
   and does "crazy automation" on one specific word — assigns plugin automation,
   sets the lane to latch, and rides the parameters live ("hopping on that Xbox
   joystick") for a "VHS, late-night-in-the-club" degrade on just that moment.

## Presets he name-drops
"Bad cell-phone connection" (really sounds like it's jittering in and out),
"dial-up" lo-fi, and a plain MP3 sound — "a bunch of dope presets."

## The honest 'why'
"I got a hundred million EQs, a hundred million compressors, tape plugins — but
sometimes when you see a dope character plugin like this you've got to try it...
it's very different." Use it when the lyric/vibe is "dirty," not to make everything
clean and pristine.
