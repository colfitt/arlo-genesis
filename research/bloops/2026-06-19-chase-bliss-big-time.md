# Bloop — Chase Bliss Big Time
Date: 2026-06-19 · Category: gear · Corpus: gear/Chase Bliss Big Time

The Big Time corpus was already mature going in, so this pass was a depth-and-correction sweep rather than a discovery one. The wins: it fills the two high-priority gap-modes (MOD and LOOP) with reproducible recipes, hardens the rackmount-lineage story past name-dropping, and — most importantly — catches a wrong footswitch mapping in an existing patch and several lineage over-reaches that had crept into the recon framing. No new artist-attributed numeric recipes exist anywhere on the web; the corpus's first-principles-only status holds.

## What we already knew  (from recon)

- **Lineage is first-party documented.** Big Time models early-'80s rackmount hybrid delays, specifically the **Lexicon PCM42** (mis-calibrated limiter → COMPRESSED / "Blue Mode") and the **Ursa Major Space Station** (→ CLUSTER), reuses EAE circuit blocks (Sending, Clean, Halberd, Glaive), and nods to Snyder's circuit-bent DigiTech PDS 20/20. The journalist-cited PCM70 / SDD-3000 attribution was already debunked via a manual-vs-web flag.
- **Architecture.** Four-mode structure — Mod (3–46ms) / Short (46–736ms) / Long (736ms–12.2s) / Loop. One shared-control stereo engine (NOT dual-independent), spanning chorus / flange / reverb / comp / delay / looper via STATE × VOICING × MODE × SCALE × CLUSTER. An analog preamp sits at the input and an analog limiter sits **inside the feedback loop**.
- **Reproducible recipes are deep.** 36 patch files in `Patches/Chase Bliss Big Time/` plus 64 rig-chains referencing Big Time, each with clock-face fader values, STATE/VOICING/MODE settings, MIDI recall (PC/CC27), and honest 🟢factory / 🟣designed source tags. Owner-rig combinations (Generation Loss, MOOD MkII, Blooper, Chroma, H90, fuzzes, Onward/Microcosm, samplers) are extensively mapped.
- **Known gaps:** MOD mode under-covered (one patch), LOOP mechanics thin as actionable recipes (one patch), the Object Worship developer podcast unmined, and the community recipe ecosystem empty as of 2026-06-14.

## New findings  (verified)

### Lens: identity / lineage — rackmount ancestry grounded in behavior

- **The PCM42 → COLOR/limiter lineage is real, and the "Andy Wallace trick" is the documented technique behind it** (confirmed). The PCM42 carries a pair of CLM50 optical compander chips, only one in the limiter path; the documented abuse is driving the input hard while pulling output down so the built-in overload compressor "bites down" harder the harder you drive it, and a failing CLM50 gives "fritzy, fartypants" sag — the exact failure-mode Big Time weaponizes. Maps to the manual's gain rule: high COLOR → "echoes bonk into the limiter immediately." Honest caveat: Wallace himself reportedly used the PCM42 "as a simple delay, the one he could reach first," so the named *trick* is partly folklore, but the limiter *behavior* it describes is the real ancestor. [EAE blog; gearspace PCM42 threads; vintagedigital]
- **EAE scopes the PCM42 influence narrowly to the COMPRESSED / "Blue Mode" state** — not to the whole pedal. (See Flagged: the recon's "founding technique of the whole pedal" framing was refuted.) [EAE blog]
- **Space Station SST-282 mechanism is Christopher Moore's modulated-feedback-tap trick** (mechanism confirmed; ancestry scope corrected). One ~255ms delay line, 15 summed feedback taps + 8 audition taps, ~3.5s; multi-tap feedback is normally capped at 1/(taps) but Moore modulated the taps to push feedback gain ~3× past stability, yielding spectral smearing, modulation noise (62µs steps, no smoothing) and "spooky voices at high feedback." EAE confirms the Space Station was a **"spiritual influence on Big Time's Cluster slider"** — an inspiration scoped to CLUSTER, not the limiter architecture. [Valhalla DSP / Sean Costello 2010; vintagedigital; EAE blog]
- **The factory preset bank encodes the lineage map.** Presets #1 COMPRESSED CHORUS, #2 SLAP/DOUBLE and #5 BROKEN DYNAFLANGE all live in MOD mode (3–46ms) — the under-recipe-ized "multi-effect but not exactly" territory where Big Time is a chorus/flanger engine, not a delay. [gearnews; synthtopia]
- **CB's own launch blog gives a clean three-archetype thesis:** a "saturated wall of sound," a "melting double-tracking chorus" (names the under-covered MOD voice), and "Thermae-style echo sequences." Architecture stated verbatim: "an analog preamp at the very front of the circuit, and a limiter in the feedback path." [chasebliss.com/big-time-blog; gearnews]
- **VOICING is the per-ancestor tone selector** (first-party, Superbooth): HiFi = flat ("useful for modulation"); Focus = gentle band-pass; Warm = brick-wall ~10k like vintage digital racks (PCM42 X2 character); Analog = callback to EAE's Sending V2, gentler ~4kHz slope. [Superbooth transcript; manual]
- **No new rackmount attributions surfaced.** First-party naming stays PCM42 + Space Station only; no SDE-3000, TC 2290, AMS DMX, PCM70 or SDD-3000 claim is supported. Snyder's framing is generic-by-design ("a 1980s rack delay from a parallel universe"), which is *why* journalists keep mis-applying broad attributions. The corpus's debunk stands and needs no revision. [chasebliss.com; EAE blog; gearnews]

### Lens: patches — MOD-mode recipes (fills the #1 gap)

> Honesty flag for all three: the control *routing* (MODE/STATE/VOICING/SCALE/MOTION choices) is first-party-sourced; exact clock-face fader numbers are dialable DOUG builds, not published.

- **MOD-mode chorus / DRY-KILL vibrato** (factory #1 COMPRESSED CHORUS territory). MODE Mod, SCALE Off, MOTION Sine; manual: "When SCALE is off you will get classic, bendy pitch modulation, useful for creating chorusing, flanging, and tape-style warble." Dry+wet beating = chorus; pull DRY to zero (alt under WET) = true vibrato — the "kill dry for vibrato/lo-fi" move Snyder calls out at Superbooth. STATE Compressed for the dreamy factory #1 character. [manual; Superbooth]
- **MOD-mode "Broken Dynaflange"** (factory #5 territory). MODE Mod, STATE #!&% misbias (the "starved" flanger character) or Saturated, MOTION Env so the flange sweep tracks playing dynamics ("fills the gaps between your notes… react to loudness"). Low COLOR so soft playing stays clean and only digging in starves the flanger. Distinct from the existing `mod-slap-comb-flange-double.md` (Compressed, Sine, clean slap) and from both existing Env patches (which live in Short/Long, not MOD). [factory-presets link; Superbooth; CC chart]
- **Env-mode sensitivity lever the existing Env patches miss** (confirmed verbatim from manual p.33): "The envelope detection follows both the input and delay line. Turning up COLOR and/or FEEDBACK will make the envelope more sensitive, because the envelope also responds to your echoes." So COLOR/FEEDBACK — not just DEPTH — set how hair-trigger and self-feeding the dive/swell is. Second confirmed behavior: **with SCALE ON, Env becomes envelope-controlled STEP sequencing** — "waits for a transient or a distinct note, then moves the sequence forward… like a version of Step controlled by your playing," i.e. a play-triggered Thermae-style pitch-stepper, distinct from the SCALE-Off dive-bomb. [manual p.33, p.41]

### Lens: patches — LOOP-mode mechanics (fills the #2 gap)

- **LOOP-mode evolution recipe is buildable and fully manual-confirmed.** Playback is frozen ("will not change or deteriorate, regardless of how you change the controls"); overdub makes all faders live ("if CLUSTER is turned up, your loops will gradually scatter; if FEEDBACK is turned down, your loops will fade away"). Critically: "Big Time switches over to digital feedback behind the scenes… changing STATE or adjusting TEXTURE will not be heard until you start overdubbing again," and "For stable, 'traditional' looping use the Digital state. Every other state is designed to change and disintegrate over time." TIME sets the fidelity/length trade: center ≈ 48s @ ~44kHz, up ≈ 3.2 min @ ~11kHz, down ≈ 12s @ ~172kHz. So the **OVERDUB pass is the difference between a frozen bed and a self-disintegrating one** — VOICING (Warm/Analog filter-carve), CLUSTER (scatter), FEEDBACK (fade) and STATE all bite, but only on re-overdub. [manual; gearnews corroborates structure]
- **Loop-disintegration drone** weaponizes the digital-feedback quirk as a feature: build in Long, switch to Loop (audio carries over), ride playback as a frozen bed, then set STATE Saturated/#!&% + push TEXTURE (hear nothing yet) and re-engage OVERDUB to write progressive degradation into each new layer while old layers persist. Distinct from `frippertronics-carve-loop.md`, which deliberately treats STATE as cosmetic and carves only via VOICING. [Superbooth; manual; existing patch]
- **MIDI loop choreography:** discrete CCs exist for hands-free control — RECORD CC104, PLAY CC105, DELETE CC106, STOP CC107, and the PLAY/DUB Options toggle CC42. [CC chart]

### Lens: combinations — owner-rig routing (verified mechanics)

- **Big Time uses full-size 5-pin DIN MIDI (IN + THRU), default channel 2.** This means a Digitakt 2 / Octatrack MkII clocks it with a direct DIN run — **no TRS adapter or CB MIDIBox** (unlike MOOD/Blooper/Generation Loss, which take MIDI over ¼" TRS). It can also be clock *master* (CC110>0, THRU→OUT, 60–240 bpm), free-run (CC111=1), or set tap subdivision against incoming clock (CC54). ⚠ This corrects an error in an existing chain — see Suggested corpus edits. [CC chart; gearnews; chasebliss.com]
- **DRY KILL + DRY CLEAN make Big Time a purpose-built parallel/aux/reamp box.** DRY KILL removes dry entirely (aux-send / reamp); DRY CLEAN strips COLOR from the dry path only (wet stays saturated, dry stays clean); WET is a wet *volume* with separate dry volume. Enables: Elektron aux-send return (mute/solo/EQ the wet independently), wet-dry-wet three-amp rigs (per-channel analog preamp+limiter), and reamping pure wet repeats into the Blooper. [Mark Johnston transcript]
- **Delay-into-reverb voicing (Big Time → Big Sky).** Big Time first (MODE Long, STATE Compressed for articulate repeats, DIFFUSE low so taps stay distinct) → Big Sky (Cloud/Bloom, low mix). The limiter being in the *feedback* loop means repeats arrive already gain-shaped, so the reverb blooms discrete taps rather than washing mush. Inverse (reverb→delay) with Big Time DIFFUSE/FEEDBACK high catches and smears the tail. Avoid both at high diffusion/decay simultaneously (stereo field collapses). [Mark Johnston transcript; chasebliss blog]
- **Big Time ↔ Blooper are complementary loopers.** Big Time → Blooper "captures the destruction" (each Blooper layer grabs already-aged repeats); Blooper → Big Time "applies the destruction" (clean structural loop on Blooper, re-colored live by DIFFUSE/CLUSTER/0.5X). Order by whether degradation should be baked in (Big Time first) or live/reversible (Big Time last). [Perfect Circuit; GPX; existing patch]
- **SPREAD vocabulary for stereo placement:** Off = true-stereo/dual-mono; Mode-2 = gentle widening of the primary delay; Top = ping-pong on the final added delays; CLUSTER + MOTION = very wide without full ping-pong. Mono-in auto-engages MISO. Place Big Time as the first stereo-izer so downstream inherits a coherent field; per-channel preamp+limiter gives genuine independent coloration in hard-panned dual-amp setups. [Mark Johnston transcript; kvraudio]
- **Controlled self-oscillation:** limiter is per-channel and in the feedback path with STATE selecting three overdrive levels — ride FEEDBACK toward max with Saturated/misbias and the limiter saturates the runaway into a sustained musical oscillation rather than a digital scream; TILT steers its weight; hold MODE ~2s = panic reset; "Fun mode" AUX LEFT = real-time 0.5X half-time drop, RIGHT = clear buffer (clean 2:1 against a master clock). [gearnews; chasebliss blog; Mark Johnston transcript]

### Lens: comparison — where it sits against peers

- **Closest spiritual peer is the Oto Machines BIM, not a Strymon/Meris/Empress workstation.** BIM is first-party "a 12-bit stereo delay inspired by studio delay processors built in the early 80s… mainly analog processing with 12-bit AD/DA," reprocessing feedback through analog+12-bit circuitry — the same design thesis as Big Time (digital core wrapped in analog, early-'80s hybrid target, bit-reduction + feedback-loop coloration). Big Time's edge: stereo generators (CLUSTER/SPREAD/MISO), motorized-fader recall + deep MIDI, SCALE pitch-quantizer, dynamics-reactive misbias. BIM's edge: a more focused single-box vintage voice at a fraction of the price. ⚠ The *specific* claim that Snyder praises BIM/BAM on the podcast could not be verified — see Flagged. [otomachines.com; ModWiggler threads]
- **The single differentiator vs every workstation:** character lives in an analog limiter/compressor *inside the feedback loop*, ridden in real time by playing dynamics (STATE) — not in selectable DSP algorithms. "Play gently and it stays clean; dig in and you get texture." No TimeLine machine, LVX module, Echosystem algorithm or H90 block is a dynamics-reactive analog destruction engine. Reach for Big Time when the delay should fall apart in response to *performance*, not preset. [Superbooth; EAE blog]
- **Within CB's own catalog:** Big Time is the stereo, digital-core successor that absorbs Thermae's pitch-sequencing (→ SCALE + Square MOTION, "very reminiscent of our old pal Thermae") and Tonal Recall's analog warmth (→ Analog VOICING) into one box — arriving right as Tonal Recall was retired and Thermae left as the lone CB BBD delay. [GPX; Superbooth]
- **CLUSTER + DIFFUSE = "reverb but not quite"** is a distinct competitive niche: ambient space *without* a reverb algorithm, so in a rig with a dedicated reverb (the owner's H90) it's the right division of labor — let Big Time make diffuse stereo space from delay taps, let the H90 do true reverb — whereas LVX/Echosystem would duplicate the reverb you already own. [Superbooth; EAE blog]
- **GFI Specular Tempus vs Big Time = open platform vs opinionated voice:** Specular Tempus lets *you* patch external FX into the feedback loop; Big Time bakes its coloration (analog limiter + TILT EQ) permanently into the loop. [SoundOnSound; Superbooth]
- **Decision summary for the owner's ambient/lo-fi/post-rock work:** reach for Big Time when degradation must be *performed* and stereo-diffuse, when you want 12-bit/half-rate lo-fi in a feedback loop (0.5X), reverb-adjacent diffusion without duplicating the H90, Thermae-style pitch cascades, or motorized-fader live recall. Reach for a competitor for many discrete delay types (TimeLine/Echosystem), modular self-design (LVX), reverse/shimmer/pitch-perfect + true reverb (H90/TimeFactor), warm multi-head tape + spring (Volante), cheap dark analog slapback (DM-2W), or the same early-'80s character cheaper in a single-voice box (Oto BIM). Big Time is never "most features for the money" ($999, CB's priciest) — it's "one performable, stereo, recallable degradation engine for an all-CB MIDI-clocked board." [chasebliss.com; Superbooth]

## Flagged / uncertain

These recon claims were **refuted or scoped down** during verification. Corrections below should govern any corpus edits.

- **REFUTED — "PCM42 limiter abuse is the *founding technique of the whole pedal*; the entire COLOR/STATE/TEXTURE axis is a generalization of it."** EAE's own blog scopes the PCM42 mis-calibrated-limiter influence specifically to the **COMPRESSED / "Blue Mode" state only**. Big Time's limiter is its own analog clipping design in the feedback loop, not a clone of the PCM42's *input optical* limiter. Several supporting details were also wrong/unsourced: the PCM42 input limiter is an **optical CLM-50**, not "soft-knee"; its x2 reduces *bandwidth* (~6kHz), the cited source does not describe it as "halving the sample rate"; the "10ms crossfade loop" detail is in neither cited source. Use the narrow scope, not the grand thesis.
- **REFUTED — "PDS 20/20 is the *concrete circuit lineage* for MOD-mode chorus/flange and true-stereo."** Snyder's blog acknowledges the PDS 20/20 only as a sentimental *"love letter to my old circuit-bent PDS 20/20 that I've had since age 16"* — the misbias/lo-fi-character aesthetic, NOT an engineered circuit genealogy for MOD mode or stereo behavior. (The PDS 20/20's specs — stereo digital flanger/chorus/delay, ~2s, 13:1 sweep, infinite-repeat — are real, but no source attributes Big Time's MOD voices to its circuit.)
- **REFUTED — "CLUSTER produces metallic/comb resonances at short (MOD) times; raise CLUSTER to tune comb spacing as a Karplus-style resonator depth control."** The manual says CLUSTER in MOD mode is a **"thickener, stereo widener, and secondary modulation"** — explicitly *not* a comb/metallic resonator. ## Candidate index chunks

- Big Time uses full-size 5-pin DIN MIDI (IN + THRU), default channel 2 — clock it from a Digitakt 2 / Octatrack with a direct DIN cable, no TRS adapter or CB MIDIBox (unlike MOOD/Blooper/Generation Loss).
- In Big Time LOOP mode, playback is frozen and STATE/TEXTURE changes are inaudible (hidden digital feedback) — they only take effect on the next OVERDUB pass; VOICING Warm/Analog is the always-live carve.
- Big Time LOOP footswitches: TAP LEFT = record then set endpoint then OVERDUB↔PLAYBACK toggle; TAP RIGHT = STOP/reset; HOLD RIGHT = DELETE. (Discrete CCs: REC 104, PLAY 105, DELETE 106, STOP 107, PLAY/DUB toggle 42.)
- Big Time STEP does not function in MOD mode or LOOP mode — no footswitch sequence-stepping in either; in MOD, TAP LEFT instead toggles MOTION on/off.
- Big Time Env motion: COLOR and FEEDBACK set envelope sensitivity (the follower tracks both input and delay line); with SCALE on, Env becomes a play-triggered step sequencer (Thermae-style), with SCALE off it does continuous pitch bends/dive-bombs.

- Big Time VOICING per-ancestor tone: HiFi = flat (modulation/looping), Focus = gentle band-pass, Warm = ~10k brick-wall (PCM42 X2 character), Analog = ~4k callback to EAE Sending V2.
- The PCM42's mis-calibrated limiter inspired Big Time's COMPRESSED / "Blue Mode" state specifically (per EAE) — not the whole COLOR/STATE/TEXTURE architecture; Big Time's limiter is its own analog clipping design in the feedback loop.
- The Ursa Major Space Station was a "spiritual influence on Big Time's CLUSTER slider" (scoped to CLUSTER); CLUSTER in MOD mode acts as a thickener/stereo-widener, not a comb/metallic resonator.
