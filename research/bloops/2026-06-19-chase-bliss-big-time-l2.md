### Lens: env mechanics (Thread 2) — sensitivity + SCALE split resolved; polarity is NOT

- **Env sensitivity is set by COLOR + FEEDBACK because the follower tracks both the input AND the delay line** (confirmed verbatim, manual p.33; the cited local PDF is byte-identical to the official Squarespace-hosted copy, MD5 247793f3…). Verbatim: *"The envelope detection follows both the input and delay line. Turning up COLOR and/or FEEDBACK will make the envelope more sensitive, because the envelope also responds to your echoes."* So COLOR/FEEDBACK — not just DEPTH — govern how hair-trigger and self-feeding the Env motion is. (Honest framing note: the manual presents this as a *consequence* of the follower hearing its own echoes, not as dedicated "sensitivity levers"; DEPTH/RATE are the labelled Env alt-controls.) [manual p.33]
- **SCALE-on = play-triggered stepped/quantized sequencing; SCALE-off = play-triggered un-quantized pitch bends** (confirmed verbatim, manual). SCALE-on column: *"ENV - Envelope-controlled step sequencing."* SCALE-off column: *"ENV - Envelope-controlled time bends,"* under *"When SCALE is off you will get classic, bendy pitch modulation, useful for creating chorusing, flanging, and tape-style warble."* The real distinction is **quantization, not continuous-vs-triggered** — Env is transient/event-triggered in **both** states: *"Instead of constantly responding to your signal, it instead waits for a transient or a distinct note, then moves the sequence forward… like a version of Step (pg.41) that is controlled by your playing."* (So "Thermae-style" and "continuous" are editorial glosses, not manual wording — see Flagged.) [manual p.32–33]
- **STATE Digital removes the limiter entirely** — verbatim: *"DIGITAL - No limiter… uses a completely digital feedback path, useful when you want cleaner textures and stable, steady feedback (when looping). TEXTURE introduces aliasing and lowers the bit depth."* This first-party-confirms the env-divebomb-lead patch's rationale (Digital = no limiter pumping on top of the pitch movement) and is a useful reminder that in Digital, TEXTURE = aliasing+bit-depth, not misbias amount. [manual p.32–33]

### Lens: MOD resonator (Thread 2) — settle to manual-named, not provisional

- **A pitched/metallic comb resonator IS a manual-named MOD-mode use — the patch can be upgraded from "exploratory/provisional" to "manual-listed MOD use, dialed by physics."** This was the finding I most tried to refute and could not. Positional glyph-extraction from the manual (confirmed against a byte-identical official re-download, SHA-256 f8d72dee…) settles every sub-point:
  - MOD (3–46 ms) reads verbatim *"useful for creating modulation, **resonators**, and doubling effects"* — and "resonators" occurs **exactly once** in the entire manual, here under MOD. So MOD is the manual-named resonator mode; SHORT is the adjacent (not exclusive, not manual-named) home.
  - MOD's literal callouts are **only** "Turn on MOTION" + "Crank FEEDBACK" (HOLD RIGHT in MOD ramps COLOR+FEEDBACK to max). The often-mis-cited "Turn up CLUSTER" / "Set TIME very low" callouts are in the **adjacent SHORT column** (x-position confirmed), not MOD — do not cite them as MOD.
  - Physics holds: a short feedback delay line is a comb/Karplus resonator with peaks near 1/delay-time (at MOD's 3 ms minimum the fundamental is ~333 Hz, an audible pitch). Resonator **pitch = TIME, ring = FEEDBACK.**
  - Keep both refutations intact: **CLUSTER in MOD is a thickener/stereo-widener/secondary-modulation, NOT a comb-tuner** (verbatim: *"In MOD mode — due to the extremely short delay times — the clusters clump together into a modulated mass. In this range, CLUSTER acts like a thickener, stereo widener, and secondary modulation."*); and **STEP is non-functional in MOD and LOOP** (verbatim: *"STEP does not work in the Loop mode and Mod mode."* — in MOD, TAP LEFT instead toggles MOTION on/off). [manual MODE page (PDF p.14), Cluster page (PDF p.19), Options Menu (p.41)]
  - Honest residual: at MOD's 3–46 ms the fundamentals run ~22–333 Hz, so the resonance sits **low and bass-heavy and is prone to self-oscillation** (the analog limiter turns runaway into a sustained ring rather than a digital scream). Settle the patch to *"achievable; works, sits low, watch self-oscillation"* rather than claiming a clean high tunable comb. Factory STATE note: SATURATED is the manual's **default** destruction state, so the biting-ring (Saturated) variant is reachable without going all the way to misbias.

## Candidate index chunks

- Big Time Env sensitivity is set by COLOR and FEEDBACK because the envelope follower tracks both the input AND the delay line (manual p.33) — raise COLOR/FEEDBACK to make Env more hair-trigger.
- Big Time Env is play-/transient-triggered in both SCALE states: SCALE-on gives quantized step sequencing, SCALE-off gives un-quantized pitch bends — the difference is quantization, not continuous-vs-triggered.
- Big Time Env pitch direction is NOT a fixed polarity — with SCALE on, DEPTH set above or below TIME determines whether pitch rises or falls; the manual states no fixed attack-vs-release direction.
- "Resonators" is a manual-named MOD-mode use on Big Time (occurs once in the manual, under MOD 3–46 ms); resonator pitch = TIME, ring = FEEDBACK, movement = MOTION — but the tone sits low (~22–333 Hz) and self-oscillates easily.
- Big Time CLUSTER in MOD mode is a thickener/stereo-widener/secondary-modulation, not a comb-tuner; "Set TIME very low / Turn up CLUSTER" are SHORT-mode callouts, not MOD.
- Big Time STATE Digital removes the limiter entirely (clean digital feedback path); in Digital, TEXTURE = aliasing + bit-depth reduction, not misbias amount.
- Big Time takes native 5-pin DIN MIDI (IN + THRU, default channel 2) — clock it directly, no TRS adapter or CB MIDIBox; only MOOD MkII uses 1/4" TRS MIDI and needs the MIDIBox for 5-pin.
- Big Time MIDI CC106 = HOLD in delay modes but DELETE in Loop mode (a shared, mode-dependent CC); loop CCs are REC 104 / PLAY 105 / DELETE 106 / STOP 107.
- To approximate the Oto BIM / early-'80s rackmount character on Big Time: 0.5X ON (12-bit + half rate) + VOICING Warm or Analog + STATE Saturated + moderate COLOR/FEEDBACK.
- Big Time VOICING Warm ~10k = PCM42 X2 brickwall; Analog ~4k = EAE Sending V2 callback (Snyder, Superbooth).
- The Oto BIM is Big Time's closest peer per Oto's OWN architecture copy (analog processing + 12-bit AD/DA for the delay), not per any Snyder statement; the "Snyder praises BIM/BAM" quote is an unverified forum paraphrase.
- Object Worship has TWO relevant Snyder episodes: the 2026 launch "John Snyder Goes BIG TIME" (audio-only, names the Caroline Wave Cannon — not the BIM) and the earlier Apr 2023 "John Snyder and the Digitech PDS 20/20" (companding / Nyquist / clock-trimpot origin story).
- The dedicated Elektronauts Big Time thread (/t/.../249961) is dead (404 on HTML and .json) and was only an announcement/reaction thread — not a recipe source.
- As of 2026-06-19 (~2.5 weeks post-ship) no owner/artist-shared numeric Big Time recipes exist anywhere reachable — fourth confirmation; all corpus recipes are first-principles DOUG builds.
