Concise, accessible feature/character writeup — the clearest plain-language source on the three clip modes and the EQ. Confirms the V4/V4.5 DIODE labeling used by the manual.

**Clip modes (matches the repo manual exactly):**
- **Up = MOSFET** — "medium compression with moderate clipping."
- **Center = no diodes** — "minimal compression, no diodes engaged." The dynamic / clean-boost-and-EQ mode. *This is the mode for stacking into an already-driven signal* (op-amp only, doesn't pile compression on compression).
- **Down = Silicon** — "maximum compression with aggressive clipping." The squared-off brick.

**Gain range:** spans "from clean boost to massive distortion" on the Drive control alone — so the same box is a clean EQ/boost (Center, low Drive) and a wall (Silicon, high Drive).

**EQ:** "bass and treble shelving filters with an active, frequency-selectable midrange section," lets you "sculpt a diverse array of tones." This is the studio-EQ behavior — small moves are drastic.

**Boost:** "separate Boost footswitch provides dynamic mid-boost capability (up to 20dB gain), enabling real-time tonal shifts during performance" — i.e. a performance stomp, not a set-and-forget.

**Instrument breadth (verbatim):** "broad frequency response which makes it work equally well with guitar, baritone, and bass." Directly supports the baritone-Jazzmaster + banjo use.
