THE ONE NEW CONCRETE TECHNIQUE — "glitchy 8-bit fuzz" via stacking-into-another-drive:
- VERBATIM (Equipboard editorial): "One of the funniest things to do with the Electronic Audio Experiments Longsword is to max out volume, drive, and boost, and drive that into another overdrive pedal (like a Soul Food or DS-1) to absolutely overload it and create a weird glitchy 8-bit sounding fuzz."
  → Longsword EVERYTHING-maxed (Level max, Drive max, Boost knob max + Boost footswitch ON) slamming a downstream drive til it gates/sputters into broken-8-bit fuzz. This is the inverse of the rig's usual "Longsword as restrained tone-shaper AFTER the fuzz" placement — here the Longsword is the FRONT slammer. On-aesthetic for the "recorded-wrong"/degraded lens (gated, sputtery, broken). In this rig the downstream "another overdrive" = the Oxford Drive (the one-button brick after it).

CONTROL LABELS confirmed (Equipboard, matches manual):
- Diode/Clip switch: Up = MOSFET (medium compression), Center = no diodes (minimal compression), Down = Silicon (maximum compression).
- Shift selects the Mid pot's center frequency (1kHz up / 300Hz down).
- Drive = "light grit to heavy overdrive"; Boost is routed BEFORE the drive section (pre-gain), so it adds gain/saturation more than a clean volume jump.
