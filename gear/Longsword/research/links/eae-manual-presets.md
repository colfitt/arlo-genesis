THE authoritative settings source: EAE's own V4.5 Technical Manual ships with EIGHT named factory "Suggested Settings," each with a printed knob diagram plus a one-line description. Knob layout (from the diagrams): TOP row L→R = Level, Drive, Low; BOTTOM row L→R = High, Mid, Boost; two mini-toggles between the rows = DIODE (left) and SHIFT (right); Boost-channel LED icon bottom-right. Clip modes on V4.5 = MOSFET (up) / none (center) / silicon asymmetric (down). Shift = 1kHz up / 300Hz down.

Knob positions below are read off the manual's diagrams (clock-face) — QUOTED descriptions are verbatim; the numeric clock positions are my reading of the line drawings (±, treat as approximate).

--- THE EIGHT FACTORY PRESETS ---

(a) QUEST HOOK — "It's dangerous to go alone! Take this. This preset is a great starting point for a wide variety of instrument and amp combinations. Use the Boost to take it over the top." [VERBATIM]
   Diagram: all six knobs ≈ noon. DIODE up (MOSFET). SHIFT up (1kHz). Boost off.
   → The neutral home base. Equivalent to the manual's day-one "everything at noon" starting point.

(b) ANDURIL — "A classy, refined flavor of gain with smoother highs and softer compression. Try pushing your amp with the Level knob." [VERBATIM]
   Diagram: Level high (≈2–3 o'clock), Drive ≈ noon, Low ≈ noon; High ≈ noon, Mid ≈ noon, Boost up (≈2 o'clock). DIODE up (MOSFET). SHIFT up.
   → Low-gain, high-output "clean-ish boost into the amp" voice. MOSFET = the softer/smoother clip mode.

(c) STORMBRINGER — "Combine the Drive and Boost and push the Mids at 1kHz for a tone reminiscent of an old distortion pedal into a practice amp. Very Cvlt." [VERBATIM]
   Diagram: Level ≈ 1, Drive ≈ 1–2 (up), Low ≈ noon; High ≈ noon, Mid ≈ 1 (boosted), Boost ≈ 1 (up). DIODE ≈ down. SHIFT up (1kHz, per text). Boost CHANNEL ON.
   → Drive + Boost both engaged, hi-mid (1kHz) pushed = vintage-distortion-pedal-into-a-small-amp grind.

(d) BASTARD SWORD — "A heavy, raw overdrive that feels like swinging a big chunk of steel. Very potent on bass." [VERBATIM]
   Diagram: Level ≈ 2, Drive ≈ 3 (near max), Low ≈ 2 (boosted); High ≈ 10 (cut), Mid ≈ noon, Boost ≈ 2. DIODE down (silicon). SHIFT down (300Hz).
   → The heaviest preset: max-ish Drive, silicon clip, Low boosted + High cut + low-mid (300Hz). EAE flags it "very potent on bass" — the closest factory preset to the rig's standalone doom/baritone wall.

(e) ELECTRIFY ARMAMENT — "Disengage the clipping diodes and set Drive to minimum, which lets the EQ shine. Use this as your foundation sound and kick on the Boost channel for grit." [VERBATIM]
   Diagram: knobs varied to taste; DIODE CENTER (none/no diodes). Drive at minimum.
   → The "clean EQ/boost" foundation. This is EXACTLY the rig's stacked-on-fuzz approach: no-diode center mode, Drive at minimum, lean on the EQ, and use the Boost footswitch as the intensifier. EAE's own words endorse the dossier's stacked-intensifier setting.

(f) GREAT WEAPON FIGHTING — "Maximize dynamics by putting the Diode toggle in the center position, then crank the Mids at 300Hz for a sturdy sustain. Great for 70's flavored rock, desert/stoner riffs, and various shades in between." [VERBATIM]
   Diagram: DIODE CENTER (none). SHIFT DOWN (300Hz), Mid cranked (boosted). 
   → No-diode max-dynamics + boosted low-mid (300Hz) = thick sustaining stoner/desert tone. This is essentially the dossier's "Banjo lead / body" move (Mid SHIFT-down boosted for body) — and the stoner/desert framing fits the doom aesthetic.

(g) BERSERKER — "It's time to rage. Pull out the midrange entirely to go big or go home. Might kick a fuzz pedal off your pedalboard." [VERBATIM]
   Diagram: Mid pulled hard down (full mid CUT); Drive up; Low & High up. DIODE up area. Boost LED on.
   → Full mid-scoop, high gain. EAE explicitly positions this as a fuzz-replacement scooped wall — relevant to the standalone "fuzz off" use.

(h) FINESSE WEAPON — "Focus your instrument into a full bore mid boost. Great for pushing amps and other pedals. Try with both Shift settings for best results." [VERBATIM]
   Diagram: Mid boosted hard (full mid BOOST); Low & High pulled back. DIODE up (MOSFET). SHIFT — try both.
   → Full mid-BOOST (opposite of Berserker). A focused, cutting lead/push voice — try Shift up (1kHz, cutting) vs down (300Hz, body).

--- EQ TRICKS & OPERATING NOTES (all VERBATIM from manual §4.3 / §4.4) ---
- MID-SCOOP / MID-BOOST trick: "If you boost both the Low and High bands, you can get an effective mid scoop. Conversely you can cut both to get an effective mid boost."
- TIGHTEN trick: "The Longsword has a great deal of low frequency content, so a tighter tone can be achieved by reducing the Low control. This is particularly useful with an already overdriven amplifier."
- CLARIFY-MUD trick: "Don't be afraid to scoop the low midrange (set the Shift switch down) to clarify a muddy tone, especially when using a neck pickup or a down-tuned instrument."
- DRIVE rolloff: "the Drive control introduces a natural high-end rolloff as it is increased, reducing harsh upper harmonics."
- BOOST channel: "+20dB of gain and a pre-gain low frequency cut at 300Hz. This mid-focused approach is ideal for tightening up the Longsword at higher gain settings, as well as providing two distinct voicings for low and high gain." — i.e. the Boost is a SECOND channel of distortion, not an independent clean boost.
- CLIP/EQ interaction: "In the center position at high gain, the tone controls will be more effective when cutting rather than boosting. With the diodes lifted, the dynamic range can eventually run out." Also: rebalance the Level knob whenever you change clip mode (center mode "can get extremely loud at higher Drive settings").
