EAE's own EQ + clip-mode guidance, the maker's intent behind the controls. Use alongside eae-manual-presets.md.

CORE VOICE (EAE's words):
- "An open, clear distortion character with lots of thickness," "incredibly versatile thanks to its hybrid active/passive EQ."
- Signature: "thicker low midrange content … fills out bright guitars" — the single most rig-relevant trait (banjo/baritone body).
- The midrange control "can get pretty extreme" and is built for "cutting through a mix with a blade of midrange." The low-mid emphasis fills out bright single-coils.

EQ RECIPES / TRICKS (EAE):
- OUTPUT-STAGE OVERLOAD: "Boosting both the Low and High EQ controls causes the output stage to become overloaded for an even more aggressive sound." (This is the flip side of the manual's mid-scoop trick: boost-both-Low-and-High = mid scoop AND a more aggressive, slightly hotter/dirtier output. Useful when you want a scooped wall with extra bite — relevant to the standalone "Berserker"-style fuzz-replacement voice.)
- MID-SCOOP vs MID-BOOST (manual): boost both Low+High = effective mid scoop; cut both = effective mid boost.
- TIGHTEN: reduce the Low control to tighten — "particularly useful with an already overdriven amplifier" (i.e. exactly when stacked after the fuzz wall).
- CLARIFY MUD: Shift DOWN (300Hz) and scoop the low-mid to clear up a muddy/down-tuned/neck-pickup tone.

CLIP MODES (V4/V4.5 — MOSFET / none / silicon):
- MOSFET (up): medium compression, high headroom, smooth into clipping, "fairly aggressive at higher gain." The "classy, refined, smoother highs / softer compression" voice (cf. Anduril preset).
- None / center (no diodes): minimal compression, max dynamic range, op-amp clipping only. EAE: "recommended if you want high dynamic range, or if you are using the pedal as a boost and tone shaper." THE stacked-on-fuzz / clean-EQ-shove mode.
- Silicon (down): high compression + pronounced asymmetry (emphasizes even harmonics). Tightest, most squared, "brick wall." Naturally trims a little low end (restore with the Low shelf if wanted).

BOOST CHANNEL (V3+):
- "Adds extra gain up front and tightens the midrange, giving you two distinct levels of distortion without changing the overall character." +20dB, fixed 300Hz front-end low cut. NOT an independent clean boost — it's a second channel of overdrive that also tightens. Use as the intensifier stomp.

VS SIBLINGS (to confirm the Longsword is the right rig drive):
- vs DAGGER: Dagger = germanium clip, "more compressed, less low-mid punch, more sizzly treble." Longsword = more low-mid, fuller bottom. (Rig wants the Longsword's low-mid mass.)
- vs HALBERD: different circuit family (transistor, dynamics-first), not EQ-focused. Longsword is the "twiddle EQ knobs, medium-to-high gain, versatility" choice — "Buy [the Longsword] if you want medium to high gain tones, versatility, twiddling EQ knobs."
