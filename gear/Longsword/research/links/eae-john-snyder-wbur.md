The definitive maker-story source and the ONLY solid documentation of EAE's artist roster. Use this for the "no single Longsword signature artist, but a real maker story" framing.

**John Snyder / EAE background (verified):**
- Snyder is "a longtime guitarist with a doctorate in electrical engineering" who started building pedals in 2015 to solve a problem for his own band.
- His Boston University lab work was "integrated photonics, a field at the intersection of quantum physics and electrical engineering" — and he notes "pedal circuits haven't moved far from where they began in the 1960s."
- Started "from a basement apartment in Brighton just nine years ago" (i.e. 2015); now a four-person company in Waltham — "a footprint that is outsized for a company of four."
- Perpetual backorder: the demand outstrips the four-person shop.

**Documented EAE artists (the honest roster — NOT Longsword-specific endorsements):**
- **Pile** (Boston noise-rock) — frontman **Rick Maguire** co-built the **Mirror House** pedal with Snyder ("detunes a guitar tone into an ethereal warble"). Maguire's tone philosophy: "You don't need a lot of it, it goes a long way."
- **Kal Marks** (Boston) — named as a band whose sound EAE shaped.
- **Touché Amoré** (LA post-hardcore) — guitarists **Clayton Stevens** and **Nick Steinhardt** approached Snyder in 2019 "chasing a sound" for the album *Lament*; the collaboration produced the **Limelight** (released 2020), which Snyder calls "a chimera of classic circuits."

**HONEST FLAG:** These are EAE *company* artists — noise-rock / post-hardcore, **not doom or shoegaze**, and **not Longsword endorsers**. None is documented playing a Longsword specifically. The Longsword has **no single signature artist** (unlike the Hizumitas's Wata). Do not present Pile/Kal Marks/Touché Amoré as Longsword users — they're evidence of EAE's credibility and scene, nothing more.
