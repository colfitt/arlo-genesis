The "how the EQ actually works" reference, distilled from EAE's product copy and reseller listings that quote it. This is the concrete basis for the "dial it like a studio EQ, not a RAT Filter knob" tip.

- **The EQ is studio-grade, not a stompbox tone knob:** "more studio-like, offering precise control over the tone rather than typical pedal EQ." Practical consequence (per manual + owners): **small moves are drastic and the bands barely interact — adjust gradually.** Players who twist it like a one-knob RAT Filter will overshoot.

- **Topology:** "Low/High use a James/Baxandall shelving stack for smooth, musical shaping, while the Mid control is active (boost/cut) and paired with a Shift selector to choose the mid frequency being shaped." (SHIFT up = 1kHz hi-mid, down = 300Hz low-mid.)

- **The two memorize-these EQ tricks (from the manual, the highest-value power-user moves):**
  - **Boost BOTH Low + High = effective mid-scoop.**
  - **Cut BOTH Low + High = effective mid-boost.**
  - These two non-obvious moves are why the 3-band behaves like a real console EQ.

- **The stacking/tightening move:** EAE notes the Longsword has "a great deal of low end available, so a tighter tone can be achieved by **cutting the bass, particularly with an already overdriven** [source]." This is the antidote to mud when stacking after a Muff/fuzz — cut Low to tighten the flubby fuzz bottom. The Boost circuit's fixed 300Hz front-end low cut does the same thing in one stomp.

- **Mid as a mix weapon:** the active Mid lets you "carve out a niche in even the most dense of mixes" — EAE's "blade of midrange." Use SHIFT-up (1kHz) boosted to cut through; SHIFT-down (300Hz) boosted to add body a bright source lacks.

- **Buffer-friendly:** input Z ~500kΩ, output ~1kΩ — happy after buffers (unlike fuzzes). No placement gotcha with buffered pedals upstream.
