The key owner community for this pedal. Bass-leaning but the tips transfer to baritone.

- **OP characterization:** "an outstanding drive pedal with a powerful EQ that does a good job of tone shaping." The Drive control gives "a wide range of distinctive distorted breakup from barely grainy to industrial grind." — sets the expectation that this is a *full-range* dirt box, not a one-trick RAT.

- **The Boost = a second, tighter channel (the most-repeated owner insight):** "The Boost is like a second channel with more gain and a *different, slightly tighter EQ and feel*." Owners treat the Boost footswitch as a voicing change, not just a volume bump — its fixed 300Hz front-end low cut is what makes it "tighter." This matches the manual ("two distinct voicings for low and high gain").

- **18V tip — VERSION-SPECIFIC, DOES NOT APPLY TO THE REPO UNIT:** Owners report the Longsword "slays at 18V": "at 18V with increased headroom I could dial in a really nice clean drive with only the slightest hint of break-up, then kick in the second gain stage." **CAUTION: this is a V3-era tip** (V3 had a 9–18V bipolar supply). The repo unit is V4/V4.5, which is **9V-only** — its V4.5 over-voltage protection circuit *shuts the pedal down above 9V*. Do not run the repo Longsword at 18V. Logged here only to flag the version trap.

- **Clipping character:** owners describe it as "a very transparent drive with well-executed EQ," but note the silicon (down) mode produces "hard-clipping op-amp distortion … artifacts at higher gain settings, similar to Rats and OCDs." Center (no diodes) is the cleanest/most dynamic.

- **Bass EQ workflow consensus (general TalkBass, applies here):** with EQ at multiple points (instrument, pedal, amp), "set the amp for the room, the bass to taste, and the pedal to fit when it's in use" — i.e. don't fight the Longsword's studio EQ against the amp; carve the *band's* niche with the pedal.
