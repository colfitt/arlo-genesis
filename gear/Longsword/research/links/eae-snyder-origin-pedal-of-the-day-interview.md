Pedal of the Day · interview with John Snyder · 2016-04-27

Snyder in his own words, dated 2016 — closest contemporaneous account of the Longsword's birth and his design ethos. Best primary source for the "first pedal / maker philosophy" story.

**Founding & the Longsword as first pedal (Snyder verbatim):**
- "EAE started on a whim, to be honest!" He built DIY pedals for friends at cost, traded gear, gave rig advice — then formalized it.
- The company "formalized when he launched the **Longsword V1 in spring 2015** — his first original design taken 'from concept to reality.'" Confirms the Longsword = EAE's first product.

**Background:**
- Undergrad physics (electromagnetics) + electronics classes; "hooked" on audio circuits. Interned at a Connecticut electronics company in high school (learned assembly + manufacturing QA).
- At time of interview was in an EE grad program researching **quantum optics**: "I research quantum optics and do essentially zero actual electronics work in my lab." (His pedal work was the hands-on electronics outlet.)

**Design philosophy (the ethos, verbatim):**
- Inspiration = gaps in his own rig. Workflow = LTSpice simulation → breadboard → PCB. Build times "anywhere from an afternoon (the mu blaster) to six months (the Model feT)."
- The anti-clone stance, important for framing the Longsword: **"We don't really need more clones of the Tubescreamer or Rat or Big Muff."** The Longsword is his RAT/Distortion+-family circuit done *differently* — bigger, with a real studio EQ — precisely because of this view.

**Musical context (directly relevant to THIS rig's aesthetic):**
- Influences: **Radiohead's *Kid A*, Sigur Rós, Godspeed You! Black Emperor** — ambient/drone solo work plus a screamo band. The Longsword's designer comes from the same drone/post-rock world this banjo/doom/shoegaze rig lives in.

**NOTE:** The widely-repeated "2015 snowpocalypse / breadboarded in a basement / grad-school-notebook margins" origin is stated in EAE's own Longsword manual and corroborated in spirit by the BU profile (basement Photonics Center, side project during the PhD). This 2016 interview gives the "spring 2015, on a whim, V1 was the first" version of the same story — consistent, just less dramatized.
