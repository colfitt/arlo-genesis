All right, let's take a deeper dive into the Longsword from Electronic Audio Experiments. Let's start with the controls. Up top we have a volume control — that's the overall output volume of the pedal. Then we have a Drive control, and that brings you from a light grit all the way to just a very searing overdrive. Between those two we have a Mids control — there you can either cut or boost your midrange depending on which side of noon you are on. Then down here we have the High and Low — this is a Baxandall tone-stack EQ, so they're shelving filters. Then we have a Boost knob, and this will add up to 20 decibels of gain when the Boost button is engaged.

Then we have a couple of toggles. We have the Shift toggle, and that selects the frequency that is controlled by the Mids pot. And then over here you can choose Diode: so up is MOSFET clipping, which is medium compression; center is straight-on, it's no diodes, minimal compression; and then down you have silicon clipping, and that is maximum amount of compression.
