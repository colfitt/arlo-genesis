Zachary Rizer · Electronic Audio Experiments Model feT and Longsword (Bass Demo) · 2018-11-21

[NOTE: This is a V3-era Longsword (2018). Rizer's clip labels are the OLD set — op-amp / LED / silicon — not the current V4/V4.5 MOSFET / none / silicon. The Boost footswitch he describes ("sends a ton of extra volume into the front end") is the modern front-end Boost. Bass = Dingwall ABZ 6-string, neck pickup. This is the single most rig-relevant demo: bass-focused, stacking-focused, doom/sub textures.]

Hi, Zach Rizer here, welcome back to another bass FX review. Today is a double-drive demo of the Model feT and the Longsword by Electronic Audio Experiments. I'll start with the Model feT, then switch over to the Longsword, and towards the end combine them, because they stack really really well. My bass dry tone: Dingwall ABZ 6-string on the neck pickup.

[Model feT section — Rizer covers the Sunn Model T-derived preamp: normal vs bright channel, Fender/Marshall/Vox tone stack, "tons and tons of low end," very interactive EQ, blending channels, stacking with the Boss OC-2 octave and into reverb. Not Longsword-specific.]

Time to finally move on to the Longsword. This is an unbelievably versatile op-amp distortion — you can go from really subtle, almost tube-like breakup to ridiculous amounts of gain. Quick explanation of the controls: you have two shelving filters, the Low and High are both shelving filters; and a midrange control that is kind of somewhat parametric, where you can pick either 1kHz or 300Hz for your frequency range. You also have three different clipping options: just the op-amp, which is really raw, more touch-sensitive, a little bit less gain; the LED, which is a nice amount of compression; and the downward, which is the silicon, which is super-compressed and tons of gain. And finally you have this Boost knob, connected to this Boost footswitch, which essentially just sends a ton of extra volume into the front end of the pedal to give you even more gain, or a soloing boost.

Let's start with just clipping the op-amp at noon. [Plays] ... now the LED for a little bit more clipping, a little more compression ... and finally the silicon for the most clipping and most compression.

[Sweeps Drive at 9 o'clock across all three modes, then Drive maxed across all three.]

So similar to the Model feT, without even touching the EQ controls you have a ton of range of timbres and textures and gain ranges with just this clipping switch and the Drive knob. You probably also notice the different clipping switches have different characteristics on the EQ: the op-amp is the most open, the most touch-sensitive; the LED is a little more snarly; and that silicon actually cuts a little bit of low end — but you can bring that back with this Low up here if that's what you're going for.

[Named settings Rizer demonstrates:]

"A really raw high-gain pure op-amp distortion — so there's no diodes involved here." The Longsword plays incredibly well with my Boss OC-2 for some really really fat synth-bass textures; this particular setting excels at that. [OC-2 → Longsword]

"Now I switched over to the silicon diodes clipping, I've turned the Mid switch down, and I'm getting almost that kind of a RAT-like snarl out of the Longsword." [silicon clip + Shift down/300Hz]

The one control I haven't talked about is the Boost. I have a very very high-gain LED-clipped sound here, with quite a bit of mid scoop going on, and I'm going to switch in that Boost in the middle for just some absolute insane amounts of gain. [high-gain LED clip + mid scoop + Boost on]

Another sub-bass setting: the Longsword in LED clipping, without the OC-2, then run the Boss OC-2 into that. [Then:] I turned the Drive up just a little bit more, now running OC-2 → Longsword → Moog filter → massive reverb on the Eventide H9 for ridiculous synth-y swells.

[Stacking finale — feT + Longsword combined:] The Model feT is an incredibly good amp simulator, so you get a lot of that natural build-up of breakup when you stack more gain into it, especially an op-amp gain pedal like the Longsword. [Just feT → just Longsword on LED → both together.] I find when you stack things into the feT it just gets super raw and thick and heavy, similar to if you really pushed an amp. Let's run a more raw distortion from the Longsword — this is the pure op-amp clipping. Nah, screw it, let's just go crazy gain.

So that's my double-drive demo. Both are unbelievably expansive drive pedals with tons of EQ options and ridiculously wide gain ranges.

SETTINGS / WORKFLOWS QUOTED (V3 labels):
- "RAT-like snarl" recipe: silicon clip + Mid Shift DOWN (300Hz). On V4/V4.5 silicon = the down/max-compression mode; directly maps to the dossier's standalone "squared brick" voice.
- Sub-bass / synth-bass: pure op-amp (= V4/V4.5 center/no-diode) clipping handles octave-fuzz textures cleanly; LED (= V4/V4.5 MOSFET up) for fatter compressed sub-bass.
- Mid-scoop + Boost: high-gain LED clip with "quite a bit of mid scoop" then kick the Boost for "insane gain" — confirms Boost-as-intensifier-stomp and the mid-scoop voicing.
- Silicon "cuts a little low end, bring it back with the Low" — i.e. the down mode is naturally tighter; the Low shelf restores body if wanted (inverse of the rig's "cut Low to tighten" — same control, opposite goal).
- Stacking lesson: op-amp drive stacked INTO a pushed-amp-sim "gets super raw and thick and heavy" — the same destructive-but-musical stacking the rig wants (Longsword after the fuzz/Brothers wall).
