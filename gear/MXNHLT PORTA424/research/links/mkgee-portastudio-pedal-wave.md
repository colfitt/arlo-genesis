WHY THIS PEDAL CATEGORY EXISTS — the Mk.gee Portastudio-channel-strip wave of 2024.

THE TECHNIQUE (the thing everyone is chasing):
- Mk.gee runs guitar STRAIGHT INTO a Tascam Portastudio (a Tascam 424 in his case) INSTEAD of a guitar amp, using the deck's PREAMP/input stage as his primary tone-shaping tool — no amp, no distortion pedal.
- "The magic occurs when Mk.gee pushes the preamp section hard — essentially overdriving the mixer's input stages rather than using distortion pedals. This gain-staging approach extracts harmonic coloration and compression artifacts native to the analog tape path."
- The result: "elastic, lo-fi tones" — JHS calls it the Portastudio's "unparalleled gooey texture and lo-fi fuzz." Josh Scott (JHS): "it's really new and fresh and different. It's not the classic thing, it's this really interesting new way of using this sound."
- TRIGGER EVENT: Mk.gee's 2024 album *Two Star and the Dream Police* sent guitarists scrambling, prompting the pedal makers to bottle it.
→ RIG RELEVANCE: this confirms the PORTA424's signature "clean to filth with just your attack" move is the SAME gain-staging technique — crank the input trim so the preamp lives at the edge of breakup and let the picking hand modulate from clean to grit. Mk.gee uses a real Tascam, NOT this pedal; no famous PORTA424 user exists.

THE WAVE — the 4 Portastudio-channel-strip pedals (from GPX "4 of a Kind," https://www.guitarpedalx.com/news/gpx-blog/4-of-a-kind-tascam-424-portastudio-style-preamps):
- JHS 424 Gain Stage ($249) — models the LATER Tascam 424 on its actual UPC4570 + NJM4565 op-amps; TWO gain stages (Gain 1 / Gain 2); balanced XLR DI out + ground lift; the biggest splash / best-seller. (https://jhspedals.info/products/424-gain-stage)
- Benson Portable Distortion 424 MkII ($199, was ~$188) — Benson's take, inspired by "the raw and grimy distortion texture and broad EQ control from a classic cassette 4-track recorder preamp." 4 controls (vs 5 on the others). Signature feature is a BYPASSABLE BUFFER: buffer engaged = clean boost → warm/dark → grind; buffer bypassed = "the faithful recreation of the sound of plugging an electric guitar directly into the original." (No dedicated Lo-Z switch on the MkII per Benson's page — the buffer toggle is the impedance/character control.) (https://www.bensonamps.com/guitarpedals/424-mkii-preamp)
- MXNHLT PORTA424 ($170) — THIS pedal. The early/rawer Porta MK1 channel; 5 controls incl. the slide fader. GPX: "a highly commendable choice."
- Neutrino Labs MX 424 ($141) — the budget option; near-identical 5-control layout; notably also has an XLR out.

GPX's "which one" verdict: "you could really go with any of these," noting a "$100+ price differential." The differences are source-machine (Porta vs 424), interface (XLR on JHS + Neutrino, not MXNHLT), and price — not a huge tonal gulf.

→ FOR THE RIG: the PORTA424 + JHS 424 PAIR covers the wave's two poles in one chain — the raw early-Porta character box (MXNHLT) feeding the flexible, XLR-equipped later-424 interface box (JHS). Benson and Neutrino are redundant once you own both.
