The authoritative source. PORTA424 = recreation of a single channel from a classic MK1 4-track cassette recorder, "the entirety of the signal path with authentic gain staging." Price $170 USD, made-to-order. Builder-reported rating 98.2/100 across 59 reviews (93% 5-star).

CONTROLS (5), as labeled on the box:
1. Input Trim — input level / front-end gain (where the saturation lives)
2. Treble — shelving EQ
3. Bass — shelving EQ
4. Channel Fader — volume SLIDER (not a knob). Builder note: "It doesn't sound as good with a knob instead of a fader." (the slider is deliberate, not just aesthetic)
5. Master Volume — output level

SETTINGS GUIDANCE (no specific knob positions given — directional only):
- "Dial in your settings exactly the same way as on the original" — i.e. use it like a real Portastudio channel strip (Trim sets drive, EQ shelves, fader sets channel level, master sets output).
- Two stated use cases, both directional, no numbers:
  • DI tone at the END of the chain (clean/warm print) — low Trim.
  • "A thick, trashy distortion" at the FRONT of the chain — Trim cranked.
- The circuit delivers "a special kind of grit."

POT / FADER NOTE (manufacturing): Current units ship with B10k slide pots in place of A10k. Builder's exact words: "They are electrically the same, but the B taper will increase the volume faster, and actually allows finer control at the top."
