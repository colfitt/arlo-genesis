BIO / TIMELINE (verbatim-ish from About page):
- Built his FIRST pedal in 2016 — "sparked his passion for audio electronics and led him to pursue a degree in Electrical Engineering."
- From 2022: committed to building seriously — "studying and auditioning a wide variety of circuits, searching for hidden gems and interesting ideas, and adding new features to [his] favorite designs."
- MISSION (quote): "to provide musicians with tools to find their sound by bringing attention and updates to underrated pedals, creating new original circuits, and collaborating with players on custom designs."
- MODEL: made-to-order, builder-direct, with a custom-builds page. Small batch / hand-built.

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY (from the "Behind the Void Ripper" blog, https://mxnhlteffects.com/blogs/blog/behind-the-void-ripper):
- He approaches classic/vintage circuits "not with blind reverence, but as a foundation for exploration" — learns a circuit deeply, then improves it while preserving its essential character. This is EXACTLY the PORTA424 ethos: faithfully recreate the Tascam Porta channel strip, but as a deliberate, modern, true-bypass build.
- Concrete example of his EE chops: on the Void Ripper (a Univox Superfuzz derivative) he swapped the discrete op-amp input stage for a TL072 to cut part count and shrink the enclosure, converted buffered→true bypass, and added an octave-off switch by "grounding one input to the differential pair rectifier," exploiting the asymmetrical phase splitter so "one side sounds like the original octave mode, and the other has quite a bit more sag and warmth."
  → Relevance: the same hands-on circuit fluency is why the PORTA424 is described as recreating "the entirety of the signal path with meticulously reconstructed gain staging," not just a single gain stage.
