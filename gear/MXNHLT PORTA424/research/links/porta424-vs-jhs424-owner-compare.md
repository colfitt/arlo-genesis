=== WHY OWNING BOTH ISN'T REDUNDANT (the structural answer) ===
They recreate DIFFERENT Tascam machines and do DIFFERENT jobs:

PORTA424 (MXNHLT, $170) — the CHARACTER box:
- Recreates the EARLIER/RAWER Tascam Porta MK1 channel (single tape-speed bandwidth, fixed Baxendall 100Hz/10kHz shelves) → grittier, more lo-fi voice.
- Controls: Input Trim, Treble, Bass, Channel Fader (slider), Master.
- MONO 1/4" out only. No XLR, no second gain stage.

JHS 424 (JHS, $249) — the OUTPUT/INTERFACE box:
- Recreates the LATER Tascam 424, "perfectly replicated the original 424 MKI circuit using the original UPC4570 and NJM4565 op-amps."
- TWO gain stages: Gain 1 (Trim) "sets how much preamplification will be added to the input signal" + Gain 2 (Channel) "sets the volume level of the second gain stage" → wider range "from lo-fi flavours to zingy fuzzy tones."
- Has a 1/4" out AND a BALANCED XLR (DI) out with a GROUND-LIFT dip switch → the actual interface/FOH handoff. Also a DI-blend heritage ("blend a miked amp signal with a DI, just like producers used to do with the original Portastudio").
- Controls: Volume (Master), Gain 1, Gain 2, Bass, Treble. Mono TS in/out + XLR out.

PRACTICAL DIVISION FOR THE RIG (PORTA424 → JHS 424):
- Let the PORTA424 set the CHARACTER (warmth + early-Porta grit) with its Trim+Fader.
- Let the JHS 424 do FINAL LEVEL + the XLR/ground-lift handoff to the Apollo (use its twin gain stages for output, not a second wall of distortion).
- DON'T crank both into heavy distortion at once unless you want total collapse — pick ONE as the saturator, let the other glue/output. "Track to the Porta, then re-amp/print through the 424 to the desk."

=== vs THE OTHER WAVE PEDALS (why not Benson/Neutrino instead) ===
- Benson Portable Distortion 424 MkII ($199): 4 controls, bypassable-buffer character toggle, "raw and grimy" voicing — a fine standalone but adds nothing the PORTA424+JHS pair doesn't cover, and lacks the JHS interface features.
- Neutrino Labs MX 424 ($141): budget, near-identical 5-control layout, also has an XLR — the cheap entry, redundant once you own the MXNHLT.
- vs Benson/Neutrino generally: the rig's choice is about pairing the rawest CHARACTER box (MXNHLT) with the most INTERFACE-capable box (JHS), not about which single Portastudio clone is "best."
