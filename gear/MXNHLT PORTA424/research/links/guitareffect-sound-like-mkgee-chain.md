Captured for: (a) the Tascam-424-as-preamp technique stated plainly, (b) the 424's PLACEMENT in a real Mk.gee-style chain (which differs from this rig), and (c) concrete settings for the SURROUNDING pedals (useful when staging the PORTA424 inside a Mk.gee-flavored signal flow). Still NO numeric 424 preamp settings — consistent with every other source (the 424's character is dynamics-driven, not a fixed knob patch).

=== THE 424-AS-PREAMP TECHNIQUE (verbatim) ===
"Guitar plugged WITHOUT a traditional amp, directly into a Tascam Portastudio 424 MKI preamp (the famous 4-track cassette)… pushed hard."
SOUND: "This preamp, used as a 'false head', gives an ELASTIC, SLIMY, VERY MIDRANGE SATURATION, with a lo-fi grain that breathes transients and gets nicely dirty when attacked."
CHAIN ROLE: "Tascam 424 (used as a preamp): guitar → 424 → console/interface. No cabinet, no cab sim."

=== THE 424's PLACEMENT IN A MK.GEE CHAIN (verbatim) ===
"The guitar first passes through the utilities (tuner, Drop, Hum Debugger), then through the TASCAM 424, which colors and compresses the signal. Next come modulations (CE-2W, BF-2), then delays and reverbs (Belle Epoch, Echo-X, Reverb-X) at the end of the chain to broaden the spectrum."
→ IMPORTANT CONTRAST: In the *Mk.gee* recipe the 424/preamp sits NEAR THE FRONT (utilities → 424 → mod → delay → reverb) — i.e. the preamp is the "amp," and time/space go AFTER it. THIS RIG does the opposite: the PORTA424 is the END-of-chain print AFTER the H90's delays/reverbs. Both are legitimate; the front-of-chain version is the literal Mk.gee "amp-replacement" mode (matches K_YEON_J), the end-of-chain version is the "bounce the whole bus to cassette" print mode. Same box, two placements, two jobs.

=== NO 424 PREAMP NUMBERS (verbatim confirmation) ===
The article gives NO dial positions for the Tascam 424 or the JHS 424 Gain Stage — only: JHS "424 Gain Stage, a pedal that faithfully recreates the Portastudio preamp (with dual gain stage, EQ and balanced XLR output for direct console connection)." The preamp character "derives from how it responds to playing dynamics rather than fixed knob positions" — the same conclusion every source reaches: there is no copyable numeric 424 preamp patch, you push the trim into breakup and ride pick attack.

=== SURROUNDING-PEDAL SETTINGS (for staging the PORTA424 inside a Mk.gee-style flow) ===
These are this author's distilled Mk.gee-chain settings for the OTHER boxes — NOT PORTA424 settings, but useful context for the rig's modulation/time stages that feed (or follow) the print:
- Rainger FX Reverb-X:  Decay 2–3 s | Mix 20–25% | Tone dark
- Rainger FX Echo-X:    Time 300–400 ms | Feedback 20–25% | Mix 15–20%
- Catalinbread Belle Epoch: Delay 250–350 ms | light feedback | Mix 10–15% | Record Level ~11 o'clock
- Boss CE-2W Chorus:    Rate 9–10 o'clock | Depth 11–12 o'clock | Mode CE-2
- Boss BF-2 Flanger:    Manual 11 o'clock | Depth 10 o'clock | Rate 9–10 o'clock | Resonance 9 o'clock
- Chase Bliss Mood:     very short loop | Mix 15–20% | Clock standard | Decay short
- DigiTech Drop:        Shift −1 or −2 | Latch mode | Dry signal ON
- Empress ParaEQ MkII:  Low +2 dB | Mid Q tight | High very discreet
- Boss DD-20 Giga Delay: Time 400–500 ms | moderate feedback | Tape/Analog mode

TAKEAWAY FOR PORTA424 PATCHES: confirms (1) the technique = push trim to edge of breakup + ride pick attack (no fixed numbers), (2) the dark, midrange-forward target voicing ("very midrange saturation," reverbs kept dark) — so a Mk.gee-leaning PORTA424 patch should shelve treble DOWN and let the mids dominate, and (3) the front-vs-end placement duality. The "dark + midrange + dynamic" target informs the designed patches' EQ choices.
