THE LINEAGE DISTINCTION — why the PORTA424 (early Porta MK1) is NOT redundant with the JHS 424 (later Tascam 424). They recreate DIFFERENT machines from different ends of the Portastudio line.

=== THE EARLY PORTA-SERIES MINISTUDIO (what MXNHLT recreates) ===
TASCAM PORTA ONE Ministudio — debuted 1984, the budget/entry, BATTERY-CAPABLE machine. From the Dec 1984 muzines review (Paul White):
- INPUT: 4 unbalanced jack inputs, each with a TRIM control that "[accommodates] anything from a low impedance mic to a high impedance line input" without separate selectors. → This trim is exactly the PORTA424's "Input Trim" — one knob spanning mic-level to line-level, which is WHY it overdrives so musically when you crank it past a guitar's hot output.
- EQ: a "standard Baxendall shelving circuit," TWO FIXED-FREQUENCY bands — 100Hz bass + 10kHz treble — each ±10dB cut/boost. → This is precisely the PORTA424's Treble + Bass shelving EQ: fixed-corner shelves, NOT sweepable, NOT parametric. The dossier's "two broad shelving bands, not surgical" is confirmed down to the corner frequencies.
- SIGNAL FLOW quirk: "the level fader connected before EQ, meaning electronic noise would feed to the mixing bus even with the fader down." → The Fader is pre-EQ/channel-level; the MXNHLT reproduces this Trim→Fader→EQ→Master staging.
- NOISE REDUCTION: dbx; "handled most types of programme material without unpleasant side effects."
- VERDICT: Tascam "made all the right compromises" — "all the facilities needed by the songwriter/home recordist." A budget machine, by design.
- LIMITS (vs later 424): single tape speed (1 7/8 ips), records only 2 tracks at once at normal speed, NO aux bus, frequency response ~40Hz–12.5kHz. The lower bandwidth + single speed = the rawer, lo-fi-er, grittier voice.

=== THE LATER TASCAM 424 (what the JHS 424 recreates) ===
- Switchable tape speed (1 7/8 AND 3 3/4 ips — double speed = higher fidelity, lower hiss).
- SWEEPABLE MID-BAND in the EQ (vs the Porta One's fixed 2-band shelves) — more flexible tone shaping.
- Records all 4 tracks at once; expanded I/O including proper XLR mic inputs; electronic counter, location markers, auto-punch.
- Positioned as the more capable (if "plasticy") machine in the line's maturity (414/424/424 MkII/MkIII era).

=== WHY THIS MATTERS FOR THE RIG (PORTA424 → JHS 424) ===
- PORTA424 = EARLIER, RAWER, SIMPLER Porta channel: single-speed-bandwidth grit, fixed Baxendall shelves, more lo-fi. Sets CHARACTER.
- JHS 424 = LATER, HI-FI-ER, MORE-FLEXIBLE 424 channel: built on the actual UPC4570 + NJM4565 op-amps, TWO gain stages, sweepable-mid heritage, and a balanced XLR DI out + ground lift for the interface handoff. Sets OUTPUT/INTERFACE.
- Running them in series is two literal Tascam generations stacked: early Porta character → later 424 output. They are complementary recreations of different machines — which is exactly why owning both is not redundant.

LINEAGE CAVEAT (lightly flagged): the broader Portastudio family history is tangled (TEAC 144 in 1979 → 244 in 1982 → Porta One 1984 → 246/414/424/424 MkII–MkIII). MXNHLT's marketing says "MK1 version" loosely; "early Porta-series ministudio (Porta One lineage)" is the best-supported read for what the PORTA424 voices, vs the JHS's explicit Tascam-424 modeling.
