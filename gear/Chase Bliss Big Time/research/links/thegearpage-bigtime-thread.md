## MOST IMPORTANT: what the pedal actually IS / ISN'T (Tabor, May 5, #1041 — verbatim)

Answering "are there 2x independent delay lines?":
> "It's only one delay line as far as I can tell. That one delay line can do stereo things but, no, there isn't a second line to set independently. (There's also **no reverse, no random modulation, no ms display, and no fx loop**). On the other hand it does have an unusual setup with **a limiter in the feedback path that can exist in 3 'states' with a control to make the effect of that state more or less intense** and it has some capability to do **odd sequenced sounding pitch jumps kinda like Thermae.**"

GOTCHA confirmed by this + a later post (#1078, dickens): Chase Bliss confirmed there ARE two delay lines internally, but **you cannot set fully independent delay times/feedbacks per output** — "on a technical level, no, it doesn't seem possible to have total control over offsetting the delay times." So: stereo, yes; true dual-independent delay (e.g. Output 1 vs Output 2 with separate time+feedback like an Eventide Rose pair), NO.

Also note the 3-state limiter count here (Tabor says "3 states") vs the manual's 4 STATE options (Digital/Compressed/Saturated/#!&%). Likely Tabor is counting the 3 *active limiter* states excluding "Digital" (= limiter off). Reconcile against manual; treat Tabor's "3" as the non-bypassed limiter flavors.

## Big Time vs Eventide Rose (Tabor, #1063 — concrete capability list)

Prompted by "for similar money would a 2nd Rose + Morningstar ML10X do series/parallel routing do more?":

**Big Time can (Rose can't):**
- compressed delay with ducking
- saturated delay that has audible grit
- mis-biased fuzzy delay
- cluster feature for some programmed motion in the stereo field (Rose is mono)
- display the position of secondary functions [the motorized faders re-seat to show saved values]
- have a clear, well designed, informative manual

**Rose can (Big Time can't):**
- random modulation
- reverse

Tabor also notes a Rose + ML10X gives you "two separate delays with their own controls vs shared controls on the Big Time" — reinforcing that Big Time is one shared-control stereo engine.
