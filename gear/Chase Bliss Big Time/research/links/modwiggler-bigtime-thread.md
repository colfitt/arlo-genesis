Synth/modular community angle (relevant since this rig feeds Big Time modeled/synth sources via VG-800). Short thread, mostly pre-purchase reaction + price grumbling, but a few genuinely useful practical/desktop tips and EAE-lineage notes.

## Practical / desktop & live tips (concrete)
- **AUX jack lets you keep Big Time on the desk and put a separate footswitch on the floor.** (Happyanimal, replying to a "shouldn't pedals go on the floor?" jab.) Useful given the Automatone is desk-friendly.
- **Optional flip-up fader cover** "so you can leave it on the floor permanently without worrying about dust buildup." (X-Mod) — protects the motorized faders.
- Small-board idea (folpon, May 6): "a board that's just **Preamp mk2 → Big Time → CXM** could cover a lot of territory. Plus volume pedal and chorus." (i.e. EAE/CB preamp into Big Time into CXM 1978 reverb.)
- Usage philosophy (folpon, an OTO BIM/BAM owner): his BIM/BAM "live on aux sends and get used constantly," whereas his CB pedals "live on a pedalboard... just for guitar." Notes CB textures feel most worthwhile "in a room with a bunch of musicians" for options. Echoes the Elektronauts insert-vs-aux debate — CB stuff reads as a guitar-pedalboard tool, BIM/BAM as the studio aux-send tools.

## EAE-lineage / character (confirms dossier)
- CursedFrogurt (May 1): "EAE's **Halberd** is by far my favorite dirt pedal I've ever tried, so the idea of getting a precisely tuned feedback system incorporating EAE's circuitry is really appealing. Grimy flange, exploding chorus, burning tape delay, degradation loops — a treasure trove of gritty textures." (Halberd is one of the EAE circuit blocks the dossier names.)
- DukeOfPrunes: sonic reference points "old TC Electronic 2290 rack effects and some earlier Eventide rack gear." Frames it as "$100 more than an H90... gets you a piece of 80s historical effects."

## Lore (flag: lore, not feature)
- The reference rack delay people keep naming is the **Lexicon Prime Time** (folpon wants it "ever since that Daniel Lanois video"; hopes Big Time saves him buying a Prime Time 93). NB: this is community framing — EAE's own blog names the **PCM42 + Ursa Major Space Station**, not the Prime Time. Treat Prime Time as crowd association.
- A real **1-of-1 rackmount Big Time** exists: Joel (CB) said on IG it's real, made by **Löve Hultén** (lovehulten.com), planned for the Superbooth booth. (muddy ranks / jimfowler.) Decorative/marketing piece, not a product.
