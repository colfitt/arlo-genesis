# EAE lineage, design intent, and artist roster — the honest "who uses it" angle

Big Time shipped **June 2026**, so there is **no Big-Time-specific artist mythology yet** — none is confirmed, and anyone claiming a famous Big Time user right now is guessing. The honest, useful frame is **EAE's lineage**: who already plays John Snyder's pedals (the likely early Big Time adopters) and how Big Time inherits sounds from EAE's prior delays. Treat the artist list as "EAE players," not "Big Time players."

## How Big Time inherits sounds from prior EAE delays (Snyder's own blog)
The EAE Big Time blog is the only first-party source for the circuit lineage:
- **Reference units:** the **Lexicon PCM42** — Snyder's unconventional trick was "throwing the limiter calibration out of whack and using it for hyper-compressed, disintegrating infinite delays," which "directly shaped the Compressed / 'Blue Mode'." (He also flags the PCM42's "X2 filter mode — steep brickwall at 10kHz," which maps to Big Time's **Warm** voicing's 10K cutoff.) The **Ursa Major Space Station** (restored in 2025) was the "spiritual influence on Big Time's **Cluster** slider."
- **Reused EAE circuit blocks (quoted):** "Big Time contains ideas or circuit blocks from **Sending, Clean, Halberd, Glaive**, and at least 2 things I haven't released yet."
- **Analog voicing = Sending V2:** in the Superbooth walkthrough (captured at `research/transcripts/sonicstate-superbooth-john-snyder-eae-interview.md`) Snyder says Big Time's **Analog VOICING** is "a callback to our Sending V2 delay" (gentle ~4 kHz slope) — so the Sending's darker BBD-ish tone lives on as one of the four voicings.
- **Personal touchstone (quoted):** "This pedal is partly a love letter to my old circuit-bent **PDS 20/20** that I've had since age 16."
- **Misbias naming:** the **#!&%** state name was chosen over candidates "disintegrated, wrecked, melted."

## EAE technique that transfers to Big Time (the instructive part)
EAE's whole house style is **a colored preamp / saturation stage feeding a delay that degrades gracefully as you push it** — exactly Big Time's COLOR→limiter architecture. The **Sending** (analog BBD delay) is the closest spiritual sibling: up to ~1 s of clear repeats, then "up to two full seconds of gritty, aliased ambient wash" — the same "play clean, push for disintegration" gesture Big Time gets from COLOR/FEEDBACK + the misbias state. The **Halberd** drive ("medium-gain… harmonic fullness and grind") shares the preamp DNA that became Big Time's COLOR preamp. So the instructive EAE move for this rig: **keep COLOR moderate for clean repeats, then dig in / ramp COLOR+FEEDBACK (OVERLOAD) to push the echoes into the limiter for the disintegration wash** — the Sending workflow, now stereo and with four selectable limiter states.
