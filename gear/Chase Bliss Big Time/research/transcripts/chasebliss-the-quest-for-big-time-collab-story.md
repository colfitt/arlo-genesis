## Design philosophy / EAE character (the useful parts)
- John Snyder on the misbias state: "I like that the weird **rumbles and crackles that come in are really, really musical**... because of the chance for weird interactions to occur. That is the real complexity of this project."
- On CLUSTER vs. typical multi-effects: "What we're achieving here, building off of [the CXM 1978 Meris collab], is that **there aren't discontinuous jumps between algorithms.** That's what makes the cluster slider so compelling — you're traversing a realm of reverb-ish things, but it's **continuous**, and you can come back at any time or stop at a scenic outlook along the way." (i.e. CLUSTER is a smooth delay→multitap→diffuse→reverb continuum, not stepped modes.)
- On the development obsession: "We're so concerned about the **first 1/3 sweep of this control**" — the team tuned the low end of the fader throws so the sweet spots are easy to reach (echoes Secret Weapons' note that faders have most action in the first ~10%).
- On the overall UX intent: "There are **little worlds in Big Time that you'll get lost in**, and we made it so you can go really deep down one of these pathways and then **jump back to a safe center point** — delve into the dungeon and come back with treasure. That's the balance: wrapping all these analog building blocks in digital control to make it a choose-your-own-adventure experience." (The press-and-hold-MODE "reset to clean delay" hack is the practical expression of this "safe center point.")
- Snyder built the Automatone platform ~10 years prior; this is the culmination. Beta bug count "broke 100" (Kanban-tracked) — explains why launch firmware still had final bugs being squashed (per the Superbooth interview).

## Practical residue
- Reinforces: CLUSTER as a continuous delay→reverb traverse; MODE press-hold as the "panic/return to center" control; the design is tuned so the most usable settings live in the lower part of each fader's throw.
