First-party Q&A for the X-Amp specifically. The most useful operational/troubleshooting answers:

- **X-Amp vs. JCR (active vs passive):** the X-Amp is active, the JCR passive. The X-Amp's key practical advantage: the **ability to drive more than one amp at the same time without having to adjust the output to compensate** (the active buffer holds level across the two outputs).

- **Cable length:** the buffered output "will easily drive unbalanced cables upwards to 50 feet." For longer instrument-level runs, use a Radial SGI. (Active buffer = long-cable-friendly; matters if the X-Amp sits in the rack and the pedalboards are across the room.)

- **LEVEL control setting:** "We recommend they be set at **12 o'clock** and adjusted up or down as needed." Match relative loudness by connecting your guitar to the amp directly, noting the level/tone, then setting the X-Amp LEVEL to reproduce it.

- **Ground lift:** if you get hum/buzz, "try depressing the ground lift switch. This will eliminate the audio ground connection… which will usually solve the problem by eliminating the ground loop."

- **180° polarity reverse:** use it when running **multiple amps** — "your amps may be out of phase. Simply depress the 180° reverse to check." (A/B the switch, keep whichever sounds fuller.)

- **Outputs into effects, not just amps:** "Can I use the ¼" output jacks to feed studio effects instead of guitar amps? Sure — so long as they have **instrument-level inputs.**" (Confirms the rig's core use: X-Amp out → front of the pedalboards / VG-800.)

- **Other instruments:** the X-Amp works with **bass and other instruments**, not just guitar (matches the rig's banjo/baritone/bass reamping).

- **XLR input is line-level only:** the XLR input "accepts line level, designed for prerecorded studio tracks," **not** guitar-level signals. (Don't try to feed a guitar into the X-Amp input — it's the wrong end of the box.)
