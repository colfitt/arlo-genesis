# Clean — demo index & artist coverage (honest)

**Artist reality check:** Clean released Nov 2024. There is **no developed signature-artist mythology** and no rig rundown citing Clean as a named signature piece (confirmed across web + forum + video search, consistent with the dossier §7). Coverage = demo creators and use-case framing, not "X plays a Clean." What follows is the most instructive material, grouped by what it teaches. Transcripts captured for the four starred items live in ../transcripts/.

## Most instructive demos (with concrete value)
- ★ **chase bliss — "Clean — Video Manual"** — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=26AH-aL4b6Q — the authoritative walkthrough; confirms Dynamics clock-face (1:1@7, 4:1@10, ∞:1@noon), Unity@noon on Wet/Dry. Runs SPREAD ON throughout. [transcript captured]
- ★ **Ricky Tinez — "I didn't like this... then I did"** — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y6RGAi91Ggk — electronic/house producer, Clean end-of-chain on a drum/synth bus; step-by-step LIVE limiter dial-in + "pseudo-sidechain" (hot kick ducks the bus). Best non-guitar / modeled-source-relevant use. [transcript captured]
- ★ **Rhett Shull — "How Did They Even Think Of This?"** — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=63-1X006UyU — notable guitarist; clearest "who it's for" + the finding that DUSTY makes Clean a usable overdrive/boost. [transcript captured]
- ★ **Devin Belanger — "the most USEFUL pedal I've bought all year"** — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4gQdIsLrStc — uses Clean on EVERY track of a composition: Dusty on drums, Modulated-EQ stereo "Leslie/harmonic-tremolo" on pads, basic comp on Moog bass, DI-fattening on guitar; strong on the mono→stereo creative-comp angle. [transcript already in repo]
