# Rhett Shull — "who it's for" + Dusty as a boost/overdrive

No concrete knob settings given — Rhett demos by feel. Value is in use-case framing and one key finding.

## Key finding — Dusty makes Clean an overdrive/boost
"I stumbled into something I really love... it actually makes an amazing overdrive or boost pedal" via the **DUSTY** dip. Funny because the pedal's whole pitch is "what if clean was as fun as dirty — but you can also still make it dirty." (Confirms the dossier's Dusty-as-pre-dirt-texture use and adds: Dusty is usable as a standalone boost/OD voice.)

## Who it's for (verbatim-ish)
- **NOT for you** if you "just want a compressor to put on their pedalboard to use with their guitar/bass in front of an amp" for basic evening-out/sustain — "you'd really be underutilizing this pedal... there's plenty of other options better and cheaper" (he names the UA 1176 and JHS Pulp 'n' Peel).
- **FOR you** if you use pedals "for sound design or to mix" — true stereo in/out lets you use it "like an outboard rack compressor... put drums through this, put vocals, put your entire mix through this," from gluing → hard limiting → adding distortion → EQ.
- Rewards experimenters who'll use the physics, EQ modes, and Dusty.

## Use-cases he calls out (relevant to this rig)
- "Sounds amazing on bass." (Baritone Jazzmaster low end.)
- Great after reverb/delay/modulation: "putting it AFTER a reverb or delay or modulation effect you can start to do some really interesting things" — an argument for an end-of-chain Clean as well as front-of-chain.
- SIDECHAIN input "lets you feed in a separate audio signal to trigger the compressor, like a kick drum" — "really cool to see on a pedal like this."
- Praises the CB manual's "Compression 101" section as genuinely beginner-friendly.

## Verdict
"Clean will work its way into that regular rotation of Chase Bliss stuff" — i.e. an always-on keeper for the sound-design/mixing user, which matches this owner's profile.
