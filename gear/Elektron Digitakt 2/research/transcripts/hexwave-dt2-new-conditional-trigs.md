## What's new vs the OG conditionals
- DT2 adds **NOT** variants: alongside `1:2`, `1:3`, etc. you can now exclude — e.g. **NOT 1:3**, **NOT 2:3** — so you can subtract trigs from a pattern, not just add them.
- The headline pair: **LAST** and **NOT LAST**. A trig with **LAST** fires *only on the final pass before a queued pattern switch*; **NOT LAST** fires on every pass *except* that final one. (Set via the conditional knob on the TRIG page — hold the trig to access it.)

## The hands-free transition workflow
1. Build your main pattern and the next pattern (e.g. a higher-energy "chorus").
2. In the main pattern, put extra fills/risers on **LAST** trigs (e.g. extra kicks that only land on the final pass), and put trigs you want to *drop* on **NOT LAST** (e.g. the kick/snare drop out on the final pass).
3. When you cue the next pattern, the box plays the whole transition automatically on that last pass — a built-in fill/ramp — then lands in the next pattern. Drop the kick/snare back in on the *next* return.

## Gotchas / tips
- Trigs on **early steps (step 1–2)** won't fire on the cued pass — you've already passed them by the time you queue. For a **riser that starts on step 1**, use a **pattern chain** instead (chain triggers anything even on the first step).
- **Pattern chains have improved**: press the PATTERN button (now a single press, not a hold-timer) → **FUNC + YES for chain** → select patterns (e.g. pattern 1 ×2, pattern 2 ×2) → PLAY. A background noise riser set up to come in on the second pass; kick/snare drop on the LAST round, then back in.
- Cancel the chain: press PATTERN → select a single pattern.
- Because transitions are now programmable, chaining is genuinely useful (vs OG where you'd "just change the pattern yourself"). It frees your hands to do live tweaks (filter sweeps, raising noise on external gear) while the transition plays itself.

Hexwave notes these conditionals "could be added to other Elektron boxes and I hope they do — for now they're just in Digitakt/Digitone 2."
