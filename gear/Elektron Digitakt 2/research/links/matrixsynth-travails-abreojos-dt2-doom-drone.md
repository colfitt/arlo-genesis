## Technique — DT2 as a polyphony/layering engine for a mono synth
- The Mono Evolver is monophonic; he **samples it directly into the DT2**, then uses the **Grid machine + Euclidean sequencing** to layer the sampled Evolver material against the live synth — effectively turning a Mono Evolver into a "Poly Evolver."
- The result: DT2 holds stacked, Euclidean-sequenced drone layers (sampled) while he plays the live Evolver on top — a two-instrument doom wall.

## Evolver drone patch (the source he samples)
- Oscillators with **wave sequencing**, **filter modulation via mod-wheel**, **tuned feedback**, and **distortion** to build "oppressive ambient texture."

## Why it maps to this rig
- Same move as sampling the baritone/banjo-through-pedalboard into the DT2 and Euclidean-sequencing the captures into an evolving wall. Validates the Grid-machine + Euclidean approach for doom drone specifically (§13d in the dossier).
