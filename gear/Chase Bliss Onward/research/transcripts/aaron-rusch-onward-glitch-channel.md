Aaron Rusch Guitar · Chase Bliss Onward Demo 3: Glitch · 2024-09-12

Part 3 of Aaron Rusch's series — the GLITCH channel deep-dive. The most useful single video for understanding SIZE / sample-capture behavior and how Error shapes the glitch. (Defers dip switches.)

Concept: it samples and repeats small chunks of audio. The sample size of that audio can be controlled — from a very small chunk up to 1 second. There's a dip switch (HALF SPEED) that puts the pedal in half-speed mode and gives you 2 seconds of sample time; understanding is that you're lowering the clock speed, so the audio won't be as pristine.

To demonstrate: MIX full (only hearing the pedal in Glitch), SUSTAIN all the way to the right so it sustains the grabbed chunks infinitely (so you can hear them).

SIZE: when you turn Size to the left, it's only catching the first part of the sampled audio. Think of it this way: it grabbed a second's worth of audio; turning Size down grabs only the front part of it. If you play something with a harder attack, that front transient is captured, so it doesn't disappear at the beginning (versus a swell, where the front is quiet). That's fundamentally what the Glitch section is: grabbing a sample and repeating it. The pedal comes to life when you introduce variations — Error, effects, Sustain.

ERROR (grabbing a sustained chord so you can hear each error clearly):
- TIMING — randomly changes the sample size.
- CONDITION (middle toggle) — introduces muting and sample-rate shifts; pretty chaotic, you hear glitchy sample-rate-reduction moments.
- PLAYBACK (right) — destabilizes the playback speed and the direction; you hear reversing.

SHAPE: SUSTAIN determines how long sounds are held before fading away — "almost like the feedback knob of a delay pedal." At ~50% mix you hear it sustain and fade; all the way right, it sustains infinitely.

FADE toggle (three fade-in types, controlling when the new recording replaces the old): FAST (right) is very immediate. SLOW (left) — note that when playing fast staccato bits, the pedal often wasn't even grabbing/committing them, either because it wasn't loud enough or it hadn't recognized it as long enough. So the Fade toggle is very important: it changes how the pedal interacts with and feels to you — it can feel faster or slower. (Aaron mostly uses fast or slow, not the USER value.)

EFFECT (mostly same as the Freeze side): OCTAVE blends a harmonic — left half = half-speed (lower), right half = double-speed (upper); at noon it's disabled. TEXTURE = gritty overdrive on the right, lower-sample-rate bit-reduction on the left. ANIMATE toggle: middle = off, left = vibrato (impacted by Size — small glitch size = less vibrato; larger size brings the vibrato in), right = a nice slow chorus that just adds movement.
