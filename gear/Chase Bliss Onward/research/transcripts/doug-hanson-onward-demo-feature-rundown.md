Doug Hanson's demo is the single most efficient, accurate feature rundown of the Onward — a clean spoken spec of every control, the routing, and all eight dip switches in under two minutes before the sound demo. Confirms MIDI clock behavior.

Today we're checking out the Chase Bliss Onward. Onward is a dynamic sampler that is triggered by the input signal. It's made up of two channels that run in parallel: the Freeze channel and the Glitch channel. Freeze produces pad-like sounds; Glitch produces rhythmic, repeated sounds. Samples on each channel can be locked in with the footswitches.

SIZE sets the length of the sample, and when synced to MIDI clock it sets the subdivision. SUSTAIN sets how long the sample repeats for, and when all the way up the sustain is infinite. The FADE toggle lets you have the samples fade in and out slowly, immediately, or at a rate that you decide on (USER).

On the right side is the effect section. OCTAVE blends in a layer that is either double-speed (octave up) or half-speed (octave down). TEXTURE adds soft clipping on the right side of the knob or sample-rate reduction on the left side. The ANIMATE toggle adds either chorus or vibrato.

Onward has three error types, selected by the toggle: TIMING changes the sample size; CONDITION introduces mutes and sample-rate shifts; PLAYBACK changes the playback speed and direction. The likelihood and intensity of these errors is decided by the ERROR knob. The timing of the errors is linked to the SIZE knob, meaning they stay in time rhythmically with whatever the sample size is. A hidden option turns the Error knob into ERROR BLEND, which lets you use all three error types simultaneously.

Errors, effects, as well as the Fade and Sustain controls, can be routed to both the Freeze and Glitch channels or just one channel. For example, you could have a sub-octave Freeze that slowly swells in and out and pair it alongside a repeating Glitch with playback-speed errors.

MANUAL mode lets you re-trigger the sampling on each channel manually instead of it being envelope-triggered. HALF SPEED cuts the recording quality in half for the Glitch side, resulting in double the max sample size. REVERSE plays the Glitch side in reverse. DUCK causes sounds from Onward to dip in volume when input signal is present. SIDECHAIN causes the Freeze channel to dip in volume whenever the Glitch channel resets. The other dip switches let you modulate parameters and customize Onward for your specific setup.
