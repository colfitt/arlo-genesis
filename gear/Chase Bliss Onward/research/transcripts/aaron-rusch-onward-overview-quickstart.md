Aaron Rusch Guitar · Chase Bliss Onward Pedal Review & Demo: Overview · 2024-08-06

Part 1 of Aaron Rusch's 5-part Onward series (bought the pedal himself, not sponsored). This is the quick-start overview: I/O, the two channels, presets, and a control-by-control pass. The most methodical walkthrough on YouTube.

It's a sampler pedal: it takes the sound you play, plays it back after affecting it, and if it hears another sound you play, it replaces the original and plays back the new one. That's the high-level premise. Two channels, run in parallel. But this pedal does not stack sounds, so it's not a looper per se — it's almost like a "looper light." It's not a looper.

JACKS: there's an input and an output. They can be mono or stereo. There's a dip switch on the back labeled MISO (Mono In, Stereo Out) — flip it and this can be the pedal in your chain that changes your signal from mono to stereo.

The GLITCH channel samples and repeats small chunks of audio. The FREEZE channel captures and sustains a single moment in time. The left footswitch turns on Glitch; the right footswitch turns on Freeze. (Mix at 50% here.) Both footswitches work similarly — tap to enable — but you can also hold them down to lock a sample that you can play over.

PRESETS: the middle toggle is the preset toggle. You get two presets, one on the right and one on the left; the center of the toggle is the live setting (whatever the knobs are set to). To save to the right slot, hold the right switch for 3 seconds and then add the left for 3 seconds. To save to the left slot, hold the left for 3 seconds and then the right. The middle LED blinks: green means saved to the left, red means saved to the right.

SIZE does a lot: it sets the length of the Glitch, the overall timing of other aspects of the pedal, how long Freeze holds one note before grabbing the second, and it controls the subdivisions when MIDI is clock-synced.

MIX sets the balance between the input and the pedal (dry/wet) for both effects. If ramping is engaged, this knob controls the ramp speed (set via a dip switch).

The rest breaks into three sections, the way the manual explains it: an ERROR section, a SHAPE section, and an EFFECT section.

ERROR knob sets the likelihood (and sometimes intensity) of an error occurring. Three types set by the toggle: TIMING changes sample size; CONDITION introduces mutes and sample-rate shifts; PLAYBACK destabilizes playback speed and direction. (Demos each at full mix on Glitch — timing "does an awful lot," condition "gets pretty extreme," playback gives reversing.)

SHAPE: the SUSTAIN knob sets how long sounds hold before falling away; maxed out it goes infinite. The Size knob also impacts the sustain. The FADE toggle has three types: SLOW (left), USER (center, fine-tuned in hidden options), and FAST (right). Slow fade takes a long time to come in; fast is immediate.

EFFECT: OCTAVE blends in an octave — left half brings in a half-speed lower-octave voice, right half brings in an upper-octave voice; centered it does nothing. TEXTURE adds grit — overdrive on the right half, sample-rate reduction on the left; at noon it's disabled. The ANIMATE toggle: centered = off, left = vibrato, right = a slow chorus.

I/O recap: right side has a port for expression pedal or CV; left side has a port for MIDI, or you can hook up an external footswitch to turn on the Glitch only. On the back: lots of dip switches (covered later). Needs a 9V 200 mA supply. There's a USB port (presumably for firmware updates).
