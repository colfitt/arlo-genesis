Aaron Rusch Guitar · Chase Bliss Onward Demo 4: Hidden Options · 2024-10-10

Part 4 of Aaron Rusch's series — the most parameter-dense video on the Onward. Covers how to access the hidden menu, the factory reset, every hidden option, the power-up gestures, tap tempo, and per-section routing.

ACCESS: hold down both footswitches, then turn a knob. Most knobs have white text under them indicating which hidden option they control.

FACTORY RESET (all hidden options to default): flip the preset toggle left and back to center three times; the lights blink; then press both footswitches. (Done at the start of the demo.)

SENSITIVITY (on SIZE): how easy it is to trigger Onward with your playing dynamics. They say around noon works best in most cases. Turned way down (less sensitive), it stops catching softer notes — you have to dig in harder to get it to grab what you're playing. Turned high, it grabs things you didn't want — little clicks, string scrapes. It's a feel thing; Aaron keeps it at noon.

BALANCE (on MIX): adjusts the relative loudness of the two channels. Hold both switches and turn Mix toward Glitch for all-Glitch, toward Freeze for all-Freeze. Very useful when you freeze a sound on one side and play on the other — e.g. a frozen drone underneath and the glitchy part on what you're actively playing; balance the two to taste.

DUCK DEPTH (on OCTAVE): controls the sensitivity and intensity of the ducking and side-chaining (those are dip-switch features; this hidden option sets their depth).

USER fade value (on SUSTAIN): the middle position of the Fade toggle is a USER setting. Hold both switches and turn Sustain left for a very slow fade-in, right for fast. This lets you fine-tune the fade beyond the fixed slow/fast positions. (Aaron likes it set around the middle.)

ERROR BLEND (on ERROR): lets you blend in the two unselected error types so you get a mix of all three. The Error knob still controls the overall chance of an error; this hidden option sets the likelihood that an error will be one of the unselected types. With it cranked right while set to Error 1 (Timing), random moments of Errors 2 and 3 (Condition, Playback) come in — incredibly chaotic.

EQ (on TEXTURE — not labeled, but it's the EQ): turn the knob to the right to thin out the signal and remove low frequencies; turn left to remove highs and darken the overall signal. Intuitive.

TAP TEMPO: tap both footswitches twice to enter tap-tempo mode (very helpful with the Glitch — set the glitch rate by tapping).

POWER-UP GESTURES (remembered across power cycles):
- DRY KILL — remove the dry signal from the output: hold the Glitch switch while powering up.
- TRAILS — let Onward's effects fade away smoothly rather than cutting on bypass: hold the Freeze switch while powering up.

ROUTING (per-section isolation): each toggle switch can isolate its section to one channel. Hold both footswitches, then flip the toggle. Example: to make ERROR only affect Freeze, hold both switches and flip the Error toggle right — now cranking Error leaves Glitch clean and only affects Freeze. The other toggles (Fade/Shape, Animate/Effect) work the same way — e.g. flip the Effect/Animate toggle left to route the effect (octave, texture, vibrato) to Glitch only.

To reset everything again: preset toggle left, left, left (wait for the flashing), press both buttons. Closing thought: "the Onward is a bit of a beast; to get it memorized you really have to spend time with it and play it."
