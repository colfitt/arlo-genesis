# Delay Dude review — distilled USAGE tips

- **Playing style IS the macro control:** *"the style of playing changes the freeze effect"* — gentle playing yields flat/smooth sounds, attacking quickly makes the effect sound *"choppy."* (Same "you get what you give" principle as the manual; confirms it in independent listening.)
- **MIX = float-away control:** *"if you want to float away completely, you can turn the mix pot all the way up"* for pure wet, no dry. (Useful for the rig's all-wet drone walls.)
- **Freeze-lock-and-play-over (the core trick, confirmed again):** *"Hold down the freeze footswitch while triggering glitches to play over the freeze sound,"* with incoming audio passing through unaffected.
- **Hidden-options value call-out:** worth setting *"the sensitivity… to your own guitar playing or the pickups,"* plus per-section routing to assign an effect to Freeze **or** Glitch independently — explicitly to *avoid* unwanted distortion on one channel while keeping it on the other. (Direct rig application: keep TEXTURE drive on Glitch only, leave Freeze pad clean.)
- **Honest gotcha:** *"Fast rock riffs are certainly not ideal"* — this is an ambient/textural box, not a tight rhythmic effect. SIZE strongly affects Freeze sustain/swell timing, so re-tune it when chasing a specific swell.
- Overall: *"absolute recommendation for fans of unusual glitch and freeze sounds."*
