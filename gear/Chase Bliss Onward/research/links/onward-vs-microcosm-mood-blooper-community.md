# Community framing: Onward vs Microcosm / MOOD / Blooper (the glitch-pedal field)

Pulled from reviews + forum framing to answer "how is it different from the rig's other glitch boxes." This is the community consensus, distilled — it backs the dossier's overlap analysis with outside voices rather than duplicating it.

- **Onward's job is fundamentally different from MOOD/Blooper (per guitar.com):** *"Unlike the MOOD and Blooper pedals which are about creating wholly original sounds, Onward is less about creating wholly original sounds and more about accompanying your playing with a bit of left-field ambience."* I.e. Onward = **dynamics-driven accompaniment that follows you**; MOOD/Blooper = **things you build/commit.** Complementary, not redundant.
- **vs Microcosm (community read):** Microcosm = *"granular looper & glitch"* with preset rhythmic patterns and a steeper learning curve, polished/washed-out ambient; you drive it from presets. Onward has **no preset patterns** — it's triggered by your pick attack. They're different engines that stack well in series (the dossier's Onward → Microcosm pipeline). Owners who compared MOOD vs Microcosm generally preferred Microcosm for depth (KVR / TheFretboard threads), but that predates the Onward and isn't an Onward verdict.
- **Long-term owner (Aaron Rusch, see transcript):** independently reports Onward does **not** replace a delay or an EHX Superego (freeze pedal) — consistent with "none of the named devices replicate it." His favorite use is as a **warping/sound-design layer over a loop**, very Microcosm/Stammen/Bockling-adjacent in spirit.
- **Multi-source angle (gearnews):** *"an excellent playground for glitch effects for field recordings, vocals, or drums"* and it *"automatically chops up the new signal"* when you feed it chord after chord — relevant for this rig because it'll happily sample the VG-800's synth/COSM output and banjo, not just plain guitar. Courtney Swain (Bent Knee) demoed it specifically on **vocals**.
