# Onward official manual — the FULL named factory "Ideas" list

The closest thing Onward has to a factory preset list is the manual's named **"Ideas"**
starting-points (it ships with only 2 onboard preset slots, no preset names — so these
manual recipes ARE the official "factory presets"). The on-disk dossier §13 captured only
**4** of them; the manual actually has **11**, grouped Freeze / Glitch / Error / Routing.
Settings are given as **per-knob icon directions** (knob graphic with a pointer = turn that
control up / to that side), not full clock-faces — so the directional emphasis is exact and
official, but precise clock values for the un-iconed knobs are "to taste." All quotes are
verbatim from the manual; provenance = **factory (Chase Bliss)**.

## Freeze Ideas (printed p. 24–25)

- **SUB SYNTH** — icons: **OCTAVE down (left/min)**, **SUSTAIN up**.
  *"Turn Onward into a doomy synth that follows along and fills out the lower register. As
  long as OCTAVE is at the minimum position, the rest is up to you. Experiment with FADE,
  TEXTURE, and ANIMATE."* (Already in dossier.)
- **LIVING PAD** — icons: **FREEZE (held/locked)**, **ERROR up**.
  *"Hold the Freeze footswitch down to lock in an infinite pad – the classic freeze effect.
  Now experiment with different ERROR settings to introduce motion and variance to bring
  that pad to life."* (Already in dossier as "Living Pad / Dancing Drone".)
- **INSTANT ENO** — the only patch shown with a **full 9-knob icon grid**: SIZE, MIX,
  OCTAVE, ERROR, SUSTAIN, TEXTURE, TYPE, FADE, ANIMATE all marked active (each knob icon
  is turned up to a moderate/upper position; OCTAVE icon centered-ish, SUSTAIN upper).
  *"This setting will turn any sounds that you play into a sprawling ambient composition.
  Play slowly and be patient; it will take awhile for your new samples to fade in."*
  (Dossier distilled this to: MIX noon, SIZE ~2:00, OCTAVE noon, ERROR ~10:00, SUSTAIN max,
  TEXTURE noon, TYPE=Timing, FADE=Slow, ANIMATE=Chorus — note those exact clock values are
  the dossier author's reading of the icon grid, not printed numbers.)

## Glitch Ideas (printed p. 29) — 3 of these are NEW vs the dossier

- **AUTO-REPEATER** — icons: **SUSTAIN down**, **FADE (toward fast/right)**.
  *"A classic repeater effect that will sample and infinitely loop each note you play. A
  simple and fun starting point. Turn SUSTAIN down and the repeats will cleanly and
  instantly disappear after a chosen amount of time."* (Dossier had this; dossier added
  SIZE ~11, ERROR center — those are dossier additions, not printed.)
- **SWELLING DELAY** *(NEW)* — icons: **SUSTAIN down**, **SIZE down**, **FADE (slow/left)**.
  *"A vaporous pseudo-echo with repeats that gradually swell in and crossfade with one
  another as you play. These settings are just starters – adjust to taste."*
- **TRUE GLITCH** *(NEW)* — icons: **ERROR up**, **TYPE = middle/Condition**.
  *"The middle ERROR TYPE makes it easy to create more natural sounding glitches that pop in
  and out instead of repeating steadily. Adjust ERROR to control how broken and sporadic the
  glitches are."*
- **SPEEDY STARTER** *(NEW)* — icons: **SIZE up**, **OCTAVE up (right/double-speed)**,
  **SUSTAIN up**.
  *"Turn up OCTAVE to introduce a secondary, harmonizing voice that plays at 2x speed.
  Pairing this with Glitch produces dense phrases with rhythmic interplay that act as great
  launching points. Play around for a while until you hear a phrase you like, then hold the
  Glitch footswitch to lock it in."*

## Error Ideas (printed p. 36–37)

- **DANCING DRONE** — icons: **ERROR up**, **TYPE = Playback**, **FREEZE**.
  *"Each of the errors are useful in their own way for lending a bit of motion and intrigue
  to a frozen sound. Having TYPE set to Playback will introduce momentary pitch shifts that
  can make a simple freeze feel like a generative composition."* (Dossier merged this into
  Living Pad; manual lists it separately with TYPE=Playback as the key.)
- **UH OH** *(NEW)* — icons: **ERROR up**, **ERROR BLEND (hidden, on)**.
  *"Anything can go wrong in this setting. Each error is equally likely to occur, giving you
  lots of variation and surprise. Adjust the ERROR knob to have more or less errors overall."*
  (ERROR BLEND = the hidden option under ERROR that runs Timing + Condition + Playback at
  once — printed p. 17.)

## Routing Ideas (printed p. 38–39)

- **COMPLEX COUPLE** *(NEW)* — *"This is a special one. The various effects give energy and
  nuance to the Freeze channel, while the errors create motion and variation on the Glitch
  side. The two channels move as one, but make very different, complementary effects with
  loads of depth."* (A routing patch: Effects → Freeze, Errors → Glitch, via the hidden
  per-section TYPE/FADE/ANIMATE routing toggles, p. 38.)
- **FRANTIC COMPANY** — icons: **SUSTAIN**, routing **all → Glitch**.
  *"With these settings, and everything routed only to Glitch, you will be greeted with a
  wild burst of energy whenever you play a note. In between you will have the opposite – a
  soft, infinite freeze."* (This is the same "frantic company" Courtney Swain reads off in
  the demo — confirming it's a real factory-named idea.)

## Why this matters for the rig
- **Sub Synth + Octave-down** = the baritone-JM doom bed already targeted.
- **Speedy Starter** (NEW) = a banjo-lead engine: 2x harmonizing voice + lock-to-follow,
  great for the banjo-as-lead-over-walls move.
- **Swelling Delay** (NEW) = vaporous crossfading pseudo-echo = the shoegaze swell wall
  without a dedicated reverb.
- **Uh Oh** (NEW) = the maximal "recorded-wrong" / all-errors chaos patch (Error Blend on).
- **Complex Couple** (NEW) = the cleanest official template for the rig's signature
  two-personality Onward patch (clean evolving Freeze bed + chaotic Glitch top).

⚠️ Value-confidence: knob *directions* are official/exact (read off the manual icons); precise
clock-face numbers are NOT printed for these — treat any clock values as starting interpretations.
