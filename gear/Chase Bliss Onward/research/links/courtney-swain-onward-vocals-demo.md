# Courtney Swain — Onward on vocals (CB demo)
Companion IG post: https://www.instagram.com/chasebliss/p/C761k1QsyTb/
Full local transcript: `research/transcripts/courtney-swain-onward-vocals-demo.md`

Courtney Swain (vocalist, **Bent Knee**) is "let loose on Onward for the first time" in CB's video studio. Most instructive artist use captured so far because she narrates her technique AND reads named factory presets aloud while improvising. She's a singer, not a guitarist — but the dynamic-sampling workflow is instrument-agnostic and maps directly onto this rig's banjo/baritone sources.

## Her core technique — voice as "20 more musicians"
- **Freeze = base / Glitch = rhythmic texture, played together:** *"the freeze side for me is serving as the base, and then the glitch side is serving as sort of this active rhythmic textural voice that kind of chimes in."* The two-channel division of labour (sustained bed + stuttering top) is the whole move.
- **Detach the event from the trigger:** *"by just detaching the event from when you engage it… it feels like a different voice kind of coming into the room… you're adding a randomness to your own music."*
- **One phrase → scattered fragments:** *"I could be singing one phrase and it could just split out just moments of that phrase and capture that as a tone or texture."*
- **End-of-chain placement:** *"integrating this right off the bat… hello at the end of my chain… like bringing in 20 more musicians."*
- **MANUAL mode for control:** *"with manual I can just be singing one line and then I have two ways of capturing or stopping the sound"* — taps to resample/lock instead of dynamic triggering.
- **SIDECHAIN vs DUCK, in her words:** side-chain pumps the Freeze when Glitch resets; *"the duck is when there's incoming signal"* (both channels dip while you sing/play).
- **"Bounce the size"** — she ramps/bounces the SIZE knob (the dip-switch Bounce LFO on SIZE) for evolving glitch subdivisions.

## Named factory presets she reads off (copyable starting points)
- **Sprawling-ambient setting:** *"size to 3:00, mix all the way, baby — this setting will turn any sounds that you play into a sprawling ambient composition."* → **SIZE ~3 o'clock, MIX full wet.** (Hardest concrete numbers in the whole demo.)
- **"frantic company":** *"you will be greeted with a wild burst of energy whenever you play a note; in between you will have the opposite — a soft infinite freeze."* (Dynamics-extreme glitch on attack + infinite Freeze pad in the gaps.)
- **"a classic repeater effect":** *"simple and infinitely loop[-able]."*
- A fourth **"special one":** two contrasting textures at once — *"so much rhythm coming out of one side and then one side's just kind of giving you something to stand on."*

## Rig relevance
Her "Freeze = harmonic bed under a glitch lead" framing is exactly this rig's **banjo-as-lead over sustained walls** aesthetic: lock the Freeze on a baritone-JM chord for the bed, let the banjo's percussive picking drive the Glitch top voice. The "split one phrase into scattered fragments" idea is the banjo-roll → rhythmic stutter use from dossier §6.
