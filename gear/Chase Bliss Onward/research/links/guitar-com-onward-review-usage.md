# guitar.com hands-on review — distilled USAGE / placement tips

The best-written review; the dossier already cites it for sonics and the BigSky pairing. Captured here for the concrete *workflow / placement* tips that are forum-grade practical:

- **Chain placement (the reviewer's recommended spot):** *"the easiest way to use Onward is after your volume section but prior to any modulation or time-based effects."* Caveat: *"there is no right or wrong way of placing Onward in your chain"* — placement just changes how it responds. (For this rig: that argues for Onward EARLY on the texture board, feeding Microcosm → Dark Star, exactly the dossier's proposal.)
- **The one dip switch he singles out as most useful:** *"the one that controls momentary and latching functions"* (i.e. LATCH). He notes the dips are *"well-labelled and clearly explain how each one operates."* (Matches the Aaron Rusch owner workflow — LATCH on is what makes hold-to-lock practical.)
- **The defining gotcha (dynamics → volume):** *"no matter what side you're on, this pedal will jump in volume depending on the dynamics of your pick attack. In short, when you get loud, it gets loud."* → budget headroom / watch a downstream limiter; this is the #1 thing to tame for the rig's hot GK-5 source.
- **Freeze-side sweet spot:** MIX **at noon** gives sustaining *"glitchy ambient pad-like chords,"* and the differentiator vs a plain freeze is that *"freeze [moves] along with your playing dynamics."*
- **Most creative interaction:** *"The interaction between the Error knob, Type switch, and Glitch section is the most creatively adventurous experience"* — ranges from a *"precursive ambient style pad that ebbs and flows"* to chaotic layering.
- Pairing the reviewer loved: Onward → Strymon BigSky = *"luscious dreamscapes."* (Rig analogue: Onward → Dark Star V3 / H90.)
