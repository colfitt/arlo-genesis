On today's video I'm going to show you how to turn the Strymon TimeLine into a **self-contained drone machine**.

## Signal chain (front of pedal)
Baritone guitar (a standard electric is fine too) → **compressor** (Wampler Ego, just squishing the dynamic range on a clean tone) → **distortion** (Strymon Sunset, set fuzzy — use whatever dirt you have) → **volume pedal** (Morley Little Alligator, for swells) → **Strymon TimeLine**.

## Step 1 — pick a smeared delay for the drone bed
The Strymon delays that work for drone are the more smeared/diffuse ones. He likes the **Ice** machine. He uses a preset called "Melt Away": a shimmer-type delay with the upper note set to a **perfect fifth above the root**, and the Ice machine set **very smeared / diffuse**. That gives a good foundation for a drone.

## Step 2 — looper after the delay
In the TimeLine **globals**, set the **looper to POST** ("looper after"), so it loops the signal *including* the delay.

## Step 3 — make a silent loop, then volume-swell into it
Enter looper mode. Use the known YouTube trick to create an **empty/silent loop**: turn the loop on to **Record**, count out **eight beats**, then switch to **Overdub** ("two three four five six seven eight — overdub"). You're now overdubbing onto a silent loop of a pre-determined length. Now use the **volume pedal to create swells** with the Ice delay machine and feed them into the looper, playing **longer than one loop** so the layers blend together. Then set the looper to **Play** — it loops over and over, a nice-sounding bed.

## Step 4 — exit the looper but keep it playing, then play over it
In globals, set **Loop Exit to continue Play** (so the loop keeps running when you leave looper mode). Exit by holding the TAP switch a few seconds — the loop keeps playing. Now you're free to **select a different delay preset** to play live over the drone.

## Step 5 — Frippertronics layer over the top
He switches to a **dTape** preset set for **a lot of tape decay, fuzziness, and grunge**, at the **longest time (2.5 s)** with **a lot of repeats**, and plays in a **Frippertronics** style over the loop (overdrive switched on). "A really grungy tape sound." Playing different notes over the held drone lets you work the low drone around. (In the mixdown he adds a touch of reverb from the DAW to fill it out — how he'd normally mix a full piece.)
