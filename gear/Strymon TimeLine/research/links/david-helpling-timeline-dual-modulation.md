Award-winning ambient recording artist. His TimeLine approach is a Dual-machine modulation technique that's very usable for drone/pad work.

## TimeLine preset: "WETISH" — Dual machine
- Machine: DUAL — **two independent delay lines, NOT ping-pong**, panned hard Left and Right, sharing a single LFO.
- He deliberately uses the Dual machine to get **two very colorful modulation delays** spread across the stereo field.
- Numeric knob values are shown only as a settings image on the page (not printed in text) — capture the image from the link if exact values needed. The defining choice is the parallel/independent-L-R Dual config + heavy SPEED/DEPTH modulation.

## Quotes / technique
- On Dual: he loves having "TWO very colorful modulation delays" with independent L/R panning, not a Ping Pong effect.
- "Having dedicated speed and depth knobs is so cool and lets me dial things in while playing." → he performs the modulation live via the SPEED/DEPTH knobs.
- Companion BigSky preset "LO 5TH": Shimmer machine tuned **down a 5th, shimmer off** — generates a lower fifth under the dry note. Advice: "Lean toward the higher register and play sparsely to leave room for the magic."
- Production note: the track is "a live improvised piece recorded in one take with minimal post processing" — i.e. these are real live-playable settings, not studio overdubs.

## Rig fit
Maps to the dossier's stereo-spread thinking: TimeLine Dual (parallel) gives baritone/banjo rhythmic motion across L/R. Helpling's "play sparsely, leave room" + the down-a-5th reverb pairing is a clean blueprint for banjo-as-lead floating over a drone.
