strymon.net · "This Week's Preset" · October Crifasi (feat. Joel Gilardini) · 2018-08-30

NEW artist preset set — three TimeLine presets Joel Gilardini (experimental/ambient guitarist, The Land of the Snow) uses for live looping improvisation. Free .syx for all three via Strymon Librarian/Nixie (links in post). Machine + load-bearing behaviour quoted verbatim; exact knob numbers are in the settings images / .syx.

## "Megaphone" — Lo-Fi machine used AS A FILTER (not as a delay)
- TYPE: LO-FI
- Used to "create a filter effect instead of a delay" — i.e. very short/zero delay, the Lo-Fi gadget filter doing the work (megaphone/telephone voicing).
- EXPRESSION PEDAL: assigned to feedback — sweep feedback live with the EXP pedal.
Rig fit: this is the "Lo-Fi as a tone-mangler, not an echo" trick — sits with the Hammock Lo-Fi approach and the rig's degraded/"recorded-wrong" lean; EP-on-feedback = performable runaway.

## "Descent" — Ice machine, slow downward pitch drift
- TYPE: ICE
- "the pitch of the repeats is shifted down really slowly" — a slowly sinking/descending Ice tail.
- "High feedback option available" (long, piling repeats).
Rig fit: a darkening, sinking drone tail — pairs with baritone for a seasick descending wall; the down-pitch Ice is the inverse of the bright Solstice/Thanks-a-Million octave-up Ice.

## "Dirty Swell" — Swell machine + Grit
- TYPE: SWELL
- "crank a bit of the Grit, which gives a cool crunchy, creamy texture"
- In Gilardini's chain: paired with a long reverb and a wah pedal.
Rig fit: a gritty volume-swell pad — the crunchy cousin of the factory Swell presets (Infinite Swell, Smeared Swell); add Smear + HIPASS for the bowed banjo-as-lead pad with dirt.
