Two notable experimental/art-rock TimeLine sightings, captured with honest caveats. (Plus the Bon Iver rack, sourced separately via XTS — see note below.)

## Jonny Greenwood (Radiohead / The Smile)
- First public TimeLine use: **The Smile's Instagram live stream, Dec 2, 2021.** One TimeLine floating to the right of his pedalboard, plus **a second TimeLine velcro'd to the band's Prophet '08 poly-synth** (i.e. used as a synth FX delay, not just guitar).
- King of Gear notes this was "a bit of a surprise, since Jonny is usually averse to pedals with menu-diving and multiple modes."
- Possibly inherited from bandmate Thom Yorke — both Ed O'Brien and Thom Yorke had toured with TimeLines previously.
- **CAVEAT — short-lived:** by the Jan 2022 The Smile rehearsal streams the TimeLine was **replaced with a Boss DD-200**; King of Gear suggests Jonny "preferred the simpler layout of the DD-200 for live performances." No specific TimeLine songs/settings documented for his use.

## Bon Iver (Justin Vernon)
- A Strymon TimeLine appears in the **XTS-built rack gear for Bon Iver** (visible in one of the rack trays in XTS's published photos). Source is XTS's own rack-build documentation referenced across gear sites; treat as a "present in the rack" data point rather than a documented technique — no specific settings or song attribution found.

## Honest framing
These are real but illustrative-of-ubiquity sightings, not deep signature uses. They reinforce the dossier's point: the TimeLine's fame is *ubiquity* across art-rock/ambient boards rather than one artist's signature voice. For actual on-aesthetic *technique*, Hammock and David Helpling (separate captured files) are far more useful than these two.
