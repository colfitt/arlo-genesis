Two NEW official Strymon Ice-machine presets (free .syx via Strymon Librarian/Nixie, linked in each post). These are the shimmer/banjo-as-lead-pad territory and are NOT in the factory-card subset already on disk. Strymon's posts give the machine + the load-bearing parameters in words; the exact bar-graph knob numbers live in the settings image / .syx, noted qualitatively below. Quoted phrases are verbatim.

## "Solstice Ice" — Ice machine (Matt Piper, 2016-12-21)
A crystalline +2-octave sparkle from a very short delay — bell/harp-like, the bright end of the Ice range.
- TYPE: ICE
- TIME: "60 milliseconds" (very short)
- INTERVAL: pitched "up two octaves" (+2 OCT)
- SLICE: "short slice size"
- FILTER: "turned up pretty high" (toward the right → brighter / tape-like EQ on repeats)
- GRIT: dialed in — "adds some distortion" and "tames the sound"
- DEPTH (mod): up — "Adds a nice stereo image"
- (BLEND / SMEAR / HIPASS / MIX / REPEATS numbers only in the image/.syx)

Rig fit: this is the +2-oct counterpart to factory "Ice Organ" (22B, also +2 OCT but 210 ms). 60 ms + short slice = a fast glittering top layer to float a banjo line into; pull FILTER back and add HIPASS ~80–160 Hz to keep bright banjo from ice-picking.

## "Thanks a Million" — Ice machine (Matt Piper, 2015-12-04)
A lush, gooey +1-octave Ice wash — warmer and more washed-out than Solstice.
- TYPE: ICE
- INTERVAL: slices "pitched up one octave" (+1 OCT)
- BLEND: "set just about in the middle" (≈ 50/50 Ice line ↔ dry delay line)
- SMEAR: "set at maximum to soften the attack of the repeats"
- FILTER: "cranked all the way to the right, resulting in an EQ curve similar to that of a Tape delay"
- GRIT: "dialed in some"
- MOD SPEED & DEPTH: "turned up … to add some gooey goodness and stereo lushness"
- (TIME / MIX / REPEATS numbers only in the image/.syx)

Rig fit: Smear-max + Filter-full-right + Blend-mid + mod-up is a near-recipe for a smeared, tape-voiced octave-up pad — drop the +OCT to root or a 5th for the banjo-as-lead pad; this is the most directly copyable Ice voicing of the two.
