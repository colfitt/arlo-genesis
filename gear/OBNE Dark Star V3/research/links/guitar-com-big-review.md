# Dark Star Stereo — guitar.com "Big Review" (directional recipes)

NOTE: This source gives directional knob guidance, not full copyable patches. Verbatim/near-verbatim guidance distilled below. Flagged where it disagrees with our dossier.

## Neutral starting point
- "Everything set to halfway" already "sounds magical" — the reviewer's recommended starting position is **all knobs at noon**. Filter, both Pitch shifters, and Crush all have center detents at noon, so noon = neutral pad reverb. (Matches dossier §3.)

## Shimmer / pitch
- Pitch knobs are **stepped in semitones**; turn a Pitch knob **clockwise (right)** for a "heightened pitch reverb, reminiscent of shimmer-style reverbs."
- For octave-up: keep it simple with a "straight octave-up effect" on one Pitch knob.
- Detune/chorus: set a Pitch knob "just a smidge either side of the middle notch" for modulation.

## Crush (the headline move)
- Counter-clockwise (left of noon) = "low-resolution effects" / bit-crush.
- Clockwise (right of noon) = "a mighty blast of overdriven reverb in full stereo" — reviewer calls the overdrive side "the absolute best sound on offer here."

## Stereo-into-mono shimmer trick (copyable technique)
- If you set the pedal to STEREO routing but only run ONE amp, the **Spread** control can be used to bleed some of the **Pitch 2** (right-channel) output into the unused channel — a way to dial in subtle shimmer amount even in a mono rig. Useful note for rig flexibility.

## ⚠️ Filter-direction conflict to resolve
- Several review sources (incl. guitar.com framing and russomusic) describe the Filter as **LPF to the right / HPF to the left**. Our dossier §2 (from the manual) says **LPF below noon / HPF above noon**. The manual is authoritative; flag the reviews as using loose "left/right" shorthand. Treat dossier as correct.
