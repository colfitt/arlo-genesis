# Dark Star Stereo — Premier Guitar review (recipes + caveats)

The "reverb-based synthesizer" review. Distilled control guidance:

## Neutral pad starting point
- "Starting with the Filter, both pitch shifters, and Crush knobs at noon — all have center detents — affords the most neutral settings and results in a pad reverb, as synthetic as but less sparkly than a shimmer." → **noon / noon / noon** is the reference pad.

## Shimmer + low octave simultaneously
- Two independent shifters mean you "can have both a shimmer effect and a low octave at the same time" — e.g. **Pitch 1 = octave up, Pitch 2 = octave down** for a stacked octave pad. Pitch is continuously variable in the center zone for detune.

## Drone / scanning technique (copyable performance move)
- "Cranking the Multiply and Decay knobs, you can drop in a few notes or a chord and work by scanning the Pitch knobs and sculpting with the Filter." → **Multiply high + Decay high**, play a chord, then sweep Pitch + Filter by hand to morph the drone. This is the core ambient-performance recipe.

## Self-oscillation
- High Decay + Multiply builds runaway sustained textures; PG's quip: "spend too much time there and you might need a name for your new ambient band."

## Tone references
- Neutral textural pads explicitly likened to **Harold Budd and Brian Eno**.

CONFIDENCE: Best-written review of the three; gives the most usable "drop a chord, scan the knobs" technique. Still directional, not numeric patches.
