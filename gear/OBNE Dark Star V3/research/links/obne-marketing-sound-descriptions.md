# Dark Star Stereo — OBNE's own sound/feature descriptions

OBNE has NO centralized preset-sharing platform (no op1.fun / Nixie equivalent), so the manufacturer's marketing copy + manual are the closest thing to "official sounds." Distilled:

## How OBNE frames the voice
- "A characterful lo-fi reverb, ideal for slow attack and pad generation." Core voice = long, dark, slightly-aliased reverb; "almost an instrument of its own."
- The V3/Stereo's key change vs originals: the three original algorithms (Pitch, Delay/Lag, Crush) now run **simultaneously and continuously** instead of as a 3-way toggle.

## Aux footswitch named jobs (OBNE's three assignable modes)
- **Hold** = infinite sustain / freeze (blocks new input, freezes current wash).
- **Filter** = slams low-pass closed while held (swell/duck move).
- **Pitch** = jumps both shifters to **+1 octave** while held (instant octave-up stab).

## Control intents (OBNE copy)
- Filter: LPF or HPF on the reverb only (wet-tone shaper).
- Pitch 1 / Pitch 2: independent ±2 octave shifts (reverb "chords" possible).
- Crush: sample-rate reduction (CCW) OR overdrive (CW).
- Lag: predelay before the reverb.
- Multiply: feedback into the whole system; "enhancing effects like rising and falling pitch shifts" → enables self-oscillation.
- Decay: "from something like a large tiled bathroom to a space slightly wider than the universe" (→ near-infinite).

## Artwork credit (not an endorsing musician)
- Pedal artwork by illustrator **Jon Carling** (joncarling.com) — not a player.

## No factory-preset names published
- The 3 onboard presets ship user-overwritable; OBNE does NOT publish named factory presets with knob charts. Confirmed gap — patches live as community knob settings, not official files.
