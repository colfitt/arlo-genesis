THE BIG HOLD GOTCHA (most important practical finding)
- When the Aux footswitch's HOLD (near-infinite sustain) is engaged, "anything you add is LOCKED OUT of the reverb circuit and will therefore be BONE-DRY." (guitar.com)
- Meaning: Hold freezes the EXISTING wash but BLOCKS new input into the reverb — you can play over the frozen pad, but your new notes stay dry (only the pre-Hold wash is wet). This is by design (it's how the freeze stays static) but it surprises people expecting to keep layering wet notes.
- Reviewer's recommended workflow around it: use an EXPRESSION PEDAL for swell/sustain-style moves instead of the Hold footswitch, and reassign the Aux footswitch to preset toggling. "Plugging in an expression pedal can do everything the footswitch can do but a hundred times better." The momentary pitch/filter footswitch jumps are called "limited in musical usefulness."

AUX FOOTSWITCH — three assignable jobs (Russo Music walkthrough)
- HOLD: sustains the reverb tone infinitely (freeze).
- PITCH: momentarily sets BOTH pitch shifters to +1 octave, auto-returns to their set values on release.
- FILTER: momentarily slams the low-pass filter closed, reverts on release (duck/swell move).
- BONUS: hold the Aux switch + tap Preset to step through presets from your foot.

EXPRESSION SETUP PROCEDURE
- Assign to ANY combination of knobs. Set heel/toe endpoints by moving the desired knob(s) WHILE HOLDING the On/Off (Bypass) footswitch; untouched knobs are unaffected. Saved per preset.

RIG-RELEVANT TAKEAWAY (banjo/baritone, Dark Star as Board-2 terminus)
- For the "freeze a drone, solo over it" move this rig wants: the frozen bed will be wet, but the banjo lead you play over it will be DRY through Dark Star (unless a downstream reverb on Board 3 wets it). That's actually ideal here — Board 3 (H90/Chroma) can add space to the lead while Dark Star holds the static pad. But know the dry-during-Hold behavior going in.
