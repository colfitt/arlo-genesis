The lineage / "original mono vs Stereo V3" community framing — what changed and why owners of the original care.

ORIGINAL DARK STAR (mono) vs DARK STAR STEREO (V3)
- Original had "3 SEPARATE and INDEPENDENT modes of operation — Pitch / Delay / Crush" selected by a toggle. You could only be in one at a time.
- The Stereo edition lets you "use all those textural elements TOGETHER — with more options and a far higher degree of granularity." This is THE headline change: the toggle is gone, all three voices run simultaneously and continuously.
- Added vs original: stereo I/O (+ mono routing options), TWO independent pitch shifters (±2 oct each, vs the original's dual-interval single Pitch mode), the "Multiply" feedback loop, Stereo Spread, 3 onboard presets, MIDI + expression, and — noted explicitly — an "interestingly textured OVERDRIVE" on the opposite side of the Sample-Reduction (Crush) knob (the original only crushed; the V3 adds OD past noon).
- Launch price $299 / £299 (now $329). Colorways at launch: blue, black/monochrome, pink/red.

WHY IT MATTERS (community read)
- Karlsson frames it for players who like "Reverb Workstation pedals" with "lots of different options and flavours" — i.e. the V3 moves Dark Star from a one-trick character box toward a deep, recallable ambience workstation, while keeping the curated/limited-palette feel.
- Counterpoint from elsewhere (guitar.com, gearnews): original-Dark-Star loyalists "bonded with its warmth and aliasing... and the way it encouraged you to turn the Mix fully up" — some prefer the simpler mono original. The V3 is a superset, not a re-voicing, so it can reproduce the original's sound but asks more of you to dial it.
