AndyDemos · Old Blood Noise Endeavors Dark Star Stereo · 2024-12-19

A clean, structured demo that builds sounds layer by layer — core voice first, then pitch, multiply, lag, crush, and the Aux moves (filter sweep, pitch ramp, infinite freeze).

The Dark Star Stereo picks up where the original best-selling reverb left off. It strips away the confines of selective modes and offers all the previous sounds at once. Two independent pitch-shifted voices range from two octaves below to two octaves above, while a multi-functional bit Crush / overdrive knob adds the lo-fi touch to slow-blooming pads and shimmer effects. The Filter knob controls low-pass and high-pass of the wet signal — to embellish long decay times or head in a more lo-fi direction. On the back is an expression input assignable to any parameter such as Mix, Decay, and Lag time (which extends the pre-delay for more rhythmic, reflective reverb). Use Multiply to add feedback to the whole processed sound; Spread controls the width of the right and left channels. The new Aux switch can be momentary or latching, serving many functions from infinite Decay to pitch ramping to instant filter sweeps. Plus MIDI control and three onboard presets.

CORE VOICE — Filter straight up and down, no pitch, no bit-crushing. At only about 10:00 on the Decay knob you're already at a huge space — a lively hall or a cavernous space.

LONGER REFLECTIONS — tweak the Filter and increase the Lag (a longer delay time gives more reflections); a brighter filter sound gives a different "material on the walls."

FATTEN THE TAILS — two pitch voices fatten the long tails. Dial a simple low octave on Pitch 1. Then add a little Multiply, which helps the octaves spiral up or down.

LONGER LAG + DETUNE — a longer Lag time makes the effect swell in and separate from the dry signal. Dial in a bit of Pitch 2 detuning UP — almost like a classic whammy on the detune setting — while Pitch 1 goes down, for a sound that spreads.

CRUSH — for a more distinct rush of reverb. Left of center gives the earlier bit-crush sounds like Dark Star V1; right of center adds overdrive into the mix. With an even longer Decay and dual detuning, going left on Crush gives classic bit-crushing in a strong lo-fi direction (combined with going left on the Filter / low-pass).

AUX MOVES — treat the pedal as an instrument. Use the Aux switch to do a filter sweep to close it, or add infinite Decay at any point. Change the filter on the fly with Aux. Use Aux to ramp into octaves — as easy as holding Aux and moving the Pitch 1 knob. For infinite Decay/freeze, the Aux mutes the input signal and feeds the dry signal through while a frozen pad sits in the background (he turns up the amp's own reverb so the dry signal doesn't stick out).
