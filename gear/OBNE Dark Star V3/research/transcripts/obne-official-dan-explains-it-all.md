The official OBNE control-by-control walkthrough. The canonical reference for what every knob does.

Congratulations on your new Dark Star. At this point you know it's a reverb with pitch shifting, bit crushing, and filtering. It's really very simple if you take it one knob at a time. A written manual is also available online.

VOLUME — the first knob. All the way down, no sound comes out; all the way up, signal is boosted above unity. A simple control to be used as needed.

MIX — sets the blend of wet and dry (affected and unaffected) signals. All the way down is all dry; all the way up is all wet; the middle is a nice blend of the two. Note that the dry signal is analog dry-through, meaning it is never digitized — zero latency.

Now you're hearing the core voice of Dark Star, familiar to those who have had Dark Star before.

DECAY — sets the length of the reverb trails. It maintains that recognizable reverb but can go shorter if turned all the way down, and when turned all the way up can go quite long, taking a long time to trail off — allowing notes to build and stack on each other as they enter the reverb trail. If we hit the Aux button we can freeze those trails indefinitely and stop new signal from coming in. (There are a couple other things Aux can do, but we're getting ahead of ourselves.)

FILTER — can be used as a low-pass or high-pass filter. Turning it down from noon engages a low-pass filter that is lightly resonant; down a little it sounds like the classic Dark Star. As you continue, the filtering gets more intense until eventually no signal gets through. Above noon the filtering is gentler and is high-pass, so you can take some of the weight out of the signal. At max it's at about 2 kHz, and there's no resonance.

PITCH 1 — engages pitch shifting. At noon, no pitch shifting happens. Around noon you can detune upward or downward; once that detuning hits a semitone it continues semitone by semitone until hitting an octave, then finally at the very last step there's a second octave. So it goes from two octaves down to two octaves up, and it's pretty easy to find the sound you're looking for.

PITCH 2 — works exactly the same way on a second pitch-shifted signal. In stereo, Pitch 1 is on the left and Pitch 2 is on the right. In mono they are blended in parallel — like the pitch mode on the original Dark Star. Note that in mono, Pitch 2 at unity won't show up, to avoid stacking of multiple unity signals.

CRUSH — used to distort the signal. Below noon it introduces sample-rate reduction, like the Crush mode of the original Dark Star. This creates aliasing out of high frequencies and is a very unique form of distortion. Above noon it sends the signal through a soft-clipping saturator, kind of like an overdrive pedal but full-frequency and very soft — a more pleasant type of distortion.

LAG — adds a delay determining the time it takes for reverb to be heard. All the way down, reverb starts very soon after the dry signal; all the way up there's a quite noticeable delay time. When you wiggle this knob it adds a nice chorus sound.

MULTIPLY — can be thought of as a slightly chaotic feedback loop. It takes basically everything about the pedal — the trails themselves, the pitch shifting, the lag, the filtering, some of the crush elements — and feeds it all back into the input. This can create shimmering, noticeable delay repeats, or added trail lengths depending on how you use it with the other controls. In certain settings it may want to run away and start to cry out louder and louder; simply turn Multiply back down to calm it.

PRESETS — tap the preset switch to move through three onboard presets; the lights next to the switch indicate which one you're on. If no lights are illuminated, that's live mode (doing exactly what the knobs say). To save, hold the preset switch for 5 seconds — the light begins blinking, then cycle through to choose which slot (or live mode if you don't want to save). Additionally there are 127 presets that can be saved and recalled via MIDI; all of Dark Star is MIDI-controllable via PC and CC messages.

AUX SWITCH — previously shown to indefinitely sustain the trails (freeze). It can also be set to close the filter (low-pass all the way down) or set both pitches to one octave up. To assign, hold the Aux switch and move either the Filter, Decay, or Pitch 1 knob depending on the effect you want, then let go — now it's set. This setting is saved per preset. Aux can also switch presets: hold Aux then tap the preset switch to assign it; now pressing Aux moves through the three presets and live mode. To return, hold Aux and tap preset again, or wiggle a knob.

EXPRESSION — plug in an expression device via TRS cable to externally control any and all of the knobs on Dark Star. To assign, hold the On/Off footswitch and move the knob(s) you'd like to set, then let go. A moved knob's starting position is the expression heel position and its ending position is the toe position. You're free to express a number of gestures all at once. Saved per preset.

ON/OFF — turns Dark Star on and off; the reverb continues to trail when the pedal is off (many people like this). For true-bypass operation instead, hold bypass and hold preset for about 5 seconds until the preset lights blink — one blinks fast to show trails on, or slower to show trails off; let go of On/Off to lock it in.

ROUTING — this pair of switches also sets routing. Out of the box it's in mono (TS cable in, TS cable out). For mono-in-stereo-out, hold On/Off and tap the preset switch until the middle LED blinks. For true stereo, tap preset while holding On/Off until the bottom LED blinks. To return to mono, tap until the top LED blinks. Remember: TS cables for mono, TRS cables for stereo.

SPREAD — only used in stereo routing scenarios; pans the left and right sides. All the way clockwise, panning is as expected (left reverb on left, right reverb on right). In the middle, both signals are represented equally on both outputs. All the way counterclockwise, signals are reversed — reverb of the left signal shows up on the right side and vice versa. This can create interesting crosstalk when used in tandem with the Multiply control.

USB JACK — for firmware updates; don't worry about it for now. If your Dark Star needs an update OBNE will make a public announcement.
