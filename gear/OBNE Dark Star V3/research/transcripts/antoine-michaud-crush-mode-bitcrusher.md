A short, mostly-playing exploration of the ORIGINAL Dark Star's Crush (bit-crusher) mode. No talk track, so this is a notes-only entry from the title/description plus the same creator's settings video.

What it is: the demonstrator playing through the Bit-Crusher mode of the original Dark Star Pad Reverb, exploring the degraded sample-rate-reduction texture. His own framing in the description: "It sure is a very weird pedal, but I like how it is so different. I'm still trying to figure out how I can use this Crush mode to make beautiful music that doesn't sound too spooky." So the video is the "raw, slightly spooky" demonstration of the degraded texture before the later (2021) settings video, where he solves the "non-chaotic" problem with the pitch-tuning trick (aim the pitch knob for the "a" in "Dark," not exactly noon) and a lighter sample-rate amount.

Relevance to the V3: Crush mode on the original = the V3's Crush knob BELOW noon (sample-rate reduction / bit-crush aliasing). The takeaway carried into the settings video: heavy sample-rate reduction reads as "spooky/sci-fi"; back the bit-crush amount off toward the lighter side so you hear the crushing without it dominating, and tune the pitch carefully, to make the crushed wash musical rather than chaotic. The 100%-wet Mix move turns this crushed texture into a standalone bit-crushed drone.
