The Pedal Zone · Old Blood Noise Endeavors Dark Star Stereo Soundscape Reverb · 2024-11-21

The most-viewed deep demo (86k+ views, ~22 min). Heavily playing-focused (lots of music passages) with concise framing of the V3's design philosophy and the Aux/expression workflow. Best for hearing the actual textures; spoken content is lighter than Mark Johnston's.

Framing: one of the demonstrator's all-time favorite pedals "leveled up big time." A true stereo Dark Star has been a long-held dream; the Dark Light gave a subtle taste of it by combining the original Dark Star and Sunlight with a Spread control for stereo width. A similar Spread control is integrated into the new Dark Star, giving full control over the stereo image. He starts by setting up classic crushed ambience but in gloriously wide stereo.

KEY DESIGN POINT — the new Dark Star is "basically an open canvas." It gives access to all the parameters of the original Dark Star, but instead of being split into three different modes, you can have (for instance) two pitch-shift voices, some bit-crushing, AND some delay offset going all at the same time — something simply not possible on the original. On top of the familiar controls you get parameters that weren't available before, since several knobs are multifunctional depending on which way you turn them.

MOTION / AUX — Dark Star offers different ways to put motion into your sound. Use the onboard Aux switch to trigger predetermined momentary or latching effects. Set it up by holding down Aux and turning either: Filter (for filter sweeps), Pitch (for pitch whammies), or Decay (for the classic infinitely-sustaining reverb pad). You can also press the preset button and use Aux for preset switching.

EXPRESSION / MIDI — use the dedicated expression and MIDI ports for full customized control over every parameter. Examples he highlights: hook up an expression ramper set to control Filter for automatic cinematic filter sweeps; set it to control Mix for tremolo-like tones, combined with momentary pitch-shifting from the Aux control. His FAVORITE move: set expression to control LAG and put it on a random LFO wave, to get "beautiful, slightly sunbaked lo-fi tape modulation."

Closing reflection: he got his first Dark Star in 2016 and his sonic world was never the same — he'd never heard tones or textures like that. The big new Dark Star leaves the same feeling: the familiar tones and textures are all there, but vast new atmospheric worlds open up, without sacrificing the immediacy and ease of use of the original. The praise is that every new feature was added to ADD to the original Dark Star experience, not change it.
