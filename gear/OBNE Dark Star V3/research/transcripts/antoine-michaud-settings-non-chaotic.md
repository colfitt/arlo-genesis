Antoine Michaud · OBNE Dark Star Reverb: My Settings To Get Beautiful Sounds (non-chaotic!) · 2021-01-28

NOTE: This demos the ORIGINAL (mono, mode-toggle) Dark Star, but its core dial-in tips transfer directly to the V3's Crush, Pitch 1, and Pitch 2 knobs. The single most useful practical-technique video for taming the pedal so it sits in a song instead of sounding like "horror movie territory."

The problem he's solving: a viewer asked how to get usable sounds — it sounds cool on its own but as soon as you play over chords or in a song it sounds too chaotic. Whatever the knob positions, it never quite seems right.

THE PITCH-TUNING TRICK (the key takeaway). In Crush mode the pitch control sets the interval. Octave-down (knob fully left) sounds too low for playing low chords; octave-up (fully right) is too present/high. The sweet spot is the middle — but here's the catch: setting the knob EXACTLY at noon does NOT give clean unity pitch; it's still slightly detuned and sounds out of tune when you play over it. His fix: go to the RIGHT just one hair, NOT exactly at noon — specifically, aim the knob pointer at the center of the letter "a" in the word "Dark" printed on the pedal (the little hole of the "a"). It's not visually at noon, but it tunes the pitch much better, so you get a clean, in-tune reverb you can play chords over. (On the V3 this maps to finding true unity on Pitch 1 / Pitch 2 — the knob's mechanical noon is slightly off from pitch-unity, so nudge just off-center by ear until it's in tune.)

CRUSH / SAMPLE-RATE AMOUNT. The second control sets the sample-rate ratio (bit-crush amount). Turned far one way it gets very low-resolution — "too much" for him. He prefers it more toward the lighter side, where you hear the crushing of the bit-crusher but not too much. (On the V3, Crush below noon = this sample-rate reduction.)

PITCH MODE. The same principle applies: keep one pitch tuned (aimed at the "a"), and on the second pitch, try to replicate the exact position of the first knob but go just a hair to the right to slightly detune it — play at the same time to find the sweet spot. A tiny nudge in either direction starts to give modulation/chorusing. (On the V3 this is Pitch 1 vs Pitch 2 detune against each other for chorused width.)

THE 100%-WET DRONE MOVE. In Crush mode, use the Mix knob at 100% wet. Now tune the first control exactly how you want, choose the sample-rate ratio, and you hear NO dry signal — only the bit-crushed texture. It's like a drone sound on guitar, a bit-crushed drone. He really likes this.

Summary tip: aim the pitch knob for the "a" of "Dark" instead of exactly at noon, and you get beautiful tuned sounds out of the pedal.
