B's Music Shop · Old Blood Noise Endeavors Dark Star V3: The Ultimate Ambient Reverb Test · 2024-11-21

A full manual-style control read followed by a loose hands-on jam with an ambient-player guest. Good for the explicit per-knob behavior (it reads OBNE's official copy verbatim) and a relaxed real-world "poke at it" exploration.

OFFICIAL COPY READ. "Dark Star is a reverb pedal — historically our most popular reverb pedal. People bonded with its warmth and aliasing, its long trails, and the way it encouraged you to turn the mix fully up. Now it's a pitch-shifting, bit-crushing, filtered stereo reverb with just about every feature you could ask for — one massive voice with a wide suite of controls that likes to go anywhere in your signal chain."

CONTROLS (read from the manual):
- FILTER — low-pass or high-pass on the reverb. At noon it gently filters highs like the original. Below noon the low-pass cutoff decreases until no signal passes. Above noon the high-pass cutoff increases to cut lows and low-mids.
- PITCH 1 — pitch shift to the LEFT channel. At noon bypassed (unity). Near noon, slight detune up/down. Further out it snaps in semitones, down an octave or up an octave, with additional two-octave settings at the extremes.
- PITCH 2 — same, on the RIGHT channel. Note: in mono mode, if Pitch 2 isn't bypassed (not at noon) it is added in parallel to the left signal's pitch shift, like the original Dark Star's pitch mode.
- CRUSH — distortion in the reverb. At noon clean. Below noon sample rate is turned down for distortion and aliasing (like Crush mode in the original). Above noon the reverb passes through a soft-clipping overdrive with increased gain as it's turned up.
- LAG — sets the pre-delay time, letting the trail start well after the note is played.
- DECAY — from very short to near-infinite.
- MULTIPLY — adds feedback to the whole system, increasing the intensity of every effect and allowing self-oscillation in some settings.
- SPREAD — stereo L/R blend. Up = left reverb on left, right on right. Center = both signals to both outputs equally. Down = fully reversed (left reverb on right out, vice versa). (He sets it most of the way up for the stereo demo.)
- VOLUME — overall volume. MIX — wet/dry, 100% dry to 100% wet, roughly unity in center. Analog dry-through, dry never digitized, zero latency.

FOOTSWITCHES / AUX. The Aux footswitch has three primary functions; tap to latch or hold for momentary. HOLD creates nearly infinite sustain of the reverb trail — maxes Decay beyond the knob setting and stops any new input from getting in; when disengaged Decay returns to the previous setting. FILTER closes the low-pass when engaged. PITCH sets both pitch shifts to +1 octave when engaged. To set the Aux function, hold the Aux switch and move the related knob (Decay, Filter, or Pitch 1); if a knob is moved when you let go, the setting is saved — per preset. Aux can also cycle presets (tap preset while holding Aux). Bypass footswitch: tap to latch, hold for momentary.

EXPRESSION. Externally control any combination of knobs via a TRS expression device. Assign by turning a knob (or knobs) while holding the bypass footswitch — knob position when you press = heel, position when you release = toe. Unmoved knobs are unaffected. Saved per preset.

PRESETS / ROUTING / POWER. Three onboard presets via the preset switch (LEDs show preset 1/2/3 or live mode with no LED). Save: hold preset 5 seconds until the LED blinks, cycle to the slot, hold 5 seconds again to lock. Routing: mono, stereo, or mono-in-stereo-out. True bypass or tails, and a factory reset are available. Requires ~350 mA, center-negative 9V DC (he says "358 milliamp"). MIDI functions exist but he doesn't use them.

HANDS-ON NOTES (with an ambient-player guest). Walks the presets (preset 1, 2 "not as dark," 3, then live mode). On Filter: putting it up makes it "only highs." On Pitch: all the way down is two octaves down, then an octave, then semitones; going the other way is up — "you can pick what pitch is going to poke out, you can get really weird chords." Crush below noon = "distortion and alias... creepy, creepy sci-fi"; above = overdrive. Confirms Pitch 1/2 are left and right channels ("we're only here in one of the channels"). General verdict: it gives Afterneath-like vibes, wraps up what the original Dark Star and V2 did into a bigger, better package; "take one note and stretch it into an entire soundscape." Ten knobs, presets, Aux, expression, MIDI.
