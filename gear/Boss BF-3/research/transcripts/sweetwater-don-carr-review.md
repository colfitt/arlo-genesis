Sweetwater · BOSS BF-3 Flanger Pedal Review by Sweetwater · 2015-01-05

[Intro demo — standard flange.]

Hi, I'm Don Carr, and this is the Boss BF-3 flanger. The BF-3 is a really versatile flanger and it's in Boss's tried-and-true format again. To me it's just so amazing how they managed to cram so much stuff in this one box.

You can see here it's actually got four modes: you've got Ultra mode, Standard mode, a Gate/Pan mode, and Momentary mode. To explain all those really quickly: Ultra mode is a really super-deep-sounding flange, and Standard mode — which is what we heard in the intro — is a standard-sounding flange. The Gate/Pan mode, it depends on how you're running it: if you are running a stereo output it's a pan mode, and it actually flanges back and forth from side to side. And if you're in mono mode like I am here, then it actually has a gated effect along with it, which is really interesting sounding. And Momentary mode — when you're in that, the effect is off until you depress the pedal, and then the effect is on when you depress the pedal, so you can just pop it in for a phrase or a note or whatever.

So I've got the BF-3 bypassed now so we can hear the straight signal, playing my Fender American Standard Stratocaster and Fender Princeton Reverb amp with a little bit of reverb on it. And here it is with the BF-3 bypassed, and here it is engaged with the current settings.

[Demo — bypass vs engaged.]

Okay, so let's listen to it in Standard flange mode and I'm going to twist a few knobs here. This is the RESonance control, and here is the MANUAL underneath it. All right, here's the DEPTH, and here is the RATE.

[Demo — sweeping each knob.]

So a lot of flexibility in the controls, very usable. Let's listen to it in Ultra mode. So it's just a really, really deep flange — it's the same sort of tone, just a deeper flange.

Okay, so let's give a listen to Gate mode.

[Demo — gate/slicer.]

Right, so it adds a slicer effect along with the flange, and the RATE actually controls the rate of the slicer and the rate of the flange. So switching down here to Momentary mode: what happens is the effect is off until you depress the pedal, so check this out. Right — if you wanted to kick it in to highlight a certain phrase or a certain note, or right on the last note of the song or something, pretty cool.

So if you depress and hold the footswitch for two seconds, you can see you enter Set-Tempo mode, which basically just becomes tap tempo, so you can tap in the rate of the flange at that point.

[Demo — tap tempo, slow then fast — and then just press and hold to get out.]

One last feature that I did want to mention is the fact that there's a Bass In and Guitar In which are both optimized for bass-guitar signal and guitar signal. I'm obviously using Guitar In, and it cancels out the other input whichever one you're using. So it's mono in and stereo out.
