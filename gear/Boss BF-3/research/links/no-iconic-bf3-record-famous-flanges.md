The honesty anchor: a 10-track famous-flanger canon with per-track pedal attributions. Confirms the BF-3 has NO iconic record — and that the ONE Boss flanger with a famous track is the analog BF-2, not the BF-3.

## Famous flanges and their pedals (per this list)
- **Van Halen — "Unchained"** → **MXR M117** ("The King of the MXR 117 Flanger, Eddie Van Halen"). NOT a BF-3.
- **Heart — "Barracuda"** → flanger Roger Fisher **built from a Phoenix Systems kit**. NOT a Boss.
- **Police — "Walking on the Moon"** (Andy Summers) → **EHX Electric Mistress**, set subtle/chorus-like.
- **Rush — "The Spirit of Radio"** (Alex Lifeson) → Electric Mistress.
- **David Gilmour** → Electric Mistress.
- **Magazine — "Permafrost"** (John McGeoch) → **MXR M117R**.
- **Black Sabbath — "E5150"** (Geezer Butler) → **Tycobrahe Pedalflanger**.
- **The Cure — "A Forest"** (Robert Smith) → **Boss BF-2** ("Smith preferred to use a Boss BF-2"). ← the ONE Boss-flanger record here, and it's the **analog BF-2**, not the BF-3.

## Takeaway for the guide
Do NOT invent a BF-3 signature artist. The flanger canon belongs to MXR, Electric Mistress, Phoenix and Tycobrahe; the only Boss entry is the BF-2 (Robert Smith / The Cure). The BF-3 is a best-selling workhorse with no defining record — sell it on mode flexibility + front-of-chain placement, not someone else's tone. (Robert Smith's BF-2 use further corroborated by pedalplayers.com/robert-smith-pedalboard and guitartoneoverload.com.)
