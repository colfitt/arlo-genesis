Best accessible power-user settings cookbook + RES technique notes. Numbers are clock-position-ish on a 1–10 scale (their convention).

## Concrete recipes (mode / Manual / RES / Rate / Depth)
- **"Freak on a Leash" gate-pan modulation** — Gate/Pan · M10 · RES10 · Rate5 · Depth5. "Cranking the RES and MANUAL control is what did the trick, giving the modulation a distinct 'other-worldly' quality." (Maxed RES + maxed MANUAL = the extreme voice.)
- **"Black Hole Sun" verse** — **Ultra** · M6 · RES5 · Rate9 · Depth6. KEY tip: "Depth around 60–70% is ideal; too low loses shimmer, too high becomes chaotic." Good guardrail for the doom wall.
- **Classic thin flange** — Standard · M5 · RES5 · Rate4 · Depth4.5. Subtle "swooshing"; they suggest a noise gate to kill ambient hiss.
- **Vocal/wah-like flange** — Standard · M7 · RES4.5 · Rate7 · Depth4. "RES control drives the vocal character; adding distortion or fuzz enhances this sound." (Direct corroboration that flanging *into/with dirt* = more vocal/talking movement.)
- **"Almost nothing" chorus-ish** — Standard · M3 · RES5 · Rate3 · Depth5. At low settings it "sounds more like a thin chorus effect than a full flange."
- **Wes-Borland laser swell** — Standard · M7 · **RES10** · Rate3 · Depth5. Maxed RES + slow rate = the screaming resonant comb; pairs with tape delay.

## RES is the whole pedal
"The RES control... just does so much in the way of expanding the BF-3's range." Pattern across recipes: RES low = chorus-ish/subtle; RES high (8–10) = metallic, vocal, near-self-oscillation. Slow RATE + high RES = sustained screaming peak (the jet-swell); fast RATE + high RES = chaos.

## Bass note
For bass: stay in **Standard**, Rate + Depth ~12 o'clock, use **RES** to set intensity, and place at END of chain (stereo outs).
