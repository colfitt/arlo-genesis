## Lineage (as stated here)
- **BF-1 (page says "1976")** — Boss's answer to the EHX Electric Mistress; four controls Manual/Depth/Rate/Resonance; "lush, rich flanging tones... swirling jet-like sweeps to subtle warbles." Names Andy Summers, David Gilmour, Eddie Van Halen as users of the *line* (NOT specifically the BF-3 — see famous-flanger file).
  - FLAG: page dates BF-1 to 1976; the dossier and most sources say **1977**. Minor discrepancy.
- **BF-2 (June 1980)** — compact redesign, SAME four controls, "no functional changes" from BF-1. Black knobs early, white-top knobs ~1990 when production moved Japan → Taiwan. **BF-2B bass variant (1987)** had the "circuit modified to respond optimally for the lower frequencies," keeping bass clear while flanging the harmonics — this is the conceptual ancestor of the BF-3's BASS IN jack.
- **BF-3 (February 2002–present)** — the break: "from BBD analogue circuits" to "digital electronics." Gains listed: **Ultra** + **Gate/Pan** modes, **tap tempo**, **dual guitar/bass inputs**, **stereo outputs**, **Momentary** mode.
  - FLAG: page says tap tempo with "external footswitch control." The BF-3 has **NO external footswitch jack** — tap is done on the unit's own footswitch. Treat that phrase as an error.

## Usable takeaway
The article is neutral on which sounds better (no sonic A/B). For the analog-vs-digital *opinion* war, see the digital-sheen file — that's where the "cold/clear/metallic vs warm/organic" loyalist split actually lives.
