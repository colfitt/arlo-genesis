Corroborates the dossier's §5 placement call (flanger-after-dirt = conventional; before-dirt = the "broken" experiment). Concrete, quotable both-sides breakdown.

## Flanger AFTER distortion (the BF-3's current slot: …Oxford → BF-3 → Somersault)
- "Stronger, more metallic swooshing effect"; tones are "pronounced and metallic," cleaner stage presentation at lower mix.
- DRAWBACK quoted: with heavy distortion it "can overwhelm the tone," becoming a "swooshing mess." → matches the dossier's warning that flanging a fuzz wall at high RES is violent/additive; keep RES + DEPTH modest on a sustained wall.

## Flanger BEFORE distortion (the deliberate "broken" patch, pre-fuzz)
- "Wobblier, organic tone," "thicker," "chewier," more integrated/natural.
- DRAWBACK: "clarity can suffer with high-gain pedals, potentially resulting in muddy tones." → exactly the gated-splatter / thinning you'd expect putting a buffered BF-3 in front of a Fuzz-Face-style input.

## Verdict
"There's no single right answer... success depends on your specific gear setup." For predictable jet-on-the-wall, keep BF-3 after the dirt. Move it pre-fuzz only as an intentional lo-fi/gated texture, not as a default.
