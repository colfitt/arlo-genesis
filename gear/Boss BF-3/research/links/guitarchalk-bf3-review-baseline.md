Guitar Chalk's firsthand BF-3 review (companion to the settings cookbook). Useful for the baseline starting point and control behavior, less for specific recipes.

QC FLAG: same author/site as the cookbook — treat as a reasonable but not authoritative source.

CONTROLS (author's descriptions):
- MANUAL — adjusts the central frequency of the effect (where the sweep is centered).
- RES (Resonance) — "adjusts the resonance of the sweeping comb filter (sharper or softer)." This is the subtle-vs-intense / soft-vs-jet control.
- DEPTH — controls the intensity/difference between the dry and modulated signal.
- RATE — speed of the modulation.

BASELINE STARTING POINT (quoted):
- "The Boss BF-3 sounds fantastic out of the box, with all settings at 12 o'clock." → Everything (MANUAL, RES, DEPTH, RATE) at noon, Standard mode, is the recommended neutral starting patch. (Matches Alex Messano's "all knobs at noon, RES cranked to 3–4 o'clock" for the classic jet.)

MODES (author's summary):
- NORMAL/Standard — standard flange.
- ULTRA — wider, more intense modulation; "a deep, ethereal warbling sound" with "an almost vocal, wah-like quality."
- GATE/PAN — tremolo-like panning effect.
- MOMENTARY — engages only while the pedal is pressed.

USAGE NOTES:
- Excels with subtle settings over clean signals and open chords (author's stated preference), but higher-intensity modes have legitimate uses.
- "Ultra mode... great paired with distortion when soloing." (relevant to flanging the dirt wall.)
- Limitation: no dedicated wet/dry mix knob.
