Official BOSS "five creative ways to use a flanger" article. Generic flanger technique, but TWO recipes explicitly name the BF-3 (Jet Engine + Slicer/Gate-Pan), so these are factory-voiced starting points. Settings are described, not all numeric — quoted verbatim below. Boss uses "Regeneration" as a synonym for RES.

## 1. Tape Warble (subtle vintage modulation — picked parts, gentle chords, basslines)
- Resonance (RES): "relatively low"
- Rate: "as low as possible while still being able to hear the effect's movement"
- Depth: "between 10 and 12 o'clock"
- No model named (generic), but maps to STANDARD mode subtle drift.

## 2. Jet Engine (the airplane-takeoff special effect) — references BF-3
- Regeneration (RES): "relatively high"
- Depth: "relatively high"
- Rate: "around 10-12 o'clock"
- BF-3 SPECIFIC quote: "The BOSS BF-3 Ultra setting increases the depth and ferocity of the flanging effect even more." → official endorsement that ULTRA mode = the more violent jet (direct support for the rig's Ultra-on-the-wall use).

## 3. Van Halen Sound (movement on riffs/solos without overpowering)
- Rate: "around 2 o'clock"
- Depth: "slightly higher"
- Regeneration (RES): "dialing in only the necessary amount" (i.e., modest)
- Generic; STANDARD mode.

## 4. Slicer Style (robotic ring-mod-ish via gate/pan) — references BF-3
- BF-3 SPECIFIC quote: "The BOSS BF-3 comes equipped with a gate/pan mode" for this; "in mono operation acts as tremolo." → official confirmation of the mono-slicer-vs-stereo-pan behavior the dossier already notes.
- Settings: Rate "set to fast speed" with "naturally high depth."

## 5. Auto Wah (wah-like character from the flanger)
- Res / Manual: "relatively low"
- Depth: "a little over 12 o'clock"
- Rate: "fast speed, rhythmically matched to the guitar part"
- Generic; STANDARD mode.

## Usable takeaways
- Boss itself frames ULTRA as the deeper/more-ferocious jet — corroborates Guitar Chalk + Audiofanzine and the rig's Ultra-chaos target.
- "Tape Warble" (low RES, slowest audible RATE, DEPTH 10-12 o'clock) is the official subtle-drift recipe — the closest factory voice to the rig's "subtle drift on the wall."
