User-review consensus on the four modes, the bass input, and the digital-sheen complaint. The forums where this debate lives natively (TalkBass, My Les Paul) are 403/paywall-blocked to fetchers — these aggregated user reviews are the accessible stand-in.

## Modes (user descriptions)
- **Standard** — "a normal flanger, very pretty."
- **Ultra** — "a longer, more present flanger... a bit intrusive but totally usable." (Confirms Ultra = the bigger/scooped/aggressive voice.)
- **Gate/Pan** — behaves like tremolo/panning; in mono gives a "rotation around the amps" feel; one reviewer flagged the "pedal is saturated" (= level/output jump) when combined with tap tempo → corroborates the additive-gain / output-spike watch.
- **Momentary** — effect on "only when the pedal pressure" is held.

## Controls / gotchas
- **RES** = "overall level of the flanging effect"; the small outer knob "takes practice" to set precisely.
- **Tap tempo** = hold footswitch ~2 sec, then tap; "may cause saturation in certain configurations" (again, watch output).
- **Battery** = "ULTRA greedy" — run it off an isolated supply, not a battery (matches the 60 mA digital draw).

## BF-2 vs BF-3 (the loyalist complaint, in their words)
- BF-3 is "more metallic sounding than the BF-2" and "sounds cold and clear digital"; multiple reviewers prefer the BF-2's "warmer, organic character."
- Common knocks: "digital sheen lacks warmth," "limited personality relative to vintage flangers."
- NOTE: a reviewer cites the BF-3 at "40mA" — this is the WRONG figure that circulates online; Boss's official spec is **60 mA**. Flagging so it doesn't get repeated.

## Bass input
Dual inputs let you tailor tone; **BASS IN** "delivers nice results for slap" specifically. (For the deeper low-end-retention discussion the authoritative bass voices are on TalkBass/Rex-and-the-Bass — both currently un-fetchable here, see flags.)
