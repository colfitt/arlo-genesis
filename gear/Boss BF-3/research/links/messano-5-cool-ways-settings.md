Settings extracted from the "5 Cool Ways to Use The Boss BF-3 Flanger" demo. These are the most directly copyable, spoken-aloud knob positions found in any BF-3 demo — quoted verbatim from narration. Clock positions (6 o'clock = full CCW, 12 = noon, 3–4 = toward CW).

1. CLASSIC JET-PLANE FLANGE (the whoosh everyone wants first)
   - MODE: Standard (noon) · MANUAL: noon · DEPTH: noon · RATE: noon
   - RES ("resolution"): cranked to about 3 or 4 o'clock
   - "All the knobs basically at noon, RES cranked to 3–4 o'clock." Best general-purpose flange and the closest match to the rig's "jet swell on the wall" target.

2. EVH-STYLE MOMENTARY BURSTS
   - MODE: Momentary · (keeps the classic-jet knob settings)
   - Hold the footswitch down for the phrase, release to drop the effect — no double-tap on/off. Cited "Unchained" / "Talking About Love" usage.

3. GATE / SLICER (mono) — extreme tremolo with a hard cutoff
   - MODE: Gate/Pan (knob ~9 o'clock) · RATE: ~9 · DEPTH: ~9
   - MANUAL: way down ~6–7 o'clock · RES: way down ~6–7 o'clock
   - In mono this is a volume-slicer/chopper. In stereo the SAME mode pans L/R (lush swirl) — he demos both. Directly relevant to the dossier's "Gate/Pan = slicer in mono, rotary in stereo" point.

4. CHORUS-LIKE
   - MODE: Standard · DEPTH: 10 o'clock · RATE: about 1 o'clock
   - RES + MANUAL: down low, about 7 o'clock
   - Low RES + faster RATE + shallow DEPTH = chorus shimmer rather than jet. Demoed mono then stereo (Gate/Pan adds panning).

5. ROTARY / LESLIE SIMULATOR
   - Uses Gate/Pan in stereo for the rotor spin (mono first, then stereo). No separate knob numbers given beyond the Gate/Pan setup.

WORKFLOW TAKEAWAYS:
- RES is the subtle-to-jet knob: low RES (~7 o'clock) = chorus/subtle; cranked (3–4 o'clock) = screaming jet.
- The same Gate/Pan mode is a mono slicer OR a stereo pan/rotary depending only on whether OUTPUT B is connected — confirms the rig note that Gate/Pan's rotary is wasted in a mono slot.
