Andy Bassist · EarthQuaker Devices | Hizumitas Fuzz | Bass Demo · 2023-03-03

Andy Bassist coming at you once again, and this week it's not the newest pedal — it's been out for a while — it's the Hizumitas from EarthQuaker Devices. Love EarthQuaker, have many of their pedals over the years; they're always really solid, never had any issues.

It was basically made for guitarist Wata from Boris, who are an epic band — saw them at ArcTanGent many years ago, fantastic. You either love them or hate them. It's based on some form of Big Muff — I think it's like an Elk — with maybe some EarthQuaker tweaks here and there to make it more stable and fit in their box so it'll work week in, week out.

Signal chain is simple: bass into the Hizumitas, then into my Cali76 compressor, then into my bass rig — Origin Super Vintage — then straight into Ableton with nothing going on, no EQs, so you hear it raw. I'll show you the clean sound first.

[Clean bass.]

And this is the Hizumitas in the setting that I like: Volume straight up (noon), fuzz/sustain up at about 3/4, and the Tone — it's like a reverse tone — it's at like 10 o'clock, but that's more the treble side. It's reversed: the more clockwise you go, the more bass.

[Demo of fuzz.]

Now just going to roll that tone back. [Tone swept toward bass side.]

[Demo — sustained low fuzz.] And it's not massively feeding back for the amount of gain that's going on there either. There's a little bit of hiss, but it sounds great — proper Cliff Burton vibes.

[Demo, lower sustain] The fuzz just straight up, it's actually a genuinely really nice overdrive. It sits in that middle ground — obviously not like a normal overdrive pedal.

[Demo.] Well, that sounds lovely. Three controls — fuzz/saturation, volume and tone. You're not going to get crazy different levels of intricacy, lots of switches and stuff — it is what it is, a three-knob fuzz, and it sounds killer. Not a lot of money, the artwork's great, loads of color variations (I got the standard silver with blue artwork). A no-nonsense fuzz, built to last — it's EarthQuaker. Check it out.

---
KEY SETTINGS / NOTES (BASS):
- Andy's preferred BASS setting: Volume noon (straight up), Sustain ~3/4, Tone ~10 o'clock (treble side).
- Confirms Tone is reversed: CW = more bass.
- Low feedback even at high gain on bass — controllable; only slight hiss.
- Low Sustain = "genuinely really nice overdrive," sits in a middle ground (not a normal OD).
- Pairs into a Cali76 compressor after; "Cliff Burton vibes" for sustained low fuzz bass.
