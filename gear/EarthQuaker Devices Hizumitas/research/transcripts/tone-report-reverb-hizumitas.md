Hey everyone, it's Andy with the Reverb Tone Report, and today we're checking out an epic collaboration between EarthQuaker Devices and the band Boris — it's the Hizumitas fuzz, which I'm pronouncing flawlessly.

The Hizumitas is based on Wata's go-to pedal throughout her career, the Elk BM Sustainer. As big-muff connoisseurs will tell you, not one sounds the same, and this goes for many of the variants that came out in 1970s Japan. Although the Elk Sustainer is based on a Triangle Muff, EarthQuaker knew they had to get their hands on Wata's personal pedal, and the result is an honest sonic recreation.

Starting with an unusual tone circuit: clockwise will roll off treble while increasing mids and bass, while counterclockwise increases the treble while reducing mids and bass. The Sustain and Tone are highly interactive and always seem to let more string definition through than other circuit variations.

Like the sound of Boris, the output is massive — with unity gain only around 10 o'clock, which is perfect for matching with a wide variety of pickups. Boris has timed the release of this pedal with their latest album, where you can hear Wata playing a brand new Hizumitas fuzz. Look for a black limited-edition colorway while supplies last, or go for the standard purple right here on Reverb.

KEY SETTINGS / NOTES:
- Unity gain ~10 o'clock on Volume; output is massive past that.
- Demo settings: humbuckers, Sustain ~75%, Tone swept across full range to show bass-boost (CW) vs treble (CCW).
- Tone CW = roll off treble, boost mids+bass; CCW = boost treble, cut mids+bass.
- Lower Sustain = "creamy"; back off Tone to let treble through, still round low end; raise output to compensate for low-output pickups.
- Cleans up well via guitar volume knob (esp. with treble-bleed-equipped guitar) — unusual for a Muff circuit.
- Demonstrated on baritone in drop G# — tracks tight low end well.
