The Distortion Principle · Most Aggressive Big Muff I've Tried · 2021-11-28

Today we're going to take a look at the Hizumitas by EarthQuaker Devices. If you like guitar players that can barely play but do have kick-ass amps, and I love fuzz pedals — let's get to it.

This pedal has been very well received in the pedal community, especially in the stoner-rock / doom community, because it's a clone of an Elk Sustainar, which was a Japanese knockoff of a Triangle Big Muff. More importantly, it was the pedal used by Wata from Boris. The Wata pedal was sent to Jamie at EarthQuaker Devices, and he modeled this pedal off that exact pedal to get that exact sound. So when you're playing this, you are playing the pedal owned by Wata — it's not a random pedal with tolerance differences, it's the same one she had.

The community was really excited about this pedal. I've used a lot of muffs, and I feel like this thing is a lot different in certain aspects. We're going to run it through the Orange TH30 and the EVH 5150, because the Orange clean channel is more like a Plexi channel — it's not really clean, it starts breaking up really early — while the EVH I can get a lot cleaner. Good to show you two ways: a slightly broken-up amp and a more clean amp.

Controls: we have them set at noon, the Volume is around 9–10 o'clock which is where it's supposed to be. True bypass, three knobs — Tone, Sustain, Volume, that's it. The Tone knob works almost like a RAT knob — it's in reverse. When you go clockwise you start bringing in more of the low end (and some low mids too); when you go counterclockwise you start bringing in the treble.

[Orange amp alone — already broken up, not even noon on the channel volume.]

[Pedal on] And it kind of rolls back to almost a crunchy, slightly hairy, slightly fuzzy overdrive — that's pretty cool.

[Tone rolled clockwise ~3/4 up = big low end.]

[Tone all the way counterclockwise = all the way into the rolled-in high setting.]

[Sustain dialed back, then all the way down] With Sustain all the way down you basically get an overdrive — a fuzzy overdrive.

I want to kick the Plumes on before it and see what we get that way. [Plumes + Hizumitas stacked.]

Side note: when dialing in your pedals, do it where you can't really see the knobs, so you focus on your ears, not on where you think the settings should be. I'm just going to dial in the Tone and Sustain to where I like it. When you dial the Sustain all in, it gets a lot more low end; dialing it back brings a little more of the highs and mids back in. So that's where I'd set this pedal. It's really not too far from where I saw Wata had hers set in a video — she had hers at noon but mine's at one o'clock, so it's basically her setting.

Now we switch to the EVH on the clean channel — exact same settings we had at the end with the Orange. [Demo.] I think I might actually prefer it on the EVH (more power, 50W vs 30W). Let's dial the Sustain back.

[Plumes on top of that] You could use this as your go-to metal setting — Plumes and the Hizumitas going. [Plumes off, roll back the tone knob to bring in some highs] starting to sound a lot more like a Big Muff there — that really scooped, lots-of-high-end tone. [Take the sustain all the way out] need a little bit of it with this channel because it's too clean.

Last thing: dial it in to where I like it on this amp to see if I end up with the same settings as the Orange. Pretty close — almost identical. So if you get one: dial it to nine/ten o'clock on the Volume, put everything else at one o'clock, and this is the tone you'll get.

Do I think the pedal's any good? Yeah, it's great — this has been my go-to fuzz, knocked my Russian Pickle off the board. I've tried a lot of muffs — Deluxe (Tech?), Fuzz... none could knock it off. It sounds different than a muff: more open, more clarity. If you have a muff you may ask if you need this — you probably do, because it's more open. They're cheap, ~$149. See you next time.

KEY SETTINGS / NOTES:
- RECOMMENDED SETTING (his "best"/Wata-adjacent): Volume 9–10 o'clock, Sustain 1 o'clock, Tone 1 o'clock. "Basically her [Wata's] setting" (Wata's was noon, his at 1).
- Tone knob = "like a RAT knob, in reverse": CW = low end + low mids, CCW = treble. CCW extreme = "scooped, lots-of-high-end, more like a Big Muff."
- Sustain min = fuzzy overdrive / crunch (no fully clean floor, but usable lightly-distorted). Sustain max = much more low end.
- Stacking: Beetronics/EQD Plumes IN FRONT of Hizumitas = "go-to metal setting." Demoed on Orange TH30 (broken-up) and EVH 5150 (clean ch.).
- Settings translate across amps (near-identical sweet spot on both amps).
