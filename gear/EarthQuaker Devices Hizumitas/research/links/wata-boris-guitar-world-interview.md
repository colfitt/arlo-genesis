Guitar World · Jonathan Horsley · 2021-11-19

Wata of Boris interview tied to the Hizumitas launch. The signature-artist primary source.

A/B VERDICT vs the original Elk BM Sustainar:
- "The Hizumitas is not inferior to my ELK when I A/B them." She had already gigged it and intended to tour with it instead of the vintage Elk, which "has been broken many times and it's getting beat up."

STACKING PHILOSOPHY (key quote for muff-stacking facet):
- "I often use the Hizumitas with a distortion to create more chaotic feel." She also layers it with "a wah, reverb and echo."

ELK / "Feedbacker" CONTEXT:
- She found her Elk fuzz via internet auction while exploring fuzz pedals. She and bandmate Takeshi both owned similar Elk units and used them on the song "Feedbacker" (from the album *Feedbacker* / *Boris at Last - Feedbacker*). So pre-2022 fuzz-wall recordings = original Elk; 2022-onward main fuzz = likely Hizumitas.

CORE RIG:
- Her unchanging foundation: '86 Gibson Les Paul Custom + the fuzz (now Hizumitas) + Roland tape echo. She calls these "the core of my expression."

TAKEAWAY: The only signature claim on this pedal is Wata's, and she explicitly endorses stacking it with a distortion (she uses a Boss DS-1 per other sources) for chaos — that is the canonical Hizumitas muff-stacking move.
