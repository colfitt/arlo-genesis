Jamie Stillman (EQD) + Wata on building the Hizumitas. Dev-process and usage source.

DEVELOPMENT:
- Stillman: "Eventually I got up to seven revisions, and it took over the course of a year, sending it back and forth" before getting Wata's actual unit to A/B against.
- On why it was hard: "I actually took mine apart and measured each piece just to see what the tolerances were, and everything had drifted, pretty wildly." Decades of component drift = the Elk's character is partly aged tolerances, not just the schematic.

TONE KNOB (consistent with EQD/PG): clockwise simultaneously boosts bass and scoops mids; counter-clockwise cuts treble [NOTE: EQD's own noodlers post says CCW *boosts* treble toward RAT — treat CCW = treble-forward, CW = bass-forward/darker as the reliable reading; this article's "cutting treble in the opposite direction" phrasing is ambiguous].

USAGE TIP FROM WATA (concrete, high-value):
- "I want people to try it in an environment where they can set amps very loud." The pedal is voiced for loud-amp interaction — feedback sustain and the fuzz wall only fully bloom at volume. Bedroom-level testing undersells it.

WATAFUZZ CONTEXT: She earlier did the MASF Watafuzz (Foxx Tone Machine-based, 2015). Both live on her board; she picks per song.
