Mixdown Magazine · Peter Hodgson · 2022-10-25

Best source on bass / low-tuned / non-guitar use and the "all on noon" default.

SOURCE-AGNOSTIC ROBUSTNESS (key for the user's banjo/baritone/VG-800 rig):
- "the circuit is robust enough that frankly it doesn't give a crap if you hit it with an active or passive pickup, a six, seven, or eight-string, or a bass." (Corroborates the ~150kΩ input handling buffered/varied sources well.)

BASS USE:
- Tone toward center = "a mid-boosted voicing that works great with bass (and my Bass VI), especially for those who play very bass-led music."
- Doubles as a studio tool: "it's also lots of fun to use as a recording preamp for distorted bass parts."

TONE = THREE ZONES (concrete dial map):
- CCW (treble) = "the whole Iommi treble-booster sound."
- Center = "a mid-boosted voicing" (the cut-through-a-mix sweet spot).
- CW (bass) = "stupidly fat" low end.

DEFAULT THAT WORKS: "the sound really comes alive at that 'all on 12' setting" — Volume/Sustain/Tone all at noon = "aggressive saturation yet smooth sustain" with punchy mids. Matches EQD's own "all controls at noon, go nuts" starting point.
