EQD-partner editorial framing the Hizumitas for the doom/sludge/shoegaze crowd. No NEW concrete clock positions — it corroborates the on-disk Tone geography and the "cut through the sludge" character. Captured for the shoegaze/Jazzmaster framing.

CONTROLS (quoted/paraphrased):
- Three controls "fairly straightforward": V (Volume), S (Sustain), T (Tone).
- TONE GEOGRAPHY (quoted): "the Tone control boosts high frequencies left of 12:00 and boosts lows right of 12:00." (= CCW treble / CW bass, same inverted reading as EQD Noodlers, Premier Guitar, SixStringSensei.)

CHARACTER (quoted):
- Using Wata's own pedal, EQD "found a unique tonal characteristic that offered a heavy bottom end, with clear, gritty saturation — allowing her tone to cut through even the most sludgiest of sounds." (The mid-forward / cut-through-the-wall signature, restated.)

SHOEGAZE/JAZZMASTER ANGLE (from the Perfect Circuit "Spaced Out: The Gear of Shoegaze" companion piece + this article's framing):
- Jazzmaster-style offsets are the canonical shoegaze guitar for "articulate playing and note clarity through heavy fuzz" — pairs with the Hizumitas for "crushing shoegaze walls of sound." (Maps onto the rig's baritone Jazzmaster.)
