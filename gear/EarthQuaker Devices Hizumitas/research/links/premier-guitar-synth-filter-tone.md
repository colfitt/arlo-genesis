Best written source on the "synth-filter" Tone behavior — the headline power-user technique.

THE SYNTH-FILTER TECHNIQUE (verbatim):
- "I could use the tone knob as an almost synth-like filter control on sustained notes, letting embers of drop-D riffs slowly burn and change shape as they decayed."
- Practical: hold a sustained note/chord and SWEEP the Tone knob through the decay — the wall changes voicing as it rings, behaving like sweeping a filter on a synth. This is the move for drone/doom textures (and ideal for the user's sustained-banjo wall).

TONE GEOGRAPHY: CCW = most treble-heavy; CW progressively adds bass. Fully clockwise + high Sustain = "rich, substantial low-end without muddiness."

PAIRING: Tested into a Deluxe Reverb (Gibson SG), and also great minimal — guitar straight to interface with only reverb added. Sustain holds long enough that reverb is all you need for ambient/drone.

GOTCHA: High-treble (CCW) settings "can sound harsh or excessively noisy to some players." Output runs HOT — unity gain ~9 o'clock on Volume (everything past is a boost).

CLEANUP: the Tone knob actively sculpts the DECAY of held notes, so sustained passages "evolve organically rather than fade uniformly."
