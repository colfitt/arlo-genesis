MusicRadar · Michael Astley-Brown · 2017-08-07

Pre-Hizumitas Boris rig tour (2017) — establishes the fuzz-wall architecture the Hizumitas later slots into, and how Wata + Takeshi build the wall together.

WATA (guitar) — fuzz/dist:
- Main fuzz = Elk Big Muff (Japanese Elk Gakki clone). Wata: "It's a Big Muff clone, made by the Japanese company Elk Gakki, but it's thick and heavy, and I really like the way the chunky sound comes through with a bang." (This is the pedal the Hizumitas recreates.)
- MASF Watafuzz (Foxx-derived): "It emits a unique, gravely low-end sound."
- Boss DS-1 distortion (the stacking distortion she still uses).

TAKESHI (bass/guitar) — fuzz/dist:
- Pro Co RAT, modified by Soul Power Instruments — his primary bass dirt. "On the bass, I don't really use many effects besides this distortion pedal."
- DOD Buzz Box + Dwarfcraft SOMMS for noise: "My DOD Buzz Box and Dwarfcraft Devices SOMMS pedals are also crucial to create an atmosphere of noisy outbursts."

SIGNAL ARCHITECTURE:
- Both run a Soul Power Instruments custom loop-switcher (2 in / 4 out / 8 loops) as a central hub: "With one switch I can switch between everything - not only pedals but also inputs and amps." The fuzz wall is built by switching whole pedal/amp combinations, not by always-on stacking.

RELEVANCE: The "wall" is two players' dirt summed (Elk/Hizumitas Muff + RAT bass dirt). The Hizumitas's CCW Tone = "near RAT territory" mirrors Takeshi's RAT — the band's tonal palette is Muff + RAT by design.
