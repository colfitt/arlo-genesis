THE cornerstone settings reference — EQD's own "Hizumitas For Noodlers And Fuzz Freshman" post. Every clip below names actual knob positions (quoted).

CONTROL BEHAVIOR (quoted/paraphrased):
- Volume: unity gain "around 10 o'clock"; controls output of the fuzzed signal.
- Sustain: at zero gives a "usable, lightly distorted sound" (no fully clean floor); cranking "kicks up the distortion, adding a hint of compression" but "retains enough tightness to keep chords articulate."
- Tone: "not just a basic treble roll-off or bass cut." From noon CLOCKWISE → "boosts bass frequency to a hefty bottom end." From noon COUNTER-CLOCKWISE → "boosts the treble into near RAT territory." Interacts heavily with Sustain.

STARTER SETTING (for fuzz newcomers):
- "Put all three controls at noon, pick your favorite power chord or riff, and just go nuts."  → Volume 12, Sustain 12, Tone 12.

NAMED CLIP SETTINGS (quoted positions):
- DOOM / MAXIMUM HEAVY: "Full Volume, full Sustain, and the Tone knob cranked for maximum low-end heaviness." → Volume max, Sustain max, Tone fully CW (bass).
- LOW-GAIN / CRUNCH: "Min Tone. Volume at around noon, Sustain at minimum." → Volume 12, Sustain min, Tone fully CCW (treble) = lightly-distorted, bright/RAT-ish crunch.
- BALANCED / MID-FORWARD: "Sustain at noon, Tone at about 10 [o'clock]." → Volume ~10–12, Sustain 12, Tone ~10 (treble side of noon) = articulate mid-forward lead/rhythm.

NOTES:
- Tone fully CW = the "synth filter" dark/scooped-bass doom voice; fully CCW = bright near-RAT mid-forward voice. Noon ≈ flattest.
