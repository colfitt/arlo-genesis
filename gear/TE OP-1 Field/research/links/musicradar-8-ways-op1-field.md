Field-specific tips, several straight from TE. The Field-only / new-feature ones:

## New-feature highlights
- **Stereo drums (Field-only):** press **Shift + the Ochre key** to access stacked channels A and B. von Hofsten: "We can load our own samples on to these channels, from two different drum machines — for example an 808 and 909 — and then stack these together to create our own stacked drum machine." (For this rig: stack a banjo one-shot on A and a fuzz-wall hit on B for a layered chromatic kit.)
- **Mother reverb (Field-only):** Distance param 0–99 simulates room proximity; **Gate** param simulates sound passing through a door before it reaches you. The lush new stereo reverb the OG lacked.
- **Velocity sensitivity comes via firmware update** — install via TE-Boot (device acts as a hard drive for firmware). Onboard keys still on/off, but the engines respond to velocity from external MIDI (use the Novation 61SL here).

## Workflow tips
- **Tape:** 4 tracks, **6-minute max** per tape; full real-tape workflow incl. audible reel sounds.
- **Tombola:** blue knob = hexagon rotation speed; grey knob = open the hexagon walls so notes escape — evolving generative patterns.
- **Drum kits:** access via **Shift + 1–8**; dual-osc drum synth (DBox) — tweak waveform + decay for custom percussion.
- **Pattern sequencer:** set tempo, set loop length (e.g. 2 bars), program drums in the pattern sequencer, then record onto the beat.
- **External mic:** TE notes people use the OP-Z as a mic, often via the TX-6 mixer.

## Rig takeaway
The two genuinely new things to exploit here are **stereo drums (Shift+Ochre)** for layered banjo/fuzz kits and the **Mother** reverb's Distance/Gate for room/doom space before the signal even hits the boards.
