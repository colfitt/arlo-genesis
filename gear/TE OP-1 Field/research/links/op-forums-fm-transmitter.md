Community findings on the Field's headline new toy, the FM TRANSMITTER (broadcast your own station). Verdict: charming but weak; needs an antenna hack.

## The complaints
- "Almost useless" without near-direct contact with the receiver.
- "Limited frequency bandwidth. Noisy and poor signal volume."
- Device orientation matters — tilting increases noise. FCC power limits are baked in by design.

## The fix: improvise an antenna
- **Plug something into the audio OUT to act as an antenna.** "the M1 [headset] plugged into the out as an antenna. works like a charm!" — "almost any kind of cable will help here."
- Reception ALSO improves with "a pair of Apple headphones plugged into the REC IN adjusted like antennae."
- With an antenna, one user got clean transmission across "a normal sized living room" to an OB-4.

## Use cases reported
- Broadcast to a car stereo, TE OB-4, Teufel 3sixty radio.

## Rig takeaway
Mostly a novelty for this studio. The genuinely useful inverse — sampling FM/any audio INTO the OP-1 — is better served by the receiver (or by broadcasting a phone signal in with a cheap FM transmitter plugged into the OP-1's out; see the Field workflows thread "KenTech Transmitter FM" trick). Keep a short cable handy as a TX/RX antenna if you ever do use the radio.
