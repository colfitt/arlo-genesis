# OP-1 Field — a full album made on it (concrete workflow, very rig-relevant)

Andreas Roman made an entire album on the OP-1 Field and wrote up his actual workflow. This is the most concrete *Field-specific* (not original OP-1) artist-use source found, and the techniques map almost 1:1 onto this studio's drone/texture aesthetic and Chase Bliss pedalboards.

## Sampling technique (the headline move)
- **One-note polyphonic sampling.** He sampled a **single C3 note from a Prophet 12**, then played it polyphonically across the OP-1 Field's **sampler engine**. He called the Field's one-shot-to-polyphonic sampling "the best I've encountered." → Sample one note of any source (here: VG-800 pad, banjo, baritone) and you instantly have a playable polyphonic instrument.

## Tape modes
- Used **all four tape types** ("studio quality to worn-down cassette, each with eight slots").
- Workflow: capture clean **field recordings on Studio tape**, then **re-process onto Vintage/cassette tape** for character — i.e. a deliberate clean-capture-then-degrade pass. (Matches this rig's generation-loss aesthetic.)

## Field recording + tape loops
- Recorded environmental sounds (birds, wind, water) through the **internal mic**, then used the **tape loop function** to find and isolate favorite portions for further processing.

## Engines + sequencers
- Leaned on the **Dimension** engine (the Field's clean subtractive synth) and **FM bells**.
- Favorite generative move: the **Tombola sequencer on FM bells**, described as having "raindrop-like qualities."
- Otherwise mostly **recorded improvised synth jams straight to tape** rather than relying on step sequencers — treated the onboard sequencers as "just enough convenience."

## External FX integration (directly relevant — overlaps this rig's pedals)
- Recorded material **out to and back from Chase Bliss pedals**: **MOOD, Habit, Blooper**, plus a **Chase Bliss/Meris-style CXM 1978 reverb** — printing those effects back into the Field.
- Also ran material through a **Chase Bliss Generation Loss MkII (GenLossMKII)** for VHS-style degradation, and ran outputs through "tape live" effects during mixdown.
- This is exactly the OP-1-Out → pedalboard → resample loop the dossier recommends; here it's documented working with Chase Bliss boxes this studio also owns (Blooper, MOOD MkII, Big Time).

## Rig translation
The whole album method = (1) sample one note/source, (2) play it polyphonically, (3) print to clean tape then re-print to degraded tape, (4) send out to Chase Bliss/Gen Loss and resample, (5) sprinkle Tombola-on-FM-bells for generative shimmer. That is the OP-1 Field's drone/texture playbook, validated on a real release.
