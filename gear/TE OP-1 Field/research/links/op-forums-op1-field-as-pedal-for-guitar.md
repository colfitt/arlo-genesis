The opposite of the "OP-1 out into pedals" thread: guitar IN → OP-1 Field as an FX processor. The community's verdict: it kind of works but it's awkward.

## How monitoring/processing actually routes
- Incoming audio "just goes through the whole chain of Main Output when monitoring, so it is affected by 1) EQ 2) FX 3) Mastering Limiter." So you DO get the OP-1's master FX slot + EQ + limiter on the input signal.

## Limitations
- **One effect at a time** — only the single master FX slot; you can't stack OP-1 effects on the input.
- **Mono on the OG OP-1:** incoming audio "becomes mono though (except the Master FX applied)." (Field has the stereo path, but the input chain is still constrained.)
- **Ergonomically rigid** — small screen, no flexible routing; "not comfortable enough to process the guitar as an FX rig."
- **MIDI control limited** — you can drive transport over MIDI but "i dont think u can toggle record via midi."

## Workaround
- Use the **Vocoder synth set to 100% dry** (no vocoding) as a pass-through, then apply FX + LFO modulation through it for "a somewhat functioning live FX" — still limited/rigid.

## Verdict / rig takeaway
Experienced users: "you're better off processing the guitar with a pedal FX before hitting the op1f." For THIS rig that confirms direction: don't use the OP-1 as a guitar FX box — use the boards for that. The OP-1's value is SAMPLING the wall and being a source INTO the boards, not processing guitar live.
