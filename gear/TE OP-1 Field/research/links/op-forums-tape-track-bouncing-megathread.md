How the community bounces/merges the OP-1's 4 tape tracks (you only get 4, so merging is constant). Methods, combos, and the gotchas.

## The methods (4 tracks → fewer)
1. **Bounce 1+2+3 → empty track 4 "by ear"** — the classic. Pan/level the three, then record them onto track 4 monitoring through the speaker/out.
2. **Bounce to Album** — render to the album for a final-mixdown commit.
3. **Bounce to Synth Sampler** — Lift, Drop into the synth sampler.
4. **Bounce to Drum Sampler** — Shift+Lift ALL tracks at once, Drop into the drum sampler: "merges tracks perfectly with mixer volumes." Cleanest internal merge.
5. **Out-to-in / record-everything-you-hear** — toggle the **out-to-in function under the Mic/Input key** so the OP-1 records its own full output (all tracks + master FX/EQ/limiter) to a chosen track. Doubles as a bounce.
6. **Mute + overdub** — record onto a muted track that already holds audio; "seems to avoid the volume drop you get when you do it that way."

## Gotchas (quoted/paraphrased from the thread)
- **Sampler-method length limit:** "12 seconds is limiting when using the sampler method for merging" (OG sampler buffer; Field's is longer, ~20s, but still finite — a 6-minute tape won't fit the sampler).
- **Volume drop on naive overdub-bounce:** plain overdub-to-occupied-track drops level; the mute method or careful gain-staging avoids it. Getting the two-track mix "just right is a bit tricky."
- **Double compression:** if you use gain/release compression at an intermediate stage AND again in the final mix, you compress twice — watch the master Drive/limiter.
- **Always Lift first:** before any destructive bounce, Lift the source so you can re-Drop if the bounce is wrong (no undo on tape).

## Rig note
For this studio's generation-loss aesthetic, repeatedly Drop-to-sampler → re-record-to-tape (esp. in Porta/Disc tape style) is the intended degradation loop — each merge pass adds loss in the right direction.
