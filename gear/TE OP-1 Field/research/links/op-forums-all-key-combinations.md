The community's full OP-1 key-combo reference. Carries to the Field (same key paradigm). Hidden/undocumented ones flagged.

## Hidden / easy-to-miss combos
- **Shift + Help = set date / time** (system clock; affects tape/recording metadata).
- **Shift + Tempo = detune notes in cents** (global fine-tune; "fake analog drift" trick — also see metronome detune for doubling).
- **Shift + Tape = erase tape** (DESTRUCTIVE — wipes the current tape).
- **Shift + Synth / Shift + Drum = undo edits, revert to the loaded preset** (the closest thing to a per-patch undo).

## Tape transport / playback
- Shift + Play = **reverse playback**
- Shift + `< >` = jump bar to bar
- Stop + `<` = jump to the very beginning
- Play + `< >` = fast rewind / forward
- Blue (while recording) = change pitch on the fly

## Recording
- Rec (hold) = stand ready for recording (arm, reels still)
- Shift + Rec = stand ready for tape roll (armed/paused record)

## Editing
- Split = cut a take into pieces
- Shift + Split = join takes (glue)
- **Shift + Lift = advanced lift — lift ALL tracks into memory** (the basis of the merge-to-sampler bounce)
- Shift + Green = slide loop in
- Shift + Blue = slide a take

## Synth / Drum
- Shift + 1–8 = change the sound on that instant-access key
- Shift + T1–T4 = change the modules of the sound without changing the sound
- `< >` = octave shift

## Diagnostic / boot (hold SHIFT + COM while powering on → TE-Boot)
- Shift-2 (under display) in bootloader = motherboard test
- Shift-3 = old tests: colour bars, loopback, etc.

NOTE: button labels differ cosmetically on the Field (e.g. "Mic" vs "Input", color names) but the combos are equivalent. Treat the destructive ones (Shift+Tape erase) with care.
