The definitive in-depth review (~43 min). Less a how-to than a "what it is, what it can't do, and where it fits" — directly relevant to the dossier's "front of the funnel" framing. Disclosure: loopop received the units from TE at below retail.

## vs a laptop/iPad
A laptop with interface + mic + a knobby MIDI controller does far more for less money (and supports velocity). loopop bought his OG OP-1 not because a computer can't do more, but because of portability, a focused menu-free interface, fast sampling (mic/USB/radio), interesting sequencers, and the tape workflow. If you're comfortable making music on a laptop/phone/iPad, you may not need an OP-1 at all.

## vs the original OP-1 — the two most material differences
Core workflow is IDENTICAL between the two; try the (cheaper, used) original first to see if the tape workflow appeals.

1. STORAGE.
   - OG: ONE 6-minute tape, 4 MONO tracks (plus two stereo "album" mixdowns as a one-time bounce).
   - Field: up to EIGHT tapes in memory, switched near-instantly (can't load a tape while playing). Each is a 6-min, 4-track STEREO tape. Tapes save tempo + audio but NOT full machine state (no per-tape snapshots, mix levels, or global effects — loopop's main wishlist).
   - User presets/snapshots increased to ~500. Synth sampler still 6 s; sample-based DRUM KITS up to 20 s each (was 12 s) — eight kits = up to ~160 s of immediately-accessible samples. ~2.7 GB free for user samples over USB.

2. STEREO almost everywhere.
   - Samples record/play in stereo; tape records in stereo; nearly all synths upgraded to stereo (subtle widening on Cluster, FM topology spreads in stereo, String, etc.).
   - On the OG, stereo effects (Spring, CWO) were stereo only as MASTER effects; on the Field, stereo effects (Spring, Grid, CWO) are stereo at the per-INSTRUMENT/track level too. (Delay is oddly still mono.)
   - Drum instruments can be PANNED per sample; STACKING mode treats L/R as morphable layers (assignable as a Value-LFO destination).
   - Melodic samples got crossfaded loop points (much smoother sustains) and you can ZOOM into any point of a melodic sample / drum kit by pressing the encoder and moving it.

## Other Field improvements
- New DIMENSION synth (waveform morph, stereo width, filter with resonance) and the MOTHER reverb (spacious, with color, frequency, dry/wet mix, and a gate).
- Higher-res, brighter LCD flush with the surface; shows MORE info (9 params vs 6 in lists), writes parameter names on screen, shows the LFO DESTINATION NAME (a big OG pain point — it used to only show the knob color). NOTE: the Field REMOVED the Bend and Crank LFOs that existed on the OG.
- Encoders now PUSH (e.g., quick-mute tracks, hold sequencers, A/B EQ settings); spectrum analyzer behind the EQ.
- Four TAPE STYLES: Studio (cleanest), Vintage (in between), Porta (lowest-fi, most degraded), MiniDisc (closest to digital, with minidisc-style artifacts on fast-forward/stop). KEY QUIRK: a project commits to ONE tape type — you can't swap mid-project without deleting it. The degradation is applied only WHEN YOU RECORD (preview is clean); each overdub pass STACKS more noise. To preview the noise/distortion without recording, hold [Shift]+[Record]+[Stop]. Tape with nothing recorded makes no noise. You CAN copy/paste audio between projects (so a clean take dropped into a Porta project stays clean — it's the record processing that's lo-fi, not the tape playback). loopop's wish: allow changing tape style mid-project to apply different character per track.
- HOLD "sequencer" (really a performance tool): holds a chord below a split point so you can solo on top without interrupting; can transpose the held chord (strum-like).
- Connectivity: USB-C can act as HOST (power and connect an external MIDI controller directly — tested with Launchpad Pro and KeyStep). FM RX + TX (broadcasts its own station name). BLE MIDI (no audio over Bluetooth in this firmware). Headphone port accepts headset mics. Much better mic and speaker (side + bottom grille adds real bass; speaker is mono).
- Velocity: keys aren't velocity-sensitive, but external velocity DOES map — e.g., route the VELOCITY LFO to filter cutoff so harder hits open the filter.
- Battery ~24 h as claimed.
- Computer connection: MTP (faster, more content) or disk mode ([Shift]+4 to mount; slower).

## The big-picture "what's different about ANY OP-1" (vs MPC/Maschine/Deluge/MC707)
1. TAPE-BASED workflow: beyond a single preset/kit, you build songs by recording the AUDIO of live performances to tape. The onboard sequencers are short (Pattern 16, Finger up to 32, Endless 128), you use only ONE at a time and store only one (except Finger stores 14 patterns, 2-note, combine two). To make an actual track you record them as audio — so editing a single note/automation means re-recording that portion (unlike MIDI-track groove boxes). Sequencers store NOTES only, not automation (workaround: bake levels into kit samples, e.g. two kicks at different volumes). Only FOUR tracks; once used, you can overdub but there's no undo on the OG (workarounds: lift→drop buffer; lift all four and drop back to revert).
2. NOT multi-timbral (except drum kits): one timbre at a time; loading a new sound cuts the previous. It only becomes multi-timbral once committed to tape (and then it's baked in).
3. FEWER parameters by design: ~4 params per synth, a simple amp envelope, ONE instrument effect (no per-instrument effect chaining) plus one master effect, and an LFO (6 LFO "types," but only 3 are真 LFOs — others are MIDI mod, velocity, and Element which routes envelope/audio-follower/accelerometer to a destination).

## Pros / verdict
Ultra-portable, focused menu-free workflow, fun sequencers, fast at sampling radio/external audio. loopop's first choice for SIMPLE ideas while traveling, but he would NOT do a complex / >4-track idea on it. "It's about what inspires you to create." Wishlist: undo, lift more than ~22 s across multiple tracks (single-track lift caps at 90 s, not the full 6 min), more than 8 projects with variable length, per-tape machine-state recall, a toggle to see all four track waveforms instead of the tape animation, a granular synth, and no track-swap hiccup (it interrupts using the OP-1 as a live looper).
