A "loose sampling / make mismatched loops groove together" technique. Filmed on the OG OP-1 but explicitly applicable to the Field (which adds MPC-style micro-zoom chopping). Contains sponsor segments (DistroKid) which are omitted below.

## Philosophy
Don't overthink it; be LOOSE with the samples you combine. They don't need to be the same tempo or key. Quantizing/gluing everything makes it rigid — the magic is in samples that "bump off each other" rhythmically. He prefers slightly-different-tempo samples for the artifacts. The OP-1's lo-fi "je ne sais quoi" is why he keeps sampling through it.

## Building a groove from mismatched loop samples
Pre-loaded into drum slots: a chords loop, a bass loop, a short shaker — all roughly ~120 BPM but not exact.

1. FIND THE TEMPO of your material first; he lands on ~118 BPM and sets a 4-bar loop.
2. KICK: pick a sample (he avoids the slightly-thin D-Box kicks, uses a "bronze" kick), four-on-the-floor. Tune the kick (e.g., to D for more punch). Add the PUNCH effect — sweep to the low-end "sweet spot" that boosts the kick's frequency. Record to track 1 (the kick doubles as a metronome for the loose samples).
3. CHORDS: pitch the loop down 1–2 semitones (note: pitching also changes the sample SPEED — no time-stretch — but it still works at 118). Let it lag slightly behind the kick on purpose.
4. GLUE with the TREMOLO LFO as sidechain:
   - It's on Tremolo by default. Set SPEED to 4 (dips on quarter notes). Change the envelope SHAPE; add a slight TUNE difference so it warbles. The WHITE knob = INTENSITY of the sidechain pump.
   - Apply a subtle tremolo-LFO pump to the chords (and later the bass), keyed to the kick.
5. Perform the chords into track 2 (NOT track 1 — don't record over the kick).
6. BASS to track 3: bring it down a semitone to stay in key; add tremolo LFO for a subtle pump (less than the chords). The slight rhythmic mismatch (one note lands like a 16th, another like a triplet) is the desired "life."
7. SHAKER to track 4: very short sample — pitch it WAY down so it sits behind the beat; add NITRO to tame harsh frequencies; blast the tremolo LFO (white knob full) with ~10 warble. Treat it as a vibe/feel LAYER, not a lead — its sloppiness makes later quantized parts feel less rigid.

## General sampling pro-tips
- Keep samples on the WHITE KEYS — simplifies things.
- EXTEND samples: set the end point as long as possible even if you only trigger the front (e.g., "done done").
- Set one note (e.g., the 4th slice) as a ONE-SHOT safety so you can jump to a specific sound within the loop.
- Clickiness is expected on the OP-1; he doesn't mind it (fixable in the DAW). It disappears once all tracks are layered.

## Tape-reel varispeed
On the tape reel, turn the WHITE knob to change TEMPO/pitch: +1/+2 goes into "Mickey Mouse" territory; -1 is his preferred zone (darker, slower) though it slowed the groove too much here so he kept it as-is.
