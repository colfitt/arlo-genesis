Digiphex Electronics · OP-1 Field Tutorial - Sequencers · 2022-07-11

Covers all of the OP-1 Field's sequencers, reached via [Shift]+[Sequencer]. Press the sequencer button again to open the control page for the active one. (Note: the OP-1 Field also has the HOLD sequencer, covered in the ADG and loopop transcripts but not here.)

## ARPEGGIO
"Don't let the name fool you" — there are extra idea-generation features beyond a plain arp.
- Top row (four encoders): time signature; TRIGGER MODE (gold — adds notes to the sequence in creative ways); TRIGGER PATTERN (gray — skips steps to create different rhythms); NOTE LENGTH (blue, under shift).
- Hold [Shift] for the bottom row (colors map to encoders): ORANGE is typically HOLD (turn it on to latch).
- TYPE (gold): octave up; octave up-and-down; add the 7th/9th/-5; add 2/4/5/7/9.
- TRIGGER PATTERN (third/gray encoder): dots that disappear become RESTS; if the pause symbol is set to the arrow symbol instead, those steps are SKIPPED entirely (rather than rested).
- SWING ([Shift]+orange): 50% = no swing.

## ENDLESS
Like an SH-101 sequencer with extra functionality.
- ENTER a sequence by HOLDING [Shift] and pressing keys; use the ARROWS to add RESTS.
- Then just play it. Hold [Shift] and play it in different DIRECTIONS: forward, reverse, random.
- Apply a PATTERN to the sequence (patterns shown on the left of the screen) to generate variations.
- Capacity (per loopop): up to 128 steps.

## FINGER
- Each KEY holds a SEPARATE sequence. Play TWO keys together and the second syncs to the first, producing a unique combined (and often unexpected) third sequence. Great creative tool.
- Works for DRUMS too (shown as "two monkeys playing drums") — same concept, combine two per-key sequences for unexpected drum results.
- Capacity (per loopop): stores 14 patterns, two notes/polyphony max, combine two at a time.

## HOLD
- Set a BREAK POINT: notes BELOW it are held; notes above it are not. (See ADG/loopop transcripts for full detail.)
- Transpose the held note(s) with the GRAY encoder — works on single notes or whole chords (a strumming-like effect), staying in key.
- Clear held notes by pressing them again or pushing in the orange encoder.
- GOLD encoder switches mono / poly.

## PATTERN
- Looks like a conventional pattern sequencer (cells in a table), but triggers sit on the VERTICAL lines, not in the cells.
- Move the cursor with the BLUE encoder (or the arrows); place notes by HOLDING [Shift] (like endless); erase by holding [Shift] and moving the cursor BACK over a note.
- GOLD = swing; GRAY = pattern length. Use gold to OFFSET notes while playing for creative/unexpected results.
- [Shift]+orange = play mode: forward / forward-and-back / reverse.
- Capacity (per loopop): 16 steps.

## SKETCH
- Used like an Etch-A-Sketch: draw your own custom lines; the HORIZONTAL lines are what gets played. Two encoders move the cursor for drawing. SHAKE the unit (accelerometer) to erase and start over.

## TOMBOLA
- A physics sequencer: drop balls (each a chosen note) inside a hexagon; they sound when they hit a surface.
- Adjust the SPEED of the hexagon and the WEIGHT/mass of the balls; RELEASE the balls by turning the GRAY encoder.
- (Crank mode, per TE: [Shift]+blue lets you spin the tombola by hand.)

## Takeaway
The emphasis across all of these is creativity and UNEXPECTED results — new twists on old ideas. The presenter notes the OP-1 is "the only game in town" partly because of the analog tape simulation that nothing else pairs with these sequencers.
