## Build quality (hands-on)
Feels very well-made, nice weight, silky-smooth rubber pads. Endorses it as a genuine working tool, not a toy.

## Tempo + Warp interaction
- A recorded loop sits "perfectly on the grid." Change the **tempo** (e.g. 90 → 120) and, **because the sample is in Re-pitch mode**, the sample speeds up *by pitching up* (varispeed behavior).
- **Shift + minus = undo** (back to 90). To keep timing without pitch change, switch the sample out of Re-pitch into time-stretch (Warp).

## Real-time swing (the groove angle — IMPORTANT for AC50)
- **In Sequencer mode, "Real-Time Swing" is shown in the bottom-right corner of the screen.** Increase it and the off-beats shift forward to add "bounce and shuffle."
- Demo: with swing up, a **ghost kick on Pad 5 (in chop mode) gets shifted ever so slightly forward** → the J-Dilla-style bounce.
- **Side-effect to watch:** adding swing shifts the *last chop* forward and can leave a tiny gap. Tubedigga's fix: take that pad **out of Re-pitch mode** (so the slice doesn't varispeed when shifted) — a real workflow gotcha when swinging chopped breaks.
- NOTE: Tubedigga never mentions a separate "Quantize amount" control in the sequencer-record flow (unlike the bigger MPCs' Time Correct). The AC50's groove is driven by this **Real-Time Swing** value plus how tight you play. Treat swing-percentage as the main groove knob; flag that the AC50 may lack the bigger MPCs' full Timing-Correct/quantize-template depth — verify.

## Chop types (confirmed again, on-screen bottom-right)
Threshold → Regions 4 → Regions 8 → Regions 16 → Manual. (Matches the official chop video.)

## Saving / storage
- **Save Project** menu: a knob toggles **Internal vs External (microSD)** drive; top button cancels. Projects/samples can live on either.

## Honest limitations (his wrap-up, ~55:00)
- **No EQ.**
- **No mixer "to speak of"** — you adjust individual pad levels + the main mix, that's it.
- "There's not much more to it" — the depth comes from creative use, not deep menus. He frames the reduced feature set as the point.
