> Official, AC50-specific. Applies chop mode specifically to break-flipping. Cleaned to prose.

## Flip a break with Transients
- Take a drum break with a set pattern you want to change. Press **Chop** → choose a chop mode (regions / manual / transients). Andy uses **Transients** (detects the peaks of the break) → slices spread across the pads.
- Now **play the slices in a new order over the loop** to make a completely different pattern from the same break.

## Tighten each slice
- Go into an individual slice → edit **start/end** with the control knobs.
- **Hold Shift + encoder to zoom** per slice for accuracy (e.g. snap to the exact start of the snare transient). Zoom even tighter to land right on the transient.
- **Chop off** = original break; **Chop on** = the slices again.

## Manual ("lazy") chopping for breaks
- Change chop mode to **Manual** → hit the pads in real time where you want the chops to fall (play along to the running break).
- When done, use **Trim** to refine each slice's start/end; **encoder zoom** for accuracy.
- Add the pattern over your beat → record your own creative break.

## After chopping
- Add creative effects (Pad FX / Knob FX) over the chops.

Workflow takeaway for THIS rig: this is the exact recipe for the dossier's "Chop the fuzz wall" / banjo-break ideas — sample the bus, Chop → Transients, re-sequence the slices, then optionally bake LoFi/Color via Resample.
