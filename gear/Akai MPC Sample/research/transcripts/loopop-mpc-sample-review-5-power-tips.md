## What it is / isn't
- An **own thing**, NOT another form factor of the MPC OS 3 line. Reduced feature set + simpler workflow than other current MPCs.
- **IMPORTANT — CORRECTS THE DOSSIER:** loopop states you **CAN load MPC Sample projects into MPC OS 3 and the desktop software, and (within its limitations) export tracks back to MPC Sample.** The dossier §4/§11 says project import/export "is not currently supported (promised in a future firmware)." Treat loopop's claim as the newer reality — likely a firmware update landed it. **Flag as needs-verification against current firmware**, but lean toward "now supported."

## Sampling sources & buffers (a standout feature)
- Sources: built-in mic, computer/phone over USB, rear inputs, and **resample itself**.
- **Always-on recall buffer:** continuously records a **25-second audio buffer + the last played MIDI loop**. Recall either after the fact if you forgot to hit record, then chop/save.
- **Very long samples — up to 20 minutes.** Solves "play it perfectly twice" — just record long and trim.

## Project capacity
- Up to **128 bars** per sequence; **128 sequences** on board (**16 sequences/bank × 8 banks**). Up to **8 banks × 16 pads = 128 pad slots** per project.

## Pad modes (loopop's full list)
Beyond triggering + Shift secondary functions:
- **Chop mode:** threshold-based / region splits / manual chops; fine-tune start/end with or without zoom (knobs or encoder).
- **Sequence mode:** pads move sequence→sequence (works while playing or stopped); **swapping sequences is quantized.**
- **Mute mode:** pads mute instruments; **Shift clears the mutes.**
- **Pad FX mode:** pads control the **mod depth** of effects.
- **Flex Beat mode:** pads trigger Flex Beat patterns.
- **16 Levels mode:** by **Type** — different velocities / filter cutoff frequencies / chromatic pitch (**16 semitones up & down**).

## AC50-SPECIFIC LIMITATIONS (loopop, "at least on this firmware")
- **Single sequence track for all 128 pads** (32 voices). loopop's power-tip workaround: use **chops to play individual pads as polyphonic mini-tracks.**
- **No scale options** in 16 Levels.
- **Pad Bank does NOT transpose the grid** like the bigger MPCs.
- **Undo/Redo only 1 layer deep** (last action only).
- **No kit preview** (kits ship with sample patterns so you can hear them indirectly).
- **No way to see how much of the 8 GB is free.**
- **Can't access the internal storage over USB** (USB drive-mode is SD card only). User samples/projects can live on internal OR external; **microSD up to 1 TB** reportedly accepted.
- **Chop threshold adjustment is "a bit laggy"** and sample-dependent — fix split points manually by zooming.

## Knobs / takeover (matches dossier §11 fix)
- The bottom pot knobs have a **Scaled** mode (immediate control — loopop's preference) vs **Pickup** mode (move-to-catch, shows little arrows). Set per the firmware-1.3 Knob Takeover options.
- Push **encoder** (detented) + plus/minus buttons navigate/select lists; Shift gives secondary parameter access. On-screen arrow indicates a Shift option exists.

## Build / IO / output reality
- Pads: small but playable, **MPC-style** (need a bit of initial velocity), velocity + pressure sensitive — pairs well with Note Repeat.
- Screen: sharp, **not OLED**, tilted (good viewing angles).
- **Mono speaker** — fine for mids/highs but **drops off hard below ~200 Hz**; use headphones/monitors to judge bass.
- Mic is "surprisingly sensitive."
- IO: USB (MIDI/audio/SD drive access), stereo 1/4" in + out, 3.5 mm MIDI in/out, 3.5 mm analog sync out.

## Sample-edit parameters (loopop detail)
- Loop with a **separate loop-start point** (unlock loop-start, turn on Loop).
- Amp env: attack, decay, and a parameter that's **actually release**; **Shift makes "decay" work from the start** (i.e. decay-from-start vs release behavior).
- **Tune: ±24 semitones.**
- **Warp:** re-pitch OR time-stretch+pitch-shift based on the parameter value. In re-pitch, it works as a **function of play length** (100% = original; slower = pitched down).
- Velocity sensitivity per pad on/off; mono/poly play mode; multimode filter with its own envelope.

## Competition framing
Positioned vs the SP-404 MkII and EP-133 K.O. II (entry-level portable samplers), not its own bigger siblings. loopop also wrote a small **auto-sampler app** to expand what you can feed it over USB (an external-tooling idea, not an onboard feature).
