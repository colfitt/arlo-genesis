> Official, AC50-specific. The definitive chop-mode walkthrough. Cleaned to prose.

## Basic chop
- Select a sample → press **Chop** → slices land **across the 16 pads** → play your own melodies/patterns.
- Press **Chop again** to return to the original (un-chopped) sample.

## Editing chops (Trim within chop)
- Use **Trim mode** to adjust each chop's start/end with the knobs.
- **Hold Shift** to zoom into a chop for precision; or **hold Shift + turn the encoder** to zoom in/out — find a chop, then zoom. ("Hands-on control.")

## The five chop modes
1. **By Transients** — detects peaks. **Hold Shift to change the threshold.**
2. **By Region — 4 regions** (auto-maps to the first four pads).
3. **By Region — 8 regions.**
4. **By Region — 16 regions** (uses all pads).
5. **Manual** — the classic MPC **"lazy chop"**: hit the pads to create slices in real time.

## Per-chop sound shaping
- Go into an individual chop → change **start/end** with knobs; Shift zooms in.
- **Tune** — change the whole tuning of the chops.
- **Filters** — apply the classic MPC filters to chops (demoed low-pass; bring out resonance).
  > NOTE / AC50 limitation: in this video the tune and filter are demoed changing "the whole tuning of our chops." Per loopop's review (separate transcript), chopped slices **share** pitch/filter settings — you can't independently edit one slice's tune/filter without extracting it. Confirm against the dossier §11 ("No independent slice editing").

## Extract a chop to its own pad
- Select the chop you want → **hold Shift → press Extract** → it lands on the **first available pad** as an independent sample (which you can then tune freely, or chop again). This is the manual route around the single-kit / no-auto-convert limitation.

## Creative FX on chops
- Go to **Knob FX** (e.g. vinyl emulator) → select the pad where your chop lives → add vinyl noise + tape drift (per-pad).
- Play a beat over a looping break with a melodic chop on top.
