## Portability / sampling-source framing
- The pitch is **portability + immediacy**: backpack-sized, rechargeable, sample from your **iPhone over USB-C**, sample **from vinyl via the rear inputs**. "Takes things to an extremely fun level."
- Quick chop demo flow they keep returning to: **Loop on/off → switch to Melodic → Chop it** → replay slices. The fastest possible "grab a loop, flip it" loop.

## Pad-play modes shown (consistent with other sources)
Loop / Melodic / Chop, plus 16 Levels and the FX engines. The whole review emphasizes how **fast** it is to move between modes with dedicated buttons rather than menus.

## Verdict (close)
- "Built on **40 years of legacy**" — lineage from the Akai S1000/S950 samplers back to the MPC60's iconic look. "For once you're getting a lot more than what you're paying for."
- Calls it a likely "knockout" at the price; plan a future shootout vs other samplers. Strongly recommends it.

## Note for the rig
Confirms the two clean inputs path this studio cares about: **rear 1/4" inputs for line/vinyl** and **USB-C from a device**. This is the route for sampling the pedalboards off the Apollo bus or streaming a stem from Logic/Ableton (see dossier §5). No deep new technique beyond what the official videos cover.
