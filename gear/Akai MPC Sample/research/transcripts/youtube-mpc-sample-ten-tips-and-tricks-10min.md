## Tips (concrete)

1. **Sequences are NOT limited to 2/4/8 bars — hold SHIFT to get ANY bar count.**
   - Turning the bar-length encoder normally snaps to 2, 4, 8 bars. "But if I hold shift... now I get as many bars as I want." Turn it slowly while holding SHIFT to dial in odd/arbitrary bar lengths. Fixes the common "I can only get even bar counts" complaint.

2. **SHIFT is your best friend — every on-screen control is a double-function input.**
   - "Everything that you see on this screen is essentially a double-functioning input." Examples given: Pad 16 = projects, Pad 10 = Record Quantize (via SHIFT). Reiterated as the closing tip: "learn every button which has its function listed out. Remember, you have to hold shift to access that."

3. **The RECALL button(s) are extremely powerful — there are TWO recalls.**
   - One recall works at the **MIDI/performance level** (recall the last loop of played events), the other works **on the pad/sample itself**.
   - **SHIFT + Input Configuration** exposes **Source Recall**. NOTE: Source Recall does NOT work on the built-in microphone ("That does not work"). It DOES work on **Resample**.
   - Workflow: with Resample as the source, anything you were just jamming gets **recalled into an empty sample** (e.g. lands on pad B1) even if you didn't hit record. "Use that recall button like it's insanely helpful." (Great for the rig's "I just played the perfect thing" capture.)

4. **ZOOM in for trimming/slicing via SHIFT.** Holding SHIFT zooms into the waveform for precise trim/chop edits (the zoom function isn't labeled on the panel — it's a SHIFT hidden function).

5. **Step Edit: erase one drum across the WHOLE sequence at once (don't go step-by-step).**
   - SHIFT → Step Edit. Instead of turning to each step to delete a hit (the thing "everybody's been complaining about online"), just **select the pad, hold ERASE, press the pad → "Erase Events"** removes that sound from every step in the sequence at once. (e.g. kill the kick everywhere instantly.) Undo restores it.

6. **Don't forget 16 LEVELS for melody — but turn WARP OFF first.**
   - 16 Levels (Tune) is "where you're going to get the majority of your melody." If a sample is on **Warp** (tempo-following) the MPC won't let you 16-Level it ("can't do 16 level on that") — **turn Warp off**, then 16 Levels works and you can play it pitched.

7. **In 16 Levels there are "usually at least four notes available across any scale."** (i.e. you can find in-key notes within the 16-pad chromatic spread — pairs with the community 16-Levels scale-map cheat sheet.)

8/9. **It's a minimalist single-track device, but you're NOT limited to 16 samples — use the BANKS.** "This is not the MPC X." It's a "one track" workflow but there are many pad banks (A–H), so far more than 16 sounds per project.

9. **Your phone (iPhone/iPad) is a first-class companion** — used as the USB audio/stream source here; the AC50 works over USB-C with iOS devices.

10. **It's portable — take it everywhere** (battery + size; the whole point of the box).

11. **Loading another KIT is somewhat hidden:** in Load Project, go into **Kits**, then hit the dedicated button up top. The creator found it non-obvious ("I don't know why that seems so hidden") — initially he was only loading whole tracks/projects.

12. **GOTCHA — you can only load ONE kit at a time, and loading a kit ERASES everything.** "When you load a track or when you load a kit, everything is erased." Loading an additional kit wipes your current work INCLUDING samples on OTHER banks. Save first. (Confirms the dossier's single-kit limitation, with the destructive-wipe detail.)

13. **It's a LINEAR workflow "in the air" — plan the arrangement in your head.** Know where you are in the song; the box rewards committing.

14. **Half Sequence / Double Sequence buttons are key for arranging** — "this is how you're building your song." Don't sleep on the transport/arrange buttons row. There's also a **quick hotkey for Warp**.

- **PLAY-from-position = "Continue":** if you stop mid-sequence and want to resume from that spot (not the top), that's the Play/Continue behavior, equivalent to "Play" on the MPC X.

## Rig takeaways
- SHIFT + bar encoder = odd bar lengths for drone/non-4-bar structures.
- Source Recall via SHIFT+Input-Config (Resample source) = lossless capture of a jam you forgot to record — ideal for catching a one-take fuzz/banjo performance.
- Save BEFORE loading any kit (destructive, single-kit, wipes all banks).
- Turn Warp OFF before pitching a sampled banjo/baritone note across 16 Levels.
