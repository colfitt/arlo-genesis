The best **free, raw-WAV, on-aesthetic** source for the AC50. Everything is **24-bit WAV** (loads natively on the MPC Sample as plain audio — no kit/edition compatibility issue), and Goldbaby's whole identity is **tape-treated, valve-driven, lo-fi, vintage-sampler grit** — exactly the rig's "recorded-wrong" flavor. These are sample-by-sample drum/FX libraries you load to pads and build kits from by hand. All listed packs are FREE.

## Most on-aesthetic free packs for the drone/doom/lofi rig
- **The Cassette808** — TR-808 drums recorded to cassette. 21 × 24-bit WAV (+ Maschine kits). Wow/flutter 808s.
- **Tape606** — "analog TR-606 given the Tape treatment." 128 × 24-bit WAV.
- **TapeTR66 / TapeVermona / TapeMR16 / TapeDP50 / The TapeTonic** — Roland 70s analog, rare German/Korg/Technics machines + µTonic, all run through tape. Saturated, degraded one-shots.
- **ValveDR220e / Valve SK1 Drums** — vintage 80s + Casio SK1 drums through valve gear. Dirty, harmonic.
- **Toy Record Sounds** — "super lo-fi samples recorded on a toy record-maker." Maximum degradation.
- **SP1200 Collection Free / SP1200 vs MBase1 / MPC60vsMDvsRytm / MPC60 vs VolcaBeats** — sounds run THROUGH an SP-1200 (12-bit) or sampled into an MPC60's 12-bit sampler. The literal vintage-sampler crunch the AC50 emulates with its Vintage Emulator Knob FX — here as real source audio.
- **DecoClapTrap** — ClapTrap through a Strymon Deco (the rig owns a Deco V2!) tape double-tracking. Direct gear tie-in.
- **8R8 Free Pack** — drums through a Hologram **Chroma Console** (rig owns one). **StompBox Volca Kick / ND2 vs Plasma Kicks** — drum sources through guitar pedals. On-brand for a pedal-centric studio.
- **LoFi Holiday24** — 147 lo-fi drum/bass/synth/FX WAVs from cheap gear (PO-20, Kastle, Bleep Drum).

## Bonus: MPC60 Groove Templates
Free MIDI groove templates that impart the "MPC60 feel" to a DAW — not for the AC50 directly, but useful if you print MPC beats into Logic/Ableton and want to nudge other tracks into the same swing.

## How to use on the AC50
Download → unzip → drop the WAVs onto the microSD (via SD Card Access) → load to pads, build a kit, set choke/mute groups, finger-drum. Format note: the Sample accepts 16/24-bit, 44.1/48/96 kHz — these 24-bit WAVs are fine. Sampling the 12-bit SP1200/MPC60 packs and then adding the AC50's own LoFi/Color FX is a double-degradation move that suits the rig.

No compatibility flag needed — raw WAV is the universal path. Quality is high; Goldbaby is widely regarded as one of the best free drum-sample sources.
