AC50-SPECIFIC. Owner-written comparison of the MPC Sample vs its real rivals (TE EP-133 KO II, Roland SP-404 MkII / AIRA, Roland P-6). Best community summary of what the AC50 actually CAN'T do, plus where it wins. Distilled and quoted.

## MPC Sample — community-stated PROS
- 32-voice polyphony; "Best usable display" of the comparison group.
- Huge memory: 2 GB RAM + 8 GB internal storage + SD (rivals are tiny by comparison).
- "MPC compatible" / nice pads / rechargeable battery.
- Can sequence external MIDI gear (acts as a MIDI sequencer for the rig).

## MPC Sample — community-stated CONS / LIMITATIONS (the important part)
- **No keygroups.** Repeated as the #1 gripe. bitermc: "I wouldn't even be considering any other options if the MPC Sample had keygroups." A keyboardist's frustration — there's no multi-sample/velocity-layered instrument structure.
- **Chromatic samples play on only 16 notes.** Melodic playing is limited to the 16 pads in 16-Levels Tune mode — i.e. ~16 semitones, no wider range on the box itself.
- **Can't play CHORDS from chromatic samples natively.** "workarounds available, but no chords natively." (You'd have to pre-sample chords or stack pads.)
- bitermc says he confirmed others on **Gearspace** voicing the same keygroup/chromatic complaints — so this is a broadly-felt limitation, not one user.

## How this maps to the rig
- For melodic banjo/baritone-sample playing beyond 16 notes or for chords, drive the MPC from the **Novation 61SL MkIII over MIDI In** (real keyboard range) rather than relying on the pads — or pre-sample full chords and trigger them as one-shots.
- The AC50's win in THIS studio isn't keygroup-instrument depth; it's the **huge sample buffer + MPC pads/swing** for chopping drones and finger-drumming. Use it for what it's good at; lean on the Elektrons/61SL for the melodic/keygroup jobs it can't do.

## Other boxes (owner notes, for positioning)
- **TE EP-133 KO II:** quick & usable, sequences external MIDI, but no song mode, no storage beyond RAM (64/128 MB), AAA batteries. Owner: storage absence "kills me... having to use a PC every time I want to load or save... really??"
- **Roland P-6:** considered "more a pocket sized toy than a real instrument."
