The MPC Sample is, per the dossier, "the box that feels like fingers on pads making a beat." This file captures the concrete finger-drumming craft that turns it from a step-sequencer into a performance instrument — core to facet 2 (technique).

## Hand technique (the single biggest thing)
- **Hit, don't press.** "Make a downward wrist motion every time you hit a pad" — "Think **hitting**, not pressing. Think **drums**, not piano." MPC-style pads (incl. the AC50's) need a bit of initial velocity, so a wrist strike reads better than a finger poke.
- Right (dominant) hand carries the foundation; left hand adds 16ths, ghost notes, rolls, opens/crashes between the right-hand hits.

## Pad layout (where to put core sounds)
Two common philosophies — pick one and keep it consistent across kits so muscle memory transfers:
- **Vertical / right-column layout:** kick, snare, closed-hat stacked in one column so the whole groove foundation plays with the **right hand** (kick=thumb, snare=index, hat=middle); left hand on the rest for ghost notes, open hat, crash, rolls.
- **Center-row layout:** core kick/snare/hat on the **second row**, secondary sounds on the rows immediately above/below for minimal finger travel.
- General: kicks bottom corners, snares center, hats upper, toms/cymbals fill the rest. The "core never changes" — keep it stable.
- AC50 note: the Sample is **velocity AND poly-aftertouch** sensitive (pressure = Pad FX mod depth), so layout + pressure both perform.

## Velocity & ghost notes (the groove)
- Don't play everything full-level. Typical: **full velocity on accents, ~60–70% on regular hits, ~30–40% on ghost notes.** Soft ghost snares are the "human" glue. The AC50 captures and plays back your exact velocities.
- Use **16 Levels (Velocity type)** to spread one sound across 16 dynamic steps for practice/expressive fills; 16 Levels (Filter) and (Tune) are the other modes.

## Practice
- Start slow: kick + snare + hat only, **8th-note metronome at 70–85 BPM** (DIBIA$E's boom-bap go-to is also ~80–85). "Play as slow as possible and resist the urge to play faster." Don't set pads to full level while learning timing.
- Consistency: ~20 min, 5×/week. Expect to lean on quantize early, then wean off as the hands lock in (the Dilla path).
- **Note Repeat** (with Triplet) on the AC50 + pressure pads is the assist for hat rolls and machine-gun fills.

## Rig application
This is how you make the AC50 *perform* the rig's chopped drones/banjo rather than step-program them — keep a fixed layout, drum the foundation with the right hand, add human ghost-note swing with the left, and record unquantized for the Dilla/DIBIA$E feel.
