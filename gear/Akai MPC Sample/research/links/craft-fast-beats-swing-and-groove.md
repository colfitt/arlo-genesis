Concrete swing/groove values and a repeatable fast beat-making routine. Swing math is from Roger Linn himself (the man who invented MPC swing); the velocity and workflow recipe is from Audeobox's MPC drum-programming guides. All of it maps directly onto AC50 controls (RT Swing knob in Sequence Mode, global Swing in Time Correct, Note Repeat, 16 Levels, per-pad Time Correct).

## How MPC swing actually works (Roger Linn, in his own words)
- Swing = **delaying the second 16th note of every 8th-note pair** (all even-numbered 16ths get pushed late). That's the whole mechanism.
- Expressed as the ratio of the first to the second 16th within each 8th:
  - **50% = no swing** (both 16ths equal, dead straight).
  - **66% = perfect triplet swing** (first note 2/3, second note 1/3).
  - **54% = loosens rigid 16ths** without sounding like obvious "swing" — the subtle pocket.
  - **62% = noticeably looser** (Linn cites this at ~90 BPM as looser than a beginner expects).
  - Between **50–70% live "lots of wonderful little settings"** that turn a rigid beat into one that makes people move.
- **The feel depends on tempo** — what grooves at one BPM feels wrong at another. Don't default to 66% (math-perfect triplet); dial swing while the loop plays and stop where it pockets.

### AC50 application
- **RT Swing (K3 in Sequence Mode)** is a *live, non-destructive* swing you can ride while the loop plays — perfect for finding the pocket Linn describes, then leave it.
- **Time Correct → Swing (K3)** is the global/destructive swing applied on "Do It"; it also governs how **Note Repeat** feels.
- Practical starting points for this rig's chop-and-flip beats: **54–58%** for a subtle hip-hop pocket, push toward **62%** for an obvious shuffle. 58% is the most recognizable "MPC" swing (Audeobox).

## Velocity = the difference between a beat and a drum machine
From Audeobox's MPC drum-programming guide — vary velocity across the pattern:
- **Accented hits (downbeats, the "1"): ~100% (full velocity).**
- **Regular hits: 60–70%.**
- **Ghost notes: 30–40%.**
On the AC50: play it in with the velocity/poly-aftertouch pads (set **Vel Sens** high so the pads respond), or edit per-step velocity in **Step Edit**. **16 Levels → Velocity** spreads one sample across all 16 pads at rising velocities so you can finger-drum dynamics from a single sound — instant ghost-note/accent control without loading variations.

## Note Repeat for natural hats/rolls
Pressure-sensitive Note Repeat: hold **Note Repeat**, set the division with B1–B3 (Shift = triplet divisions), then **vary finger pressure light→medium** for a natural-sounding hi-hat roll "with far less skill required." This is the fast way to lay convincing hats and snare rolls. Its feel follows the global Swing setting.

## A repeatable START-TO-FINISH beat flow (keeps the fun, finishes fast)
Layer-by-pass, letting the loop cycle — this is the classic MPC pad-recording routine (Audeobox / SFTraxx), all AC50-doable:
1. **Set the canvas:** tempo (Tap Tempo or Shift+B1 BPM), 1–2 bar sequence (K1 bars), Q on (K2, start at 1/16), RT Swing ~56% (K3). Count-In on (Shift+Pad 4) if you want a lead-in.
2. **Pass 1 — kick + snare.** Seq Record + Play; finger-drum the foundation (snare on 2 and 4). Don't chase perfection — you have one undo, and the **recall buffer** (Shift+Recall) if you forgot to arm record.
3. **Pass 2 — hats.** Overdub keeps running; add hats with Note Repeat, varying pressure. Use a closed/open Mute Group.
4. **Pass 3 — percussion/accents + ghost notes.** Lower velocity (30–40%) for ghosts.
5. **Humanize:** if it feels stiff, use **per-pad Time Correct** — tighten only the hats, leave kick/snare slightly loose (J Dilla off-grid feel). Or nudge with the **Shift** value in Time Correct.
6. **Develop fast:** **Double Seq** to make bar 2, then change a few hits for variation; chain variations.
7. **Sweeten + transition:** ride a **Knob FX** (filter/delay) or punch in a **Flex Beat** stutter/gate (quantized) between sections — has a Mix control so you don't lose the groove.
8. **Glue + finish:** master **Color-Compressor** on (Color for warmth/grit), check level on headphones (not the mono speaker), then **Resample** the loop (FX baked) for a chop-and-rebuild pass, or build a **Song** (chain sequences) and **Export** to audio.

## Pro habits that keep it fun and fast
- **Start with two sounds (kick + snare) and a loop** — momentum beats planning. Add in passes.
- **Don't quantize everything.** Quantize hats, leave the snare/kick human. Linn famously built feel by *not* perfectly aligning.
- **Resample early and often** to commit and free pads — turns "menu fiddling" back into "playing."
- **Use the recall buffer as your safety net** so you can jam freely without babysitting the record button.
- **Finish the loop before chasing the next pedal/sample** (the responsible-Dad rule): a finished 8-bar beat beats ten half-ideas.
