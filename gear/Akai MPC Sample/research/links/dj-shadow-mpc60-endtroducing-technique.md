DJ Shadow built *Endtroducing.....* (1996) — the album credited as made entirely from samples — on an **Akai MPC60**, the direct ancestor the MPC Sample is a tribute to (dossier §1). Best illustration of how much you can do with a tiny rig and pure sampling, which is precisely the AC50's pitch.

## The minimal rig (the lesson)
- **Akai MPC60 + Technics SL-1200 turntable + Alesis ADAT.** "He kept his rig bare bones throughout the creation of his first album." The MPC60 did "almost all composition." Two years of work, mostly one sampler.
- Parallel for the AC50: one small sampler + a source (the rig's pedalboards / banjo / records) + somewhere to print = a full record. Limitation breeds identity.

## Technique
- **Crate-digging as composition.** Spent hours daily at record shops; **rule to avoid popular/recognizable material** — obscure sources only. The sample *selection* is half the craft.
- **Turntable pitch control to layer samples.** Before time-stretch was easy, Shadow used the **SL-1200's ±pitch fader to tune/align samples to each other** so layered loops at different original tempos/keys would lock — a hardware workaround for matching samples.
- **Editing + layering by ear.** "Using ingenuity and workarounds to pluck finite fragments of sound... and rearrange them into a dizzying array of compositions." "Relying on his keen ear for guidance."

## On the AC50 today
- The AC50 gives you what Shadow improvised by hand: **Warp (time-stretch or re-pitch)** to align samples to project tempo/key without a turntable; **Chop** to grab "finite fragments"; long disk-streamed samples to capture whole passages.
- Shadow's "obscure sources only" rule maps perfectly to a drone/doom/shoegaze studio — sample your *own* fuzz walls, banjo, accordion, room noise via the built-in mic. Nobody else has those sources.
- Free **DJ-Shadow-style kit** exists at mpc-tutor.com (see mpc-tutor-free-kits-breaks-dilla-shadow.md) to start in that palette.

## Honest note
Shadow has since left the MPC for DAWs (and publicly said going back to a new MPC "doesn't work" for him now). The *Endtroducing* method — minimal sampler, deep obscure sources, layer-by-ear — is the transferable lesson; he's not a current MPC-Sample user.
