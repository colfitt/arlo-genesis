The defining MPC artist. J Dilla is the patron saint of "MPC swing/feel" — the technique the MPC Sample's whole philosophy inherits (dossier §7). Concrete, transferable craft below.

## The gear
- Settled on the **Akai MPC3000** as his sampler of choice after the E-mu SP-12 and SP-1200. His personal MPC3000 is now in the **Smithsonian** (National Museum of African American History & Culture).
- Late-period *Donuts* (2006) was made bed-ridden on an **SP-303 + Numark PT-01 portable turntable + a crate of 45s** — proof the *feel*, not the flagship gear, is the point. (Directly relevant: the AC50 is a tiny portable box; Dilla shows portable/limited gear is no excuse.)

## The technique (the "Dilla feel" / Dilla Time)
- **Per-note swing, not global swing.** "The MPC allowed the user to adjust swing values for each sound and each note individually, as opposed to adjusting the global swing of an entire beat." This is the core unlock.
- **Quantize off / hand-played.** Many beats were "played in by hand and unquantized." He treated quantize as optional, often mixing quantized and unquantized tracks in one beat so "human imperfections" stay in.
- **Deliberate micro-timing.** "Precise adjustments to micro-timing — a kick hits 30ms early here, a snare drags by 20ms there." Off-grid on purpose → "near-polyrhythmic take on the classic boom-bap template."
- **Micro-chopping.** Chopping samples into tiny pieces and reassembling them "with meticulous technique that made his splicing seamless and fluent."

## Workflow discipline
Ritual daily routine: up at 7AM, ~2 hours curating samples from a meticulously organized record collection, then beats "often completing them in just 15–20 minutes each." Speed + a deep, organized sample library.

## How to apply it on the AC50
- **Turn quantize OFF and finger-drum** the kick/snare/hat; let timing be human. (loopop confirms the Sample sequences quantized OR unquantized.)
- **Use the AC50's Offset (silence-in-front) + Pad Link** to hand-build swing per pad, the way DIBIA$E demos (youtube-dibiase-mpc-sample-demo.md) — the modern equivalent of Dilla's per-note nudges.
- **Micro-chop** the rig's drones/banjo with Lazy Chop, then reassemble off-grid.
- Vary velocity hard (the AC50's poly-aftertouch pads make this expressive) so accents/ghosts breathe.

## Caveat
Dilla never touched the MPC Sample (he died 2006). This is the *lineage* the box descends from — transferable craft, not this-model history. Load the free Dilla-style kit (mpc-tutor-free-kits-breaks-dilla-shadow.md) to practice it concretely.
