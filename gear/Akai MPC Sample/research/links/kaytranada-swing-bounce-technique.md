A *contemporary* MPC/groovebox finger-drummer whose whole sound is "bounce" — a modern, danceable extension of the Dilla swing lineage. Useful as the present-day reference for the swing-forward craft the AC50 is built for.

## The signature: "bounce"
- Swing is central. His grooves sit between straight and swung, with deliberate timing looseness that makes drums feel like they "bounce."
- **Side-chain / ducking on the samples, triggered by the kick** — "gives extra bounce and helps the kick drum and claps cut through." (On the AC50, the equivalent is the **pumper Knob FX** assigned per pad — see DIBIA$E demo and loopop's clarification that it's a pumper, not true side-chain.)

## Drum programming approach
- Backbone: **kick on every beat, claps on 2 & 4, hats/shakers carrying 16ths for syncopation.** Then he subverts expectation — e.g. dropping drums back in on the "3" instead of the "1" for an unpredictable, direct feel.
- Sound palette: "golden-era hip-hop" samples combined with "faster-paced, almost house-tempo rhythmic structures" — the swing of boom-bap at dance tempos. (DIBIA$E's "build a set working up to 120 BPM" approach is a similar tempo ladder.)
- Started on FruityLoops; cites drum breaks and new jack swing as touchstones (RBMA lecture).

## On the AC50
- Program a simple kick-every-beat / clap-on-2&4 / 16th-hat skeleton, then **add swing** (the MPC swing param) and **assign the pumper to the melodic/chord pads keyed to the kick** for the bounce.
- Try the "don't come back on the 1" arrangement trick in Song Mode — leave a beat of space, re-enter on the 3.
- His house-leaning tempos show the box isn't just for 85-BPM boom-bap; the same swing-and-duck approach scales up.

## Honest note
Coverage of Kaytranada on *this specific box* is nonexistent (young product) and detailed rig-rundowns of his exact hardware are thin — this is transferable technique (swing + kick-ducking + expectation-subverting drum placement), drawn from production breakdowns rather than a Sample demo.
