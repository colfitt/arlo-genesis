# Attack Magazine — "90s Boom Bap Hip Hop" (Beat Dissected) — concrete settings

URL: https://www.attackmagazine.com/technique/beat-dissected/90s-boom-bap-hip-hop/
site · attackmagazine.com · Beat Dissected series · accessed June 2026

A DAW walkthrough but every number translates 1:1 to AC50 controls. This is the most
concrete boom-bap drum-craft recipe found.

## The numbers (quoted / paraphrased from the breakdown)
- **Swing: "MPC 3000, 8ths, 57"** for kick & snare; **"MPC 3000, 16th, 74"** for hi-hats.
  → On AC50: global Real-Time Swing ~57% feel; hats get the harder shuffle.
  (AC50 swing is global, so apply ~57% and let hats ride; or sample hats already-swung.)
- **Sample transposed down -5 semitones** (the chopped melodic/source sample).
- **Kick EQ: small boost ~60 Hz, significant dip ~150 Hz** (scoop the boxiness, keep the thump).
  → AC50 has no EQ; approximate with the per-pad **Filter** (LPF to tame >150 Hz mud) + tune.
- **Snare layer:** middle snare ("Snare Dobbs") pulled to **-21.5 dB** under the main; a **clap
  nudged later** (a few ms) for a "late"/dragged feel.
- **Reverb on snare/clap: a "Vocal Pop Hall" preset.** Low-end rolled off the source; highs slightly cut.
- **Auto-Pan on the hat channel**; **sidechain compressor on the sample, triggered by the kick**
  (→ AC50 **Pumper** Knob FX keyed to the beat); **limiter on master**.

## AC50 translation summary
- Swing 57%; tune chop **-5 st**; kick Filter LPF + slight tune-down; snare = main + 2nd snare at
  ~-21 dB + late clap (use **Offset** to drag the clap pad); Pumper for the kick-duck; finish in
  Apollo since there's no master EQ/limiter on the box.
