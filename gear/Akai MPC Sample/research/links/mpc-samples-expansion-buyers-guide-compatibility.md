The primer that explains WHAT an MPC expansion is and HOW the AC50 fits the compatibility picture. This is the file to read first to understand why "does this pack work on the MPC Sample?" has a nuanced answer.

## What an MPC expansion contains
- "Samples, kits, instruments, sequences & projects... all specifically formatted and optimised for use in an MPC," usually themed by genre.
- On the big MPCs, kits/instruments come **pre-mapped across the pads with FX, filters, envelopes and parameters configured**, and you get **browser previews** (single-tap to audition any kit/instrument/MIDI/preset).

## The two standard installers (neither is the AC50's)
1. **Standalone installer** — drag the expansion to the `Expansions` folder on an MPC One/Live/X/Force disk; the MPC OS 3 browser scans it. (Built around `.xpm` programs.)
2. **`.xpn` installer** — for MPC Software / MPC Beats desktop only. **`.xpn` will NOT install into any standalone MPC or Force.**

## Where the AC50 stands (the key takeaway)
- The MPC Sample runs **its own firmware, not MPC OS 3** (loopop, Akai FAQ). So the generic standalone installer's `.xpm`-based, auto-scanned `Expansions` model does **not** apply to it the same way, and browser kit-previews aren't present (loopop: "no kit preview").
- BUT: **"Many [expansions] also include a version built specifically for the MPC Sample"** — the **"MPC Sample Edition"** (e.g. Dirty Drummer = 60 MPC-Sample projects, one kit per project pre-sequenced). Those DO load on the AC50 via SD Card Access (see mpc-samples-mpc-sample-expansion-install.md).
- **Always raw-WAV-compatible:** regardless of edition, the underlying samples are WAV/AIFF/etc., which the AC50 loads as plain audio. Worst case you skip the prebuilt kit and chop/load the WAVs yourself.

## Practical buying rule for this rig
1. Prefer packs that **explicitly list an "MPC Sample Edition"** (MPC-Samples Dirty Drummer, Underground Crates). Turnkey on the AC50.
2. **Do not assume** generic "for all modern standalone MPCs / Force / MPC Software" packs (incl. NI Drift Theory [needs MPC OS 3.5+], Faded Reels [hardware list excludes the Sample]) will load as kits — they target MPC OS 3.
3. **Free raw-WAV libraries** (Goldbaby, mpc-tutor breaks) are always safe — universal compatibility, and the most on-aesthetic for the rig anyway.
4. Park third-party content on the **microSD** (8 GB internal isn't USB-mountable).

## Flag
The "MPC Sample Edition" ecosystem is new and growing; availability per title changes. Verify each product's compatibility/tech-specs note at purchase. Official Akai expansions are sold via **thempcstore.com**; confirm Sample compatibility there too — many official titles are MPC-OS-3-oriented.
