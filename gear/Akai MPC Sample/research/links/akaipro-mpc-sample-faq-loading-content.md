Official MPC Sample FAQ. Authoritative on factory content, file handling, and the project-import limitation. Distilled from the page (WebFetch returned partial content; cross-checked against the Sweetwater quickstart and the official product spec).

## Factory content (what ships on the box)
- **~2 GB factory content**, including **over 100 drum-kits and melodic loops** across multiple styles. Each factory kit comes with its own sample sequences/patterns (loopop confirms; no in-firmware kit preview).

## Loading your own content (official method)
- The MPC Sample mounts as a **removable storage drive** when connected to a computer via the included **USB-C** cable. Transfer content this way, or via the **microSD** slot.
- Steps mirror the SD Card Access flow: connect, hold **SHIFT + PAD 16 (PROJECT)**, encoder → **"SD Card Access,"** drag-and-drop samples to the Sample drive; then Sample Select → SD Card Access to use them. (Sweetwater quickstart, same steps.)
- Supported import formats (dossier §12, corroborated by Sweetwater): **.wav, .mp3, .aif/.aiff, .snd, .s1s, .s3s, .flac, .ogg**; 16/24-bit, 44.1/48/96 kHz. Internal processing is 32-bit float / 44.1 kHz.

## Compatibility limitation (official, important)
- **"No, MPC 3 projects should not be exported to your MPC Sample."** — i.e., you cannot bring a full desktop/standalone MPC 3 *project* down to the AC50 wholesale.
- Forward path: MPC Sample projects "will be able to be opened with MPC hardware/software running version **3.8 and higher**." (loopop says 3.8 import already works and flattens to a single track; treat the round-trip down to the Sample as limited/lossy.)
- The FAQ does **not** explicitly bless generic MPC Expansions on the AC50 — consistent with the need for "MPC Sample Edition" packs or raw WAV loading. Flag: official Akai content channels (thempcstore.com) may or may not gate Sample compatibility per pack; verify.

## Bottom line
The AC50 is a class-compliant USB/SD removable drive that loads standard audio files. Anything as raw WAV/AIFF/FLAC loads fine; structured "kits/programs" need to be the Sample's own format/edition. 100+ factory kits are already on board to start.
