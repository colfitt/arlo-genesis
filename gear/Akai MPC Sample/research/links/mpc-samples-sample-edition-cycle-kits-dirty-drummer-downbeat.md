These are the AC50-native commercial packs. They're worth capturing because they reveal **how
a "proper" MPC Sample drum kit is structured** — the template to copy when you build your own
banjo/baritone kits.

## Dirty Drummer (Complete) — MPC Sample Edition
- Boom-bap hip-hop drum kits + MIDI patterns "for All MPC, Force & Akai MPC Sample."
- The **MPC Sample edition = 60 projects**, each project = **a kit + a matching drum-groove sequence**.
- All kits configured as **"cycle kits" with round robins** (multiple samples per pad that
  rotate on repeated hits to dodge the machine-gun effect).

## The Downbeat Kit (3) — MPC Sample Edition
- Multisampled **acoustic** drum kit for modern MPCs, MPC Sample & Force.
- MPC Sample edition = **17 kits, all configured as cycle kits with up to 4 round robins per pad.**

## Why this matters for the user's own sample-making
- **Round-robin / cycle-kit structure** is the pro way to make sampled drums feel alive. On the
  AC50 (no native round-robin engine) you fake it by spreading 2–4 takes of the same hit across
  adjacent pads in one **Mute Group** and triggering them in rotation, or using **16 Levels**.
- The "kit + matching groove sequence in one project" packaging is the template: build the kit,
  then save a demo sequence alongside it so the kit always loads with a usable beat.
- These prove the AC50 needs the **dedicated "MPC Sample Edition"** format — generic `.xpm`
  expansions (MPC OS 3) do NOT load as kits (confirmed elsewhere in this folder).
