AC50-SPECIFIC TIP. A community "cheat sheet" for playing in-key melodies with the AC50's **16 Levels = Tune** mode — directly useful for this rig's "sample a banjo/baritone note, play it chromatically" workflow.

## The tip: play Major/Minor scales on the 16 pads in 16-Levels (Tune) mode
On the MPC Sample, **16 Levels (Tune)** spreads one sample chromatically (one semitone per pad) across all 16 pads. The pads ascend chromatically pad 1 → pad 16 starting bottom-left (standard MPC chromatic layout). So consecutive pads are a half-step apart; to stay in a key you have to SKIP the out-of-scale pads.

The infographic's reference points:
- **Root note = Pad 4** (bottom row, rightmost column) in the author's map.
- **Pad layout: chromatic sequential 1→16, starting bottom-left.**
- Workflow: "tune your sample to the root note of the scale you want to play, then... follow these visual patterns to stay perfectly in key" — i.e. memorize the major/minor pad-skip pattern and you can finger a scale without hitting wrong semitones.

WHY IT MATTERS / LIMITATION: the AC50 has **no keygroups and no built-in scale/key lock** (see the "vs The Alternatives" capture). 16 Levels Tune is the only way to play a sample melodically, and it gives you only **16 semitones of range** with NO note quantize — so an external pad map like this is the practical workaround to stay in key. For melodic playing over a wider range, drive the MPC from the rig's Novation 61SL MkIII over MIDI In instead.
