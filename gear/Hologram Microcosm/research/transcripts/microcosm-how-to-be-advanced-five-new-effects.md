# Hologram Microcosm: How to Be Advanced — 5 ways to design new effects (transcript)

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MnSR022Biig
YouTube · "How to Be Advanced" tutorial · Nov 2021 · ~7:45

Concept-rich walkthrough of using the Microcosm as an *effect-design engine* rather than a
preset box. Exact on-screen knob values are not narrated (the presenter shows the panel), so
treat these as technique recipes — engine choice + the controlling idea — not literal knob
charts. Quotes verbatim from captions.

Framing: "anytime you play anything, it takes a lot of little samples of whatever you just
played and then mangles those samples in intense ways… five ways you can use that to create
brand new effects."

## 1. Glitch at Mix 100% — play the pedal, not the guitar
"Go into Glitch mode and put the Mix on 100%… everything you play is going to come out of
this pedal sounding completely different. It's actually going to be hard to play because
everything you're hearing is going to be so different from what you think you're putting into
the pedal." Pro tip: "as weird as the pedal is, everything it does will have a regular rhythm
to it. And if you can just lock onto the pulse of what the pedal is doing, you can still
control it… find that pulse and hang on to it for dear life." (Relevant to feeding chaotic
fuzz walls in — lock to the granular pulse and play to it.)

## 2. Tunnel mode — "edited in real time"
"There's a way to set up Tunnel mode… where it actually sounds like, while you're playing,
someone is already messing with it in post-production, even though there is no
post-production… you can play in real time and have it sound like someone in the future is
editing what you're playing while you're playing it." This is the "recorded-wrong / degraded"
aesthetic the rig wants — Tunnel as a live re-edit engine.

## 3. Infinite slapback you control by playing
"A little bit like having a slapback delay with infinite repeats, but… you can control the
infinite repeats by playing more. If you play something, it'll immediately grab that and start
giving you an infinite slapback repeat of that. But as soon as you play anything else, it'll
ditch what it was doing and start to give you infinite repeats of the new thing you just
played." (Self-replacing infinite repeat — newest phrase wins. Good for evolving beds without
a looper.)

## 4. "Trippy" delay
"More than a delay sound… sort of indescribable… There might be other ways to create this
effect, but you would need a lot of pedals to do it, and the Microcosm just gives it to you."
(Settings shown on screen, not narrated.)

## 5. Lo-fi guitar
"A lo-fi guitar sound I discovered that just sounds not trendy… lo-fidelity guitar sounds are
sort of having a moment… a way to sort of cop that vibe with the Microcosm that to me just
does not sound played out yet." (Bit-crush/degrade engines — Blocks D / Seq D / Interrupt
territory — for the "recorded-wrong" texture.)

Takeaway for capture: the most useful general principle here is #1's pro tip — the Microcosm's
output always has an internal pulse; performances come together when you lock to that pulse
rather than fight it.
