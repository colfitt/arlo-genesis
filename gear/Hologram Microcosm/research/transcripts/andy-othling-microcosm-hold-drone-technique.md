## The Hold-drone technique (verbatim workflow)
"I use this next feature a lot." He hits the **Hold button** to capture the current sound — "now we've captured this sound and it's going to stay doing that until I tell it to stop." The held buffer keeps feeding the effects section, so:

- **Scroll through the sub-modes (A/B/C/D) over the frozen sound to access different pitch ranges.** Move up one mode → up an octave. Move to the next mode → up another octave while also adding a very low one underneath. (Demonstrated in Mosaic-style modes: each step restacks the octave content over the same held bed.)
- **If you don't want to switch through seven modes but still want less high end, roll the Filter back** instead.
- **You still have full live control while frozen:** "I just find the Hold mode great because you still have access to the Activity knob, you can mess with stuff, you can mess with the Time." Speeding up the Time gets it "all choppy."

## His mental framing
He treats the whole pedalboard like a DAW; the Microcosm is "ear candies alongside everything else," used looser/more generatively than the rest of the rig — a texture layer you freeze and then evolve by hand over the top of loops.
