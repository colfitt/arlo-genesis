## The Microcosm's role in his generative loop
- **Engine: classic MOSAIC mode D**, doing **two octaves up and two octaves down** with the sound, with a **very long Time** on it — "it'll take a while for us to hear" (the octave-stacked content drifts in slowly). That's the textural layer the Microcosm contributes before the signal goes back into Polymoon (delay) and the reverb.
- **Mix level for unity:** he runs Mix at roughly **2 o'clock**, NOT noon — "if I put it at noon that doesn't sound like 50/50 to me; it's at about two o'clock to my ear, that's like unity, I'm going for unity there." (Useful calibration note: on his ear the Microcosm's perceived dry/wet balance sits right of center.)
- **Expression / ramper on the Filter (the generative trick):** he assigns a slow ramper to the **Filter** while at **full Mix**. The filter opens to let the high "twinkles" come through, then "filters itself out again" — a continuously moving, self-modulating element that makes the parts land differently every cycle. Rolling the filter further back also lets the dry signal back into the mix a little.

## Rig context (for cross-referencing other gear in the repo)
The full loop chains the Microcosm alongside a Chase Bliss **Blooper** (he's "always in overdub"), **Generation Loss** (filtered/degraded layer), a Boomerang looper, and Polymoon — everything MIDI-clocked so the asynchronous parts land together. The thesis: see the pedalboard as a DAW, with the Microcosm as the moving/generative texture voice rather than a structured looper.
