## Setup
Yamaha Pacifica (only source), light compression/overdrive, stereo signal into a Boss RC600 looper, which can hold three asynchronous stereo loops. The RC600 sends out a MIDI tempo derived from the recorded loop length, so the Microcosm's time-based effects sync to whatever you played. A Chroma Console sits after the Microcosm for a little extra glitter/character. (Both units supplied free by Hologram; no editorial strings.)

Workflow throughout: record a deliberately simple 2-4 note loop into the RC600, then audition each Microcosm algorithm against it so the effect's behavior is audible. "This is just two notes on a loop" is repeated to emphasize how much the engine generates from minimal input.

## MICRO LOOP — "layers of short loops played back at various speeds combined to form new rhythmic phrases and tonal colors"

### MOSAIC — overlapping loops playing back at multiple speeds. Activity = number of active loopers.
- **A:** normal + double speed (x1 and x2) — octave-up harmonies.
- **B:** normal + half speed (x1 and x0.5) — effect one octave below. Octave-down pitch is "really rich," not artificial.
- **C:** like A but no normal speed — all double speed (x2).
- **D:** half / normal / double / quad — from one octave below to two octaves above.
- Turn Activity up to add more loopers; Repeats makes them longer. Press the effect-selector knob to **reverse** the whole buffer. With Activity and Repeats high, then muting the source, you get "wonderful fade-outs."

### SEQ (Sequence) — short looping samples rearranged into new rhythmic sequences. Described as the most complex effect.
- **A:** live samples are filtered and shuffled into random rhythms; Activity introduces filter variations. Picks up the RC600 MIDI so the sequence stays in time. Shape changes the sound a lot; Time knob is "hours of fun."
- **B:** samples alternate between normal and half speed; Activity = the bounce rate between the two speeds. **Fully clockwise on one knob introduces a sustainer layer** so soft pads sit under the sequence.
- **C:** overlapping layers of samples with filter sweeps; Activity adds layers. Called "a beautiful effect."
- **D:** interlocking layers of rhythmic samples with bit-crushing; Activity adds rhythmic layers; **fully clockwise gives a bit-crushed sub-octave (octave-down) layer.**

### GLIDE — short overlapping loops shift pitch over time. Activity = rate of pitch shifting; Shape = shape of the glide pattern.
- **A:** glides between half and normal speed (x0.5 to x1).
- **B:** between double and half speed.
- **C:** between normal and double speed.
- **D:** between half and double speed, gliding both up AND down (not just one direction). Very spacey.

## GLITCH — "real-time rearrangements of your incoming signal played back at random or controlled intervals"

### BLOCKS — incoming audio triggers predictable glitches and random bursts. Activity = amount of variation / sample manipulation.
- **A:** rearranges your playing, adds bursts of sequenced runs of recent notes.
- **B:** rearranges AND pitch-shifts the incoming signal (octave-lower content available).
- **C:** filtered samples overlap and fade out — a softer, less angular glitch ("filter and fade").
- **D:** rearranges using pitch shift + bit crushing.

### INTERRUPT — glitches interrupt the dry signal with pitch-shifted bursts, micro-montages, other modifiers. Activity = variation; **Repeats = how often glitches are triggered.**
- **A:** glitched/rearranged versions of your playing interrupt the live signal.
- **B:** interruptions include pitch-shifted samples ("super glitchy").
- **C:** filter and delay.
- **D:** bit crush. ("Bit crush reversed" sounds great.)

### ARP — sequences samples of recent note onsets into arpeggios. Activity = number of steps in the arpeggio.
- A basic, B pitch shift, C random filter, D bit crush (same A/B/C/D pattern as Blocks/Interrupt). Play distinct notes to hear the arpeggios clearly.

## GRANULES — "fragments of sound create giant atmospheres and subtle textural effects"

### HAZE — clusters of grains create a wash of sound. Activity = grain density and spread.
- **A:** short diffused effect from stretching/overlapping samples.
- **B:** many simultaneous randomized grains — diffused textural effect ("random diffuse," smoother).
- **C:** mix of normal + double speed grains (x1 and x2) — diffused texture.
- **D:** mix of normal + half speed (x1 and x0.5, octave down). "Very much a grain wash." Behaves like a granular delay / reverb. Cycle Shape to find an envelope you like.

### TUNNEL — cyclical micro-loops generate hypnotic drones ("micro-loop drones"). Activity = depth of each modifier.
- **A:** drone sample length compresses and lengthens (a sample-length LFO). "Really quite a nice subtle effect."
- **B:** sub-octave content audible.
- **C:** resonant bandpass — "a lot of resonance there, got to be careful with that one"; can be fast, slow it down.
- **D:** envelope LFO; the D variations tend to be more bit-crush/noisy than the others.

### STRUM — rhythmic chains of recent note onsets create pointillistic textures. Activity = density.
- **A:** repeats the most recent note continuously.
- **B:** many copies of the most recent note onset overlap to create phasing effects; cascading chains of recent onsets.
- **C/D:** cascading chains; **D adds double-speed grains** for more density.

## MULTI-DELAY — "a delay line with a selectable number of taps." Only two effects here (the fourth slot is the User preset bank).

### PATTERN — delay taps arranged into four rhythmic patterns. Activity = number of active delay taps.
- **A:** classic linear delay. (Demonstrated against a loop with lots of empty space so the delay has room.) RC600 MIDI syncs the delay.
- **B:** less linear, more syncopated.
- **C / D:** further rhythmic patterns (the manual gives no detail beyond "four different rhythmic patterns"). Pattern D is "pretty subtle."

### WARP — delay taps manipulated with filters + pitch shifting for spacious textural effects. Activity = number of active delay taps.
- **A:** envelope-controlled filter on each delay tap.
- **B:** resonant bandpass on each tap.
- **C:** adds pitch shifting to each tap.
- **D:** delay taps crossfade with double-speed grains. "Warp D. Beautiful." Four repeats sounds great.

## Takeaways the host emphasizes
- The Microcosm always has small internal variation happening within any one setting, so it never sounds static or stale.
- Feeding it MIDI clock (here from the RC600, derived from loop length) is "a fantastic tool" — it brings the Microcosm in time with whatever you played for all the time-based effects.
