# Hologram Microcosm: Hold Sampler Overview (official video transcript)

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9dE6f16Z1Lg
YouTube · Hologram Electronics (Matt, Hologram) · April 2022 · ~4:40

Distilled prose from the official subtitle track. This is the single most authoritative
walkthrough of the Hold Sampler — the rig's headline drone feature (Board 2). Quotes are
verbatim from the captions.

## What the Hold Sampler is

"Microcosm's Hold Sampler is a unique feature that takes a recent segment of your playing
and sustains the resulting effects infinitely." As Microcosm samples your incoming signal it
"collects buffers or segments of audio to process"; the presets resample those segments "in
different ways to achieve the most musical result." So the Hold Sampler freezes whatever is
currently in the buffer and the active engine keeps re-processing it forever.

## Same phrase, two presets — engine choice changes the freeze

The official demo compares the same played phrase frozen through two engines:

- **ARP A** — "with activity all the way down it repeats the most recent sample of audio, but
  as you turn the Activity knob up it will introduce more samples to the arpeggiating
  pattern." Frozen, you "hear it looping an arpeggiating pattern of the recently sampled
  sounds." Multiple notes from the buffer are present.
- **TUNNEL B** — "will only process the most recent sample regardless of where the Activity
  knob is." Frozen, it sustains that single most-recent sample as a drone.

Key insight: even when Tunnel B is "sustaining the most recent sample, other samples exist
in the buffer when the Hold Sampler is engaged, which is why the other notes are still
present in Arp A." The buffer holds more than what you hear; switching engines reveals it.

## Scroll engines while frozen (the core creative move)

"You can scroll from preset to preset while the Hold Sampler is active to experiment with
Microcosm's effects." Hologram's own framing: "this is actually a really good way to get to
know the pedal and I learned a lot from it just by using the Hold Sampler." → Freeze a chord,
then audition engine variations A–D over the sustained drone without re-triggering.

## Toggle vs. momentary (Global Config)

- **Default = Toggle:** "when I press Hold and release, it remains holding the sample, and
  then to deactivate it you press it again."
- **Momentary:** enter Global Configuration, "move this Repeats knob and move this white
  cursor over to the second bar and press in the Preset Selector." Then "the Hold Sampler is
  only active so long as the footswitch is depressed." (Good for gestural, stab-and-release
  freezes.)

## Drone-under-playing technique (most important for the rig)

"The Hold Sampler is a super cool way to create complex patterns and drones that sit
underneath your playing by reducing the Mix knob on Microcosm." Critically: "when the Hold
Sampler is active, what you're playing into the pedal is not getting put into the buffer that
Microcosm is processing, and your dry signal just passes through unaffected." → Freeze a bed,
pull Mix back, and play a fresh dry lead on top of the frozen drone. This is exactly the
"freeze a wall, play banjo lead over it" move the dossier calls for.

Workflow shown: "play one phrase, engage the Hold Sampler, and just mess around with some
different presets."
