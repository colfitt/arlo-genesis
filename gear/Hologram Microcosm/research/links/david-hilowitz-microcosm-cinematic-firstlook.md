# David Hilowitz — the most-watched Microcosm video (first-look, NOT a settings recipe)

The single biggest Microcosm video on YouTube (2.5M views). Captured to log it as reviewed and to record what it
does/doesn't offer for patches.

- **Format = "first look," explicitly NOT a deep dive.** Hilowitz's own pinned description: "This video was very
  much intended as 'first look' rather than a deep dive." The official AI summary confirms it just "demonstrates
  how the pedal's various algorithms and reverb manipulate these sounds" across synth/microphone/violin sources.
- **No numeric knob positions are stated** — the engines/modes are shown audibly, knob values are not recited. So
  nothing control-by-control is liftable. (Consistent with the no-shareable-preset reality on-disk.)
- **One genuinely useful community recipe — top comment (@lilreotardo, 5.2K likes), a "do it free" replication of
  the Microcosm-cinematic sound elsewhere, NOT a Microcosm patch:** "Record the sound, put it into [an]
  arpeggiator with **2–3 octaves range, short gate, 1/16 speed**, finish by adding some cool reverb/delay like
  **Valhalla Supermassive** = same result." Worth noting because it maps the Microcosm "cinematic pad" sound to a
  recipe the rig can also build in-the-box (sampler/arp + Supermassive, which the Valhalla research already
  flags as the free infinite-drone reverb).

Source character of the demo (for matching to engines): violin/voice/synth → granular pad + heavy reverb; the
"cinematic pad" he gets is the **Mosaic/Haze-into-onboard-reverb-fully-wet** territory the dossier already
documents — but no A/B/D or clock positions given here.
