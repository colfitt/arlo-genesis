# OffsetGuitars NPD review — shoegaze owner's go-to engines + a reverse-delay technique

First-hand shoegaze-player review. No numeric knob recipe, but two on-aesthetic techniques and an engine-preference
map worth recording:

- **Go-to engines (verbatim):** "I find myself gravitating towards the **haze, tunnel, strum, pattern, and warp**
  effects. You can get weird subtle sounds from them as well as bizarre sci-fi sounds." (Granular/smear engines —
  exactly the drone/shoegaze side of the pedal; the glitch engines he finds "harder to incorporate.")
- **The usable reverse-delay technique (NEW, concrete-ish):** because most engines are **envelope-triggered**, you
  can **press the selector knob in to Reverse** and get reverse delays that "behave how a normal delay would" —
  your pluck *triggers exactly when the reverse starts*, instead of the pedal cycling reverses on a fixed grid
  (the sloppy behavior of typical reverse-delay pedals). Practical recipe: **any envelope-triggered engine +
  Reverse on**, then play *into* the trigger → clean reverse swells that follow your phrasing. On-aesthetic for
  banjo-as-lead reverse swells.
- **Secondary mod knobs praised:** "I LOVE the secondary knob functions that control overall **modulation depth and
  rate**. It just makes everything sound so good. From subtle to fast and weird." (= Shift→Shape = pitch-mod rate,
  Shift→Repeats = pitch-mod depth on this pedal.) Supports adding a touch of pitch-mod depth to any drone patch.
- **Reverb caveat (matches dossier):** the 100%-wet stereo reverb is **applied only to the wet/effected signal**,
  not the global mix, so even with all knobs at zero "you'd still have whatever effect is going on in the reverb
  section mixed with your bare guitar." Plan level-matching accordingly.
- **Level-jump gripe (matches dossier):** "as you turn up the mix knob your dry guitar tone gets turned down… I
  have to change the amp master a lot" — reinforces using **Effect Volume (Shift+MIX / CC 16)** to tame the jump.

No A/B/C/D + clock-position settings given.
