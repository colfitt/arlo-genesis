# Sean Shibe — Hologram Microcosm on *Lost & Found* (verified quote)

Sean Shibe is a Scottish classical/electric guitarist (Pentatone). On his 2022 album
*Lost & Found* he plays electric guitar through hardware effects so the processing is
reproducible live. He names the Microcosm directly:

> "I had something sent over called a Hologram Microcosm — a kind of reverb pedal — and there
> are several full wet sounds that can be made, for example the **gong sounds that open
> *Buddha***."

> "the hardware that I pursued, as opposed to post-effects, means that all of this is possible
> live as well."

## Correction to circulating claim
A lot of secondhand coverage (and the working dossier) attributes Shibe's Microcosm use to his
**Hildegard von Bingen** arrangement ("psychedelic swirl of celestial light"). In *this*
interview Shibe specifically ties the Microcosm to the **full-wet gong sounds that open
"Buddha,"** and does **not** mention granular/looping technique or the Hildegard track in
connection with the pedal. Treat the Hildegard-specific attribution as **unverified** and the
"Buddha" gong use as the documented one.

## Relevance to the rig
Shibe's use is the cleanest documented "Microcosm as 100%-wet ambient/reverb texture
generator" precedent from a serious player — Space/Mix cranked, the pedal generating a
self-standing wash (gong-like swells) rather than a subtle effect. On-aesthetic for the
"SUSTAINED WALLS" mandate; confirms the dossier's point that at full wet the Microcosm becomes
its own sound source.
