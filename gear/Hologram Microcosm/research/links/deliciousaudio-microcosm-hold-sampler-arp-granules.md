# 3 creative features: Hold Sampler, Arp, Granules

## Hold Sampler — captures a SEGMENT, not a single note
- The Hold footswitch captures **"a small segment of the wet signal from whatever you were just playing"** — a phrase/cluster, not one note. Result depends on the active engine:
  - **Mosaic** → loops the captured group of notes.
  - **Blocks** → "typically produces a pad or drone sound."
- **Hidden behavior (the key tip):** you can **manipulate ANY Microcosm parameter while material is held to "dramatically change the sound of the held sample."** Freeze first, then sweep Filter / twist Activity / change Space — the held buffer keeps feeding the effects section. This is the rig's drone-evolution workflow in one sentence.

## Arp (Glitch bank)
- A sequencer-style feature; **complexity expands by raising the Activity knob** (more steps / busier arpeggio). Good for chaining banjo/baritone onsets into runs.

## Granules bank
- "Chops your signal up into tiny pieces and then recombines them" into textural reverbs/ambient soundscapes. Sub-effects: **Haze, Tunnel, Strum.**

Distilled value: the Hold-then-twist technique and the Blocks=pad/drone, Mosaic=note-cluster-loop behaviors are concrete, repeatable recipes.
