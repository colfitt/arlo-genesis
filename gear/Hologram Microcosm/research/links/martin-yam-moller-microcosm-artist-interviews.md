# Ambient/post-rock players on the Microcosm — Martin Yam Møller interview series

Martin Yam Møller is a Norwegian ambient/experimental guitarist who runs an interview series
with ambient/drone/post-rock players about their gear. The Microcosm tag collects several
working musicians who use it on-aesthetic. The most instructive concrete points:

## Toto Ronzulli (Truemantic) — the "holy trinity" ambient processing chain
> "the trio **Particle, Microcosm and Space**. I'm using this combo heavily for my next
> record" — for processing **guitars AND vocals** into atmospheric textures.
Red Panda Particle (granular delay) → Hologram Microcosm (granular) → Eventide Space (reverb)
is repeatedly cited by ambient players as a near-complete texture rig. Maps onto this repo's
Board 2 idea (granular event-generator into a smear/reverb stage). Note the parallel:
**Microcosm → reverb stage**, exactly the Microcosm → QI Etherealizer → Dark Star division of
labor.

## TJ Dumser (Six Missing) — re-processing the looper through live effects (key technique)
TJ Dumser layers by "capturing it into the looper and then sending that loop back through the
live effects **pre-effect**" — i.e. record a phrase into the Microcosm's looper, set looper
routing to **Pre-FX**, then twist the live engines so the *recorded loop* is re-granulated in
real time. This is a documented, on-pedal way to build evolving beds without a second pedal:
print a loop, then mangle the loop live. Directly usable for banjo-bed building (record a
banjo roll, re-process it through Tunnel/Haze live).

## Aqeel Phillips — honest limitation
Calls the Microcosm's results "absolute gold," but: "I often wish I had access to the sounds
**'between' the settings** that it offers." Honest caveat — the engine/variation system is
stepped (44 discrete variations), not a continuous morph, so you land on the designed sounds
rather than interpolating between them. (Expression pedal on Activity/Shape/Filter is the way
to get continuous motion *within* a variation.)

## Honest framing
These are working ambient/drone artists, not famous names — but they are exactly the
on-aesthetic user base the brief asks for, and the techniques (granular→reverb chain;
loop→Pre-FX re-granulation; expression-pedal motion to dodge the stepped settings) are
concrete and rig-relevant. Treat interview quotes as the artists' own words (self-reported,
not independently verified).
