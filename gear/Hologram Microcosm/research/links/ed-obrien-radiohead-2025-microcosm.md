# Ed O'Brien (Radiohead) — Microcosm on the 2025 touring board

The most rig-relevant famous user, and directly relevant to this repo's Radiohead song work.

## Placement (concrete)
- Ed O'Brien's 2025 Radiohead board is **three smaller boards**: left = overdrives, **center =
  "weird ambient effects" (the "Pitch and Glitch" board)**, right = delays.
- The **Hologram Microcosm sits on the center "Pitch and Glitch" board, top row**, with the
  **Diamond Tremolo TRM-1 to its left and the Hologram Chroma Console to its right.** Both
  Hologram pedals are on the same ambient board (note: this repo runs the same pairing —
  Microcosm on Board 2, Chroma Console on Board 3).
- Built by **TheGigRig** (routed through a GigRig G3S). The center board is described as
  "newer effects he fell in love with while working on solo material" (his *Earth* / EOB
  output) — i.e. textural/ambient processing, not lead tones.
- No direct quote from Ed about Microcosm-specific settings in this breakdown; placement and
  role (ambient/texture) are well documented, the knob recipes are not.

## Why it matters for this rig
- Validates the Microcosm as a center-of-the-board *ambient texture engine* in a serious
  touring rig, sitting alongside the Chroma Console exactly as this repo splits them
  (granular core vs. character/print stage).
- O'Brien's role in Radiohead is the textural/atmospheric guitar layer — the same use case as
  this rig's "sustained walls / generative beds." If chasing specific Radiohead-adjacent
  textures for the Songs/Radiohead work, the Microcosm + Chroma pairing is the documented
  hardware behind the 2025 live ambient sounds.

## Honest sourcing
Placement is well-corroborated (King of Gear + MusicRadar + TheGigRig). Specific Microcosm
*settings* O'Brien uses are not published — treat any "Ed O'Brien preset" claim as
speculation.
