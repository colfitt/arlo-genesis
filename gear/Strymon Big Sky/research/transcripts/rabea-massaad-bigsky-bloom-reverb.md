Hi, it's Rabea — we're about halfway through the Strymon Big Sky demonstration series. This pedal keeps blowing me away. (If you want to see how to edit it and create your own sounds, watch the overview video linked in the description.)

Now we're on the **Bloom reverb**. It's similar to Swell in one sense: if you set the Swell machine to swell the *reverb* (rather than the dry signal), you play a note and the reverb swells in around it. The Bloom idea is that you hit the note and then *everything* swells around it.

**Parameter mapping for Bloom:** Param 1 = Low End. Param 2 = Length (the length of the bloom). Inside the VALUE/parameter menu you have Low End, Length, and **Feedback** — Feedback lets you lengthen or shorten the bloom. Then the usual Boost and the rest.

What you're hearing is this lovely all-encompassing reverb that engulfs the notes you play, but because I'm just striking the notes you still hear the attack and *then* the reverb comes in afterward. I've got it on quite a high Decay and Bloom setting — I lean toward the more ambient, epic-sounding approach. The nice thing is that even running a fuzz (I added a germanium fuzz here for an oddball, weird sound) it doesn't get too cluttered — you keep the attack and then the reverb just swells over everything. Great reverb; you'll need a couple of hours with this pedal to really explore it.
