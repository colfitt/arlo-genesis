Hi, it's Rabea. By now you've hopefully seen the short introduction video. Quick recap: I'm a big Strymon fan — I use the TimeLine for delays, the Mobius for modulation, and I've just acquired the Big Sky, the latest of the multi-effect range. The next series of videos is a part-by-part review of each reverb machine in the Big Sky. The pedal features an analog dry circuit and an insanely powerful SHARC processor, built with top components throughout. Strymon researched 50 years of reverb technology and narrowed it down to 12 machines. This overview just covers the controls so I don't have to keep re-explaining editing in every machine video.

**Footswitches and banks.** Three stomp switches — the layout matches TimeLine and Mobius, except the third switch here is a third reverb in the bank rather than Tap Tempo. So you get three reverbs per bank (A, B, C). To move up a bank, hit B+C; to move down, hit A+B. With 100 banks that's essentially 300 presets. You can also control all this over MIDI with a controller or Gig Rig, but I'm running it straight in/out of an effects loop and editing on the pedal itself.

**The VALUE knob (left).** Turning it scrolls through the banks (shown by the green LED). Click it once and it shows the value of a specific parameter — by default DECAY — so you can dial decay and see the value. Hold this knob and you get the SAVE option; hit it again and the preset is saved. Very simple.

**The PARAMETER menu (push VALUE).** Push down and you enter the parameters of the current preset. Scroll to a parameter (e.g. Low End), click again to get the minus/plus adjustment, set it, click to exit, scroll to the next. On Room you get Low End, Size (Studio / Club), Diffusion, then Boost (±3 dB, currently at Unity — useful when you want certain patches louder than others). Next is **Persist** — turns on reverb trails so the tail spills over when the effect is bypassed; I'm leaving it on, I like it. Next is **Hold** — a new Big Sky feature: hold any footswitch and depending on setting you get **Freeze** or **Infinite**. Infinite gives you infinite sustain — hold it as a drone and play over the top. Freeze freezes/holds the sustain but lets you add new notes to it to build a sustained chord — really cool. Then Name, then Expression pedal on/off, then EP Set (start at heel or toe).

**GLOBAL menu (hold VALUE).** Bypass — true bypass or buffered. MIDI programming lives here (I'm not running MIDI in this demo). Dry signal: Normal (standard dry) or **Kill** (dry muted so the MIX control acts as an effect level — good feature). **Spillover** — lets the effect spill over into the next bank; on/off. Name display: number or name (shows first six characters, e.g. "MASSIV"). Preset Dump — a MIDI feature: presets can be sent via MIDI to another Big Sky or a MIDI recorder, individually or all at once. The VALUE knob does a lot.

**The main knobs.** Decay, Pre-Delay, Mix, Tone, Param 1, Param 2, Modulation. You can change most of these in the parameter menu, but it's easier to grab the knobs directly. On Room: Param 1 = Low End, Param 2 = Size (Club/Studio). **Tone** is a high-cut shelf — full = all the highs in the tail, pulled back = high shelf cut, darker. **Modulation** is the same kind of subtle warbling tape-style movement as on the TimeLine/Mobius. **Mix** is the amount of reverb; all the way down = dry, around 3:00 = full reverb + full dry, and past that the reverb gets louder than the dry — good for a distant, ambient sound. **Pre-Delay**: think of it like moving the wall the amp faces further away so the sound takes longer to come back; on full it's a good ~2 seconds before you hear it. **Decay** is self-explanatory and goes up to a whopping 20 seconds — any of these reverbs on full decay is like floating through Utopia.
