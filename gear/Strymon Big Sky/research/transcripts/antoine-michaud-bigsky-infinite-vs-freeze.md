Today let's explore the Strymon Big Sky again — answering a student's question: what's the difference between the **Freeze** and **Infinite** functions, and how do I use them?

On the Big Sky you can **hold the footswitch of any patch you're on** and it freezes the reverb in the background. There are two different modes for that hold, and they interact differently with your playing.

**Accessing the setting:** click the VALUE button to enter the inside parameters, scroll past Boost and Persist to **Hold**, click once more, and choose between **Freeze** and **Infinite**. (Works in any machine — I'm in Cloud here, but it doesn't matter.)

**Infinite mode.** Hold the switch to freeze the chord, but **whatever you play on top after holding is ALSO added** to the held pad — everything is affected by the reverb. It creates a nice pad, but it can become **very chaotic** if you play too much on top, because everything keeps getting added.

**Freeze mode.** You can still hold your chord by holding the switch, but **anything you play on top is NOT affected** — it comes through completely dry. So you can jam over the frozen pad cleanly. The compromise: Infinite gets chaotic fast if you jam; Freeze is clean but **lacks any ambience on top**.

**Fix #1 — a reverb BEFORE the Big Sky.** I have a (NUX Atlantic-class) reverb placed *before* the Big Sky. So I stay in Freeze mode, freeze my chord, then turn on the reverb that sits before it — now my playing on top has some ambience, but it doesn't last indefinitely like Infinite would. It's a great compromise; really nice to jam on top like this. *(Rig note: this is exactly the wash-on-wash placement — a feeding reverb/delay ahead of the Big Sky's frozen wall.)*

**Fix #2 — a looper AFTER the Big Sky.** Freeze your chord in any mode, then record a short loop of that frozen pad on a looper placed *after* the Big Sky. Close the loop and release the footswitch — now you hear only the loop, and you can switch to any patch on the Big Sky and jam on top with full reverb. Fade out or stop the loop when done.

So the two alternatives I use for the Freeze function: (1) use Freeze mode plus a reverb placed before it, or (2) freeze in any mode and capture the frozen pad with a looper after it, then play whatever you want on top with your other pedals.
