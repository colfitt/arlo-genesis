Hi, it's Rabea — moving on to the last few Big Sky demos. Today: the **Magneto reverb**.

Strymon's background on it: a new style of music emerged in the late '50s featuring the guitar as the lead voice, enhanced by the reverberated wash of multi-head echo. The Magneto machine sets up a **multi-head echo with all heads on**, while the **Pre-Delay knob adds feedback**. The **Diffusion** parameter adds a new dimension of ambience — smearing the response of the heads and blurring the line between reverb and delay.

**Parameter mapping for Magneto:** Param 1 = Low End. Param 2 = Diffusion. Inside the menu: Diffusion, then **Heads** (the number of tape heads — between three and six; I went with four, which gives me roughly four repeats of whatever note I play), then **Spacing** (Even / Uneven — a characteristic of the tape).

That's the Magneto — a very specific, more rhythmical machine, great for writing parts. Important to remember: the **Even** spread gets you a lot of cool sounds, but the **Uneven** spacing is great for creating textures. Hitting it on and off, and especially **teaming it up with a delay** and tapping it on and off, gives you set repeats of certain notes and a quite edgy sound.
