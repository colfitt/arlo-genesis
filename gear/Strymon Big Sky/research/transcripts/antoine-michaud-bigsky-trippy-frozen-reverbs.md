Antoine Michaud · Trippy Frozen Reverbs (tutorial with the Strymon BigSky) · 2019-04-06

(Auto-captions, cleaned to prose. The single best parameter-level drone-wall workflow found — Nonlinear machine pushed to extremes, frozen, looped. Maps almost 1:1 to the rig's "sustained walls / recorded-wrong" aesthetic.)

Today I'll show how I came up with the main sound of my Ambient Sketch (#75). Usually I write a riff or chord progression first and pick pedals at the end — but this time I wanted the opposite: start from **one single pedal sound** and see how far I could push it. I chose a **Nonlinear** reverb on the Big Sky (a machine I don't use often) and the **Freeze** function.

**Freeze reminder:** hold the footswitch of any patch and it freezes the reverb of that patch underneath — so I can play a chord and freeze it infinitely.

**The patch (bank 15B), Ramp shape.** Push VALUE to enter the hidden parameters; the **Shape** I chose is **Ramp** (you have many shapes available — I usually pick Reverse, but wanted something different here). **In Nonlinear, the DECAY knob becomes a Time knob and the PRE-DELAY knob becomes a Feedback knob.** I pushed it as far as possible: **maxed-out Time, Feedback, and Mix** — a 100% wet reverb with very long decays and time. Very extreme. Then I use the **volume knob on the guitar to swell on top of it**.

The sound goes on for ages, and **turning the DECAY/Time knob** while it sustains creates morphing, psychedelic motion.

**Building the song.** I played a single chord, froze it, and added **a little modulation** so it's not too static — then I **looped the frozen pad** (looper after the Big Sky) for a few seconds so I could keep it underneath while I play more on top.

**The remaining hidden parameters of this Nonlinear patch:** Low End ~ middle, Diffusion ~ middle. Then **Late Decay** and **Late Level** — there's effectively *another reverb underneath* this patch that comes in late, so I dialed a small amount of both to layer the reverbs together. There's also **Mod Speed** — I keep it very slow; with a fast speed it gets seasick/wobbly, but a slow speed plus more depth on the **Mod** knob is what I want. I also set a **very dark Tone** so it doesn't get too metallic.

**Performance recap:** freeze the first chord, run it with the loop, then swell chords in with the volume knob — they last very, very long. Play with the Decay/Time knob for psychedelic effects while the frozen loop holds underneath so it never sounds empty.

**Warning:** with a reverb this long-decaying, chord changes get dissonant very fast — choose chords that are far enough apart in your harmonization so the held tails don't clash.
