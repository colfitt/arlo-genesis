Strymon's long-running blog series where staff/artists share a single preset with a
downloadable **.syx** file (load via Nixie 2 / Strymon Librarian). The posts show a knob
dial-image, so exact knob NUMBERS aren't in the text — but each post states the machine,
key menu params, and the design intent. The .syx download is the authoritative value
source; the text below is what's explicitly quoted. These are the best official-but-
non-factory presets, and all are on-aesthetic for ambient/drone.

## "Star Cloud" — CLOUD — Matt Piper
URL: https://www.strymon.net/this-weeks-preset-bigsky-star-cloud/
- Machine: **Cloud**. **Mod = 2:00** — described as "the maximum modulation depth but at
  the slowest speed." Intent: an "ethereal wall of sound."
- Piper's technique: play it at **zero dry** (Mix full wet) so the guitar becomes an
  "organic ambient pad," not a recognizable guitar. Les Paul bridge pickup, half-note
  picking, expression pedal on **Tone** for hands-free filtering sweeps.

## "Bloombient" — BLOOM — Matt Piper
URL: https://www.strymon.net/this-weeks-preset-bigsky-bloombient/
- Machine: **Bloom**. **Length ≈ 20 s** (per author comment). Feedback assigned to
  **Param 2** (noted as having pitch-shifting potential at extremes). Mod modulates the
  bloom-generating delay lines.
- Intent: "a wash of noise like a wave in an electronic ocean." Decay turned down for a
  fade-out; sweep Tone/cutoff via expression pedal. Input-dependent — adjust live while
  playing.

## "Late Bloomer" — BLOOM — Ethan Tufts (Jul 31 2014)
URL: https://www.strymon.net/weeks-preset-bigsky-late-bloomer/
- Machine: **Bloom**. **Pre-Delay high** (reverb arrives well after the note),
  **Length long**, **Mix ≈ 3:00** ("relatively high"). Raise Feedback + Decay for a
  bigger, more ambient result.
- Intent: a slowly building envelope that "blooms" — "a lush trail of sound" that "hangs
  out just behind the notes" without overpowering the dry. Good for filling sparse parts.

## "Ever After" — SHIMMER — Ethan Tufts  (best fully-specified one)
URL: https://www.strymon.net/this-weeks-preset-bigsky-ever-after/
- Machine: **Shimmer**. **Shift 1 = +2 OCT**, **Shift 2 = −1 OCT**, **Decay = 13.7 s**,
  **Amount ≈ just over 50%**, **Mode = INPUT** (Input mode stops the shifts
  self-regenerating, keeping it controlled).
- Intent: "resplendent, orchestral ambience." Demoed on an Oberheim Matrix-6 synth pad —
  directly relevant to running the VG-800's modeled pads into the Big Sky.

## "Falling Angel" — SHIMMER — Matt Piper
URL: https://www.strymon.net/this-weeks-preset-bigsky-falling-angel/
- Machine: **Shimmer**. **Mode = REGEN** (shimmer on the regeneration tank only),
  **Tone cranked** (back off if shrill), **Mod ≈ halfway**. Shift voices reassigned so
  the pitch **falls** with each regeneration cycle.
- Intent: "a long, super-spacey reverb that slowly falls in pitch." It can swell
  excessively — assign an expression pedal to **Mix** for dynamic wet control.

## Takeaways for the rig
- For the **banjo-as-lead / VG-800 pad** wall: "Ever After" (Shimmer, +2OCT/−1OCT, Input,
  13.7 s) and "Star Cloud" (Cloud, slow-deep Mod, zero dry) are the closest factory-grade
  starting points.
- The recurring official technique = **expression pedal on Tone or Mix** for hands-free
  swells/filtering, and **playing fully wet** to turn the source into an ambient pad.
