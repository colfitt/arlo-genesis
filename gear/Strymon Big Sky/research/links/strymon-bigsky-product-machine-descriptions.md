Strymon's own one-line characterizations of the ambient machines (useful as the canonical
"what each machine is for" reference; quoted). The widely-cited "play Cloud at ZERO DRY to
turn guitar into an organic ambient pad" line is from Strymon's **machine descriptions /
deep-dive docs** (also echoed in the "Star Cloud" This Week's Preset, where Matt Piper says
he runs it with "zero dry signal") — NOT from any single artist. Attribute the technique
to Strymon's own docs + the Star Cloud post, not to Lowercase Noises (a separate ambient
artist who is listed in Strymon's ambient feature but is not the source of that quote).

## Machine descriptions (quoted)
- **Cloud** — "a gorgeously big, ambient reverb that draws from techniques developed in the
  late '70s." (Longest decay machine on the pedal; the drone-wall anchor.)
- **Bloom** — "big ambient reverbs that sit nicely with the dry signal even at high Mix
  levels." (Slow-rising swells that grow after the note; usable at high Mix without burying
  the dry.)
- **Shimmer** — "resplendent, unearthly ambience" with "a range of shimmer effects from
  laid-back and subtle to full-blown majestic splendor." (Pitched cathedral walls.)
- **Magneto** — "multi-head echo with all heads on," with feedback + diffusion control.
  (Tape-echo reverb; the rig's degraded/tape-adjacent machine.)

## The "zero dry pad" technique (Strymon docs + Star Cloud post)
Run **Cloud** (or any long machine) at **100% wet (zero dry)** so the source signal stops
reading as "guitar/banjo" and becomes an "organic ambient pad" — described as sounding
"more organic than a synth pad." Combine with **FREEZE** hold to sustain the pad while you
play new notes over the top. This is the core move for the rig's "sustained walls" +
"banjo-as-lead over a frozen bed" idea.
