strymon.net · Strymon pedalboard feature: Andy Othling / Lowercase Noises · ambient guitarist (Albuquerque, NM)
Also plays in Archabald and Future of Forestry. Listed across Strymon's ambient features.

The other heavily-documented, verified Big Sky ambient artist (alongside Hammock). Othling
is a reference name for textural/experimental ambient guitar and runs the Big Sky as his
core reverb. On-aesthetic for the rig's "non-guitar / recorded-wrong" leanings.

## Big Sky usage (verified)
- **Big Sky = final reverb, LAST in chain**, placed after the Strymon TimeLine delay.
  (Corroborates the dossier's recommended end-of-time-section placement, and the Strymon
  ambient feature's "wouldn't sound like this without the Big Sky as the end of the chain.")
- **MIDI-controlled** via a **Disaster Area DMC-3 XL** controller (preset recall), plus
  **two this1smyne mini expression knobs** mapped to specific Big Sky parameters for
  hands-on real-time control. → exactly the EXP/MIDI-driven failover/control approach the
  dossier describes; the Big Sky's deep MIDI + EP SET is built for this.

## Technique / philosophy (quotes)
- **Textural, anti-"pretty" ambient**: "I'm also a fan of the more textural side of ambient
  guitar, which to me is about **very non-guitar and even non-musical sounds to fill the
  space**." → matches the rig's degraded/"recorded-wrong" aesthetic; push the Big Sky into
  Nonlinear/Magneto/heavily-modulated Cloud rather than polite hall.
- **Stacking time-based effects for the wall**: "one of my favorite things is to **stack
  two or even three delays to get a big soupy sound**" — same principle applied to reverb
  layering. → feed multiple delays (Big Time / Gen Loss in this rig) INTO the Big Sky's
  Cloud/Bloom for the soupy wall.
- **Volume-pedal swells** for "synthy/swelly sounds" (analog to Big Sky Swell machine + EXP
  on Mix), **compressor** (Walrus Mayflower) for sustain/fatness feeding long reverb tails,
  and **lo-fi/octave texture pedals** (Lo-fi Junky, Micro POG, Superego) ahead of the verb.
- **Exploration ethic**: "I always take the time to really explore all the possible sounds
  from the pedal" — the Big Sky rewards deep per-machine exploration over preset-grabbing.

## Takeaways for the rig
- Confirms the two pillars: **Big Sky last in chain**, **delays stacked into it**, and
  **EXP/MIDI control of parameters live**. The rig already has the EXP + MIDI hooks.
- The "non-guitar / non-musical" framing is the permission slip to run banjo/VG-800 into
  Cloud/Nonlinear at 100% wet (frozen) until it stops sounding like the source instrument.
