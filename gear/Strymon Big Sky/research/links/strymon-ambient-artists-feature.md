strymon.net · Strymon "Artists Feature: Ambient Music" roundup · undated

Strymon's own curated list of ambient artists/tracks made with their gear. Useful for
confirming Big Sky appears in real ambient/post-rock work, though most entries are
song-credit only (light on settings). The Big-Sky-specific mentions:

- **Hammock** — "Cold Front" (album *Departure Songs*). Confirms Big Sky in Hammock's
  catalog. (Deeper Hammock technique → see hammock-bigsky-ambient-technique.md.)
- **Andy / Lowercase Noises** (reverbnerds.com) — "Famine and the Death of a Mother"
  (*This Is For Our Sins*). Known for ambient-technique video tips; isolation/loss mood.
- **Forum (Tyler)** — uses TimeLine + Big Sky; notably "**tracked a glockenspiel running
  through the Big Sky**" — i.e. Big Sky as an ambient processor for non-guitar sources
  (relevant to running the **VG-800 / banjo** through it, not just guitar).
- **David Peretz** — TimeLine + Big Sky (track literally titled "Timeline, blue sky").
- **Todd Madson** — runs **Big Sky at the END of the chain**; "wouldn't sound like this
  without the Big Sky." (Confirms the dossier's recommended end-of-time-section placement.)
- Also listed (no Big-Sky-specific detail): Mikhail Medvedev, …A Többi Néma Csend,
  On Mountains, Ivan Ujević, **Chords of Orion**, Jon Carolino.

Takeaway: corroborates the dossier's honest framing — the Big Sky's fame is **category-
defining ubiquity in ambient**, not one signature player. Multiple ambient artists run it
**last in chain** and on **non-guitar sources** (glockenspiel, synth pads), which matches
this rig's banjo/VG-800 + end-of-board use.
