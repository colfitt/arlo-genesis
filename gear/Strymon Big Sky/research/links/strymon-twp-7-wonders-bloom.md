## "7 Wonders" — BLOOM (official, fully stated)
Author quote: "I set the Bloom length to just over 50%, and the Feedback to 50%,
providing a medium 'bloomed' sound. Pre-Delay, Tone, and Mod are all set to 50%."
- **Machine:** Bloom
- **Decay:** 8.13 s
- **Mix:** 2:00
- **Bloom Length (Param):** just over 50% (≈ 1:00)
- **Feedback (menu / Param 2):** 50% (noon) → "medium bloomed" size
- **Pre-Delay:** 50% (noon)
- **Tone:** 50% (noon)
- **Mod:** 50% (noon)
- (Hold mode not stated in text; default per .syx.)

## Why it fits the rig
- A clean, balanced "everything at noon" Bloom benchmark — the swell rises and grows
  AFTER the note, sitting just behind the dry. Good neutral starting point before you
  push it toward drone (raise Feedback + Decay + Mix per the "Late Bloomer"/"Bloombient"
  recipes already on disk).
- 8-second decay + 2:00 mix = audible wall without fully burying the banjo attack;
  drop Mix and it becomes the "fill the sparse parts" ambience Tufts describes.
- Differs from the on-disk "Late Bloomer" (high Pre-Delay, long Length, Mix ~3:00) and
  "Bloombient" (Length ~20s, Feedback on Param 2) — "7 Wonders" is the middle, all-noon
  reference Bloom. New entry, not previously captured with values.
