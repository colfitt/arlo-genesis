# Community techniques — Magneto-as-drone, Cloud diffusion, Shimmer self-build

## Magneto (multi-head tape) — drone + stereo tricks
- **Crank the feedback (Pre-Delay knob) toward max → the tape-style regeneration becomes a sustained DRONE source.** This is the closest the Big Sky gets to self-oscillating runaway, and it's the drone-friendly move.
- **Diffusion blurs the line between delay and reverb** — low Diffusion = distinct repeats, high Diffusion = smeared into ambience. Use high Diffusion to turn the multi-head echo into a wash.
- **Even spacing = rhythmic** (heads land on subdivisions — described as 1/16, 1/8, dotted-8th, quarter); **Uneven = more reverb-like/non-metric.**
- **6 heads + uneven + high feedback** = thick stereo cloud; even spacing + stereo gives a big ping-pong field.
- Magneto's Mod knob = **wow & flutter** generator (pitch instability of old tape). (Note: "Tape Age / Crinkle" granular controls are Magneto *standalone pedal* / MX features — the original Big Sky Magneto machine has Low End, Diffusion, Heads 3/4/6, Spacing, and Mod-as-wow/flutter; don't expect the standalone's full tape-degradation menu.)

## Cloud — grain control via Diffusion
- **Diffusion = smoothness control. LOW Diffusion = grainier/grainy texture; HIGH = smooth pad.** (Big Sky Cloud exposes **Low End + Diffusion**; the later "Cloudburst" pedal hides both under the hood — so the original Big Sky gives you MORE hands-on Cloud control than Cloudburst.)
- Cloud's decay reaches up to ~50 s; **zero dry + Cloud = an organic synth-pad substitute** that "can sound more organic than a synth pad."
- Low Diffusion flatters transient sources (banjo plucks) — the grain rides the attack instead of smearing it.

## Shimmer — self-building swell
- **REGEN mode** (shimmer on the regen tank, not the input) makes the reverb **swell/build after the attack** rather than rise instantly — great for held drone chords.
- Voices tunable octave-down → 2-octaves-up; small detunes (±10 cents) give a lush detuned-reverb instead of an obvious octave.

## Community sentiment (gearspace / KVR / modwiggler / Andertons)
- Big Sky = the gold standard for **clean, pristine, classic reverbs + the best-in-class tweakable Shimmer** (multiple octaves, programmable intervals, more sub-controls than rivals).
- For **extreme/experimental** ambient some prefer Eventide (weirder) or Source Audio Ventris (two verbs in series at once); the original single-engine Big Sky "doesn't get as weird as the Eventide." This is consistent with the rig's call to let the H90 handle dual-algorithm reverb and keep the Big Sky as the pristine single-engine failover.
- A recurring forum line: people run **"a cheap synth/keyboard → Big Sky on Shimmer + Cloud"** and call it "godly" — i.e. the VG-800/synth-into-Cloud idea is a well-trodden, well-loved path.
