## Evert Zeevalkink — SHIMMER falling-pitch (artist, concrete)
Author quote: "The sound is created by setting both shifts to −10% with the amount set
to max. It's in Regen mode with a mix at 2:00."
- **Machine:** Shimmer
- **Shift 1:** −10% (i.e. ~−10 cents / slightly detuned DOWN)
- **Shift 2:** −10% (both voices pitched down a touch — no up-octave)
- **Amount:** Max
- **Mode:** REGEN (shimmer on the regeneration tank only → builds AFTER the attack)
- **Mix:** 2:00
- (Decay/Tone/Mod/Pre-Delay not stated.)

## Note on overlap
- This is the SAME recipe surfaced in the on-disk Falling Angel writeup's "artist
  example" line — now sourced to its named artist (Evert Zeevalkink) on the official
  Strymon artist-feature page, with the exact "−10% / max / Regen / Mix 2:00" values.
- Distinct from the factory "Detune Verb" (26C: Shift ±10 CEN, INPUT mode, INFINITE) —
  Zeevalkink's is BOTH shifts down + REGEN, so the detune compounds and slowly sinks.

## Why it fits the rig
- Both-voices-slightly-flat + Regen = a continuously DESCENDING, queasy detuned wall —
  exactly the "recorded-wrong"/degraded character the aesthetic wants, without the bright
  octave-up "worship shimmer" sound. Sits great under a sustained baritone or banjo drone.
- Max Amount + Regen self-builds, so it swells after you stop — pair with Freeze to lock
  the sinking wall, then play a banjo lead over it.
