Hammock — Marc Byrd & Andrew Thompson · ambient / cinematic post-rock duo (Nashville)
2nd GW source: https://www.guitarworld.com/features/hammock-marc-byrd-andrew-thompson

The most concretely-documented Big Sky ambient artist for this rig. Hammock are the
reference point for "sustained walls" guitar — and they're a confirmed Big Sky user.

## Big Sky usage (verified)
- Strymon chain across their boards: **Big Sky + TimeLine + Deco + Flint**.
- On the album *Mysterium* their go-to was **the Big Sky CHORALE machine** (the vocal/choir
  pad) — directly relevant to the rig's "eerie vocal-pad drone" possibilities.
- Big Sky appears as far back as *Departure Songs* (Strymon's own ambient feature lists
  Hammock's "Cold Front" from *Departure Songs*).
- (Note: their *current* top reverb has rotated to Neunaber Immerse MkII / NightSky / Walrus
  R1 — but Big Sky/TimeLine are long-standing in the catalog. The technique below is the
  transferable part, not the specific box.)

## Technique — this is the gold (quotes)
- **Manual knob manipulation as performance.** On *Mysterium*: "textural decaying type
  sounds, usually created by **one of us manually turning the knobs while the other played
  guitar or unplugged cables**." → the wall isn't a static preset; it's two hands, one
  playing, one riding Decay/Mix/Tone live. Maps perfectly to the rig's expression-pedal-on-
  Tone/Mix idea.
- **Stereo doubling spread** (Thompson): "have a **stereo guitar doing all the wash**, and
  then a **mono off to the right that's pretty tripped out**, and a **mono off to the left
  that's basically dry**." → use the Big Sky's true-stereo out for the wash layer; keep a
  dry-ish layer panned opposite. The dossier already notes Big Sky's stereo + analog dry
  path — this is the exact use case.
- **"Create more warble… make your head vibrate"** (Byrd) — lean into Mod and detune
  (cf. the −10/+10 cent Shimmer trick). Warble over cleanliness.
- **Massive ≠ many tracks** (Thompson): "some songs with very few tracks that sound
  massive… ones with a massive amount of tracks that sound very small. That's all by
  design." → density is a choice; one frozen Cloud wall can be the whole bed.
- **Orchestral layering**: avg **12+ guitar parts** per song, each "the only thing on that
  track… approach it when you mix as if you were conducting." EBow parts, swell guitars,
  counter-melodies stacked. Producer Peter Katis called it a "guitar symphony."
- **Philosophy** (Byrd): "A lot of people think they can do ambient music if they just have
  the right pedalboard, and they can't. It's an art form in itself." / "We come at it from
  a more intuitive place than a technical place." / "There are no rules… trust your ear."

- Guitars: **Fender Jazzmaster**, **baritone guitars**, Danelectro, 12-string BilT (Gold
