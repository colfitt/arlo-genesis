## "Lo 5th" — SHIMMER used as a pitch-DOWN harmonizer (artist, concept + key params)
Author quote: "By using the Shimmer algorithm and only tuning down, the reverb is playing
the lower harmony, leaving the original signal clean."
- **Machine:** Shimmer — but used with NO up-shift; **both shift voices tuned DOWN** (the
  article frames it as a lower 5th, hence "Lo 5th").
- **Lo End:** called out as "critical to avoid muddiness" — i.e. roll Lo End down so the
  down-shifted reverb voices don't turn to mud under the dry.
- Decay / Mix / Mode / Pre-Delay / Mod: IMAGE ONLY (not transcribed in text).

## Paired TimeLine "Wetish" (for context, on the other pedal)
- Two INDEPENDENT modulation delays panned hard L and R (NOT ping-pong), BPM mode,
  dedicated speed + depth. "Having these panned Left and Right is the key."

## Why it fits the rig (this is the standout technique)
- "Shimmer that only shifts DOWN" = the reverb generates a LOWER harmony (a 5th / octave
  below) while your dry note stays clean on top. This is the inverse of normal bright
  shimmer and is exactly on-aesthetic: it thickens a banjo or baritone lead with a dark
  sub-harmony wall instead of a glassy octave-up sheen.
- Directly maps to "banjo-as-lead": play the banjo clean, the Big Sky builds a lower-5th
  drone underneath — instant doom-leaning bed without a separate octave pedal.
- Lo End rolled down keeps the down-shifted bed from swamping the baritone.

## Caveat
- Concept + Shimmer-down + "Lo End critical" are text-verified; the exact intervals
  (−P5 vs −OCT), Decay, Mix, Mode are in the preset image only. Grab the .syx for the
  precise dial, or build it from the factory "Low Blow" (22C: −OCT/+OCT) and "5th Wheel"
  (30C: −P5/+P5) starting points by removing the up-voice.
