# SL MkIII firmware 1.4 — new arp + sequencer features (verbatim)

The update that made the sequencer/arp genuinely generative. Novation's own descriptions:

- **Per-part arpeggiator** — "Each of the eight parts now have individual, independent arpeggiators, for building complex polyrhythmic patterns." (Pre-1.4 the arp could only do one Part at a time.)
- **Arpeggiator probability** — "The new arpeggiator probability feature brings an element of randomness to each arpeggiator, turning SL MkIII into a generative and constantly evolving instrument."
- **Step probability** — "The probability of a programmed step can be altered, bringing creative irregularity and extending the musical palette." (This is the per-step Chance in Steps → Options → Chance.)
- **Sequencer transpose (with pads)** — "The SL MkIII's pads can now be used to make sequencer transposition a powerful performance tool." (In Scale Settings; ±11 semitones live from pads.)
- **Pattern shift** — "Now it's possible to quickly offset the start and end points of a pattern together to explore variations and manage sequences." (0–15.)
- **Pattern octave transpose** — "Allows patterns to be transposed across the octave range, to find the perfect pitch and experiment with ideas." (Steps view: Shift + Up/Down arrow next to pads = octave up/down for all notes in pattern; blocked if it would push notes out of MIDI range.)
- **Toggle swing per-part** — "With swing on or off per part, it's possible to build contrasting layers of groove for more complex rhythmic results."
- **Pitch bend to CV** — "The pitch-bend wheel can now be mapped to the CV output" (±1 to ±12 semitones).

Cross-check: firmware **1.2** (earlier) added the **6 micro-steps per step**, **gate to 32 steps**, **aftertouch→any CC**, and **NRPN** support. The V2 User Guide reflects the post-1.4 feature set.
