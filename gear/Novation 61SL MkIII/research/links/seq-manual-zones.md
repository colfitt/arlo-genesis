# Zones — splitting the keybed into up to 8 zones routed to Parts

Zones split the keybed into independent areas (one note up to the whole keyboard). Each Zone routes to a destination Part — this is how you play bass low / pad high from one keybed, or layer sounds.

## Enable + navigate
- **Enable Zones:** hold **Shift + Zones.**
- **Enter Zones view:** press **Zones.** Up/down arrows (left of screens) move between **page 1/2** of Zone settings → **14 adjustable parameters per Zone.**
- **Select a Zone:** soft buttons under names (Zone 1, Zone 2 …). **Up to 8 Zones.**
- **Activate/deactivate:** left-most rotary above 'Active' → On / Off. Off greys out all that Zone's other params.

## Key-LED behaviour & priority
- With Part LEDs on + Zones enabled, key LEDs above each key show the colour of the Zone's destination Part.
- **Overlap priority:** when zones overlap, the **lowest-numbered Zone wins** (Zone 1 over Zone 2). Overlapping is allowed and useful for layering (e.g. piano + synth).
- In Zones view only the selected Zone's LEDs light. With Scale on, out-of-scale keys in range stay unlit.

## Per-Zone parameters (page 1)
- **Dest. Part** (rotary 2): 'Selected' (default) or **Parts 1–8.** Changing it recolours the keys.
- **Low Key / High Key** (rotaries 3 & 4): set the Zone key range, inclusive. Default range = full keybed (C1–C6 on 61-key; C1–C5 on 49-key).
  - **Set range from keyboard:** select the Zone, press its soft button a **second time** → key LEDs pulse → press a low key then a high key.
- **Octave Offset / Octave Follow** (rotaries 5 & 6) + **Transpose Offset / Transpose Follow** (rotaries 7 & 8): Offsets apply a permanent octave/transpose shift to the Zone; Follow On/Off decides whether the global Octave/Transpose buttons affect the Zone.

## Per-Zone parameters (page 2)
- **Expr Pedal / Sustain / Footswitch** (knobs 1–3): On/Off whether each pedal affects this Zone (all On default).
- **Pitch / Modulation wheels** (knobs 4 & 5): On/Off per Zone (both On default).
- **Channel Pressure / aftertouch** (knob 6): On/Off per Zone (On default). Aftertouch is also customisable in Components.
