Buy/don't-buy debate thread from launch window. The most useful distillation of the "MkII → MkIII regression" sentiment — context for why the box has a mixed reputation.

## Automap removal (the central gripe)
- **chaosWyrM (2/8/19):** losing Automap removes a "main part of the device" — for him it makes upgrading from a MkII pointless. This is the canonical "Automap-gone" complaint (confirms dossier §11 / SOS "plug-in control significantly reduced").
- **chk071 / DrGonzo:** counter-view — Automap was buggy and had poor DAW compatibility, so dropping it was acceptable. (The community is genuinely split; it's not universally mourned.)

## Control-count regression vs MkII
- **Echoes in the Attic (1/30/19):** "8 knobs" fewer than the MkII.
- **david.beholder (1/30/19):** encoders only, no potentiometers; also flags the keybed is **not Fatar** and questions aftertouch quality.
- **marcora (2/9/19):** "only two pages of controllers… unlimited pages should be available" — the 16-encoder ceiling again.

## Positive
- **krankyone (2/8/19):** the five screens are "even more readable than Push 2"; keybed "much improved over the Impulse."

## GOTCHA — aftertouch inconsistency
- **krankyone (2/8/19):** aftertouch response differs between white and black keys. Worth knowing if you map aftertouch→CC for filter sweeps (dossier's recommended use) — expect some per-key unevenness on the channel-pressure strip.

## Sequencer
- **DrGonzo:** the 16-step ceiling and needing to "chain patterns if you want to do something more than 16 steps" is repeatedly called a workflow obstacle.

Community-temperature read: launch sentiment was "feels like a step back from the MkII as a pure controller, redeemed only by the new sequencer/CV." That matches the dossier's "underbaked at 1.0, repaired by firmware" framing.
