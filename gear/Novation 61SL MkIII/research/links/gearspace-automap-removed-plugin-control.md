The Automap-removal complaint, consolidated.

## What changed
- The SL **MkII** relied on Novation's **Automap** software to auto-map knobs/faders to plug-in parameters (deep per-plugin control). The **MkIII dropped Automap entirely.**
- SOS verdict (the canonical quote): plug-in control is **"significantly reduced without Automap."** You get InControl + HUI/Mackie for DAW transport + mixer, but NOT the old deep per-plug-in auto-mapping.
- **Novation Components** replaced Automap for *making templates* — but templates are manual CC maps you build yourself, not auto-discovery of a plug-in's parameters.

## Community split (from KVR / Gearspace)
- **Anti:** for some MkII upgraders this gutted the main reason to own an SL as a pure controller ("makes upgrading pointless").
- **Pro:** plenty were glad to see Automap go — it was buggy, launched an extra background app with the DAW, and had spotty DAW compatibility.

## Rig guidance
- Don't expect the SL to auto-map a Kontakt/Arturia/Soundtoys plug-in's knobs. For NI/NKS-style auto-browsing you'd want a Komplete Kontrol (dossier §14 already notes this). For the SL, deep plug-in control = either (a) build a Components template by hand against fixed plug-in CCs, or (b) use **DrivenByMoss** for screen-aware device-param control in Bitwig/Reaper (and richer transport in Logic/Ableton than stock). For mapping the VG-800 / Chase Bliss / MIDI pedals (fixed CC maps), Components is exactly the right tool — that's where the SL shines vs the lost Automap workflow.
