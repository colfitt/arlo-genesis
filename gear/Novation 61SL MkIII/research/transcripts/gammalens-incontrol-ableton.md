GammaLensMedia · Novation SL MKIII: Everything You Need To Know About IN-Control Mode (61SL MK3 / 49SL MK3) · 2023-12-12

The clearest dedicated walkthrough of **InControl mode with Ableton Live 11** (compared side-by-side with a Push 2 on screen).

## Entering InControl
- Press **InControl** — the top section becomes DAW control ("a dumbed-down Push 2 / beefed-up Launchpad").
- **Right-hand buttons**: mute, solo, and (toggle down) **monitor + arm**. Hold to arm multiple tracks.
- **Encoders** = track levels in InControl. (He notes the encoders feel smooth but a bit flimsy/under-resistanced vs the hardware knobs.)

## Navigating tracks beyond 8
- You're not limited to 8 tracks. The two-row pad grid is a **purple window** over Ableton's session grid; the four-bar grid shows what the larger Push sees.
- The **light guide matches Ableton's clip colors** (intro blue, verse red, hook bridge blue/green) and the bars hover to show position. Use the arrows to scroll deeper down the track chain — watch the grid to know where you are. The light guide mirrors what's in Ableton (clip activated = its color shown), and Push follows the same selection.

## Transport + shift functions
- Transport buttons (play/stop/record) work with Ableton.
- **Shift** reveals: **undo, redo, click (metronome), capture**. (He admits not understanding Capture — it materializes recently-played notes into a clip.)
- **Click** toggles the metronome (lights up in Ableton too); **Shift + Click** turns it off.
- Gripe: **no quantize button** — he wishes Shift+something mapped to quantize like Push has.

## Recording workflow
- **Loop** button toggles the loop region. To record an 8-bar loop: toggle Loop, hit Record → count-in → it loops. Records in arrangement OR session view.
- Undo/redo via Shift toggles.
- Session view: arm with the side button or the corresponding pad (same action); color guide shows the activated clip. Play a row, switch to the next row, toggle back and forth — like stopping/launching on a Push. He demonstrates quantizing/nudging clips after recording and matching a clip to a 5-bar grid.

## Device / instrument control
- Toggle between **instruments/devices** on a track to access device parameters. "No parameters mapped" appears for instruments whose params live inside the plugin; the 8 encoders map to the device's MIDI-controllable params (like the 2/4/6/8 knobs on Push), and values show on the screens AND in Ableton.
- For multi-param plugins use **Bank** (the up button) to page through banks (e.g. "Reverb mix" labeled on screen) — an extra toggle into the VST, then up/down through banks.
- **MIDI-mapping your own instruments**: he MIDI-mapped a synth so its params show on the SL; use **Expand** to see what's mapped, pull volume, etc. Mapping more params gives access to the second pad/row. (Works for any MIDI controller, not just the SL.)
- Exit InControl easily to regain access to other (sequencer/hardware) parameters.
