GammaLensMedia · Novation SL MKIII: Sequencing Basics (61SL MK3 / 49SL MK3) PART 3 · 2024-10-17

Part 3 of GammaLensMedia's modern (2024) SL MkIII tutorial series — the most beginner-friendly hands-on sequencer how-to. Builds a multi-zone drum + synth arrangement step by step. (Note: he uses "Zone" loosely for what Novation calls Parts — patterns live per part/zone.)

## Setup
- Turn **Zones off**, turn the **sequencer on** (Stop/Play/Record lights enable).
- Each zone/part holds up to **8 patterns**; each pattern up to **16 steps**. Color-coded: Zone 1 blue, Zone 2 yellow, Zone 3 green, Zone 4 orange (toggle parts with the arrows).
- His routing: Zone 1 → Modx (drum kit), Zone 2 → Phantom (synth), Zone 3 → Motif (synth), Zone 4 → VST.

## Building a drum pattern (four-on-the-floor)
- In **Steps**: press a step pad, then tap a key to assign that note (kick). Place kicks on steps 1/5/9/13. Pads with notes light brighter than empty pads.
- Add claps and hi-hats on other steps the same way.
- **Duplicate steps**: hit Duplicate, press the source step, then paste onto target steps (he duplicates hats across several steps).
- Any pad's note is set by pressing the pad + assigning; red highlight = currently being played by the board.

## Pattern variations + chaining
- **Patterns view**: pattern 1 holds the built sequence; an empty pattern plays nothing.
- **Duplicate a pattern**: Duplicate → source pattern → destination → it copies the steps.
- Make variations: duplicate to pattern 2 then go to Steps and **remove the kicks**; duplicate to pattern 3 and **remove the snares** → three variations to toggle between, transitions are seamless.

## Tempo + deeper step editing
- **Tempo** button shows BPM; supports **tap tempo** (left at 120 for the demo).
- **Options** (in Steps) exposes per-step: **velocity, gate, chance** (steps 1–8, arrow down for 9–16).
- **Options → Pattern**: speed (sync rate), direction, start position, end position. He halves a pattern's steps via start/end + sync rate to keep the same tempo over fewer steps.

## Recording into patterns
- **Live record**: **Shift + Record** toggles quantize off → press again for quantize on. Record straight from the keys/pads into the sequencer.
- **Recording across multiple chained patterns**: in Patterns view, press multiple patterns to chain (e.g. 4) — it shows "4 patterns" and plays 1→2→3→4→1. With record on you can record onto multiple patterns in one pass (longer evolving sequences instead of a 1-step loop repeating).
- Demonstrates **non-quantized** recording (Shift+Record, quantize off) for looser feel, and re-enabling quantize on another zone.

## Arranging
- **Mute / Solo** per part for live arranging.
- **Shift + Patterns** = vertical (expand) view, grouping all patterns by zone/part; default view is horizontal.
