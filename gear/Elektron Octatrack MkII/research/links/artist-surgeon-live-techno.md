# Surgeon (Anthony Child) — OT as the rhythmic foundation, Faderfox for no-menu performance

VERIFIED. Surgeon's tabletop techno live rig is well documented (CDM, Resident Advisor "The Art of Production" video, a 45-min SchneidersLaden talk).

## How the OT functions in the rig
- **Foundation/backbone**: the Octatrack holds **"six drum sounds, some backing tracks."** It's the structural core the whole improvised set is built on.
- **Faderfox PC4 for hands-on control**: he drives OT parameters from a **Faderfox PC4 pot controller** so he can perform **"without menu diving"** — real-time hands-on control as the OT's **filter and delay** are played against the rest.
- **LEPLOOP for the chaos layer**: a LEPLOOP semi-modular (FM oscillators, noise, sequenced sample-and-hold + LFO) feeds/interacts with the OT's filter and delay — the OT is the controlled foundation, the LEPLOOP is the "spice on top."
- **Glue**: an OTO Machines BOUM stereo compressor on the master keeps it from jumping out of the mix.

## Technique takeaway for this rig
- **External MIDI/CC controller (Faderfox-style) to escape OT menu-diving live** is the single most transferable Surgeon lesson — map OT filter cutoff/FX params to physical knobs (the 61SL MkIII can play this role here) so the OT performs like an instrument, not a menu.
- **OT as the steady rhythmic/backing-track foundation while a more chaotic source plays over it** mirrors this rig's "OT bed + live guitar/pedalboard on top" model.

Honest flag: CDM does NOT confirm whether he leans on scenes/crossfader/resampling specifically — his documented method is economical p-lock + external-knob control, not heavy scene morphing.
