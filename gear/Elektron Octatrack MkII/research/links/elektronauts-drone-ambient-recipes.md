# Drone / ambient recipes — on-aesthetic for this rig (degraded sustained walls)

Direct community techniques for building sustained drone beds — exactly the OT's role downstream of the pedalboard.

## Pitch + time
- **Pitch a sample DOWN ~2 octaves + heavy reverb** = instant ambient wall. The most-cited single move. (Flex PTCH down; Dark Reverb on FX2.)
- **Slow the sample down** (low RATE, timestretch ON to hold pitch or OFF for the tape-slow grind) — favored for stretching a fuzz/baritone capture into a bed.
- **REVERSE** the source for evolving non-obvious textures.

## The evolving-drone engine (the key trick)
- **Minimal trigs + an LFO on sample START (STRT).** With just one or two trigs and a slow/random LFO scrubbing the start point, the same sample re-enters at different points each pass → a constantly-evolving drone that never repeats. This is THE generative ambient patch on the OT.
- Layer a slow LFO on filter cutoff and/or pitch for drift on top.

## Layering / resampling
- **Resample the drone onto another track + slice it** to keep building density — stack captured layers (capture wall → bed → resample bed + live → new bed).
- Run it OUT to external processors (modular delays/filters/resonators/granular) and **return to the OT** for another mangle pass — fits the rig's "out to pedalboard / back to desk" loop.

## FX choices
- **Heavy reverb across tracks** (Dark Reverb, Gatebox Plate, Spring) + the master Compressor + reverb as the final glue stage on track 8.
- **Lo-Fi Collection** for the degraded/recorded-wrong character on the way out.

## Mapping to THIS rig
- Sustained fuzz walls (Longsword/Hizumitas) and baritone drones are ideal — dense, gapless, timestretch cleanly. Capture → STRT-LFO evolving bed → Dark Reverb → run under the live guitar. The banjo (bright, fast-decay) is the opposite material — slice on transients + conditional trigs for glitch-lead instead of drone.
