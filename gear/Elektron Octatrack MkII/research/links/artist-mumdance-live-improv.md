# Mumdance (Jack Adams) — 100%-live track-building from a one-shot loaded OT + TR-909

VERIFIED. Mumdance documented his OT use directly with Elektron (Gearjunkies spotlight, his own Facebook post) and in MusicRadar/Attack interviews. Credible high-profile user.

## The rig (stripped to essentials)
- 2 decks + 1 mixer + a **Roland TR-909** + an **Octatrack**. That's it — "you can wreck a club with the bare essentials."

## HOW he uses the OT (verified)
- **OT loaded with a ton of ONE-SHOT sounds**: samples from favourite records, his synths, and **field recordings** from his travels.
- **Builds tracks live, on the fly, reacting to the crowd** — plays tunes, mixes into the 909, drum-jams, then layers the OT one-shots over the 909 to construct whole tracks in front of the audience. Nothing pre-planned; every show different.
- Philosophy: the OT is "the perfect tool for live improvisation," kept "as raw as possible."

## Technique takeaway for this rig
- **The one-shot library approach**: pre-load the OT with a curated bank of one-shots (here: degraded fuzz stabs, banjo plucks, baritone hits, field recordings, VG-800 textures) and *build live* over a steady rhythm source (Digitakt 2 as the "909" role) rather than pre-sequencing. Improvisation-first, sample-locks and trigs played by hand.
- Pairs the "disciplined drum box + chaotic OT layered on top" model (the Digitakt+OT pairing in the dossier) with a live-performance, react-to-the-room ethos.
