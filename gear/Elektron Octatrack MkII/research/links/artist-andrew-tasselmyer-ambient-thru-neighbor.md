# ARTIST PATCH — Andrew Tasselmyer's ambient OT processing chain (named, recent, drone-aligned)

A documented ambient/drone artist using the OT exactly as the rig intends: a live stereo FX processor fed external (here iPad) audio, crossfader-morphed. Concrete setup:

## Machine layout
- **Track 1 = THRU** — takes incoming external audio (iPad; in the rig = pedalboard stereo print / VG-800).
- **Track 2 = NEIGHBOR** — stacked after Track 1 for additional serial processing.
- **Tracks 3 & 4 = FLEX** — record buffers sampling from **Input AB or Track 2's output** (resample-and-layer).

## FX chain (Tracks 1–2)
- FX slots across the Thru+Neighbor pair: **DJ EQ, Filter, and LoFi**, plus **Dark Reverb and Delay**.
- Tone goal: **cut the lows, emphasize midrange resonance** (DJ EQ low cut + filter).
- **Delay FEEDBACK cranked near/past 110** — explicit note: "it never self-oscillates, so you can quickly ramp up the feedback knob to near maximum" → safe to push to ~110+ for dense, near-infinite tails without runaway.

## Crossfader scene mapping (the performance)
The crossfader simultaneously: **raises Reverb MIX**, **raises track VOLUME to max**, and **increases Delay FEEDBACK** → one fader throw morphs a dry-ish line into a swelling, feedback-laden ambient wash.

## Routing
- A **hardware send, pre-fader**, sits after the source's own FX → lets him process through the OT while keeping a parallel unprocessed signal for layering.

## Rig fit
This is a real, recent, named-ambient-artist version of the rig's core move. The exact transferable values: **Delay FB ~110+ (won't self-oscillate)**, **DJ EQ low-cut + Filter for mid focus**, **crossfader bound to Reverb MIX + Volume + Delay FB at once**. Swap iPad → the front-board strings / VG-800 banjo. The "Dark Reverb + cut lows + mid resonance" tone matches shoegaze/drone better than a bright hall.
