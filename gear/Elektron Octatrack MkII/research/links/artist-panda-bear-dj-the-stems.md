# Panda Bear — TWO Octatracks as "Ableton in a box": DJing the stems of the record

VERIFIED with strong evidence (Reverb interview feature, MusicRadar studio photos, documented 2018 Sydney Opera House live set). One of the best-documented two-OT live users.

## The setup (verified, concrete)
- **Two Octatracks (MkI)** holding the **exact same content on both cards**, used to **"DJ the stems of the record"** — mix from one song into the next, OT-to-OT, like a DJ mixing decks.
- Per OT: **stems of the studio recording spread across 7 tracks**, with **track 8 as a master channel**. He explicitly calls it **"Ableton in a box."**
- Two identical OTs = he can have the next song cued/loaded on OT B while OT A plays the current one, then crossfade between them — true gapless live stem-DJing without a laptop.

## Technique takeaway for this rig
- **"Stems across 7 tracks + track 8 as master"** is a clean, copyable Project template: bounce a song to ~7 stems, load one per track, dedicate track 8 to a master comp/reverb (matches the recurring DIY master-track template). Perform mutes/levels/FX per stem live.
- The **dual-OT "DJ the stems" model** is the high-control cousin of Stimming's dual-OT rig — if the rig ever pairs the OT with another OT or the Digitakt 2, the "identical content on both, mix between" idea enables laptop-free song-to-song transitions.
