# Alessandro Cortini (Nine Inch Nails, SONOIO) — OT as sampler+synth, scenes for pitch/chords

VERIFIED. Cortini has repeatedly and on-record praised the Octatrack across Reverb, Gearjunkies/Elektron, and Elektronauts interviews. One of the most credible high-profile users.

## What he says (quoted)
- "The Elektron stuff is always on and always getting some action, particularly the Octatrack which is probably one of the most revolutionary instruments of the last 20 years. I've never been a sampling guy, and the Octatrack changed the way I approach sampling. Even the simplest sound bite can be transformed into a song."
- "I use it both as a sampler and a synth."

## HOW he uses it (technique, verified)
- **Crossfader as the main performance gesture**: "uses the crossfader a lot to affect the sequences, loops and sounds" live — the goal was to be able to heavily manipulate everything he plays live without it ever feeling ill-placed.
- **Sample-source minimalism → scenes for harmony**: when he first got the OT he erased all factory samples and loaded **32 short sounds recorded from his Buchla 200e**, then used **OT scenes to change chords and pitch**, building **seven tracks from nothing but those 32 samples**. This is the "OT as a synth" trick: scene-locked PTCH on the same source material gives you chord changes without more samples.
- Sits in his live rig alongside Prophet 12, tape machines, modular.

## Rig relevance
The "load a tiny set of recorded source sounds, then use scenes/PTCH to play harmony" approach maps directly onto this rig: capture 32 short fuzz/banjo/baritone/VG-800 fragments, then perform chord/pitch changes via scenes and the crossfader — turning the OT into a playable instrument off the pedalboard's raw material.
