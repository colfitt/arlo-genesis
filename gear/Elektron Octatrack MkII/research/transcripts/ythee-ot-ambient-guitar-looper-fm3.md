The single most rig-relevant signal-chain example found: a guitarist using the OT as an effects box + looper for psychedelic ambient guitar, with a complete Thru → Neighbor → Pickup track layout. Nearly identical to this rig's "pedalboard print into the OT" role.

## Signal chain
- Guitar (Behringer/baritone) → **Fractal FM3** (amp/cab sim — the OT has no amp sim — plus reverb/delay) → into OT **inputs C/D**.
- OT main outs → back into the **FM3** for end-of-chain compression + a stereo enhancer (width) → into the computer (Reaper).

## Track-by-track architecture (the template)
- **Track 1 = THRU machine** (the guitar input): a bit of **compression** + a **Lo-Fi** effect for distortion/grit.
- **Tracks 2, 3, 4 = NEIGHBOR machines** chained off the Thru, each adding more FX to the guitar:
  - **Track 2:** chorus + flanger → stereo width and ambience.
  - **Track 3:** phaser + freeze delay.
  - **Track 4:** another freeze delay.
- **Track 5:** a simple beat to play in time to (so no metronome needed while recording).
- **Tracks 6, 7 = PICKUP machines** for recording / looping / overdubbing the guitar.
- **Track 8 = MASTER track.**
- A few **scenes** set up for live manipulation of the loops via the crossfader.

## Rig relevance
This is essentially the blueprint for the OT's job in this studio, swapping the FM3 for the three pedalboards: feed the stereo print into a **Thru** track (compression + Lo-Fi), stack **Neighbor** tracks for layered chorus/flanger/phaser/freeze-delay FX racks (the Neighbor chain is how you get more than 2 FX slots on one source), use **Pickup** machines to loop/overdub the banjo or baritone live, keep a quiet click track on a spare track, and ride **scenes** on the crossfader for live drone manipulation. Confirms Neighbor machines can't sit on track 1 or 5 (Thru is on 1; beat on 5) — the layout respects that.
