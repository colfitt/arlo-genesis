# Iridium per-amp settings cheat-sheet (distilled from demos + Pete Celi tour)

Clock-face, viewed from above. Sources cited per row; transcripts in ../transcripts/.

## The MIDDLE knob — per-amp behavior (the single most amp-specific control)
| Amp | MIDDLE does | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| ROUND (Deluxe) | Noon = stock Deluxe mids; back = scoop; MAX = tweed-style strong mids AND acts as a SECONDARY GAIN (pushes the power stage) | Pete Celi |
| CHIME (AC30TB) | HIGH-CUT control. Lower = more top-end presence; raise = tames highs. Interacts with TREBLE | Pete Celi, James Ransom |
| PUNCH (Plexi) | Real Plexi mids (tone-stack). Dropping tone knobs flattens EQ + raises power-stage level | Pete Celi |

## DRIVE behavior per amp
- All amps NON-master-volume: DRIVE = the amp's volume; LEVEL = pedal output only.
- CHIME: top of DRIVE range engages a frequency-shaped analog front-end BOOST (like a treble booster in front) — adds gain + tightens lows.
- PUNCH: ~2 o'clock = a real Super Lead's max; PAST 2 o'clock = Strymon hot-rodded high gain.
- ROUND: has notably MORE HEADROOM than Chime at the same DRIVE position (stays cleaner) — push to ~1 o'clock to wake it up. (James Ransom)
- Analog front end can add up to ~20 dB before DSP.

## Copyable presets from demos
### Chime jangle (James Ransom, bridge pickup, everything wide open)
- AMP chime · DRIVE just below 11 · BASS noon · MIDDLE (high-cut) 9–10 · TREBLE 2
- Goal: full round lows that don't flub; top-of-drive used as built-in boost on strums.

### Round clean/headroom (James Ransom)
- AMP round · DRIVE 1 o'clock · BASS noon · MIDDLE 10–10:30 · TREBLE up
- Rounder, mid-rangey to start; pull TREBLE to noon to thicken. Stays clean under loud picking.

### Default cab picks (Hayley Briasco / Guitar Center, conventional balanced voicing)
- Round → cab C (2x10 Vibrolux) · Chime → cab A (2x12 AC30 Alnico) · Punch → cab A (4x12)

### Punch wall — doom/shoegaze direct (this rig; Pete Celi voicing logic)
- AMP punch · CAB c (8x12, bright) · DRIVE past 2 o'clock (hot-rodded) · BASS low · MIDDLE low · TREBLE noon · ROOM high, LARGE
- SG humbuckers → sustained chord blooms into a wide ambient wall, stereo out.

## Room
- Hybrid: stereo 250 ms room IR + algorithmic decay. Sizes SMALL/MED/LARGE.
- Select size: HOLD the ON footswitch, audition with ROOM knob, release to set. (Pete Celi)

## Drive-stacking (pedals in front)
- As gain rises you get fizzy treble → dial drives darker / more mid-rangey. Pair two drives max; three loses amp character. (James Ransom)

## Shootout verdict vs Walrus ACS1 (EytschPi42)
- Iridium edge: better IRs, rounder/fuller, best amp-in-a-room + stereo field, better high-gain.
- ACS1 edge: onboard preset memory + per-preset boost (live convenience) — irrelevant for a studio DI role.
