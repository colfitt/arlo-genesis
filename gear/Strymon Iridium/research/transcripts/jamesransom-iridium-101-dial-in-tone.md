James Ransom · Strymon Iridium 101 (part 1) - How I Dial in Tone · 2020-06-22

The best source for COPYABLE per-amp knob settings — a worship/ambient player walking through his actual Chime and Round presets, plus drive-stacking and the headroom caveat. ~18 min.

## CHIME preset (his main / "Bethel-ish" sound — bridge pickup, everything wide open)
- AMP: chime · DRIVE: a little below 11 o'clock · BASS: straight up (noon)
- MIDDLE (= high-cut on AC30 mode): ~9–10 o'clock · TREBLE: 2 o'clock
- He treats the top of DRIVE like the AC30's built-in front-end boost ("bridge booster") to fill out chords on a strum. Dial for a clean tone where the lows are full and round "but it doesn't start to flub out."

## ROUND preset (the other ~20% of his playing — "Vox vs Fender EQ space")
- Round sits in a different EQ space — rounder, and notably MID-RANGEY to start.
- Key finding: at the SAME DRIVE position as Chime, Round has a GOOD BIT MORE HEADROOM and stays cleaner. To wake it up he pushes DRIVE to ~1 o'clock.
- His Round preset: BASS straight up · DRIVE 1 o'clock · MIDDLE ~10–10:30 · TREBLE up.
- EQ advice: with mids at noon, pulling TREBLE back to noon thickens it; the Fender voice "opens up" as you push drive. He stores his go-to as the FAVORITE.

## Drive-stacking workflow (pedals in front of Iridium)
- Two overdrives: a lower-gain drive (gain just below 9 o'clock) for edge-of-breakup, and a second more mid-rangey/aggressive drive after it.
- As you add gain you get "fizzy treble" — so dial drives darker/midrangier to compensate.
- Warning: stacking too many drives loses the amp's character; he keeps it to two and pairs them rather than running three.
- Higher gain on a darker EQ = more mid-rangey, aggressive; brighter = fizzy.

## Honest caveat — headroom vs a Kemper/profiler
- He flags a "weird headroom thing": the Iridium never quite has the headroom of a Kemper at the extremes; there are 2–3 "headroom hacks" (e.g. bring the gain back, manage levels) to work around it. This matters if you push very loud cleans.
- No onboard preset memory (single FAVORITE switch only) — you "get away with it" by storing one favorite; people share great preset settings online.

## Relevance to this rig
- Direct copyable starting points for the SG/Jazzmaster-into-Apollo role:
  - Jazzmaster jangle → his Chime preset above (DRIVE <11, BASS noon, MID/high-cut 9–10, TREBLE 2).
  - Cleaner/headroom-y → Round (BASS noon, DRIVE 1 o'clock, MID 10–10:30, TREBLE up) — best if you want it to stay clean under loud picking.
- The "fizzy treble as gain rises → dial darker" note applies directly to drone/doom drives stacked in front.
