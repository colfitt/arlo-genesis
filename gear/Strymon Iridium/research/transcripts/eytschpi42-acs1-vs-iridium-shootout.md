EytschPi42 · Pedal Battle: Walrus Audio ACS1 vs. Strymon Iridium · 2021-01-23

The key A/B against Iridium's closest competitor (the Walrus ACS1 — also 3 amps, 3 cabs, stereo, DI). ~27 min. Useful for the honest "what is Iridium better/worse at" verdict. Both pedals matched controls (drive/level/bass/middle/treble), same 3 amp types: Round=Deluxe/"Fullerton", Chime=AC30, Punch=Plexi.

## Feature differences noted
- ACS1 has onboard PRESET memory (3 presets) PLUS a per-preset BOOST — effectively ~6 sounds available on the footswitches. Iridium has a SINGLE FAVORITE switch (no preset bank from the face).
- Iridium I/O: true stereo IN and stereo OUT on two actual jacks ("the left/right stereo thing is way cool"); MIDI/USB. ACS1 stereo I/O requires a TRS insert (Y) cable splitting to two mono cables, which the reviewer found annoying.
- "The cap [cabinet] frequency response" / room — Iridium voiced more as amp-in-a-room.

## Sonic verdict (reviewer's take)
- LOW-GAIN / cleans: very similar between the two; no clear winner.
- CABS / IRs: Iridium "comes with better IRs," sounds "rounder and fuller," and is "better suited" especially for amp-in-a-room sounds — a really nice stereo field. This is consistently called Iridium's edge.
- HIGHER GAIN (Chime/Punch driven): Iridium is "probably the better choice" — better, fuller; ACS1 had a tiny bit of fizziness with an overdrive in front; the big mids of the ACS1 presets are its own flavor.
- ACS1's win: the preset memory + per-preset boost (more footswitch flexibility live).
- No outright single winner — voicing-taste differences, but Iridium pulls ahead on IR quality, fullness, and the room/stereo field.

## Relevance to this rig
- Confirms the dossier's claim: Iridium's IR pedigree + hybrid stereo room is its real advantage, exactly what makes it the right pick for the wide, direct doom/shoegaze wall and the clean DI into the Apollo. The ACS1's only meaningful edge (preset memory) is irrelevant here since the Iridium is studio/DI-focused, not a live preset-switcher.
