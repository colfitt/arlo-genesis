The authoritative source on what Iridium actually is and how every control behaves, narrated by Strymon's sound designer. ~20 min. This is the single best reference for the per-amp tone-stack behavior and the IR vendor/cab list.

## Engine / architecture
- Matrix Modeling: Strymon studied the schematics, translated circuits to math, and modeled the dynamic nonlinear tube stages + power section — including multi-stage power sag and speaker distortion at the highest drive levels.
- The amps are TRUE STEREO (two instances running) and the IRs are stereo IRs, so a stereo signal path from the pedalboard runs all the way through Iridium and out.
- All three amps are NON-MASTER-VOLUME: the DRIVE knob is the amp's volume knob; LEVEL just sets the pedal's output. Turning DRIVE up = more power-stage push/breakup.
- Signal path on every amp starts with a Class-A discrete analog JFET gain stage capable of adding 20 dB of gain in the analog front end (before any DSP).

## ROUND — Fender Deluxe Reverb (Normal channel, mid-60s)
- Chosen specifically for its pairing with pedals and its warm-but-bright top end. One of the most recorded amps in history.
- The real Deluxe has only Bass + Treble and a FIXED resistor setting the mids. Strymon's MIDDLE knob: at NOON = the traditional Deluxe mid response; turn back for a deeper scoop; turn UP to MAX = the mid response of the earlier TWEED amps (strong mids, breaks up more).
- Pushing MIDDLE up almost acts as a SECONDARY GAIN — it removes the scoop AND increases the level sent to the power stage, so it dirties up while staying touch-responsive to pick attack and guitar volume.

## CHIME — Vox AC30 Top Boost (Brilliant channel)
- Bright but not harsh/shrill; harmonically rich top end. The British Invasion sound.
- The AC30 has a Bass + Treble tone stack PLUS a HIGH CUT control. Strymon put that HIGH CUT under the MIDDLE knob. Lower the MIDDLE/high-cut to keep more top-end presence; raise it to tame the highly-present frequencies. TREBLE and the high-cut interact — you tailor exactly which part of the high spectrum you hear.
- BASS note: the real Vox bass control is almost a TILT — at minimum it's full mid-range, at maximum it's full bass with no mids. So on Chime the BASS knob is a very important overall tone shaper, not just low-end.
- FRONT-END BOOST at the top of DRIVE: the top of the Chime DRIVE range engages the analog front end as a frequency-shaped boost — effectively a treble-booster in front of the amp (a classic way the Vox was used). It adds further gain AND tightens up the low end.

## PUNCH — Marshall Super Lead 1959 Plexi
- Punchy, aggressive; solid-state rectifier; strong low end; typically paired with a 4x12. Harmonically rich overdrive — NOT modern high-gain.
- ~2 o'clock on DRIVE = the actual maximum drive available on a real Super Lead. Past 2 o'clock, Strymon uses the analog front end to HOT-ROD the amp — extending gain further into high-gain territory than the original amp could reach.
- Dimed/flat tone-stack trick: turning the tone-stack knobs down flattens the EQ AND increases the level sent to the power stage, giving a bit more grind. At low DRIVE it also has a very nice clean sound with a wide spectrum.

## ROOM
- A hybrid: stereo 250 ms room IR (room-mic capture) blended with an ALGORITHMIC decay for an extended, realistic spatial tail. Acts like a stereo pair of room mics.
- Three sizes: SMALL / MEDIUM / LARGE. Audition/select by press-and-HOLD the ON footswitch (then choose with the ROOM knob).

## The 9 factory IRs — stereo, 96 kHz, full 500 ms (highest-fidelity available)
ROUND:
- a — OwnHammer, 1x12 open-back Fender Deluxe Reverb w/ Fender speaker (the classic pairing).
- b — cabIR.eu, Fender Blues Jr (smaller cab, boxier, extended highs — good for tweed tones).
- c — cabIR.eu, 2x10 open-back Fender Vibrolux (deep bass, pronounced mid scoop).

CHIME:
- a — OwnHammer, 2x12 open-back AC30 fawn w/ Celestion Alnico Blues (classic AC30 combo).
- b — Celestion, 1x12 CLOSED-back w/ Celestion Alnico Blue (focuses the low end, tighter response).
- c — Valhallir.at, Mesa/Boogie 4x12 half-closed-back — Celestions on top two, Electro-Voice on bottom two (strong EV low end + bright Celestion presence on top).

PUNCH:
- a — OwnHammer, closed-back 4x12 Marshall w/ Celestion speakers (pairs best with Punch).
- b — Celestion, 2x12 OPEN-back w/ Celestion Vintage 30s (lighter bass than the 4x12, extended highs, great for breaking-up sounds and neck-pickup tones).
- c — cabIR.eu/Valhallir, 8x12 cabinet — the 8-speaker interaction creates a very bright response; a nice complement to the other two Punch cabs.

(Vendor set across the pedal: OwnHammer, Celestion, cabIR.eu, Valhallir.at.)

## Workflow takeaways for this rig
- On a single-amp source, the MIDDLE knob is the most amp-specific control: it's mids+secondary-gain on Round, high-cut on Chime, real Plexi mids on Punch.
- For doom/wall tones with SG humbuckers, Punch + high DRIVE (past 2 o'clock) into cab c (8x12, bright) plus a LARGE room gives the widest, most aggressive direct wall.
- For a clean Jazzmaster DI, Chime b (closed-back, tighter) or Round a, with MIDDLE used as the air/high-cut control.
