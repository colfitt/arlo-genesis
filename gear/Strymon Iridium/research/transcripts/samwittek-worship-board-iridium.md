Real-world direct-to-FOH signal-chain placement and custom-IR workflow from a gigging player who's run the Iridium since launch. ~16 min. Best "where Iridium sits in a real all-direct chain + how you get it to FOH" reference.

## Where the Iridium sits (full chain, summarized)
- Front: compressor first (to round/even the signal), then octave/utility.
- Drives, in order: Benson preamp → JHS pedals (Lamb, Ruby Red, etc.) → a low-gain edge-of-breakup drive and a heavier second-stage drive (paired, not all stacked).
- Volume pedal → wet section: delay → Strymon Big Sky reverb.
- Strymon IRIDIUM sits LATE in the chain (after the wet effects) as the amp/cab — i.e. the "amp + speaker" the whole board plays into.
- From the Iridium → a high-quality DI / iso box (Radial-style "DI plus") as the very last device → FOH. The DI box ensures a clean, properly-leveled, isolated send to the house.

## Iridium settings / IR choices
- AMP: CHIME (his general go-to).
- IRs: NOT the factory cabs — he loads CUSTOM tone-matched IRs via Impulse Manager, specifically a tone-matched AC30 IR and a Pro Sonic IR. (Confirms the custom-IR-loading workflow as a real, gig-tested practice.)

## Workflow takeaways for this rig
- Validates the "Iridium is the amp+cab at the END of the chain, then a DI box to the room" architecture — directly analogous to the SG/Jazzmaster → drives/verbs → Iridium → Apollo path in this studio (the Apollo's line input replaces his external DI box).
- Reinforces that custom 24/96 IRs (loaded via Impulse Manager) are where players get their signature direct tone — the factory 9 are starting points, not the ceiling.
- Note: reverb (Big Sky) is placed BEFORE the Iridium here so the reverb tail is "in the room" with the amp; for a cleaner studio print you can instead keep verb post-Iridium or use the Iridium's own ROOM and add wide reverb after.
