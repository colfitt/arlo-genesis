# Guitar Chalk — 5 named Blackhole presets (knob-by-knob, 1–10 scale)

Five named Blackhole reverb recipes. Values are knob positions on Blackhole's 6 knobs (a 1–10 visual
scale, NOT percent/seconds) — the Blackhole *algorithm* in the H90 has the same six core controls
(Mix, Gravity, Feedback, Size, Lo, Hi) so these translate directly: set the H90 Blackhole knob to the
proportional spot (e.g. "Gravity 9" ≈ near-max forward decay; "Gravity 2" ≈ deep into Inverse/reverse).
On the H90, Gravity is bipolar (Inverse on the left, forward on the right) — low numbers here = the
reverse/inverse side. Starting points; dial by ear.

## 1. Infinite Ambient Pad — ambient/drone, cinematic/post-rock
- Mix **8**, Gravity **9**, Feedback **10**, Size **9**, Lo EQ **5**, Hi EQ **7**
- (Feedback 10 = the near-infinite-tail/freeze territory — the drone pad.)

## 2. Swirling Modulation Wash — dream-pop / shoegaze ("reverb becomes the instrument")
- Mix **7**, Gravity **4**, Feedback **8**, Size **8**, Lo EQ **3**, Hi EQ **8**

## 3. Dark Matter Reverse Bloom — negative-gravity, dark/mysterious sound design
- Mix **6**, Gravity **2**, Feedback **7**, Size **6**, Lo EQ **7**, Hi EQ **3**
- (Gravity 2 = deep Inverse = reverse-swell bloom; on-aesthetic for "recorded-wrong" swells.)

## 4. Cathedral Bloom — massive reflective chamber, sparse arrangements
- Mix **7**, Gravity **6**, Feedback **9**, Size **10**, Lo EQ **6**, Hi EQ **8**

## 5. Low Orbit Resonance — heavy/otherworldly ambient tail, synth-bass focus
- Mix **9**, Gravity **5**, Feedback **9**, Size **7**, Lo EQ **8**, Hi EQ **4**
- (Lo EQ 8 + Hi EQ 4 = dark, sub-heavy wall — good under the baritone.)

## Why it matters for this rig
Four of five are direct drone/shoegaze targets. **Dark Matter Reverse Bloom** (Gravity deep Inverse)
and **Swirling Modulation Wash** are the most on-thesis; **Infinite Ambient Pad** (Feedback 10) is the
freeze-and-solo-over pad. Provenance = community/blog (single-author). These are Blackhole-pedal knob
positions mapped to the H90 Blackhole algorithm.
