# Switch TWO scales/keys in ONE Program with ONE footswitch (HotSwitch snapshot recipe)

Use case: switch from C Ionian to A Harmonic Minor mid-solo without burning both Preset slots on two Diatonic instances. The clean answer is a HotSwitch that snapshots the Key+Scale parameters.

## Two approaches
- **Bank mode (joecozzi's first idea):** make two separate Programs (one per scale) and step between them with Bank-mode footswitches. Costs you a Program each.
- **HotSwitch (the preferred, slot-saving method):** one Program, one Diatonic Preset, the HotSwitch toggles its Key+Scale values.

## HotSwitch recipe — exact steps (joecozzi, Jan 12 2024)
1. Enter the **Parameters** menu (press the Parameters button repeatedly to reach the right page).
2. Identify which slot holds the **Diatonic** algorithm (P, A, or B).
3. Pick the HotSwitch by pressing its LED button: **P → HS1, A → HS2, B → HS3**.
4. Use the **Select** knob to find the **Key and Scale** parameters.
5. **Push the Quick Knobs** to capture/map the current values into the HotSwitch.
6. Change Key+Scale to your ALTERNATE values (e.g. A Harmonic Minor).
7. Exit by pushing the **Perform** knob.
8. In **Perform** mode, assign that HotSwitch to a footswitch.

Now one footswitch toggles between the original scale and the mapped scale. (OP confirmed it worked.)

## Rig relevance (banjo-as-lead)
This is the exact trick for the "harmonized banjo lead" Program: build a Diatonic Preset, then use a HotSwitch to flip Key+Scale for the bridge/solo section — one stomp, no extra Program, no extra Preset slot. Remember Diatonic is MONOPHONIC (single-note leads); for chordal banjo use Polyphony/Prism Shift instead, which don't have a Learn-key scale to flip this way.
