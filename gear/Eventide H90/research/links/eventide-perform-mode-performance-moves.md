# Perform mode — the algorithm-specific performance moves worth mapping

Perform mode gives **two pages of user-programmable footswitch assignments**: tap tempo, active/bypass, **momentary active**, the three HotSwitches, and **algorithm-specific performance parameters**. The algorithm-specific moves are the expressive payload — map them to the P/A/B footswitches or to aux switches.

## Footswitch requirement (important)
To use all latching/momentary/trigger footswitch functions you MUST use either:
- **momentary normally-open aux switches**, OR
- **momentary MIDI CC footswitches sending >=64 on press, <=63 on release.**
(A latching/toggle physical switch will misbehave with momentary functions.)

## The high-value algorithm-specific moves
- **Wormhole → Warp** — "a light speed journey through the Universe" (pitch/space dive). The signature Wormhole performance move.
- **Bouquet Delay → Pitch Jump** — bounce between precise musical intervals with an analog flavor.
- **Prism Shift → Freeze** (and momentary interval changes) — freeze the signal or momentarily change the pitch-shifted intervals.
- **Head Space → Boil / Break** — degrade/destroy the tape echo for "unheard-of sounds."
- **Blackhole / Shimmer → Freeze / Infinite** (Freeze is a Performance Parameter — see the Freeze thread).

## Tap-tempo nuance
- **Preset Tap Tempo:** with Tempo Sync OFF, assign Preset A or B footswitch to "Tap" — tapping adjusts that Preset's time params in ms/Hz (so A and B can run different tempos).

## HotSwitches recap
The three onboard HotSwitches map a RANGE of parameters within a Program — "three additional Programs for the price of one" (but mutually exclusive; see the HotSwitch thread file).

## Rig relevance
For drone/doom/shoegaze the standout mappable moves are **Wormhole Warp** (instant dive into a collapsing-space wall), **Head Space Boil/Break** (tape-destruction that pairs with the rig's tape-stage thesis), and **Prism Shift / Blackhole Freeze** (lock a wall and play over it). Map these to aux switches via a TRS-wired multi-switch so a single player can trigger them hands-free. Use momentary NO aux switches (or >=64/<=63 MIDI CC) for the momentary versions (e.g. momentary Warp).
