Leon Todd · Eventide H90 | HEADSPACE | Deep Dive · 2022-11-20 · 15:10

Deep dive on Head Space — the multi-head tape-delay algorithm based on old tape machines with multiple playback heads. Cleaned to prose. Directly on-aesthetic for this rig's "recorded-wrong" tape thesis.

## Concept
A virtual tape machine with four playback heads. The **Delay Time** control behaves like the physical length of the tape combined with the playback speed — together they set the core delay time. Then each of the four virtual playback heads has three parameters of its own.

## Controls
- **Delay Time** (tape length × playback speed = core delay).
- **Per head (×4 heads = 12 params total):**
  - **Level** for each head.
  - **Subdivision** for each head (the head's rhythmic position relative to the core time).
  - **Pan** for each head.
- **Boil** and **Break** — performance moves that emulate a tape machine either speeding up and spinning out of control (Boil) or powering down / braking (Break). Heard as wow/flutter pitch runaway and the classic tape power-down dive.
- **Playback Speed** — interacts with Delay Time to set the overall tape behavior.

## Settings demonstrated
- A "crusty tape" preset Todd built — degraded, lo-fi multi-head repeats with the Break move used to end phrases (the power-down dive heard at the end of his intro demo).

Demoed in the loop of an Axe-FX III on-screen; identical algorithm on the H90.

Rig note: Head Space's multi-head subdivisions + per-head pan create a stereo, rhythmically-smeared delay bed; the Boil/Break performance moves (assignable to an H90 HotSwitch or Perform footswitch) are tailor-made for the rig's degraded/tape-warped sound — a way to get tape-runaway pitch dives BEFORE the signal even hits the downstream PORTA424/JHS 424 tape stage.
