Deep dive on PrismShift — a polyphonic pitch-shifting arpeggiator (one of the H90's new algorithms). Cleaned to prose.

## What it is
You play a chord into it; PrismShift picks apart the individual notes and turns them into an arpeggiated, pitch-shifted sequence. Described as the most "far out and unique" algorithm in the box. Polyphonic (chord-capable), like Polyphony.

## Controls
- **Mix**.
- **Arpeggio Type**.
- **Step Length** — sets the speed of the arpeggiation (lower = quicker).
- **Pitch Shift Type**.
- **Arpeggio Order** — e.g. Low-Mid-High, Mid-Low-High, etc. (selectable by knob or drop-down).
- **Gain (Low / Mid / High)** — level controls for the low, mid, and high parts of the arpeggio (his preference example: a little less High, a bit more Mid, leave Low).
- **Feedback** — per part, plus a setting for WHERE the feedback is taken from (first or second sequence).
- **Spread** — stereo pan range; turn to 1 (full) to make the arpeggiations pan left↔right across the stereo field.
- **Slew Time**.

## Settings demonstrated
- **Sequential Chimes (factory)** — mix pulled down a little, arpeggiator type changed; "instantly inspiring; follow your ears." Then Spread turned to full for left↔right panning arpeggios.
- Step Length lowered for faster arpeggiation; Arpeggio Order changed (Low-Mid-High → Mid-Low-High); Gain bands adjusted; feedback added to each part with the feedback tap set to the first or second sequence.

## Takeaway
A chord-into-arpeggio generator with strong factory presets — best explored by ear. Demoed on H9 hardware editor on-screen; identical algorithm on the H90.

Rig note: feed a banjo or baritone chord and PrismShift arpeggiates it into a cascading, stereo-panned sequence — an alternative to Polyphony for turning banjo chords into orchestral, evolving texture. Spread at full + slow Step Length over a drone = a self-generating ambient bed.
