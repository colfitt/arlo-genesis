# Ariel Posen — favorite Eventide H9 sounds (signature presets)

Lineage note: this is the **H9**, not the H90 — but every algorithm Posen names (Hall, Room,
ModFilter, Rotary/Leslie) is in the H90's library, so the presets transfer directly. Posen also keeps
an H9 on his current board (per Premier Guitar rig rundowns); his use is *clean, dynamic, studio-style*
ambience rather than drone/doom, but it's a clear documented example of an artist building and naming
his own H9/H90 presets around a record.

## His philosophy
- He names every preset after what it does so he can recall it fast live: *"I kind of named these
  just so I remember what to call them."* All are factory algorithms he *"tweaked to sound the way I
  wanted them."* On stage he *"only really switch[es] between a few."*
- Deliberately keeps **backup/spare versions of his other pedals' core sounds** on the H9 — *"a spare
  reverb, a spare slap delay, a spare long delay … in case my pedal goes down at least I have a backup
  of that sound ready to go."* (Same failover logic as benching the Big Sky as an H90 backup in this rig.)

## His named presets (all used on the album *Headway* and live)
1. **"Hall Reverb"** — a straightforward Hall algorithm; his general ambience.
2. **Slap delay** — a spare/utility slap.
3. **"Filter sound"** — built on the **ModFilter** algorithm, tweaked *"so it sounded a little warmer
   and touch-sensitive, so it's really dynamic when you hit it."* Heard on the songs *Heart by Heart*,
   *Carry Me Home*, *What Are We Doing Here*.
4. **Filter + extra reverb** — same ModFilter patch with added reverb (song: *Carry Me Home*). On it:
   *"it just paints the notes really nicely … a little bit more soft attack yet really dynamic because
   it's so responsive to the notes that you play."*
5. **"Room"** — a Room-reverb preset emulating a **distant room mic** (mic at the far end of a studio):
   *"it's emulating the sound of a room mic … you can get just the sound of the space."* Used for the
   solo on *What Are We Doing Here* (with extra fuzz). *"Acts as a cool substitute when you can't
   actually put a microphone at the end of a club."*
6. **"Leslie effect"** — a modified stock **Rotary/Leslie** preset, going for a natural Leslie-speaker
   sound. Heard on the *Now I See* solo, the choruses of *Did You See It Coming*, and *I'll Be Finding You*.

## Takeaway for this rig
Two transferable ideas: (1) name presets by function for fast live recall; (2) keep H90 Programs that
back up the *other* board pedals' essential sounds (a spare reverb/delay) so a single failure doesn't
kill the set — exactly the "Big Sky as failover" reasoning in the dossier. His sounds themselves are
clean/studio, not drone — for the drone/shoegaze walls see the shoegaze-settings + ambient-pads files.
