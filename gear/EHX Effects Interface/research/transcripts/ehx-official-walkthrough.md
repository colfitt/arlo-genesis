EHX · Electro-Harmonix Effects Interface Hardware Plugin | Bridging the Pedalboard-Plugin Gap · 2026-01-12

The official EHX walkthrough — the "Bridging the Pedalboard-Plugin Gap" demo. Frames the two core questions: have you ever wanted to run a DAW audio track through your favorite pedal or your entire pedalboard, and have you ever wished your favorite plugin existed as a real pedal you could use live. The Effects Interface Hardware Plugin answers both, live or in the studio.

THREE MODES. (1) Audio mode (= Hardware Plugin Mode): send any audio track from your DAW straight through your favorite pedals "without a maze of cables or converters" — a fast, easy way to instantly turn your pedals into outboard gear with just a single USB-C connection. (2) Pedalboard mode: use software plugins from your computer like real pedals — load any plugin or group of plugins and engage them in real time with the footswitch, just like your other pedals; the way to bring favorite plugins to a live rig. (3) An extremely portable high-quality 2-in/2-out audio interface, great for mobile recordings or quick sessions.

HARDWARE PLUGIN MODE walkthrough (Pro Tools, but "will work with all the popular DAWs"). Just like any other plugin, you throw it on the insert and you are live. The plugin UI is "very easy to operate": input and output gain sliders, a wet/dry control, and on/off. The presenter has his entire physical pedalboard hooked up to the Effects Interface, so each pedal is live in the channel strip.

Worked examples of pedals-as-plugins on tracks:
- Effects Interface loaded on a Hammond B3 track playing the melody; wants to thicken it — adds octaves with the POG 3, then saturation/color from analog drives ("all of my analog drives right up front here"), trying different flavors. Memory Man for analog delay, Oceans Abyss for reverb. "Just like that, my pedals on my tracks."
- Effects Interface on the horn bus (horns mixed to one track) — wants a vocal-like sweep, reaches for the Q-Tron Plus envelope filter, adds a touch of Oceans Abyss reverb. KEY TIP: uses the wet/dry MIX control to bring in a little of the natural attack and clarity of the original (dry) horns alongside the wet Q-Tron return.
- A previously recorded MIKED guitar solo (not a DI) — "I can't really do much as far as reamping it" — but with the whole pedalboard on the Effects Interface he just makes it sound better: colors it with analog drives, adds delay and reverb. (Useful distinction: a miked source can still be sweetened through real pedals even though it can't be properly re-amped.)

PEDALBOARD MODE walkthrough — plugins-as-pedals. "Pedalboard mode works by loading TWO instances of the plugin in one of your DAW's channel strips. One is the IN and one is the OUT. Everything in between those two runs through the Effects Interface Hardware Plugin in real time."
Worked examples:
- Three Neural DSP amp plugins, automated to switch on/off through the track — "basically amp switching inside the DAW." Pedalboard sent out to an EHX 44 Magnum power amp into a 2x12 cab. Pure Neural amp tone + real pedals + real cab = "total hybrid rig with digital flexibility and real-world power."
- Soundtoys Decapitator (analog saturation plugin) with drive at 10, sent through a 50W tube amp (Mig 50) — produces a fuzz flavor that doesn't exist on the physical board, and stacks with the real drive pedals; kicks in a real East River Drive for a mids boost on the heavy section.
- FabFilter Pro-Q 4 as "a studio-grade EQ on your pedalboard" — a guitar curve plus an automated high cut moving with the track; a real Big Muff runs IN FRONT of the FabFilter, sending out to the Mig 50. ("Great for removing mud or adding sparkle.")
- Output Portal (granular delay / pitch-shift) as a pedal — "a total game changer"; a real Flatiron Fuzz on for color into a 1x12 combo (Dirt Road Special).
- Ravish Sitar pedal on a dub track to do something "no plugin I have does."

DUAL MONO MODE example — vintage vibe: left channel runs a Small Stone phaser on a Wurlitzer; right channel runs a kick+snare bus through a Black Finger tube compressor and a Deluxe Memory Man delay. Demonstrates the two channels processing independent material at once.

Recurring workflow themes: pedals/plugins live "on the insert" of any track or bus; automation of plugin on/off and parameters; mixing real outboard with software (real Big Muff in front of FabFilter; Decapitator into a real tube amp) in either direction; the wet/dry MIX as the dial for blending processed return with original signal.
