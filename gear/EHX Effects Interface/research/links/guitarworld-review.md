The most substantive independent written review. (Author is Alex Lynham — verify before crediting; the dossier did not name the author.)

THREE MODES, restated:
1. Hardware Plugin Mode — insert physical pedals into your DAW as effects.
2. Pedalboard Mode — insert DAW plugins into your hardware signal chain.
3. Audio Interface — standalone 2-in/2-out interface.

SETUP. Operational "in under five minutes," BUT "you will need to consult the manual and follow the instructions" — i.e., not blind plug-and-play; first connection needs configuration. Controls used for the workflow: level/monitor sliders for GAIN MATCHING; the stereo input meters are called "crucial" for monitoring levels (watch them to set return level); footswitch for the DAW effects-insert mode; BUFFERED BYPASS. Power by 9V DC center-negative or USB-C; stereo in/out.

PRIMARY USE — PEDAL-AS-PLUGIN (re-amp). The device "excels at applying analog gain stages to tracks in your DAW projects" — e.g. running drums through physical pedals like a Big Muff, Tube Screamer, or analog delays/reverbs that are "easier to create with plugins" in hardware form. (Concrete re-amp idea: drums → real Big Muff; the active gain stage adds punch.)

PLUGIN-AS-PEDAL (more involved). Confirmed possible but "requires switch[ing] around the settings on the plugin, and follow[ing] some suggestions for reducing latency." So Pedalboard Mode is the fiddlier of the two — expect to tune plugin settings and apply the manual's latency-reduction tips (lower buffers).

PRICE / VALUE. Street ~$323.10–$359, "roughly equivalent to an audio interface." Verdict: "Every once in a while a pedal comes along that takes you by surprise… a pedal I didn't even know I needed." Main con: "Price might deter some."

(Earlier-wave dossier notes attributed to this review — buffered bypass, the "bit of gain"/punchiness from the active amp section, the slight-lag "double-tracked" effect being "actually pretty useful," and "unnoticeable with a fast computer" — are consistent with this review's framing; the gain-matching sliders + crucial input meters are the operational takeaways.)
