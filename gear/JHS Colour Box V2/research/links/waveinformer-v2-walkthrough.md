The most detailed V2 control walkthrough. Confirms measured ranges and the named-sound framework.

CONTROLS / RANGES (as measured/stated by WaveInformer — these AGREE with JHS's own current V2 spec, and DIFFER from the dossier §2's older manual figures; see digest):
- STEP (5-pos): +18 / +23 / +28 / +33 / +39 dB.
- TREBLE ±15 dB, flat at noon. TREBLE SHIFT 2 kHz–30 kHz (CCW = lower freq, CW = higher).
- MIDDLE ±10 dB, flat at noon. MIDDLE SHIFT 150 Hz–2.4 kHz (CCW = warmth/body, CW = bite/honk).
- BASS ±15 dB, flat at noon. BASS SHIFT 20 Hz–440 Hz (CCW = sub-bass, CW = upper-bass/low-mids).
- HIGH-PASS: on/off toggle + knob; **160 Hz–650 Hz, 2nd-order 12 dB/octave slope.**
- HI/LO: LO = clean headroom; HI = hotter distortion/fuzz circuit.

GAIN-STAGING: PRE-VOL is the drive — "increasing Pre-Vol adds warmth, saturation, overdrive, and eventually fuzz." MASTER is the final output trim. (Classic clean = MASTER high, PRE-VOL low.)

NAMED SOUNDS (WaveInformer's recipes — described, not numbered):
- **Clean Neve preamp:** LO mode with subtle EQ. "Hi-fi and pristine" with subtle analog coloration.
- **Console crunch:** mid PRE-VOL with mild STEP gain.
- **Fuzz chaos / broken console:** HI mode, STEP at the 5th (max) position, cranked PRE-VOL — blistering, gated, gnarly.
- **DI usage:** direct recording, "especially when overdriven."
- **Bass tracking:** saturation + EQ for character.

THE "HONK/TELEPHONE" TRICK (quoted): "Set all 3 bands [SHIFTs] to a midrange frequency, and boost each of them as much as possible" → a "snarly, honking, almost comical mid-forward tone."

HIGH-PASS guidance: use it to reduce low-end mud; a great pre-fuzz / low-end-control tool. Start ~100–160 Hz and adjust.
