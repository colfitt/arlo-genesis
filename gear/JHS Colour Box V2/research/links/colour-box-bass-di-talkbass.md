# Bass DI use — the community's #1 non-guitar application

## Why bassists buy it
It's a one-box **saturation + semi-parametric EQ + transformer DI** — "an XLR output, transformer-based DI, and a hi-pass filter, making it perfect for direct recording or live use" (Bass Gear Reviews). The XLR out goes straight to the interface / FOH — no separate DI box needed. Bass Musician Magazine: "XLR input and output means you can use it as a proper DI or mic preamp."

## Concrete bass tips
- **Gain-staging is the whole skill.** "Watch gain staging carefully — the pedal can get distorted quickly, but if you use the master volume and keep your gain stage under control, it can be clean." Push STEP/PRE-VOL for "fat, vintage grit," pull MASTER back for level.
- **Use the 20 dB PAD with hot / line-level sources.** TalkBass tip: feeding a line-level source (e.g. **HX Stomp**) into the XLR input *with the −20 dB PAD engaged* gives "a massive array of different sounds at different gain levels without hitting the clipping limit quite so quickly." (Directly relevant to the rig's line-level VG-800 feed.)
- **HI-PASS for sub cleanup:** "a powerful tool for cleaning up sub-frequencies" / "taming sub-bass... without sacrificing clarity." Roll it up to stop the low B/E flubbing.
- **It does NOT do edge-of-breakup well** — multiple bassists note that once it breaks up, the breakup "can be pretty harsh," especially flagged for **upright/double bass**. Treat it as clean-DI-or-full-grind, not subtle hair.
- **EQ shifts adapt per instrument:** sweep the band centers "to adapt to different instruments, playing styles, or room acoustics."

## Consensus
The V2 is "more polished and more usable, especially for bassists — quieter, more flexible, and way more refined when it comes to EQ and gain staging" than the V1. Verdict across bass sources: this is one of its best, most-recommended uses — arguably better on bass than on guitar.
