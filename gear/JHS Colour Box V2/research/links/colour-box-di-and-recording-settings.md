Consolidated DI / console-print / recording settings + interfacing (cross-checked against Off Shift Productions "like a plugin" https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ApiWeqaIjCo and Reverb Tone Report's classic-direct-in technique https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=65tJ13ePY8E). Settings narrated, not numbered, unless noted.

CONSOLE-PRINT / DUAL-OUTPUT WORKFLOW (Produce Like A Pro + Reverb):
- Use the two parallel outs at once: 1/4" out → amp (or pedalboard), XLR out → interface. Print BOTH simultaneously — the direct preamp track carries the present/forward "direct" character, the mic'd-amp track carries weight/round-ness; blend to taste.
- EQ the guitar going INTO the amp and print it as-is: "the EQ going into the amp is more interesting to me than applying it afterward." Crunch level + a MID-SHIFT into the low mids (within 160 Hz–2.5 kHz) adds "low-mid thickness," "classier sounding."

CLEAN DI / FUNK-INTO-CONSOLE (Produce Like A Pro, EQ-flat baseline then thickened):
- HI/LO = LO (on verge of breakup), EQ flat → classic late-'70s/'80s funk-straight-into-console (Chic / Grace Jones reference). For a thin Telecaster bridge pickup: boost low mids + some BASS SHIFT + a little high-mid to "push the guitar forward."
- MASTER higher + PRE-VOL lower = cleaner (his stated rule of thumb).

BROKEN-CONSOLE PREAMP FUZZ (DI, no amp): EQ flat, STEP 5 (max), PRE-VOL maxed → "Hendrix on/off" / "full-blown pure preamp distortion, no amp." Flip HI mode + engage HIGH-PASS for a tighter overload. Back PRE-VOL/STEP down for "crunch level" and use EQ for subtlety.

VOCAL / MIC-PREAMP (Produce Like A Pro, SM7 → XLR input): flip side switch INSTRUMENT→XLR. PRE-VOL up = overdriven vocal; PRE-VOL down = "clean as a whistle" with lots of low end (good "radio/podcast" voice). EQ as desired.

HARDWARE-PLUGIN / RE-AMP INTERFACING (Off Shift Productions — the load-bearing practical detail):
- To insert it on line-level DAW tracks you MUST use the **XLR input** (the combo jack's 1/4" path is instrument level only). Cables: interface line-out → male-XLR-to-male-TRS → Colour Box XLR IN; Colour Box XLR OUT → female-XLR-to-male-TRS → interface line-in.
- **Engage the 20 dB PAD** — line-level sources are "way too hot going into the Colour Box" without it.
- His recipe: MASTER cranked to max, then set STEP + PRE-VOL for a decent signal, then EQ. Use a DAW insert plugin (Studio One "Pipeline" / Ableton "External Audio Effect" / Logic "I/O") for latency-compensated routing; photograph knob settings for recall. Verdict: slower than a plugin but "brings something sonically I haven't found in a plugin."

CLASSIC DIRECT-IN TARGETS (Reverb Tone Report, parallel DI + mic'd-amp blend, parametric EQ to sculpt, HI/LO for headroom-vs-fuzz): Beatles "Revolution," Nirvana "Territorial Pissings" (+ Caroline Somersault chorus + wah in front), ZZ Top "Fool for Your Stockings" (Strat neck+middle, optical comp), Pink Floyd "Another Brick in the Wall Pt.2" solo (P90s/mini-hums, comp), The Byrds "Mr. Tambourine Man" (12-string).
