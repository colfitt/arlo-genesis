Consolidated BASS settings + gain-staging from the most detailed bass demo (cross-checked against The Bass Channel demo https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fnkU8aMF2Tg and Bass Musician Magazine https://bassmusicianmagazine.com/2025/07/review-jhs-colour-box-v2-a-neve-preamp-in-a-pedal/). All positions QUOTED from The Bass Negus unless noted.

GAIN-STAGING / LOW-NOISE RULE (his core technique, quoted):
- Lean on PRE-VOL, keep MASTER conservative: "keep the output [MASTER] volume LOWER than the gain [PRE-VOL]" → avoids the hiss "some people were getting."
- For DYNAMIC MICS through it: HI/LO = **HI**, STEP **low**, PRE-VOL **9–12 o'clock**, set level with MASTER. ("You don't get all that noise.")

NAMED BASS TONES:
- **Clean / transparent color bass DI** (his main use): HI/LO = LO, STEP ~2–3 clicks in from full-left, PRE-VOL ~9 o'clock, MASTER ~3 o'clock — "pushed up into it a little just to have enough color." The point is saturation/warmth, not distortion. EQ flat-ish or his clarity curve below.
- **His clarity EQ curve (bass):** boost ~1 kHz (MID), cut ~200–300 Hz, small touch of ~2.5 kHz; uses BASS SHIFT swept up (~350–500 Hz) so the BASS knob acts as a low-mid CUT, freeing the MID band. "Adds a ton of clarity without taking any booty out."
- **Extreme distorted/synth-bass:** PRE-VOL (gain) ~3 o'clock, MASTER ~9 o'clock, STEP 2–3 clicks in, HI mode. Self-gating "almost noise-gate / bit-crusher" breakup. Run a NOISE GATE BEFORE the Colour Box to tame the cranked-gain hiss. He stacks octaver → resonant filter / detune / parallel compression after it.

BASS EQ FREQUENCY CHOICES (his quoted ranges + picks):
- TREBLE SHIFT 2 k–30 kHz → he sets ~5 k–7 k.
- MID SHIFT 160 Hz–2.5 kHz → ~200–300 Hz to cut mud (9 o'clock); ~800–1,200 Hz to boost; ~2–2:30 o'clock for upper-mid clarity.
- BASS SHIFT 20 Hz–400 Hz → boost ~11 o'clock (~40–60 Hz) for support; can also cut.
- EVERY band bypasses at its center detent (boost one band without the others coloring the signal).

I/O FOR BASS: passes 48 V phantom for condensers; XLR out to FOH/interface + 1/4" to pedalboard simultaneously; can run another preamp (e.g. ToneX) INTO the Colour Box to stack.

Bass Musician Magazine (Cláudio Ribeiro, 2025-07-28) adds only qualitative guidance: PRE-VOL "crank it up" for fat vintage grit, "keep it low" for clean hi-fi; high-pass is a "sleeper feature for bass" to clean sub-frequencies; tones range "warm vintage Motown-style fatness" to "articulate modern mid-forward growl." No numbers.
