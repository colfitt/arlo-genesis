# Engineer's-eye take: it's a real DI / mic-pre / console-print stage

The studio review that treats the Colour Box as outboard, not a guitar toy — the best source for the rig's "Neve front-end → tape print → Apollo" philosophy. (This is the V1, but the DI/preamp behavior is the same circuit family.)

## Headline verdict
**"This thing is worth the price alone just as a DI."** The engineer's takeaway is that its *non-guitar, source-print* uses justify it before you ever consider it a drive pedal — exactly the rig's "better on non-guitar sources" consensus.

## Documented studio uses (all from the review)
- **Bass DI:** tracked bass through it for "a beautiful mid-'70s rock way," with the bonus that the onboard EQ lets you re-voice song-to-song without re-patching. → maps to the rig's always-on bass DI into the Apollo x8.
- **Mic preamp:** ran a **Beyerdynamic M160 ribbon** for live vocals; result was **"reminiscent of something off of Gene Clark's *No Other*,"** and "amazing" when blended with delay/modulation. Confirms the 48V-phantom XLR-in path is a legitimate channel strip.
- **Anything-as-a-source:** an entire session went through it — **Moog synths, percussion, even a tabla machine** — and it "lent a real legitimacy to the sound" of unconventional sources. This is the "console color makes it sound *recorded*" trick.
- **Live gain-staging fix:** on tour it solved a dynamic vocalist's level swings, "eliminating need to ride faders." (The preamp + its gain structure as a leveling tool.)

## Technique tips the engineer gives
- For clean DI guitar: balanced **XLR out → DAW interface**, then EQ to taste — yields "attractive and surprisingly amp-like clean tones." (The DI-into-interface workflow.)
- In front of a real amp it "alters the amp's fundamental character," but **at serious gain you must keep MASTER at its lowest** to avoid runaway level — the PRE-VOL↔MASTER discipline again.
- The pushed/extreme tone is genuine **broken-console fuzz** ("of a type we usually avoid"); soften direct-recorded harshness with reverb after it.

## Rig relevance
This source is the backbone of the rig's "console-to-tape" arc: Colour Box = the Neve *front end* (DI/preamp/print color in) → PORTA424 / JHS 424 = the *tape* at the back → Apollo print. Use it as a real channel strip for bass, acoustic, violin, and vocals — and lean on its EQ to re-voice per source the way the engineer does.
