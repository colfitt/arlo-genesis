# Gain-staging discipline, the EQ "honk" trick, and DI workflow

The most detailed accessible V2 control walkthrough. Core technique themes that recur across every source: master the **STEP → PRE-VOL → MASTER** chain, it is *not* a twiddle-the-knobs pedal.

## Gain staging — the whole game (STEP / PRE-VOL / MASTER)
- **STEP** = coarse input gain, a 5-position rotary (+18 / +23 / +28 / +33 / +39 dB). "The higher you go, the more the pedal starts to saturate like a real preamp."
- **PRE-VOL** = the drive knob, sits *between* the two gain stages: "Increasing Pre-Vol adds warmth, saturation, overdrive, and eventually fuzz." This is where you dial the *character*.
- **MASTER** = final output / level trim. Discipline: push PRE-VOL (and STEP) for grit, then **pull MASTER back to claw the volume jump back to unity.** Because PRE-VOL sets both gain AND level, every gain change needs a MASTER trim or it jumps loud.
- **HI/LO mode:** LO = "more clean headroom and less distortion" (clean preamp / DI work); HI = "hotter circuit for more distortion and fuzz tones."
- **Universal community warning (TalkBass, echoed everywhere):** it distorts *quickly* — "if you use the master volume and keep your gain stage under control, it can be clean." For clean tracking, keep STEP low + PRE-VOL low + MASTER doing the level. It does NOT do edge-of-breakup gracefully — once it breaks up it gets harsh fast (noted specifically for upright bass).

## The EQ "telephone / honk / snarl" trick (the party trick)
WaveInformer's signature move, verbatim: *"One of my favorite tricks is to set all 3 bands of EQ to a midrange frequency, and boost each of them as much as possible. The resulting sound is a snarly, honking, almost comical mid-forward tone."* Useful as a radio/lo-fi voice — and in this rig, a deliberate mid-stack to make the fuzz scream.

## HI-PASS as a cleanup tool
Sweepable (~60–800 Hz, the reviewer measured ~160–650 Hz on his unit), "especially useful for tightening up low-end muddiness on vocals, guitars, or synths." This is the control you roll up *before* the fuzzes to keep the wall tight.

## DI / preamp into an interface
"When tracking bass, the Colour Box is super valuable for both its saturation and EQ." XLR in with **phantom power pass-through** takes a real condenser. Consensus: it's "a legitimate Swiss Army knife for producers and recording engineers. Run vocals through it, sweeten drum overheads, or distort synths" — i.e. **better/more at-home on non-guitar sources** than as a guitar drive.
