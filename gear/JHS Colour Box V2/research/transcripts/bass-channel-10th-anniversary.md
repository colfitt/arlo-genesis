Today on The Bass Channel we're checking out the 10th-anniversary edition of the Colour Box from JHS. At its heart this is the same V2 Colour Box we've come to know and love — same tone and functionality — but in a new finish we feel is even better than the original. If you're not familiar with the Colour Box, it's essentially a Neve preamp in a stompbox. It works with vocals, guitars, drums, keyboards and of course bass. (We did a video on the standard V2 Colour Box a while back where Chris talks through the pedal; we're not going to talk through it today but we'll run down the controls.)

CONTROLS:
- The red section is the gain section. STEP is a five-position rotary that selects five different gain stages — lower settings give a nice warm clean sound, higher settings get into more extreme fuzz tones.
- PRE-VOLUME is best thought of like an input gain — it determines how hot the signal is going into the pedal. Turn it up to hit the pedal harder for more saturation, or dial it back to keep it cleaner.
- MASTER is like your post volume — it only sets the output level without affecting the tone.
- The HIGH/LOW switch sets the amount of clean headroom. In LOW mode you get ultra-clean sounds without breakup; LOW is better for tone-shaping when distortion is not needed. In HIGH mode the pedal distorts much more easily, giving a range of overdrive, distortion and fuzz.
- The blue section is a three-band parametric EQ. The TREBLE, MID and BASS knobs set the level of each band, while the respective SHIFT knobs select the specific frequency to be cut or boosted.
- The HIGH-PASS (we ignored this in our previous video, much to the dismay of the comment section) is enabled by the toggle switch below the high-pass knob — flip it up to engage. The HPF is a **2nd-order, 12 dB-per-octave high-pass filter, −3 dB cutoff frequency adjustable from 160 Hz to 650 Hz.**
