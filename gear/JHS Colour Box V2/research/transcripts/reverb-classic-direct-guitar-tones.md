(Host: Andy / Reverb Tone Report. The canonical "guitar straight into the console" tone-recreation video — four famous direct-in records, using the V2's parallel outputs to mix a direct DI signal with a mic'd amp.)

PREMISE: Four guitar tones that share something in common — they all went DIRECT into the console. We're using the JHS Colour Box V2 to get those console tones in a pedal: whether it's raunchy direct fuzz (Beatles "Revolution") or jangly clean tones, the Colour Box delivers. With the added parametric 3-band EQ in this second version you can sculpt guitar, bass, keys or anything you want to mic with studio-level precision. The five selectable gain steps work with the PRE-VOLUME knob to dial in subtle analog coloration or all-out fuzz — which sounds even better going through your pedalboard and into an amp. The new HIGH/LOW toggle preserves the highest headroom when clipping isn't what you're after.

PARALLEL-OUTPUT TECHNIQUE (used throughout): He sends one output of the Colour Box DIRECT into the interface and the other into a Suhr 112 combo, then mixes the two. The direct signal gives the raw, present, "direct" character; the amp signal holds the weight and roundness together.

THE FOUR TONES:
1. Nirvana "Territorial Pissings" — start with the clean direct sound into the interface (dull and flat on its own), then the Colour Box adds a little grit and uses the parametric EQ to its fullest. Adds a Caroline Somersault for slow lush chorus modulation, and a wah in front (signal goes wah → Colour Box → amp, splitting to the interface). Final blend of the direct + amp signals = a raw but well-rounded sound.
2. ZZ Top "Fool for Your Stockings" — "about as clean as it gets," especially with the neck-and-middle position on a Strat-style guitar; adds a Thorpy Fat General optical compressor for a studio-like tone.
3. Pink Floyd "Another Brick in the Wall Pt. 2" (solo) — direct + amp together. (Gilmour used a Les Paul with P90s, not his Strat; host uses mini-humbuckers.) Compression in front; the direct signal pushes the compression to the front of the mix while the amp holds the weight.
4. The Byrds "Mr. Tambourine Man" — on a 12-string; McGuinn reportedly used two compressors for the jangle. "Whether it was recorded direct or into an amplifier, the Colour Box really delivers that signature sound."
