The Bass Negus · Unlocking JHS Colour Box V2: A Complete Bass Tutorial · 2024-07-15

(All Bass Creations / The Bass Negus — a ~59-minute deep bass walkthrough. Strong on real-world gain-staging, noise avoidance, and specific EQ frequency choices for bass. He stresses the pedal "doesn't really change your sound, it just adds saturation," and that it's hard to capture on video — you need it in your hands.)

OVERALL: Quickly became one of my favorite preamp pedals — versatility and a very clean, pristine, high-quality preamp sound, with a nice analog warmness. Designed after the 1073 preamps (not claiming it IS one). I actually use this more on the clean side as a bassist; I don't really like the way it distorts, but I'll show you. The "coloring / saturation on it is amazing — it sounds great even when it's being very transparent." It doesn't translate as well on video as in person.

GAIN-STAGING / NOISE TRICK (important, quoted): For dynamic mics, "turn the gain on HIGH, keep the STEP low, and push the [PRE-]gain to anywhere from 9 to 12 o'clock, then do the rest with the output volume. Keep the output volume LOWER than the gain — use the gain to gain a little bit more, and less of the output volume — because for some reason when you do it that way you don't get all that noise that some people were getting." (i.e. lean on PRE-VOLUME, keep MASTER conservative, to avoid hiss.) For his distorted bass example he ran STEP at the "second or third click" (from full-left), and at one point PRE-VOLUME ("gain") at 3 o'clock with MASTER at 9 o'clock for an extreme distorted/gated tone. Normal clean bass: STEP 2–3 clicks in, PRE-VOLUME around 9 o'clock, MASTER around 3 o'clock, "pushed up into it a little bit just to make sure I have enough color."

EQ — FREQUENCY RANGES + HIS BASS CHOICES (quoted):
- TREBLE SHIFT: 2,000 Hz to 30,000 Hz. He sets his around 5k–7k.
- MID SHIFT: 160 Hz to 2.5 kHz. Around 9 o'clock (~200–300 Hz) is good for cutting low-mud on bass; ~800–1,200 Hz for a boost. Later he boosts mids around 2 o'clock / 2:30 (~upper mids) for clarity.
- BASS SHIFT: 20 Hz to 400 Hz. He likes to boost around 11 o'clock (roughly ~40–60 Hz for support), and can also cut with it. "You want enough booty to support things, not too much."
- All bands BYPASS when set to the center detent — "you put them in the middle and it bypasses the EQ, whichever knob individually." So you can boost mids without the bass/treble knobs tone-stacking into the signal. (He notes JHS users asked for an EQ-bypass switch but it isn't needed because of this.)

DISTORTION CHARACTER: With PRE-VOLUME cranked all the way up it has a "natural breakup, almost like a noise gate" — it gates/cuts off on its own as you let notes ring; sounds "almost like a bit-crusher but not." Running a noise gate BEFORE it helps tame the hiss when you crank the gain. He builds an extreme synth-bass tone: Colour Box pushed + a Pigtronix-style octaver (mostly octave-down) → a resonant filter / detune / Empress parallel compression. "That's fire — going in the arsenal."

ADVANCED EQ TRICK (using BASS knob as a mid-cut): Because each band's SHIFT can reach into adjacent ranges, he uses the BASS knob (with BASS SHIFT swept up toward ~350–500 Hz) as a low-mid CUT instead of a bass boost — freeing the MID band to boost ~1 kHz for clarity while cutting ~200–300 Hz, plus a touch of ~2.5k. "Adds a ton of clarity without taking any booty out."

I/O NOTES: Side switches — XLR/INSTRUMENT select, −20 dB PAD. Other side — XLR out and quarter-inch out (run the quarter-inch into your pedalboard and the XLR to the house). Passes 48 V phantom power for condenser mics. Demos his bass through custom IRs of a Genz-Benz Neo X 2×12 (D6 + SM57) after the Colour Box, and notes you can even run other preamps (e.g. a ToneX) INTO the Colour Box to "step that sound up even more."
