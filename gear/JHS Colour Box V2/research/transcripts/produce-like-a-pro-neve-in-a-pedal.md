Produce Like A Pro · NEVE In A Pedal? JHS The Colour Box V2 Pedal @jhspedals · 2024-12-30

(Warren Huart / Produce Like A Pro — a recording-engineer's take, simultaneously printing the preamp DI and a mic'd Marshall. The most settings-narrated of the V2 videos. JHS-sent unit.)

WHY EXCITED: I'm a guitar player but I spend most of my life sticking microphones in front of guitars. About 10–12 years ago I did an Aerosmith album; Joe Perry is a huge Jimmy Page fan, and one of the most famous Page sounds is plugging into the front of a mic preamp — probably a 1073. This Colour Box does that. It acts as a preamp too, so you can come out of the XLR and also go into an amp. I want to do that Jimmy Page sound: plug the guitar into the front of the mic pre, crank it, and get that disgusting-but-beautiful sound of a mic-pre overloading. We're also going to plug into a Marshall in the other room — use it as a guitar pedal into an amp AND as an overloaded preamp printing straight into the DAW.

SETUP: Mexican Fender Telecaster (maple neck) → Colour Box. The pedal's quarter-inch output → a Marshall JCM2000 in the live room (set pretty clean — gain ~1, presence pushed a little above treble, treble cut slightly, mids slightly boosted, bass slightly more than slightly boosted, not maxed). An SM57 on the Marshall (4×12, mic up close, top, 12 in) → a Neve-ish mic pre → Avid HDX. The Colour Box's XLR output → straight into the interface as a preamp. So we simultaneously record (a) the mic'd Marshall — no EQ/compression, just the amp — and (b) the direct preamp sound, which is whatever the pedal does. Also testing an SM7 for vocals later.

CONTROLS as read from the manual ("destructions"):
- MASTER = overall master volume; works in tandem with PRE-VOLUME. For cleaner sound, set MASTER higher and PRE-VOLUME lower. (Noted as "active EQ" — EQ boosts can drive it harder.)
- STEP changes the gain of each preamp stage in five stages: **+18, +23, +28, +33, +39 dB.** "You just kind of go to the maximum because that's what you do" — at STEP 5, max PRE-VOLUME, zero EQ, it's a "Hendrix on/off sound" but with no warmth/color until you apply the EQ.
- Set EQ to the center detent (noon) = fairly neutral; it's not coloring much until you move things.
- EQ = "highly modified Baxandall type," updated for V2. The SHIFT knobs select the frequency range each band controls. Manual frequency ranges quoted: **TREBLE SHIFT 2 kHz to 30 kHz; MID SHIFT 160 Hz to 2.5 kHz; BASS SHIFT 20 Hz to 400 Hz.** Brightest treble is full-left on the shift. ("What's great is how it blooms — builds up and blooms.")
- HIGH/LOW toggle: selects lowest-gain clean mode vs. higher-gain distorted mode. On clean mode it's on the verge of breakup; push HIGH and it starts to overload.
- HIGH-PASS: between **160 and 650 Hz, 6 dB-per-octave** (gentlest slope but still a slope). Toggle switch turns it on/off. (NOTE: he says 6 dB/oct here while the on-screen manual/other reviewers say 12 dB/oct, 2nd order — discrepancy flagged in digest.)
- Side switches: INSTRUMENT/XLR input select; 20 dB PAD.

KEY WORKFLOW INSIGHT (guitar recording): "The EQ on this going into the amp is more interesting to me than applying it afterward." Regardless of gain, you can really shape the sound of your guitar amp — stick a mic in front and print it as-is. Demonstrates: with crunch level + a MID-SHIFT move into the low mids (160 Hz to 2.5 k range), it brings low-mid thickness in — "classier sounding," "low-mid thickness," "tasty." BASS SHIFT (20–400 Hz) can get ultra-low-mids going.

DEMO — DIRECT PREAMP (into interface, EQ flat): All the distortion is from the preamp. Gives that classic late-'70s/early-'80s funk-guitar-straight-into-the-console sound. Pushing HIGH mode + the high-pass on, you get a "kny / starting-to-overload" sound; this is "full-blown pure preamp distortion — no amp." For clean funk coloration on a Telecaster bridge pickup (which can be "wily, thin and disgusting"), boost some low mids, some bass shift, a little high-mid — "this will really push the guitar forward." References Chic / Steven Lipson / Grace Jones guitar parts plugged straight into the console.

DEMO — VOCAL (SM7 → XLR input): Flip INSTRUMENT/XLR to XLR. With his EQ still set (lots of low-end thickness) he can overdrive a vocal hard, or back off PRE-VOLUME for a "pretty darn clean vocal" with lots of low end — even good for a podcast/radio voice. Backing PRE-VOLUME down = clean as a whistle.

VERDICT: Serves as a preamp for a mic, as a guitar pedal through an amp while simultaneously recording through the interface, and sounds fantastic with that console-overdriven sound. Loves it especially for clean thickening of thin/funky high-line Telecaster parts. "It does lots of jobs."
