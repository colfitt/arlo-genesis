# Sam Wittek — Chroma Console preset showcase (distilled)

A worship/ambient guitarist's walkthrough of his 10 presets, ordered classic → experimental →
"familiar with a twist." Companion to his MultiTracks pack. Concrete recipes below.

## Approach / philosophy
- Uses Chroma as an **additive "Swiss-army knife"** layer, **not standalone** — almost always
  stacked with his other reverb/delay (TimeLine, BigSky). "I don't really use it just by itself."
- Wanted every preset, even the wild ones, to be **usable live**.
- Big on the pedal's habit of taking a familiar effect and "throwing its own twist into things."

## Classic effects
- **Vibrato (go-to):** built on the **PITCH** effect, not the VIBRATO effect. Gives an irregular,
  intermittent waveform — "not like a regular vibrato where there's a normal wave to it." His
  favorite effect on the pedal.
- **Chorus:** also uses the **PITCH** function. "The pitch function on here has so much versatility…
  a ton of different sounds."
- **Tremolo:** keeps a trem in his back pocket without a dedicated pedal taking board space.

## Experimental
- **Supernova:** **COLLAGE** delay with a recorded **GESTURE** (knob automation) that sweeps octaves
  up and down — "reminds me of a spaceship." COLLAGE spits the signal back intermittently in
  different octaves ("scattered delay type of thing"). "Throw a ton of reverb on this" → ambient
  soundscape generator.
- **Reverse Ice:** **REVERSE delay, 100% wet** (no dry attack audible), repeats pitched **up an
  octave**. Produces "this side-chain sort of thing — a pulsing rhythmic texture."
- **Dream:** uses the **FILTER** to remove a lot of the highs, "like turning the tone knob down on
  the guitar," for a soft pad.

## Familiar with a twist
- **Analog:** warm analog/BBD delay (CASCADE) whose repeats develop "really nice modulation… the
  more repeats, the more modulation." **MIDI-clock-synced to an eighth-note**, then stacked against a
  **dotted-eighth on the Strymon TimeLine** (both on one MIDI clock) for a bouncy rhythmic/ambient
  delay. His personal favorite of the set.
- **Lo-Fi:** the "Vibrato" preset + **FILTER** rolling off highs = "chewy vibrato that sounds really
  lo-fi… kind of blends into the background."
- **Wide Vibes:** uses the **VIBRATO** effect's stereo side — "immediately it pulls everything to
  the sides, sounds super wide, super huge," with a touch of chorusing.
- **Huge Fuzz:** **HOWL** (resonant filter fuzz) + **DOUBLER** (width) + **SPACE** (a little reverb).
  Big, wide, full — but controlled enough for a "big driving lead guitar part." Rates Chroma's
  digital fuzzes/drives highly: "for them being digital are actually really, really awesome."
