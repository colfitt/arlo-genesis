Hologram Electronics (official) · Chroma Console: Sound from Scratch #1 · 2023-12-05 (5:26)

Matt from Hologram Electronics builds a sound on Chroma Console from scratch. Source: a Fender Mustang into a Milkman amp sim in Logic, nothing else in the chain.

First, engage the pedal and turn the MIX knob all the way up. Hologram recommends starting at full wet and mixing dry signal back in to taste — but here Matt leaves MIX up the entire time.

Start with SWEETEN (Character module). Put both knobs at 50%. This is a gentle compressor: it smooths out the transient peaks. Turn the AMOUNT knob all the way up and you get subtle saturation as well. Pull it back to 50%.

Switch to CASSETTE (Texture module) — this has more compression, saturation, and modulation. Turn it all the way up to hear it: more heavily modulated, more saturation, some stereo effects introduced. Leave it at 50%.

PHASER (Movement module): put both knobs at 50%. Turn the RATE down (he likes that slower sound) and turn the AMOUNT knob up a hair for a nice "chewy" phaser.

REELS (Diffusion module) — this is the tape delay. TIME increases the delay time; AMOUNT increases the delay feedback. In the secondary menu, turn up DRIFT, which adds extra modulation to the delay — the delay line becomes more saturated, "as though the tape has aged or degraded," plus additional stereo content and fluttering. He turns DRIFT down to 50 and leaves the secondary.

Right now the signal flows SWEETEN → PHASER → tape-delay REELS → CASSETTE. He enters EFFECT SETUP, which displays the module order, and reorders it: tape delay (REELS) first, then he wants the tape-delay trails to be modulated by PHASER, so he places PHASER after it; both of those subtly compressed by SWEETEN; then runs all of that into CASSETTE.

He configures it in DUAL BYPASS so that the BYPASS footswitch turns PHASER and REELS on and off (and it works with trails — the delay keeps decaying after the footswitch press), which is helpful for bringing the delay in at different parts of a song. He demos SWEETEN + CASSETTE as the always-on base, then footswitches in PHASER and the tape delay for an extra "dimension."

Key takeaways:
- Workflow: MIX full up, then dial each module's AMOUNT to taste.
- SWEETEN = gentle compressor/smoother at ~50%; pushes into subtle saturation when cranked.
- CASSETTE = compression + saturation + modulation + stereo, all in one knob.
- REELS DRIFT = the "aged/degraded tape" character + stereo flutter on the delay.
- Module reorder lets you decide what modulates the delay trails (here PHASER after REELS).
- Dual bypass assigns specific modules (PHASER + REELS) to the footswitch as a song-section "add."
