KVNDRA · Turn YOUR synth into a sound lab | Microcosm & Chroma Console sound design tutorial · 2024-08-14 (20:56)

Directly relevant to the rig's Hologram pairing. KVNDRA runs a DAWless stream: Elektron Digitone (audio + MIDI) → CHROMA CONSOLE → MICROCOSM → Ableton/interface. MIDI chains Digitone → Chroma → Microcosm so the effects clock-sync to the sequence; a return MIDI line from the interface starts the Digitone in sync with Ableton's record.

NOTE ON ORDER: she runs Chroma BEFORE Microcosm (Chroma = drive/movement/texture morph; Microcosm = granular/space on top). In this studio's rig the order is reversed (Microcosm is upstream on Board 2, Chroma downstream on Board 3), but the layering logic — Chroma shapes character/movement, then granular space sits on top — is the same idea.

CHROMA CONSOLE MODULE PASS (on a Digitone arp sequence, MIDI-synced):

CHARACTER (drive):
- FUZZ and HOWL are the two heaviest distortion units.
- DRIVE = basic overdrive.
- SWEETEN = a very soft, gentle distortion; great to just boost/thicken a sound a little. (Her go-to clean lift.)
- SWELL = has a little swelling/volume-onset effect.
- The top (TILT) knob sets how bright or dark the added drive is.

MOVEMENT:
- DOUBLER, VIBRATO, PHASER, TREMOLO, PITCH.
- Because it's MIDI-synced, the tremolo/rate locks to tempo — "quite nice."
- PITCH "can really mess up your sound."
- Favorite on the arp: PHASER with the RATE brought all the way DOWN (slow) and AMOUNT all the way UP — adds super-nice slow movement, "a little bit like an LFO."

DIFFUSION:
- CASCADE (liked best on this sound), REELS, SPACE (can completely wash the sound in reverb), COLLAGE, REVERSE (reverses the sound).
- REVERSE works best on LEADS or PADS — on arpeggiated/staccato sequences it doesn't sound as good. On a lower note the top knob controls the pitch of the reverse (very high to very low) for a low reversed note.
- On the arp she settles on CASCADE with the AMOUNT pulled back, finding the right TIME.

TEXTURE:
- FILTER, SQUASH (heavy compressor — "very much squashed the sound"), CASSETTE, BROKEN ("even wilder"), INTERFERENCE.
- INTERFERENCE "reminds me of the Microcosm — it adds funky artifacts."
- She adds just a tiny bit of SQUASH to glue.

Result: bypassed → fully built, "so much more character and movement." Then she brings up MICROCOSM (MIX down then up): MOSAIC mode = instantly dreamy; GLITCH mode with different shapes = rhythmic; BLOCKS mode; GLIDE 1 = favorite, turns the arp into a rhythmic bass-like texture. The Microcosm's looper can grab a tiny bit of the synced sequence as a song starter.

SECOND SOUND (lead/pad):
- CHARACTER: a heavier distortion, AMOUNT pulled back.
- MOVEMENT: VIBRATO with a fast RATE, AMOUNT brought down a bit.
- DIFFUSION: she SKIPS SPACE here ("space is something we can do with the Microcosm instead") and uses REVERSE on a low note for a low reversed tone, AMOUNT pulled back. KEY LABOR-SPLIT INSIGHT: let one box own the reverb/space rather than doubling it.
- TEXTURE: a little CASSETTE/interference for wobble; MIX brought down to let some dry through.
- Then Microcosm on the long sustained notes: MICROLOOP and GRANULES sections; MOSAIC always sounds amazing; GRANULES (haste mode) is great; bringing the TIME down slows the added-artifact repeats. Beautiful evolving pad.

Takeaways:
- Don't double reverb across the two Hologram boxes — pick which pedal owns SPACE.
- Slow PHASER (rate down, amount up) = the cleanest "LFO movement" on a synth.
- SWEETEN = the gentle clean boost/thickener.
- REVERSE = leads/pads only, not staccato arps; its top knob pitches the reverse note.
- INTERFERENCE on Chroma overlaps tonally with Microcosm's glitch artifacts — keep their jobs distinct.
