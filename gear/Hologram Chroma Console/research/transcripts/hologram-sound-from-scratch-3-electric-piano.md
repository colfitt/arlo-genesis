Hologram Electronics (official) · Chroma Console: Sound from Scratch #3 · 2023-12-15 (9:24)

Matt from Hologram Electronics, this time with a Pianet electric piano (mid-range-heavy timbre) instead of a guitar. Two parts: navigating presets, then building a new vibrato/reverb/filter patch.

PRESET NAVIGATION
- The first preset he built earlier (SWEETEN + CASSETTE on a dual-bypass footswitch) is in Bank A, slot 1. Pressing the bypass footswitch toggles just the two modules routed to dual bypass.
- To enter the preset browser: press and hold the right footswitch. The top LEDs show which BANK you're in: one lit LED = Bank A, next = Bank B, then C, then D (4 banks).
- Fast navigation: use the bottom AMOUNT knobs to scroll through presets 1–20 within a bank, jumping between banks quickly. Or use the footswitches to increment/decrement one at a time.
- Blank vs saved: scroll to a slot — all LEDs white = blank slot; colored LEDs = the effects saved in that preset (it flashes the effect colors briefly, then returns to showing the bank).
- The "swell + pitch + space + interference" preset from a prior episode is in Bank A, slot 4. SWELL = a slow volume onset (volume-swell attack); brings in the interval/pitch for "smarter-sounding chords."

BUILDING A NEW PATCH (VIBRATO + SPACE + FILTER, with GESTURE)
1. Go to a blank preset. All effect controls at zero/minimum. MIX all the way up.
2. VIBRATO (Movement): RATE sets the speed; AMOUNT controls the depth. Full depth is pretty extreme — dial AMOUNT back.
3. Enter the secondary menu and adjust DRIFT for vibrato. DRIFT does two things: by default at zero it's a straightforward SINE-WAVE vibrato modulation; as you turn DRIFT up it morphs increasingly toward a RANDOM waveform AND adds quite a bit of STEREO WIDTH. With DRIFT up and MIX backed off slightly you get a really nice CHORUS sound. Here he wants more of a vibrato, so MIX stays up.
4. Add SPACE reverb (Diffusion). Classic vibrato-into-reverb combo. Dial the reverb TIME. DRIFT for SPACE adds PITCH MODULATION to the reverb tail. Drop MIX to taste (~not full).
5. Enter EFFECT SETUP to (a) choose the FILTER type and (b) reorder. FILTER type: default is TILT; turn the blue encoder left for LOW PASS, right for HIGH PASS. He picks low pass. Let it time out to return to module-order view.
6. Reorder: he taps out the CHARACTER block (not used today). He wants VIBRATO, then he wants the FILTER feeding INTO the reverb (not reverb into filter), so he selects TEXTURE (filter lives in the Texture module) before SPACE, making reverb last in the chain.
7. Engage FILTER — with AMOUNT all the way down in low-pass it's fully open; nothing is removed yet. He wants to create an almost-tremolo effect with the filter by recording GESTURE movements: enter GESTURE (C+D), perform the filter-amount motion for a bit, exit. Bring the reverb back in. Patch done.

Key takeaways:
- The most musical CHORUS on the pedal = VIBRATO with DRIFT turned up + MIX backed off (random waveform + stereo width).
- DRIFT on SPACE = pitch-modulated reverb tail (warble on the verb).
- FILTER type is set in EFFECT SETUP (tilt / LPF / HPF), and chain position relative to reverb matters — put FILTER before reverb to filter the source, not the tail.
- A recorded GESTURE on the FILTER amount = a "manual tremolo"/auto-filter sweep.
