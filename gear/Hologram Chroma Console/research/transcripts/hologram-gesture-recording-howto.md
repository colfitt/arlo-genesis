Hologram Electronics (official) · Hologram Chroma Console: Gesture Recording · 2024-05-03 (1:29)

The official short demo of the GESTURE (knob-motion recorder) feature.

THE PROBLEM IT SOLVES: If you've played a delay pedal you've probably tried to turn knobs while playing (e.g. sweeping a delay, or a tremolo) — but the problem is your hands are on your instrument and you can't turn knobs with your feet. GESTURE recording solves this by letting you RECORD AND LOOP knob movements, so you can build complex evolving sounds that keep moving hands-free. You can make subtle movements that add tasteful coloration, or really big drastic movements that add a lot of excitement.

HOW TO USE IT (step by step):
1. Press the TWO RIGHT BUTTONS simultaneously to begin recording.
2. Move the knob of your choice (the motion is recorded).
3. To end the recording, press the two right buttons again. The motion now loops.
4. To LAYER more knob recordings: repeat the process for each additional knob (you can record multiple knobs).
5. Save your work at any time in one of Chroma Console's 80 user presets.

EDITING:
- To remove a single knob's recorded movement: just move that knob again (live).
- To clear ALL gestures at once: press and HOLD the two right buttons for about 2 seconds.

Note on button labels: the "two right buttons" here are the C and D module buttons (the GESTURE prompt printed on the face as C+D). The official text says "two right buttons"; other tutorials describe the same combo as "C and D."
