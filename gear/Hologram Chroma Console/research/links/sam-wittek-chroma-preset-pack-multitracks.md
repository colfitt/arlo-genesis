# Sam Wittek — "SW Chroma Console" preset pack (the one real commercial pack)

The most prominent shareable Chroma Console preset set. **10 hand-crafted presets.** Sold on
MultiTracks.com (price not shown on the public listing page).

## Critical format note — confirms "recipe-only"
> "Upon purchase, you will receive a **high-quality PDF guide featuring knob positions and detailed
> settings for each preset, along with step-by-step instructions for saving them to your device.**"
> "This patch requires a Chroma Console pedal. These Patches **cannot be loaded into a DAW.**"

There is **no preset file** — you buy a PDF of knob positions and **dial each one in by hand**, then
save to a user slot (COPY/SAVE = B+C). Even a paid pack ships as a recipe. This is the strongest
single confirmation that Chroma presets are not transferable as data.

## The 10 presets (names, from the pack + the demo video timestamps)
Classic / practical:
- **Vibrato** — his "go-to" wobble; actually built on the PITCH effect (not VIBRATO) for an
  irregular, intermittent waveform — "not like a regular vibrato."
- **Chorus** — also built on the PITCH module ("the pitch function has so much versatility"),
  voiced as a unique chorus.
- **Tremolo** — grab-and-go trem so you don't need a dedicated trem pedal on the board.

Experimental:
- **Supernova** — GESTURE-recorded knob automation on the **COLLAGE** delay, octave shoots up/down
  ("reminds me of a spaceship"); scattered, intermittent multi-octave delay. "Throw a ton of reverb
  on it" → ambient soundscape generator.
- **Reverse Ice** — **REVERSE delay, 100% wet** (no dry attack), reverse repeats pitched **up an
  octave**; produces a pulsing, side-chain-like rhythmic texture.
- **Dream** — soft pad-like setting that uses the **FILTER** to roll off highs (like rolling the
  guitar tone knob down).

In-between (familiar-with-a-twist):
- **Analog** — warm BBD/analog-style delay (CASCADE) with modulation that blooms on later repeats;
  MIDI-clock-synced to an **eighth-note** subdivision, stacked against a **dotted-eighth on the
  Strymon TimeLine** for a bouncy rhythmic/ambient delay. (Direct in-rig parallel: Chroma + TimeLine
  on one MIDI clock.)
- **Lo-Fi** — the "Vibrato" preset + **FILTER** rolling off highs = a chewy, lo-fi, background-blend
  vibrato. ("Chewy vibrato that sounds really lo-fi… blends into the background.")
- **Wide Vibes** — uses the **VIBRATO** effect's stereo-spread side; collapses everything to the
  sides for a huge, wide stereo image with a touch of chorusing. (On-aesthetic for re-widening a
  mono signal — same job the dossier gives Chroma after the mono Blooper.)
- **Huge Fuzz** — **HOWL** (resonant filter fuzz) + **DOUBLER** (width) + **SPACE** (a little
  reverb) = big, wide, full fuzz that still works as a controlled lead voice. Praises Chroma's
  digital drives/fuzzes as "really, really awesome."

## Why it matters for this rig
Wittek runs a worship/ambient board (MONO rail, Morningstar MC6 Pro, Strymon TimeLine + BigSky,
Walrus Julianna, Hologram Chroma Console) and MIDI-clocks Chroma + TimeLine together. His preset
philosophy — Chroma as an **additive "Swiss-army" layer on top of reverb/delay**, not a standalone —
matches the dossier's "texture-morph, don't re-degrade" framing. The Wide Vibes / Huge Fuzz / Analog
recipes are directly portable to the baritone/banjo rig.
