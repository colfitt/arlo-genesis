# wolfewithane — "Why would I buy this pedal?" (Chroma Console) — named chain recipes + control-position facts

Distinct from the on-disk `wolfewithane-blog-chroma-console-usage-tips.md` (that one = harmonic-trem gesture,
reorder, tap extension, dual-bypass, power/whine). This page adds **named effect-chain recipes** built by module
order, plus several **exact control-position facts** (where on the knob a behavior lives). The author is explicit
these are "starter thoughts," so they are chain + control-range recipes, NOT full per-knob dial-ins — but the
module assignments and the position facts are concrete and quotable.

## Named effect-chain recipes (by module/effect order)
- **"Reverse Strings"** — chain: **SWELL → VIBRATO → REVERSE.** (Envelope swell into pitch-wobble into reverse
  delay = bowed/string-like backwards texture. Directly on-aesthetic for banjo-as-lead "recorded-wrong" leads.)
- **"Mo Betta Repeats"** — chain: **CASCADE *or* REELS → SWELL → SQUASH.** (Delay first, then swell the repeats,
  then compress/overdrive them — gives the echoes a swelling, squashed sustain instead of a hard attack.)
- **"Gated Reverb"** — **reorder so a reverb (SPACE) sits BEFORE SWELL** → the swell envelope chops the reverb
  tail into a gated-reverb effect. (Order trick, not a knob value.)
- **"Harmonic Trem"** — record a **GESTURE on the FILTER knob sweeping between the 7 o'clock and 10 o'clock
  positions**, controlled by **Tap Tempo.** (Same harmonic-trem idea as the other wolfe page, stated here as a
  7a→10a sweep range.)
- **"Artifacting Delay"** — record a **GESTURE in CASCADE, REELS, or COLLAGE moving the TIME knob** → the delay
  pitch-bends/glitches on the move, then loops the artifact. (COLLAGE especially self-edits destructively.)

## Exact control-position facts (quotable knob ranges)
- **FILTER style ranges (TEXTURE → FILTER knob):** *"High pass is from about 2p–5p, Low pass is about 7a–10a,
  and bandpass is between the two."* → so **LPF lives ~7–10 o'clock, HPF ~2–5 o'clock, band-pass in the middle.**
  (Useful concrete dial range for the rig's "roll off the banjo top" LPF move.)
- **DRIFT / RATE LFO shape:** *"At 7a, the LFO is a sine wave"* with **randomness increasing as the knob turns up.**
  → keep RATE/DRIFT near **7 o'clock for a clean sine** (chorus/vibrato); turn up for tape-random instability.
- **SENSITIVITY:** *"7a is the highest fidelity"* → **7 o'clock = cleanest/least-triggered**; turn up to lower the
  breakup/swell-trigger threshold.
- **CAPTURE:** *"recorded at unity dry volume by default"* → looper prints at unity, no built-in gain change.

## Why it matters for this rig
"Reverse Strings" (SWELL→VIBRATO→REVERSE) and "Mo Betta Repeats" (delay→SWELL→SQUASH) are turnkey banjo-as-lead
"recorded-wrong" lead voices. The FILTER position ranges give the banjo-top-roll-off LPF an actual dial range
(~7–10 o'clock). The "Gated Reverb" reorder (SPACE before SWELL) is a free extra reverb behavior for the
mono→stereo re-expansion job. All are module-order/position recipes the author flags as starting points.
