# Ed O'Brien (Radiohead) — Chroma Console on the 2025 touring board

The brief's flagship name. Confirmed on Ed O'Brien's **2025 Radiohead touring pedalboard** (built for
the 2025 European dates).

## Placement & role
- **Center board** of his three-board, GigRig-G3S-switched rig. The center board is the **"Pitch and
  Glitch" / weird-ambient-effects** section.
- The Chroma Console sits in the **top row alongside the Hologram Microcosm**, plus the Sonic
  Research Turbo Tuner and a Diamond Tremolo TRM-1. Other center-board neighbors: Red Panda Tensor,
  EHX Pitch Fork+, DigiTech Whammy Ricochet, Gamechanger Plus.
- Left board = overdrives; center = weird ambient/pitch/glitch (Chroma + Microcosm live here);
  right = delays (multiple Boss DD-3/DD-3T, OTO BIM, Line 6 DL4 MkII, OBNE BL-44 Reverse).

> **Direct rig parallel:** O'Brien runs **Chroma Console and Microcosm side-by-side on one ambient
> board** — exactly the two-Hologram setup in this rig (Microcosm on Middle/Texture, Chroma as the
> tape/lo-fi character box). His board is third-party confirmation that the two coexist without
> redundancy, both treated as "weird ambient texture" tools rather than primary voices.

## Honest coverage note
- **No song-specific or knob-specific Chroma settings are published** for O'Brien — the King of Gear
  archive lists the pedal and board section but no per-module dial-ins, and the MusicRadar pedalboard
  walkthrough is general. Treat "Ed uses it for X sound" claims skeptically; what IS verified is
  placement + role (center ambient/pitch-glitch board, paired with Microcosm).
- O'Brien's documented general approach is on-aesthetic for this rig: he favors **sounds "that
  didn't sound like the guitar,"** atmospheric/ambient layers, and "you have to invest time into
  those sounds" — i.e. building evolving textures, not stock presets. That philosophy is the right
  lens for the Chroma here (GESTURE-automated, slowly-evolving beds).
- Hologram has publicly acknowledged O'Brien using the pedal (Hologram Facebook "shout out from
  Radiohead's…" post), so the sighting is genuine, not fan speculation.
