# Artist / notable uses — HONEST (it's thin, and that's the truth)

## Bottom line
There is NO signature-artist mythology for the Del-Verb. It's a 2023 curated convenience box, not a tone-chaser's grail, and no credible named artist is publicly identified as "a Del-Verb player." Multiple searches (MusicRadar, Guitar.com, Anatomy of Tone, equipboard, forums) surface only ANONYMOUS users (small-board players, HX Stomp owners outsourcing time-fx, home-studio producers). Don't invent endorsements — there aren't any to cite.

## Where UAFX artist endorsements actually live (not the Del-Verb)
- UA's named-artist marketing is concentrated on the AMP-emulator pedals, e.g. the **UAFX Lion** demoed by **Pete Thorn, Blues Saraceno & James Santiago** at Sound City. James Santiago (UA senior sound designer) is the recurring face. These are AMP pedals, not the Del-Verb.
- The credible "endorsement" for the Del-Verb is UA's own **in-depth demo roster** (YouTube) and UA staff/sound-designers — promo, not artist signature.

## The honest sonic-lineage framing (how to talk about "who uses this sound")
- The Del-Verb's engines are lifted from the **Golden Reverberator** (Spring '65 / Plate 140 / Hall 224) and **Starlight Echo Station** (Tape EP-III / Analog DMM / Precision). The Golden won a **TEC Award for Best Effects Pedal (2022)**. Anyone citing "the Golden's plate" or "the Starlight's EP-III" is, sonically, citing the Del-Verb's voices.
- Starlight/Golden ship with artist-flavored VOICINGS/presets (names like "Bionic Limb," "Dream or Memory," "Monday Drive," "Solo Magic" appear in UAFX preset lists) — but the contributing artists aren't clearly attributed and these are pedal presets, not a player roster. Treat as marketing color, not endorsement.
- The deeper hardware these model HAS a famous-user history you can honestly invoke: Lexicon 224 (the '80s digital-reverb sound on countless pop/alt records), EMT 140 plate (60+ years of records), Echoplex EP-III (huge in '60s–'70s). That heritage transfers to the Del-Verb's voices — but it's the ORIGINAL boxes that have the famous users, not this pedal.
