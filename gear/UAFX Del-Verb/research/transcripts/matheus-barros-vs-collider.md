## Del-Verb overview (spoken)
- Three delays: **Tape EP-III, Analog DMM, Precision.** Three reverbs: **Spring '65, Plate 140, Hall 224.**
- "Designed to be very simple on the surface — knobs for delay time, feedback, mix, color, modulation, and a reverb level control — while still offering deeper voicings via the mobile app."
- Two footswitches; assignment set in the UA app.
- **Footswitch-mode tradeoff (corroborated):** "One of the limitations is that you cannot have both a dedicated tap-tempo switch AND independent reverb on/off." (i.e. Delay|Tap mode forces reverb always-on; you give up independent reverb toggle to get tap.)
- App = voicing changes + firmware. "Awesome if you want to expand the tone of each algorithm."

## Sound-test method
Reverb alone → add delay; clean (via UA Lion amp model) and overdriven (via a Protein-style drive). Three sounds per pedal. Demonstrates the Del-Verb sounds great with minimal fuss and takes drive in front cleanly.

## Verdict (directly relevant to this rig's benched-travel framing)
- **Del-Verb wins on:** "great ambient sounds quickly, lush reverb and delay, great sounds straight out of the box… one good ambient pedal that just works… classic tape/plate/hall ambient textures — the Del-Verb nails them. Premium sounds." Pick it "if board space is limited and you want one pedal that just works."
- **Collider wins on:** experimental textures (reverse, shimmer, big swell, pads), onboard presets (8 + 128 via MIDI), full MIDI in/out, expression, routing — "more room to grow" for an ambient specialist who tweaks.
- Net: confirms the rig's logic — Del-Verb is the *grab-and-go premium ambient box*, not the deep-editing centerpiece. A Collider/H90-class tool owns the experimental/preset role.
