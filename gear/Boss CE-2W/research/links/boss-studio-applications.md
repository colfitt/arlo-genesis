# A Chorus of Tones: CE-2W Studio Applications (BOSS official)

Boss's own studio-applications feature by Fletcher Stewart. This is the most authoritative source of *named-tone settings* for the CE-2W and the canonical "two-amp wall of sound" technique. RATE/DEPTH positions below are quoted from the article.

## Named settings (quoted)

**1) Subtle "skeletal arpeggios" / vintage SoCal tone (mono, Standard/CE-2)**
- Mode: CE-2 (Standard) — the "classic" voicing he prefers for mono
- RATE: 9 o'clock ("a crawl")
- DEPTH: "just shy of noon"
- Result (quoted): "subtle movement that enhanced skeletal arpeggios." Subtle high-end animation. Used for a vintage/SoCal death-rock-adjacent clean tone.

**2) Stereo "Wall of Sound" — two-amp studio technique (2015 session)**
- Mode: CE-1 Chorus ("flick to the new CE-1 setting" for "deeper pitch modulation and slightly darker character")
- RATE: 10 o'clock
- DEPTH: 2 o'clock
- Technique (quoted): "Two Victory Amps are cooking in the live room… send the signal directly into the front of both amps from CE-2W… track in stereo and hard pan the channels, leaving room for a dry track to shoot right up the middle."
- This is the wet/dry stereo split feeding two amps, hard-panned, with a separate dry track up the middle.

**3) Vibrato — psychedelic passages**
- Mode: CE-1 Vibrato ("the icing on the cake")
- No specific RATE/DEPTH given; described for "weird and wiggly" psychedelic passages.

## Other applications
- "Running loops and samples through the pedal to simulate the magic of a dying old cassette tape." (Lo-fi sampler/loop treatment — directly relevant to running OP-1/Digitakt/MPC through it.)

## Pull-quote
- "The CE-2W is still the chorus to beat, even after the boutique boom of the '00s."

## Notes
- The two named numeric settings (9 / just-shy-of-noon, and 10 / 2 o'clock) are QUOTED from Boss. The mode pairings (mono=CE-2, stereo-wall=CE-1 Chorus, psych=Vibrato) are explicit in the article.
