Gigs & Guitars · review (+ corroborating settings from TDPRI/Guitar Chalk threads) · 2016+

Practical power-user tips: mode-switch layout, sweet-spot dials, the no-mix-knob limitation, and a double-tracking trick.

MODE-SWITCH LAYOUT (confirmed across sources):
- **Far LEFT = Standard (CE-2)** classic chorus.
- **MIDDLE = CE-1 Chorus** (deeper, more spatial/dramatic).
- **RIGHT = CE-1 Vibrato ONLY — no dry signal** (pure pitch wobble).

SWEET-SPOT SETTINGS (from reviews + TDPRI setting-recommendation thread):
- Classic chorus: **RATE ~10 o'clock (low-medium), DEPTH ~2 o'clock or higher.** (Some prefer DEPTH just shy of noon for subtler.)
- Vintage-CE-2 match (Legendary Tones A/B): RATE ~11, DEPTH ~1–2.

LIMITATIONS / GOTCHAS:
- **No MIX knob.** Unlike many modern choruses you can't dial back wet/dry blend on the front panel — the effect is at its fixed wet level (in mono OUTPUT A you get the factory-fixed wet+dry mix; to get LESS effect you must run lower DEPTH or use the stereo split / a dummy plug, not a blend control).
- **Quiet for analog** — reviewers repeatedly note it's "silent with virtually no additional noise whether off or on."

DOUBLE-TRACKING TECHNIQUE (reviewer): record standard L and R DRY takes, then record a middle take with **CE-1 Vibrato ON** — layering a vibrato take between two dry takes gives a thick, three-dimensional studio sound without washing everything in chorus.

STEREO: one cable in, two out → run each output to its own amp for "a thick, wide chorus sound" (wet on A / dry on B). Buffered bypass feeds the same signal to both outs when off.
