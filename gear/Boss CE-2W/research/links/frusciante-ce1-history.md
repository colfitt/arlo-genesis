The canonical VERIFIED CE-1 artist story — and a direct historical parallel to using a CE pedal as a SPLITTER, not just a chorus.

VERIFIED FACTS:
- **John Frusciante (RHCP) used a Boss CE-1 Chorus Ensemble** from ~1990 to 2010, and again on the 2022 *Unlimited Love* tour. It is one of his constants alongside the Boss DS-2 and Ibanez WH-10 wah.
- **He primarily used the CE-1 as a SIGNAL SPLITTER, not (just) for chorus.** Studio: split guitar between a Marshall Major and a Marshall Bass head. Live: split between a Marshall Major and a Marshall Silver Jubilee. A Californication-era board image shows two CE-1 units.
- Heard as a chorus "on many tracks... such as the ending of 'Under the Bridge.'"

PRIMARY QUOTES:
- Frusciante, Guitar Player (April 1990): "I use a Boss Distortion pedal, an Ibanez wah-wah, and a big old ugly Boss chorus pedal."
- Frusciante, Vintage Guitar (April 2009): "Another pedal is a Boss Chorus Ensemble, which **I use to split the signal in my rig.**"

WHY THIS MATTERS HERE: the most famous CE-1 user is famous in part for using it as a SPLITTER — exactly the role the CE-2W plays as the mono→stereo split point of this rig. The CE family's stereo/dual-output design has always doubled as a signal router; Frusciante is the historical proof.

LINEAGE CONTEXT: the CE-1 (1976) was the first standalone chorus pedal ever made and the first Boss product, derived from the Roland JC-120 amp's chorus circuit. The CE-2W's "CE-1 Chorus" and "CE-1 Vibrato" modes recreate it.
