# TDPRI — "Boss CE-2W setting recommendations" (community sweet spots)

Community thread of CE-2W owners sharing their favorite RATE/DEPTH/mode dials. Body was paywall-gated on direct fetch (tollbit redirect → HTTP 402); values below are distilled from the WebSearch result summary of the thread, which surfaced concrete quoted settings. Treat as community-cited (🔵), not artist.

## Concrete settings (distilled / quoted-as-summarized)

**CE-1 Chorus mode — "slow and steady / flangy"**
- Mode: CE-1 Chorus
- RATE: low — "first quarter of turn, sometimes backed off all the way" (≈ 7–9 o'clock, often fully CCW)
- DEPTH ("mod"): "two o'clock or higher"
- Use: slow, deep, almost-flanger-adjacent wash.

**CE-1 Chorus mode — classic favorite**
- Mode: CE-1 Chorus
- RATE: "10–noon"
- DEPTH: "always at noon"
- Note: "The CE-1 chorus mode is definitely mild and not as deep as many expect, and really needs depth turned up."

**CE-2 (Standard) mode — tempo-matched chorus**
- Mode: CE-2 (Standard)
- RATE + DEPTH: start at noon, then "match song tempo with rate (usually turning up from noon until it sounds good)."

**CE-2 (Standard) mode — subtle sweetener**
- Mode: CE-2 (Standard)
- RATE: ~11 o'clock
- DEPTH: below noon (12:00)
- Result: "a very subtle sweetening effect on the tone."

## General consensus (quoted-as-summarized)
- "The CE-2 setting is slightly more subtle than the CE-1 setting, but many prefer the CE-1 setting."
- Emphasis on experimenting with RATE/DEPTH to taste; no single right answer.

## Relevance to the rig
- The "CE-1 Chorus, RATE fully CCW, DEPTH 2:00+" recipe is the strongest new community match to the drone/oceanic goal — slowest possible movement, deep modulation, into the Deco/Board 2 wash.
- The "Standard, RATE 11, DEPTH below noon" subtle-sweetener corroborates the on-disk everyday "on for the split" widener.
