Sound on Sound · review · 2016

Authoritative source on the CE-2W's stereo behavior and buffered-bypass routing — the two technical points the rig depends on.

KEY POINTS (quoted / distilled):
- **Stereo = wet/dry split, not L/R:** across all three modes "one output is effected and the other remains dry." Feeding two amps gives "a very three-dimensional modulation." The reviewer notes this mimics the classic live workaround of a mono chorus into one amp while the other runs unprocessed.
- **Buffered bypass keeps both legs fed:** when disengaged "the pedal...offers a buffered bypass feeding the same signal to both outputs when bypassed" — both downstream destinations receive consistent signal whether the chorus is on or off. (This is exactly why it works as a permanent stereo split point: the stereo pair never collapses when the chorus is switched off.)
- **Modes:** CE-1 Chorus (original CE-1 chorus, now with adjustable depth) · CE-1 Vibrato (pure vibrato) · Standard/CE-2 ("a more subtle modulation than the CE-1" plus "a subtle mid boost").
- **RATE/DEPTH range:** identical across all modes, sweeping from "an extremely slow and gentle, almost phase-like chorus to a fast warble."

TAKEAWAY: SoS independently confirms the two non-obvious behaviors — wet/dry (not L/R) stereo, and buffered bypass feeding both outs when off — that justify the CE-2W as a buffer/splitter, not just a chorus.
