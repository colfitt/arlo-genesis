# Premier Guitar — Boss CE-2W Waza Craft Chorus review (mode-by-mode + DEPTH ranges)

Best mode-by-mode sonic map. No numeric clock positions; Saufley describes the **DEPTH knob in thirds of its range**, which is the most useful settings guidance here.

## DEPTH-range map (Standard / CE-2 mode), quoted
- **First third of DEPTH range:** "chorus textures subdued enough to be subliminal in a multi-instrument mix." → the subtle stereo-widener / always-on zone.
- **Middle of range:** classic '80s chorus tones.
- **Faster RATE + higher DEPTH:** "feel icy and regular in their undulations."

## CE-1 Chorus mode
- "deeper, queasier, and at certain settings seemingly just a little murkier" — good for "menacing, in-your-face chorus tones." Darker/thicker than Standard.

## CE-1 Vibrato mode
- **Mid-range RATE/DEPTH:** "tend[s] to take on the sound of a metallically tinged rotary speaker."
- **Lower DEPTH + lower RATE:** "space-drunk, nitrous textures." (Subtle pitch shimmer close to a second chorus voice.)

## Settings takeaways for this rig
- For the everyday "on for the split" widener: **Standard mode, DEPTH in the lowest third** (subliminal), slow RATE.
- For rotary-ish drama: **CE-1 Vibrato, RATE + DEPTH around noon** (rotary-speaker zone).
- For seasick/drone: **CE-1 Vibrato, low RATE + low DEPTH** ("space-drunk, nitrous").
- All DEPTH guidance is QUOTED-as-ranges, not exact clock positions.
