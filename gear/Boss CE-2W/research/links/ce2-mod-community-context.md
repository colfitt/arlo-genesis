Context on the deep CE-2 MOD COMMUNITY — what people change on the original CE-2, and how that frames what the CE-2W reissue already gives you. (CE-2W-SPECIFIC internal mods are NOT well documented; this is the classic-CE-2 ecosystem.)

COMMON CE-2 MODS (clones / vintage units):
- **Depth mod** — extends the modulation depth range beyond stock.
- **Vibrato mod** — adds a true vibrato (pitch-only, dry removed) mode the stock CE-2 lacks.
- **Bass / "bass version" mod** — re-voices for bass (preserves low end).
- **Blend / Effect-Level (mix) control** — adds the wet/dry blend knob the stock CE-2 (and CE-2W) doesn't have (Tonepad "Effect Level" mod).
- Source kit reference: "Delyk's 2nd Choral Kit has a bass version mod, vibrato mod, depth mod, and a blend control."

BIAS / TRIMMER (for clones, e.g. Pork Barrel): set RATE to midpoint and DEPTH to maximum, then adjust the internal trimmer for "maximum chorus effect with minimal distortion." (This is the BBD bias adjustment; vintage CE-2s have a similar internal trimmer.)

WHY THIS MATTERS FOR THE CE-2W: the reissue already bakes in TWO of the most-requested mods — **variable DEPTH in the CE-1 modes** and a **dedicated vibrato mode** — so a CE-2W owner doesn't need the depth/vibrato mods. It still lacks the **blend/mix** mod (no front-panel wet/dry control). NOTE: vintage purists on forums argue the original CE-2 has a "slightly thicker swirl / more midrange push" and find the CE-2W a touch brighter on top — an honest minority view; A/B reviews (Guitar World, Legendary Tones) call the match near-perfect.

CIRCUIT: the CE-2 (and CE-2W Standard mode) is built on the Panasonic **MN3007 BBD** — better headroom and noise specs than the later MN3207 (ElectroSmash teardown).
