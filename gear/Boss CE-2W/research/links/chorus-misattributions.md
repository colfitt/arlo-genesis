TWO common CE-chorus MISATTRIBUTIONS, corrected with sourcing. Keep these honest — neither famous "chorus" tone was a Boss CE.

=== MYTH 1: Andy Summers / The Police — "Walking on the Moon" ===
NOT a Boss CE chorus. It was an **Electro-Harmonix (Deluxe) Electric Mistress FLANGER** that Summers used on "Walking On The Moon" and "Message In A Bottle." EHX's 2024 signature "Walking On The Moon" pedal is a recreation of the Electric Mistress, not any chorus.
- The chorus-vs-flanger confusion is exactly why people misremember it as a CE: Summers himself, shown his new signature flanger, said **"Who calls it a flanger? I would have called it a chorus."** (MusicRadar). So the artist conflates the two effects — but the device was a flanger, not a Boss chorus.
- Studio-recording complication (cross-source, theFretBoard / Roland AU / Police equipment wiki): assistant engineer accounts also mention an **Audio & Design SCAMP S24 ADT / AMS rack** flanger-delay on the original recording. Either way: a flanger/rack effect, **NOT a Boss CE-1/CE-2**.

=== MYTH 2: Kurt Cobain / Nirvana — "Come as You Are" ===
NOT a Boss CE chorus. It was an **Electro-Harmonix Small Clone** (analog chorus). Per Ground Guitar (https://www.groundguitar.com/kurt-cobain-gear/kurt-cobains-electro-harmonix-small-clone/): Cobain bought a Small Clone ~early 1990 (eventually owned ~five) and used it for the "watery" intro of "Come As You Are" and other *Nevermind* tracks. Chain detail: Small Clone went AFTER the Boss DS-1 distortion, so the chorus processes an already-distorted signal — the thick warbling quality, not a clean shimmer.

VERIFIED CE USERS (for contrast, cite as users not specific tracks): John Frusciante (CE-1), James Honeyman-Scott / Pretenders (CE-2), and David Gilmour, Johnny Marr, Robert Fripp, Rory Gallagher, John Mayer, Keith Urban (CE-2, per Guitar World).
