User thread on the CE-2W's stereo/wet-dry output and how to exploit it. Confirms the wet/dry-not-L/R behavior and surfaces two practical tricks.

KEY TIPS (quoted / distilled):
- Per the manual, in stereo mode the **mono (OUTPUT A) carries chorus-only, the second jack (OUTPUT B) carries the dry/direct signal** — it is a wet/dry hard split, NOT two modulated L/R voices. (Original question by Jonathanthomas83 wanting "100% wet (chorus only) with no dry in the mix.")
- **Dummy-cable trick (Beexter):** to force the wet-only split you can plug a dummy/dead plug into OUTPUT B — "Whenever I've needed a dummy jack plug, I've either used a George L's plug or one of those adapter plugs that convert a 1/8″ plug to a 1/4″ plug — worked fine." Plugging *anything* into OUTPUT B flips OUTPUT A to wet-only, so a dead/dummy plug on B = 100% wet chorus out of A.
- **Simpler route to wet-only (ICBM):** "The CE-1 vibrato is the wet-only sound of the chorus." CE-1 Vibrato mode removes the dry path entirely, so you get a pure modulated (pitch-wobble) signal from OUTPUT A in mono with no patching trick — but it's vibrato character, not chorus.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR A SPLIT-POINT RIG: plugging OUTPUT B is what *activates* the wet/dry split; with only OUTPUT A you get the normal mixed chorus. So using the CE-2W as a mono→stereo split point requires both jacks populated, and the dummy-plug trick is the lever that controls whether A is mixed or wet-only.
