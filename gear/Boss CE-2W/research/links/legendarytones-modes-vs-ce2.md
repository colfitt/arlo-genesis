Legendary Tones · review (A/B vs vintage CE-2) · 2016

Hands-on A/B against an original CE-2, with concrete dial settings and a clear mode-by-mode breakdown.

REISSUE ACCURACY (concrete):
- "To get the CE-2w to sonically match my CE-2 with a typical setting of Rate at 11 o'clock and Depth at 1 o'clock, the CE-2w only needed to be set just a little higher in depth to 2 o'clock to mimic the original perfectly." So the reissue is near-identical; if anything dial DEPTH a hair higher to match a vintage CE-2.
- Unlike the old stereo CE-3 "which contained some brittle artifacts," the CE-2W keeps the warm tone — no brittleness.

MODE CHOICES (when to use each):
- **Standard (CE-2):** warm, soft, subtle classic chorus. Traditional/subtle applications. Sweet spot RATE ~11 o'clock, DEPTH ~1–2 o'clock.
- **CE-1 Chorus:** uses a SHORTER delay time than CE-2 → "a more spatial effect" with "more dramatic intensity." Best when "you really want to get those seasick effects going" — pronounced, movement-heavy textures.
- **CE-1 Vibrato:** pure vibrato only; the added RATE/DEPTH give "greater versatility" vs the original fixed CE-1.

OUTPUT: stereo = "a dry signal in one and effected-only in the other"; fully mono-compatible using OUTPUT A alone.

TAKEAWAY: for a subtle always-on chorus, Standard at RATE 11 / DEPTH 1–2 is the documented vintage-CE-2 match. Flip to CE-1 Chorus for the dramatic/seasick spatial wash.
