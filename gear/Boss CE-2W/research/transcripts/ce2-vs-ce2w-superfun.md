# Boss CE-2 vs CE-2W — which one is better?? (TheSuperFunAwesomeHappyTimePedalShow)

Welcome to The Super Fun Awesome Happy Time Pedal Show — Gabor and Alex. We're going to check out some Boss choruses today: an **old Japanese CE-2 versus the Waza Craft CE-2W**. See which one we like better.

Setup: into a Creme Brulee (amp by Cherry Tone), a single 12 cab with a Greenback, and in the effects loop a cab loader doing a basic 4x12 cabinet IR. Guitars: an Airline Twin Tone (two humbuckers, three-way) by Eastwood, and later an Eastwood Sidejack Baritone Deluxe.

## CE-2 (Standard) mode, both at 12 o'clock

The CE-2W is on the **Standard setting (not the CE-1 setting), everything at 12 o'clock**. Starting with the old one: there's definitely a bit more volume coming out, it's more pronounced as an effect — a little bit faster and deeper.

Then **DEPTH all the way up** on both: "there's definitely more there in the old one." Then **RATE all the way up but DEPTH halfway**, and the opposite (**DEPTH all the way up, RATE all the way down to almost flanger-ish**).

## CE-1 setting vs the old CE-2

Let's see how similar the **CE-1 setting** is to the old CE-2. The CE-1 setting is much faster — the CE-2 (Standard) can't go that fast on the rate; if you run something deeper it's faster and deeper. But the *sound* is more similar to the old CE-2 when using the CE-1 setting. The waveform seems a bit more intense: from a prior CE-2W demo they found the normal CE setting is more of a sine wave, whereas the **CE-1 setting seems to be more of a "rampy" (ramp-like) waveform**.

## Baritone

On the Eastwood baritone (clean), there's an '80s vibe — police-ish "Every Breath You Take" territory (though that's actually a flanger). On the **Standard setting they had to push DEPTH up a little to match the older one**. It doesn't go quite fast enough to do the Leslie/vibrato-ish top thing in Standard mode.

## Verdict

Both hosts preferred the **old CE-2 over the CE-2W in the CE-2 (Standard) setting** — the old one has "more vibe / character," not a depth or rate thing. But they agreed they **prefer the CE-2W in the CE-1 setting** out of all options — "the CE-1 setting is killer on this." They joked the CE-2W in Standard "sits somewhere between the [CE-]1 and the [CE-]2 — call it a CE-1.5."
