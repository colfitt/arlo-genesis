# BOSS CE-1 vs CE-2W Comparison — Leon Todd

What's up everybody, I hope you're all doing really well. We're going to take another look at this mighty grey box, the Boss CE-1 Chorus Ensemble. I did a video with this guy a couple of weeks ago and lots of people agreed it's their favorite chorus pedal, and quite a few wanted to hear it side by side with the **Boss CE-2W**, the Waza Craft version of the classic CE-2 chorus — probably a lot of people's second-favorite chorus of all time.

What's cool about this CE-2W is that it has a **CE-1 chorus and a CE-1 vibrato mode** in there on the little toggle switch. When you put it in the middle you get the CE-1 chorus mode emulated, and all the way to the right you get its vibrato mode emulated. So let's hear some clean and dirty sounds. I'm going to use the CE-1 as the reference point and dial in a tone in stereo with it, then re-amp the same DI track through the CE-2W and see how close we can get. Starting with a clean tone.

## The wet/dry stereo behavior

If you really want to hear the full effect, make sure you're listening in stereo, and you can hear that **both of these do the cool thing where one side is dry and the other is wet with the chorus effect** — that's how you get that classic '70s/'80s style stereo chorusing.

Let's hear it on a dirty sound — same track, this time through a pair of dirty amps.

## Verdict

Let me know if you thought the Waza Craft got close enough for rock and roll. In my opinion it definitely did. It even sounds like, in that CE-1 chorus mode, there's a bit of that **preamp coloration that you get from the CE-1** — that's kind of the magic of the big grey box: it's not quite ideal for running a guitar signal into it, it loads your pickups in a weird way and you get some mild distortion from that, but that's part of the fun, and it sounds like that mild grit is built into the original CE-1.

The chorus modes definitely sound spot-on, and the vibrato sounds really nice — it's actually a cool feature that you can run it as a standalone vibrato on top of the other two chorus modes. The CE-2W is obviously a lot smaller, can run off a battery or a fuel-tank-style power supply (no big cable needed), and it's also a lot quieter, which is nice. Quick little comparison; let me know what you thought.
