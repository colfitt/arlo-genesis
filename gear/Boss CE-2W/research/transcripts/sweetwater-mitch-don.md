# BOSS CE-2W Chorus Waza Craft — Sweetwater (Mitch Gallagher & Don Carr)

Hi, I'm Mitch Gallagher. Welcome to Sweetwater Guitars and Gear. This time out, a reissue of a vintage chorus pedal from Boss. Today I'm joined by Don Carr from here at Sweetwater. We are checking out the CE-2W, the new Waza Craft version of the Boss CE-2 chorus.

Boss originally introduced the chorus effect inside their Jazz Chorus amplifiers. There was basically one dry speaker with no effect, then the other speaker was modulated with a chorus effect. It created a beautiful lush stereo effect, especially with both of them being side by side. The combination of the two is a really cool effect.

They released that in a pedal format in the CE-1, which was the original chorus pedal. Then they came out in 1979 with the CE-2. The CE-2 went through several different revisions: a made-in-Japan version with a black label about 1979 to 1984, then a green label manufactured in Taiwan, ending production around 1991.

So what Boss has done with the Waza Craft version is they've combined the sound of that original made-in-Japan CE-2 chorus pedal along with a switch that gives us the original CE-1 sound as well as the original CE-1 vibrato effect. With the CE-2W you've got stereo outs, which is really cool — that's like the original CE-1. The original CE-2 was mono only. The CE-1 had a dry out and a wet out; this has the same thing, but you can also apply that to the CE-2 sound, so it gives us a new sound we didn't have with the original pedal.

The other thing that's been improved here is it has the new Waza Craft buffer in it. It's a nice sparkling clean buffer, very uncolored, so it gives you a very natural sound. Some of the early Boss pedals had a different buffer that wasn't quite as clean sounding, a little more colored — a little different in the top end and the mids.

## Walkthrough — the three modes

We'll begin by turning the pedal off. We're playing through a couple of '65 Deluxe Reverb reissues, and Don has a Music Man Cutlass guitar with single-coil pickups. (Dry sound demonstrated.)

Now we'll engage the effect with **the RATE and DEPTH controls both set to about 12:00**, and we're in the **CE-2 (Standard) setting**. That gives us a really rich, chiming, sparkling tone.

Now we'll switch over to the **CE-1**, the original chorus effect — the same thing found in the original Jazz Chorus amplifiers. How would you describe that tone compared to the CE-2? Warmer. Definitely different in the mids and a little rounder on the highs; the bottom end seems a little different, and the modulation is slightly different too — it seems just a bit wider, because they had that original stereo effect in mind when they created the CE-1 (as opposed to a combined mono like the CE-2).

Now we switch over to the final setting, the **CE-1 vibrato position**. This gives us pitch modulation out of one channel and a dry signal out of the other. We can emphasize that even more by **turning up the DEPTH**. If we turn that down we'll get a more subtle effect; we can slow it down a little as well. It almost has a tremolo feel to it — that cyclical pitch vibration. If you push it too far it starts to sound out of tune, which you'd expect with an effect like that. But when you dial it back, especially in stereo, you get a really wide spread with two amplifiers.

## Vs the original CE-2

Just for fun, we have an original CE-2 chorus pedal (bought in 1982, black label / black screw, one of the very early made-in-Japan ones). This is mono only, so we're hearing it through one amplifier.

Bypassed, right off the bat you can hear a difference compared to the earlier dry tone — that's the buffer. The original makes the mid-range a little more cloudy, a little darker on top. (Note: any two CE-2 pedals from this era sound a little different — components age, and there's a trim pot inside that can be set.) Engaging the original CE-2, then switching to the new CE-2W: the new one is a little cleaner, has a little less coloration in the mid-range — doesn't have that cloudiness — and the top end is a little more open. But it has the same modulation pattern; the chorus effect itself is the same. What you hear is the older circuitry plus the much cleaner buffer in the CE-2W. Don also noticed the original CE-2 drives the amp a little differently (mid-range pushes a bit harder).

Then the CE-2W's CE-1 mode in mono, and the vibrato (VBR) effect in mono, were demonstrated.

## Summary
If you're looking for that vintage Boss effect with some extra versatility — the added stereo outputs, the CE-1 mode, the CE-1 vibrato mode — the CE-2W is a great choice. With the Waza Craft buffer it's clean sounding, open sounding, has a lot of sparkle. A great-sounding chorus pedal that adds vintage analog tone to your rig.
