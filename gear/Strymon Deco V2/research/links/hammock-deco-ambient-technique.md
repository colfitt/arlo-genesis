strymon.net · Strymon "Pedalboard Feature: Hammock" (interview re: the album *Mysterium* + *Columbus* film score) · Marc Byrd & Andrew Thompson
Hammock = the most on-aesthetic confirmed Deco user for this rig: ambient/cinematic post-rock, "sustained walls," baritone + Jazzmaster territory. The Deco is a CONFIRMED part of their signal chain (not just an Equipboard sighting).

## Confirmed Deco placement (their full board, in order)
Volume pedal → Tuner → Keeley compressor → old ProCo RAT → Expandora → **Strymon Deco** → hand-built custom overdrive → Boss Octave → Fulltone Choral Flange → Strymon Flint → Line 6 DL4 → Boss RV3 → Strymon BigSky → Strymon TimeLine.
- Note placement: **Deco sits early, right after the dirt (RAT/Expandora)** and before the time/reverb stack — i.e. tape coloration ON THE SOURCE, same philosophy as the Pedalxly Board-1-closer slot.
- Caveat (their words): boards are "almost always in flux" / modified per project.

## Deco-specific settings/technique
- **None documented.** This interview names the Deco in the chain but gives NO Deco knob settings or Deco-specific song examples. (Honest flag — the concrete quotes below are about their *method*, which transfers to the Deco, not about the Deco itself.)

## Transferable ambient-wall technique (the gold — direct quotes)
- **Manual knob-riding as performance:** textural decaying sounds were "usually created by **one of us manually turning the knobs while the other played guitar or unplugged cables**." → the wall is two hands: one plays, one rides the effect. Maps to riding the Deco's Saturation/Lag/Wobble (or an EXP pedal) live rather than a static preset.
- On *Mysterium* their go-to color pedals were the **BigSky CHORALE** machine and the **TimeLine LO-FI** setting for "textural decaying type sounds" — the Deco feeds that lo-fi/decay aesthetic from the front.

## Rig note
Hammock validate the architecture: tape (Deco) early on the source, big time/reverb late. For the Pedalxly rig, the transferable move is **performing the Deco** (ride Wobble/Lag or EXP) during a sustained chord rather than parking it — and using its saturation as the warm front-end that makes the downstream lo-fi/decay (Generation Loss, etc.) read as intentional.
