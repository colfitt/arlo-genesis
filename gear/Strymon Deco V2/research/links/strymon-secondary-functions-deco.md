## Secondary functions (real values)
- **High Trim** — a **4 kHz shelf**: "goes from flat at the minimum setting, to a 4K low pass at its maximum setting." → use to tame fizz / darken the saturation top end. (This is the high-frequency tame the dossier didn't name.)
- **Low Trim** — a **low-cut filter**: "goes from 15 Hz at minimum, to 150 Hz at maximum." → use to tighten/clean rumble and mud before the stereo send. Directly useful for taming a flabby baritone or banjo low-mids.
- **Doubletracker Boost/Cut** — **±3 dB** level for the doubletracker side, "add some extra oomph when engaging that side."
- **Auto-Flange Time** — sweep speed for the press-and-hold auto-flange. CCW = faster, CW = slower. "Virtual studio engineer" managing the reel.
- **Wide Stereo Mode** — sends "the reference deck to the left output, and the lag deck to the right output." Engaged by turning the **Wobble knob right of 12:00** (in the secondary/Live-Edit layer).
- **Studio Mode** — extra headroom for hot line-level sources (DAW/synth/mixer insert). Power-up: hold both footswitches, then turn **Lag Time right of 12:00**. (Leave OFF/Normal for the instrument-level CE-2W feed in this rig.)

## How presets/favorites load (this page does NOT cover it)
- This page has **no info on saving/recalling presets, the Favorite switch, or MIDI program change.** Preset loading lives in the V2 manual + the Strymon Deco support page (V2 = 300 MIDI preset locations; Favorite switch stores one preset; full Program Change over TRS-MIDI/USB-C). The Live-Edit secondaries above **are saved per Favorite/MIDI preset.**

## Rig note
High Trim (4 kHz shelf) + Low Trim (up to 150 Hz cut) are the two cleanup tools for keeping the tape coloration from re-brightening the already-tamed banjo and from getting flabby on the baritone before the Board-2 handoff.
