(Scale 0–10. "DT Vol" = Doubletracker Boost/Cut secondary.)

## 1. Classic Tape Saturation Boost — "vintage warmth + soft compression"
- Saturation 8 · Volume 6 · Tone 5 · Lag Time 0 · Blend 0 · Wobble 0 · DT Vol 0
- Saturation engine only (doubletracker effectively off). The everyday warm-up / push voice.

## 2. Watery Analog Chorus — "lush, warbled stereo chorus, classic tape modulation"
- Saturation 3 · Volume 5 · Tone 4 · Lag Time 6 · Blend 7 · Wobble 8 · DT Vol 7
- Chorus zone with heavy wobble. On-aesthetic for dreamy/lo-fi.

## 3. Slapback Echo for Leads — "tight slapback with stereo width behind a lead"
- Saturation 5 · Volume 6 · Tone 6 · Lag Time 4 · Blend 5 · Wobble 2 · DT Vol 6

## 4. Stereo Flange for Rhythmic Pads — "1970s tape flanging, sweeping movement-rich stereo image"
- Saturation 4 · Volume 5 · Tone 5 · Lag Time 9 · Blend 5 · Wobble 7 · DT Vol 8
- (Note: high Lag Time = longer offset; for true through-zero flange the maker walkthrough says short Lag Time + Blend 50/50 — so this "flange" is really a long wobbly chorus. Cross-check against the official walkthrough.)

## 5. Crushed Lo-Fi Mono Texture — "degraded, compressed, reduced highs + width"
- Saturation 9 · Volume 4 · Tone 2 (dark) · Lag Time 3 · Blend 4 · Wobble 9 · DT Vol 5
- High saturation + dark Tone + max wobble. The closest of these to the rig's doom/lo-fi aesthetic.

## Technique tips (no artist refs on this page)
- Roll guitar volume to control how hard the saturation drives; bridge vs neck pickup changes the saturation character; place Deco before reverb/delay.

## Rig note
For the Board-1 doom/glue role, #1 (saturation-only warm-up, ride guitar volume) and #5 (crushed lo-fi, dark tone) are the relevant starting points. Verify by ear — these are not authoritative values.
