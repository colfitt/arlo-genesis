Power-user guide to the **original/V1** Deco's secondary functions. Captured here mainly to RECONCILE a V1-vs-V2 discrepancy that appears across the other research files. The author's frustration ("every knob has a secondary function unlocked by holding both footswitches... deceptively complicated") led them to make a printable overlay sticker.

## V1 secondary-function access (DIFFERENT from V2)
- **V1:** hold **BOTH** footswitches simultaneously (while powered on) to unlock secondaries.
- **V2:** hold **only TAPE SATURATION ON** until both LEDs flash (per Deco V2 Manual Rev D). The "both footswitches" gesture on V2 is instead used to ARM MIDI preset-save / Compare mode.

## V1 secondary functions (per this guide)
- **High Trim** — on the SATURATION knob (V1): "4 kHz shelf that goes from flat at minimum, to a 4K low pass at maximum."
- **Low Trim** — on the VOLUME knob (V1): "low-cut filter, 15 Hz at minimum to 150 Hz at maximum."
- **Doubletracker Boost/Cut** — on BLEND, ±3 dB, 12:00 = unity.
- **Auto-Flange Time** — on LAG TIME (CCW faster, CW slower).
- **Wide Stereo Mode** — on WOBBLE (right of 12 = on).
- **Studio Mode** — power-up: hold both FS, then LAG TIME right of 12.

## CRITICAL V1→V2 RECONCILIATION (flag for orchestrator)
The V1 had a dedicated front panel with **5 knobs** and TWO Live-Edit EQ trims (High Trim on Saturation + Low Trim on Volume). The **V2 has 6 knobs** — it added a physical **TONE knob** to the saturation side, which absorbs the High-Trim job.
- On **V2** the manual lists only **FOUR** Live Edit secondaries: **Low Trim (on TONE), Auto-Flange Time (LAG TIME), Doubletracker Boost/Cut (BLEND), Wide Stereo (WOBBLE)** — plus the two MIDI toggles (Clock Sync on TYPE, MIDI Expression on VOICE).
- There is **NO separate "High Trim" Live Edit on V2.** Any source (incl. Strymon's own legacy "secondary-functions-deco" page and this daftparagon post) that lists High Trim as a hidden knob is describing the **V1**. The frequencies (4 kHz high-trim shelf / 15–150 Hz low-cut) are still a useful mental model for what the V2's TONE knob (bright↔dark) and Low Trim (high-pass) are doing, but the control layout differs.

VERDICT: For the V2 in this rig, use the front-panel TONE for top-end shaping and the Live-Edit Low Trim (on TONE, accessed via TAPE SAT hold) for the high-pass cleanup. Ignore "High Trim" as a V1-only secondary.
