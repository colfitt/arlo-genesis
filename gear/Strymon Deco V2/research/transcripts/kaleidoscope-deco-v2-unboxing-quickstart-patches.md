## The five V2 Quick Start "Sample Settings" patches (names verified against Strymon's Deco V2 Quick Start Guide, Rev A)

The host runs these in order. Names and the official knob-diagram positions are below (positions read from the Quick Start Guide diagrams, since the host's verbal settings are unreliable). All use factory-default Live Edit values.

1. **WIDE WOBBLES** — Doubletracker only (Saturation off). Voice: classic. Type: **bounce**. Lag Time: ~1–2 o'clock (chorus/short-slap region). Blend: ~12. **Wobble: high (~3–4 o'clock).** A heavily warbling, wide tape-chorus.
2. **WET SLAPBACK CASSETTE** — Doubletracker only. Voice: **cassette**. Type: **sum**. Lag Time: ~1 o'clock (slapback). Blend: ~12. **Wobble: low (~7–8 o'clock).** A clean-ish wet slapback. (Host: "basically just turning the Wobble way down.")
3. **ON THE EDGE** — **both engines on.** Saturation: ~10 o'clock (light drive). Voice: classic. Type: **invert**. Lag Time: ~1. Blend: ~11 (favor reference). Wobble: low. A driven tone with a through-zero-adjacent flange edge.
4. **WORN OUT DECK** — both engines on. Voice: **cassette**. Type: **bounce**. Lag Time: ~2–3 o'clock (longer). Blend: ~12. **Wobble: high.** The "degraded/pitchy" worn-tape sound.
5. **PRETTY TAPE FLANGE** — both engines on. Voice: classic. Type: **invert**. Lag Time: ~9–10 o'clock (**flange region**). Blend: ~12 (strongest flange). Wobble: low–mid. The clean musical flange.

## Host's takeaways (audible, paraphrased)

- Guitar used: a Tom Anderson custom into a Positive Grid Spark amp; later mentions running a real amp/delay.
- He was **most interested in the Tape Saturation as a form of drive.** Comparing **cassette vs. classic** voice: he found the difference "pretty subtle," but said the **cassette** version "fattens it up / sounds more compressed" and the **classic** gives "a cleaner saturation that sounds more like a typical overdrive box." He preferred the **cassette** version of the saturation settings.
- On the saturation as always-on: with the level matched, kicking the pedal on vs. off was "a nice add-on" even at modest settings — fattening without obvious distortion.
- On the **Doubletracker** he was candid that it's not intuitive from familiar-pedal terminology: with Blend low the effect is subtle ("fattening it up a little"); adding **Wobble** quickly becomes "almost like a vibrato effect," and he wasn't sure he'd want it that strong. He noted you can use it like a chorus "without being so obviously chorus," and that moving Lag Time while playing is "kind of fun." Honest summary: "I don't feel like I fully understand how I would use it yet... I'm going to need to do some actual tracking with it" — i.e. the Doubletracker rewards studio experimentation more than a quick demo.
