## Why he uses it

"I like the Deco V2 because it's one of those pedals that I can get a two-for-the-dollar type usage out of — it has the saturation side, but it also has the doubletracking side, which I can manipulate to be either slapback delay, flanger, or chorus-type sound. All in all I get two sounds in one pedal." The tape-saturation drive is different from his other drives because you can pick whether it acts as a **classic** or a **cassette** saturation. For him the **cassette** saturation is "just like those iconic overdrive tones from the records I love from the 50s and 60s — I can replicate that at my fingertips."

## His Tape-Saturation setting (the "50s/60s overdrive" tone)

- **Saturation:** maxed out
- **Voice:** Cassette
- **Volume:** right under 12 o'clock
- **Tone:** right above 12 o'clock
- Guitar: Fender American Vintage II 1961 Strat, bridge pickup, volume & tone maxed
- Into the front end of a Marshall JVM.

He demos this saturation, then kicks in slapback delay (the Doubletracker), then stacks both — "that slapback-plus-saturation sound is one of my favorite sounds of all time."

## His Doubletracker setting (the "retro" doubling/chorus)

- **Blend:** 12 o'clock
- **Wobble:** "probably six or seven o'clock" (i.e. nearly off / very low)
- **Volume:** right past 12

"It just has a very retro feel that I like, but it also keeps the characteristics of the old machines' delays." He points out that even with the low wobble setting, on longer/sustained notes you can hear "that little wobble in between" — an effect that many other pedals don't even offer as a possibility. He calls the chordal artifacts "funky, vocal funkiness" that comes from the type of doubletracking.

## On the three Doubletracker Types (his ranking)

He demos Bounce → Invert → Sum:
- **Bounce** is his favorite — "it can have like a ping-pong effect, but it's all still coming from just the doubletracking side." He describes Bounce as the "everything maxed-out" / most pronounced flavor.
- **Invert** sits in the middle.
- **Sum** is "the cleanest one to me — the sum portion is the one where I can't really hear the wobble and the lo-fi functioning as much."

His mental model: going Bounce → Invert → Sum, you progressively lose the "warble/lo-fi" character (Sum = cleanest, Bounce = most warbly/characterful). That's why he prefers Bounce.

## V2-specific notes he calls out

- The **Cassette vs. Classic voice** choice is a V2 feature (V1 had no voice switch on saturation).
- The **Tone knob** he sets just above noon is V2-only.
