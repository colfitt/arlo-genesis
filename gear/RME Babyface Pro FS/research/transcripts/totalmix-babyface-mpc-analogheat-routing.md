RME Audio · Mixing & Routing in TotalMix FX (Babyface Pro, MPC, Analog Heat, Circuit) | RME Blueprints #01 · 2018-05-22

Welcome to RME Blueprint, where we show advanced setups for your RME audio interface.

A while back, we talked about the integration of old ADAT gear into your setup with the Digiface USB. Now we are taking it a step further by showing you an actual setup for mixing and recording with the Babyface Pro and TotalMix FX.

For this demo we are using the Akai MPC Live and the Novation Mono Station as our main sound sources. The whole session is mixed in TotalMix FX and is then sent to the Elektron Analog Heat for a bit of glue and some lovely analog filtering and distortion. Finally the whole processed mix as well as the individual instruments are being recorded into Ableton Live.

This is how it's done. We plugged the MPC and the Mono Station straight into the channels of the Panasonic mixing desk. The channels are routed straight to the ADAT outs and go directly into the ADAT inputs of the Babyface Pro. Therefore the Panasonic just acts as an ADAT preamp and has no other function. (Note for our rig: this is the "ADAT expander" trick — feed extra sources into the Babyface's two-channel analog ceiling via the optical ADAT input.)

All the mixing and routing is taking place within TotalMix FX. The connected instruments show up in TotalMix FX as AS 1/2 for the MPC and ADAT 3 for the Mono Station.

To send both signals to the Analog Heat, we simply select the first output channel AN 1/2 on the bottom row of TotalMix FX — which are the XLR outputs of the Babyface Pro — and turn up the individual channel faders. (This is the core submix workflow: click the destination hardware output in the bottom row, then raise the source faders in the top/middle rows to route them to it.)

After the routing was set up in TotalMix, we connected the XLR outputs with the stereo input of the Analog Heat. To get the processed signal back into the computer we hooked up the stereo output of the Analog Heat back into the XLR inputs of the Babyface Pro. Hardware inputs AN 1/2 are therefore the wet signal of the Analog Heat. (This is the classic external-hardware-FX insert loop done entirely in TotalMix routing — out the XLRs, through the outboard, back in the XLR ins.)

To get more punch and clarity, we also used the EQ in TotalMix FX.

With this setup we stay as flexible as possible because we can record the individual tracks and the processed mix at the same time. So we can come back to the individual tracks and re-edit them, if something wasn't as expected or we would like to re-arrange some parts.
