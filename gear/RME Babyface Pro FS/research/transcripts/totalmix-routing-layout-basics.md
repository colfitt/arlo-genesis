RME Audio · TotalMix FX for Beginners - Routing & Layout Basics · 2018-12-03

TotalMix FX is one of the most comprehensive tools for audio engineers and musicians alike — from recording yourself at home to huge live shows with over 300 audio channels, everything is possible. It comes standard with all RME audio interfaces, so if you master TotalMix FX once you're good to go for the rest of your life.

THE THREE ROWS. When you open TotalMix FX you have three rows at your disposal.
- First row = the HARDWARE INPUTS, i.e. the physical inputs of your audio interface. On the Babyface Pro used here that's 12 hardware input channels: the two XLR inputs, the instrument inputs, and the ADAT inputs. (Channels can be linked into stereo pairs or used unlinked as mono.)
- Second row = the SOFTWARE PLAYBACK channels — the audio channels you assign from your DAW, or from your Mac/PC/iOS device. With an Ableton session playing, the first software playback channel (renamed "Ableton") shows signal; the DAW can assign any of these twelve channels.
- Bottom row = the HARDWARE OUTPUTS — the physical outputs of the interface. On the Babyface Pro: the XLR outputs on the back, the headphone output on the side, and the four ADAT outputs.

MIXING & ROUTING (the key benefit). Before mixing, select SUBMIX mode under Routing Modes. There are two types of input source — hardware input channels and software playback channels — and on the bottom you have hardware outputs. What makes TotalMix special is that EVERY OUTPUT CAN HAVE ITS OWN MIX. Highlight one output channel, then increase or decrease the level sent to that particular output by turning the input-channel fader or the software-playback fader up or down.

Worked example: with the Ableton session playing, the "Ableton" software playback channel shows signal. To hear it on the main speakers, click the main speaker output (it highlights), then turn up software playback channel 1 — now the main speaker gets the Ableton signal. To also hear it on headphones, click the headphones output and turn up the software playback channel there. You do this INDEPENDENTLY for every output — that independence is the whole point of the three-row submix model.
