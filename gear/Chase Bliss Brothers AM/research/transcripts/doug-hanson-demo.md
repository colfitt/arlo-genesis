Doug Hanson · Chase Bliss X Analog Man Brothers AM | Demo · 2025-04-15

Hey everyone, today we're checking out a collaboration between Chase Bliss and Analog Man. This is Brothers AM, a King of Tone in the Chase Bliss format with added features.

Brothers AM has two identical multi-mode gain channels, and channel one always flows into channel two. Each channel has controls for gain, volume, tone, and presence — presence being a secondary control accessed by holding down the foot switch and turning the tone knob.

Each channel has three modes. Boost is a clean boost capable of low-gain overdrive. Overdrive is a soft-clipping overdrive. Distortion is a hard-clipping distortion.

In addition to the two gain channels, Brothers AM has a two-voice treble boost that comes before the gain channels. With the toggle in the center position, the treble boost is off. To the right is a classic Rangemaster-style treble boost, and to the left is a treble boost with increased treble.

The right set of dip switches sets which parameters will be controlled with expression or CV. The other set of dip switches allows for more customization:
- The high gain dip switches increase the gain of all modes by about 25%. When high gain is engaged, the LED color displays red instead of green.
- Momentary bypass lets you engage and disengage the channels momentarily.
- When presence link is engaged, the tone knob will also simultaneously control the presence.
- The high gain, momentary bypass, and presence link options are available for each channel.
- The master dip switch turns the volume two knob into a master volume control, which is useful when exploring different stacking combinations so that volume jumps are minimized.
- The bank dip switch changes over to an alternate bank of onboard presets, giving you access to preset slots three and four.

Brothers AM is expression, CV, and MIDI capable. All parameters, including dip switches, can be controlled with MIDI.
