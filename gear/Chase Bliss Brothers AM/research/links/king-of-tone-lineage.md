# King of Tone lineage + verified famous users

The AM's real heritage and "famous user" base — the KOT itself, inherited by association.

## Lineage (verified)
- Built from the **Marshall Bluesbreaker** (1991 pedal, itself chasing the Bluesbreaker *amp* tone). **Jim Weider of The Band** brought his frustrations with the Marshall to Mike Piera, who modified it for him.
- Piera didn't just upgrade parts — per analogman.com: "we totally redid the circuit with different diodes, chips, capacitors, treble trimpots, modes — pretty much everything." Uses a **JRC** chip ("warm yet clear") and open-sounding diodes (clarity over compression).
- Added a **second volume + footswitch** and **internal DIP switches** for **clean boost / overdrive / distortion** per side, plus an **internal treble trimpot**. Released **~2003**.
- Piera's own framing: "It's not a heavy overdrive — it's **less gain than a Tube Screamer**, so it's not going to change your amp." → the AM keeps this transparent, sub-TS character.

## Stacking, from the source
analogman.com factory default: **yellow (clean boost) feeds red (OD)**. Piera: "A clean boost **into** a distortion pedal will add more distortion." Conversely: "A clean boost **AFTER** a dirt pedal WILL increase the volume, without adding a lot more distortion." → This is precisely the rig's plan: AM's BOOST *after* the Hizumitas = level/cut, not more fuzz.

## Scarcity (why the AM exists)
~**4–6 year waitlist**; 20–30 people/day join; ~50–60 hand-built units/week. Used V2s top **$2,000** — second only to the Klon among ODs. Demand exploded after a **2017 "That Pedal Show"** feature (~200 daily orders). Variants: **Prince of Tone** (single, China-made), **Duke of Tone** (2022 MXR collab, mini — MXR's best-seller by 2023), **Brothers AM** (2025 Chase Bliss).
