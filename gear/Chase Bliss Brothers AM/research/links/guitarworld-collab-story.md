# The collaboration story (Joel Korte × Mike Piera) + verified artist data

The single best source for the AM's actual "story." There is no AM-specific touring-artist mythology (2025 pedal) — the real narrative is the development collab + the KOT lineage.

## Development timeline
- Joel Korte (Chase Bliss) reached out to Mike Piera (Analog Man) **early 2020**: he was discontinuing the original Brothers and believed the King of Tone circuit was perfect for that 2-channel layout. Korte: "I became obsessed with the idea and decided to reach out… 'Would you be open to this if I worked on a prototype?'"
- **Five years** prototype-to-market (2020 → 2025). Piera: "From the first prototype, I was confident that the tone and feel were there."
- Korte on why the original Brothers got replaced: it was polarizing — "people didn't gel with the circuits as much as I hoped."

## The circuits
- **King of Tone** = Piera's Bluesbreaker-derived OD; 3 modes (Clean Boost / OD / Distortion).
- **Beano Boost** = Rangemaster treble booster. Piera: "The Beano Boost — the Rangemaster/treble boost **into** the King of Tone is my favourite sound. It's thick and crunchy." (This favorite pairing = why the booster is fixed at the front feeding Ch1.)
- **Akihabara transistor hunt:** in a Tokyo vintage-surplus shop (where Piera started building in the 1980s) they found "a bin of transistors that looked germanium." Korte didn't use that exact part but it led him to "a family of silicon transistors in old-school **TO-18** packages, one of which… absolutely perfect."

## "Mike's Sound" preset
One factory preset = Piera's favorite KOT setting — his signature tone made instantly accessible.

## VERIFIED artist data (honest)
Piera names the genres/acts that adopted KOT-family pedals: **Nashville/country session players**, **blues artists**, heavier rock acts including **Dream Theater** and **Porcupine Tree**, and **jam bands** as a significant user base. ⚠️ These are KOT-ecosystem users Piera cites, **not** documented Brothers-AM-specific touring rigs — present them as lineage, not AM endorsements.
