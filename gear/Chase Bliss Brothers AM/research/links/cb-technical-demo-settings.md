chase bliss · Brothers AM – Technical Demo (Joel Korte, founder) · 2025-04-22

Settings and behaviors stated directly by Chase Bliss founder Joel Korte in the official technical demo. These are the most authoritative "how it's meant to be used" notes outside the manual.

## Stock channel modes (quoted)
- "The way that these are set up stock generally is that **Channel 1 is set for OVERDRIVE and Channel 2 is set for BOOST**." (This is the standard King of Tone configuration.)
- Mode character: BOOST = no diodes in the path, more headroom, open. OVERDRIVE = "classic soft-clipping musical overdrive that the King of Tone is so known for." DISTORTION = "harder and more aggressive clipping," and naturally quieter/more compressed (compensate with VOL).

## Joel's personal go-to (quoted)
- "Probably my favorite space I live in a lot is to have **the Rangemaster on and going into the BOOST**." (Treble booster engaged → Channel 1 in BOOST.) Note: "the noise floor increases with the treble booster, and that's normal — we're emphasizing upper-mid-range and trebly frequencies."

## Presence (quoted)
- Ships **fully counter-clockwise (all the way down)** on both the original King of Tone and the AM. "I prefer to have the presence control all the way down."
- Dev tip: "Whenever I was confused by how something was sounding, it was usually because I had the presence up and forgot to turn it back down."
- Counterpoint worth knowing: "Some folks have written the [King of Tone] pedal off until they turn the presence control UP, and that really opens up for them." So: start down, but try it up if the tone feels closed-in.

## HI GAIN dip
- Normal mode = green LED; HI GAIN engaged = **red LED**. Can be set per channel, saved per preset.

## PRES LINK dip (quoted use)
- Links the TONE and PRESENCE controls so they move together — "makes for a more dramatic tone control." Demoed in DISTORTION with gain way up: goes from very dark to very bright across the TONE sweep.

## MASTER dip (quoted use)
- Turns **VOL 2 into a master volume.** Demonstrated: with Channel 2 OFF, VOL 2 still controls the level. "Master mode can be super useful, especially if you are wanting to crank the output of Channel 1 going into Channel 2." Without MASTER on, popping Channel 1 in (when set loud to slam Channel 2) "is so loud you could get a huge volume jump" — the MASTER dip prevents that.

## External control
- "All these knobs can be controlled by expression... either individually or simultaneously, accessible with the left side of the dip switch bank." MIDI jack available for those who want it.
