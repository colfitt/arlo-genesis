# Guitar.com "Big Review" — usability + control tips

The most usability-focused review. Verdict: Brothers AM is "by some distance the easiest Chase Bliss pedal to use" — deep but intuitive — and it "nails the vibe of the King of Tone original and captures even more of the little nuances than the impressive MXR Duke of Tone." Frames it as an "expanded counterpart," not a 1:1 clone: it expands KOT's core sounds rather than replicating Mike's hand-built process. "It could easily be the last drive pedal you ever buy." Price flagged as "not cheap" at $399 (still ~half a real KOT).

## Concrete tips distilled
- **Dip switches** do three headline jobs: (1) **Hi-Gain** per channel = adds ~**25%** gain to that channel; (2) **momentary** footswitch mode for quick on-the-fly boosts; (3) **link Presence to the Tone control** (PRES LINK). Dips also reconfigure footswitches for momentary bypass, repurpose VOL2/TONE as **master volume / presence**, save & switch presets, and assign expression control.
- **Hidden PRESENCE per channel:** hold that channel's **footswitch** and turn its **TONE** knob to set it. **Default/recommended = all the way down.**
- **Internal trim pots** (pop the back cover, "classic Analog Man style"): tweak **impedance, output, and bias** of the boost/treble-booster circuit. The reviewer calls one a **treble/presence trim** that "should be adjusted to taste in conjunction with the tone control on top as it can make a big difference."
- **Treble-booster flavor switch:** brightness/sparkle mode vs. **classic Rangemaster-style upper-mid emphasis**.
- **Presets:** 4 onboard + **128 via MIDI**; expression can control any parameter (configured via dips).

## Rig relevance
The "treble/presence internal trim adjusted in conjunction with the top Tone knob makes a big difference" note is the practical key for clawing banjo cut back through the downstream wall.
