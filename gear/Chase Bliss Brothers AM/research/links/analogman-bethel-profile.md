# Analog Man / Mike Piera human-interest profile (the artist-story backbone)

The richest narrative source for the Piera/KOT story — use for color in the "artist/lineage" section since the AM has no artist mythology of its own.

## The story
- Mike Piera spent the **mid-1980s as a software engineer in Japan** before pivoting to vintage guitar gear; found a knack for reverse-engineering and improving badly-made pedals; founded Analog.Man (Bethel, CT) around 2000.
- **King of Tone (~2003)** came from modifying the Marshall Bluesbreaker for **Jim Weider** (then of The Band). Piera: "I didn't realize how many people wanted to have a good overdrive pedal. I had no idea."
- **Six-year waitlist** today; demand exploded after the **2017 "That Pedal Show"** feature (~200 daily orders). He hand-builds **50–60/week**; 20–30 people join the list daily. Piera on its status: "Yeah. It's one of the icons."

## Verified famous users (this source)
**John Mayer** and **Jason Isbell** named explicitly as KOT users.

## Business philosophy (color)
KOT priced ~**$325** despite used units fetching $700–$1,000+; Piera resists price hikes: "best sound, hand-made, with the best parts at a reasonable price… musicians, these days, it's really hard for them to make money." → Context for why the **$399 Brothers AM** ("half a KOT, available now, no waitlist") is framed as democratizing the circuit — Piera's stated aim was "to get our pedals in the hands of more players."
