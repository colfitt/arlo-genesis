Concrete, copyable two-channel and stacking setups walked through in the AndyDemos video. Knob positions are quoted where given ("about halfway," "about 25%"); modes are exact.

## Rhythm/lead two-channel base setting (quoted)
- "Each channel is set up for the OVERDRIVE mode — the classic King of Tone sound. The **right channel set a little bit lower in gain and the left a little bit higher.** So we basically have rhythm and lead individually." (In the AM's layout, right = Channel 1, left = Channel 2.)
- Stacking both adds even more gain. "Even when stacked with the **gain about halfway on both**, it's not overly compressed — it still stays very detailed."

## "Organic" boost-at-end stack (quoted)
- "Use the channel on the **left as a volume BOOST** [BOOST mode] — a lot more dynamic even than the overdrive mode because we're clipping less. And use the **right channel as a heavier OVERDRIVE as our foundation.**" The boost at the end pushes the amp into overdrive — "the more organic approach."

## "Saturation" stack (quoted)
- For more saturation/compression instead of volume: "**DISTORTION mode on the left and OVERDRIVE on the right to add all the extra gain. We're not really adding extra volume boost — just more gain and compression — it's going to make everything sound meatier and bigger.**"

## Old-school treble-booster-first (quoted)
- "Treble booster first [front of circuit], keep that in OVERDRIVE or even DISTORTION and then go into the second channel." Bare-bones version: treble booster → **BOOST mode on the right** to hear the two booster voices. The brighter booster voice "shaves off even more low-end... a lot more bright and strident, really good with stacking with other effects."

## Presence demo (quoted)
- On a spankier 25.5"-scale guitar: "hold the bypass switch and **reduce the presence to about 25%** — it's a lot smoother even with a higher gain setting." Then adjust tone to taste with a little more gain.

## HI GAIN dip (quoted)
- "OVERDRIVE on both sides with identical settings — you'll hear what the HI GAIN dip switch adds. A cool way of getting a little bit more out of a pretty classic-sounding overdrive pedal."

## Expression setup (quoted)
- "Simple activation of the **gain control on Channel 1**, with the classic treble booster engaged. For the foundation sound, **Channel 2 with OVERDRIVE selected** (amp clean by itself)." Sweep gain 1 with the expression pedal.
