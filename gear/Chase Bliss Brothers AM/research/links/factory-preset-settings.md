## The 4 factory presets

Presets are recalled with the toggle between the footswitches: left = preset 1, right = preset 2, middle = live. All controls AND dip switches are saved per preset.

### DEFAULT BANK (BANK dip OFF)

**PRESET 1 — "THE ANALOG MAN"**  *(dip: HI GAIN 1 ON)*
> "Mike Piera's preferred settings, adapted from his King of Tone. A solid starter for experiencing the pedal as intended. Kick on Channel 2 when you need a lift."
- This is Mike's Sound / the flagship always-on starter. Channel 1 is the always-on voice with HI GAIN 1 engaged (so the bypass LED is red); Channel 2 is the kick-on lift.
- Per the technical demo, the stock King-of-Tone channel config is **Channel 1 = OVERDRIVE, Channel 2 = BOOST** — a reasonable read for this preset's modes (manual does not print the exact knob values).

**PRESET 2 — "SUNNY SKIES"**
> "A mixture of everything Brothers AM has to offer. Treble boost into distortion into overdrive for a sound that is bright, focused, and charged."
- Routing intent: **Treble Booster ON → Channel 1 = DISTORTION → Channel 2 = OVERDRIVE.** Bright/focused. (Treble booster engaged = NOT the middle/off position.)

### ALT BANK (BANK dip ON → presets 3 & 4)

**PRESET 3 — "2-IN-1"**
> "A useful performance combo. Channel 2 is your always-on overdrive and Channel 1 is there to boost it beyond its limits. Turn on Channel 1 when you want more bite."
- Routing intent: **Channel 2 = OVERDRIVE (always-on); Channel 1 = BOOST** used to overload Channel 2. This is the manual's "OVERLOADER" idea saved as a preset.

**PRESET 4 — "BAD BROS"**  *(dip: HI GAIN 1 + HI GAIN 2 ON)*
> "As big and brash as Brothers AM can get. It's an overflowing distortion made by stacking and cranking everything."
- Both channels on, both HI GAIN dips engaged, modes/gain cranked. The maximum-saturation "wall" preset.

## Manual's named starter recipes ("Finding Your Sound")

Two-channel ideas (channels used as two separate pedals, swap by tapping both footswitches at once):
- **CLEAN CHANNEL, DIRTY CHANNEL** — "low-gain overdrive on Channel 1 and a mid-gain distortion on Channel 2 — two clear, distinct stages, but with a common character." (Mimics a classic two-channel amp.)
- **AMP PUSHER (double boost)** — "BOOST mode on both channels with the same or similar settings, but set one to have a louder output." Make your amp's own overdrive the focus. Take it further by engaging the treble booster on Channel 1 and/or pushing up the GAIN: "one clean, transparent boost, and another that offers some edge and a richer character."
- **MOMENT MAKER** — one channel for an airy/nostalgic specialty sound, the other for your always-on sound.

Stacking ideas (both channels on, influencing each other):
- **OVERLOADER** — "OD for Channel 2 and BOOST for Channel 1. Gradually turn up the volume on Channel 1 and notice how it then oversaturates Channel 2 and pushes it further than it can go on its own. Leave Channel 1 off by default and pop it on when you need an extra charge."
- **EXPANDER** — "the first channel to OD and the second channel to BOOST with the TONE knob turned up... the second channel enhances the first, amplifying it and opening it up. Turn on Channel 2 when you want to increase intensity without adding distortion."
- **COMBO** — "both channels to BOOST and using Channel 1 to push Channel 2... a versatile, loud, and open-sounding drive. We recommend turning on the MASTER dip switch while exploring boost stacking. This will let you turn up VOL 1 without having disruptive jumps in volume."

## Treble booster (manual)
3-position toggle, linked to Channel 1, sitting at the very front of the circuit:
- **Up = "MORE FORWARD AND BRIGHT"** (shaves more low end, brighter/strident).
- **Middle = OFF** — "The original King of Tone did not have this Rangemaster-style circuit, so leave the toggle in the middle position if you want that classic sound."
- **Down = "CLASSIC RANGEMASTER"** (Beano Boost voice, upper-mid/treble emphasis).
- Popular uses (quoted): "STAND OUT IN A MIX," "GET MORE FROM YOUR AMP," "BRIGHTEN UP," "CREATE CUTTING LEAD TONES."

## Presence (manual)
- Hidden per-channel control: hold that channel's footswitch and turn its TONE knob.
- **"The recommended and default setting is turning PRESENCE all the way down"** (fully counter-clockwise). Ships there on both the KOT and the AM.
