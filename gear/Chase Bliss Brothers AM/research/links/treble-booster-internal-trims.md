# Official: adjusting the treble-booster internal trims

Chase Bliss's own post on the three internal trimmers for the treble booster. Headline: "Do you wish you could adjust the treble booster on Brothers AM? You can — there are **3 tiny trimmers that let you control the bias, loudness, and impedance**." Links to an official YouTube walkthrough that "breaks down how to do it and each of the controls."

⚠️ Facebook/the embedded video are not deep-fetchable; the three trims and their directions are documented in the **Brothers AM manual "Internal Trimmers"** section (authoritative). Captured here so the orchestrator knows CB officially blesses these tweaks (not a third-party mod).

## The three trims (from the AM manual, "Internal Trimmers" — all treble-booster)
1. **Input Impedance** — default **fully clockwise / max**. Lower it (counter-clockwise) so the booster **"behaves much better after a buffer."** → directly relevant: CB Clean + Colour Box buffers sit upstream of the AM. If the front Rangemaster feels off after those buffers, back this trim off.
2. **Output level** — default **noon**. CW hits the KOT circuits harder (more drive into Ch1); CCW for a more "unity" feel.
3. **Bias** — default **noon**. Adjusts headroom / how symmetrical the Rangemaster break-up is — squeeze more headroom or a more symmetrical/asymmetric germanium-ish breakup.

Use a **very small flathead**, tiny movements — easy to damage. These mirror the internal trim philosophy of the real King of Tone / Prince of Tone (which expose treble + bias trims internally).

## Rig relevance
**Input Impedance is the load-bearing trim for this rig** — with two CB buffers in front, backing it off CCW is the documented fix so the treble booster sits right after a buffered front end.
