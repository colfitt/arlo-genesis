Launch-coverage piece framed "More Ambient than Hologram?" — the clearest published positioning of the Qi against the Hologram Microcosm (and Chase Bliss Mood).

## The Microcosm / granular comparison (the relevant verdict)
- The Qi's **Phrase Sample mode "creates rhythmic grain effects akin to those found in the Microcosm Hologram or Chase Bliss Mood."** So the community/press read is: the Qi's *Phrase Sample* engine is the part that overlaps the Microcosm's rhythmic granular — NOT the whole pedal.
- The article poses "more ambient than Hologram?" but does NOT answer it — the honest takeaway is that the two are positioned as siblings, not one replacing the other. Overlap is specifically Phrase Sample vs Microcosm; the Qi's freeze-pad + four-engine wash is the differentiator.

## Routing / MIDI confirmation
- "Cascading delays, fluttering, glitch-y granular effects – parallel or serial – it's all there."
- MIDI: "The other 123 presets can be accessed through MIDI program changes… In addition, almost every pedal parameter can be changed through MIDI CC messages." (3 onboard + 123 over MIDI = the 128 total; confirms near-total CC coverage — your only path to expression-style sweeps since there's no EXP jack.)

## Takeaway for the rig
- Confirms the dossier's division-of-labor call: let the Microcosm own rhythmic granular motion; use the Qi's Phrase Sample sparingly so the two don't compete, and lean on the Qi's freeze-pad (which the Microcosm doesn't do the same way) as its unique job.
