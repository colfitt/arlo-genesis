## Power-user / workflow notes
- Series vs Parallel both available on the fly via Flow; routing is the central creative lever.
- Grain modes confirmed in practice:
  - **Phrase Sample**: turning the **X knob to minimum syncs grain playback to the Delay time** → rhythmically locked grains (great for tempo-married glitch).
  - **Grain Cloud**: random grain triggering, **X sets grain density** (dense → sparse).
- **Tone** = resonant LPF; counter-clockwise removes highs from the WET effects only.
- **Reverb (Space) applies to wet effects ONLY, not the dry signal** — so in Parallel with dry blended in, your dry stays clean/defined while only the wet gets washed. Useful for keeping a banjo/baritone fundamental present.

## MIDI / presets
- 3 onboard presets via footswitch; up to 127 (128 incl. live) recallable over MIDI.

## Firmware gotcha (real-world, NEW detail)
- **First firmware update was cumbersome**: on Windows it required manually installing a driver and updating it via Device Manager before the updater would see the pedal. Subsequent updates are easier once the driver is configured. (Walrus's updater is the Chrome/WebUSB walrusaudio.io flow — on Windows the WebUSB device may need a manual driver assignment the first time.) No specific firmware version number was named.

## Owner starting-point recipe (corroborated by a Sweetwater owner review)
- Widely repeated "just get sound fast" recipe from owners: **put EVERYTHING at noon, set Grain Playback to 2x, then dial in to taste and use tap tempo to match your BPM.** "Everything at noon + granular at 2x" is the canonical hands-off ambient starting point — 2x adds a +1-octave shimmer that flatters most material. Then pull Space back from noon if it's mushing the delay.

## Manual gripe (real owner friction)
- An owner complained the printed manual is "horrible with its colorful, 'artistic' design… repetitive images, inconsistent text alignment… cumbersome and confusing to read." Practical note: the layout fights you when hunting CC numbers / freeze gestures — budget time to map it once.
