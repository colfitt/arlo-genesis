Best written review on the THREE freeze gestures, the dominant reverb, and the Parallel-routing fix. Review-grade rather than forum, but the most concrete on power-user mechanics — worth keeping.

## The THREE freeze options (clarified — there are three, footswitch-driven)
1. **Delay Freeze** — hold the Tap/Osc footswitch → "nicely saturated" snowball that builds over several seconds (delay self-oscillation).
2. **Grain Freeze** — hold the center (Freeze) switch → "instantly creating a hypnotic loop of pure texture." This is the headline pad-maker.
3. **Reverb Freeze** — long-hold center switch → transforms the grain loop into a "galaxy-sized stereo wash" (ramps Space to 100%).

## Power-user setup advice
- **Tone knob: keep fully open to start**, then "yanked down for dramatic shifts in and out of darkness — particularly effective when you want to add some movement to an endlessly looping ambience." So Tone is your hands-free expression substitute on a frozen pad (sweep it instead of an exp pedal you don't have).
- **Reverb dominance (concrete):** the reverb "has a tendency to eat everything you put in front of it — even at halfway it will mush out fairly strong delay repeats." This is the key quantified data point — even at NOON the Space already mushes strong delay repeats. For this rig (Qi → Dark Star), that means keep Space well below noon if you want the delay/grain to survive into the Dark Star.
- **Fix = Parallel routing**, "whereby the four effects are processed separately then summed together at the end" — keeps engines discrete instead of pre-drowned.

## Ergonomics / limitations flagged
- "Unless you enjoy playing the guitar while hunched over," it's a desktop-tweak pedal — lots of knob movement, no per-engine footswitches for manual on/off.
- Effects "can soon get predictable if you're not creative" — i.e. the magic is in the gestures, not set-and-forget.
- This review didn't dwell on MIDI or the clipping/level issue.
