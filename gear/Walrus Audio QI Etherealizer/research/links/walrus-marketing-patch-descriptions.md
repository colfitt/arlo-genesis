walrusaudio.com / Qi instruction manual intro · Walrus Audio · 2025

Official Walrus "how to think about settings" framing — not numbered presets, but the company's own description of the two ends of the pedal's range, which maps cleanly onto patch territory. Directly quoted from the product copy / manual intro:

> "Go from a traditional chorus with a little bit of delay, or go all in and let the Qi take over with unexplainable washes of glitchy and lush ambiance."

This defines the two poles every patch sits between:
- **"Traditional chorus + a little delay" pole** = the Green-preset / down-to-earth zone. Chorus is the star, Grain off/low, Delay light, Space modest, Series routing. The everyday usable tone.
- **"Let the Qi take over" pole** = max grain + freeze + big Space + delay oscillation. The galaxy-wash / glitch-cloud zone. Use Freeze long-press (reverb to 100%) and Tap/Osc hold (delay oscillation) to push here.

Engine-character notes from official copy (useful for patch-building):
- **Space**: "small room sounds at minimum to large ambient sounds with long decays at maximum." Reverb applies to WET ONLY, not dry.
- **Tone**: resonant low-pass on the wet path; CCW removes highs. (The "make it less guitar-y" tool.)
- **Grain Cloud**: random grains; **X knob = time between grains** (dense clusters → sparse accents).
- **Phrase Sample**: peak-triggered rhythmic grains; **X knob = playback tempo; X fully min syncs grain repeats to the Delay time.**
- **Stereo chorus, Mix fully CW = VIBRATO** (officially documented hidden sound).
- **Freeze**: short-press = loop grain buffer (LED blue, play over it); long-press = ramp Reverb to 100% (LED green); long-press again releases reverb, grain keeps looping; short press releases both.
- **Tap/Osc hold** = Delay Feedback to max → delay self-oscillation.
- **Bypass long-press** = clear the grain buffer.

These are the official building blocks an orchestrator can combine into named patches. No factory knob NUMBERS published here — the only knob-value-level factory data is in the Red/Green/Blue diagram on manual p.5 (see yvette-factory-presets-rgb.md).
