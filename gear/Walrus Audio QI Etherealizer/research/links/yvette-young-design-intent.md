truthinshredding.com (+ guitar.com, guitarworld.com) · Yvette Young / Walrus Audio press · 2025

Yvette Young's design intent and stated approach — the single best window into how SHE thinks about the Qi, since she co-designed it and built the Red/Green/Blue presets. All quotes below are directly attributed to Yvette in the cited sources.

**THE core design-intent quote** (labeled "the scoop direct from Yvette Young"):
> "I wanted to create something that was an instant vibe and mood machine that would take the control away from you, abstract everything you are doing, and generate pads and things you could jam over by yourself."

This is the whole philosophy of the pedal in one sentence — and it explains the Red and Blue presets exactly: FREEZE a pad, then JAM OVER IT solo. The "take the control away from you / abstract everything" framing is why the grain engine is deliberately unpredictable (Grain Cloud = random) and why RNDM playback exists. The pedal is meant to surprise the player into new melodic ideas.

**On the chorus + abstract sounds** (guitarworld.com, attributed to Yvette):
> "I'm actually soooo thrilled to get this one out and give ppl access to my fav chorus sounds and so many fun abstract sounds that inspire me."

Note: this confirms the Green preset ("THE Chorus tone") is literally HER favorite chorus — not a generic factory chorus.

**On the creative vision / why granular** (guitar.com + musicradar.com, attributed to Yvette):
> "I hope that it will inspire people to be excited to have colours and textures to play with. I think this is going to be a wonderful tool for people to create ambient sounds and add interest to their playing! I want people to think about their melodies and how they can start to sound a little less guitar-y and more magical!"

The "less guitar-y and more magical" line is Walrus's headline framing — it's why the pedal is pitched as instrument-agnostic (works on vocals/synths/any source). The Tone low-pass + Space hall are the tools for stripping "guitar-ness" out of the wet path.

**Technique implied by design (not a step-by-step she published, but consistent across all sources):**
Yvette's documented workflow on the Qi is: build a texture → FREEZE a granular pad (short-press Freeze) → improvise a math-rock-style tapped/fingerstyle lead over the self-sustaining bed. She has talked (Premier Guitar Rig Rundown context) about producing/film-scoring work pushing her toward abstraction and "thinking in a more painterly way with sound" — the granular freeze workflow is the pedal embodiment of that. The Red preset is the canonical setup for this.

Her own walkthrough video: "explore my signature pedal with me — the Walrus Qi Etherealizer!" (youtube zum5SglooYc) — sound demo begins ~12:00 min. She also has an album literally titled "qi" (yvetteyoung.bandcamp.com/album/qi).
