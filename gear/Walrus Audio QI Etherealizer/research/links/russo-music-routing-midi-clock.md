Retailer hands-on review (Russo Music). Useful for the routing framing and the MIDI-clock-sync angle that matters for a MIDI-controlled board.

## Series vs Parallel — "no wrong answers"
- Series: effects cascade (chorus → delay → granular → reverb), each influencing the next → "a more melded mix of effects."
- Parallel: effects independent → "more precise control over each effect as they do not affect one another."
- Verdict framing: "There are no wrong answers here." (Reinforces that the split-opinion on Series vs Parallel is a taste call, not a right answer — Parallel for control, Series for melded glue.)

## Reverb character
- Reverb described as "sweeter than your usual springs or halls" — ambient/dreamlike, not a realistic room. Controlled only by Space (size/depth) + Tone (wet LPF).

## MIDI clock sync (rig-relevant)
- Two 1/8" TRS Type A MIDI jacks; the review specifically calls out the value of "syncing it all up to a MIDI clock" so the **delay and granular (Phrase Sample) lock to tempo.** For a MIDI-clocked board this is the way to keep the Qi's rhythmic grains married to the rest of the rig.

## Freeze
- Freeze footswitch "captures moments before they've passed" — the core temporal/grain-freeze gesture.

## Verdict
- 10/10 ("CLASSIC COOL — masterpiece"), aimed at ambient/math-rock (Yvette Young context). Retailer enthusiasm — weight accordingly.
