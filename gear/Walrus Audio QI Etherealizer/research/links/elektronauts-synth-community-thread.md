## Real-user technical observations
- **jayhosking (Jan 22):** the **delay is "pretty unexceptional (just a clean mono delay line down the middle)."** Confirms the delay is NOT stereo-spread and not characterful — it's a clean mono tap. Don't expect ping-pong/stereo delay from the Qi; it's the clean utility delay.
- **jayhosking (Jan 22–23):** reverb "sounds great but **having mix and decay on the same knob somewhat limits its use**" — i.e. the single Space knob conflates reverb size/decay and wet amount, so you can't get a long decay at a low mix (or vice-versa). This is a concrete design limitation that explains WHY the reverb dominates: you can't dial a big space quietly. Real workaround = Parallel + low Space, or use the Dark Star for the actual big space.
- **jayhosking (Jan 22):** "favourite moments… when I was combining everything"; Parallel mode "enables independent effect paths." No "obvious catch" otherwise.

## Synth use (rig-relevant — hot source)
- **jayhosking (Jan 22):** ran it with **SH-101, Prophet Rev2, Nymphes, OP-XY, TB-303** — "will be super useful as a multi-effect that gets you into the ballpark of ambient and spacy really quickly." So synths DO work into it (with attention to level — see the separate clipping file).
- **Claid (Jan 18):** great as "a gorgeous send effect for a drum machine and/or synth" — supports using the Qi as a stereo send/aux rather than strictly inline.
- **jeye_musik (Jan 21):** theorized softer sources / fewer-harmonic tones ("8-bit sine waves or simple triangles") would granulate especially nicely — relevant to baritone-drone material.

## Comparison verdicts (community)
- **Azzarole (Jan 20):** the granular **reverse/random modes "remind me of Microcosm's best mode when phrase sample is active."** Direct user-level Microcosm overlap confirmation — the overlap is specifically Phrase Sample + REV/RNDM playback.
- **metalfalcon (Jan 22):** "**very similar to the Chroma Console with more focus on the ambient side**." Useful: community sees Qi as a Chroma-Console-adjacent playground box, leaning ambient. (Relevant since Chroma is on the End Board — run one as primary glitch at a time.)
