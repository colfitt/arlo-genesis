# The "Forbidden" mode — what the Kilt's "both toggles up" emulates

Deep-dive on the Expandora's secret high-gain mode = the Kilt's G1-up/G2-up gated fuzz.

ACTIVATION (Expandora): "flip your internal dip switches both to UP." (The Kilt maps this to both
G-toggles up.)

SOUND — concrete, and well-suited to the owner's degraded/drone aesthetic:
- "a **spitty, gated, raspy, and squealing** affair, one that's very hard to tame."
- Generates "complete sonic chaos" that **"doesn't clean up with your guitar's volume knob"** — note
  the contrast vs the Kilt's other modes, which DO track guitar volume.
- Compared to "a broken radio being played through broken speakers."
- Amp-dependent: Fender amps yield "harsh cutting tones"; Marshalls give "bassier sounds with
  extended gated feedback."

USAGE TIP: "a violent assault on your ears" rather than smooth OD; the author recommends players
"embrace that it isn't always controllable" and use it for abstract ideas / stage performance — i.e.
park it before granular/smear pedals for broken-wall textures rather than treating it as a lead voice.

ARTIST NOTE: "Billy F. Gibbons of ZZ Top famously chained six Expandoras together and likely adopted
the pedal partly for its Forbidden Mode's old-school character" (Mississippi-Delta aesthetic).
