# Bixonic Expandora — circuit, "Forbidden" mode, and famous users (incl. Billy Gibbons)

The definitive lineage roundup for the circuit the Kilt clones. CONFIRMS the artist roster.

CIRCUIT (matches the dossier):
- Shares "many similarities in wiring" with the ProCo RAT but adds a "clever design": a "special
  expander circuitry that delivers exceptional response to the pick attack and a creamy tone rich
  in a multitude of harmonics" — i.e. the opto-FET reactive/touch-sensitive gain the Kilt inherits.
- Key part: a **Sharp PC419 optocoupler** (had to special-order from the USA) — the envelope/expander
  element.

MODES (the Kilt's G1/G2 reimplementation):
- "three modes (plus one 'secret one')": **Crunch / Drive / Distortion / Forbidden Mode**.
- Forbidden: "a fun sizzling wild fuzz — but still controllable and usable for noise experimentation
  thanks to the expander circuit." (The Kilt's "both toggles up" emulates this.)

FAMOUS USERS — VERIFIED here:
- **Billy Gibbons (ZZ Top)** — the marquee user. Displayed "six (!) identical stompboxes plugged
  behind each other" on stage and attached the effect directly to his guitar; visible in the
  *From Dusk Till Dawn* film. (This is the "half-dozen Expandora stompbox stack-o-wack" Gibbons
  joked about for the darker, nastier *Rhythmeen* tone — see other sources.)
- Also documented: **Megadeth, Joe Satriani, Bootsy Collins, St. Vincent** ("surprising names").

HONEST FLAG: This source has **no Thom Yorke / Radiohead** quote (see thom-yorke-expandora.md for that).
