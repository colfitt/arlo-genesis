# Kilt 10 — GPX critical review (candid; named modes + a real settings recipe)

The most honest critical take, from a self-described Expandora fan who disliked the old Kilt.

NAMED FOUR MODES (note: GPX labels the G1↓/G2↑ combo "Distortion," slightly different framing than
JHS's "more fuzz" — useful to flag the two mappings agree on extremes, differ on the middle wording):
- **↓ ↓ = Crunch**
- **↑ ↓ = Overdrive**
- **↓ ↑ = Distortion**
- **↑ ↑ = Forbidden Fuzz**

CANDID CRITICISM OF THE OLD KILT (why the 10 matters):
- The earlier compact Kilt was "somewhat cold and dense — and not nearly bright enough or
  sufficiently expressive," likened to "a wet blanket."

WHAT THE 10 FIXES:
- Now "much more in line with the warm and expansive nature of JHS's **Morning Glory**."
- Improvements in brightness, output level, and expressiveness; the "former 'dankness'" is
  "eliminated entirely."

RED REMOTE: optional (£34); "switches the second clipping mode between blue and red settings,"
letting you toggle OD↔fuzz "without bending down."

KARLSSON'S DIALED-IN SETTING (a concrete starting point — max-character lead/fuzz):
- **Volume 3 o'clock · Gain 4 o'clock · Tone 5 o'clock · Bass-Cut = Flat · both G1 + G2 engaged
  (Forbidden Fuzz).**
