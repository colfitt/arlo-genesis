# Kilt on bass — the gap the live board doesn't cover

Bass is a legitimate, documented Kilt use (and the rig's live dirt stack isn't set up for bass).

CONFIRMED (JHS official + retailer/review summaries):
- The cut/flat toggle is the bass key: **"When in the 'flat' position, the bass response is much
  beefier than the original Expandora"** — so for bass, run **Low-Cut = FLAT** to keep low end; flip
  to **cut** only when the bass grit needs to sit in a busy mix.
- "designed to work well with both electric guitar **and bass**," "from subtle overdrive to nuclear
  fuzz."
- Repeatedly-cited owner anecdote: a player "bought this pedal to use on their guitar board and liked
  it so much they bought a **second one for their bass board** and loved it even more on bass." The
  Kilt V1/V2 is noted as "popular among bass players... especially for church musicians" (the Stu G /
  worship-guitar lineage).

PRACTICAL BASS RECIPE (synthesized from the above + the touch-sensitive circuit):
- Low-Cut FLAT · modest gain (G1 up for grit / G2 up for fuzz) · Tone to taste. The opto-FET reactive
  gain tracks bass dynamics well; back off picking to clean up.
