# Thom Yorke (Radiohead) — Bixonic Expandora

The rig-relevant lineage user. The Kilt 10 clones the Expandora circuit, so this is the
closest in-production stand-in for Thom's 2008–2009 distortion tone.

CONFIRMED (well-documented in this rig database):
- Thom acquired the Bixonic Expandora **circa 2007**; it "quickly became Thom's primary
  distortion for Radiohead's 2008 and 2009 tours in support of *In Rainbows*."
- It **replaced his ProCo Turbo Rat** — the Turbo Rat was the sole distortion on *Pablo Honey*
  and *The Bends* and much of *OK Computer*; it stayed on his board through ~2006, and he
  "ceased using it circa-2008 upon the acquisition of his Bixonic Expandora."
- Circuit tie: the Expandora "incorporates circuitry from the Rat but is capable of many more
  sounds" — i.e. RAT-derived, which is exactly why the Kilt (two-cascaded-RAT Expandora clone)
  is the right Thom stand-in.
- Tech Plank **rehoused the Expandora into a larger square enclosure in late July 2008** because
  "the small original enclosure of the Expandora likely made it easy to accidentally hit the
  knobs when stomping."
- DIP-switch setting used live: **"D1 set to Lo, D2 set Hi, and Ins set to B"** = distortion
  mode with the **bass-guitar preset**, producing "a clear yet full distorted tone."
- Best-known track: it "created Thom's distortion tone on the *From The Basement* performance
  of *Bodysnatchers*."

HONEST FLAG: No mention of **Jonny Greenwood** using or relating to the Expandora appears in this
source. Keep the Radiohead-Expandora tie to **Thom**, not Jonny.

Repo relevance: Songs/Radiohead folders (Let Down, Full Stop, Creep) — the Kilt 10's G2-up
distortion (and the bass-preset analogue: Low-Cut flat) is the practical modern route to this tone.
