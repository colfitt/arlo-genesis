# Kilt 10 — official usage tips (G1/G2 strategy, Red Remote, stacking, Low-Cut)

The authoritative how-to-use guidance, quoted from JHS.

THE G1/G2 FOUR-MODE STRATEGY (JHS's own mapping):
- "Both toggles **down** is the lowest gain setting."
- "**G1 up** gives you more overdrive."
- "**G2 up** gives you more fuzz."
- "Both toggles **up** gives you the heaviest gated fuzz." Also described as "that massive wall of
  saturated British overdrive that made the original famous."

STACKING / BOOST (the key recipe for this rig):
- **"With G1 down and G2 up, it acts like a higher gain stage boost — perfect for pushing your amp's
  front end or stacking with fuzz."** → this is the purpose-built front-end-into-Brothers / slam-the-
  Hizumitas/108 move.
- Headroom enables clean-boost use: "More headroom means you no longer have to turn your volume up
  past noon and you have more ability to use as a boost."

RED REMOTE (hands-free trick):
- "proprietary JHS Red Remote circuitry that allows you to switch the **gain toggle on-the-fly** for
  instant changes between **overdrive and distortion, or distortion and fuzz!**" (Switches G2 only.)

LOW-CUT TOGGLE:
- "The down position is **flat EQ**, while the up position is the **'cut' mode with less bass**. In
  'cut' mode lows are reduced for an overall tone that cuts through the mix."
- Modeler-friendly: works "well across a wider range of amplifiers (including amp modeler pedals and
  software)" — relevant to VG-800 / Iridium re-amp.

REACTIVE GAIN: "more touch-sensitive feel and nuance" via the updated clipping circuit.

LOW-GAIN RE-MAP (the V2 fix): "Never before has the first 25% of the gain range been so usable. You
will find transparent overdrive textures that were never before possible."

SPECS: 64 mA, **9VDC maximum** (do not exceed). Created in 2015 with Stu G.
