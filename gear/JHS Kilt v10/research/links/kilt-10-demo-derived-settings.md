=== FORBIDDEN GATED FUZZ (GPX written source, corroborated by demos) ===
- Volume 3 o'clock · Gain 4 o'clock · Tone 5 o'clock · Low-Cut FLAT · G1 UP / G2 UP.
- The heaviest gated fuzz; bright and high-output. [QUOTED from Guitar Pedal X.]
- Demo variant (Worship Tutorials): same both-up fuzz but with Low-Cut = CUT to tame the woolly/beefy low end instead of brightening. [Toggle states QUOTED; knobs not specified.]

=== EDGE-OF-BREAKUP / TRANSPARENT LOW GAIN (Worship Tutorials, Eirik Stordrange) ===
- Toggles: BOTH DOWN (crunch mode), Low-Cut FLAT. Tone ~noon.
- Gain low (in the now-usable first 25%); Volume up to compensate for the low gain (the 10's headroom makes this practical).
- Use: beef a clean amp toward edge-of-breakup; run a dirtier pedal into a cleaner amp so downstream delays/reverbs don't wash out. Eirik: "a really good transparent overdrive — not as transparent as the Morning Glory, but really good." [Toggle states + verbal gain region QUOTED; exact clock positions not shown.]

=== G1-UP OVERDRIVE (thicker drive / leads) ===
- Toggles: G1 UP, G2 down. "A little extra saturation and compression under the fingers; gets a little fizzier" — good for leads (Worship Tutorials). [Toggle state QUOTED.]

=== G2-UP DISTORTION ===
- Toggles: G2 UP, G1 down. "More fuzz" per JHS, but at lower-to-noon gain it reads as DISTORTION rather than fuzz (Worship Tutorials). Brighten with Tone, or cut lows with Low-Cut. Stu G: "G2 up is more hard-clipping distortion." [QUOTED.]

=== BASS SETTING (The Bass Channel, LsdtEvAZ16E) ===
- Best on 4-string (jazz or P); 5-string low end "gets a little wonky."
- Recommended to run a PARALLEL CLEAN LOW-END BLEND alongside the Kilt (it's a guitar pedal) to keep bass usable.
- Exploits the newly-usable first 25% of the gain sweep for subtle low-gain bass grit. [Verbal QUOTED; no clock positions.]

=== A/B BASELINE (Tone Nerds) ===
- "Everything at noon," all toggles off = the classic Kilt voice; the 10 reads brighter, slightly higher-gain, and more compressed/balanced than the V2 at the same positions. The 10 is notably brighter — roll Tone back to match darker rigs / humbuckers / P-90s.

WORKFLOW NOTES (cross-demo):
- The G1/G2 4-mode logic: both-down = crunch; G1-up = overdrive; G2-up = distortion; both-up = Forbidden gated fuzz. Fuzz REQUIRES G1 engaged — G2 alone (Red Remote with G1 off) is distortion, not fuzz.
- Red Remote only flips G2 → so its live job is either OD↔distortion (G1 off) or OD↔fuzz (G1 on).
- Touch/reactive gain + guitar-volume cleanup is a core part of the workflow (cleans up "exceptionally well").
- Low-Cut at CUT is also usable as a studio "telephone/filter" effect (Stu G, Eirik) — but Eirik finds it too aggressive in a quiet studio context; better suited to a band/live mix.
