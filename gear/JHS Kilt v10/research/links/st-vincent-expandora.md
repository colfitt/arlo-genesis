# St. Vincent (Annie Clark) — Bixonic Expandora

Third VERIFIED Expandora lineage user (loosely attributed in the dossier; now corroborated with a
how-she-got-it story).

CONFIRMED story:
- St. Vincent discovered the **Bixonic Expandora EXP-2000R** through producer **John Congleton**,
  who had one in the studio cupboard during recording sessions; she liked it enough to **buy her
  own**. Congleton's pitch reportedly was that it's the pedal **Billy Gibbons used** — so the
  Gibbons → Congleton → St. Vincent chain is the documented path.
- Touring rig (per gear roundups): the Expandora ran alongside **two Eventide H9s and a Z.Vex
  Mastotron fuzz into a Kemper** modelling amp — i.e. it survived into a modern modeler/Kemper rig,
  which is directly relevant to the owner's VG-800/modeler re-amp use of the Kilt.

About the pedal (corroborates lineage): "a unique Japanese hockey-puck-shaped multi-distortion
gadget from the mid-1990s" that can "switch between distortion, overdrive, fuzz, and crunch" — the
same four-mode arc the Kilt's G1/G2 toggles reproduce.

HONEST FLAG: equipboard is a crowd-sourced gear DB; the Congleton/Kemper details come from St.
Vincent interviews quoted in roundups. Attribution is solid (she has spoken about it) but this is
not a primary first-person transcript.
