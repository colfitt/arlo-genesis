# Stu G (Stuart Garrard, Delirious?) — why the Kilt exists

The signature/direct-user story for the Kilt itself (vs the Expandora lineage on the other files).

CONFIRMED (JHS official copy):
- "In 2015, JHS launched the Kilt distortion pedal **with Stu G** (legendary guitarist, writer,
  producer, and founding member of UK band **Delirious?**)."
- The design brief, in JHS's words: **"He wanted a pedal that could cover any ground he needed for
  his studio work and extensive touring gigs across the globe. From that request, the Kilt was
  born."** → the entire one-box-does-everything (OD→crunch→distortion→gated fuzz) value proposition
  comes from a working studio/touring guitarist wanting versatility from a single pedal. This is
  exactly why the Kilt is a generalist — and why it's benched in a rig already full of specialists,
  but ideal for the owner's studio re-amp / travel-board uses.
- "ever since, it has been one of JHS's best-selling overdrive/distortion pedals year after year."
- Circuit: "a heavily tweaked **Expandora** style dirt box that gives you more tonal options than
  you might ever be able to use" + a hand-picked low-noise op-amp.

Version note: original Kilt = 2015 (large, two footswitches, independent boost). **V2 = 2017**
(compact, boost removed, **Red Remote added**). Kilt 10 (2025) = re-voiced anniversary V2.
