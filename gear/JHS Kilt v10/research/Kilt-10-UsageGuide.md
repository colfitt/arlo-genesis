# JHS Kilt 10 — Usage Guide

How people *actually* use the Kilt 10, to complement the spec/reference dossier in `Kilt-10-DeepResearch.md`. This pedal is **benched** in the rig (Board 1's drive coverage is full), so this guide is oriented to where it genuinely earns a slot — **studio re-amp, travel/fly board, bass, the Thom-Yorke-era Radiohead distortion, and as a reactive front-end stacker** — plus the two things you must internalize to use it well: the **G1/G2 four-mode grid** and the **Expandora reactive (opto-FET) gain** that makes it touch-sensitive.

> Captured this wave (Tier B, 2 agents): 5 video transcripts + 9 distilled links = 14 sources (see §8). All video attributions verified clean via yt-dlp (no dossier corrections needed this time). Coverage is launch-cluster-heavy — it's a Nov-2025 release — so exact on-camera knob positions are scarce; most demos give toggle states + verbal gain regions.

## 1. Essential workflows

**The G1/G2 four-mode grid** (JHS's own mapping; GPX labels match at the extremes):
- **Both down** = lowest-gain chimey **crunch** / edge-of-breakup.
- **G1 up** = **overdrive** (riffy grit, thicker leads).
- **G2 up** = **distortion / fuzz** (more saturation + sustain; Stu G calls G2-up "more hard-clipping distortion").
- **Both up** = the heaviest **gated "Forbidden" fuzz** (the Expandora's secret extreme-gain mode — spitty, sputtery).
- **Key fact:** *fuzz requires G1 engaged.* The Red Remote flips **G2 only**, so with G1 off you jump OD↔distortion, and with G1 on you jump OD↔fuzz ([jhs-official-kilt-10-mod-walkthrough](transcripts/jhs-official-kilt-10-mod-walkthrough.md); [kilt-10-official-tips](links/kilt-10-official-tips.md)).

**The reactive gain is the signature.** Inherited from the Expandora's opto-FET (Sharp PC419) envelope detector — **the gain itself varies with pick attack**. It tracks dynamics and guitar-volume rolloff well and "cleans up exceptionally well" in every mode **except Forbidden**, which by design *doesn't* clean up with the volume knob ([worship-tutorials-kilt-10-reboot](transcripts/worship-tutorials-kilt-10-reboot.md)).

**The low-gain re-map is the headline V2 fix.** "The first 25% of the gain range is finally usable" — transparent low-gain textures the old Kilt's dead zone couldn't reach. The added headroom means "you no longer have to turn Volume past noon," so it works as a clean boost too.

**Low-Cut:** down = flat (beefy lows), up = cut (reduces lows to cut a mix). The 10 made it genuinely amp/modeler-friendly — relevant for VG-800 / Iridium re-amping (though one reviewer finds the cut a touch aggressive in a quiet room).

## 3. Power-user tips, tricks & hidden features

- **Red Remote** ($46/£34 latching footswitch) flips **G2 only** — hands-free OD↔distortion (G1 off) or OD↔fuzz (G1 on) mid-song. Not MIDI, not multi-parameter.
- **It's "maybe the best second-stage stacking pedal"** (Josh Scott) — the reactive gain adds touch-sensitivity that platform drives lack.
- **Forbidden mode is a texture source, not a lead voice** — "violent assault," "broken radio through a broken speaker"; park it before granular/smear pedals for the drone-doom degraded wall.
- **Cleans up with the guitar volume** in all modes but Forbidden — useful for getting edge-of-breakup out of a distortion setting.
- **Tone is a single brightness tilt** (not a Muff-style bilateral control) — blunter than the Hizumitas's filter sweep for surgical banjo taming.

## 4. Stacking recipes (its strongest on-board case)

- **The headline move (JHS-documented): G1 down / G2 up = a "higher-gain stage boost — perfect for pushing an amp's front end or stacking with fuzz."** This is the purpose-built reactive front-end: Kilt → MXR 108 / Hizumitas gives a thicker, more sustained fuzz than the fuzz alone ([kilt-10-official-tips](links/kilt-10-official-tips.md)).
- **Reactive front-end into the Brothers AM:** low/mid-gain (G1 up, gain ~9–11:00) into Brothers' clean-ish channel adds Expandora touch-sensitivity that Brothers itself doesn't have. If it were ever boarded, this is where it goes — *before* Brothers.
- **Don't stack it into the Longsword or Oxford** — that's piling redundant distortion. Pick one; the Kilt's role (if any) is the reactive low/mid-gain stage that *feeds* them.
- **Lineage precedent:** Billy Gibbons chained **six** Expandoras for *Rhythmeen* — stacking is in this circuit's DNA.

## 5. Notable users & lineage (verified)

- **Stu G (Stuart Garrard, Delirious?)** — CONFIRMED signature/co-developer. JHS: "In 2015 JHS launched the Kilt with Stu G… he wanted a pedal that could cover any ground he needed for studio work and extensive touring." That generalist brief is exactly why it's benched here (specialists cover the arc) but suits studio/travel/bass ([stu-g-kilt-origin](links/stu-g-kilt-origin.md)).
- **Thom Yorke (Radiohead)** — CONFIRMED **Expandora** user (the circuit the Kilt clones): got it ~2007, it **replaced his Turbo Rat** and became his primary distortion for the **2008–2009 *In Rainbows* tours** (live DIP setting "D1 Lo, D2 Hi, Ins B"; defined the *From The Basement* "Bodysnatchers" tone). **This is the rig-relevant tie** — the Kilt 10 is the closest in-production stand-in for Thom's distortion of that era, for tracking the repo's Radiohead material ([thom-yorke-expandora](links/thom-yorke-expandora.md)).
- **Billy Gibbons (ZZ Top)** — CONFIRMED, the Expandora's most famous user; *Rhythmeen*; the six-deep stack; pedal strapped to the guitar ([expandora-billy-gibbons](links/expandora-billy-gibbons.md)).
- **St. Vincent (Annie Clark)** — CONFIRMED Expandora user; found it via producer John Congleton, ran it into a Kemper ([st-vincent-expandora](links/st-vincent-expandora.md)).

## 6. Rig-specific recipes — where it earns a slot (it's benched)

- **Studio re-amp (its real home).** Via the benched EHX Effects Interface + Radial X-Amp, the Kilt is the ideal single-pedal drive-coverage tool for re-amping a printed track — OD, distortion, or Forbidden fuzz from one box, into Iridium or VG-800 models (the 10's modeler-friendly Low-Cut suits this).
- **Travel / fly board.** One drive that does everything from one footswitch (Red Remote for hands-free OD↔fuzz) replaces three specialists when weight matters — same minimal-board logic as the benched Del-Verb.
- **Bass board (a gap the live board doesn't fill).** None of Brothers/Longsword/Oxford is set up for bass; the Kilt's Low-Cut-flat + reactive gain handle the Fender Jazz Bass well (multiple owners buy a second Kilt for bass).
- **Thom-Yorke-era Radiohead distortion.** For tracking the repo's Radiohead folders (Creep, Let Down, Full Stop), this is the practical modern Expandora stand-in — G2-up distortion, dig in for bloom.
- **Sub-in if a specialist dies** (Oxford/Longsword) — it covers that ground immediately.
- **On banjo/baritone:** baritone is its home (reactive gain loves the fat low strings; engage Low-Cut **cut** to keep the bottom defined); banjo works at modest gain (G1 up, Tone left) but the single Tone knob is blunter than the Hizumitas for taming ice-pick.

## 8. Common pitfalls / gotchas

- **Analog island** — mono, no MIDI, no expression; the odd one out in a MIDI-heavy rig (another reason it's benched).
- **Red Remote is a separate purchase and switches G2 only** — don't expect a general/multi-param remote.
- **Forbidden mode is uncontrollable by design** — a texture, not a lead voice; it won't clean up with the guitar volume.
- **Mono → lives early/pre-stereo** on any board (Board 1 goes stereo at the CE-2W).
- **9 V DC only** (exceeding voids warranty), **64 mA** draw — modest but ~6× the Hizumitas; budget for it on an isolated supply.
- **Low-Cut "cut" can be aggressive** in a quiet room — fine for cutting a mix / modeler use, less so for solo bedroom playing.
