Tone Nerds · We Tested Both JHS Kilts So You Don't Have To · 2025

A head-to-head Kilt 10 vs Kilt V2 A/B at matched ("everything at noon") settings, mode by mode. Two-host worship-leaning channel; red tele into clean. Auto-captioned; cleaned to prose. ("Heat" lines are auto-caption garble over the playing.)

---

It's been 10 years since Josh Scott and Stu G collaborated on the Kilt 1.0 — a two-button version (four switches, four knobs) based on the Expandora ("everybody's favorite hockey-puck overdrive"). The hosts have run the V2 a ton on worship boards — "classic Expandora sound, dip switches on the outside make it super versatile, everything from a stage-one to light crunch to full-blown gated fuzz." The question: is the 10th anniversary a real upgrade over the V2, or just a different flavor that's maybe not for everyone?

METHOD: red tele into clean. Everything (all knobs) at noon. Play something simple and recognizable, A/B V2 vs Kilt 10, then flip switches and explore.

THE SWITCHES (their rundown): "Three switches. First is a flat/cut switch — turn on Cut and it drops out some low end so it sits better in the mix. Second is G1 — lower gain, gives a little more dirt/grit when flipped on. Third [G2] is the 'insane button' — flip it up by itself and it's distortion; flip both [G1+G2] and you get the gated fuzz, that sagged-out, saggy tone."

A/B FINDINGS BY MODE (everything otherwise at noon):

- Both switches off, knobs at noon: "Love that classic Kilt sound" (V2). Kilt 10: "That has a little more hair on it, a little higher gain, and it's definitely brighter." Volume measured ~87 dB on both — about the same volume, but the 10 is brighter with more hair.

- G1 up: "I'm really liking the juice on the Kilt 10."

- G2 alone (distortion): "A little more sustain, a little dirtier" on the 10.

- Both up (gated fuzz): both sound nice; at this already-wild setting the hosts actually preferred the V2 a little — "this is already a pretty wild setting, and to have it tamed up is actually a little bit nicer." The 10's fuzz has more going on. (This is the ONE mode where they lean V2.)

- First 25% / low gain: initially they liked the V2 ("more chiminess, a little more transparent"), but the more they played, they swung to the 10 for single-note arpeggio work — "I don't know if it's compression or what, but it plays a little nicer, sounds a little better, and it compresses nicely for chords — more balanced." On the V2, "not as much fullness on single notes," it sounds "a little more open / more natural, maybe a little less compression." Both recanted and went Kilt 10 at low gain.

- Lead tones (cranked): "I really hear the note clarity a little more with the 10."

KEY CAVEAT: the 10 is "quite a bit brighter" — they were taming it (rolling tone back) to even it out against the V2. They like having that extra brightness available: "I tend to go north of noon on tone, so it's nice to have more room up there for a darker pickup, P-90s, or humbuckers. It translates to humbuckers really well — easier to tame the brightness than to add it back in."

USAGE NOTE: because the 10 has more juice/output, "you might have to adjust how you've been using a typical Kilt — before, people would crank the volume and leave it; now you may approach it differently."

VERDICT: both sound awesome. They give the 10 the edge on everything EXCEPT the wide-open G1+G2-everything-cranked gated fuzz (where the V2 edges it). Recommendation: if you already own a Kilt V2, don't necessarily upgrade (spend on a delay first); if you don't own a Kilt at all, spend the extra and get the 10.
