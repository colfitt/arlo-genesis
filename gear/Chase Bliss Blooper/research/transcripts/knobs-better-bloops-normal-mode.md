**Blooper has three modes.** Normal and Additive share a common control set; Sampler is its own thing. The distinction between Normal and Additive is *how modifiers and Stability are treated*.

**Normal mode.** Modifiers and Stability behave like **external effects** — as if they were other pedals placed after Blooper. They affect the playing loop but do NOT apply to your dry signal, and they are NOT baked in. Turn an effect off and the clean loop is waiting underneath. This is the safe starting point.

**Additive mode (for contrast).** The effects are basically inside the feedback loop — they get recorded into the loop (covered fully in the Additive episode). Additive unlocks the real point of Blooper, but it can also get wildly out of control: if you've got a Trimmer set one way and another effect another way and it sounds right in Normal, then start overdubbing in Additive, things crumble, fall apart, and get very weird.

**Takeaway:** capture/arrange a loop cleanly in Normal (auditioning modifiers non-destructively), then deliberately flip to Additive only when you want to commit those effects into the loop.
