## Andy's rig context (how he uses Blooper)
- Ambient-focused board. Signal: compressor first (used for a slight volume boost), then a **signal blender** (parallel mixer giving independent volume over ~3 devices), then an "evil filter" with its own in/out volumes, etc.
- **Volume management across layers** is, in his words, a big deal — many places to set level. His approach: **keep everything at unity** and constantly do on/off A/B checks at each pedal so a device isn't secretly boosting/cutting. (Asked how to manage volume between loop layers, his practical answer is gain-staging discipline, not a Blooper-specific trick.)
- Drives Blooper hands-free with an **external footswitch** (EXT RECORD jack), and at times an iPad MIDI controller app.

## Stability (degradation) — his favorite feature
- **Stability is his favorite Blooper sound.** Notes a community debate: some players dislike the **white noise** Stability introduces and only want the warble/pitch wobble. (This is exactly what the v3.0 BLIP "Stability slider" addresses — dial the noise amount down independently while keeping the wow/flutter; see firmware-3 transcript.)

## Blooper vs MOOD (he uses both)
- "Very different pedals that work very well together." MOOD is far more **textural and out-of-time**; Blooper does **time-based** looping/manipulation MOOD can't. Keep both for both ends; pick one if your style clearly leans textural (MOOD) vs structured time-based looping (Blooper).

## Blooper vs Strymon El Capistan (the main segment — degraded looping shootout)
- Records the **same signal into both simultaneously** (already split via the blender) to compare degradation.
- **Verdict:** El Capistan "does its own thing / has its own sound" — one fixed way to degrade; **Blooper can do that and more** — "there are a ton of ways to degrade on the Blooper but only one on Capistan." Each has its own character.
- At moderate settings, **Blooper's Stability degradation can be more SUBTLE than Capistan's** — Blooper lags Capistan in *obvious* degradation early because Stability is cumulative/gentle by design (recorded over many additive passes). Crank it / let it build for the broken sound.
- Gotcha hit on stream: when feeding a still-playing Capistan loop into Blooper and hitting record, **Blooper re-records whatever is currently coming back around** (captures live output, doubling the source). Be intentional about when you arm record.
