**What Sampler mode is.** The final mode. Designed to be fast and performative — different workflow and different footswitch commands than Normal/Additive.

**Footswitch commands in Sampler:**
- **Left (record)** = records the loop. **No overdubbing in Sampler.** Each time you record, the old loop and its measure are completely discarded — you instantly replace one loop with another (no need to delete-then-record).
- **Right** = **triggers the sample.** Right-foot behavior toggles between two trigger modes: **loop** or **one-shot trigger.** **Hold the right footswitch** to toggle between looping and one-shot. By default, entering Sampler mode starts in looping (so you can move freely between modes without your loop stopping).
- **Hold left (record)** = **momentary recording** — records only as long as it's held; release to stop.
- The LED indicates which state (loop vs one-shot) it's in.

**Why it's useful even if you don't want sample triggering.** It's a nice space to record stutters or throwaway loops you don't intend to build — just capture sounds/moments quickly, replacing one loop with another. All modifiers and Stability still work in Sampler.
- Triggering samples *with effects applied* is unique — e.g. set a Trimmer so just a sliver of the sample plays rapidly when triggered.

**Carryover (the rig-relevant move).** Sampler doesn't allow overdubs, BUT if you record overdubs/layers in **Normal or Additive mode first** (build a complex multi-layer loop), you can **carry that loop over into Sampler mode and retrigger/perform it.** Even if you don't want full sampling, you might appreciate being able to retrigger and perform a built-up loop.
- **Watch out:** as soon as you hit the record footswitch in Sampler, it wipes everything away. So if you spent time building a nice loop in Additive/Normal, don't accidentally tap record once you're in Sampler.
- You can move freely between Normal / Additive / Sampler without interruptions.
