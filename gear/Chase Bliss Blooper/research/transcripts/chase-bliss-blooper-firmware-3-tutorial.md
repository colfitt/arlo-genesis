**Updating firmware.** Connect Blooper to your computer with a micro-USB cable AND connect Blooper to power. Visit the Blooper firmware page in **Google Chrome (no other browser)**, click Allow on the dialog; you should see "ready" (computer can see Blooper). Mac: press Update, wait for the progress bar, reboot Blooper. Windows: a driver needs installing (easy).

## Four new modifiers

**Stretcher** (decouples time from pitch — slow/speed without changing pitch). Time and pitch are normally linked (a slowed tape loop also drops in pitch); Stretcher breaks that link. Demo on a piano loop:
- **Counterclockwise on Mod A**: the loop slows down without changing pitch. "A whole universe of sound between the notes."
- **Clockwise**: the loop takes on an almost percussive quality; all the way clockwise gets into pseudo ring-mod territory.
- Favorite combo: **speed up a loop and add Stutter.**

**Pitcher** (the other side of the coin — change pitch without changing playback speed/length). Engaging Pitcher drops (or raises) the pitch dramatically while the spacing/timing of notes (e.g. a strum) stays the same. Fun to pair with a Filter.

**Chromatic Speed** (third speed option). Recap of the existing two: Stepped Speed is quantized to octaves and fifths; Smooth Speed is not quantized at all. Chromatic Speed is quantized *just barely* — to the chromatic scale — giving the freedom of Smooth Speed but guaranteeing you land in a key.
- Power technique demonstrated: take a loop that's **all root notes** and use Chromatic Speed in Additive mode to **punch in transposed intervals** (a major third, a fifth, an octave down, backwards) at different places — transforming a single-root-note sequence into a "strumming through a chord" effect / a chord built from one note.

**Stopper** (stops your loop). On the mod knob:
- **Clockwise of noon** = fade the loop out (further = longer fade).
- **Counterclockwise of noon** = tape-stop effect (further = longer to stop).
- Blooper can still record while the loop is stopped, so you can use a tape-stop to *begin* a loop and it becomes present in the loop.
- Performance tricks: with tape-stop, toggling the modifier on/off introduces a unique **vibrato**; with fade-out, toggling gives **tremolo**. You can print these.

## BLIP interface (renamed from the v2 control interface) — two key new features

**Stability slider.** Customizes how much *noise* is introduced as the Stability knob is swept clockwise. Lets you choose: really noisy/gritty, OR only the pitch fluctuations (wow/flutter) with no noise, OR something in between. Handy for tailoring the Stability character to taste.

**Additive Assist.** Solves the "print a modifier and lose part of your loop" problem. Why it happens: if your loop is a fixed length and you slow it down ×2, you're effectively making the played-back loop *longer* than the container — so when you commit it with one-shot recording, part of the loop is lost (you can't fit a longer thing into the fixed container). With Additive Assist enabled in BLIP, **playback accurately previews what will actually happen if you commit the effect** — so what you hear is what you'll get when you bounce it down. (And as always, everything in Blooper is undoable.)

- Interface renamed **BLIP**, adding the **Stability slider** and **Additive Assist**.
