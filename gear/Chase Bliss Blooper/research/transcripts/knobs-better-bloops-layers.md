**What the Layers knob does.** Controls **eight levels of undo and redo**. You can record more than eight layers, but anything beyond the eighth is lumped into one big clump layer. (KNOBs uses an external footswitch to record hands-free while demonstrating.)

**Knob behavior.**
- The knob is always evenly divided into equal zones, and it constantly resizes itself so that whether you have 2 or 8 layers, each slice is an equal size (not cramped at the front of the knob).
- The knob **goes to sleep** when you're doing things, so you don't have to be conscious of where it's set — if you navigate back to layer 2 and then record several more layers forward, you won't be stuck on layer 2; you go with it.

**Layers are NOT isolated.** You're not hearing just layer 2 and you can't edit just layer 2. The layer you're on includes that layer and everything before it — it's basically an extension of undo/redo. **Critical gotcha:** if you navigate back to (e.g.) the second layer and press record, all subsequent layers (3, 4…) get swept away, and you go forward from that point — exactly like undo/redo on any other looper. The LAYERS knob and the undo/redo footswitch submenu are identical / the same thing.

**A layer is only created when you go from RECORDING to PLAYBACK.** It has no relation to the loop resetting — it's a conscious decision to go from record to play. **Whatever you hear at the moment you press play is what becomes the layer.**

**Advanced technique #1 — "Loop Capsules" / collecting separate scenes.** Because of how layers commit, you can record completely independent ideas on separate layers:
1. Turn Repeats down (so old material fades to silence).
2. Record an idea, go to playback (commits a layer). Because old material faded out, the new layer is a completely new, unrelated idea to what came before.
3. Use this to record independent samples / strange chords / melodies — KNOBs demos alternating between a C idea and a G idea, navigating between them with the LAYERS knob.

**Advanced technique #2 — recording separate multi-layer "tracks."**
1. Start with Repeats up; record three layers of one idea (e.g. a C chord progression).
2. Turn Repeats down, let it go away, record something else in a different key (e.g. G); turn Repeats back up.
3. Now you have two completely separate three-layer ideas living in the loop, navigable with the LAYERS knob (chords / melody / random notes per slot). "Bottomless" — how you manipulate and move around is unlimited.
- You can drive this with MIDI/CV/expression for precision, or just use ramping (KNOBs demos random ramping on the LAYERS knob to auto-shuffle between captured ideas) — all without using any modifiers or Stability.

**Undo is total.** You can undo/redo anything you record with Blooper, including recorded effects. Even if you record a loop you love, you can boldly tear it apart and still go back eight times.
