**What Repeats does.** Lets your old layers fade away while you're recording. Important caveat: **loops only fade while you're OVERDUBBING (recording), not during plain playback.** Max = standard infinite looping (no fade); lower it for progressively faster fade.

**Use #1 — Blooper as a delay.** Turn Repeats to ~50%, then treat the initial recording (that sets the loop measure) like tap tempo — that's your delay time. **Leave it recording (don't go to playback)** — that's essential — and you have a delay. Press the footswitch to go to playback and two things happen: a new layer is created, and the material no longer fades. You can toggle back and forth between looping and normal delay.
- Delay times can be outrageous — as long as the loop, i.e. 30+ seconds of delay.
- You can build custom delays using the modifiers (speed, scramble, etc.) inside the repeating signal.

**Use #2 — Frippertronics-style fade looping.** Keep Repeats fairly high so all layers subtly fade out over time. Benefits: keeps things from getting cluttered, lets you keep looping forever without hitting capacity, and lets you progress from one idea to another within a loop instead of being attached to the initial material.
- Technique KNOBs likes: start recording with Repeats high, turn effects off, and just react to / improvise over what you already played. Don't worry about bad notes — they fade away. Improvise your way to a nice ambient base layer, then turn Repeats all the way up to lock it (nothing fades anymore). You don't have to stay in fade mode just because you started there.

**Repeats math.** With Repeats maxed → normal looping: everything you record stays at the same volume you recorded it; anything less than max fades away. The lower the setting, the faster it fades. At nil, everything you record just "poofs" and is gone — but it's **not really gone** because you still have eight layers of undo/redo.

**Use #3 — delay ↔ stutter on the fly.** A delay setup can become a stutter at a moment's notice just by pressing the footswitch. KNOBs uses the external footswitch to go back and forth between delay and stutter, and you can turn Repeats up and make a very short loop for stutter effects. As a byproduct of playing this way you create individual recallable moments.
