## Andy's worked example — building a multi-layer loop with DISTINCT layers
- He builds a loop by recording, then **deliberately re-tapping the left footswitch to commit each layer separately** rather than circling and stacking on one layer: *"I could have just kept recording and stacking on top of each other and not pressing this button again, but because of the whole layers thing I wanted to get all those layers distinct."* His example loop = layer 1 (a bed), layer 2 (stacking), layer 3 (chords underneath), layer 4 (melody on top), final layer = "the one with the effects applied."
- Key reason: this is how you get **independently-recallable layers** (the "Loop Capsules" idea). He confirms the limitation viewers ask about: *"you can't pull out any layer by itself… you can only go through the stack — you can remove stuff off the top of the stack. But there is a way to record into Blooper such that you have unique layers."*

## STABILITY knob — demoed across its full range
- *"This is the one that gives it a warble, it gives it a little noise, and it gives it some high-end roll-off."* He sweeps it: noon = obvious warble; higher = *"it'll get very pitchy"*; full = *"original loop totally destroyed."* This is his go-to for aging a clean loop. Pairs it with the reverse-delay trick below to get *"a little chorus-y."*

## Blooper-as-DELAY recipes (he stresses it's a serious delay, not just a looper)
- **Make it a delay:** roll REPEATS back (not max), then tap RECORD at the delay's tempo. *"This pedal as a delay is no joke."*
- **Filter-fades-on-repeat (ADD mode):** Filter modifier (MOD 6) very slightly engaged + Additive mode → *"as it's recording… it is printing the effects over and over… every time the loop goes around"* so each repeat gets darker (low-pass side of the Filter knob). Turn the knob down for faster/heavier filtering each pass.
- **"Rainbow Machine" shimmer/pitch-cascade:** use a pitch modifier in ADD mode so each repeat steps in pitch. He distinguishes the two pitch modifiers explicitly: *"modifier one is the unquantized [octave]… four is quantized pitch."* In ADD with quantized octave-up, repeats **cascade up** octave by octave (shimmer); octave-down cascades down (sub-drone). In NORM the pitch shift is fixed (one octave, no cascade). Add STABILITY for chorus, raise REPEATS for more of it, optionally stack the Filter for darkening each pass.
- **Reverse fifths/octaves:** MOD 4 (quantized) set to "reverse octave down" → *"every repeat is going to be reversed and an octave down from the repeat before it"* (he notes it lands on fifths too).
- **Forward/reverse sweep on one note:** long tapped tempo, REPEATS up, quantized pitch modifier set so the loop *"plays it forward and then backwards… constantly sweeping up to it and then back."* Gets *"less stable over time… because we're in Additive mode [and] stability's up."*

## SCRAMBLER (MOD 5) for delay/loop
- Reads the manual live: *"Scrambler rearranges your loop turning it into a pattern. Going counterclockwise will be random; going clockwise will create a repeating sequence. Lower levels [mild], higher levels will chop everything up."* At noon it does nothing; CCW adds random disturbance, CW builds a repeating rhythmic pattern. He likes it on a falling/octave-down delay: *"just kind of falling apart… I like that a lot."*

## SAMPLER (SAMP) mode walkthrough
- *"In sampler mode there is no overdubbing — each time you tap the left foot switch your old loop will immediately be cleared and a new one recorded. That's actually kind of similar to how Mood works when it's looping."*
- **Momentary record:** *"if you hold this left button… you will do a momentary record — it will only be recording as long as you're holding down."* Great for grabbing a split-second of a pad (he freezes a Dark Star-reverb pad and momentary-records a sliver of it).
- **One-shot trigger:** *"holding the right button will allow you to deactivate looping in sampler mode, allowing you to perform a one-shot trigger of your sample. The right LED will turn green."*

## Storage / preset confirmation
- *"Not only can you save your loops, but you can save the whole layer stack — all the different layers — it'll save all of it."* Confirms 16 saved loops. (Note: it saves AUDIO, not knob/control settings — see links capture.)
