NOTE on "settings": Blooper has NO numeric knob presets — the manual specifies patches as a combination of
(mode) + (modifier) + (which knobs get a ramp dip) + (ramp dip flavor: BOUNCE / RANDOM / SYNC / WIGGLE +
RISE/FALL polarity). Quoted exactly below.

## ADDITIVE-MODE patches (manual p. 32-33)

### ACCUMULATOR — modifier: STABILITY
"Leaving a (generally mild) modifier on while you overdub, causing your loop to gradually evolve over time.
STABILITY is a good starting point — older layers will start to wobble, lose frequencies and get noisier,
while newer layers will sound fresh and pop out. Try Scrambler for something more adventurous."
Settings: Mode = ADD. Engage STABILITY (mild). Keep overdubbing. (Optionally swap in Scrambler.)

### NOISE GENERATOR — modifier: STABILITY (no input signal)
"Turns Blooper into a strange instrument, no input required. Record a layer of silence, then turn up the
STABILITY knob and record an overdub. Blooper will record the noise from stability into the loop. Now you
can go wild modifying that noise — slow it down, filter it, make yourself a nice white noise soundtrack."
Settings: Mode = ADD. (1) Record a layer of SILENCE. (2) STABILITY up. (3) Record an overdub (captures the
noise). (4) Then slow/filter/modify the captured noise.

### PUNCH-IN — modifier: ANY
"Start overdubbing first, then turn on a modifier for just a brief moment. Now when the loop plays back only
that moment will be altered." Mode = ADD; engage any modifier momentarily mid-overdub.

## DELAY-STYLE patches (manual p. 36-39)

### Delay setup (base) — knob: REPEATS
"Clear your loop. Instead of recording a new loop, think of the record footswitch like tap tempo. Tap once,
then quickly tap again — the time between sets your delay time. Press a third time — Blooper needs to be in
the overdubbing state to work as a delay. Play a note and enjoy echoes. REPEATS works just like a feedback
knob: the higher it's turned up, the more echoes you get."
TRICK 1 LATCH: press the record footswitch during playback = infinitely loop the current echo; press again
to resume echoing. (Each latch becomes a recallable LAYER.)
TRICK 2 DELOOPING: don't choose delay-vs-loop — ride REPEATS. "Max it out for looping, lower it for delay."

### BLIPPERTRONICS — knob: REPEATS (high)
"This old classic. Works best with a nice, long delay time. With the REPEATS set this high you get the
impression of a loop, but one that always shifts and moves. A nice way to break up the repetition and create
slowly evolving meditations." Settings: long delay time (long tap-tempo), REPEATS set HIGH.

### TAPE MACHINE — mode: ADD; knobs: STABILITY + REPEATS
"Use a mild STABILITY setting in ADD mode for echoes that get a bit more aged and worn with each repeat."
Settings: Mode = ADD (delay-style base), STABILITY mild, REPEATS to taste.

### LOOP CAPSULES — knob: REPEATS + LAYERS (6 steps)
"1. Record a loop. 2. Turn REPEATS all the way down. 3. Engage overdubbing. 4. Once the audio has faded,
turn REPEATS all the way up. 5. Record some new sounds. 6. Go into playback. Use the LAYERS knob to move
back and forth between the two totally distinct layers." (Builds separate scene-layers you navigate by LAYERS.)

## RAMPING patches (manual p. 44-45)
Ramping = automate a knob via dip switches. Dip choices: pick which knob (LAYERS/REPEATS/STABILITY/MODA/MODB),
flavor (BOUNCE / RANDOM / WIGGLE), SYNC on/off, polarity RISE/FALL. SWEEP sets max-vs-min of range; RAMP knob
sets speed. (You can still set VOLUME while ramping by holding the right footswitch.)

### TIME MACHINE — Idea 1 (Variation): dips = LAYERS, BOUNCE, RANDOM
"By randomizing how many layers are playing, your loops will change and shift constantly, all on their own."
### TIME MACHINE — Idea 2 (Sequencing): dips = LAYERS, BOUNCE, SYNC
"Steadily roll the layers of your loop in and out in a synchronized pattern. Quicker ramp speeds = unique
glitches; slower speeds = remixes of your loop."

### ARPEGGILOOP — dips = MOD B, BOUNCE, SYNC, WIGGLE; modifier = STEPPED SPEED
"Turn your loops into synth-like patterns. The WIGGLE dip keeps the range tight and controlled, and SYNC
makes the movement consistent and synced to your loop." Settings: MOD B = Stepped Speed; ramp MOD B with
BOUNCE + SYNC + WIGGLE.

### REPAIR ME — dips = STABILITY, BOUNCE, RANDOM; ramp = fairly slow
"Get the sound of a tape machine on the edge of death. Use a fairly slow ramping speed. Your loop will
sometimes be pure and clean, other times wobbly and disheveled, and occasionally volatile and obnoxious."
Settings: ramp STABILITY with BOUNCE + RANDOM, RAMP knob slow. (← drone-rot gold for this rig.)

## Dip-switch reference for these patches (manual p. 40-42)
- DRY KILL = remove clean signal from output. CV CLOCK = CV jack syncs to external clock (record arms; starts
  on a clock pulse). PLAY/DUB = after first record, go straight to overdubbing instead of playback (good for
  reverb-trail / ambient). BANK A / BANK B = the two modifier banks (or pick your own via BLIP).
