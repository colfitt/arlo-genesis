## Concrete settings quoted from the article
- **Tape-echo starting point:** "Turn the Stability knob to about 10 O'Clock for an excellent tape echo
  sound." Pair with **"Repeats to about 10 O'Clock."** (Use Blooper in delay-style mode — see manual delay
  setup — for a worn tape-delay voice.)
- **Infinite loop:** "Maxing [Repeats] out clockwise shows a pointer landing on an infinity symbol, meaning
  whatever you play will playback in an endless loop." (REPEATS fully CW = infinite / no fade.)
- **Fade speed:** "The further counter-clockwise Repeats is set, the faster layers will fade away." And the
  key caveat: **"Repeats only has an effect while you are recording — if the loop is in playback, it will
  never fade away."**
- **Stability behavior:** at minimum (fully CCW) Stability is bypassed for pure clean looping; it was tuned
  for additive overdubbing so the loop wears out the more you record over it (tape-reel aging in real time).

## Distilled recipe
**WORN TAPE DELAY (Vintage King starting point):** delay-style base (tap-tempo the record footswitch into
overdub state) · Repeats ~10 o'clock · Stability ~10 o'clock · play notes → aged tape-echo repeats. Push
Repeats fully CW for an infinite frozen loop; pull it CCW (while recording) for faster decay.
