## MIDI / control gotcha (community-confirmed)
- mr_bernard: "Every knob and toggle switch on the pedal can be accessed via CCs and PGs. **You just cannot access the dip switches on the back (top) of the pedal via midi.**" → Important workflow consequence: the Bank A/B, Dry Kill, CV-Clock, Play/Dub, and ramping dip switches are PHYSICAL-only. You cannot flip banks or change those behaviors per-preset/per-song via MIDI — only knob/footswitch/mode state is MIDI-recallable. Plan dip state as a fixed setup decision, not a performance/per-song change. (Deep CB-stack MIDI/clock specifics owned by a separate agent — not duplicated here.)

## Foot-control workaround (the "shoegazer" problem, solved)
- JohntheSavage: criticized limited foot control — needs hands when standing.
- mokomo: practical fix — put Blooper at HAND level (desk/riser) and drive the left footswitch via an **external floor momentary button + an expression pedal** for the modifier/knob you want underfoot. This is the standard community answer to "how do I gig the Blooper." (Matches dossier §8: external NO momentary footswitch via the MIDI jack controls the left footswitch hands-free.)

## Sonic observation worth internalizing
- kairos: isolated modifiers (Stutter, Dropper) aren't that compelling solo, but "the ways things end up getting layered up sound fantastic." → The pedal's payoff is in STACKING/ADD-mode accumulation, not single-modifier showpieces. Aligns with the rig's drone/wall use: build, don't spotlight.
