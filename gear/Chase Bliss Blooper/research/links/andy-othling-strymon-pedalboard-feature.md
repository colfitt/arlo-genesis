A pre-Blooper (2014) Strymon feature documenting Andy Othling's (Lowercase Noises) ambient pedalboard and looping technique. Predates the Blooper, so NO Blooper settings here — value is the documented ambient-looping METHOD and the gear/aesthetic that he later brought to the Blooper, plus a citable record of his stature as an ambient looper.

## Documented technique (Othling's words / Strymon's writeup)
- Stacking delays for wash: *"one of my favorite things is to stack two or even three delays to get a big soupy sound."* (He also does this on Blooper-as-delay — see Office Hours capture.)
- Textural, non-traditional guitar voices: *"the Lo-fi Junky, Micro POG, Superego and even the wacky modulation on the Ekko 616 can provide sounds like that."*
- For ambient boards he recommends: solid reverbs + delays, a **volume pedal** for synthy/swelly sounds, and a **compressor for sustain and fatness.**

## 2014 signal chain (for context on his looping approach)
buffer → Walrus Deep Six (comp) → EHX Micro POG → Walrus Mayflower → EQD Bit Commander → Xotic EP Booster → Ernie Ball MVP (vol, w/ TU-3 tuner out) → **EHX Superego (freeze/looping-style sustain)** → Malekko Ekko 616 → Dr. Scientist Tremolessence → **Strymon El Capistan (one of two — dedicated to LOOPING)** → Boss DD-5 (hold mode = stutter/glitch) → ZVex Instant Lo-fi Junky → Strymon TimeLine → Strymon BigSky → Disaster Area DMC-3 XL MIDI controller (+ two this1smyne mini expression knobs).
- Note: *"Andy has two El Capistans: one is just for looping, the other for the delays."* — i.e. he has long dedicated a whole device to looping, the role the Blooper later took on his board.

FLAG: 2014 article — the Blooper did not exist yet. Use this for his ambient-looping pedigree and method, not for Blooper specifics. His current boards (per Equipboard / Pedal Zone) add Chase Bliss Mood, Blooper, Generation Loss, OBNE Dark Star + Signal Blender, EHX POG2 — heavily overlapping THIS rig.
