Sound Methods (Substack) · author: Andrew Tasselmyer · interview with Andy Othling (Lowercase Noises) · 2024-02-15

The best on-aesthetic articulation of Andy Othling's looping/resampling/degradation philosophy. Andy is the artist most closely associated with the Blooper (co-development + the official "Office Hours" teaching streams), and this interview lays out *how he thinks about loopers in a chain* — directly useful for the drone/doom/shoegaze, degraded "recorded-wrong" goals of this rig.

## Core philosophy — listen, don't play
- *"I'd like to play my guitar and have a good time, and enjoy what is coming back into my ears."* He prioritizes listening to the processed return over executing parts. The looper is the instrument as much as the guitar.

## Looper at the START of the chain (key structural idea)
- He **moved his primary looper from the END of the chain to the BEGINNING**, which changed his whole workflow: *"I'm storing bits of myself [in the looper] and I like that. I have a sound… if I can just throw a little bit of that into the looper and then it still comes through and sounds like me, that's really cool."*
- Translation for this rig: a Blooper-style looper early means you capture your raw identity/tone, then run the *loop itself* through everything downstream (degradation, reverb, modulation) so the bed evolves under live playing. (In THIS rig the Blooper is 4th on the End board / after the time stage — a deliberately different choice; Andy's point is a reminder that looper placement is a creative decision, not a fixed rule.)

## Pedalboard as a DAW / parallel loops
- *"taking things that I would do in my DAW, in Logic, or with certain plugins and [asking] 'what if I could do that before it even hits the computer.'"*
- He runs **three parallel loops feeding a matrix mixer → Condor EQ (tonal sweetening) → compression (peak control) → stereo verb → mastering-stage plugins."* The matrix mixer lets him blend/route loop sources rather than committing to one series order. (This is the "L crap [looper] before the Signal Blender… so I can have three loops with separate mix control" idea he also states in the Office Hours stream.)

## Multi-generational resampling / degradation (the on-aesthetic gold)
- On the album with Dave Mantel he stacked re-sampling generations: *"sounds on that record that are… you're hearing the fourth sampling of it… recorded, then put into Primal Tap, repitched and put into a VSS 30, and then back out."*
- This is exactly the "degraded / recorded-wrong" target: capture → process → re-capture the processed result → process again. Blooper's SAMP-mode Carryover + ADD-mode STABILITY is the on-pedal version of this (build a loop, switch to SAMP, trigger it, re-record it through more degradation).

## Chase Bliss connection
- Andy builds **MIDI controllers for Chase Bliss pedals using Max for Live** — deep ecosystem integration. (No specific Blooper knob recipe is given in this interview; for that see the Office Hours transcript and the cheat-sheet capture.)

FLAG: the resampling-generation quote is about a specific album's process (Primal Tap / VSS-30), not Blooper-specific — but the METHOD (iterative re-capture + degrade) maps cleanly onto Blooper's intended use and this rig's aesthetic.
