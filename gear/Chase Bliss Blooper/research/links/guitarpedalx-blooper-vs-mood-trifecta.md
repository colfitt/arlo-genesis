Long-term owner's take comparing Blooper / MOOD / the delay line. Useful for the "is it foot-friendly?" and real loop-time questions.

## Real loop time (corrects the marketing number) — VERIFY-worthy
- Stefan: official figure is ~40 seconds but his "actual chronometer measure says 32!" So plan for **~32 sec of loop time**, not 40. (The dossier already lists ~30 sec; this is a second independent data point in the 30–32 sec range. Treat 40 as marketing-optimistic.)

## Foot-friendliness verdict (consistent across every reviewer)
- Blooper is "the least practical of the three" for floor use — "really needs to be part of a tabletop environment," rewards constant hands-on manipulation. He "persevered with this for a good couple of months" and concluded it works best as "a tertiary supporting pedal" rather than a primary floor looper.
- By contrast MOOD has "the shortest learning curve" and works fine as a floor pedal once presets are set. → Supports the rig's MOOD → Blooper ordering: MOOD is the set-and-forget continuous source, Blooper is the hands-on capture/rebuild stage.

## Mono limitation
- All three are mono in/out, which Stefan (a stereo player) flags as compromising "that spatial projection I so enjoy with stereo output." Independent confirmation of the dossier's mono-collapse concern — but note this rig deliberately re-stereoizes downstream (Chroma/H90).
