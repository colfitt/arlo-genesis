MATRIXSYNTH post built around a Molten Music Technology demo that runs a BASS LINE through Parting. Synth/Eurorack-leaning take, useful as a contrarian/placement data point.

KEY VERBATIM TAKE (Molten Music): "It's a very touchy feely pedal that's wasted on guitarists and really should be a Eurorack module." Reviewer calls it a "glitch/ambient machine" that "functions better as a synth effect than a guitar pedal."

PRACTICAL TAKEAWAY for this rig: corroborates that Parting shines on SUSTAINED / line-level sources (bass, drones, synth pads) more than fast staccato guitar — i.e. feed it the baritone drone and VG-800 pad patches for the richest results; let dynamics-reactive Envelope Shape handle transient sources.

CONTROL NOTES restated in the demo (matches manual): Dissolve below noon = sample-rate reduction, above noon = increasingly degraded reverse; Rate sets LFO freq AND chance-trigger timing; Shape has 7 LFO options incl. envelope; Smear = diffusion/feedback → reverb; Glitch = clock subdivisions; Filter = post-dissolve cutoff.
