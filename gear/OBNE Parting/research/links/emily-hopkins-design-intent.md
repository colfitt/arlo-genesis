EMILY HOPKINS — DESIGN INTENT (VERIFIED, primary co-designer source).

The verbatim Hopkins statement, carried across the Jan-21-2026 press wave (Synth Anatomy / Gearnews / Guitar Bomb all reprint OBNE's launch PR):
- "Parting goes a lot further than putting my favorite effects together in one box. We spent a ton of time curating these sounds to create something really special when they all work together, using the knowledge that I've accumulated over the years from trying pretty much every effect pedal that comes out. This really is a physical and a sonic representation of this entire journey that I've been on."
- She stresses it is NOT a re-faceplated existing OBNE pedal — "a completely new development" built around her favorite "glitch, signal crushing, and ambient sounds."

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY (verified from press framing):
- Built to "fracture, bend, and recontextualize incoming audio" and to encourage the player to LET GO OF CONTROL — discovery via chance/interaction rather than pristine repeats or predictable modulation. The "isn't about knowing exactly what's coming next... and that's by design" framing is the core ethos.
- The three sections (Modulation / Glitch Delay-Reverb / Dissolve) were curated so they "all work together" — Hopkins' contribution was sound-curation and voicing, not just a co-brand.
- Range explicitly spans "subtle vibrato to shattered, octave-jumping textures" and balances "chaotic digital degradation with ethereal ambient textures" (GuitarBomb framing).

A second Hopkins quote (carried by Russo Music review): "This pedal can do a lot of stuff... it can also create beautiful, ambient soundscapes that no other single pedal can do."

CONFIDENCE: HIGH on the quotes (consistent across 3+ outlets, all sourced to OBNE PR). No outlet reprints a longer design-process narrative or named-preset list from Hopkins.
