EMILY HOPKINS — WHO SHE IS + HOW SHE PLAYS (VERIFIED artist-interview source; richest background found).

BACKGROUND:
- Classically trained electroacoustic HARPIST and composer from Long Island, NY. Took up harp in 2001 after seeing Edmar Castaneda play live; started on a rented lever harp.
- 15+ years playing; YouTube channel "Harp Lady" (@emilyharpist / @emilyharpist2) started July 2019, now 400K+ subscribers. First upload was a Chase Bliss MOOD harp demo (confirmed) — the channel began as a PEDAL-DEMO RESOURCE FOR HARPISTS: "originally my motivation for the channel was to help other harpists."
- Scores/commissions: Ubisoft, the animation studio The Line, film work. (Press also cites DreamWorks-adjacent/animation work; "Ubisoft + The Line + film" is what this interview verifies.)
- Works with partner Russ (audio engineer / co-composer) who handles recording, editing, production: "having a partner isn't twice as productive, but 'infinitely' more productive."

TECHNIQUE / APPROACH (directly relevant to how Parting was voiced):
- PIONEERED running classical harp through guitar effects — reacted against the "rigidity" and competitiveness of classical-harp culture; prefers experimentation "without pretension."
- COMPOSITIONAL PROCESS IS MOOD-FIRST: begins by improvising at the harp or sketching synth textures with Russ, building from a feeling rather than a chart. "if something makes us laugh, we HAVE to pursue it in some way."
- Gear-selection criterion: "The most important criteria right now is how compelling the gear is in creative applications" — she values surprise/creative provocation over surgical control. This is exactly the chance/discovery ethos baked into Parting.
- ESSENTIAL PEDALS she names: Chase Bliss MOOD MkII, EarthQuaker Avalanche Run (delay — her first loved pedal), Blackskycraft Unusual EAS (modulation/BITCRUSHER), Empress Heavy Menace (dist). The bitcrusher + MOOD-style buffer love directly prefigures Parting's Dissolve (crush/reverse) + Chance buffer engine.
- Aesthetic influences: Jay Tholen, Marina Herlop, Windows96, Jack Stauber — "not taking themselves too seriously while... making incredibly fun and unique music."

WHY IT MATTERS FOR THE GUIDE: Parting is essentially Hopkins' taste rendered as one box — mood-first, chance-driven, crush/reverse-forward, played by a sustaining polyphonic acoustic instrument (harp). Her harp technique = long sustained plucked chords + fast rolls, which map cleanly onto the rig's banjo rolls and sustained baritone chords.
