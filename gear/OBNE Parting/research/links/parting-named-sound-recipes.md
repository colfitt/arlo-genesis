NAMED-SOUND RECIPES (best copyable settings found — NOTE: OBNE has no preset-sharing platform and Parting is chance-driven, so published "settings" are CONTROL-DIRECTION recipes, not exact clock values. All positions below are DIRECTIONS/RANGES, flagged INFERRED where I've translated prose into knob clocks from the documented knob behavior).

1) "PSEUDO-SYNTH" CHOP (directly described by Russo; clocks INFERRED):
   - Mod switch = TREMOLO; Dissolve ABOVE noon (~1–3 o'clock, into reverse); Rate moderate-fast; Shape Sine or Random Sine.
   - Result: "furious, shimmery, chopping waves" — Russo calls it darkwave-suited. Combine fast tremolo + reversal.

2) "TAPE EMULATION" WOW/FLUTTER DELAY (Russo; clocks INFERRED):
   - Light DISSOLVE just below noon (gentle sample-rate grit) + modest MODULATION (Vibrato, slow/shallow) + moderate TIME; Chance ~noon for steady repeats; Smear ~9–11.
   - Result: "wow-and-fluttered tape reel delay tones," "analog graininess and reel slip-and-slide."

3) "TIME-STRETCH / WARP" (Russo; clocks INFERRED):
   - GLITCH above noon (random clock reassignment + random sample-rate halving); Dissolve above noon for reverse; Chance noon–1.
   - Result: "warped blinks in the time-space continuum."

4) "DIGITAL RUBBLE" BIT-CRUSH (GuitarBomb/Gearnews term; behavior per manual):
   - DISSOLVE below noon (further CCW = more crush, ~8–9 o'clock for heavy aliasing); Filter LP ~1 o'clock to tame harsh aliasing; Mix to taste.
   - Result: "digital rubble" / lo-fi degraded grit.

5) "HAUNTING BACKWARD TEXTURE" LONG REVERSE (Gearnews term; behavior per manual):
   - DISSOLVE well above noon (~3 o'clock = longest, most degraded reverse); Smear ~11–1 to bloom into reverb; Time moderate-long.
   - Result: "haunting, backward textures" / "long and degrading reverse trails."

6) CONSTANT / SURPRISE-FREE DELAY-REVERB BED (per OBNE manual behavior):
   - CHANCE = NOON (constant, no random buffer surprises); Glitch below noon (normal clock); Smear up for reverb. The reliable "tame it" baseline.

7) CHAOS SOUND-ON-SOUND WALL (per manual behavior):
   - CHANCE above noon toward max (climbing old-signal feedback → "chaotic sound-on-sound looper"); Smear high; ride Mix/Chance to avoid runaway.

ENVELOPE/DYNAMIC mode: Shape = ENVELOPE makes your attack gate what enters the buffer (Chance becomes sensitivity) — the most playable mode for plucked/transient instruments (harp, banjo).
