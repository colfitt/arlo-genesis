Most useful hands-on written review to date. Eric Lapp frames Parting as "a massively powerful multi-effect that quickly creates captivating effect mixes" built for exploration over exact control.

USAGE / TONE-RECIPE TIPS (concrete):
- "Pseudo-synth" voice: couple TREMOLO with signal REVERSAL (Dissolve above noon) to get "furious, shimmery, chopping waves" — Lapp calls this darkwave-suited.
- "Tape emulation" voice: dial in "wow-and-fluttered tape reel delay tones" with "analog graininess and reel slip-and-slide fluctuations" — i.e. light Dissolve + modulation, moderate Time.
- "Time-stretching" voice: push GLITCH randomization (above noon) for "warped blinks in the time-space continuum."
- The LFO "seven waveform shapes" run from traditional to randomized; tremolo/vibrato both have "impactful effects on the shape of the overall output."

SONIC DESCRIPTORS (verbatim, useful for expectation-setting): glitch section yields "chirps and barks at random intervals," "light, twinkling, whistling, harmonic peculiarities," through to "resonant, grumbling tears." Glitch delay is "a delay that quickly runs off the rails to new sonic pastures."

FEATURE CONFIRMATIONS: expression + MIDI; stereo routing (mono / stereo / mono-to-stereo via onboard switching); programmable presets + tap tempo via Aux footswitch; true-bypass OR buffered-trails; dedicated Mix for background blend.

Emily Hopkins quote carried by the review: "This pedal can do a lot of stuff...it can also create beautiful, ambient soundscapes that no other single pedal can do."

NOTE: review does not flag power draw, runaway-feedback, or stereo-width gotchas — coverage is enthusiast/positive, not a deep critique.
