Dealer page with the most beginner-friendly "how to dial it in" copy found — useful starting-point heuristics.

DIAL-IN HEURISTICS (verbatim/close paraphrase):
- RATE: "Slower settings create drifting, ambient movement"; faster settings "feel more rhythmic and unpredictable."
- DEPTH: low = "subtle and steady"; high = "more noticeable and expressive."
- GLITCH: "can divide, double, or randomly shift timing for broken, stuttering, or pitch-shifted effects."
- SHAPE menu in plain terms: "smooth waves, sharp steps, reversed motion, random patterns, or envelope-based response that reacts to your playing dynamics."
- MOD switch: tremolo = volume-based, vibrato = pitch-based.

FUNCTION CONFIRMATIONS:
- EXPRESSION: "assign an expression pedal to control one or multiple knobs for real-time sound changes" (confirms the multi-knob morph behavior).
- AUX: "multifunction control that can be set for tap tempo, half-speed toggling, or preset switching."
- POWER: 9V DC center-negative, ~350 mA, no PSU included. (Confirms the hungry-digital-pedal power gotcha.)
- Presets: "onboard presets" + MIDI preset management (count not stated here; manual = 3 onboard).
