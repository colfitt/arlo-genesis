JHS Pedals (Josh Scott) · A Pedal Made For Harp!? Old Blood Noise "Parting" | Emily Hopkins (Reverb, Delay, Reverse, Glitch) · 2026-03-25
(Live-stream format; Josh Scott jamming and commentating. Distilled to the substantive walkthrough/opinion content.)

OVERVIEW. "This is the Parting by Old Blood Noise Endeavors" — a collaboration with Emily Hopkins (Harp Lady). Per OBNE's copy Josh reads on stream: it "combines chance-based delay, reverb, modulation, degradation effects, and randomized clock changes to give pleasant surprises at each turn of a knob. Happy with many different signal sources — consider it your ready-made ambient texturizer." Comes in three colors (Josh likes the green — "looks like an Andes mint"). This is an ambient/glitch episode; "if you came looking for a Blues Driver and a Stevie Ray Vaughan tone, this is not the episode."

CONTROLS as he demos them:
- Smear: "adding and making it a reverb — essentially, it's massive." Turns the delay into a big reverb.
- Glitch: "doing what you think." Backing the Glitch off "normalizes it somewhat" when things get chaotic.
- Chance: "really interesting... these controls are just wildly interesting."
- Dissolve (he calls it the standout): on the left side "it's like a normal delay... and when I go past 50% it's a reverse sound." He sits on the reverse: "just sit in that for a minute, I'm not even doing anything" — the pedal self-generates.
- Modulation section: a Mod button toggles between chorus-ish and vibrato ("this becomes vibrato, which I much prefer always"). Sped up with max depth: "that's a univibe trapped in a reverse." Many LFO shapes — "you have so many shapes, it's exhausting... I'm a three-shape guy: sine, square, triangle. This has stuff in the middle... it's dazzling."
- Also notes Filter control, Mix control, presets, and tap tempo (which he set).

STEREO. "I am running it stereo, so if you have headphones, make sure you're checking that out. I'm using TRS jacks on the back." He's "fully stereo" now and has "been falling in love with glitch delays, tape sounds."

WHY HE RATES IT. "I think this is the best all-in-one glitch ambient pedal I've ever played, for several reasons." (1) Made in the Midwest. (2) "It's just really easy — I don't have to dive a menu... no screen, no app, no Bluetooth module. I just look down, there's a knob, turn the knob. The user interface is wonderful." It still has all the MIDI/TRS/aux "toys" on the back if you want them. (3) "Very intuitive and simple... everything is super tactile, laid out in a very logical way. I love the division of the modulation side and the glitch side." He praises the difference between making something for the sake of it vs. with real inspiration that's actually usable — "they knocked it out of the park."

ON EMILY / DESIGN INTENT. "This was designed for her — she and Russ are masters of taking the harp and building big textures. Well, they made sure when they made this for her that they made it for everybody, so it handles every type of instrument really, really well." Josh patches it "right into the middle of my board, no issues, it functions perfectly." (He doesn't play harp, though Emily once taught him a bit after a show.)
