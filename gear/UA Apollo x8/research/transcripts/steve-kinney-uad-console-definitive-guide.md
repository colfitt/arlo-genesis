(Auto-captions, cleaned to prose. ~40 min comprehensive walkthrough of the modern UAD Console app. Steve Kinney teaches a Luna master course; demoed on an Apollo + Volt.)

WHAT CONSOLE IS. UAD Console is the software CONTROLLER for your Apollo (or Volt). The most important distinction: no audio is actually passing through or being processed by the computer application. The app is exclusively controlling parameters inside the device — it just represents what's happening inside the Apollo's own hardware/DSP. Analogy: in 2001 this would have been a $10,000 physical mixing desk with literal faders; now it's a rackmount box and the faders are drawn on screen. This matters for signal flow and troubleshooting: all processing lives on the device, not in the computer's domain. That's why, if you use Logic/Pro Tools/Studio One, you have to software-monitor to hear what the DAW hears, and it's why live-collaboration plugins are technically tricky.

INTERFACE LAYOUT. Title bar (top) swaps between connected devices (Apollo vs Volt). Info bar (bottom) shows tempo, sample rate, clock, and DSP usage. Note: in Logic you must type your tempo into Console manually if you want tempo-synced time-based effects while monitoring. Left = plugin browser (toggle with the backtick key). The scribble strip at the very bottom is purely cosmetic for your own organization (name a channel "SM57," "vocal," etc.) — and it hides the STEREO LINK function: click to link e.g. channels 7+8 into a stereo pair (gives you two pan knobs; pan disappears in the aux/cue sends because it's now stereo L/R).

SESSIONS / SCENES. At the top of the mixer you can Save/Load Scenes ("Sessions"). Save a frequently-used tracking setup (e.g. a vocal chain plus auxiliary effects) and recall it all at once. Save As → name it → done.

UAD REC vs UAD MON — the all-important pair (for non-Luna users). Global toggle plus a per-channel version. In UAD REC mode, every plugin in that channel's insert chain is COMMITTED to the wave file as you record. In UAD MON mode, the insert plugins are NOT committed — you only hear them as you perform, and the clean pre-amplified signal is what goes to wave. Most people prefer UAD MON: you get your "vibe/sauce" while tracking but if you over-compressed, no problem — only the clean signal was recorded. The per-channel override turns yellow as a visual cue.

MODIFIERS. REMOVE / COPY / POWER let you click-and-drag across channels: copy an 1176 across many channels at once, bulk-remove plugins, or bulk power-off. ISOLATE is a key feature: it freezes a channel so nothing changes on it during recalls/session changes/parameter changes (Kinney isolates his cue channel so a Luna takeover or session recall won't break his audio path).

SIGNAL FLOW (top → down). Mic hits analog 1 → first the UNISON slot and the preamp itself (pad, 48V, HPF, polarity) → then the INSERT chain. From there it splits two ways: (1) the monitor path continues down through Aux 1, Aux 2, the cues, to the output (the fader lives at the output stage); (2) a second path goes directly to the DAW. In UAD MON the DAW feed is taken essentially PRE-inserts — only the Unison/preamp section goes to wave; everything after is monitoring only. In UAD REC the insert section is added to the DAW feed.

AUTO GAIN (Gen 2 Apollos / Volt 876 only): set a duration, click Start, perform; the system listens ~15 seconds and sets gain automatically. "After listening" mode avoids hearing gain changes mid-take.

LOADING / PRESETS. Click a slot and scroll, or drag-drop a plugin onto a slot. Drag a plugin OUTSIDE the insert tab to remove it (drag within the slots just reorders). Right-click → Remove/Assign/Presets. Channel presets: click the "+" just left of the word "inserts" to load; same menu → Save / Save As to store a whole channel chain.

SENDS — AUX vs CUE. Two Aux channels for time-based/spatial effects (reverb, delay). Best practice: load the reverb on the AUX channel, not on the channel itself (loading it on the channel forces awkward wet/dry gain-staging). Send signal from a channel to Aux 1, then to actually HEAR it in a given cue, raise the Aux 1 channel's send to that cue. PRE/POST FADER matters hugely: in post-fader, an aux receives nothing if that channel's bottom fader is down ("sending 100% unity gain of nothing"); in pre-fader it sends 100% before any fader moves. For reverb on an aux you usually want PRE-FADER. (This pre-fader behavior mirrors how the DAW receives its feed — pre-fader, taken at the pre or right after inserts.)

CUE MIXES. By default cues "follow the mix" (the mix = the bottom Console faders + whatever comes out of your DAW master). To build a custom independent headphone mix, go to the Cue Outputs menu and DESELECT "follow the mix" for that cue — now it's grayed/off until you assign it a color and ride up its own sends. You can MIRROR headphone cues out to line outs or ADAT to feed external personal-mixer boxes (Behringer P16, etc.). You can also mirror individual channel outputs to line/ADAT outs, with a "mirror to monitor" toggle so the bottom mixer still controls your monitor L/R.

THE BOTTOM FADERS DO NOT AFFECT YOUR RECORDING. The bottom faders, pans, mute, solo are EXCLUSIVELY the monitor mix going to the L/R monitors. The DAW receives your signal way up top (at the preamp in UAD MON, or post-inserts in UAD REC) — before the sends, auxes, cues, output, and fader. So riding a fader while recording does not change the level going to the DAW. Corollary: when you SOFTWARE-monitor in the DAW, mute the Console channel (or pull its fader down) so you don't hear the Apollo's direct signal doubled against the DAW's processed return. Same when using hardware inserts/outboard in the DAW — mute those channels in Console and let the delay-compensated DAW path manage monitoring.

CONTROL ROOM / TALKBACK. Pop-out gives talkback (route to auxes, specific cues, or the monitor; you can add plugins/compression/EQ to the talkback mic, or even use it as an extra room mic since it's an input source in the I/O Matrix). The SOURCE section lets you set the control-room source to a cue instead of the monitor — so you can listen in on exactly what a performer hears in their headphones. Remember to switch the source back to Monitor before leaving the menu.

SETTINGS. Five tabs: Hardware (device type, output reference levels, BYPASS the input gain stage for analog line-ins, talkback assignment, cue bus count), I/O Matrix, Options, MIDI, Network. Options page: metering can be PRE or POST fader — pre-fader is useful while setting the initial preamp level (meter stays constant regardless of fader); switch to post once the level's good. Clip-hold infinite is recommended so you always know if you clipped. MIDI is useful for live (scene changes, Auto-Tune settings via Ableton). Settings shortcut: Command-comma.

I/O MATRIX (the dense part). Not as complicated as it looks. The CHANNEL NUMBER is what your computer/DAW sees — the literal Thunderbolt digital channel pathway. Inside the Apollo you ALIGN physical analog sources/destinations to those digital channels. So when Logic says "Input 1," Input 1 is whatever you've mapped to digital channel 1 (by default mic/line/Hi-Z 1, but you could map it to talkback or anything). It's just aligning physical sources to digital channels — fully flexible and re-mappable.
