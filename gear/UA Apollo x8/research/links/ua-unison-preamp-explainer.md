WHAT UNISON IS:
- UA's analog/digital integration that makes the Apollo's mic preamps and Hi-Z instrument inputs "sound and behave like" sought-after tube/solid-state preamps and amps — Neve, API, Fender, etc. It works at the INPUT stage, capturing the impedance, gain-stage sweet spots, and component-level clipping behavior of the modeled circuit. When you change the plugin's gain, the HARDWARE preamp physically responds (impedance morph) — it's not just a downstream EQ/saturation.

HOW TO USE IT (the key rule):
- The Unison plugin must be loaded into the SPECIAL UNISON SLOT at the very TOP of the Console channel strip — NOT a regular insert slot. A Unison plugin placed in a normal insert does not engage the hardware interaction.
- To load: click the dedicated "UNISON" insert tab at the top of the Console (or LUNA) channel, choose a Unison plugin from the "PREAMP & CHANNEL STRIP" category, and pick a preset that matches your source.
- Available on: the mic preamp inputs and the front-panel Hi-Z instrument (DI) inputs. (Not on line-only inputs.)

PRACTICAL TIPS:
- Gain-stage with the model in mind: driving a Neve/610-B Unison harder pushes its modeled input stage into its characteristic color, just like the hardware.
- Match the model to the source — Neve 1073/610-B for SM57 on a cab; transparent/light 610-B for a detailed condenser; amp models for DI guitar/bass.

FOR THE RIG: Unison is the front end for mic tracking (SM57/AT2020/KM184) and the Hi-Z DIs — it is the ONE place the otherwise-transparent x8 imparts a "sound." For printing the already-colored stereo board it is bypassed (preamp-to-A/D) on purpose.
