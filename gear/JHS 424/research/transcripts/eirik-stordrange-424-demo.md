Eirik Stordrange · The sound we've been avoiding for years (JHS 424 Gain Stage) · 2025-08-11

NOTE: Release-week demo. Most relevant for the XLR-DI-into-an-audio-interface workflow (the rig's exact path) and the clean-DI vs blown-out duality. Player runs it both as a DI and in front of a Fender Deluxe Reverb. Transcript distilled (musical passages omitted).

---

The JHS 424 Gain Stage is based on a Tascam Portastudio 424 MK1 — a cassette-recorder thing made popular recently by Mk.gee. The originals are super expensive on Reverb now, so you can get this instead.

CONTROL MAPPING:
- Volume = master volume on the original unit.
- Gain 2 = sets the volume level of the SECOND gain stage = "your fader" on the Portastudio.
- Gain 1 = "sets how much pre-amplification will be added to the input signal" = the TRIM knob.
- Bass + Treble.

SETUP (note for the rig): "At the moment I'm going out from the XLR output directly into my audio interface. So you can run it like a DI unit, or you can run it into an amp." He demonstrates BOTH. Guitar tuned to C STANDARD "because of the whole Mk.gee thing." Intro had flanger + reverb added (in an HX Stomp); also shown dry.

OBSERVATIONS:
- Initially he liked it BEST through the XLR as a DI pedal. Then he found sounds in front of his Fender Deluxe Reverb that "don't sound like any other overdrive or fuzz pedal" — a useful addition to his "gain vocabulary."
- On the DI sound: at first "it sounds like I didn't set my levels in my audio interface" (i.e. it sounds 'wrong' / over-driven into the converter) — "and now that's a sound that we like, all of a sudden, thanks to guitar players like Mk.gee." Compares the cultural shift to transistor amps (PV Decade) we used to hate and now like.
- Compares it to the JHS Colour Box — "to some degree it is that," but the into-the-amp sound was unique: "felt like I was plugged into my old hi-fi system... then into an amp," like boosting with a CD player / hi-fi.
- Mk.gee tone caveat (honest): "a lot of the drive he's getting is actually from the REVERB pedal" (a Ranger-style gated/distorted reverb) — Eirik adds reverb + chorus/flanger in his HX Stomp to compensate.

VERDICT: His preferred uses = DI stuff, "either a pretty clean DI signal or the blown-up stuff. You get those Mk.gee tones and some Tame Impala tones." Surprised himself by also liking it into a guitar amp. "A pretty versatile thing — won't replace your Klons or Tube Screamers, but a nice add-on to your drive/saturation vocabulary." Thumbs up.

---

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- Confirms the rig's path: XLR direct out → audio interface, run as a DI. The "over-driven into the converter" sound IS the point.
- Two sweet spots for DI: (1) pretty clean DI signal, (2) blown-out. The clean and the destroyed both live in the DI path.
- C-standard / lower tunings suit the Mk.gee aesthetic (relevant to baritone).
- The full Mk.gee tone needs a gated/distorted reverb (Ranger-style) AFTER the 424 — the pedal alone is only part of it.
- Adjacent reference tones it nails: Mk.gee, Tame Impala.
