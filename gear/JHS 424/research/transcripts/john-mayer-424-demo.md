ForTheLoveOfJohnMayer (re-up of John Mayer's official JHS guest demo) · John Mayer — Playing New Pedal from JHS "424 Gain Stage" · 2025-08-30

NOTE ON ATTRIBUTION: This is a re-upload (channel "ForTheLoveOfJohnMayer") of the guest demo John Mayer recorded for JHS Pedals while Josh Scott was recovering from a mountain-bike accident. The content/audio is John Mayer's official JHS demo; the JHS-channel original (TGEne6M1EoY) is now private. Settings spoken aloud below are John Mayer's.

---

Music is changing. There is a revolution taking place right now in guitar playing and it has to do with dynamics. I am not Josh Scott, but I am John Mayer, and I thought I'd take this time while Josh is out of commission to do a little of his job — which is to show you a new pedal. It's the JHS 424 Gain Stage. This is a perfect, exact replica of the front end of a Tascam Portastudio. If you're a musician of a certain age, you may have had one in your room and that's all you had to play through. If you're a young musician, you'll know it from its resurgence in modern music.

For most of guitar history, guitars were plugged into amplifiers, and the way a tube amplifier responds has defined how people play guitar. But now people have been plugging into things that aren't tube amplifiers — USB interfaces for DAWs, MPCs (like I like to do), and most recently the Tascam Portastudio. You no longer have to go to Reverb and buy a Tascam Portastudio in various states of disrepair. It's right here.

Not long ago I was playing into an MPC, doing this exact arpeggio, and I loved the way it was answering back to my playing. I spent a whole day in the studio trying to get that sound. We tried Sansamps, we tried Rockman amps going straight into an API. About a couple weeks after I gave up the search, Josh reached out and let me know he'd developed this pedal. When I plugged into it — as I have the last couple weeks — I've really been having fun.

It's reacting completely differently. It's hard to explain; you have to get a pedal going and the guitar in your hands to feel how it's reacting. Sonically is 50% of the thing, feel-wise is the other 50%. The fun part is it makes you play different stuff. So I've got the 424 Gain Stage, I've got the Artificial Blonde [guitar] going straight into the DI. There's no other tricks — a little bit of reverb and that's it. It just makes you want to play.

This is where I want to get into the breakup of the 424. Josh wanted me to play some grunge. So let's turn up the gain all the way on the second and the first — so that is **trim all the way up, channel all the way up**. This is "Good Love Is on the Way." [plays Stone Gossard / Pearl Jam-style riff]

So let's see what happens if we move gain two up and **gain one is at 12** o'clock. That's rubbery — that's like every note you play just sort of falls off the cliff. But it's interesting; it makes you want to [play]. Okay, now I'm just going to mess around. I got it nice and bassy. [plays]

If you want a completely different experience on your electric guitar or any instrument, I suggest you pick up the 424 Gain Stage by JHS Pedals. What you can't see is how good it feels to play. There's a lot of pedals you could buy that will change the sound, but how many can you buy that will actually change your relationship with the instrument itself? I cannot do most of my tricks — that forces me to explore.

This thing is so fast. The notes are coming out before I'm finished playing them. When you're playing through an amp, the notes are kind of contained — almost like going down a halfpipe. This just spills everywhere. It's like mercury that just rolls across the top of a table. It's a basketball hoop without a net — doesn't go swish, it just falls. Unforgiving but exciting. It makes me want to sing something. That's what you want out of a song.

[On dynamics] Okay, check this out. Where most guitar dynamics start to disappear when you get really low — **all the good ones on this pedal are really, really down here.** [demonstrates low-volume / low-gain playing]

What you just saw was me experimenting with a pedal — not so much showing it off in a professional manner, but putting it through the musical paces that I would if I were at home. I am not Josh Scott, but I'm a dear friend and admirer of his, and I want to make sure a pedal this good doesn't go unheralded just because Josh is recovering. This pedal is going to be a lot of people's secret weapon. A lot of times when people have the same pedal, they have the same sound. That will not happen with this pedal, because this is a whole new dynamic approach. It is not so much a paint color, but a whole different brush.

Get the JHS 424 Gain Stage. You will absolutely love it. I'm not Josh, I'm John Mayer. Thank you very much.

KEY SETTINGS QUOTED:
- "Grunge / breakup": Gain 1 (Trim) ALL THE WAY UP + Gain 2 (Channel) ALL THE WAY UP — Pearl Jam / Stone Gossard territory.
- "Rubbery": Gain 1 at 12 o'clock, Gain 2 turned up — "every note falls off the cliff," elastic/mercury feel.
- "Bassy" exploration: dialed Bass up while messing around.
- Dynamics insight: the best/most expressive sounds live at LOW volume/gain settings ("all the good ones are really, really down here") — counterintuitively, dynamics open up at the bottom of the range, not the top.
- Rig in demo: guitar (Fender "Artificial Blonde") straight into 424 → DI, plus a touch of reverb. No other pedals.
