Riley Thornton · ‼️NEW JHS 424 GAIN STAGE ‼️ - Pedal Demo · 2025-08-11

NOTE: One of the earliest demos (Aug 11 2025, release week). Mostly playing with sparse talking; long musical passages. Useful for the control walkthrough and the direct-vs-amp comparison. Transcript distilled below (musical/laughter segments omitted).

---

Hey guys, my name's Riley and I'm super stoked today because JHS sent me a pedal to demo. It's not a typical overdrive — it's more of a preamp circuit based off of the Tascam Portastudio 424. I believe the intended tonal space for this is Mk.gee-type tones. Today I'm going to see how it does as a preamp for a direct-type tone, and then also we'll plug it into some amps and hear how it sounds for that.

CONTROL WALKTHROUGH (as Riley understands it):
- The **first gain knob (Gain 1)** is really your preamp gain — where you're going to get the most grit and overdrive.
- The **Gain 2 knob** is a trim, setting the volume level of it [the second stage]. [NOTE: this is Riley's read; per the JHS manual Gain 1 = Trim and Gain 2 = Channel fader — he has the trim/channel labels swapped, but the functional point — Gain 1 makes grit, Gain 2 sets the second-stage level — is right.]
- Then you have an overall **Volume** which is like the master fader on the actual console.
- Then of course **Treble and Bass** frequency controls.

DIRECT TONE DEMO:
First, reference tone: "It's just a guitar — the guitar direct in to a little compressor." [plays clean DI]
Then: "And here's the 424." [plays — same guitar now through the 424 as a DI preamp; noticeably thicker, compressed, lo-fi]

[Several musical passages exploring direct tones — from warm clean-ish preamp up through gritty breakup and into thick fuzz, with comments of "Heat" as he pushes the gain.]

MK.GEE-STYLE PATCH:
"All right, going for more of a Mk.gee style — with a little bit of tape breakup beforehand, and I have a Ranger-style reverb afterwards with a gate." [plays the elastic, gated, lo-fi Mk.gee-coded sound; later adds "let's get some more drive" and adds chorus]

AMP DEMO:
"Okay, so that was an overview of some of the direct tones you can get with this guy. Now let's plug it into some amps." [Plays amp clean tone first, then 424 into the amp — using it as a front-of-amp drive/preamp; pushes into heavier gain.]

WRAP-UP:
"So that's the JHS 424 Gain Stage. Baller pedal, amazing stuff. Really helps with the Mk.gee-style tones, any direct stuff. The fuzz tones are incredible as well. Super dope in the direct stuff — I think that's my preferred way I'd use this — but it's actually really cool on amps too, and works really cool with other overdrives. Go check this out on JHS's website, it just dropped."

---

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- Gain 1 = the grit/overdrive control; Gain 2 = second-stage level; Volume = master.
- Best application per Riley: as a DIRECT (DI) preamp, not in front of an amp — though both work. Stacks well with other overdrives.
- Mk.gee recipe shown: 424 with light tape breakup BEFORE it + gated reverb AFTER it; modest-to-pushed gain, optional chorus.
- Fuzz tones (gain pushed) called "incredible."
- No specific clock numbers given (knob-twisting demo, not a settings tutorial).
