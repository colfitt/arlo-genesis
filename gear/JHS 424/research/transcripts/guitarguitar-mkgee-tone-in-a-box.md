---

CONTEXT: Mk.gee's tone sparked a global tone hunt; the source is the Tascam Portastudio 424 MKI, which he uses "as an amplifier" — driving the preamp. The 424 Gain Stage recreates that. (Eric Clapton has compared Mk.gee to Prince.)

CONTROL WALKTHROUGH:
- Gain 1 = the TRIM = "basically the actual gain of the channel." This is where the grit lives. "If you dime that back a little bit, or dime the volume just back a little bit, raise the gain on there" you get the fuzzy, blown-out console sound.
- Gain 2 = "basically like a CHANNEL fader" — the second of the two gain stages. "You can sculpt two stages of gain on it."
- Volume = MASTER fader (he stresses "incorporating the master volume is quite important — as you raise the gain the pedal gets louder, so you need to do a balancing act").
- Bass / Treble = EQ, like a classic recording console. CENTER = flat; you can cut OR boost from there. Push up = boost.

TECHNIQUE — DYNAMIC CONTROL OF THE BLOWN-OUT SOUND:
"It's almost this kind of fuzzy, really blown-out console sound... but where a lot of that comes in is if you just trim that just off the top end there — you're able to control that with your dynamics. So you play soft [clean], and you can dig in just a little bit." Mk.gee uses his FINGERS to play, which gives more dynamic control over when it breaks up.

GAIN-STAGING DEMONSTRATED:
- Gain 2 all the way up = "a bit of a fuzzy noise... no real use for subtlety there."
- To stay clean instead: "Pull your input gain [Gain 1] down and then raise the channel fader [Gain 2] to keep it nice and clean." (i.e. low Gain 1 + higher Gain 2 + Volume = clean DI; high Gain 1 = grit.)

PER-SONG / SOURCE NOTES:
- BARITONE guitar used throughout — pulls out the full low range. (Directly relevant to the rig.)
- "Candy" (Mk.gee): by cutting or boosting the EQ you get closer to that sound.
- "Dream Police": adds a Boss SY-1 synth pedal — "with the baritone sounds absolutely awesome." (Mk.gee uses an old Boss synth pedal with the little pickup; Kieran subs the SY-1.) The big "crash" with open strings = just using dynamics, boosting the pedal hard on the dramatic hits to "catch you off guard."
- "Rockman": pairs the MXR Rockman X100 (chorus) with the 424 — Mk.gee released a song called "Rockman" as a clue.
- "Daisies" (Justin Bieber x Mk.gee): "super super low-key... not much going on... a pretty low-key, almost just DI tone." To dial it: keep it mostly clean with the gain "a little bit pushed up," CUT the treble a little, keep the clean tone coming through.

RIG IN THE DEMO:
- A subtle phaser left on "for most of the examples" — "really really subtle, just creating a little bit of movement."
- Going straight into a DESK via the BALANCED (XLR) output — "very similar to how it'd be used live." (Output 1 / top jack = traditional guitar output → would go to an amp.)
- A tiny bit of reverb on the desk.
- Mk.gee's actual reverb = a Ranger Effects reverb (gated/ducking style that ALSO adds distortion) — Kieran doesn't have it but notes the 424 does "a really good job of emulating that big dramatic sound."

WRAP: Praises that you can plug it straight into an amp, an interface, or a mixer while still being travel-friendly. A new sound that "sparks imagination."

---

KEY SETTINGS TAKEAWAYS:
- GRIT is on Gain 1 (trim); to stay CLEAN: Gain 1 LOW + Gain 2 higher + Volume to taste.
- BASS/TREBLE: 12 o'clock = flat (true cut/boost EQ, not just boost-only).
- Mk.gee "Daisies" clean-DI recipe: gain only slightly pushed, TREBLE CUT a touch, stays clean.
- Mk.gee blown-out recipe: gains up so it sits near clipping, then TRIGGER the blow-out dynamically by digging in (finger-style), back to soft for clean.
- Baritone-specific: pairs beautifully; add a synth pedal (Boss SY-1 / old Boss synth) for the "Dream Police" texture.
- Mk.gee's secret extra ingredient = a gated/ducking distorted reverb (Ranger Effects) AFTER the 424.
