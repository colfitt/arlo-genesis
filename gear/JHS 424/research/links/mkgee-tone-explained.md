# How Mk.gee actually gets his tone (and what to pair the 424 with)

The clearest signal-chain breakdown of the Mk.gee sound — useful for what to put AROUND the 424 to get the "elastic" result.

## The core source (honest: hardware, not the pedal)
- The sound centers on a **Tascam Portastudio 424 MKI used as a preamp, not a tape machine** — guitar plugged straight in, preamp pushed hard. There is no tape involved; the 424's preamp is being used as an overdrive.
- Result: "elastic, slimy, very midrange saturation" with lo-fi grain that "breathes transients and gets nicely dirty when attacked."
- Guitar: a **Fender Jaguar in baritone tuning with heavy strings** — thick, compressed, bass-forward; the low tension + finger-style attack is what makes the 424 bloom and squish. (Matches the rig's baritone Jazzmaster.)

## Chain order (utilities → 424 → mod → time → console)
`Utilities (tuner, pitch/Drop, hum filter) → Tascam 424 preamp → Modulation → Delays/Reverbs → Textures → console`
The 424 sits EARLY (right after utilities), then modulation and time effects come AFTER it. (Note: this rig instead uses the 424 as the terminus print — a deliberate inversion; the upstream texture/time work has already happened.)

## What to pair it with — concrete settings to chase "elastic"
- **Chorus — Boss CE-2W:** Rate 9–10 o'clock, Depth 11–12 o'clock. (Wobble/movement is core to the Mk.gee DNA.)
- **Delay — Rainger FX Echo-X:** Time 300–400 ms, Feedback 20–25%.
- **Tape echo — Catalinbread Belle Epoch:** Delay 250–350 ms, Mix 10–15%.
- **Reverb — Rainger FX Reverb-X:** Decay 2–3 s, Mix 20–25%, dark tone.
- Also in the chain: Empress ParaEQ, Boss BF-2 flanger, DigiTech Drop.

Takeaway: the "elastic" sound is 424 squish + subtle chorus + short dark reverb/delay + compression — not the drive alone.
