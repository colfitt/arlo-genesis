# JHS 424 on bass / low-end sources + the gated-fuzz behavior

Best source on the two-gain interaction stated in plain terms, the Gain-2 gated-fuzz threshold, and EQ-as-clarity. Directly relevant to the rig's baritone Jazzmaster + GK banjo.

## The two gains, restated for bass
- **Gain 1 = input saturation** — "acts like the Trim of the original tape machine"; at low settings it's a "warm, harmonically rich preamp."
- **Gain 2 = output level** — mimics "the channel fader" of the original Tascam. So the dual gain "lets you separate input saturation from output level" — same loudness, different grit.

## The gated-fuzz threshold (the Gain 2 gotcha)
- Push **Gain 2 past ~3 o'clock** and "the 424 reveals itself as a gated fuzz monster, tight, aggressive, and full of attitude." Intentional, but it gates/chokes sustained material — for a wall-of-drone print, keep Gain 2 BELOW 3 o'clock and add grit with Gain 1 instead.

## EQ for low/murky sources
- **Treble = clarity dial:** "works like a clarity dial, cutting through the haze when needed, especially helpful if you're stacking pedals after it." The 424 trends dark/gooey by default — Treble is the de-haze recovery.
- **Bass:** "solid low-end sculpting for different rooms or rigs." Watch it at high gain (it can flub on a baritone/bass — pull back before the print).

## Pairing with synth/octave (relevant to VG-800 COSM + octave textures)
- "Pairs shockingly well with modern octavers and synth pedals" — yields "Moog-style subs" and "tape-crushed synth fuzz." Good for de-perfecting the modeled VG-800 signal.
