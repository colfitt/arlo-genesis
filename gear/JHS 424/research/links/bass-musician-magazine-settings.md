DISTILLED SETTINGS / DIRECTIONAL GUIDANCE (this source gives relative positions, not clock numbers — flagged as DIRECTIONAL, not quoted clock faces):

CONTROL MAPPING (confirmed):
- Gain 1 = the TRIM of the original tape machine (input pre-amplification).
- Gain 2 = the CHANNEL fader (second gain stage).
- Volume = output / MASTER.

NAMED SOUNDS:
- SUBTLE TAPE WARMTH — "With Gain 1 LOW, the 424 works as a warm, harmonically rich preamp," adding "subtle tape-like bloom to notes" before any audible grit. → Recipe: Gain 1 low, Gain 2 moderate, Volume to taste. The always-on glue setting.
- SATURATED GRIT — "With Gain 1 HIGHER, things get more saturated, landing in a distinctly vintage overdrive/fuzz voice." → Grit comes from Gain 1.
- BLOWN-OUT GATED FUZZ — "Push Gain 2 PAST 3 O'CLOCK and the 424 reveals itself as a gated fuzz monster — tight, aggressive, full of attitude." → The fuzz/gate threshold is Gain 2 > 3 o'clock specifically. Below that = no gating.
- TREBLE = CLARITY DIAL — "works like a clarity dial, cutting through the haze when needed." → Raise Treble to de-haze a dark/gooey print; the pedal trends dark by default.

BASS-SPECIFIC (directly useful for the rig's baritone Jazzmaster):
- "Warm, tape-like saturation rather than modern distortion." Low strings get "that near-breakup texture we associate with old tape heads."
- Bass "sits in the mix beautifully" with the tape-channel compression.
- Watch BASS knob at high gain — it can get flubby; pull Bass back a touch before committing/printing.
- The dual gain "lets you separate input saturation from output level" — saturate with Gain 1, set level with Volume independently.
- Pairs "shockingly well with modern octavers and synth pedals."
- The XLR DI makes it a one-box bass recording chain.

INTERACTION SUMMARY: Gain 1 = how much grit; Gain 2 = how hard the 2nd stage clips (and the gate above 3 o'clock); Volume = loudness, independent of grit. Same loudness is reachable many ways — balance Gain 1 vs Gain 2 for the texture, then set Volume.

NOTE: No specific clock-face numbers in this source; all positions are relative ("low," "higher," "past 3 o'clock").
