DISTILLED — JHS 424 vs the other three Tascam-424-style preamps (the "why two Portastudio boxes" comparison):

JHS 424 GAIN STAGE — five controls: "Volume, Gain 2, Gain 1, Bass, Treble." Independent gain staging (separate input Trim + Channel + Master). Tonal range "pretty vast — from Lo-Fi flavours to quite zingy fuzzy tones." $249 (priciest of the 4). The only one of the 4 available locally in the UK and "the one that has sold the most." Author OWNS it and is "broadly very satisfied."

COMPARISONS:
- vs MXNHLT PORTA 424: the MXNHLT uses a SLIDER-based level control (a fader) and the same input/output gain organization as the JHS's dual-gain approach. (Both give full channel-strip staging.)
- vs Benson Portable Distortion 424: Benson omits independent gain controls — simplified FOUR-knob layout (no separate input/output gain management).
- vs Neutrino Labs MX 424: the Mexican brand includes an XLR output. [GPX notes JHS specs in the article didn't mention XLR — but the JHS DOES have a balanced XLR out + ground lift per JHS's own page; GPX simply didn't list it. Flag this as a GPX omission, not a JHS lack.]

USAGE TAKEAWAY: GPX gives NO clock-face settings — it's a feature/voicing comparison. The decision-relevant point for the rig: the JHS is the one with independent Trim/Channel/Master staging AND a balanced XLR out + ground lift, which is why it earns the terminus-into-Apollo slot over the simpler Benson; the MXNHLT (also full-staging, fader-based, no XLR) sits upstream as the cleaner "tone," the JHS as the XLR "print."

NOTE: clock-face caveat — no numeric settings in this source.
