=== THE CORE MK.GEE METHOD (observed across all demos) ===
1. The sound is OVERDRIVEN PREAMP / GAIN-STAGING, not tape. (Happy Mag myth-bust; JHS.)
2. Set the two gains so the pedal sits NEAR the clipping threshold, then TRIGGER the clip DYNAMICALLY by digging in — play soft = clean-ish, dig in = the lo-fi crack/blow-out. (guitarguitar, Andertons: "the Mk.gee thing is very much about triggering that clipping," "fairly infrequently.")
3. Play FINGERSTYLE for dynamic control (Mk.gee does). Lower-output pickups = more controllable; hotter pickups clip more readily. (Andertons.)
4. Run it DI / into the interface (XLR), NO cab sim. Judge the tone on monitors, not a guitar speaker. (Eirik, Andertons.)
5. The FULL Mk.gee tone needs more than the 424: a gated/ducking DISTORTED reverb AFTER it (Mk.gee uses a Ranger Effects reverb), subtle modulation/phaser/chorus, and lower tunings (C standard / baritone). The 424 is the gain core. (guitarguitar, Eirik, Andertons.)

=== RECIPE A — "Daisies" clean-DI Mk.gee (low-key, barely-broken) ===
Source: guitarguitar dialing "Daisies" (Bieber x Mk.gee).
- Gain 1 (Trim): LOW–moderate · Gain 2 (Channel): moderate–higher · Volume: to taste · Bass: ~12 (flat) · Treble: CUT a touch (below 12)
- Quoted: "a pretty low-key, almost just DI tone... gain a little bit pushed up... cut the treble a little, but still want that clean tone coming through."
- INFERRED clocks: Vol 12 · G1 9–10 · G2 1–2 o'clock · Bass 12 · Treble 10–11.

=== RECIPE B — "Candy" / general blown-out Mk.gee (clipping on demand) ===
Source: guitarguitar; John Mayer ("rubbery").
- Gain 1 (Trim): around 12 (the grit control) · Gain 2: up · Volume: balance for level · Bass/Treble: cut or boost to taste.
- John Mayer's "rubbery" setting: "gain one at 12 o'clock, gain two up — every note falls off the cliff." Elastic, mercury-like.
- Technique: keep dynamics in your hands — soft passages clean, accents blow out.
- INFERRED clocks: Vol 11–12 · G1 12 · G2 1–2 o'clock · EQ to taste.

=== RECIPE C — "Grunge / full breakup" (max both gains) ===
Source: John Mayer ("Josh wanted me to play some grunge").
- Gain 1 (Trim): ALL THE WAY UP · Gain 2 (Channel): ALL THE WAY UP · Volume: set for sane output.
- Pearl Jam / Stone Gossard territory. The most aggressive, gated-fuzz end.
- INFERRED clocks: Vol 10–11 · G1 5 · G2 5 · EQ to taste (watch Bass for flub).

=== RECIPE D — "Dream Police" baritone + synth ===
Source: guitarguitar (baritone guitar + Boss SY-1; Mk.gee uses an old Boss synth pedal).
- 424 set medium (Recipe B-ish), baritone guitar, add a Boss SY-1 (or old Boss guitar-synth) BEFORE/with it. Big open-string "crashes" = boost the pedal hard on the dramatic hits via dynamics.
- Note: directly relevant to the rig's stereo banjo (GK-5/VG-800 modeling) and baritone Jazzmaster — the synth-pedal + 424 combination is the texture.

=== JOHN MAYER'S DYNAMICS INSIGHT ===
"Where most guitar dynamics start to disappear when you get really low, all the good ones on this pedal are really, really DOWN here" — the most expressive sounds live at LOW volume/gain. Counterintuitive but key: don't just crank it.
