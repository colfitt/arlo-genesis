# JHS 424 Gain Stage — gain-staging power-user tips

The single best how-to source on the Volume / Gain 1 / Gain 2 interaction.

## How to build the drive (the core workflow)
- Build "from Gain 1 into Gain 2" — the most intuitive approach. Gain 1 first (set the character), then Gain 2 to shape/blow it out.
- "Gain 1 will be your primary amount of gain in the circuit and this gain is no joke." It replicates the Portastudio's preamp **input trim**; it handles input-level saturation and is where the dirt is born.
- Gain 2 is the original unit's **channel volume fader**: "Where this leads us is to a place where we can shape the preamp input gain to cool it down or blow it out totally." Gain 2 either tames or detonates whatever Gain 1 set up.

## The loudness/grit trade (the interaction the dossier flags)
- "Both Gains together have the capacity to get big and crunchy when pushed. Keep this in mind when negotiating with the pedal's Volume control — **this sucker gets loud.**"
- Practical read: you can reach the same perceived loudness at different grit levels by balancing the two gains against the master Volume. More Gain 1 + lower Volume = more grit at the same output; less Gain 1 + higher Volume = cleaner at the same output. Volume (master) sets final level independent of the dirt.

## EQ as console-style shaping
- "Want to bottom out the signal for something truly bassy? The Bass control gets you there. Want to spike the highs to get clippy? The Treble control hears you out." Treble pushes the signal toward clippier, brighter breakup; Bass fattens/loosens the low end.

## XLR / DI
- "An onboard XLR out connection with a ground lift switch" runs to an interface or board, **simultaneously** with the ¼" amp out. Ground-lift on hand for hum. (Relevant to the rig's XLR-into-Apollo terminus.)
