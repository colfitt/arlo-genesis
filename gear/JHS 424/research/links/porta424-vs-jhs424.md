# JHS 424 vs MXNHLT Porta424 (and Benson, Neutrino) — why two boxes

Four Tascam-424-style preamps compared head-to-head. The directly relevant pair for the rig is JHS 424 vs MXNHLT Porta424.

## JHS 424 Gain Stage ($249)
- 5 controls: Volume, Gain 1, Gain 2, Bass, Treble.
- "Vast range — from Lo-Fi flavours to quite zingy fuzzy tones." Best-selling of the four; called a "textbook release."
- Highest price; most widely available; **balanced XLR out + ground lift** (the studio/terminus advantage).

## MXNHLT Porta 424 ($170)
- 5 controls: In, EQ Hi, EQ Lo, Out, plus a **physical Level slider** (the real Portastudio channel-fader feel) — the standout aesthetic/UX touch.
- "Excellent aesthetic choices"; author notes it "may have been the one I went for" if more accessible.
- **No XLR output.** This is the key functional gap vs the JHS.

## The other two (context)
- **Benson Portable Distortion 424 MKII ($188):** only 4 controls (Treble, Fader, Bass, Trim) + a **Lo-Z impedance** option; "most attractive" finish; simplified, no independent in/out gain.
- **Neutrino Labs MX 424 ($141):** cheapest; 5 controls; **also has an XLR out** (the JHS's closest functional rival for the DI/terminus role); Mexican brand, import charges apply, fewer demos.

## Why the rig keeps BOTH a PORTA424 and a JHS 424
- MXNHLT = the fuller, faithful channel-strip DI **tone** (slider fader, complete signal path) but no XLR.
- JHS = the gain-stage **commit/print** with the **XLR direct-out + ground lift** and buffered bypass — the right interface to the Apollo. Running PORTA424 → JHS 424 stacks two cassette channels in series (the period-correct "bounce one track to another"). Not redundant: tone box, then print box.
