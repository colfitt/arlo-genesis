OFFICIAL CONTROL MAPPING & DIALING GUIDANCE (verbatim where useful):

CONTROL MAPPING (JHS maps each knob to the real 424 MKI):
- VOLUME = the MASTER fader (output level). Left = less, right = more.
- GAIN 1 = the TRIM control (preamplification / input sensitivity).
- GAIN 2 = the CHANNEL fader (second gain stage volume).
- BASS / TREBLE = shelving EQ. Left CUTS, right BOOSTS.

GAIN STRUCTURE: The three gain controls "work in tandem for dialing up your desired tone." It's a cascading Trim → Channel → Master progression — stack them to go from clean saturation to overdriven distortion. "The 424 MKI's gain controls — trim, channel volume, & master volume — all work in tandem and can be further refined with the Low and High EQ settings."

OUTPUTS (both usable SIMULTANEOUSLY):
- ¼" output → for amplifier.
- XLR balanced output → direct to mixer / interface.
- GROUND LIFT dip switch: "If you experience a hum/buzz while using the XLR output, use this switch to lift the ground." (Directly relevant for the rig's XLR run into the Apollo if the rack hums.)

OFFICIAL TONE COPY (verbatim):
- "rubbery clean tones, high head room, and smashed out fuzz."
- "the same unparalleled gooey texture and lo-fi fuzz you get when plugging directly into a 424 without the hassle of dragging around your beloved vintage unit."
- "a multi-stage gain device giving you everything from a clean, lo-fi sound to blown out fuzz, accurately replicating the full signal path of a vintage Portastudio 424 MKI."
- Use it as "a gain stage to boost your signal, a unique and colorful new overdrive sound, or push the gain knobs into full-on overloaded and broken fuzz."

OP-AMPS: original UPC4570 + NJM4565 (the real 424 MKI parts). Price $249.
