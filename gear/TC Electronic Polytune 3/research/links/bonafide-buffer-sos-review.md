Sound on Sound · review of TC Electronic BonaFide Buffer (same circuit built into the Polytune 3)

The authoritative writeup on the buffer half of the Polytune 3 — the BonaFide is the exact circuit TC dropped inside the tuner.

Specs / what it does:
- "Providing an input impedance of 1MΩ and an output impedance of around 100Ω" — lets it drive downstream pedals and long cable without treble loss. (1 MΩ in / ~100 Ω out is the standard impedance-matching buffer job: present a high-impedance load to the source so it isn't dragged down, then re-drive from a low output impedance.)

Audible difference (the reviewer DID hear one):
- "Patching in the BonaFide made a significant difference to the high end."
- "The guitar also feels more lively when connected to the BonaFide — all that unwanted capacitance otherwise drags it down and loads the pickups."
- His outboard treble booster became unnecessary afterward; he got the same tone as a direct amp connection with short cables.

Placement advice:
- Put it at the START of the pedalboard chain to fix the treble loss caused by long cables and the interconnects/input-capacitance of bypassed pedals between you and the amp.

The one caveat that matters for a fuzz-front-end rig (quoted verbatim):
- "There are few pedals that wouldn't be happy following the BonaFide, with the possible exception of some antique germanium fuzz boxes that can be rather picky about what's fed into them."

Takeaway for a buffer-in-front-of-fuzz decision: a buffer ahead of dirt is fine for essentially everything EXCEPT vintage germanium fuzz, which wants to see the guitar's raw high impedance. Silicon Fuzz Faces and Big-Muff-derivatives are not in that picky category — so a buffered tuner in front of them is a non-issue.
