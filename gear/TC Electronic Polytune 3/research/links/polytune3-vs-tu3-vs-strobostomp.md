Accuracy (spec record, reviewer-confirmed):
- Polytune 3 strobe: ±0.02 cent; chromatic: 0.5 cent.
- Peterson StroboStomp HD strobe: ±0.1 cent.
- Boss TU-3: ±1 cent (no strobe, no polyphonic). The TU-3 is buffered-bypass ONLY — its buffer is always in your signal path, no true-bypass option.
- So the Polytune 3 has the tightest stated accuracy of the three. Forum consensus caveat: "most folks can't hear the difference between a typical vs a high-accuracy tuner," and a more accurate tuner takes longer to settle and more often reports you out of tune.

Polytune 3 — strengths:
- Polyphonic strum-and-go: tune all strings in one glance — unique to the PolyTune line. Fastest between-songs tuning of the three.
- Switchable True Bypass / Buffered (the only one of the three that lets you choose true bypass — relevant for a fuzz front end).
- Auto-dimming display.

Peterson StroboStomp HD — strengths (the banjo/intonation case):
- 80+ (per Peterson, 130+ on current units) Sweetened & Guided tunings — presets that compensate for harmonic drift on fretted instruments, including DEDICATED banjo presets. Users report "intonating more accurately with it."
- One player who does both bass and banjo valued having per-instrument presets with quick setup ("didn't have to scroll").
- Clearer strobe display: users described the PolyTune's strobe as "little pieces of drifting spaghetti," vs the Peterson's more readable readout.
- Verdict from the banjo-specific angle: "the Peterson StroboStomp HD is likely better for banjo-specific tuning needs due to its sweetened tuning presets and superior display clarity in strobe mode, despite slightly lower accuracy specs than the Polytune 3."

Boss TU-3 — strengths:
- Rugged, simple, the other industry standard. "Fine for what most people do." But coarsest accuracy, no poly, no strobe, no true-bypass.

Rig takeaway: for a board that's 90% fast guitar/baritone tuning with occasional banjo, the Polytune's poly speed + true-bypass choice + ±0.02 strobe wins day-to-day. The Peterson is the genuine upgrade ONLY if banjo/open-tuning intonation becomes a recurring bottleneck (its sweetened banjo presets and readable strobe are the real edge). The TU-3's always-on buffer makes it the worst fit for a fuzz-front-end philosophy.
