## 3. Artists & techniques — HONEST assessment
**No notable touring artist has claimed the Lost + Found as a signature voice as of mid-2026.** It is too new and too limited-run. There is no rig rundown (Premier Guitar / That Pedal Show / etc.) that features it. This is genuinely thin — do not expect an artist mythology.

What stands in for "artist usage" right now, and the most instructive uses:
- **Chase Bliss themselves (with Knobs / Joel Korte):** the launch content IS the reference usage. Knobs's studio hosted the walkthrough; the mini-documentary is the Knobs prototyping collaboration. Knobs is the de-facto "signature user." Their demonstrated favorites: **Slow-verb** as the flagship shoegaze swell (pushed with heavy "chunky GLUE"), and the **Sympathetic Resonator** ("12 little pipes tuned to the chromatic scale," called "my favorite mode") used as a forgiving, note-gated drone source — see the walkthrough transcript.
- **Lineage users (transferable technique, not literal Lost+Found rigs):** the modes are condensed versions of CB pedals with established artist followings — **6B Gen Lite ≈ Generation Loss MKII** (the lo-fi/VHS degradation crowd, e.g. ambient/shoegaze producers who use GenLoss for tape-wow walls), and the granular/looping ethos shared with **Mood / Blooper** users. The way those artists use GenLoss (set-and-drone, freeze, heavy degradation) maps directly onto Lost + Found's Gen Lite + FREEZE workflow.
- **Early-adopter community:** ambient/drone/shoegaze and synth/modular players (the TalkBass + MOD WIGGLER threads above) — texture-explorers, not a named star.

**Bottom line for the rig:** treat CB/Knobs's own demos as the "artist reference," lean on the factory Combos as the recipe baseline, and borrow Generation Loss MKII drone technique for the Gen Lite mode. The signature-artist facet is honestly bare; the recipe facet is well-covered by CB's official Combos.
