## Concept (in CB's words)
- "This is our first multi effect that we've ever made. We didn't set out to be like 'what are the staples that every person needs'... What we wanted to do was gather up the most inspiring things to us in one place."
- Recurring pitch: "it makes me feel feelings. It's a real feeler." — i.e. it's an emotion/texture box, not a utility staple.

## Mode-by-mode usage notes (as demoed in order)
- **Mode 1 (Slow-verb):** "Very ambient shoegaze inspired reverb that swells in. Slow ambience." Demo'd first as the flagship sound, then sprinkled with **GLUE** — "It's like chunky glue" (i.e. push GLUE for a thick, compressed/overloaded finish on the ambient swell).
- **Mode 2 (pitch):** "It sounds like pitch to me." Pitch Repeater described as a "little skeleton xylophone player" — the skittering/plinking rhythmic-pitch character.
- **Spectral Modulator:** "we're calling this one a spectral modulator because it's modulating your spectrum."
- **Sympathetic Resonator (5B) — called out as a personal favorite ("this is my favorite mode"):**
  - "You got 12 little pipes, each tuned to a note in the chromatic scale. And if [you're] playing a note that corresponds to a pipe, that pipe will open up and let the audio through. As soon as you stop playing something... it stops making sound."
  - Performance tip from this: "you can be more bold in your musical choices and include things you only want to hear for a second because they get replaced by the things you do want to hear. It's a very freeing algorithm — you don't have to think about what you're doing." (Strong banjo/baritone read: the resonator self-selects pitched content, forgiving of messy input — good for turning banjo plinks into a sustained pipe drone.)
