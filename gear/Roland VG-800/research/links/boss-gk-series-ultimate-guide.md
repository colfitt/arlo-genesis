# The Ultimate Guide to Roland and BOSS GK-Series Divided Pickups

Grounding source for how the GK-5 / Serial GK platform actually works, used to confirm the off-label banjo/baritone premise in the VG-800 dossier.

## Distilled facts
- A divided (hexaphonic) pickup provides **a separate magnetic pickup element under each string**; each string's signal is transmitted on its own channel to the synth/processor. This is *why* the GK-5 can track per-string pitch on ANY string regardless of instrument — it does not "know" it's a banjo.
- **GK-5 released 2023** (the VG-800 host launched Jan 2025). GK-5 outputs the per-string data over the BOSS **BGK serial cable** (1/4" TRS), vs the legacy 13-pin GK-3 (2006).
- Install: **screw-mount or adhesive-strip mount**, shipped with **shim spacers of various thickness to set string clearance** — directly relevant to the banjo, where you must get the element close and parallel under the strings on the flat solidbody surface.
- A **Gibson-style bridge mounting plate** exists; kit versions exist for 6/7-string guitar and 4/5/6-string bass. **No official banjo kit** — banjo mounting is DIY/off-label (confirms dossier).
- Community note from the search layer: a real GK user reports successfully "laying down bass lines and banjo stuff" with a GK divided pickup, calling tracking "impeccable" with bends and picking style translated. First-hand corroboration that banjo tracking is achievable, not just theoretical.

## What it does NOT cover
- No exact string-distance numbers, no per-instrument SCALE guidance, no banjo-specific calibration. Those are inference + the VG-800 GK SETTING manual (DISTANCE 10.0–30.0 mm range, SENS 1–7 per string, SCALE presets).
