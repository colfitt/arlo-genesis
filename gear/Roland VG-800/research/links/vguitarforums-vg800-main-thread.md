vguitarforums.com · "BOSS VG-800" main discussion thread (Elantric/mod, jwhitcomb3, stephenc, admin_shawnb, pasha811, loopman, Scratch17, Smash) · Jan 2025 onward (pages 1–6)

The deepest real-world VG-800 owner thread. High-signal distilled points:

## Hidden features / architecture confirmed by owners
- **Stereo FX SEND/RETURN is re-routable to ANY point in the signal chain.** Elantric: *"The VG-800 Stereo FX Send was my demand… re-routable to any part of the signal chain. Was not present on the initial specification."* (Added after NAMM 2024.) This is a power-user routing win — insert external pedals anywhere.
- **DUAL GUITAR / DUAL BASS mode** works like the VG-99's CHAIN A / CHAIN B. Elantric: *"DUAL GUITAR MODE… like VG-99 CHAIN A, CHAIN B, with 2 separate FX BLOCKS, 2 Separate Amps"* — BUT current firmware **restricts which models are available** in dual mode (you can't pick every instrument on both chains).
- **Per-string / fretboard SPLIT confirmed working** (split the neck so low strings = one instrument, high strings = another).
- **Individual-string bend** via footswitch/expression pedal (poly harmonizer-style).
- **Assignable footswitch combos:** jwhitcomb3 praises being able to assign or turn OFF the [▼]+[▲] and [▲]+[CTL 1] combos so you don't trigger tuner/bypass by accident.
- **"Int pedal" replaced by RISE TIME / FALL TIME parameters** (jwhitcomb3) — *"could craft your own poly slow gear"* using attack/release envelope params even though there's no dedicated poly-slow-gear block.

## Gotchas / limitations owners flag
- **Only ONE distortion block and ONE parallel path** (vs GT-1000's 5 dist / 3 parallel). stephenc: lack of distortion blocks is a *"big limitation… (Ouch)."*
- **2 EQ blocks (parametric only, no graphic), 2 delays** — missing ANALOG/TAPE/REVERSE/SHIMMER delay types and SHIMMER/DUAL/TERA ECHO reverbs that GT-1000 has. Reverb/delay called shallow.
- **UI is "endless button pushing"** (Smash) — no touchscreen; Boss Tone Studio strongly recommended.
- **Wave pedal: no way to retrigger or control phase** (admin_shawnb).
- **Pedal assigns switch at the halfway point**, limiting heel/toe switching flexibility (admin_shawnb).
- **Missing vs older units:** no internal sequencers, no LFO/control waveforms, no HRM (harmony) algorithms, no Vari-guitar (loopman, bosetuno). Several feel only *tracking* improved over SY-1000.
- **GKC-AD external converter** (to use legacy GK-3 pickups) "negates the compact nature" (Vaultnaemsae) — use the native GK-5 instead.

## Comparison verdicts (owner consensus)
- **vs GT-1000:** jwhitcomb3 — *"amps and distortion block are straight out of the GT1000 — a major step up."* This confirms the dossier: the VG-800's amp/dist engine = GT-1000 generation (the good part). 48 kHz vs GT-1000's 96 kHz, but jwhitcomb3: *"48KHz perfectly sufficient"* for guitar.
- **vs VG-99:** Scratch17 — patch portability only *"50% like for like,"* with ~30% *"impossible without dual units"* due to fewer FX blocks.
- **vs SY-1000:** guest posters + admin_shawnb agree it's **guitar-modeling-focused, NOT synth-focused** — one calls it *"basically redundant"* for a dedicated synth user vs legacy hardware. SY-1000 stays the synth flagship.
- **pasha811's framing:** the VG-800 is a *"hybrid between VG (models and synths) and GP (size and focus on guitar)"* — less DSP than GT-1000 but built on the SY-1000 effects foundation.

## For this rig
The single-distortion-block limit doesn't matter — distortion lives downstream in the fuzz wall. The re-routable stereo FX loop and the GT-1000-grade amp/dist block are the keepers. Dual Guitar split (clean low / dirty-ready high) is confirmed real.
