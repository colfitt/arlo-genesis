## What it is, per Boss
"VG" = **Virtual Guitar** — Austin frames it as "a virtual world of creating your own electric guitar / your own electric instrument, in any tuning you want, within an octave of each string." Key points he makes:

- **Serial GK / GK-5:** reads each individual string via the **GK-5 pickup over a TRS cable — "no more 13-pin connectors like in the old GR's and VG's."**
- **Per-string alt tuning within an octave:** standard open tunings (open D / open G / open E), Nashville, baritone, plus **fully custom USER tunings** per string within ±1 octave; one-button retune so you bring one guitar instead of several. Also **12-string guitar models.**
- **Guitar modeling:** turn whatever guitar the pickup is on into variations of Strats, Les Pauls, Teles, semi-hollows, hollow-bodies, plus your own custom guitars — and you can do it **twice over a stereo signal (two different guitar models on a stereo path).** He has patches that do exactly that.
- **Amps & effects ported from the GT series** — "a lot of the AIRD technology" plus a plethora of effects.
- **All-effects bypass:** press two buttons together to bypass every amp/effect and use the unit purely to retune the guitar before feeding the rest of your rig (relevant for this rig if you want raw model → fuzz wall). (This is the [▲]+[CTL 1] FX BYPASS in the dossier.)
- **"A mild sprinkling of synthesis":** GR-300, VIO guitar, and a couple of synth classics from the GP/GR series — explicitly described as light synth, not the focus.
- **NORMAL pickup blend:** you can integrate the guitar's normal magnetic pickups alongside the models — you don't have to use only the synth/model sounds.

## VG-800 vs GM-800 (manufacturer's own distinction)
- **VG-800** = guitar **modeling** + alternate tunings, with sprinklings of guitar synth; kept in a **compact pedal format** for pedalboards/travel.
- **GM-800** = **PCM / "post-modulation" synthesis** — keyboard-type sounds (pianos, Rhodes/electric-piano, cellos, typical synthesis), also off the GK-5.
- Both run off the same GK-5 pickup.

## Controls / signal flow (as he demos)
- Two parts of the signal chain that blend: a **NORMAL (magnetic pickup) side** and the **guitar-modeling section**, combined with the GT-style **dividers / splitters / mixers** (the DIV architecture). You can also control the blend **from the pickup** itself.
- **GK-5 install kit includes two switches:** one scrolls patches up/down, the other **blends the normal magnetic pickup with the GK-5 signal** over the TRS line. (Note: this contradicts the dossier's "no controls on the GK-5" assumption — the GK-5 install kit's S1/S2 switches do exist and do patch-scroll + NORMAL blend. Worth flagging for the orchestrator.)
- **INST menu:** pick instrument (electric, acoustic, dual guitar [e.g. acoustic + electric], bass, VIO guitar, basic synths like GR-300) and set alt tuning / build new guitars.
- **EFFECTS menu:** blend normal vs modeled, scroll with the [SELECT] knob through built-in chorus/reverb/delay and the GT-series AIRD amp models.
- Save the patch; recall it every time. Editable on computer via **BOSS Tone Studio** (Mac/Win).
