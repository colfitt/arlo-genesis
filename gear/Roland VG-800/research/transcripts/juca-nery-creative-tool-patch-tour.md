## Setup caveats he flags
- GK-5 pickup sent by Boss along with a special Roland/Boss TRS jack cable (required — "you have to have a special cable"). He mounted the GK-5 only temporarily with duct tape for the video, "not in the best way," and stresses the proper install needs the full mounting hardware from the Roland bag. **Watch string-to-pickup clearance** ("pay attention that all the strings are close enough to the pickup").
- **No volume control on the GK-5 / no onboard volume slider.** He repeatedly notes he can't easily roll the unit's volume down from the guitar (his temp-mounted pickup, and the unit itself, lack a handy volume); suggests you'd need a CTL/expression pedal for that. (Note: the GK-5 *install kit* does have S1/S2 switches per the Premier Guitar interview, but on his temporary mount he isn't using them — and confirms there's no volume treadle onboard.)
- **Tuner:** press the two footswitches together ([▼]+[▲]) to get the tuner (took him an extra press to trigger).

## Factory patches toured (names + behavior)
- **Ultimate Split** — acoustic on the high strings, different model on the low strings (DUAL split).
- **Wide White 12-String** — CTL-1 toggles between 12-string and normal 6-string; "sounds really good."
- **E V Drop C** — drops tuning without retuning the guitar; he notes Drop C / Drop B / Drop B♭ / Drop A all available; **CTL-1 returns to standard.** Also D-MODAL/Open D, Nashville, etc.
- **DADGAD** acoustic patch — "beautiful sounding," 12-string toggle.
- **Pickup Selector** — CTL-1 switches between **bridge (rear) and neck (front) pickup** position of the modeled guitar — change pickup position with a footswitch.
- **Jazz** patch — a bass on the low strings + jazzy guitar up top, with chorus + delay on CTL-1; good for mixing low-tuned chords with jazz voicings.
- **Wide/Nylon** patch, 12-string-capable.
- **Dual Standard-less-P Blend** — a blend of a Strat and a Les-Paul-type guitar; CTL-1 changes the rig (two guitars at once).
- **Organ / Rotary** — rotary speaker on an organ model; CTL-1 engages the rotary; "really convincing, sounds great"; reacts beautifully to pick attack.
- **Fretless guitar.**
- **Metal Acoustic** — blend of an acoustic guitar and a metal electric, good for rhythm where the acoustic doubles the electric.
- **B-Bender** — bends the B string only (country).
- **Pickup Splitter** — splits the pickups between low and high strings (rig changes on CTL-1).
- **Open G five-string** (the low string muted to emulate a 5-string open-G banjo-style tuning) — different distortion flavor, everything set in the tuning.
- **Razor Bass**, **GR-300 Lead** ("sounds really cool"), patches with a **pitch shifter** on CTL-1 ("kind of crazy but really cool").
- **Fluty Guitar** — flute-like voice; CTL-1 adds distortion to the flute ("crazy tones but really inspiring to write new stuff").
- **Acoustic Ambience** (CTL-1 = pitch-shifter blend), **24th string**, **Mild Jazz** (CTL-1 = chorus), **City Pop**, **Polyharmonic in E minor** (CTL-1 = distortion), **Bluesy Crunch** ("fabulous tones from the GT-1000 core"), **Bass + Acoustic** (bass on low strings, acoustic on g-string up, delay + 12-string), **Double Neck**, **Pickup Morphing**, **Octave Unison**, **Sweep**, **Crystal Echo**.

## Editing note
In BOSS Tone Studio you can deep-edit every parameter — "you can do a lot of stuff you cannot do in other pedals." Each patch's tuning (e.g. Open G) is stored but can be toggled active; EQ to taste.

## His verdict
Loves it as a creative tool — "a guitar synthesizer as well as all the effects and amps you have on a GT-1000/Core, so it's kind of a perfect blend." If you're used to dialing in a GT-1000 Core, the editing workflow is very similar but with many more options.
