# Generation Loss MkII — notable users beyond Stringer (on-aesthetic for this rig)

Honest coverage note: real credits exist but **technical detail (exact settings) is thin** for almost everyone. What's verifiable is *who uses it and in what role*. (Mike Stringer / Spiritbox is captured separately in `spiritbox-mike-stringer-rig-rundown.md`.)

## St. Vincent (Annie Clark) — studio, *All Born Screaming* (2024)
- Confirmed (Reddit AMA, surfaced via Equipboard's St. Vincent gear page): she used the **Generation Loss MkII in the studio** while self-producing *All Born Screaming* (released 2024-04-26).
- Telling for THIS rig: in the same AMA/coverage she also names the **Hologram Chroma Console** and **Hologram Microcosm** as studio textures — the *exact* lo-fi/granular trio this board pairs Gen Loss with. The Microcosm did "kaleidoscopic delay trails... across the record." So a top-tier producer reached for the same Gen Loss + Chroma + Microcosm cluster the dossier's chain is built around — validation of the pairing, even without published knob values.
- No dip-switch settings published. Treat any "St. Vincent setting" as **unverified**.

## Andy Othling (Lowercase Noises / Reverb Nerds) — ambient guitar
- Confirmed user: the **Chase Bliss Generation Loss** is on his ambient board alongside CB **MOOD**, CB **Blooper**, **CXM 1978**, EHX **POG2 / Micro POG**, **Superego**, DBA Evil Filter (Equipboard; Strymon pedalboard feature).
- His *method* (interviews) is the instructive part for using Gen Loss in this style, even though he doesn't give Gen Loss-specific dials:
  - **"Stack two or even three delays to get a big soupy sound"** — Gen Loss feeds that soup as the degraded source.
  - Loves the **"very non-guitar, even non-musical"** textural side — Gen Loss as a texture generator, not a tone-shaper.
  - Treats the **pedalboard like a DAW** with **parallel signal chains** and a volume pedal for swells — i.e. Gen Loss lives on a texture *send*, mirroring how Stringer uses it (ambience layer, not core tone).
- Best primary sources for hearing his approach: his **YouTube (andy othling / "tubescreamer")** and **Reverb Nerds**, plus the Pedal Zone "Picking Andy Othling's Ambient Brain" interview. No Gen-Loss-specific settings doc found — **thin on exact values**.

## Bottom line for the rig
Two independent, credible, on-aesthetic deployments (Stringer live, St. Vincent studio) converge on the **same usage**: Gen Loss as a **degraded-texture layer paired with granular delay (Microcosm) and another color box (Chroma / MOOD)**, engaged for moments rather than always-on. That's the actionable artist intelligence; **published numeric settings essentially don't exist** for any of them.
