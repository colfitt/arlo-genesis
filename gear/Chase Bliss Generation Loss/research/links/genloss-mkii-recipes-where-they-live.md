# Generation Loss MkII — where recipes live, and the concrete dip/settings that exist

## HONEST STATE OF THE LANDSCAPE (read first)
There is **no rich body of named community "recipe shares"** (à la a Hizumitas fuzz thread) for the Gen Loss MkII online. Repeated searches of Reddit, Gearspace, The Gear Page, and the Elektronauts mega-thread surface **almost no copyable dip-switch-value recipes with patch names** ("worn VHS," "dying cassette," "AM radio" etc. as *shared community presets* do **not** appear to exist as a documented set). The pedal's culture is **demo-video-driven, not preset-share-driven**. The authoritative concrete settings are:
1. **The manual's own starting points** (already distilled in `Generation-Loss-DeepResearch.md` §13 — Subtle tape age / Seasick wow-flutter / VHS dropout chaos / Filtered lo-fi bed). Those are the closest thing to "official recipes."
2. **CB's official "tips & tricks"** on the product page + manual (below) — these are *technique recipes* (which dips to flip to get a result), not full patches.
3. **The kevwyxin "read the manual" transcripts** on disk (`research/transcripts/kevwyxin-read-the-manual-part1/part2`) and the model-by-model demo videos — for hearing the Model knob A/B'd.
- Where a real recipe *culture* exists, it's the **Elektronauts thread** (50+ pages, `elektronauts.com/t/chase-bliss-generation-loss-mkii/174731`) and CB's own **Discord** — both gated to web fetching, so I could not extract verbatim user recipes. Flag as **uncovered/thin**, worth a manual skim by the owner.

## OFFICIAL TECHNIQUE-RECIPES (verbatim concept, from CB page + manual)
These are *dip/toggle recipes*, the most concrete published "how to get X" moves:

- **Freeze / infinite sustain (hidden Aux mode):** set the **AUX toggle to FAIL**, then turn ON both **DROP BYP** and **SNAG BYP** dip switches. The left footswitch now freezes the current tape moment. (CB tips + dossier §2.)
- **Tape chorus / "sun-soaked" modulation:** flick **DRY toggle → UNITY**, then bring up **WOW** (Flutter low). "Wow is your key to getting some unique chorus sounds... reminiscent of vibrato but random and rubbery." Unity-dry is required so the clean blends for chorusing. (CB tips, manual pg.17.)
- **"Vintage but not broken":** a little **FLUTTER with WOW off** under an otherwise clean tone — subtle tape-quiver without warble/dropout. (CB tips, manual pg.19.)
- **Tape overdrive / EQ only:** **SATURATE up, everything else off** — uses it as a tape-saturation drive / EQ colour box rather than a wobble effect. (Manual pg.27.)
- **Unpredictable stereo auto-panner / "skitters L↔R but otherwise clean":** turn ON **SNAG BYP + SPREAD + MISO** dips and turn the main knobs *down* — Failure-driven SPREAD splits the (mono→stereo) signal into a randomly panning image while staying mostly clean. (CB tips, manual pg.31.)
- **"Tape Hopper" (auto model-jumping):** BOUNCE the **MODEL** knob with the **RANDOM** dip — the pedal jumps between tape machines on its own. **"Maintenance Phase":** bounce the **FAILURE** knob for ever-changing malfunction. (Manual pg.38–41, dossier §4.)

## COPYABLE STARTING PATCHES (clock-face; sourced from manual §13 of the dossier — the de-facto official recipes)
These ARE the "worn VHS / dying cassette / subtle / destroyed" archetypes, just under the manual's own names:

- **Subtle tape age ("recorded a while ago", near-always-on):** Wow 10:00 · Flutter just below audible · Saturate 9:00 · Failure 8:00 · **Model 4 (Portamax-RT)** · Dry **Small** · Noise off. (Manual's "nice and wistful" home base, pg.4.)
- **Worn VHS / dropout chaos (broken, skipping, glitched):** Wow 11:00 · Flutter 1:00 · Saturate 1:00 · Failure 2–3:00 · **Model 1–3 (CPR-3300 VHS Gen 1/2/3)** · **SPREAD on** · Dry **None** · Aux = **STOP** for tape-halt punctuation. Non-repeatable by design — record it.
- **Dying cassette / seasick wow-flutter (drunken, melted):** Wow 1–2:00 · Flutter 12:00 · Saturate 11:00 · Failure 9:00 · **Model 5 (Portamax-HT, half-speed)** · Dry **None**. Into a long delay + hall = the doom-ambient drone move.
- **Filtered lo-fi / "AM radio" / small-speaker bed (dark, band-limited mids):** Wow 9:00 · Flutter 8:00 · Saturate 10:00 · Failure low · **Model 7–8 (Dictatron) or 9 (Fishy 60 toy)** · Dry **Small**. The dictaphone/toy models roll off both extremes for the narrow "AM/telephone/small-speaker" voice. (Closest thing to an "AM radio" recipe — built from the band-limiting models, not a named preset.)
- **Total destruction:** push everything — Wow 2:00+, Flutter 2:00+, Saturate maxed (mis-biased), Failure maxed, VHS model (1–3), Noise/Hiss on. Nauseating on purpose; reviewers warn the hiss + pitch-warp get fatiguing fast, so this is a moment-effect not a tone. (Synthesizes dossier §3–4 cautions.)

## Model picks by target sound (from the model table, dossier §3)
- Cleanest/biggest "tape" with least colour → **12 M-PEX** (reel-to-reel). Heaviest band-limiting/lo-fi → **7–9 Dictatron/Fishy**. Most low-end retained (good for baritone/bass drones) → **1–3 VHS**. Half-speed melt → **5 Portamax-HT**. Generic warm cassette → **4 Portamax-RT**. Model **0 = OFF** = no EQ colour (use the wow/flutter/saturate as pure effects).
