# Mike Stringer (Spiritbox) — Generation Loss MkII in a live metal/ambient rig

The marquee on-aesthetic artist user for this facet. Stringer runs the Generation Loss MkII alongside a **Chase Bliss MOOD** and a **Hologram Microcosm** (exactly the pairing called out in the brief). Premier Guitar's written rig page only *lists* the board; the concrete usage comes from the Rig Rundown video (transcript captured at `research/transcripts/spiritbox-rig-rundown-067jy4EhQeE.en.srt`).

## How he organizes the board (his own words, video)
- His board is two halves: **"the bottom of this one is stuff that I really need for songs. The top of this one is just like textures and ambience that I can kind of throw in."** The Chase Bliss pedals (MOOD + Generation Loss) and the Microcosm live on that **textures/ambience top row** — not in the core rhythm tone.
- **"If you see two of something it's because it's redundant"** — he runs duplicate ambient pedals (e.g. a pair of EQD Rainbow Machines) for live fail-safety, a useful tell about how mission-critical he treats the ambient layer.

## Generation Loss + MOOD usage (concrete)
- **Song: "Crystal Roses."** Asked specifically about "the Chase Bliss and then the black DL4," he says: **"the Chase Bliss stuff is for when we play a song called Crystal Roses. Um, Zev does a drum solo... so it's like a really over-the-top, just — it just goes. It's just like crazy ambience."** So the Gen Loss / MOOD pair is deployed as an **over-the-top ambient wash during a drum-solo break**, not as an always-on texture.
- **Used sparingly but heavily when on:** "some of these are used kind of more sparingly throughout the set, but when they are on, they add a lot. ... I use them a couple times throughout [the set]."
- He's candid that the pair is newer to him: **"These two, I need more time with because they're just so incredible."** (i.e. no fixed signature dial-in disclosed — treat any "Stringer setting" claim as unverified.)
- The **intro of "Crystal Roses"** (the more structured ambient part) is built from the **Microcosm + Line 6 DL4 MkII + EQD Sunn O))) Life pedal**, with the Chase Bliss pair adding the chaos over the top during the solo. So in his world: Microcosm/DL4 = structured granular delay/ambience; Gen Loss = the degraded chaos layer on top.

## Full board (from PG list + video)
Fractal Axe-Fx/FC-12 + RJM Mastermind PBC/6X core; Peterson StroboStomp tuner; Boss DD-6; EHX Freeze; 2× DigiTech Whammy Ricochet; Line 6 DL4 MkII; **Chase Bliss MOOD + Generation Loss**; EarthQuaker Devices Sunn O))) Life; 2× EarthQuaker Rainbow Machine; **Hologram Microcosm**. Board built by Omilion Audio.

## Takeaway for this rig
Stringer's deployment validates the dossier's placement logic: Gen Loss as a **textural "chaos/ambience" send** layered with granular delay (Microcosm) and a fuzz/octave (Life), engaged for specific moments rather than always-on. He does NOT publish dip-switch values — coverage of his exact Gen Loss settings is **thin/unverified**; the usable signal is *role and pairing*, not knob positions.
