# Four-version A/B by an owner of ALL FOUR — what MkII actually changed

Full cleaned transcript: `research/transcripts/genloss-4version-comparison-prose.txt`. This is the most concrete community comparison of Cooper FX V1 / Cooper FX V2 / CB Gen Loss MkI / CB Gen Loss MkII (including MkII's Classic mode). Reviewer notes Wow, Flutter, Gen and Noise all DIFFER between MkII and its own Classic (MkI) mode — Classic mode is NOT a bit-perfect MkI clone.

## Per-control findings (mono; stereo excluded since only MkII has it)
- **Flutter:** V1 has **no flutter** at all. V2's flutter "drifts in and out, sporadic, not as quick/textural." MkI (CB) is "the most harsh and sporadic." **MkII has a pleasant, mostly-solid texture you can use across its full range without it getting in the way."** → MkII flutter is the most musical/controllable.
- **Wow:** V1 = separate speed AND depth control (the only version that does). V2's wow "drifts in and out, mildest of all." MkI = "harshest wow." **MkII = more extended, subtle range than MkI** but no separate speed/depth (fixed-character random like MkI).
- **Gen (Classic-mode sample-rate knob):** V1 goes lowest (full degrade). V2 bottoms out at **complete silence**. MkI "pleasant but doesn't go as low as V1." **MkII Gen takes you to "a garbled low-frequency mess" but NOT to silence** — so MkII Classic-mode Gen won't fully gate out like V2.
- **Mixing:** V1 = standard wet/dry. V2 = separate dry AND wet level knobs. **MkI & MkII = Dry toggle (None/Small/Unity) + Volume knob for wet** (the toggle-not-knob dry, confirming the manual).
- **Filtering:** V1/V2/MkI all have HP+LP filters. **MkII MkII-mode replaces them with the 12 tape EQ profiles — you do NOT get the standard HP/LP in MkII mode, ONLY in Classic mode** (in Classic, knobs become LP / GEN / HP).
- **Noise:** MkII is by far the most complex — "hiss, two types of mechanical noise, snags, drops, crinkles and pops," mix-and-matchable. Even **MkII's Classic mode has MORE noise options than the original MkI** because it layers MkII's noise sources on top.

## Aux / Freeze — the headline behavioral upgrade
- V1: **no aux functions.**
- V2 aux: tape stop / garbled tape (like Failure but no drops) / spectral freeze — **but on V2 you could NOT play over the frozen note with full-wet** (had to mix dry in).
- CB MkI aux: MOD (max wow+flutter) / GEN (knob→max) / FILTER (bypass filtering).
- **CB MkII aux: tape stop / filter / failure (maxes Failure knob) / FREEZE — and MkII's Freeze LETS YOU PLAY YOUR WET SIGNAL OVER the frozen note.** This is the one big freeze advantage over V2/MkI: drone-and-solo-over-it without needing dry blend. (See `genloss-aux-freeze-classic-trick.md` for how to access Freeze.)

> TAKEAWAY for the rig: MkII's flutter is the most "usable across its full range" of any version, and its Freeze (play-over-the-frozen-drone) is uniquely suited to the looper/drone aesthetic here. If you ever want V1-style separate wow speed+depth, no version of MkII gives it — that's a genuine loss vs the original.
