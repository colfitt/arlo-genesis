# HIDDEN FREEZE access + AUX behaviors — the most-asked "where's the 4th aux?" trick

The Aux toggle shows **Stop / Filter / Fail** on the pedal, but there's a **fourth, hidden Aux effect: FREEZE** (the "spectral freeze" carried over from Cooper FX V2). It is the most-searched hidden behavior in the community and easy to miss because it isn't labeled.

## How to reach FREEZE (community-confirmed access recipe)
Set up this combination, then the Aux footswitch fires Freeze instead of Fail:
- **Aux toggle → FAIL**
- **DROP BYP dip → ON** and **SNAG BYP dip → ON** (both Failure components bypassed)
- **CLASSIC dip → ON (Classic mode)**

With drops + snags bypassed in Classic mode, the Fail aux has nothing to "fail," and CB repurposes the slot as **Freeze**: it captures and holds the last-played sound indefinitely, which you can then keep mangling with Wow/Flutter/Saturate/etc.
> NOTE: this access recipe is what surfaces consistently in community/manual-excerpt searches; treat the exact dip combination as **community-reported, verify against the on-disk manual pg. for the precise wording**. The *existence* of a hidden Freeze and that MkII lets you play over it is firmly confirmed (manual + the 4-version owner comparison).

## Why MkII Freeze is special (vs V2 / MkI)
- **MkII Freeze lets you play your WET signal OVER the frozen note.** Cooper FX V2's freeze did NOT — you had to mix dry in to play on top. (From the Tri Pedal 4-version comparison.) → On MkII you can freeze a drone and keep soloing into the same degraded voice. Ideal for this rig's drone/loop aesthetic.

## All four Aux effects (so the slot makes sense)
| Aux setting | What the footswitch does |
|---|---|
| **Stop** | Tape stop — slows the wet signal to a rubbery halt (and back). Great for punctuation/song-ends. |
| **Filter** | Momentarily **bypasses the model's filtering** (opens it up / "unfiltered") while held. |
| **Fail** | **Maxes out the Failure knob** while engaged — instant dropout/snag chaos burst. |
| **Freeze** (hidden, recipe above) | Holds the last sound indefinitely; **play wet over it.** |

## Momentary vs latching + onset speed
- **The Aux footswitch works BOTH ways: tap = latching on/off, hold = momentary** (releases when you let go). So Fail and Stop can be stab-and-release stutters, or toggled states.
- **Aux Onset speed** is set in the **hidden options menu (hold both footswitches; Aux Onset is on the WOW knob)** — controls how fast Stop/Filter/Fail/Freeze ramp in. Slow onset = gradual tape-halt; fast = abrupt.
- **External momentary footswitch:** the MIDI/aux jack accepts any normally-open momentary switch to fire the selected Aux effect hands-free (no MIDI needed) — useful since the rig already has the pedal feeding a busy stereo End Board.
