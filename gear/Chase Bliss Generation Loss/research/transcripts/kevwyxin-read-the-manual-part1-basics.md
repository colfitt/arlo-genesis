## The knobs

**Wow** — pitch modulation, more intense but smooth and random. Classic tape-style modulation. Turn it up for more.

**Flutter** — a twitchy, random, *really fast* pitch modulation; not as smooth or slow as Wow. It affects both amplitude AND pitch.

**Volume** — noon is unity, fully counterclockwise is off, fully clockwise is a 2x boost.

**Saturate** — gives a magnetic-tape-style distortion. It also boosts your signal a little, like any drive. It's a really nice saturation/drive: very warm and not harsh.

**Model** — sets the type of tape being emulated. There's a library of 12 individual tape machines:
- Fully counterclockwise = OFF (just hearing it normal, bypassing the model)
- 1 = an old VCR
- 2 = the same thing but a second copy done over on the tape (copy of a copy)
- 3 = a third copy — a second-generation re-recorded copy of a copy
- 4 = Portamax RT, a cassette — the all-in-one four-track recorder
- 5 = Portamax HT, the same thing but at half speed
- 6 = a cassette camcorder
- 7 = Dictatron EX, another cassette but a handheld voice recorder
- 8 = another version of a handheld voice recorder (quieter — he gives it a boost)
- 9 = Fishy 60, another cassette
- 10 = MS-Walker, designed by BlankFor.ms, a personal portable music player (Walkman-like)
- 11 = AMU-2, designed by AMULETS — an imaginary model based on a deteriorated tape and a barely functioning recorder
- 12 = M-PEX, designed by Marcus Fischer — a 1/4" reel reel-to-reel sound

**Failure** — fully counterclockwise is off; turn clockwise for more and more intense machine malfunctions: snags, drops, crinkles and pops. Also customizable via the dip switches.

## The toggles

**Aux toggle** — controls what the left (auxiliary) footswitch does. Set to **Stop** = a tape-style stop (signal stays off until you hit it again, then resumes). All aux effects can be momentary: press and hold and it becomes a momentary footswitch — super helpful for performance.
- **Filter** = bypasses the selected tape model, letting your signal pass through unfiltered (also momentary).
- **Fail** = maxes out the failure knob when you click it (momentary too).

**Dry toggle** — how much dry signal is routed to the output:
- **None** = only the tape-affected (generation-lost) signal.
- **Small** = brings in a little dry signal, creating subtle chorusing based on the Wow and Flutter.
- **Unity** = more dramatic chorusing.

**Noise toggle** — None / Mild / Heavy white-noise-type hiss. Noise is affected by the Failure knob (you hear the volume drops in the noise track too). The character of the noise and the whole panel changes based on the model selected.

**Right footswitch positions** = two presets + center (live), then bypass.

## Stereo & basic setup

This pedal is different from most Chase Bliss pedals: it's stereo and uses TRS in/out. Default = "what you put in is what comes out" — stereo in → stereo out, mono in → mono out (no settings change needed). The **MISO** dip (mono in → stereo out) lets a mono input create a stereo output.

## Classic mode (dip switch on the back)

Classic mode reverts to the original Generation Loss (Mk1 CB×CooperFX collab) voicing. It changes some knob functions (labeled in gray): Model becomes **GEN**, Failure becomes **HP** (high pass), and the LP filter sits under Model... (he describes: top alt labels are LP under Model, HP under Failure, GEN under Saturate; Wow/Flutter function the same with minor differences).

**Important classic-mode "no sound" fix:** when you first engage classic mode it can sound broken/silent. Baseline setup that fixes it:
- Turn **GEN** (Saturate knob) all the way UP (fully clockwise) — this is essentially the clean/full-rate mode; CCW degrades it.
- Open both filters wide: **HP** fully counterclockwise (low end open), **LP** fully clockwise (high end open).
- (He maps the filter knobs like a frequency scale: fully CCW ≈ 20 Hz, fully CW ≈ 20 kHz.)

Once set, classic mode turns the pedal into a usable **filter box**: leave GEN up and the filters become a low-pass + high-pass (combine for a band-pass) over your whole signal — a versatile utility filter, and you can use the Aux footswitch / modulation on it.
