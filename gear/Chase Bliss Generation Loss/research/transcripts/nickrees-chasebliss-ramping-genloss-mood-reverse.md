## Ramping mechanics (apply to all standard CB pedals incl. Gen Loss MkII)

Two types:
- **Ramp** = a one-off movement when you turn the pedal on.
- **Bounce** = continuous back-and-forth motion of the chosen parameter(s).

The top row of dip switches mirrors the pedal's knobs — flip a knob's dip to tell the pedal *which* parameter(s) to automate. Below that, the **BOUNCE** and **SWEEP** dips tell it *how*:
- **Mix/ramp knob = SPEED** (left = very fast, right = slower). It moves between the bottom and top points you set.
- **SWEEP** sets the range endpoints, with a **B** and a **T** position:
  - **B** = travel from the knob's *minimum* position up to wherever the knob is currently set.
  - **T** = travel from the knob's *current* position to its *maximum* (full right).
- You set the destination by where you physically leave the knob.

## Gen Loss MkII ramping recipe (as demoed)

1. Start simple: Failure off, no Wow, set a clean-ish tape tone, then dial a bit of Wow.
2. To ramp Wow: flip the pedal up, enable the **WOW** dip, enable **BOUNCE**, keep SWEEP left (B = travel left→right), set the Wow knob to a fairly extreme amount as the destination.
3. Volume/mix knob sets the speed — start fast, slow it for a more pronounced drunken-tape swell.
4. **Ramp multiple parameters at once**: flip on **WOW + FLUTTER** dips to bounce both; add the **MODEL** dip to also sweep through the tape models (big EQ shifts as it crosses models). All three bouncing = wow + flutter + model morphing together.

## Save it

Crucially, you can **save the ramp + dip-switch settings into one of the onboard presets** — recall the evolving/bouncing patch instantly next time instead of re-dialing. (Relevant for a free-running pedal: Gen Loss MkII has no MIDI clock, so internal Bounce is how you get evolving motion, and presets make it repeatable.)
