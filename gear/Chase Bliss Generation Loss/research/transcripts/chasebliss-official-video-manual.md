## Concept & I/O

"Two pedals in one box": the well-known **Classic mode** and an all-new **MkII mode** built from scratch. Think of Gen Loss as a *deconstructed tape machine* with a dedicated, discrete control for each oddity/artifact tape imparts.
- Affects mono OR stereo signals. Default = **mono in / mono out** (good to go).
- Stereo: just use a **TRS cable** in and out — Gen Loss detects it and adapts.
- **MISO** dip = mono in → stereo out.
- **SPREAD** dip (use it with either stereo option): processes each channel uniquely to create a *moving stereo image*.

## MkII controls

- **Wow** — depth of slow, smooth, random pitch modulation; classic tape motion, like an unpredictable vibrato.
- **Flutter** — intensity of fast twitchy random modulation; affects both amplitude and pitch → quivering/trembling tape.
- **Saturate** — recreates a unique magnetic saturation that occurs when loud signals are recorded to tape.
- **Failure** — gradually introduces small malfunctions that give tape its living feel: **snags, drops, crinkles and pops**. Failure is ALSO responsible for the stereo processing when SPREAD is on.
- **Volume** — output level of the wet signal; **noon = unity, max = boosted by double**.
- **Model** — steps through the library of tape-machine models, each with its own unique EQ profile (VCRs, cassette decks, camcorders and more), each designed through frequency analysis of physical hardware.

## Toggles

- **Aux** — selects one of three performance effects fired by the LEFT footswitch:
  - **Stop** = a tape-stop effect.
  - **Filter** = bypasses the selected tape model.
  - **Fail** = maxes out aspects of the Failure knob → major malfunction.
  - Left footswitch: short tap **latches**; longer hold = **momentary** (deactivates on release).
- **Dry** — volume of your clean signal: **None** = pure tape machine; **Small** = a touch of clean, keeps tape dominant but bigger/more detailed; **Unity** = clean matched to input level (useful for chorusing, and interesting under dialed-back extreme settings to sit subtly beneath the dry).
- **Noise** — various amounts of noise and hum; several flavors, set to taste in the hidden options.
- **Preset** toggle (between the footswitches) = recall either of the two onboard presets; center honors current knob/switch positions.

## Classic mode differences

Classic preserves the legacy Gen Loss sounds/controls. Two differences from MkII:
1. Three knobs do their alternate (gray-labeled) function: **LP** = resonant low-pass cutoff; **HP** = resonant high-pass cutoff; **GEN** = sample rate (roll counterclockwise to degrade audio and introduce digital aliasing).
2. An additional performance effect — **Freeze** — is enabled by selecting **Fail** on the Aux toggle + engaging **DROP BYP** and **SNAG BYP** dips. Freeze captures and holds the last sound indefinitely to process with the other effects.
- **SPREAD in Classic** = smooth panning linked to the **Wow** knob (vs the glitchy failure-driven motion in MkII).
- Classic is described as a compilation of parts you can isolate: a filtered chorus, a sample-rate reducer, etc.

## Hidden options (hold both footswitches → LEDs green → move parameter)

- **Wow** = aux onset time (farther CW = longer).
- **Flutter** = hiss volume from the Noise toggle.
- **Saturate** = mechanical-noise volume from the Noise toggle: **noon = 0, CW = hum, CCW = VCR noise.**
- **Failure** = crinkle/pop volume from the Failure effect.
- **Model** = bypass mode: CW of noon = **DSP bypass** (digital-through, click-free); CCW of noon = **true bypass** (default).
- **Dry** = input gain: left = line, middle = instrument, right = High Gain (matches saturation to source level).
- In Classic, the Failure knob's hidden option sets the level of the old "noise mod" dynamic noise.

## Customize dip switches (back panel)

- **DRY TYPE** = applies all effects except Wow/Flutter to the dry → saturated malfunctioning chorus.
- **DROP BYP** = removes volume drops from Failure.
- **SNAG BYP** = removes pitch-warping from Failure (leaves the subtle crinkles/pops).
- **HUM BYP** = removes mechanical noise from the Noise toggle.
- The remaining dips configure external control / internal modulation (ramping). "Try experimenting with various combinations — it's fun."
