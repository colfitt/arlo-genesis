## Hidden options (hold BOTH footswitches until LEDs turn green, then move the named knob/toggle)

- **Aux Onset = WOW knob.** Sets how long the chosen aux effect takes to set in. Farther clockwise = longer; counterclockwise = shorter. Maxed it gets extremely slow — nice slow fade into the Filter aux, e.g. a slow clearing-out of a muddy/dark tone.
- **Hiss Level = FLUTTER knob.** Sets the volume of the hiss introduced by the Noise toggle. You can dial it all the way down so the hiss is basically gone — gives you control over what "mild" and "heavy" really mean.
- **Mechanical Noise Level = SATURATE knob.** Noon = 0 (zero mechanical noise). Rotate **clockwise = hum** (a buzzing-type effect). Rotate **counterclockwise = VCR noise**. Plays beautifully against the Failure knob.
- **Crinkle/Pop Level = FAILURE knob.** Sets volume of the crinkle and pop sounds. Clockwise = loud, counterclockwise = very soft. (You can max Failure for the malfunction motion but pull crinkle/pop level down for cleaner result.)
- **Bypass Type = MODEL knob** (covered in the bypass section below).
- **Input Gain = DRY toggle.** Left = line level, middle = instrument level, right = High Gain (for really distorting stuff). High Gain on a synth/line source sounds more digital; line level gives a better-sounding saturation from a hot source.

## Hidden FOURTH aux effect: FREEZE (Classic mode only)

Recipe to enable it:
1. Set the **Aux toggle to Fail**.
2. Enable **CLASSIC** dip.
3. Enable **DROP BYP** + **SNAG BYP** dips.
Now the left footswitch captures and holds the last bit of sound indefinitely, which you can then process with the pedal's other effects. (This is the old Gen Loss Freeze function.)

## Dip switches (blue = features; white = ramping/expression)

- **MISO** — mono in → stereo out.
- **SPREAD** — creates a stereo image as you turn up the FAILURE knob. Drops, pops, clicks all become individual in the left and right ears — a truly unique stereo sound. (Best on headphones.)
- **DRY TYPE** — applies all pedal effects EXCEPT Wow/Flutter to the dry signal → saturated, malfunctioning chorus effect. (Demo: with it OFF there's chorusing from the wet/dry mix; with DRY TYPE ON the chorusing is gone because the dry now gets the same processing.)
- **DROP BYP** — removes the drops (the volume cut-outs) from the Failure knob. Max Failure, flip DROP BYP: no more volume dips, but still snags and crinkles.
- **SNAG BYP** — removes the snags (the pitch-warping) from Failure. With Wow/Flutter off you can isolate just the pitch movement; SNAG BYP kills it, leaving volume drops + crinkles only.
- **HUM BYP** — removes the mechanical noise/hum from the Noise toggle.

## Ramping & Bounce (no external gear needed)

- Standard Chase Bliss ramping = automate knob movement with an LFO. **Ramp** = one-time movement on engage; set the **BOUNCE** dip and it goes back and forth continuously.
- Pick the knob to ramp via its white dip switch (he ramps **Model**). The **SWEEP** sets the range. Engage **SPREAD** too if stereo.
- **The VOLUME knob becomes the ramp SPEED** while ramping (how long to travel from one end to the other; quick→long). Cool trick: flip the Aux toggle to **Filter** and tap the aux switch to effectively pause/stop the ramp when ramping the Model (because Filter bypasses the model = your ramp target).
- New to this pedal vs older CB: you can still adjust your **actual Volume while ramping** — hold the left footswitch and set Volume to taste, then release and it goes back to controlling ramp speed.

## Signal flow & bypass (Bypass Type hidden option = Model knob)

Hold both footswitches → green → turn **Model knob**:
- **Clockwise past noon = DSP bypass ON** — digital-through even when bypassed; clickless, artifact-free switching.
- **Counterclockwise of noon = true bypass** (the default) — true bypass both channels, analog drive-through on both. (You can hear a little click on true-bypass switching.)
- Third case: in standard mode (DSP bypass off) with **MISO on** you get a buffered bypass on the left channel and digital bypass on the right; analog drive-through left, digital-through right. Most people are fine on standard/true bypass.
