Martin Yam Moller · Chase Bliss Generation Loss mkii Demo of the 12 tape models using familiar sounds · 2023-12-14

A targeted A/B of all 12 Model positions using *familiar* sources (piano, acoustic guitar, drum kit, drum machine) so you can actually hear what each model does — most demos use guitar or abstract synth where it's hard to tell. The single best video for ear-training the Model knob (cross-reference the Deep Research dossier §3 model table for names).

Captions are mostly music; the useful spoken/distilled notes:

- The manual claims sources sound "dramatically different model to model," but in practice the differences are **subtle to the ear** at extreme settings — the host (and editing-Martin) admit they often *can't tell* which model is selected by sound alone.
- The Model knob has **no detents/indents**, so you can't tell which step you're on by feel — a design gripe; you have to listen. (Relevant if you ramp/recall by position.)
- Setup used: **mono in → stereo out** with **MISO + SPREAD** on, so you also hear each model's stereo behavior, not just its EQ.
- At the **top models (11 AMU-2, 12 M-PEX)** the audible EQ difference is small; "mainly it seems to be doing more in the stereo field."
- The pedal "responds quite heavily to filtering" — the model voicings read most clearly as band-limiting / EQ shaping, especially on percussive sources (drum machine, drum kit), where the high/low rolloff per model is most obvious.
- These were "quite extreme settings" — Wow/Flutter/Failure pushed — so the model EQ sits under a lot of motion.

Takeaway for picking models: differences are real but most pronounced as **EQ band-limiting + stereo spread**, and clearest on transient-rich material. For a clean A/B of just the EQ profiles, dial Wow/Flutter/Failure low.
