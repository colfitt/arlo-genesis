GAIN-STACKING WITH FUZZ + MUFF (directly applicable to 108 -> Hizumitas and the gating problem)
- *** DRIVE INTO A GATED FUZZ = LONGER SUSTAIN (key recipe): "The Fuzz Factory often gates notes with a velcro-like buzz, so we're using the Tube Screamer to slam the front-end of the Fuzz Factory and make it SUSTAIN LONGER." -> A drive/boost pushing the INPUT of a gated fuzz fattens its level and reduces the gate, extending sustain. ***
  RIG APPLICATION: the M173 silicon FF gates on decay. To tame that gate WITHOUT the Hizumitas, slam the 108's input harder (Colour Box level up, or a boost in front). And note: this is ALSO the mechanism behind the dossier's "Hizumitas -> 108" order-swap suggestion — a Muff in front feeds the 108 a hot, low-Z signal that both drives it cleanly AND masks its gating.
- MUFF FIRST, BOOST AFTER: "there's a big jump in volume, which drives the amp harder, and the Muff tone sustains longer." -> a clean boost AFTER the Muff = more volume + longer sustain (drives the downstream stage harder), NOT more dirt.
- CLEAN BOOST AFTER DIRT: "adds clean volume" + "top-end sparkle" without coloring the dirt. -> use a transparent boost (Brothers AM clean-ish) after 108+Hizumitas to reclaim level/sparkle without piling gain.
- EQ INTERACTION: a mid-pushed drive (TS) in the stack keeps "the fuzz's bass [from becoming] 'woofy'" — the TS mids tighten the fuzz low end. -> A mid-focused EQ/drive (Brothers AM, Oxford) can tighten the 108->Hizumitas low end and keep the wall from going woofy/undefined.

NET: the 108's gating is fixable by FEEDING IT HARDER (boost/drive/Muff in front) or by slamming it harder from the Colour Box; a mid-pushed drive in the stack tightens the combined low end.
