HENDRIX & THE SILICON FUZZ FACE (Band of Gypsys era) — attribute to the SILICON FF CIRCUIT, not the M173
- Hendrix "more than likely used a BC108 silicon Fuzz Face" for the Band of Gypsys concerts at the Fillmore East, NYC, New Year's Eve 1969 / Jan 1 1970. (HEDGED — "more than likely." His earlier, more famous tones were germanium.)
- "Fuzz Face pedals started using higher gain SILICON transistors instead of the germanium ones in the originals. They were nastier, edgier, and they gave his fuzzier, harder songs a biting edge." -> the silicon voice = nastier/edgier/biting = exactly the M173's character feeding the dark Hizumitas.
- The Dunlop Jimi Hendrix Fuzz Face is "a faithful reproduction of the 1969-70 Dallas Arbiter Fuzz Face that Jimi used on classic albums like Band of Gypsys."
- Roger Mayer supplied Hendrix's late-era fuzz/Octavia/modified-wah gear; the design "had evolved from germanium to silicon" by this period.
- Signal-chain order (Hendrix): wah -> fuzz -> Octavia -> Uni-Vibe, i.e. FUZZ EARLY, right after the wah, everything else after. (Same principle as the M173 sitting in the dirt stage with all time/mod/space downstream.)

RIG TAKEAWAY: The M173's silicon BC108 is literally the Band-of-Gypsys-era transistor family. Honest framing: "the nasty, edgy silicon Fuzz Face voice Hendrix moved to in 1970" — never claim Hendrix used this MXR box.
