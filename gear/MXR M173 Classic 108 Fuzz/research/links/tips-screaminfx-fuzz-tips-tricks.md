FUZZ PROBLEM/SOLUTION PAIRS (practical)
- ANY PEDAL IN FRONT CAUSES CRACKLE: "A pedal before the fuzz, EVEN IF TURNED OFF, buffers or boosts the guitar signal and causes the fuzz to get a CRACKLING sound." FIX: move the fuzz closer to the guitar. -> note: this implies even a buffered-bypass pedal LEFT IN-LINE (not just engaged) can degrade a FF. In Pedalxly the Colour Box + VG-800 are always in-line, so the M173's own buffer switch is the compensating fix.
- BEST PLACEMENT: Guitar -> Wah (optional) -> Fuzz -> Time Effects -> Amp. Fuzz right after the guitar so the guitar's volume knob controls the voltage into the pedal. (Matches the rig: 108 in the dirt stage with all time/space downstream.)
- VOLUME-KNOB CLEANUP MECHANISM: "your fuzz cleans up very easily with the volume knob of your guitar" by controlling the CURRENT into the fuzz's LOW input impedance. REQUIREMENT: "requires NO buffer between guitar and fuzz." -> confirms the cleanup magic needs a direct passive-pickup-to-fuzz path (absent in the GK->VG-800->Colour Box path; only a passive SG/JM straight into the front would restore it).
- WAH+FUZZ OSCILLATION FIX: screeching oscillation when wah precedes fuzz (impedance mismatch -> positive feedback). FIXES: put wah after fuzz, OR insert a BUFFER between wah and fuzz. -> this is exactly what the M173's buffer switch does internally ("removes oscillation caused by some wah-wahs").
- SILICON vs GERMANIUM: silicon has "more gain and aren't as smooth sounding"; germanium is smoother/more responsive.

RIG TAKEAWAY: reinforces (a) cleanup needs a passive guitar with nothing buffered in front, (b) the buffer switch is the rig's fix for the always-in-line Colour Box/VG-800, (c) the buffer switch also kills wah oscillation if a wah is ever added in front.
