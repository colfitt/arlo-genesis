# Settings — MXR M173 manual factory sample settings

Source: M173 owner's manual PDF (`Gear/MXR M173 Classic 108 Fuzz/manuals/M173.pdf`, p.2 "SAMPLE SETTINGS").
Official Dunlop-named presets. The manual prints three knob-diagram cards: **BROWN ACID**, **BLACK IRON**, **KINGS OF THE DESERT**. Layout per card: VOLUME (left) · BUFFER LED/switch (center) · FUZZ (right).

> Caveat: the printed dials are tiny line illustrations; positions below are read from the manual diagrams and are approximate clock-face estimates, not numeric callouts (the manual gives no numbers).

| Preset | VOLUME (Output) | FUZZ (Input) | Character (name implies) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Brown Acid** | ~1–2 o'clock | ~1–2 o'clock | Balanced psych/stoner "brown" fuzz — both controls past noon |
| **Black Iron** | ~10–11 o'clock | ~11–12 o'clock | Lower-output, mid-gain — darker/heavier |
| **Kings of the Desert** | ~1 o'clock | ~1–2 o'clock | Desert/stoner-rock fuzz — fuzz up, healthy output |

## Manual's own directions (verbatim sense)
- "Start with all controls at **12 o'clock**." Rotate FUZZ CW for more fuzz; rotate VOLUME CW for more output.
- "Engage the **BUFFER** switch to eliminate the oscillation that sometimes occurs when a wah-wah is in front of the Classic 108 Fuzz. You may notice a **brightening** of the fuzz tone."
- All three named presets are stoner/doom-adjacent ("Brown Acid," "Kings of the Desert," "Black Iron") — Dunlop is steering this pedal at exactly the heavy/desert/stoner-doom space this rig lives in. The named voicings cluster FUZZ around noon-to-2 o'clock, not maxed — consistent with the demo finding that the big gain jump is in the top quarter and most usable voicings sit 12–3 o'clock.

## Rig read
- "12 o'clock start" + buffer note = the manual's own baseline is a sane starting patch for this board: knobs at noon, then flip BUFFER ON to coexist with the Colour Box / VG-800 in front (dossier §5).
