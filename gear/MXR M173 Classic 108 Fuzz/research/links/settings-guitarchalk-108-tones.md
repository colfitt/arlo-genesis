# Settings — Guitar Chalk: MXR Classic 108 Fuzz sample settings

Source: https://www.guitarchalk.com/mxr-m296-classic-108-fuzz-mini-settings/
(Covers the Mini M296 — identical BC108 circuit + buffer to the full-size M173. Knobs on a 0–10 scale; Volume = OUTPUT, Fuzz = INPUT gain.)

| Tone | Volume | Fuzz | Buffer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Classic Bluesy Grit | 6 | 3 | OFF | Warm, lower-gain vintage crunch, dynamic |
| 70s Classic Rock Crunch | 7 | 6 | OFF | Full-bodied, fills out riffs + leads |
| Full-On Psych Fuzz | 8 | 9 | ON | Massive wall-of-fuzz for psych/shoegaze |
| Modern High Gain Doom | 9 | 8 | ON | Unrelenting, focused mids + huge bottom for metal/sludge |
| Stacked Fuzz Booster | 8 | 2 | ON | Low fuzz, high output — for stacking into a dirty amp |

## Guitar pairings
- **Strat / single-coils** — classic 60s/70s articulate fuzz.
- **Gibson SG / humbuckers** — pushes the 108 into "creamy, saturated" stoner/classic-rock fuzz.

## Rig read
- Note the two "wall/doom" presets (Psych Fuzz, High Gain Doom) both call for **Buffer ON** — consistent with the dossier's buffer-ON-in-this-rig default and the shoegaze stacking use.
- "Stacked Fuzz Booster" (Fuzz 2, Volume 8, Buffer ON) is the closest published analog to using the 108 as a low-fuzz, high-output PRE-stage feeding more gain downstream — i.e. the 108→Hizumitas role, where the 108 supplies grind/level and the Muff supplies the wall.
- The 0–10 scale converts to clock-face roughly: 3 ≈ 9–10 o'clock, 6 ≈ 12–1, 8 ≈ 3 o'clock, 9 ≈ 4 o'clock, 10 = max.
