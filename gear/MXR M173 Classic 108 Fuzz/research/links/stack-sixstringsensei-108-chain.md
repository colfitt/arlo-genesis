108-SPECIFIC STACKING + BUFFER + CLEANUP NOTES (NOTE: Agent 1 owns full settings/review capture — this file only logs the STACKING/BUFFER/CLEANUP-relevant bits for the stacking facet)
- *** RECOMMENDED CHAIN (validates the 108-before-Muff order with a drive between): "MXR fuzz FIRST in chain, then a light overdrive like a Tubescreamer or Boss SD-1, then a BIG MUFF." ***
  -> This is fuzz -> (light drive) -> Muff. It directly supports the rig's 108 -> Hizumitas order, and suggests a LIGHT OVERDRIVE BETWEEN the 108 and the Hizumitas (the Brothers AM or a low-gain Oxford) to push/tighten the fuzz before it hits the Muff (cf. the gating-cure mechanism in stack-musicradar).
- BUFFER SWITCH: "makes the pedal brighter... great for brightening up a darker guitar," and useful BECAUSE the pedal has no tone control + helps pair with wahs.
- VOLUME CLEANUP: "does clean up well with the volume control on your guitar... responsive to player dynamics" — reviewer reports cleanup works with buffer ON or OFF (this is with a real passive guitar straight in; in the rig's GK path that cleanup is unavailable).
- The reviewer STILL preferred the 108 FIRST in the chain despite the buffer switch -> the buffer switch is a tolerance feature, not a reason to bury the fuzz; "fuzz wants to be early" remains true (and it IS early-ish in the rig's dirt stage).
- Versatile across guitars: classic through a Jazzmaster, modern/high-gain when pushed with a Les Paul.

RIG TAKEAWAY: the canonical 108 chain is fuzz -> light drive -> Muff. The rig's 108 -> Hizumitas is the same shape minus the middle drive; consider inserting a low-gain drive (Brothers AM ch) between them to push the 108 and reduce its gating before the wall.
