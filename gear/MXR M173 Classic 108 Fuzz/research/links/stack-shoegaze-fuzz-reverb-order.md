SHOEGAZE / WALL-OF-SOUND ORDERING — FUZZ vs REVERB/DELAY (Board-1 fuzz feeding the Board-2/3 wall)
- CONVENTIONAL (Pedalxly's current layout): fuzz EARLY -> long reverb/delay LATE. Set delay long repeats + high feedback for "a haunting atmosphere that lingers." Stack analog (dark repeats) against digital (crystalline) delays for contrast. -> 108 -> Hizumitas (Board 1) feeding the Big Time / H90 / Dark Star / Microcosm wall downstream = textbook shoegaze.
- THE UNCONVENTIONAL SHOEGAZE MOVE — FUZZ *AFTER* REVERB: "If you place your fuzz AFTER your reverb pedal, you'll get the iconic and chaotic distorted reverb that modern shoegaze and noise-rock bands use." Putting fuzz after reverb creates "thick, woolly tones" and the fuzz "can interact with your reverb tails in unique ways" — "with a bit of knob-tweaking, you can even get your fuzz pedal to function... similar to a compressor, squeezing your reverb to make the tails linger and then ramp the controls to spiral everything into chaos."
  RIG NOTE: the M173 is hardwired into the Board-1 dirt stage (before all time/space), so fuzz-after-reverb isn't its slot. BUT this is a strong reason to keep a fuzz available to drop AFTER a reverb (e.g. via the H90/Board-3 routing, or running the 108 into a reverb send) for the chaotic distorted-reverb shoegaze texture if the owner ever wants to experiment. Worth an A/B in the studio.
- "An easy way to experiment... a go-to trick for many shoegazers... is toying with the ordering of your fuzz and reverb." -> permission/encouragement to A/B fuzz->reverb vs reverb->fuzz.

NET: current 108-early/wall-late order = the classic wall. The fuzz-AFTER-reverb trick (distorted reverb, fuzz as a reverb-tail compressor/chaos engine) is the experimental shoegaze alternative the rig could reach for in the studio.
