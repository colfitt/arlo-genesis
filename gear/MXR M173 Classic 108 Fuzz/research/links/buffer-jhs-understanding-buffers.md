DISTILLED (general buffer lore as it applies to a Fuzz Face in a buffered chain like Pedalxly):
- Fuzzes need to see a HIGH-impedance source at their input to sound right; a buffer in front presents a LOW source impedance and the fuzz sounds thin/weak.
- "Certain fuzz designs only work if there's no low-impedance circuit (like a buffer or engaged overdrive pedal) in front of it" — so they only work if (a) first in chain, OR (b) every pedal before them is true-bypass AND disengaged.
- Standard fix: put buffers/buffered-bypass pedals AFTER the fuzz, not before. Let the fuzz see the guitar (or, here, the closest thing to a raw source) and buffer everything downstream.

RIG NOTE: In Pedalxly the M173 is NOT first — it sits after the Colour Box (buffered preamp) and the VG-800 (modeled buffered line) and after the CB Clean. By the textbook rule it should misbehave. The M173's onboard BUFFER switch is the engineered exception: it gives the fuzz a defined high input Z so it tolerates the buffer in front instead of going thin/gated. This is the single feature that lets this Fuzz Face survive 5th in a buffered chain (a vanilla FFM-series Fuzz Face would not).
