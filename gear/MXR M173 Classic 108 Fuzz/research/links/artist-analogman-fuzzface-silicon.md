SILICON FUZZ FACE HISTORY (authoritative builder source)
- "Silicon fuzzfaces came out in about 1969. Dallas Arbiter used both of these types [BC108, BC109] in their Fuzz Face pedals."
- Silicon transistors (BC108/BC109/BC183) are "fuzzier than the germaniums, and brighter" with "much higher gain."
- BC183 "is a little warmer than the BC108 and still have plenty of fuzz."

CLEANUP — HONEST NUANCE (important correction to the "silicon doesn't clean up" myth)
- Silicon FFs "don't get totally clean by turning the guitar down like the NKTs [germanium] but they do clean up QUITE A BIT and are sensitive to your dynamics."
  -> So: silicon cleans up partially (less fully than germanium), and is still touch/dynamics-sensitive. Not all-or-nothing.
- General FF volume sensitivity (germanium example): Strat vol ~7 = very little distortion; ~9 = smooth bluesy (Clapton/Cream); 10 = quite fuzzy.

PLACEMENT TIPS
- "For the purest tone, there should be no effects between the guitar and the fuzz. However, effects with true bypass that are turned OFF are OK before the fuzz." -> true-bypass-but-OFF in front is acceptable; a powered BUFFER in front is the problem.
- Vintage wahs have issues with vintage FFs; ZVex Wah Probe works; Foxrox Wah Retrofit fixes others. (This is exactly the oscillation the M173 buffer switch targets.)
- Germanium power warning: reverse-polarity; do NOT share non-isolated supply with normal-polarity pedals or you'll blow the supply. (N/A to the silicon M173, which is normal center-negative, but worth knowing as FF lore.)

FAMOUS USERS — SILICON FF, ATTRIBUTED HONESTLY (do NOT credit the MXR M173 itself)
- DAVID GILMOUR: confirmed he used Analog Man BC108 Sun Faces for the 2006 On An Island tour; in 2015 got a BC109 Sun Face ("had our BC108 Sun Face but wanted something with a bit more edge"). The often-repeated "BC109 on Dark Side of the Moon" link is UNVERIFIED here — the page only says Gilmour "may have had these [BC109] put in his Fuzz Face sometime." FLAG AS UNVERIFIED for specific DSOTM tracks.
- ERIC JOHNSON: "I THINK Eric Johnson has BC183 in the red fuzzface that he used for years" and the 2012 Dunlop EJ signature "seems to use a BC183 too." -> builder's belief, FLAG AS UNVERIFIED / "I think."
- Jon Carin (Pink Floyd/The Who touring) used BC183 live; Guy Pratt (bass, Gilmour 2006 tour) used a BC108 silicon. (Sideman uses — more solid since builder supplied them.)

RIG TAKEAWAY: The M173 is a modern BC108 silicon FF — same transistor family Gilmour/EJ-class players reached for when they wanted the brighter, higher-gain, edgier silicon voice. Attribute the SOUND to the silicon FF circuit, never to the M173 box. Flag Gilmour-DSOTM and EJ-BC183 as unverified.
