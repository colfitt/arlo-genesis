SILICON FUZZ FACE QUIRKS / PROS (why silicon earns the M173's slot vs a germanium FFM1)
- TEMPERATURE STABILITY: germanium "can overheat and change the sound of your pedal from an amazing one, to something that sounds very cruddy — very quickly"; small/moderate temp rises cause "drastic bias changes" in germanium due to leakage current (at high temp germanium can go muddy, unresponsive, or OVER-GATED, abruptly cutting notes). Silicon is far more temperature-stable -> reliable on a studio board, gig to gig, no warm-up drift.
- CONSISTENCY: germanium needs unit-auditioning ("test different Fuzz Faces at your local Music MegaMart"); silicon is "much more consistent from unit to unit." -> the M173 is a known, repeatable voice.
- TONE: silicon = "bright and cutting," "edgier," "ruder" overall, higher gain. -> ideal raw splat to feed the dark Hizumitas.
- VOLUME CLEANUP TRADE-OFF: silicon FFs "don't clean up entirely with your volume knob" like germanium — framed as a different character (favors maximum saturation), not a flat defect. (Analog Man nuance: silicon still "cleans up QUITE A BIT" and stays dynamics-sensitive — just not all the way to clean.)
- TRANSISTOR FLAVORS: BC108 = temperature-stable + smooth(er); BC183 = "extreme rudeness." The M173 is BC108 = the stable/smooth-leaning silicon voice (consistent with Premier Guitar's "darker/fuller than you'd expect from silicon").

GATED "SPLAT" AT LOW SETTINGS
- The gated, sputtery decay at low Fuzz / with bright sources is inherent silicon-FF topology behavior, intensified by single-coil/bright transients (banjo!). Tame it by FEEDING IT MORE LEVEL (drive/boost into the input — see stack-musicradar) or lean on the downstream Hizumitas to fatten + EQ; OR embrace it as on-aesthetic degraded splat for doom/shoegaze.

RIG TAKEAWAY: silicon is the pragmatic choice for a studio/gig board — stable, consistent, bright, rude. The cost (less-complete volume cleanup) barely matters here because the GK->VG-800->Colour Box path already removes the passive-pickup cleanup magic regardless of transistor type.
