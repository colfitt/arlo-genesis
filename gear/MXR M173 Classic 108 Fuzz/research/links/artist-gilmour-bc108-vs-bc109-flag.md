*** HONESTY FLAG: GILMOUR + BC109 + DARK SIDE OF THE MOON IS CONTESTED ***
- The widely-repeated claim (e.g. Mitchakes, many blogs): "Gilmour used a BC109 silicon Fuzz Face on Dark Side of the Moon — heard on 'Time' and 'Money.'"
- BUT gilmourish (the most detailed verified Gilmour-gear reference) QUESTIONS this: it asks where the BC109 claim even comes from / whether there's a source, and states the Dallas Arbiter Fuzz Face Gilmour used was a BC108, NOT a BC109.
- Transistor distinction noted: BC108 = warmer, slightly more compression (more typical silicon FF voice); BC109 = more gain, less compression, edgier. Analog Man independently supplied Gilmour a BC108 Sun Face (2006) and a BC109 only LATER (2015, "wanted more edge"), and only says he "MAY HAVE had [BC109] put in his Fuzz Face sometime."

CONCLUSION FOR THE DOSSIER (honest framing):
- SAFE TO SAY: Gilmour used a SILICON Fuzz Face on Dark Side of the Moon (1973) — heard on the "Time" and "Money" solos. Silicon FF lineage confirmed.
- DO NOT assert BC109 specifically: the authoritative source (gilmourish) says it was a BC108 and disputes the BC109 story. Flag "BC109 on DSOTM" as UNVERIFIED / likely a BC108.
- The M173 is a BC108 silicon FF — so "BC108 silicon Fuzz Face, the same transistor type behind Gilmour's Dark Side silicon tone" is the accurate, defensible line (and ironically MORE accurate than the popular BC109 myth).
