GAIN-STACKING PRINCIPLES (apply to 108 -> Hizumitas and the Board-1 dirt stack)
- THE SECOND PEDAL IS THE GATEWAY/VOICE: "the second pedal in your chain acts as a gateway that shapes the final sound." The pedal AFTER does the EQ/voicing of the combined tone. -> In 108 -> Hizumitas, the HIZUMITAS is the gateway: its Tone control and Muff EQ shape the final wall, and the 108 just feeds it raw splat. This is WHY the order works for banjo (108 has no tone control; Hizumitas supplies the EQ).
- "Place your favorite base-tone pedal LAST, or follow it with a transparent boost to preserve its character." -> If the Hizumitas wall is the signature voice, keep it last in the dirt stage (it is) or follow with the Brothers AM as a transparent boost/shaper.
- GAIN STAGING: "Volume before dirt = more gain, where volume after dirt = more volume." Cranking the FIRST pedal's output increases CLIPPING in the second pedal, not overall loudness. -> Pushing the 108's Output knob harder = more saturation INTO the Hizumitas (denser wall), not just louder. Use the 108 Volume as a "how hard do I slam the Muff" knob.
- EQ AFTER DIRT rescues a lost-in-the-mix Muff: "Getting lost in the mix using a big muff? Crank up the mids a bit." -> Use the Brothers AM (or downstream EQ) to reclaim mids that the 108->Muff scoop swallows.
- "There are no wrong ways to stack your gain" — encourages the unconventional 108-before-Muff experiment.

EXAMPLE STACKS (analogous): lower-gain/mid-pushed pedal INTO a higher-gain distortion adds low-mid punch and saturation; "fuzz into [drive]" = classic fuzzy tones.
