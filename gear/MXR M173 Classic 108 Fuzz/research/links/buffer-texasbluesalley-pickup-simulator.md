THE PROBLEM
- Certain fuzz designs require a high-impedance input and fail when preceded by low-impedance circuits like buffers or engaged overdrive pedals: "Certain fuzz designs only work if there's no low-impedance circuit (like a buffer or engaged overdrive pedal) in front of it."
- They only work if first in chain, OR if every pedal before them is true-bypass AND disengaged.

THE WORKAROUND — A PASSIVE PICKUP SIMULATOR
- Place an inexpensive PASSIVE PICKUP SIMULATOR in front of the fuzz. It presents the high-impedance, pickup-like source the vintage fuzz wants, so you can put the fuzz ANYWHERE in the chain (not just first) and still get correct behavior.
- Example named: Happy Valley Analog Deluxe Passive Pickup Simulator. (Class of product: "pickup simulator" / "fuzz interface" pedals.)

RIG TAKEAWAY (Pedalxly):
- This is the most direct real-world solution to the M173's situation. A passive-pickup-simulator placed immediately BEFORE the M173 (after the Colour Box) would re-present a pickup-like high-Z source to the fuzz and could restore Fuzz-Face-correct behavior even with buffers upstream — an alternative to relying solely on the onboard buffer switch.
- It would NOT restore the guitar-volume cleanup (no passive volume pot in the path), but it would restore the bloom/feel of a properly-loaded fuzz input.
- Cross-ref: the owner ALSO has passive guitars (SG, MIJ Jazzmaster). Plugging one of those straight into the front of Board 1 (ahead of the buffers) is the zero-cost version of the same fix and additionally restores real volume-knob cleanup.
