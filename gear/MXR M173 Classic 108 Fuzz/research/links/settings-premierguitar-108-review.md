# Settings — Premier Guitar: Quick Hit MXR Classic 108 Fuzz review

Source: https://www.premierguitar.com/gear/quick-hit-mxr-classic-108-fuzz-mini-review
premierguitar.com · Charles Saufley · 2018-06-21
(Reviews the Mini M296 — identical BC108 circuit + buffer as the full-size M173.)

CONCRETE SETTINGS / TEST RIG (quoted):
- Test rig: "Fender Stratocaster and '68 Fender Bassman."
- The reviewer's reference patch: **"BC108 output and fuzz at 100%. All volume adjustments via guitar volume."**
  -> i.e. run OUTPUT and FUZZ maxed (5 o'clock) and do ALL dynamics with the guitar volume pot. This is the classic "fuzz cranked, ride the guitar volume" Fuzz Face technique — and it requires a passive guitar straight in (NOT the rig's GK/VG-800 path; cleanup is muted there per dossier §4).

BUFFER ON vs OFF (quoted):
- Buffer OFF: "The basic voice (without buffer) is **rich and perhaps darker and fuller** than you might associate with a silicon Fuzz Face."
- Buffer ON: the unit becomes **"brighter and... behaves much more predictably when there's a wah in front."**

TONE-SHAPING (quoted):
- The reviewer liked the "slightly duskier shade — **especially when I double down with guitar tone attenuation**" (i.e. roll the guitar TONE down to darken the no-tone-control fuzz).
- Also loved "the **strangled chaos** that comes with non-buffered wah recipes" — endorses Buffer OFF for chaotic/broken textures (on-aesthetic for doom/noise).

RIG READ:
- Premier Guitar's own reference is fuzz/output MAXED + ride guitar volume + roll guitar tone to darken. In this rig the maxed-fuzz voice is available, but the volume/tone-pot dynamics need a passive SG/Jazzmaster in front of the buffers; from the GK path, lean on the Hizumitas downstream for the tone attenuation Saufley does with the guitar tone knob.
- "Darker/fuller without buffer" + "duskier with tone attenuation" = a reviewer-confirmed path to taming the bright banjo: Buffer OFF and let the Hizumitas roll off treble.
