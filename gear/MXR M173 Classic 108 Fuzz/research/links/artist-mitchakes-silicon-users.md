SILICON FUZZ FACE — FAMOUS USERS, WITH HONESTY FLAGS (attribute to the silicon FF circuit, NOT the M173)
- JIMI HENDRIX (Band of Gypsys era): "more than likely used a BC108 silicon Fuzz Face." HEDGED ("more than likely") — solid but not documented-certain.
- DAVID GILMOUR: article states as FACT (no hedge) that he "made the switch to silicon transistor Fuzz Faces in the early 1970s" and "used a BC109 silicon transistor Fuzz Face in the recording of Dark Side of the Moon."
  *** CONFLICT / FLAG UNVERIFIED: Analog Man (the actual builder who supplied Gilmour his fuzzes) only says Gilmour "MAY HAVE had these [BC109] put in his Fuzz Face sometime." The builder's hedge outranks this blog's flat assertion. Treat "Gilmour BC109 on Dark Side of the Moon" as PLAUSIBLE BUT UNVERIFIED. ***
- ERIC JOHNSON: "more partial to BC183L silicon transistors," "can be heard using them on songs like Cliffs of Dover." Fairly confident framing; Analog Man independently says "I THINK Eric Johnson has BC183 in the red fuzzface." -> LIKELY but still builder-belief, mild flag.
- GARY CLARK JR: listed as a silicon FF user, no model/detail. Unqualified mention — low confidence.

SILICON TONE: "brighter overall when compared to germanium transistors, and have more edgy distortion and fuzz to the sound."

PLACEMENT / PAIRING TIP: "Fuzz Face after your wah pedal, but before any other effect." Also notes "Ibanez Tube Screamer and a Fuzz Face are a classic pairing" (TS-into-FF / FF-into-TS gain stack).

NET HONEST LINE FOR THE DOSSIER: silicon FF lineage = Hendrix (Band of Gypsys, "more than likely"), Gilmour (BC109, early-70s/DSOTM — UNVERIFIED per builder), Eric Johnson (BC183L, likely), Gary Clark Jr (mentioned, low confidence). None used the MXR M173; the M173 is a modern reissue of this circuit family.
