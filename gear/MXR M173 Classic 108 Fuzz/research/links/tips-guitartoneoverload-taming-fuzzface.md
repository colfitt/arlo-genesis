# Tips — guitarTONEoverload: How to Tame a Fuzz Face Type Fuzz Pedal

Source: https://www.guitartoneoverload.com/2012/01/02/how-to-tame-a-fuzz-face-type-fuzz-pedal/
guitartoneoverload.com · Romain · 2012-01-02

THE PROBLEM (quoted): a Fuzz Face "only has a Fuzz (gain) setting and a Volume setting" — no tone/EQ control to tame brightness/aggression. (Same limitation as the MXR 108.)

THE FIX — OVERDRIVE *AFTER* THE FUZZ (the technique, quoted):
- "Just place an overdrive pedal after the fuzz... roll off the Treble knob a bit."
- Use **"the least amount of gain"** on that overdrive.
- Mechanism: the post-fuzz drive's treble roll-off supplies the EQ the Fuzz Face lacks, and a touch of low-gain drive smooths/fattens the fuzz.

NO numeric dial positions for the Fuzz Face itself (none to give — only Fuzz + Volume).
(The author's demo fuzz is a Z.Vex Fuzz Factory, set "stab on max and comp on low" to approximate a standard fuzz — not the MXR, noted for completeness.)

RIG READ:
- This is EXACTLY the rig's downstream-EQ strategy in third-party words: the 108 has no tone control, so a low-gain drive after it (the CB Brothers AM, or a low-gain Oxford) with treble rolled off does the taming. Corroborates the UsageGuide §5 "insert a low-gain drive between 108 and Hizumitas" and DeepResearch §9 "Brothers AM after the fuzz to shape, don't pile gain."
- For the bright banjo through the 108 (no tone knob), the documented fix is a post-fuzz low-gain drive with treble rolled back — alongside the Hizumitas Tone sweep.
