**Best for:** original-launch M173 demo with a humbucker source (Gibson SG) into a Dr. Z amp — confirms the 108 takes humbuckers well into "creamy/saturated" territory, buffer punches up high end, and it cleans up with guitar volume. (No knob numbers given; impressions demo.)

## Rig / signal path
- **Gibson SG (humbuckers) → MXR Classic 108 → Dr. Z amp.** "Blinding blue" LED (he jokes about two bright lights — true bypass + buffer LEDs).

## Findings
- Played a **dirty** sound first ("most fuzzes don't really like" being pushed hard with humbuckers, but the 108 handled it), then cleaned up.
- **Guitar-volume cleanup confirmed** — "cleaned up the sound just a little bit" by rolling the guitar volume back; "crazy... I like it. Cool fuzz box."
- **Buffer button "seemed to punch up some high end"** — consistent with every other source: buffer = brighter.
- "Kind of like a fuzz face" — easy to use. Possible caveat live: "may not totally punch out over the top" of a band mix (gated/spitty fuzz, not a thick wall on its own — which is why the rig pairs it INTO the Hizumitas for the wall).
- Liked it both clean and dirty with the Dr. Z and humbuckers.

## Rig relevance
- The owner's passive **Gibson SG** is exactly this source. If run straight into the 108 (ahead of the buffers, not via the GK path), this is the "creamy saturated humbucker fuzz" + volume-cleanup response the 108 was built for.
