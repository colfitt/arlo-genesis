Middle Aged Gear Junkie · Fuzz Face Mini Shootout: Germanium vs Silicon · 2019-05-14

**Best for:** silicon-vs-germanium Fuzz Face voice comparison (Dunlop FFM minis: germanium FFM1 red vs Hendrix silicon FFM3) — confirms the silicon = top-end sizzle / germanium = mid-range growl split, AND independently documents the same FUZZ-knob taper and the buffer-sensitivity-with-anything-in-front problem.

## Pedals
- **Germanium** Fuzz Face Mini (the red one, FFM1) vs **Jimi Hendrix silicon** Fuzz Face Mini (FFM3, BC108-family — same transistor type as the MXR Classic 108). Signal: germanium first → silicon second, guitar straight in.

## Buffer-sensitivity problem (why the MXR's buffer matters)
- He **couldn't pre-record a loop and play it through the looper into the fuzzes** — "the germanium fuzz face does not play well with pedals in front of it." Putting the looper in front made it sound broken, **"and it also affects the other [silicon] fuzz-face as well, even at low gain."** Had to plug guitar straight in.
- This is the exact failure mode the MXR M173's onboard buffer fixes — and why a plain FFM1/FFM3 would misbehave 5th in the rig's buffered chain while the M173 survives.

## FUZZ-knob taper (matches the Pedal Picassos finding)
- "The FUZZ dial doesn't do much between zero and about halfway — there's not really a whole lot — and even from there to about 3 o'clock that's still not a whole lot. It's just that last quarter of gain, that's where it all is." Same on the germanium.
- **Takeaway: the usable gain change is loaded into the last ~quarter of the FUZZ sweep** (~3 o'clock → max). Below noon does little. He flags this as a usability con of the Fuzz Face design generally.
- VOLUME: "the more you turn the gain up the volume increases quite drastically" — you have to push OUTPUT up at high FUZZ to reach unity.

## Silicon vs germanium voice
- **Germanium: more "mid-range growl."**
- **Silicon (the Hendrix/BC108 type): "a bit more top-end sizzle."**
- He thought the silicon "sounded better with the wire" (in front of a wah/buffered thing), and preferred the germanium on its own — i.e. the **silicon's brightness is the one that survives buffering better**, which is on-message for the MXR 108 living behind the Colour Box.
