The Pedal Picassos · MXR 108 CLASSIC FUZZ · 2023-05-19

**Best for:** the single most useful settings demo — the exact FUZZ-knob taper, buffer on/off A/B, volume cleanup, the fuzz-down-as-boost trick, AND a direct shoegaze/droney "stack delay+reverb in front" endorsement. Rig-relevant.

## Rig / signal path
- MXR Classic 108 → Boss Katana (clean channel first, then dirty channel), small amount of reverb on the amp. Single-coil-ish guitar.

## The FUZZ-knob taper (the key finding)
- "With the FUZZ all the way down and the VOLUME all the way up you can actually push the front of your amp a little bit and get more dirt out of it" — i.e. **FUZZ at minimum + VOLUME high = clean boost / amp-pusher**, not a fuzz.
- Even on the **lowest FUZZ setting there's still a little bit of dirt in there.**
- Sweeping FUZZ up: **"not much difference at all until you get around past 12 o'clock"** — past noon "adds a bit more girth, a bit more bottom into your signal."
- **"Around to sort of 3 o'clock things really kick in,"** and **from 3 o'clock to maximum "there's a pretty big jump in gain."**
- Practical read: the useful voicing knee is **12 o'clock → 3 o'clock**; the top quarter (3→max) is where the big saturation jump lives. Setting FUZZ at max isn't necessary to get full fuzz.

## VOLUME knob
- Lots of makeup level; he runs it "all the way up" with FUZZ down to push the amp, then drops it to roughly unity once the fuzz is engaged.

## Volume-knob cleanup
- "Really responsive to your picking and your volume — just like a good old fuzz face should be." Rolls guitar volume off and the fuzz decongests; rolls back up and it dirties. (Note: this is a passive guitar plugged straight in — the rig's GK/VG-800 path won't reproduce this; see dossier §4.)

## Buffer switch A/B (explicit)
- The 108 is a fuzz face, so "you are going to muck around with an old fuzz face — it is going to upset the signal" when you put anything (wah, tuner, buffer) in front.
- **Buffer ON "brings back all your top end and all that sort of stuff."**
- **Buffer OFF (with something in front) sounds noticeably "muffled."** He demos the dark/muffled buffer-OFF tone directly.
- Verdict: with anything buffered in front, flip the buffer ON to restore the highs and let it "play nicely."

## Shoegaze / drone endorsement (directly rig-relevant)
- "If you're a shoegazer... in theory you can stick it anywhere in your chain. You should be able to stick it absolutely anywhere — stick a delay and a reverb in front of it and have all that going into it if you're into shoegaze, to get those really big epic droney sort of sounds." The onboard buffer is what makes putting time/space (or any buffered preamp) in front of it viable.

## Dirty-channel use
- Into the Katana's dirty channel he runs it "with the FUZZ all the way down and the VOLUME all the way up" as a **boost first** — "super awesome." Fuzz-as-boost into an already-dirty amp = stacked saturation.
