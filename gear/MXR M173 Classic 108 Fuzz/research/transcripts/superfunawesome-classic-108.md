**Best for:** the deepest buffer-on-vs-off A/B across multiple guitars + gain settings — establishes the rig-critical "buffer brightens, but full-gain + buffer = brittle; buffer-off = too dark" tradeoff, plus volume-knob cleanup. (Demos the Mini; identical BC108 circuit + buffer to the M173.)

## Rig / signal path
- Pedal → "Creambaby"/Cheoah-style amp into a single 12" Greenback, captured via cab loader (MTP IR), into the computer. Boss DD-8 delay in the loop. Multiple guitars (Rio Grande "Muy Grande" bridge pickup, a Tele/broadcaster with wide-range pickup, a dark neck humbucker, an '81 Ibanez AS100).
- Two knobs: OUTPUT and FUZZ, plus the BUFFER on/off switch.

## Buffer switch — the central A/B (most useful takeaway)
- Both knobs at **12 o'clock**, effect on: buffer ON is audibly **brighter** ("it gets much brighter; I like the top end"). Buffer OFF is **darker/fuller**.
- **At full gain (FUZZ maxed):** the hosts split — one prefers it **without** the buffer ("I like it better without the buffer... it's a bit dark" buffer-off vs "a bit bright" buffer-on). Consensus that emerges:
  - **Buffer OFF + full FUZZ = almost too dark.**
  - **Buffer ON + full FUZZ = almost too brittle.**
  - This is exactly the rig tradeoff: the buffer is the brightness lever, and which way you flip it depends on the source brightness.
- **At lower gain:** buffer ON is "kind of cool" and the added brightness flatters a **dark/low-output pickup** (the dark neck humbucker) — "you kind of want that brightness from the buffer because it's quite a dark pickup." Buffer-off + low gain on a dark pickup = "almost too dark."
- Practical rule extracted: **dark source → buffer ON; already-bright source → consider buffer OFF; high gain pushes you toward buffer OFF to avoid brittleness, low gain toward buffer ON to keep life.**

## Volume-knob cleanup
- With FUZZ cranked, rolling the **guitar's volume knob down cleans it up "really nicely"** and the pick attack comes through; roll up to dirty again. Confirmed the classic fuzz-face dynamic. (Passive guitar straight in — the GK/VG-800 path mutes this; dossier §4.)

## Verdict
- "Quite versatile for something that has just volume and fuzz." Easy to use; the buffer switch + guitar volume are the two voicing levers.
