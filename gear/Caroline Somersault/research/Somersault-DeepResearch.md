# Caroline Guitar Company Somersault — Deep Research

A working dossier for the Somersault as it lives at position 11 on Board 1 — the warbly, pitch-unstable layer that sits *after* the Boss BF-3 flanger and immediately *before* the Boss CE-2W (the stereo-split point) and the Strymon Deco V2. In this rig it is the last piece of genuinely lo-fi *modulation* before the mono front-end collapses into chorus + tape saturation and goes stereo. Its whole job is tape-wow pitch instability and seasick warble — exactly the "recorded-wrong"/degraded character the owner is chasing, planted right where it'll season the CE-2W and Deco that close the board.

## 1. Lineage: Caroline's "small batch distortery" and the faux-Leslie request

Caroline Guitar Company is a boutique builder in **Columbia, South Carolina**, founded in **2010 by Philippe Herndon**, a former touring/recording musician who walked away from a corporate MBA track to build pedals ([Caroline About page](https://carolineguitar.com/about/); [Equipboard brand page](https://equipboard.com/brands/caroline-guitar-company)). The house style is hand-assembled, opinionated, slightly chaos-leaning circuits with a heavy strain of dry humor — the manuals read like zines, and the company calls its shop the "small batch distortery™." Caroline is also frequently cited as one of the first pedal companies to crowdfund a build.

The Somersault was unveiled at **NAMM in January 2019** (Herndon walks through it in a [NAMM '19 interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=75PPA_o-ue8)) and is described by Caroline themselves as "the chorus-y/vibrato-y/faux-rotary device you have been asking us to make for years" (manual). The manual's tongue-in-cheek "musical/personal agenda" quotes are the design brief in disguise: *"I wouldn't really want to live in the 1980s, but I like to pretend I'm there every once in a while"* (the 80s chorus target) and *"Sometimes a fake Leslie sound is as cool as the real thing, and it's almost always lighter"* (the faux-rotary target). So the lineage here is not a clone of any one classic — it's Caroline's take on the analog-feel BBD chorus/vibrato lineage (think Boss CE-2 / Arion / EHX small-clone territory) re-voiced for instability and lo-fi grime.

Architecturally it's a **hybrid**: per Caroline's own copy, "the digital chip sets the delayed wet signal, [while] the initial gain stage, dry signal, LFO, control set and output mixing stage are entirely analog" ([Delicious Audio](https://delicious-audio.com/caroline-guitar-somersault-lofi-chorus-vibrato/); [Reverb product page](https://reverb.com/p/caroline-guitar-company-somersault-lo-fi-modulator)). That's the secret to its lo-fi waver — the wet delay line is a digital chip, but everything you *feel* (the LFO shape, the mix, the wobble) is analog. A limited **"Asteroid" edition** exists as a cosmetic/art variant; the circuit is the same.

## 2. Controls

Four knobs, two toggles, two footswitches. Everything below is taken straight from the manual, which lists the layout explicitly.

- **MIX.** Wet/dry blend. Fully counter-clockwise = 100% dry; fully clockwise = 100% wet. This is the chorus-vs-vibrato control: any blend of dry+wet is **chorus**; pure wet (full clockwise) removes the dry reference and gives you **pitch-vibrato** (Leslie/pitch-bend behavior with no anchor). This is the single most important knob for deciding what kind of pedal the Somersault is at any moment.
- **OFFSET.** "Sets the time difference between the modulated and dry signals — higher settings will have an increasingly dramatic effect" (manual). At low offset you get tight, subtle doubling/chorus; crank it and you push into detuned slapback-chorus and seasick territory. This is the knob that takes it from "tasteful 80s shimmer" to "broken."
- **DEPTH.** Intensity of the LFO wave on the modulated line — how far the pitch swings. Low = gentle waver, high = dramatic warble approaching pitch-bend.
- **SPEED.** Rate of the oscillator sweep. Slow = lagging, woozy vibrato; fast = ray-gun/rotary wobble. (This is the control HAVOC overrides — see §2/§4.)
- **WAVE toggle.** Selects **triangle** (smooth, classic chorus/vibrato sweep) or **square** (glitchy, pulsing, stepped — the "bleep-bloop"/chopped character). Square is where the lo-fi/broken aesthetic lives.
- **TONE toggle.** Two positions: **full-range**, or a **hi-end cut on the wet signal** only. The hi-cut darkens the modulated voice without dulling your dry — this is the "lo-fi" filter, and it's what makes the wet line sit like a degraded tape track under a clean dry signal. (Note: some retailer copy calls Tone a "knob"; the manual is explicit that it's a *toggle*, full-range vs hi-cut.)
- **BYPASS footswitch (left).** Standard true-bypass engage/disengage.
- **HAVOC™ footswitch (right).** Caroline's signature momentary switch. On the Somersault, "the HAVOC™ removes the SPEED control from the equation, maxing the rate on demand" (manual). Hold it and the LFO instantly slams to maximum speed — no ramp — for a rotary speed-up / ray-gun stutter, then drops back to your dialed Speed when released. The same momentary HAVOC concept runs across Caroline's line (it triggers runaway oscillation on the Wave Cannon, Kilobyte, and Météore); here it's a speed override rather than self-oscillation ([Equipboard brand page](https://equipboard.com/brands/caroline-guitar-company); [Caroline Wave Cannon Zero](https://carolineguitar.com/product/wavecannonzero/)).

## 3. Sonic Character: chorus vs vibrato vs lo-fi

Think of it as a continuum set by **MIX**:

- **Chorus (any dry+wet blend).** With Mix at noon, Offset low, Depth moderate, triangle wave: shimmering, slightly hazy 1980s new-wave chorus and doubling. It is **not** a hi-fi, glassy chorus — even at its most "tasteful" there's a softness and a slight pitch-instability the CE-2W won't give you. Caroline's own framing: "Gentle, lagging vibrato, 1980s style choruses, and even some doubling effects" ([Reverb](https://reverb.com/p/caroline-guitar-company-somersault-lo-fi-modulator)).
- **Vibrato (Mix full wet).** Kill the dry and you get true pitch vibrato — woozy, Leslie-ish, seasick. Push Offset + Depth and it approaches "pitch-bended slapback chorus territory" ([Delicious Audio](https://delicious-audio.com/caroline-guitar-somersault-lofi-chorus-vibrato/)). This is the warble the owner wants under the wall.
- **Lo-fi / faux-rotary / glitch.** Square wave + hi-cut Tone + high Offset = the degraded end: chopped, pulsing, "bleep-bloop," fake-Leslie huff-and-puff. The hi-end cut on the wet line is what sells the "tape/recorded-wrong" illusion — the modulated voice sounds like a worn cassette under a clean dry.

How lo-fi is it *really*? Honestly, less aggressively destroyed than the name suggests at conservative settings — at low Offset/Depth it's a perfectly usable, even pretty, chorus. The "lo-fi" is **opt-in**: it lives in the wet pitch-waver, the hi-cut toggle, and the square wave, and you summon it by pushing the knobs. One widely-quoted reviewer line: "short of buying [Chase Bliss] Generation Loss, you won't find a better lo-fi chorus pedal" ([Delicious Audio](https://delicious-audio.com/caroline-guitar-somersault-lofi-chorus-vibrato/)) — which is a useful, honest calibration of where it sits relative to the End Board's heavier degradation tools (see §9, §14).

## 4. Behavioral Notes

- **Pitch wobble / instability.** The wet line is genuinely pitch-unstable by design — even modest Depth introduces an audible waver. With long sustained drones from the front-end fuzz/drive stack, this is the seasick motion the owner is after. The wobble is *consistent* (LFO-driven), not random, so it's predictable enough to play to a tempo if you watch the Speed knob.
- **HAVOC speed slam.** No ramp-up — hold HAVOC and the rate is instantly maxed, then snaps back on release. That makes it a *gesture*, not a setting: rotary-style fast/slow swells, glitch stutters at the end of a phrase, ray-gun fills. It's momentary; you hold it for as long as you want chaos. (Unlike the oscillation-style HAVOC on Caroline's dirt/delay pedals, the Somersault's HAVOC is purely a Speed override — it won't self-oscillate into noise on its own.)
- **Offset is the "danger" knob.** Most of the "broken" character comes from Offset, not Depth. Past about 2 o'clock it gets dramatic and detuned fast; that's where seasick lives.
- **No tap, no expression jack.** The Somersault has no tap-tempo and **no expression input** — Speed is knob-set, and the only "expression" is your foot on HAVOC (see §8, and the note about the GearProfile flag).
- **Mix is a true blend**, so at full-wet there's no dry anchor and tuning-sensitive material (chords, drones with clear fundamentals) will audibly seesaw in pitch — embrace it for the aesthetic, or pull Mix back to chorus if it's too much.

## 5. Signal-Chain Placement (Board 1 specifics)

Order on Board 1: `… → Oxford Drive → Boss BF-3 → **Somersault** → Boss CE-2W (stereo split) → Strymon Deco V2 → (stereo to Board 2)`. Observations for this exact slot:

- **After the BF-3, before the CE-2W is the right home.** The Somersault is the *unstable* modulation; the CE-2W is the *stable, hi-fi* chorus. Putting the wobbly one upstream means the CE-2W and Deco both receive an already-pitch-warped signal and widen/saturate it — you get instability baked into the stereo image rather than a clean signal smeared after the fact. Modulation stacking generally wants the more extreme/character pedal first, and that holds here.
- **It's the last mono-aware modulation.** Everything from the CE-2W onward is stereo. So the Somersault is your last chance to introduce pitch-waver *before* the field opens up — which is exactly where you want it, because a mono pitch-wobble feeding a stereo chorus produces the widest, most "underwater" image.
- **Stacking three modulations (BF-3 → Somersault → CE-2W) is a lot.** All three on at once is a swirling mess (sometimes desirable for the drone/shoegaze wall, often muddy). Treat them as a palette: BF-3 for jet-flange sweeps, Somersault for seasick pitch, CE-2W for width. Rarely all three full-up.
- **Into the Deco.** The Deco's tape saturation + double-tracker is the perfect downstream partner — saturate the Somersault's wobble and the lo-fi character compounds into genuine "tape-ruined" territory. The Deco's own wobble (if you've enabled it) plus the Somersault's LFO can beat against each other; nudge one slow.
- **True bypass means no tone-suck when off**, and it sits late enough in the chain (after all dirt) that buffering upstream — the CB Clean, Colour Box, Brothers AM are all buffered — keeps the signal healthy into it. No impedance concerns here.

## 6. Source-Specific Notes (banjo, baritone, VG-800, bass)

No published documentation covers the Somersault with banjo, baritone, or the VG-800 specifically, so this is inference from how the circuit behaves — flagged as such.

- **Gold Tone EBM-5 banjo (via GK-5 → VG-800).** Banjo's fast attack and bright transients will make the *square-wave* setting sound especially choppy/percussive — potentially great for rhythmic glitch, potentially harsh. Engage the **hi-cut Tone toggle** to tame the wet line's top end, and favor the **triangle** wave for musical waver. Pitch vibrato (full-wet) on a banjo drone is a genuinely novel, seasick texture.
- **Baritone Jazzmaster.** This is home turf for the lo-fi vibrato — low, sustained baritone notes under a full-wet, slow-Speed, moderate-Depth setting give you that woozy doom-vibrato wobble. Watch Offset on low notes; too much and the pitch seesaw gets disorienting against a fundamental.
- **VG-800 modeled / synth signal.** Because the VG-800 output is already a summed, processed signal by the time it hits Board 1, the Somersault just treats it as audio — pad/organ COSM patches turn into lush, drifting choruses, and synth-y patches into ray-gun vibrato with HAVOC. The lo-fi waver pairs especially well with the VG-800's cleaner modeled tones, dirtying them up.
- **Bass (Jazz Bass).** Full-wet vibrato on bass loses the fundamental anchor and can sound untuned; keep **Mix toward dry** (chorus blend) on bass so the dry low-end stays solid while the wet adds shimmer. No documented bass-specific issues, but the BBD-style wet line will thin slightly — the dry blend compensates.

Caroline is boutique, and the Somersault doesn't have a marquee signature-artist mythology the way the Hizumitas has Wata. Where coverage is thin, it's thin — flagging that honestly.

## 8. Live / Power / I/O Notes

- **Power.** 9V DC, center-negative, 2.1mm barrel (BOSS PSA standard). The manual explicitly warns: **do NOT run at higher voltage.** No 18V trick.
- **Current draw.** Retailer listings state **~30 mA** ([guitarpedalshoppe spec listing](https://guitarpedalshoppe.com/Caroline-Guitar-Co-Somersault-Chorus-Vibrato)). Caroline's manual doesn't print a number, so treat 30 mA as the best available figure — trivially small for any isolated supply slot. *(Unverified against an official Caroline spec — flagged.)*
- **Bypass.** True bypass ([Reverb](https://reverb.com/p/caroline-guitar-company-somersault-lo-fi-modulator)).
- **I/O.** Mono in, mono out — standard 1/4" (6.3mm) jacks. **No stereo out, no expression jack.** Important for this rig: the Somersault does *not* perform the stereo split — that's the CE-2W immediately downstream. The Somersault stays entirely in the mono domain.
- **GearProfile flag.** The repo's `GearProfile.md` lists `expression: true`. I found **no evidence of an expression-pedal jack** on the Somersault in the manual or any retailer spec — the manual's only "expression" mechanism is the momentary HAVOC footswitch. This appears to be a metadata error and should likely read `expression: false` (HAVOC is foot-controlled but not a jack). **Flagging — not changing any file.**
- **Footprint.** Standard compact Caroline enclosure; small pedalboard footprint. (Exact dimensions not printed in the manual; not verified.)

## 9. Pairing Recommendations (this rig, by name)

- **→ Boss CE-2W (immediately downstream).** The textbook pairing in this chain. Run the Somersault as the *unstable* layer (full-wet vibrato or seasick chorus) and let the CE-2W add stable width on top — you get a wide, pitch-drifting image the CE-2W alone can't make. Don't run both as "chorus" simultaneously at full depth or it muds; pick one to be the character and the other the width.
- **→ Strymon Deco V2 (downstream).** Saturate the Somersault's wobble through the Deco's tape sim to compound the lo-fi character; use the Deco's doubletracker for thickness. This two-pedal block is your "tape-ruined chorus wall." Beat the Deco's wobble against the Somersault's LFO at slightly different rates for a more random, organic waver.
- **BF-3 (upstream).** Use sparingly with the Somersault — flange + pitch-vibrato together is a specific, heavy swirl. Great for one big shoegaze moment, too much as a default.
- **HAVOC tricks.** Hold HAVOC for rotary-style speed-up swells into a chorus, or stutter it rhythmically at phrase-ends. Into the Deco's saturation, HAVOC bursts read as glitchy tape-flutter.
- **Honest redundancy with the End Board.** This rig has a *lot* of lo-fi/tape gear downstream: **CB Generation Loss** (the gold standard of degraded tape), **Chroma Console**, and the **PORTA424 / JHS 424** cassette-channel-strip pair. The Somersault's lo-fi is real but mild by comparison. So don't think of it as "your lo-fi pedal" — think of it as **lo-fi *modulation* that lives on Board 1**, so you can warp the dry tone *before* the front-end's chorus/saturation, where the End Board can only degrade what already exists. The synergy: Somersault wobble → recorded through Generation Loss/PORTA424 later = pitch instability *and* tape degradation, compounding. The redundancy: if you're already running Generation Loss heavy, a subtle Somersault may be inaudible — reserve it for when you want the wobble early in the signal, not as a third tape pedal.

## 11. Mods / Quirks / Known Issues

- **No mod scene to speak of** — boutique, low production, hybrid digital-wet circuit, so it's not a popular breadboard target. Caroline's warranty explicitly excludes modifications.
- **Tone is a toggle, not a sweep** — you get full-range or hi-cut, with no in-between. If you want the wet darkened halfway, you can't; it's binary.
- **Full-wet on tonal material seesaws in pitch** — by design, but it surprises people who expect chorus when Mix is maxed. That's vibrato, not chorus.
- **No tap / no exp jack** — Speed is hand-set only; HAVOC is your only foot-controlled parameter.
- **Hybrid architecture** — the digital wet chip means it's not a "pure analog" chorus despite the analog dry/LFO/mix path; mention only because some buyers want all-analog BBD and this isn't strictly that.
- No widely reported reliability failures; Caroline build quality is well-regarded in the boutique tier.

## 13. Starting-Point Settings

Knob positions clock-face, looking down at the pedal.

**(a) Subtle seasick chorus** — pretty, but slightly wrong
- Mix: 11 (mostly dry) · Offset: 10 · Depth: 10 · Speed: 11
- Wave: triangle · Tone: full-range
- Use into the CE-2W (off or subtle) for gentle width. The chorus you reach for under quiet passages.

**(b) Deep vibrato warble** — woozy, Leslie-ish, doom
- Mix: 5 (full wet) · Offset: 12 · Depth: 1–2 · Speed: 10 (slow)
- Wave: triangle · Tone: full-range (or hi-cut for darker)
- Best on baritone/sustained drones. Lose the dry anchor on purpose. Let it seesaw.

**(c) Lo-fi tape-wow on the wall** — degraded, recorded-wrong
- Mix: 1 o'clock · Offset: 2 (high) · Depth: 12 · Speed: 9 (slow)
- Wave: triangle · Tone: hi-cut
- Run into the Deco's tape saturation. This is the "worn cassette under the fuzz wall" sound. Pull Speed slower than the Deco's wobble so they drift apart.

**(d) Havoc swell / glitch** — gesture, not a setting
- Mix: 3 o'clock · Offset: 1 · Depth: 2 (max) · Speed: 9 (slow base)
- Wave: square · Tone: hi-cut
- Hold HAVOC for an instant ray-gun/rotary speed-up, release back to the slow base. Square + hi-cut sells the bleep-bloop. Stutter it at phrase ends into the CE-2W/Deco.

## 14. Versus Alternatives — why it earns its slot

- **vs Boss CE-2W (in this very chain).** The CE-2W is the *stable, hi-fi, authentic* chorus (CE-1/CE-2 voices). The Somersault is everything the CE-2W refuses to be: unstable, pitch-drifting, lo-fi, gesture-driven (HAVOC). They're complements, not competitors — and that's precisely why both are on the board, back to back. The Somersault gives you the *wrong* chorus the CE-2W can't.
- **vs Boss BF-3 (upstream).** Flanger ≠ pitch-modulator. The BF-3 sweeps a comb filter (jet/whoosh); the Somersault wavers pitch (seasick/Leslie). Different motion entirely; the Somersault covers the chorus/vibrato space the BF-3 can't.
- **vs CB Generation Loss / Chroma Console / PORTA424 (End Board).** Those are *tape-degradation* engines — heavier, more destructive, and stereo/late-chain. The Somersault is *modulation* with a lo-fi tilt, living early on Board 1. It earns its slot by warping pitch *before* the front-end chorus/saturation (something the End Board, downstream, structurally cannot do) and by being playable/gestural (HAVOC) rather than a set-and-forget degrade. Be honest: if you want maximum tape-ruin, that's Generation Loss's job, not the Somersault's.
- **vs a "normal" boutique lo-fi chorus (e.g. mid-priced analog chorus/vibe).** Most lo-fi choruses don't give you a square-wave glitch mode, a true full-wet vibrato, *and* a momentary speed-slam in one box. The Somersault's range — tasteful 80s chorus to full ray-gun chaos — plus the HAVOC gesture is what makes it more than a one-trick warble.

In this rig — drone/doom/shoegaze, "recorded-wrong" aesthetic, a CE-2W + Deco block right behind it and a stack of tape pedals on the End Board — the Somersault earns its slot as the **early, mono, gestural pitch-wobble**: it warps the signal *before* the front-end's chorus and saturation, hands a pre-degraded source to the stereo CE-2W/Deco split, and gives you a foot-controlled chaos moment (HAVOC) that none of the other modulation or tape pedals can do on demand.
