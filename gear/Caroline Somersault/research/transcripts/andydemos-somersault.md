Welcome back to Andy Demos, where I'm checking out the Somersault from Caroline Guitar Company. This versatile modulation device allows everything from classic 80s chorus and wavering vibrato to robotic bleeps and blips.

Top left is a MIX control that goes from 100% dry to 100% wet, giving vibrato, rotary, and damaged-tape effects. On the right is the important OFFSET control, which changes the delay time of the modulated signal; this, in conjunction with the DEPTH on the lower left, gives the chorus a wetter and wider sound, with extreme pitch-bending and slapback echo and chorus effects. The triangle wave is where all the classic modulation lives, but roll over into the square wave and everything is chopped into rhythmic pulses with random pitch-shifting and blips jumping out in time with the rate. The momentary HAVOC footswitch warps to maximum speed no matter what setting you've dialed in, and the tasteful TONE switch puts a lo-fi spin on the Somersault — adding a vintage voice to chorus sounds, but also taking more extreme settings down a notch.

Let's start with a classic chorus setting, with the DEPTH about halfway and the OFFSET fully counter-clockwise for the fastest delay time. Notice, as I just increase that control [OFFSET] alone, it gets a lot more warped and the depth seems to increase, so to counteract that I'll use the darker TONE setting just to smooth the whole thing out.

[Music — classic chorus demo]

To get a vibrato or Leslie sound, all you have to do is increase the MIX to 100% and either increase the OFFSET or the DEPTH. I like to keep the OFFSET at its quickest setting [fully counter-clockwise] to reduce any latency.

[Music — vibrato/Leslie demo]

You've probably got a handle on the OFFSET and DEPTH controls and how really interactive they are, but when you change the waveform it's a whole different story.

[Music — square-wave demo]

As you can tell, it gets pretty wild on that square wave with higher OFFSET and DEPTH settings. But it's not all just bleeps and bloops — you could actually tune it to be in tune with your guitar. Here's a setting I found which gives me a lower octave despite where I have the SPEED set.

Notes: The most settings-explicit demo. Concrete recipes:
- CLASSIC CHORUS: DEPTH ~halfway (noon), OFFSET fully counter-clockwise (fastest delay time / least warp), triangle wave. Increasing OFFSET alone adds warp and apparent depth; counteract by switching TONE to dark to smooth it.
- VIBRATO / LESLIE: MIX 100% wet, then increase OFFSET or DEPTH. He prefers keeping OFFSET fully counter-clockwise (quickest) to reduce latency — i.e. you do NOT need high offset for vibrato.
- SQUARE WAVE GLITCH: square wave + higher OFFSET and DEPTH = wild rhythmic pulses, random pitch-shift blips that jump out in time with the rate.
- TUNED OCTAVE TRICK: a specific square-wave setting can produce an in-tune lower octave regardless of SPEED.
Confirms OFFSET fully CCW = fastest delay / least latency (opposite-end usage tip), and that DEPTH and OFFSET are highly interactive. TONE/dark toggle used as a "smoothing/taming" tool, not just darkening.
