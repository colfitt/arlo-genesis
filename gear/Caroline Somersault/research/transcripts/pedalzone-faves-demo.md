Hey everybody, and welcome to The Pedal Zone, and welcome to another episode of "Faves," where it's time to look at my five favorite sounds inside the lush Caroline Guitar Company Somersault lo-fi modulator.

Somersault sees Caroline Guitar Company jump head-first into serene, shimmering modulation, which offers up an abundance of glorious gooey chorus tones, fast rotary swirls, warped lo-fi vibratos, slapbacks, and chaotic glitchy pitched jumps through its rich yet intuitive control set. Let's check it out.

[Extended music demo of the five favorite sounds.]

I actually tried the Somersault for the very first time back at NAMM this year, and the fact that I could actually hear the excellence of the chorus tone through the hellish NAMM noise speaks volumes about how well-voiced the Somersault actually is.

Dialing in a great tone on the Somersault is a breeze — just set everything at noon and you already have a divine modulation sound, and then you can just tweak from there if you want things to be a bit more tight, loose, shimmery, or watery. It's always a great sign when you don't have to struggle to get a great tone out of a pedal.

The vibrato side of the Somersault is warm and musical, and I love how the HAVOC switch lets you quickly jump from slow-moving waves to quick spins — letting you instantly add character to specific lines and phrases, and even letting you manually simulate vinyl skips and tape jumps. I'm telling you, this switch gets addictive fast.

The only negative thing I have to say about this pedal is that the "lo-fi modulator" moniker can be a bit misleading, because this pedal actually sounds very pristine and hi-fi — even though you can darken up the tone a bit via the bright/dark switch. So if you're expecting gritty, chewed-up, tape-like modulation tones, then you'll be sorely disappointed. But if you're looking for huge, spacious chorus tones and worn, musical vibratos, then they don't come much better than the Caroline Guitar Company Somersault.
