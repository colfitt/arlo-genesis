The Pedal Zone · Caroline Guitar Co Somersault and Megabyte - Interview with Philippe Herndon at NAMM19 · 2019-01-30

Hey everybody and welcome to The Pedal Zone at NAMM '19, currently at the noisiest place at NAMM — it's Purgatory, but also the cool place at NAMM, I like it — with my dear friend Phil[ippe] of Caroline Guitar Company.

[Banter about meeting in person, the room, drone noise from the convention floor.]

Let's talk about your new pedals — I am truly excited. Not only are you updating my favorite delay of all time, but you're also bringing out something new, completely new, like a chorus pedal. Could you just give us the quick spiel on those?

HERNDON: Sure, so Somersault is our chorus pedal, and it is a chorus / verb / low-fidelity modulator. The reason we don't call it just a straight chorus pedal or straight vibrato pedal is because the time-lag functions do some interesting stuff that kind of take it out of what you typically expect out of the chorusing. And then there's some extreme settings where it does things that candidly basically border on just glitch nonsense. The idea being that it can do these crazy things, but if those scare you, just turn everything dialed down and you're back in a nice, safe, good place.

[Riffing on NBC's "The Good Place" — the good place is a quiet room where you can listen to music, the bad place is the drum section of NAMM, and there's Purgatory in between.]

THE PEDAL ZONE: I got to try it yesterday — everything set at noon, perfect chorus sound. It is so smooth. It reminds me a bit of my old CE-2, which up until now was my favorite chorus, but this thing just sounds bigger.

HERNDON: Adding the time-lag function is a little bit [of that] as well. We worked very hard on getting this thing to give you a little bit of extra push to the amp — not so much boost, we're not trying to just make it louder, but to make your sound a little fuller along with the chorusing happening, [with everything] at 12.

[Discussion of the Megabyte (the Kilobyte delay with tap tempo). Herndon: "People want Kilobyte with tap tempo, so it's tap tempo, we double the delay time as well to keep it cleaner but give a little bit more; there's a little bit different of a modulation, and there's tap and Havoc tied to the same functions." Release timing: Somersault late spring, Megabyte late summer / early fall 2019. Jokes about a possible "Caroline Corporation" pedal / "Hawaiian pizza."]

THE PEDAL ZONE: Should we hear the new pedals? Let's do it.

[Music — playing demo of the Somersault.]

I love that this is — [Music] — that's like dead-on warped vinyl sounds, that's awesome.

[Extended music demonstration of the Somersault and Megabyte through the end of the segment.]

Notes: Builder's-own framing of the Somersault as a "chorus / verb / low-fidelity modulator" where the OFFSET (the "time-lag function") is what takes it beyond normal chorus into glitch territory. Everything-at-noon = "perfect chorus," compared favorably to a CE-2. Herndon confirms a deliberate slight output-fullness/push voicing. The interviewer calls the full-wet sound "warped vinyl."
