Perfect Circuit (perfectcircuit.com) + Caroline product/manual copy · Somersault control logic · accessed 2026-06

Distilled power-user logic for the four knobs + two toggles. Sourced from Caroline's own product page (carolineguitar.com/product/somersault), the manual (SOMERSAULT-MANUAL.pdf), Perfect Circuit, and Delicious Audio. Concrete, control-by-control.

# The MIX logic (most important knob)
- MIX is **wet/dry blend AND the chorus-vs-vibrato switch in one knob.** Any **dry+wet blend = CHORUS** (you keep a pitch-stable reference). **Full wet = VIBRATO** (no dry anchor → true pitch vibrato / Leslie pitch-bend).
- Practical: pull MIX **toward dry** for usable shimmer/doubling; push it **full clockwise (full wet)** for the woozy, seasick pitch-vibrato. Reviewer tip: "fully wet can mask articulation, fully dry defeats the purpose" — chorus blends live in the middle.
- GOTCHA people hit: maxing MIX expecting "more chorus" gives **vibrato, not chorus** — the dry reference is gone, so tonal material (chords, drones) audibly **seesaws in pitch.** By design. Pull MIX back if it's too disorienting.

# OFFSET = the danger / seasick knob
- Caroline's def: "sets the **time difference between the modulated and dry signals** — higher settings will have an increasingly dramatic effect."
- This — not Depth — is where most of the "broken" character lives. Low Offset = tight subtle doubling/chorus; **past ~2 o'clock it gets dramatic and detuned fast**, pushing into "pitch-bended slapback chorus territory" (Delicious Audio) and full seasick. Yvette Young's on-camera read matches: subtle warble low, "modulated seasick sound when fully offset."

# DEPTH and SPEED
- **DEPTH** = how far the pitch swings (LFO intensity on the wet line). Low = gentle waver; high = dramatic warble approaching pitch-bend.
- **SPEED** = LFO rate. Slow = lagging woozy vibrato; fast = ray-gun/rotary wobble. **This is the knob HAVOC overrides** (hold = instant max speed).

# WAVE toggle — triangle vs square
- **TRIANGLE** = smooth classic chorus/vibrato sweep — the approachable, musical default. **SQUARE** = glitchy, pulsing, stepped "bleep-bloop"; it can act like a **pitch-changing tremolo** (chopped, not smooth). Square is the lo-fi/broken aesthetic and the specialist mode.

# TONE toggle — the lo-fi filter (it's a TOGGLE, not a sweep)
- Two positions only: **full-range** or a **hi-end cut on the WET signal only.** The hi-cut darkens the modulated voice **without dulling your dry** — that's what makes the wet line sit like a **worn/degraded tape track under a clean dry signal.** GOTCHA: it's binary — no halfway-darkened wet. (Some retailer copy wrongly calls Tone a "knob"; the manual is explicit it's a toggle.)
- The whole concept: Caroline pitches the Somersault as a **malfunctioning vintage arcade cabinet** — "I think I can" huffs and puffs through "bizarre wowie-zowie sounds." The hi-cut + square + high-Offset combo is how you summon the "decades-of-wear" malfunction.

# Quick gotcha list
- No tap tempo, **no expression jack** — Speed is hand-set; HAVOC is the only foot control.
- **Hybrid, not pure analog** — wet delay line is a digital chip; the dry/LFO/mix/output are analog (so it *feels* analog but isn't a pure-BBD chorus).
- Full-wet on tonal material = pitch seesaw (vibrato), surprises people expecting chorus.
- ~30 mA, 9V center-negative ONLY — manual warns **do not exceed 9V** (no 18V trick).
