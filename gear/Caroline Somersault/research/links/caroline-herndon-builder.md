South Carolina ETV (scetv.org) · Q&A with Philippe Herndon · 2015-09-23

The core builder profile for Caroline Guitar Company / Philippe Herndon. Supplemented below with the Cottonwood Music Emporium "Correspondents" interview and Caroline's own About page (URLs noted inline) since all three tell one story.

# Philippe Herndon — the founder
- Founded **Caroline Guitar Company in 2010, Columbia, South Carolina.** Herndon is a **former full-time touring and recording guitarist**, contributing writer to guitar magazines (most recently *Premier Guitar*), and holds an **MBA from USC's Moore School** (undergrad at William & Mary). Caroline's own framing: he "unsuccessfully failed to sell his soul to any corporate devils" during the financial crisis and started building pedals instead — initially as a temporary project.
- Self-description of the work: doing "**original work in a copycat industry with zero barriers to entry.**" Deliberately keeps the catalog small — "only 5–6 models available because we know what real product development entails."

# The "small batch distortery" ethos
- Caroline's standing tagline (About + every product page): "**Dreamed, designed, and created at our small batch distortery™ in Columbia, South Carolina.**" ("Distortery" = distortion + manufactory — the house pun.)
- Stated values (carolineguitar.com/about): they sell "**not only our products, but our judgement and integrity as well**"; "Not content to rest on old ideas of what things should sound like"; "The same work that we deliver to noteworthy artist customers is the same work we deliver to our retailers"; and on service, "if things stop working as they should, we hustle to repair things in a hurry." Cottonwood interview adds that **every pedal is signed with a note by the person who built and tested it.**
- The manuals read like zines — humorous, opinionated. The design philosophy quote that captures it (from the Wave Cannon Zero copy): "**we didn't write any rules; we only wrote a manual. Where you take it is up to you.**"

# Crowdfunding / early pedals
- **Reportedly the first guitar-pedal company to crowdfund a pedal** — Caroline ran two Kickstarter campaigns that beat their goals by **234% and 268%.**
- The flagship/first pedal was the **Wave Cannon™ overdrive/distortion (released Oct 2010)**, named one of *Guitar World*'s top 10 pedals for 2011. Started as ~40 hand-made units sold to friends; ~4,500 pedals shipped by 2015.
- Catalog the Somersault sits in: **Wave Cannon** (drive), **Kilobyte / Megabyte** (lo-fi delay), **Météore** (lo-fi reverb), plus the **Somersault** (lo-fi modulation, NAMM 2019). Kilobyte's design brief (per Cottonwood) was wanting "just the wet part of the signal to be overdriven" — a Deluxe Memory Man reimagining. Same instinct shows up in the Somersault's hi-cut-on-wet-only Tone toggle.

# Herndon on his own role (Cottonwood)
- Describes his process as "lengthy procrastination, self-doubt, some hustle" + reimagining gear he wished worked differently.
- Sees himself as a **toolmaker, not an artist**: "once the customer buys them, the tools belong to them more than us — they simply have our brand on them." That hands-off, "go break it yourself" attitude is exactly why the Somersault ships with a chaos footswitch and a "danger" Offset knob.
