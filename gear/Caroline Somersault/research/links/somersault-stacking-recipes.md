Stacking and placement recipes for the Somersault, tilted to this rig (Board 1: … BF-3 → Somersault → CE-2W stereo split → Deco V2 …). General modulation-stacking principle + the Somersault's specific behaviors. Delicious Audio supplies the lo-fi calibration line; placement logic is standard practice applied to this chain.

# Core placement principle: unstable BEFORE stable
- Universal modulation-stacking rule: **the more extreme/character pedal goes first**, the stable/hi-fi one after. So the **pitch-unstable Somersault belongs UPSTREAM of a stable chorus** (here the Boss CE-2W). The CE-2W then receives an already-pitch-warped signal and widens it → instability baked into the **stereo image**, not smeared on after the fact.
- Bonus in this chain: the Somersault is **mono and the LAST modulation before the stereo split** (CE-2W does the split). A **mono pitch-wobble feeding a stereo chorus = the widest, most "underwater" image.** General tip: put the Somersault on the mono side of any stereo rig.

# Recipe 1 — unstable Somersault → stable chorus (CE-2W)
- Run the Somersault as the **character** (full-wet vibrato or high-Offset seasick chorus) and the CE-2W as the **width** (subtle). You get a wide, pitch-drifting field neither makes alone.
- DON'T run both as "chorus" at full depth simultaneously — it muds. **Pick one to be the character, the other the width.**

# Recipe 2 — into tape saturation (Deco V2)
- Saturate the Somersault's wobble through a **tape-sim downstream** (Strymon Deco's tape saturation + doubletracker) to **compound the lo-fi**: pitch instability + tape grit = genuine "tape-ruined chorus wall." Delicious Audio's calibration: "short of buying [Chase Bliss] Generation Loss, you won't find a better lo-fi chorus pedal" — i.e. it's a real lo-fi voice but milder than dedicated tape-degraders, so let downstream saturation do the heavy ruin.
- For this rig specifically: best settings are square wave + hi-cut Tone + high Offset, slow Speed, into the Deco — the "worn cassette under the fuzz wall."

# Recipe 3 — beating two LFOs (the organic-waver trick)
- If a downstream pedal has its own LFO/wobble (Deco's wow/flutter; or another chorus), set the **two rates SLIGHTLY DIFFERENT** so they **beat against each other** — the offset between the LFOs produces a more **random, organic, less-mechanical waver** than either alone. Nudge the Somersault's Speed slower than the partner's. Same idea works Somersault-vs-CE-2W.

# Recipe 4 — three modulations is a palette, not a stack
- BF-3 (flange) → Somersault (pitch-wobble) → CE-2W (width): **all three full-up = a swirling, often muddy mess.** Treat them as a palette — BF-3 for jet sweeps, Somersault for seasick pitch, CE-2W for width — and rarely run all three at depth. Flange + full-wet vibrato together is a specific heavy shoegaze swirl: a one-moment move, not a default.

# Stacking gotchas / redundancy honesty
- **Generic placement** (per Guitar Chalk): "after dirt, before delay/reverb." In this rig the Somersault sits after all dirt and before the stereo chorus/tape block — consistent.
- HONEST redundancy: this rig already has heavy lo-fi/tape gear LATE (CB Generation Loss, Chroma Console, PORTA424/JHS 424). The Somersault's value is **lo-fi MODULATION placed EARLY** — it warps pitch *before* the front-end chorus/saturation, which the end-of-chain tape pedals structurally cannot do. If Generation Loss is already running heavy, a subtle Somersault may be inaudible — reserve it for when you want the wobble early, not as a third tape pedal.
