=== AndyDemos (youtube.com/watch?v=llvfpEL1mQ0, 2019-07-31) — most settings-explicit ===

(A) "CLASSIC 80s CHORUS"
- Depth: ~halfway (noon) · Offset: fully counter-clockwise (fastest delay time, least warp) · Mix: blend (dry+wet) · Wave: triangle
- Tone: bright to start; switch to DARK to "smooth the whole thing out" once you raise Offset.
- Tip (quoted intent): raising OFFSET alone "gives a lot more warped and the depth seems to increase" — counteract with the dark Tone.

(B) "VIBRATO / LESLIE"
- Mix: 100% wet · then increase Offset OR Depth · Wave: triangle
- Author's preference: keep OFFSET fully counter-clockwise ("its quickest setting to reduce any latency") — you do NOT need high offset for vibrato; full-wet Mix is what makes it vibrato.

(C) "SQUARE-WAVE GLITCH"
- Wave: square · higher Offset + higher Depth · → "wild," rhythmic pulses with "random pitch-shifting and blips jumping out in time with the rate."

(D) "TUNED LOWER-OCTAVE TRICK"
- A specific square-wave setting "gives me a lower octave despite where I have the speed set" — i.e. square + the right Offset/Depth can land an in-tune octave-down artifact. (Exact knobs not stated; experiment.)

=== Zachari Smith / "Spotlight" (youtube.com/watch?v=wy9Pfi4keNU, 2020-09-07) ===

(E) "DEFAULT ADJUSTABLE CHORUS"
- Everything at 12 o'clock · Wave: triangle · Tone: bright → a "really great chorus."
- His frequent default: flip Tone to DARK for a "subtler," supportive chorus that "sits" under the mix. Demonstrated at 100% wet too.

(F) "SYNTH-Y SELF-OSC GLITCH"
- Everything at 12 o'clock · Wave: square · Tone: bright → "starts to modulate and almost self-oscillate," synth-like, "bleep bloop," unpredictable. His go-to "if I just want something a bit crazy."

=== Universal starting point (all demos agree) ===
- Everything at NOON / 12 o'clock = a usable, "divine"/"perfect" chorus (stated by Philippe Herndon at NAMM, The Pedal Zone "Faves," and Zachari Smith). Tweak from there.

=== Yvette Young / CME (youtube.com/watch?v=xZKIwIqiHIE) ===
- No clock positions, but the workflow quote that matters: OFFSET low = "subtle warble," OFFSET cranked = "like you're on a boat about to throw up." She stays on the triangle wave for musical playing.

=== Jackson Brooksby (youtube.com/watch?v=sqN_Raz4rvY) ===
- OFFSET threshold: above ~9:00 the dry/wet delay is audible and chorusing intensifies/"gets weirder"; below 9:00 it's felt-not-heard.
- Workflow: the hi-cut (dark) TONE toggle is the key lo-fi control — at 50/50 it darkens/de-prominents the wet; at 100% wet it gives the darkest "Bioshock soundtrack" lo-fi voice.
