The HAVOC™ momentary footswitch is Caroline's **line-wide signature gesture** — same idea, different chaos per pedal. Documents what HAVOC does generally and how the Somersault's version differs (it's a Speed override, not a self-oscillator). Cross-referenced with the Météore/Kilobyte/Megabyte line and the Somersault manual.

# What HAVOC is, across the line
- A **momentary footswitch** (hold = engaged, snaps back on release) that injects instability "on demand." On Caroline's **dirt/delay/reverb pedals** (Wave Cannon, Kilobyte, Megabyte, Météore) HAVOC triggers **runaway self-oscillation / feedback** — chaotic, uncontrolled noise you summon and release.
- On the **Wave Cannon Zero** it's expanded: "it has its own control for tuning and mixing the feedback signal … and thanks to our friend John Snyder at Electronic Audio Experiments, **it can be momentary or latching**." (Context: shows HAVOC is a deliberate, evolving Caroline platform feature, not a one-off.)

# The Somersault's HAVOC — different job
- On the Somersault, **HAVOC does NOT self-oscillate.** Per the manual it "**removes the SPEED control from the equation, maxing the rate on demand**" — i.e. hold it and the LFO instantly slams to full speed with **no ramp**, then snaps back to your dialed Speed when you let go. Caroline calls it "**Leslie speeds on demand**."
- So treat it as a **gesture, not a setting**: rotary-style fast/slow speed-up swells, ray-gun stutters and glitch fills at phrase-ends. It's the one foot-controllable parameter on the pedal (there's no tap, no expression jack).

# Musical-use tips (HAVOC technique)
- **Rotary swell:** dial a slow base Speed (~9 o'clock), then stab HAVOC to fake a Leslie ramping up to fast, release to let it coast back down. The instant on / gradual-feel back-off is the whole trick.
- **Glitch punctuation:** on the **square wave + hi-cut Tone**, tapping HAVOC mid-phrase reads as a bleep-bloop stutter — best at the end of a line, not under sustained chords.
- **Into downstream saturation/tape:** HAVOC bursts feeding a tape-sim (e.g. Strymon Deco) read as glitchy **tape-flutter** rather than clean rotary — the saturation smears the speed-slam into something more "broken machine."
- Because it's purely a Speed override here, it's **safe** — it won't blow up into noise on its own the way the dirt/delay HAVOCs do. You can use it musically without babysitting it.
