# The "push and pull" control interaction (new detail)
Quoted/paraphrased from the page + the Perfect Circuit search snippet of Caroline's copy:
- "The waves set the depth of the LFO and how severely it will shift the OFFSET control.
  Think of these as a push and pull — the more you use of one, the less you might need
  of the other."
- Practical read: WAVE (especially square) + DEPTH amplify how dramatically OFFSET
  detunes. So if a square-wave / high-DEPTH patch is too seasick, you can BACK OFF
  OFFSET and still keep the character — they trade. And for a tasteful chorus you can
  push OFFSET further when DEPTH is low/triangle, because the LFO isn't multiplying it.

# Control definitions (confirm existing, verbatim retailer/Caroline copy)
- MIX: "from completely dry (counterclockwise) to 100% wet (clockwise)."
- OFFSET: "sets the time difference between the modulated and dry signals — higher
  settings will have an increasingly dramatic effect." Also framed as controlling the
  "perceived width of the effect."
- DEPTH: "sets the intensity of the wave on the modulated line."
- SPEED: "sets the rate of the oscillator sweep."
- WAVE: triangle (classic 80s) vs square/rectangle (more intense).
- TONE: dark/bright toggle that "removes the brilliance from the sound" (hi-cut on wet).
- HAVOC: "removes the SPEED control from the equation, maxing the rate on demand" —
  "Leslie-style rotary effects on demand."
- Design intent reiterated: "We encourage you to explore the subtleties of how each
  control interacts with the other." Interaction-driven, not a preset pedal.

# Other "named sound" lines re-confirmed across retailer copy (russomusic / guitarpedalshoppe)
- "Underwater dream" = slow SPEED + deep DEPTH + dark TONE (matches Guitar Chalk #5).
- Vibrato = MIX 100% wet; HAVOC = instant Leslie speed-up.
