The builder's own product copy. No numeric knob presets here (Caroline never publishes clock charts), but it is the authoritative source for the NAMED SOUNDS and the design intent — the vocabulary the rest of the demos echo.

Manufacturer's described sound range (verbatim phrases):
- "the chorus-y / vibrato-y / faux-rotary device [you have been asking us to make]"
- the pedal spans "from seasick wobbles to glitchy bleep-bloops and 'I think I can' huffs and puffs" (the faux-Leslie / chugging-rotary image)
- "Gentle, lagging vibrato, 1980s style choruses, and even some doubling effects are possible."
- The intended workflow is to "explore the subtleties of how each control interacts with the others" — i.e. it is an interaction-driven pedal, not a preset pedal.

Architecture (manufacturer): "a similar framework" to Caroline's KILOBYTE and MÉTÉORE — "the digital chip sets the delayed wet signal," while the gain stage, dry signal, LFO, control set, and output mixing are "entirely analog."

How to read this for settings (inferred mapping from the named sounds to controls, per the manual's control definitions — see dossier §2):
- "1980s style chorus" → MIX at a dry+wet blend (noon), low OFFSET, moderate DEPTH, triangle, bright/full-range Tone.
- "Gentle, lagging vibrato" / "seasick wobbles" → MIX full wet, slow SPEED, raise DEPTH and/or OFFSET. OFFSET cranked = the seasick end.
- "faux-rotary / 'I think I can' huffs and puffs" → faster SPEED (or hold HAVOC for the speed-up), square wave pushes the chopped, chugging rotary character.
- "glitchy bleep-bloops" → square WAVE + higher OFFSET/DEPTH.
- "lo-fi" voicing on any of the above = the hi-cut (dark) TONE toggle on the wet signal.
