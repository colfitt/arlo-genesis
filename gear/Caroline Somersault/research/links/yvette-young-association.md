The most-cited Somersault artist association — and an honest read on what it actually is. Cross-referenced with the Chicago Music Exchange demo (chicagomusicexchange.com/blogs/news/caroline-somersault-chorus-vibrato-demo-with-yvette-young; YouTube xZKIwIqiHIE, posted ~2019-07-20).

# What it is (and isn't)
- **Yvette Young (Covet)** — San Jose math-rock guitarist — recorded an **on-camera demo of the Somersault for Chicago Music Exchange** in July 2019, shortly after the pedal's NAMM '19 launch.
- HONEST FLAG: this is a **store demo / promotional placement, NOT a confirmed signature endorsement.** No evidence the Somersault lives on her actual touring rig (her Rig Rundowns don't feature it). Frame her as "**a notable player who demoed it on camera**," not "a signature user." She is not a Caroline artist.

# What she actually said (useful as tips)
- On the waveforms (revealing for new users): "I'm new to square wave so I think I definitely go with the more traditional [triangle] wave setting." — i.e. **triangle is the approachable/musical default; square is the specialist glitch mode.**
- Found an emergent texture: "I got this cool delay thing that kind of sounded like a **ticking clock**" (the rhythmic side of square/fast settings).
- On the range: enjoyed the "**subtle warble**" at low settings but also the "**modulated seasick sound when fully offset**" — first-hand confirmation that **Offset is the knob that takes it from pretty to seasick.**
- Vibe: "nice retro tones" that "transported me" / reminded her of childhood TV themes (the 80s-chorus target landing).
- And the obligatory note on the enclosure art: "I like the little running guy. I don't know what he's running from but I relate to him."
