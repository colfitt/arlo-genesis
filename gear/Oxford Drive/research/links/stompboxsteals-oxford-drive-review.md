Best plain-language tone writeup + community-perspective verdict. Price quoted at $235.

## Verdict (the quotable line)
- "This is basically the old Marshall Shredmaster pedal but with more volume and gain."
- "A great take on a Classic" — improves on the original ShredMaster's weaknesses "without f-ing with its sound."
- Notes the original ShredMaster was "the hot pedal to have" in its day (the Bluesbreaker got more press, but the Shred was the one players wanted).

## Controls as described
- Volume, Gain, Bass, Treble + a "boost/cut sweep for midrange" (the Contour) — confirms Contour = a tilt sweep, not a fixed mid.
- Clipping toggle: asymmetrical ("Tube Screamer style"), silicon ("more tube-like"), LED ("adds aggression").
- Two-voice toggle on the V2 reviewed here (this is a two-voice unit, pre-'01).

## Usability note
- The extra features "can be ignored if desired" — works fine as a simple one-knob-ish ShredMaster, or you can dig into the clipping/voice options. Reassuring for set-and-forget use.
