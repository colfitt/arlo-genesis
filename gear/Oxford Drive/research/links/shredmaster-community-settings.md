Community/forum dial settings for the Marshall ShredMaster that transfer 1:1 to the Oxford Drive (identical Gain/Bass/Contour/Treble/Volume control set; per TKOG, "set the Oxford identically to the Shredmaster, then back off the gain a little; use silicone clipping for the stock sound"). These supplement the maker/Greenwood settings already on disk.

COMMUNITY SETTINGS (quoted from search extracts):

- **Bright / cutting tone** — Gain: 2:00 · Bass: full open (max) · Contour: minimum (CCW = max mids) · Treble: 10:00 · Volume: to taste.
  (Source extract: Equipboard/Honest Distortion aggregation. NOTE: that "Bass full open" is for a single pedal into a clean amp — on the log-taper Oxford, full bass = mud, especially stacked; drop it for this rig.)

- **Metal / high-gain** — Gain: 4–8 [numeric dial, "less for higher-gain amps, more for lower-gain"] · Bass: 7 [numeric; "lower for a tighter sound"] · Contour: 1 [numeric minimum = max mids] · Treble: 8 [numeric] · Level: 5 [numeric].
  (Forum recommendation per the TDPRI/Honest Distortion extract. The low Contour + modest Bass = the focused mid-forward ShredMaster grind.)

- **Rhythm-brightening tip** — "Turning up the Contour control to about 9 o'clock takes out some of the mids and brightens up the sound" for rhythm work. (Confirms Contour-CW = mid-scoop + treble-add tilt.)

- **General dial-in method (corroborates Premier Guitar)** — "Park the bass at noon, dial up treble and contour to levels that make your guitar sing best, then add or subtract bass to taste." "Higher treble settings generally sound best." The Contour is described as a "reverse mid control... incredibly dynamic, from a speaker-splitting mid-thump to a scooped underwater quality in a single turn"; the pedal's appeal is "the shrill, cranked-gain sound... best accessed by modestly adjusting the Contour and keeping the Gain somewhere on the right side."

TAKEAWAYS FOR THE OXFORD IN THIS RIG:
- These reinforce the on-disk rules: Contour CCW/min = max mids (lead honk); Contour ~9:00+ = brighter rhythm; run Bass LOW on the log taper despite single-pedal "full bass" advice.
