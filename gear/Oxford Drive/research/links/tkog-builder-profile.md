Who makes the Oxford Drive, and why. The builder IS the lineage.

## What TKOG is
- A New York boutique builder whose website began (and remains) "a site devoted to how Radiohead make all of those funny noises" — a comprehensive Radiohead gear/technique ARCHIVE (per-member gear inventories for Thom Yorke, Jonny + Colin Greenwood, Ed O'Brien, Phil Selway, plus Nigel Godrich; song-by-song breakdowns; a 187-page blog).
- The archive evolved into a small pedal shop building hardware that recreates those Radiohead sounds. This is why the Oxford Drive exists and why it's named "Oxford" (Radiohead's hometown).

## Product lineup
- **Oxford Drive** — ShredMaster/Guv'nor distortion (the rig's pedal).
- **Mini Glitch** — micro-sampler / glitch pedal (since ~2018).
- **Pitch Magpie** — glitchy pitch-shifter.
- **EQ-201** — preamp/EQ.
- **Feral Glitch** — discontinued.
- Sold in small numbered batches via store.thekingofgear.com; updates via mailing list + Instagram @tkogeffects.

## Artist-to-builder link (real, notable)
- Per GuitarPedalX, TKOG's **Mini Glitch is used by Jonny Greenwood himself** ("beloved by a variety of guitar luminaries — including Radiohead's Jonny Greenwood"). So the builder who clones Greenwood's ShredMaster also has Greenwood as an actual customer of another product. The Oxford Drive itself has NO documented marquee users — TKOG sells it purely on the cloned tone.
