thekingofgear.com (TKOG, "Jonny Greenwood's Effects and Pedals") · maker's documented Greenwood ShredMaster settings · accessed 2026-06

TKOG documents Jonny Greenwood's actual Marshall ShredMaster knob settings. Because the Oxford Drive is a ShredMaster recreation with a 1:1 control set (Gain, Bass, Contour, Treble, Volume), these transfer DIRECTLY to the Oxford — per TKOG's own advice, "set the Oxford Drive identically to how you'd set the Shredmaster, then back off the gain a little." Use '91 voice + Si clipping for the authentic stock ShredMaster sound.

DOCUMENTED GREENWOOD SHREDMASTER SETTINGS (quoted verbatim from TKOG):

- **"Normal" setting (2006, In Rainbows era)**
  Gain: 3:00 · Bass: 2:00 · Contour: 7:00 · Treble: 2:00 · Volume: 5:00
  (TKOG note: volume "often set lower than this.")

- **Live setting for AC30 (2010, Haiti Benefit, AC30-only)**
  Gain: 3:00 · Bass: 2:45 · Contour: 7:00 · Treble: 2:45 · Volume: 1:30

Pattern: Contour pinned at 7:00 (minimum = maximum mids / mid-focused, the classic ShredMaster honk), Gain at 3:00, Bass and Treble both high-ish (~2:00). Note: on the Oxford, the Bass knob is logarithmic, so 2:00 = a LOT of bass — fine into a clean amp, but for this stacked rig run Bass much lower.

SHOOTOUT-CONFIRMED "JONNY'S PREFERRED" SETTING (Joe Edelmann, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nlx69O9y-zY, applied to the Oxford itself in an A/B vs the real ShredMaster):
  Gain: 3:00 · Bass: 1:00 · Treble: 1:00 · Contour: 7:00 (minimum) · Volume: maxed at 5:00 (level-matched on the Oxford, whose output range is wider).
  Verdict from that test: with a Fender 85 solid-state amp + Lace Sensor pickups + SM58, all three (original '90 ShredMaster, Marshall reissue, Oxford) "were perfect at nailing that Jonny Greenwood sound"; the Oxford could match the originals' slightly brighter/more-open tone "by pushing the treble or output level a little higher and nudging the gain down a bit."

"CREEP" CHORUS (per Premier Guitar / general ShredMaster lore — INFERRED for Oxford, not a TKOG-published Oxford patch):
  The super-crunchy "Creep" choruses come from running the ShredMaster nearly flat-out ("turn all the knobs all the way up," Hendrix-style). On the Oxford this would be Gain high, Treble high, Bass run lower (log taper), Contour low for mids, Si clipping, '91 voice — then back the gain off slightly per TKOG.
