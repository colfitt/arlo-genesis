The best CONCRETE dial-in tips — they transfer directly to the Oxford Drive's identical control set.

## Contour is the critical control
- "The Contour control is probably most critical": clockwise adds midrange punch (metallic on humbuckers, can go fizzy on single-coils); left-of-center scoops mids for more detail. Confirms Contour = treble+mid tilt, not a plain mid knob.

## Dial-in method (transfers to the Oxford)
- "Park the bass at noon, dial up treble and contour to levels that make your guitar sing best, and then add or subtract bass to taste."
- Bass should be used SPARINGLY to avoid "fogginess." Higher treble settings generally work best.
- The pedal is "not particularly responsive to guitar-volume changes" — it's a set-the-character box, not a ride-the-volume box. (Matches the dossier: cleanup is OK but not Fuzz-Face-grade.)

## Tonal character + lineage
- Voice is "naturally dark, compressed" — heaviness that works across indie → metal, narrower range than other Marshall pedals.
- "Jonny Greenwood of Radiohead is probably the player most associated with the ShredMaster"; the reviewer credits it with the "super-crunchy choruses" of "Creep" (1993). Greenwood paired it with a Fender solid-state amp + humbucker Telecaster Plus.
