The ShredMaster circuit history + the verified-vs-loose artist roster. The pedal the Oxford Drive clones.

## Circuit history
- Introduced **1991** as one of a Marshall trio — **Bluesbreaker, Drivemaster, ShredMaster** — that superseded the late-'80s **Guv'nor**.
- Unlike the Bluesbreaker/Drivemaster (which chased specific Marshall AMP tones), the ShredMaster was a conventional high-gain DISTORTION pedal — Marshall's first high-gain pedal. Sold poorly at first; discontinued within a few years; later became a sought-after vintage piece → Marshall's **2023 Vintage Reissue**.
- Controls: **Gain, Bass, Contour, Treble, Volume**. Contour "altered the character of the midrange while enhancing low and high frequencies" (i.e. a tilt/scoop, not a simple mid knob).
- Reissue/dealer copy: circuit "almost identical to the Guv'nor but with an additional gain stage and an EQ with an extended range" — confirms the Guv'nor → ShredMaster lineage the Oxford Drive folds back together via its voice switch.
