TKOG's own recommended/example starting settings for the Oxford Drive. These are the maker's canonical "example settings" (printed in the manual and on the product page imagery). Clock positions quoted as published. Default voice for these is '91 unless noted; "stock clipping" = Si.

NAMED EXAMPLE SETTINGS (quoted):

- **Classic Overdrive** — Clipping: stock (Si) · Bass: 8:00 · Contour: 9:00 · Treble: 3:00 · Gain: ~12:00
  (Lowest-gain, mid-focused, trebly-but-controlled pushed-amp voice. Note the LOW bass — see logarithmic-bass note below.)

- **Shoegaze / Low-Gain Ambient** — Clipping: stock (Si) · Bass: 11:00 · Contour: 12:00 · Treble: 4:00 · Gain: 10:00
  TKOG: "best with dynamic arpeggios or chords and delay/reverb." The maker's literal shoegaze patch — relatively low gain, bright, contour at noon.

- **Scooped Sound** — Clipping: stock (Si) · Bass: 11:00 · Contour: 3:00 (mids scooped, treble up) · Treble: ~2:00 · Gain: 3:00
  Heavy mid-scoop voice; Contour at 3:00 scoops mids AND adds treble (the tilt behavior).

- **Traditional 90s Lead** — Clipping: stock (Si) · Bass: ~2:00 · Contour: 7:00 (mid-focused) · Treble: ~2:00 · Gain: 3:00
  The straight ShredMaster lead voice — mirrors Jonny Greenwood's documented ShredMaster lead setting (see jonny-greenwood-tone-match.md). Contour minimum = max mids.

- **Blues Lead** — Clipping: LED · Bass: 12:00 · Contour: 7:00 (mid-focused) · Treble: ~8:00 · Gain: ~2:00
  Lower-gain, darker, mid-rich, LED's more open/dynamic clipping for lead touch response.

HOW TO DIAL THE STOCK / SHREDMASTER SOUND (TKOG Q&A, https://thekingofgear.com/post/618212010484711424):
Question: "Is it essentially the same exact circuitry as a shredmaster... what are the ideal jonny settings on the Oxford drive?"
TKOG answer (verbatim): "Just set the Oxford Drive identically to how you'd set the Shredmaster, then back off the gain knob a little (since the Oxford Drive has some extra distortion available if you want it) and set the volume knob to fit your setup. And make sure to use silicone clipping if you want the 'stock' sound."

KEY DIAL-IN RULES (from product page / manual):
- **Bass is logarithmic** — to get the normal-overdrive bass CUT you must run it LOW, ~7–9 o'clock. Leaving it high = mud. (This is why "Classic Overdrive" sits at Bass 8:00.)
- **Contour is a treble+mid TILT, not a mid knob.** CCW (toward 7:00) = mids boosted, treble slightly attenuated (mid-focused). CW (toward 3:00) = mids scooped AND a bit of treble added. Re-trim Treble whenever you move Contour.
- **Clipping modes:** Si = stock, compressed, focused, plenty of overtones (the "stock" ShredMaster sound). LED = more open, louder, higher clipping threshold, more op-amp clipping, good for rhythm. Asym = Si/LED hybrid, wide dynamic range with the warm/fat asymmetric character.
- **Voices:** '91 = darkest, focused distortion with powerful mid-range punch (ShredMaster). '88 = crisp, perfect for rhythm (Guv'nor, input re-filtered). '01 = brightest by far, aggressive cutting distortion (later V2 units only — this unit's product page now lists all three).
