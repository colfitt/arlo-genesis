The CONFIRMED lineage: the Marshall ShredMaster as Jonny Greenwood's primary distortion. From TKOG's own Radiohead archive (the same people who built the Oxford Drive).

## Greenwood's ShredMaster — confirmed, primary
- "All of his distortion when using this amp comes from a single pedal: his Marshall Shredmaster."
- "Jonny's Marshall Shredmaster was possibly his first pedal, as it was the only one which he used at Radiohead's first shows. Prior to 1996, this pedal had also served all of Jonny's distortion needs."

## Placement in his 1997 rig
- The ShredMaster sits in a dedicated distorted signal path (the "Fender Eighty-Five" path), selected via a **Boss LS2 line selector** that toggles between a clean Vox AC30 path and the distorted Fender path. So the ShredMaster hits a CLEAN amp channel — all dirt comes from the pedal, none from the amp. (Directly mirrors how the Oxford Drive is "designed for a clean Fender-style amp.")
- Chain (distorted path): Boss LS2 → Marshall ShredMaster → Boss FV300H volume → Fender Eighty-Five (clean).

## Method takeaway for the rig
- Greenwood's whole approach = clean amp + ShredMaster as the single distortion event. The "Creep" crunch is this pedal hitting a clean Fender, not amp gain.
