# Hands-on power-user behaviors (Guitar.com, the best written review)

The highest-quality hands-on writeup. Concrete behaviors a community user should know:

## Looper behaviors
- **Always-on / instant playback:** the micro-looper "is always on" — captured notes begin playing back *"as soon as you step on that right-hand footswitch,"* even mid-experiment. You're committing to whatever was in the buffer a moment ago.
- **Pitch/speed manipulation is slightly unpredictable (by design):** *"it can be manipulated in pitch and speed, running backwards if you want it to, and different pitches bring out different elements of the loop in a way that's ever so slightly unpredictable."* → treat pitch/reverse moves as a sound-design dice-roll, not a precise transpose.
- **Overdub adds density:** the new overdub function lets you *"add density and depth to loops."*

## Wet channel
- **Granular reverb from one knob:** the ambient reverb *"can be split into flitty grains with a twist of the 'modify' knob"* — granular textures with no external control needed. (MODIFY = smear/grain amount on Reverb.)
- **Delay loves stereo:** the clean delay is *"an absolute treat in a wide stereo rig,"* and the SPREAD dip gives *"ping-pong stereo output."*

## Clock
- **Stepped vs smooth:** the CLOCK moves *"in perfect fourths and fifths (unless you flick the 'smooth' dip-switch for continuous bends)."*
- **No hiss penalty:** *"you also don't have to worry about background noise — it goes grittily lo-fi at the lowest sample rates, but without adding hiss."* (This is the MkII's headline fix vs the noisy V1 clock.)

## EXP/CV detail worth capturing (per CB docs, corroborated)
- The EXP/CV jack (TRS expression, or floating-ring TS for CV, **0–5V**) can be assigned to **any one of the five knobs OR several simultaneously** (Time, Length, Modify, Clock, the other Modify), and set to either **Bounce (modulate)** or **ramp-and-hold (rise/fall)** via the back dip switches. So one expression pedal can sweep CLOCK + MODIFY + LENGTH together for a single-pedal "morph the whole texture" move.

## Rig note
The "ever so slightly unpredictable" pitch/reverse looper behavior is exactly the "recorded-wrong" energy this rig wants — but it also means captured banjo/baritone loops won't transpose cleanly; lean into it rather than fighting for musical pitch.
