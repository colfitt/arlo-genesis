# MOOD MkII — official manual "Try This" recipes (the closest thing to a factory cookbook)

The MkII manual does NOT publish the named Othling onboard presets ("Nude Tayne" etc.) as knob
tables (confirmed again this pass via manualslib web-manual + the on-disk PDF — no named-preset
recipe tables exist). What it DOES contain is a set of official "Try this / Freeze Ideas /
Stretching 101 / Loop Tricks" recipes given as concrete mode + knob-direction instructions.
These are factory-authored and copyable. Distilled below with the exact wording quoted.

Reminder on the layout (so the recipes are unambiguous):
- **Wet Channel (left side):** TIME, MODIFY, 3-way MODE = **Reverb / Delay / Slip**, LEFT footswitch (tap=engage, hold=FREEZE).
- **Micro-Looper (right side):** LENGTH, MODIFY, 3-way MODE = **Env / Tape / Stretch**, RIGHT footswitch (tap=record/playback, hold=overdub).
- **Shared:** MIX (dry↔MOOD), CLOCK (sample rate = quality+time of Wet, length+resolution of loops), ROUTING toggle (IN / IN+micro-loop / micro-loop only).

---

## Recipe 1 — "Getting Started" good-default texture (manual p.6–7)
The manual's literal "If you're looking for a good default setting to do your own thing, try this"
illustration + build sequence:
- **Both channels start bypassed.** Play a few notes, **turn on the Micro-Looper**, then **turn on the Wet Channel** to "smear it."
- The default-knob illustration shows all six effect knobs (TIME/MODIFY/LENGTH/MODIFY) ~**noon**, both modes at their middle position, CLOCK ~noon.
- Then, in order: **"Start to roll back the CLOCK"** (illustration shows CLOCK pulled down to ~**6k**, i.e. lower-third) "get some texture in there." → **"Move around each of the MODIFY knobs."** → **"Then work your way up to the TIME and LENGTH knobs."**
- Quote: *"You should be a texture now."*
- **Use:** the canonical from-scratch starting point — neutral-noon everything, then lower CLOCK into the grit zone and explore MODIFY/TIME/LENGTH by hand.

## Recipe 2 — Clock "Try this" reveal (manual p.18–19)
- **Wet MODE = Reverb.** Play a single note; **while the reverb decays, turn DOWN the CLOCK** (counter-clockwise). "Now play another note. Notice how much the character has changed. Continue to rotate counter-clockwise from there and things will get real textural."
- CLOCK scale runs **2k…3k…4k…6k…(12k noon)…16k…24k…32k…48k…64k**; low = "heavy aliasing and downsampling… Computer noises," high = pure/hi-fi.
- **Use:** the single most impactful gesture on the pedal — sweep CLOCK live under a sustaining reverb to morph from hi-fi to crunchy/aliased.

## Recipe 3 — Freeze Idea: "Reverb Pattern" (manual p.22–23)
- **Wet MODE = Reverb.** Turn **MODIFY down (min)** → **hold LEFT footswitch to FREEZE.** "Your frozen reverb will sound like a percussive pattern." You can turn **MODIFY up once frozen to smudge the pattern** (but you can't go back).
- **Use:** rhythmic/percussive frozen-reverb texture from the multi-tap clusters (low MODIFY = distinct taps).

## Recipe 4 — Freeze Idea: "Double Time" (manual p.22–23)
- **Wet MODE = Delay.** Capture a micro-loop, then **play notes into the Delay and FREEZE it** (hold LEFT FS). "Try experimenting with different delay lengths (TIME), as well as syncing (SYNC hidden option)." A frozen delay acts as a **secondary micro-loop**.
- **Use:** two independent loop layers at once (looper + frozen-delay layer).

## Recipe 5 — Freeze Idea: "Slip Solo" (manual p.22–23)
- **Wet MODE = Slip.** **FREEZE** (hold LEFT FS), then "manipulate the **MODIFY** knob to generate bizarre chromatic melodies and arcade-style sound effects." Slip becomes a glitchy playable synth when frozen.
- MODIFY in Slip = **semitone-stepped** speed/direction (CCW of noon = reverse); TIME = sample size (low = pitch-shifter, high = trailing harmonies).
- **Use:** frozen-Slip as a chromatic glitch-synth / arcade-FX generator.

## Recipe 6 — "Stretching 101" + "Frozen" (manual p.34–35)
- **Micro MODE = Stretch.** Quote: *"Set both MODIFY and LENGTH to max, then slowly rotate MODIFY towards noon."* "Once things are moving nice and slow, gradually rotate the LENGTH knob down to its minimum position. Good ol' stretching."
- **FROZEN:** **MODIFY at noon** = "your loop will stop progressing and infinitely repeat the current slice. You can still adjust LENGTH … to shrink and expand the repeating moment."
- Note: with **MODIFY at its max position you will not hear any effect, regardless of LENGTH.**
- **Use:** classic grainy time-stretch wall (MODIFY just CCW of noon = slowest/most-stretched) → freeze a single grain at exact noon → LENGTH sizes the held grain.

## Recipe 7 — "Balance Beam" tape vibe (manual p.33)
- **Micro MODE = Tape.** **Lower the CLOCK** to introduce grit and character, **then raise MODIFY to speed the loop back up.** Quote: *"Same speed as you recorded it, but more vibe."*
- Tape MODIFY is **octave-quantized**: ½ / 1× / 2× / 4×, forward or reversed; LENGTH shrinks the loop.
- **Use:** keep the loop at its recorded pitch/speed but bake in low-CLOCK aliasing — degraded-but-in-tune tape loop. (Inverse move = slow CLOCK + 2×/4× MODIFY for the "gritty Tape loop.")

## Recipe 8 — Delay "Loop Tricks" (manual p.25)
- **Wet MODE = Delay.** **SELF-STRETCH:** "Once you have some audio repeating, start slowly rotating the **TIME** knob clockwise. You will notice a stretching effect is now recorded into your loop." (Cleanly transitions delay times with no pitch-bends — unless Classic Mode, which adds rubbery pitch.)
- **MICRO-LOOP TRANSFER:** route your micro-loop to the Wet Channel (**ROUTING = IN+micro-loop or micro-loop only**) and **crank Delay MODIFY (feedback)** to copy it; then switch ROUTING to **IN** to hear both loops.
- **Use:** hand-swept time-stretch baked into a loop; duplicating a micro-loop into the delay for layered loops.

## Recipe 9 — Env "Manual Stretching" by touch (manual p.32)
- **Micro MODE = Env.** **Crank MODIFY (sensitivity max).** Then gently tap the strings / send a quiet signal of any kind — "notice how the loop slows to a crawl. It's like time-stretching by touch." LENGTH = slice size (low = microscopic grains).
- **Use:** dynamic, attack-controlled stutter/freeze — your picking carves into the held bed (Perry Frank's "Reverb + Env" dynamic-drone idea, but the manual's own gesture).

## Hidden-option example positions worth noting (manual p.14–17, with the slider/knob icons)
These give concrete default/extreme positions for the hidden controls (hold BOTH footswitches, then move a control):
- **TONE** (Wet MODIFY): default fully **CW = brightest**; turn CCW toward **DARKER** to mellow the Wet algorithms / match the original MOOD voicing.
- **STEREO WIDTH** (Wet TIME): default mid; CW = **WIDER**, CCW = narrow (only active with SPREAD on).
- **DIRECT MICRO-LOOP** (Micro MODIFY): default **NONE** (fully CCW) → CW to **UNITY** to blend clean micro-loop into the 100%-wet routed path.
- **FADE** (Micro LENGTH): turn **down** = loops gradually fade while overdubbing (evolving loops / delay-like). *"FADE has no effect when the NO DUB dip switch is on."*
- **LEVEL BALANCE** (CLOCK): **noon = EVEN**; this is the real fix for channel-balance, not MIX.
- **SYNC** (Wet MODE toggle): LEFT = Micro-Looper synced to Wet TIME (capture in-time loops); RIGHT = Wet TIME steps related to loop length; center = UNSYNCED.
- **LOOP LENGTH** (Micro MODE toggle): HALF / FULL — cut loop length in half vs. CLOCK-set length.
- **SPREAD SOLO** (ROUTING toggle): apply SPREAD to one channel only (e.g. mono micro-loop through a ping-pong delay).
- **Reset all hidden options:** flip the PRESET toggle to left-and-back 3× → press both footswitches.

## Classic Mode behavior reference (manual p.42–43) — for the "recorded-wrong" aesthetic
Flicking the **CLASSIC** dip restores MkI flaws (knob set identical, sound/response differ):
- **CLOCK:** Classic = noise-full (turning down introduces noise); MkII = noise-free.
- **Micro-loops:** Classic = gradual deterioration/distortion over time; MkII = stable & infinite.
- **Reverb:** Classic = resonant/ringy trails (mild feedback); MkII = pure & modulated.
- **Slip:** Classic = aliased & uneven (crunchy digital artifacts); MkII = clean & consistent.
- **Delay (TIME):** Classic = bendy/warped (rubbery pitch-bends on time change); MkII = crisp & controlled.
- **Tape (LENGTH):** Classic = chops up the loop instead of shortening it; MkII = controls loop length.
- **NO DUB + CLASSIC + overdub engaged + LATCH off** = MkI "hold-to-record, release-to-play" behavior.
- **Use:** turn CLASSIC on for any drone/doom "degraded tape" patch — deteriorating loops + ringy reverb + aliased Slip is exactly the "recorded-wrong" wall this rig wants.

## Honest provenance note
All settings above are **factory** (official manual). NO new *artist* or *community* patch with
concrete knob values was found this pass — TGP / ModWiggler / Reddit / FB remain bot-walled or
login-gated, and the demo/review crowd (guitar.com, guitarsforidiots, sinesquares) explicitly
describe technique without exact positions. The pedal's culture is improvisational; the manual is
the only citable source of concrete starting positions, and these recipes were not previously
broken out as discrete patches on disk.
