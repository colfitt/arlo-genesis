# MOOD MkII — artists & techniques (honest)

Reality check up front: the MkII is recent (2023) and its mythology lives in the **ambient/experimental/looper** scene, not rock canon. There is **very little named-artist "signature MOOD setting"** content — most users treat it as an improvisation tool, so "their settings" don't really exist as patches. The exception, and the single most instructive figure, is **Andy Othling**, who literally authored the official presets. Coverage flagged below where it's thin.

## Andy Othling — "Lowercase Noises" (THE defining MOOD voice; most on-aesthetic)
- **Who:** Ambient guitarist from Albuquerque, NM; self-described "guitarist interested in playing as slow as possible." Does product design/consulting for **Old Blood Noise Endeavors and Chase Bliss** — i.e. he's tied to both halves of MOOD's design (OBNE = Wet Channel; the looper side = Drolo).
- **He wrote MOOD's official presets.** The MOOD onboard/factory presets are his, with absurdist Tim & Eric names — **"Nude Tayne," "Tittleman's Crest," "Krunkle Kick," "Sweet Berry Wine," "Uncle Muscles," "Glow Bones," "All the Food is Poison," "Where's My Chippy?," "Business Hugs."** Audio of the set: https://soundcloud.com/chaseblissaudio/sets/m-o-o-d. (⚠️ the *stored knob values* aren't published as a table; you copy them by ear.)
- **Philosophy — "pedalboard as a DAW" (directly relevant to this rig):** from the Tasselmyer "Sound Methods" interview — *"taking things that I would do in my DAW, in Logic, or with certain plugins and just saying, 'what if I could do that before it even hits the computer, so that I don't have to do it on the computer?'"* He builds **parallel signal chains** and visualizes the board like a mixer/DAW. (He's also built Max for Live MIDI-controller devices for Chase Bliss pedals.)
- **Technique on-brief for drone/ambient:** freeze notes/sounds into sustaining beds, then play over them; stacks multiple delays "to get a big soupy sound"; chases textural, "non-guitar / non-musical" sounds. This is the exact MOOD move (freeze Wet → play over the bed; capture → stretch/tape into a wall).
- **Best sources:** Sound Methods interview (https://andrewtasselmyer.substack.com/p/sound-methods-003-andy-othling); The Pedal Zone "pedalboard as a DAW" video (https://thepedalzone.com/other-videos/picking-andy-othlings-ambient-brain-its-time-to-see-your-pedalboard-as-a-daw/); Strymon feature (https://www.strymon.net/pedalboard-feature-andy-othling/); his YouTube/Patreon (https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCnXjoRC-GABh-aRYogZ7ayQ, https://www.patreon.com/andyothling). Equipboard confirms his board carries MOOD, Blooper, Generation Loss, CXM 1978, POG2.

## Knobs (Toronto videographer)
- Made the launch films for both V1 and the MkII; his demo aesthetic *is* the public sound of MOOD. Not "an artist using it for songs," but the canonical demonstrator. (Dossier links the launch films.)

## Mike Stringer — Spiritbox (heavier/metal use, but a near-identical texture stack)
- **Documented MkII user** (Equipboard) and confirmed on the **Premier Guitar "Rig Rundown: Spiritbox"** (pedalboard segment ~26:21–29:03). His ambient/texture board notably runs **Chase Bliss MOOD + Generation Loss + Hologram Microcosm** alongside EHX Freeze, EQD Sunn O))) Life, Rainbow Machines, DL4 MkII — i.e. the **same MOOD + Microcosm + Gen-Loss texture trio this rig uses**, just in a metal context. He layers "cleanly arpeggiating lines that flutter over dozens of ambience tracks and reverb tails... an otherworldly dense expanse of down-tuned guitar." Proof the MOOD/Microcosm/Gen-Loss combo scales to heavy/doom-adjacent walls.
- Source: https://www.premierguitar.com/videos/rig-rundown/spiritbox ; https://www.guitarworld.com/features/spiritbox-mike-stringer-eternal-blue-interview

## James Blake (studio, experimental)
- Seen using a MOOD MkII in a Vault.fm studio session (purple/pink enclosure), apparently for micro-looping, ambience and experimental texture processing. Documented-but-thin — no published settings. (Equipboard.)

## Mike Gordon — Phish (bass)
- Documented running **bass** through the MOOD MkII (Equipboard). Relevant caveat for this rig's Jazz Bass: keep CLOCK higher to preserve low-end (low-clock downsampling thins fundamentals); Tape octave-down = sub-drone.

## Shane Becker (repo cross-reference — OP-1 artist)
- Per the brief, Shane Becker runs vocals/instruments "thru the clean and MOOD." Couldn't locate a public rig rundown or settings for him in this pass — **artist coverage for Becker is thin/unverified online**; the cross-reference is from the repo's own OP-1 research, not a published source. Treat as internal context, not a citable artist source.

## The on-aesthetic pairing everyone actually demos: MOOD + Microcosm (+ OP-1)
- The most common on-aesthetic public demo is **MOOD MkII + Hologram Microcosm** for ambient looping, often with a synth source (incl. OP-1 / OP-1 Field). Example: The Pedal Collaborative, "Ambient Looping Demo with Mood Mkii + Microcosm // ft. OP-1 Field" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gC2piRvk8go ; writeup: https://thepedalcollaborative.com/.../). ⚠️ That article's "Micro / Judder / Sustain modes" describe the **Microcosm**, not MOOD (MOOD's modes are Reverb/Delay/Slip + Env/Tape/Stretch) — a common writeup error; don't carry it over. The takeaway that *is* solid: Microcosm generates the evolving bed, MOOD freezes/smears/micro-loops it — exactly the "Microcosm generates, MOOD warps, Blooper commits" division this rig is built on.

## Honest summary of artist coverage
- **Strong & on-aesthetic:** Andy Othling (defining, authored the presets, ambient/drone philosophy = this rig's ethos).
- **Useful corroboration:** Mike Stringer (same MOOD+Microcosm+GenLoss texture stack, heavier).
- **Documented but no usable settings:** James Blake, Mike Gordon, Knobs (demonstrator).
- **Thin/unverified:** Shane Becker (repo-internal only).
- **No "famous MOOD player with published patches" exists** — the pedal's culture is improvisational, so technique > settings is the right thing to copy.
