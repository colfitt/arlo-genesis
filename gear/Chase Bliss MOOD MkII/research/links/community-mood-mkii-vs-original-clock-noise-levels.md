# MkII-vs-original — the differences the community actually flags

A consolidated community/blog view of what changed V1 → MkII and the gotchas people raise.

## What people most consistently flag as the upgrades
- **Stereo in/out** — "the most requested feature." V1 was mono. (Universal across every writeup; Pedal of the Day literally writes "YES!!".)
- **Overdubbing on the micro-looper** — repeatedly called *"the biggest addition"*: layer infinite recordings into a loop for massive textures that were impossible on V1.
- **Loops up to ~2× longer** + a **FREEZE/HOLD on BOTH channels** (V1 froze only the wet side reliably).
- **Noise-free Clock by default.** The single biggest V1 complaint: *"the Clock knob was extremely noisy in the original MOOD… as you turned it counter-clockwise the fidelity would degrade in a fun way but introduced significant noise that persisted even with the pedal bypassed."* On the MkII that lo-fi grit is now **optional** (Classic Mode) rather than always-on.
- **TONE (wet hi-cut)** — added because the MkII voicing is *brighter/more present* than the darker/mellower V1; TONE lets you dial the MkII back toward V1 darkness.
- **Reverse** added to Stretch; full **MIDI control incl. dip switches**; **Note Mode** (MIDI keyboard transposes the wet output → play it as an instrument).

## The level/balance gotcha (V1 history worth knowing)
- On the **original MOOD** the two channels' levels were **offset by design and you could NOT balance them** — the only recourse was an internal trimmer ("turn the trimmer closest to the footswitches clockwise" to raise overall wet volume). The **MkII fixed this with the LEVEL BALANCE hidden option** (CLOCK knob in the hidden menu). If your reverb side is drowning your micro-loop (or vice-versa), that's the control — not MIX.

## Honest caveats
- Sinesquares is explicitly a *"first look"* (no extended hands-on); it's strong on the clock-noise A/B framing but doesn't provide knob recipes.
- The Sinesquares page carries a 2025 date but describes the 2023 launch — treat the date as a re-publish/refresh, not the release.
