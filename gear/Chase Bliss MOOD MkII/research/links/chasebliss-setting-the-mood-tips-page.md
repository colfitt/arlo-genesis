# "Setting the MOOD" — official tips/onboarding page

Chase Bliss's own getting-started essay for the MkII. Light on hard numbers (it punts the deep settings to the field guide / manual), but it confirms the intended workflow framing and the official starting advice.

## Concrete takeaways
- **Start with ALL dip switches OFF.** Official advice for anyone who doesn't want to dive into the advanced layer: *"it's best to start with all dip switches in the off position."*
- The complexity is deliberately tucked away: *"the complex bits [are] within the hidden options and behind the dip switches."* Surface controls stay simple; depth is opt-in.
- **Classic Mode** = the toggle for people who want the original pedal's behavior — *"preserves elements like the noise of the Clock and how micro-loops would slowly distort over time."*
- **LOOP LENGTH hidden option** exists specifically so you can *"go back to the shorter loop length of the original if the new [doubled] loop length doesn't work with your workflow."*
- **LEVEL BALANCE** (two-channel volume balance) is called out as one of the main user requests carried over from V1 feedback.
- Framing for the looper: treat it as **"fishing for music"** (always-listening, no stop), not a deliberate record-then-play looper.

## Rig note
Matches the dossier's framing. For our drone/doom use, the official "all dips off, then add one at a time" advice is the sane way to learn which misbehavior (CLASSIC, SMOOTH, SPREAD) you actually want baked into a preset — and dip settings save per-preset.
