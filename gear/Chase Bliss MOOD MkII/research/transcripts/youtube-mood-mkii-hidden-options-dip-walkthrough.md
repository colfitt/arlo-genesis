# MOOD MkII — hidden options + dip switches (community walkthrough, transcript distilled)

A power-user/community walkthrough (NOT the official CB video) demonstrating every dip switch and every hidden option by ear. Cleaned from auto-captions; behaviors below are as demonstrated/described on camera.

## Dip switches (demonstrated)
- **MISO** — mono-in → stereo-out. "If you have a mono input [and want a] stereo output, that's how you want it." (He can't demo it because he runs stereo-in/stereo-out.)
- **SPREAD** — "is *enhancing* that stereo field… it's NOT turning stereo on or off, it's just enhancing it, making it wider, creating separation." On Micro-Looper **Stretch** mode, spread "pans it from left to right." With spread OFF, Stretch is centered/static.
- **DRY KILL** — "takes out your dry signal before the pedal… your MIX just becomes a wet 0 to 100." Demo: with dry killed and MIX all the way down, "you're hearing nothing" because only the wet path remains.
- **TRAILS** — "same as Mark 1." His preference: *"I like to have trails engaged… so that it keeps a smoother effect in case I'm in the middle of performing something and I turn it off — I don't want it to kill the vibe."*
- **LATCH** — footswitch hold latches instead of momentary. *"That way you don't have to hold it down the whole time."* Without latch, the freeze/hold "stops as soon as I let go."
- **NO DUB** — *"turns off the overdub. In no dub it reduces the micro-looper feedback down to zero, [a] quick way to turn the micro-looper channel into a pseudo-live effect. You'll need to leave overdubbing engaged for this to work. The higher the CLOCK knob is turned up, the closer it will track your playing."* ← concrete power-user recipe.
- **SMOOTH** — smooth-clock function. *"Instead of having the stepped sections"* the clock sweeps continuously. He calls it "gnarly… so crazy" — great for clock-sweep sound design.
- (CLASSIC — referenced as the V1-misbehavior toggle; covered elsewhere.)

## Hidden options (hold both footswitches when green; each knob = one option)
Accessed by pressing both footswitches simultaneously while LEDs are green. In order:
1. **TONE** (wet hi-cut) — *"a simple high-cut filter for mellowing the wet algorithm… helpful for sitting back in the mix or matching the sound of the original mood. The original mood was a little darker/mellower; [the MkII] is a little brighter and more present."* Set with the **MODIFY** knob while holding. On-camera it was a **drastic** cut at max ("took all the highs out… wow, I didn't expect it to do that much").
2. **STEREO WIDTH** — set with the **wet TIME knob**. Sets the panning width of the wet channel *when SPREAD is engaged*. Min = "squished completely" (mono-ish), max = "back out sounding glorious."
3. **DIRECT MICRO-LOOP** — set with the looper **MODIFY** knob. Blends in some of the *clean* micro-loop when it's routed through the wet channel. Audible volume bump as you add dry micro-loop.
4. **FADE** — *"will cause loops to gradually fade while overdubbing, for slowly evolving loops, or the ability to treat the micro-looper channel like a delay."* ⚠️ **FADE and NO DUB are mutually exclusive: "when no dub is engaged, no more fade. No dub, no fade."**
5. **RAMPING WAVEFORM** — set with the **MIX** knob. Fully CCW = **triangle** (default) → square → sine → random (stepped) → **smooth random** fully CW. Demonstrated triangle vs random vs square sweeping the CLOCK.
6. **LEVEL BALANCE** — set with the **CLOCK** knob. *"Sets the relative loudness of the two channels (wet vs micro-looper)."* CCW = more micro-looper, CW = more wet (reverb), noon = even. (This is the MkII fix for the V1 complaint that you couldn't balance the two channels.)
7. **LOOP LENGTH** (half/full) — hold both + move the **looper MODE** toggle. Cuts the micro-looper loop length in half "to match the original mood, relative to the clock setting."
8. **SPREAD SOLO** — lets you engage SPREAD on **one channel but not the other** (e.g. Reverb spread/ping-ponging while Stretch stays a normal centered stereo). Switch the side with the **wet MODE** toggle while in the hidden menu.
9. **SYNC** — set with the **wet MODE toggle** in the hidden menu: LEFT = micro-looper synced to wet (loop length now set by the wet TIME knob); MIDDLE = unsynced; RIGHT = wet synced to micro-looper (wet TIME now moves in steps rhythmically related to the micro-loop).

## True bypass + reset
- **True bypass:** tap both footswitches **3×**; all three LEDs blink red. Tap either footswitch to exit. (Note from manual/dossier: looper does not run in true-bypass.)
- **Reset hidden options to factory:** flip the **preset toggle left → center 3×**; when lights blink, press both footswitches simultaneously to confirm. (Same gesture as MkI/Generation Loss.)
