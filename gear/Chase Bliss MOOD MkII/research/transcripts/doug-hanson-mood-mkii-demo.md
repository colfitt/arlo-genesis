Today we're checking out the Chase Bliss MOOD Mark II. Brief overview of the controls:

**Left side — Wet Channel:** contains Reverb, Delay, and Slip mode (Slip samples the input and plays it back at different speeds and directions). The function of the TIME and MODIFY knobs changes depending on which mode you're using.

**Right side — Micro-Looper Channel:**
- **Stretch** — record a loop, then stretch it out, moving through the loop at a rate you decide.
- **Tape** — record a loop, then play it back at different speeds and directions.
- **Env (envelope)** — chops the loop into slices and lets you interrupt it with your playing: whenever your input goes over the threshold, the current slice is repeated until the input signal goes back down.

**Center — shared controls:**
- **CLOCK** — controls the sample rate. Sample rate affects the quality and time of the Wet Channel and also the length and resolution of the loops.
- **Middle toggle (Routing)** — controls what gets processed by the Wet Channel: your input, your input + the Micro-Looper, or just the Micro-Looper.
- **Bottom toggle** — accesses the two onboard presets.

**New for Mark II:** stereo processing, overdubbing, freezing, syncing, full MIDI control and MIDI clock, and a bunch of other hidden options. Also different from V1 is the quality of the sounds — **Mark II sounds are more polished and stable, while the original MOOD's are lower-fidelity.** Flipping the **CLASSIC** dip switch lets you still have all the sounds that were in the original. The other dip switches give options for configuring MOOD for your setup and for ramping MOOD's parameters.

Also new to Mark II is **Synth Mode**, which lets you play MOOD's CLOCK with a MIDI controller by sending MIDI notes.
