# MOOD MkII Video Manual pt.1 — controls, channels, modes (official walkthrough)

The canonical control reference straight from Chase Bliss. This is the authoritative source for what every knob/mode actually does; use it as the basis for building recipes. Distilled to prose below; concrete mode behavior quoted where it matters for dialing in settings.

## Framing
- "MOOD is designed for playful exploration, improvisation and discovery... it's all about finding new sounds." Treat it as a chemistry set, not a set-and-forget effect.
- Two-channel multi-effect: the **Micro-Looper** ("samples and loops brief moments") and the **Wet Channel** ("a collection of real-time spatial effects"). "The channels are aware of each other and work together — you can process micro loops with the Wet Channel, or record the Wet Channel into the Micro-Looper."

## I/O
- Stereo in/out = TRS both jacks, works out of the box.
- Mono-in → stereo-out: TS cable in, TRS cable out, **activate the MISO dip switch**.

## Shared controls
- **MIX** — balance between input signal and MOOD; controls both channels simultaneously.
- **CLOCK** — sets MOOD's sample rate. "This controls the length and resolution of the Micro-Looper Channel as well as the quality and time of the Wet Channel. One of the most powerful and satisfying knobs you can twist." Slowing the clock = "wonderfully crunchy places."
- **ROUTING** — determines which signal the Wet Channel processes: your input / the Micro-Looper Channel / or both. "Only has an effect if both channels are active."
- **PRESETS toggle** — left/right store a preset, middle = live. Save to right: hold right footswitch 3s, then add left footswitch 3s (middle LED blinks = success). Save to left: start with the left footswitch.

## Wet Channel modes (engage SPREAD for stereo in the demo)
- **Reverb** — TIME = decay/size (CCW = short, CW = long). MODIFY = smear on the taps: "traditional reverb sounds are on the clockwise side; as the knob is turned counter-clockwise the taps become more and more distinguishable, like a multi-tap delay." Freeze the Wet Channel by holding the left footswitch — "the current sound repeats infinitely... you can move the knobs and manipulate the sound while frozen."
- **Delay** — "MOOD's simplest mode." TIME = delay time, designed so changing it introduces **no pitch shifting** ("a fun knob to manipulate as you play"). MODIFY = feedback (CCW = one repeat — one per side if SPREAD on; fully CW = "the delay will pile up forever like a Looper"). Tip: "try adjusting the TIME knob with MIX and MODIFY turned up to introduce a kind of manual time-stretching."
- **Slip** — "an oddity. It samples your input continuously and spits it back out at a speed of your choosing, generating whimsical harmonies and pitch shifting." TIME = sampling size (lower = instant, pitch-shifter-like; higher = "harmonized phrases that follow behind you"). MODIFY = playback speed/direction in **semitone steps**. "A common use of Slip is to create a real-time reverse effect."

## Micro-Looper Channel (always listening)
- "It's always listening and recording while it's bypassed. When you engage the channel you get to hear what's currently in the buffer... it's kind of like fishing for music." Loop length = set by the **CLOCK position**; LED blinks at the start of each loop cycle.
- **Trail Catcher / capture trick:** because the looper records while bypassed, "its effects will be recorded into your micro loops" — anything the Wet Channel (or upstream) is doing gets baked in.
- **Overdub:** once you've snagged a loop, hold the Micro-Looper footswitch to pile sounds on. With LATCH engaged, overdub stays active until you long-press to deactivate. "Once a micro-loop is recorded you can switch freely between the looping modes without it being erased."
- **Tape** — most straightforward. LENGTH = amount of the loop that plays back (slice gets shorter as you turn CCW). MODIFY = speed/direction/pitch, **quantized to octaves: half-speed, normal, 2×, 4×**. Tip: "combine slower clock speeds with faster playback speeds to introduce interesting grit and vibe."
- **Stretch** — "zoom in and explore the details of your loop, spreading short phrases into sprawling epics" by chopping into slices and moving through them. LENGTH = slice size (higher = clarity/repeating phrases; lower = "blurry and grainy"). MODIFY = direction + amount of stretch ("the closer to noon, the slower you progress"; forward = CW of noon, reverse = CCW). At **MODIFY = noon it freezes** on a single moment; LENGTH then sets the size of the repeated moment. "A few layers of overdubbing and Stretch mode can turn just about anything into an infinitely interesting texture."
- **Env (envelope)** — "interrupt the loop with your playing to create dynamic stutters and frozen notes." Chops loop into slices; "whenever sound is detected at the input it repeats the current slice until the sound disappears." LENGTH = slice size (low = microscopic grains; high = short phrases). MODIFY = audio-detector sensitivity (further CCW = less sensitive).

## On-aesthetic takeaways for a drone/doom/shoegaze rig
- Tape MODIFY at half-speed/octave-down on a captured banjo/baritone phrase = instant low drone bed (this is the documented behavior, exactly the rig's "banjo-as-lead → drone" use).
- Stretch MODIFY at noon = freeze a single grain into a sustained wall; nudge LENGTH for the size of the held moment.
- Low CLOCK = the degradation/grit that stacks with the rig's "recorded-wrong" tape aesthetic.
- Freeze Wet Reverb (hold left FS) → ambient pad to play over = the sustained-wall move.
