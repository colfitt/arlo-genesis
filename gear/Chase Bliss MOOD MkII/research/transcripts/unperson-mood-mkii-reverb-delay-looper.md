The MOOD Mark 2 is essentially a reverb and delay pedal with a built-in micro-looper, but its unique architecture turns it into an ambient texture machine. "Every time I sit down with MOOD it spits out something really unexpected and beautiful."

### Effects (Wet Channel, left side) — TIME on top, MODIFY below
- **Reverb** — on its own it sounds like a multi-tap delay; engaging MODIFY smooths out those repeats into wash.
- **Delay** — demoed in ping-pong mode (changed on the back panel via SPREAD). Moving the TIME parameter does NOT produce pitch modulations (clean time changes). MODIFY can be pushed to infinity-feedback / self-oscillating buildup.
- **Slip** — a pitch-shifting delay. Shown fully wet, then blended with the dry signal. Moving MODIFY to the left gives **reverse**.

### Looper (right side) — always recording; engage the footswitch to hear the buffer
Three loop modes:
- **Stretch** — "kind of like a granular thing."
- **Tape** — "pitch-shifty," and can also go into reverse.
- **Env** — interesting: inputting any new note information interrupts the loop.

**Overdub** by holding down the footswitch (demoed in Tape mode).

### Clock (center) — affects all effects and the looper simultaneously
As the CLOCK gets lower, sound quality decreases but **loop length increases** — useful for recording over the top of a longer, grittier loop. Combining different pitch effects (looper) with the wet effects "can bring you into really weird and unexpected territory."

### Routing demo (the key practical bit)
The Wet effects can be set to affect:
- only the **input**, or
- only the **loop** (so the input signal stays dry but the loop is drenched in reverb), or
- **both.**
The "dry input + reverb-soaked loop" routing is highlighted as a beautiful ambient setup.

### Sonic explorations (sources)
- **Vong Replay synth** — chosen deliberately because it only has basic waveforms and no effects, so it shows off MOOD's power. Engages the loop and builds a montage.
- **Mutable Rings → MOOD** — "I had a feeling they'd work nicely together and I was correct." Runs a loop on MOOD; uses a **hidden function to decrease the loop's volume so it sits in the background**, with Rings running through the reverb for a subtle, beautiful ambience.
- **OP-1 controlling an Arturia "Augmented Woodwinds" clarinet preset → MOOD.**

Closing notes: "I've only scratched the surface… you can kind of use it as a sampler, which I plan to explore." (Rig-relevant: the OP-1 Field and synth/VST sources route into MOOD exactly like this.)
