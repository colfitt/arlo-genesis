# MOOD MkII Video Manual pt.2 — customization, dip switches & hidden options (official)

Covers the dip switches and the hidden-options menu (hold both footswitches). Authoritative reference for the "recipe scaffolding" — which switch does what when you build a patch. The video explicitly points to a separate **"field guide"** (linked in the video description) for more specifics on Classic Mode and deeper recipes.

## Dip switches (top-level customization)
- **MISO** — mono in → stereo out (split a mono signal to a stereo output).
- **SPREAD** — "generate an affected stereo image. Each of MOOD's modes has a unique take on this." (Reverb scatters taps, Delay ping-pongs, etc.)
- **DRY KILL** — removes your clean signal from the pedal's output.
- **TRAILS** — Wet Channel modes "smoothly fade out after the pedal is bypassed."
- **LATCH** — hold function for each channel becomes latching instead of momentary.
- **NO DUB** — drops Micro-Looper feedback to zero for a "pseudo-live effect"; you must leave overdubbing engaged. "The higher the CLOCK is turned up, the closer it will track your playing."
- **SMOOTH (clock)** — removes the harmonized stepping from the CLOCK knob for fluid adjustment.
- **CLASSIC** — "brings back all the flaws and idiosyncrasies of MOOD Mark 1 — clock noise, deteriorating loops and more, because sometimes those are just what you need." (See the field guide for specifics.)

## Hidden options (hold BOTH footswitches — LEDs go green — then turn the listed knob)
- **Wet MODIFY** → simple **high-cut filter** for mellowing the wet algorithms (TONE).
- **Wet TIME** → **stereo width** of the Wet Channel (only when SPREAD is engaged).
- **Wet MODE toggle** → **SYNC** between channels:
  - Right position = Wet synced to Micro-Looper; the TIME knob now "moves in steps rhythmically related to the micro-loop length."
  - Left position = Micro-Looper synced to Wet; micro-loop length is now set by the Wet TIME knob — "a great way to capture rhythmic ideas; if your playing is in time with the Wet effects, your loop will neatly capture that timing."
- **MIX** → selects the **ramping LFO waveform** (shapes warp smoothly from one to the next).
- **(level balance knob)** → relative loudness of the two channels; turn toward the channel you want more of; **noon = even**.
- **ROUTING toggle** → **SPREAD SOLO**: apply Spread to only one channel (e.g. keep a mono micro-loop but pass it through a ping-pong delay). Middle = both channels get Spread; flip toward the channel you want Spread on.
- **Micro-Looper LENGTH** → turned CCW causes loops to **gradually fade while overdubbing** (slowly-evolving loops, or treat the Micro-Looper like a delay).
- **Micro-Looper MODIFY** → **DIRECT MICRO-LOOP blend**: normally a micro-loop through the Wet Channel is 100% wet, but turning this CW reintroduces some of the **clean** micro-loop into the signal.
- **Micro-Looper MODE toggle** → cut **loop length in half** to match the original MOOD's response (flip left).

## Reset
- Reset all hidden options to default: flip the **PRESET toggle left and back to center 3× in a row**, then press both footswitches simultaneously to confirm.

## Notes for this rig
- **TONE high-cut** (hidden, Wet MODIFY) is the gentle way to keep MOOD's degraded wash from getting harsh before it hits the downstream tape stages.
- **DIRECT MICRO-LOOP blend** (hidden, Micro MODIFY) lets a captured banjo/baritone loop stay partly clean under the Wet smear — useful when you want the loop legible inside a drone.
- **LENGTH-fade overdub** (hidden) = slowly-evolving loops, the ambient build move.
- **SYNC** (hidden, Wet MODE) + MIDI clock = in-time captures locked to Big Time / Blooper.
