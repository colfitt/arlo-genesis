# MOOD MkII firmware 1.1 — what it actually does (transcript distilled)

Official Chase Bliss clip announcing firmware 1.1 alongside the Light Bright limited edition. Distilled from auto-captions.

## The firmware fix (the only sonic change in 1.1)
- 1.1 restores the **original MOOD MkI character of the Stretch algorithm (the Drolo channel)**. Joel: the MkI Stretch *"sounds like that — very cool, very hip,"* and the MkI-vs-MkII difference *"was reasonably confusing to people"* even though *"the MkII hardware is way more sophisticated and flexible than the MkI hardware."*
- They struggled to reproduce it: *"we couldn't figure out how to do it for [the MkII]… we kept bashing our skulls against hard objects."* The fix came from outside: while working on the **Goodhertz** project, **Jasper from Goodhertz made a suggestion**, they implemented it, and the new firmware Stretch now sounds *"pretty damn close"* to the MkI.
- Takeaway for the rig: the original cloudy/wispy Stretch flavor lives in **firmware 1.1 inside Classic Mode** — relevant for the "recorded-wrong" Stretch granular textures.

## Updater
- They also rebuilt the firmware-update flow: *"we've also made that process way easier."* Update via the Bliss Programmer at **firmware.chasebliss.com** (said as "firmware.all" in the auto-caption — mis-transcription).
- All MkII units shipped from this point forward ship **with 1.1**; existing owners update for free.

## Caveat
- This clip demos the A/B on **MkI hardware** to illustrate the Stretch character difference; the deliverable is the **MkII firmware 1.1**. Verified: 1.1 restores MkI Stretch *within Classic Mode* (corroborated by the dossier and CB's anniversary post).
