# Ondes-Martenot Bowed Banjo Lead

Turn the banjo into a gliding, swelling, *singing* monophonic lead — no pick attack, long ducking trails — the Ondes-Martenot lead feel on the banjo voice. A lead that floats, not strikes. ⭐ signature-fit.

This is a 🟣 designed integration of owned-gear patches; no artist ever played *this* chain. The Taste-ref names the sound it chases, and the per-unit patch sheets carry their own provenance.

## Signal path

EBM-5 banjo (GK-5) → **VG-800 (#5 VIO Bowed Swell Drone** with portamento, *or* **VG-TB1 Ondes-Martenot lead voice** from the art-rock index) → **Clean (TB1 Ondes-Martenot Bowed-Swell)** → [Board 2 thru / light] → [Board 3] → **Big Time (TB3 Ondes-Martenot Lead Delay)** → MOOD (#12 Freeze Pad, light) → Chroma → **H90 (#9 Shimmer Drone — exp-pedal → Pitch Decay → Freeze)** → PORTA424 → tape.

## Per-unit

- VG-800 **#5 VIO Bowed Swell Drone** — VIO GUITAR with `LEAD EMPHASIS` up + `SLOW GEAR`/`SOUND HOLD` to remove the pick attack; `NORMAL MIX OFF`. Add **portamento/glide** (the VG-TB1 ribbon-glide) for the seamless pitch slide. Recall GK **#1 EBM5 BANJO**.
- Clean **TB1 Ondes-Martenot Bowed-Swell** — AUX Dynamic Swell; Hidden: Wet = Swell In ~noon–1:00 (slowish bowed attack), Dry = Swell Out ~noon. Sensitivity set so only intended notes bow in. The hard banjo pluck *bows in* like the Ondes' intensity key.
- Big Time **TB3 Ondes-Martenot Lead Delay** — MODE Long, STATE Compressed (sagging trails that duck under the attack then bloom), VOICING Focus (vocal-like), CLUSTER low (~15% — the *line* is the melody), MOTION Sine slow+shallow, FEEDBACK ~50% long singing trails.
- MOOD **#12 Freeze Pad** (light) — optional held ambient pad under the lead; LATCH so it holds hands-free, TRAILS on so toggling doesn't kill the wash.
- H90 **#9 Shimmer Drone (staff move)** — expression pedal → Pitch Decay, sweep up to bring shimmer tails in, push past 100 to Freeze before the pitch-loop clash; the reliable shimmer-drone-behind-a-lead.

## Routing

Mono banjo → VG-800 (glide + slow-gear swell) → Clean (the swell engine). This chain is **dynamics-and-glide first** — the swell comes from Clean, the glide from the VG-800 portamento, the long singing tail from Big Time. Keep CLUSTER low on Big Time so the single line stays a melody, not a wash. Map an expression pedal to H90 Pitch Decay (or to Big Time COLOR/FEEDBACK) for a hand-ridden swell/recede. No clock required; this is a free-time lead.

## Sound

A bowed-in, gliding banjo line with no audible pick attack, trailing long compressed echoes that duck around each note and bloom after — voice-like, "nothing closer to singing."

Taste-ref: art-rock-studio-as-instrument — Jonny Greenwood's Ondes-Martenot leads ("How to Disappear Completely," "The National Anthem," Kid A): intensity-key swell + ribbon glide. The rig *approximates* the Ondes: swell from Clean, glide from VG-800 portamento, the instrument is the banjo — not a real Ondes.

## Play

Pick a note and let Clean bow it in (no audible attack); slide between notes for the VG-800 portamento glide; ride the expression pedal to swell the H90 shimmer tails in behind the line. Hold MOOD Freeze (LATCH) for a held pad under a sustained passage.
