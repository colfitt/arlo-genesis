# 108 → Hizumitas → Big Time Endless Wall  ⭐

The marquee doom move and the single most-used wall here — one held baritone/banjo chord becomes an oceanic single-chord-forever bed on the centerpiece delay. Minimal chain INTO Big Time. This is a designed integration; no artist played this exact chain (Swans/Gira use "only volume and sustain," no pedals). It chases the documented sustaining-wall result.

## Signal path

Baritone JM / banjo (GK-5 → **VG-800 #7 Doom Baritone Wall (−7 STEP)**) → **CB Clean #7 Always-On Huge Fuzz Feeder** → **MXR 108 #9 (108→Hizumitas DOOM WALL)** → **EQD Hizumitas #3 Doom Wall** → (Front board out, mono) → **CB Generation Loss** (light tape veil, optional) → **CB Big Time #9 Hizumitas Fuzz Wall** (IN-L auto-**MISO** mono→stereo) → MOOD/Blooper bypassed → **H90 #1 Cavernous Blackhole Drone** (low Mix, body only) → PORTA424 / JHS 424 → tape.

## Per-unit

- **VG-800 #7:** runs `−7 STEP`, dark AMP, `NORMAL MIX ~30%` so the real baritone body sits under the model.
- **Clean #7:** Sensitivity hot by the left LED, Dynamics ~noon (hard limiting) so even quiet picking slams the fuzz evenly.
- **MXR 108 #9:** OUTPUT 12:00 / FUZZ 12–2:00 / **BUFFER ON** (grind + level pre-stage).
- **Hizumitas #3:** **Volume max · Sustain max · Tone full CW** (the controllable bass-side doom voice).
- **Big Time #9:** MODE **Long**, STATE **Compressed** (holds the sustaining fuzz together, no runaway), VOICING **Focus**, **COLOR LOW ~25–35%** (the fuzz already supplies saturation), FEEDBACK ~60%, TILT EQ pushed UP to cut muff mud.
- **H90 #1:** Blackhole Mix pulled back to ~30% (body, not a second wall).

## Routing

Mono front board → Big Time IN-L auto-engages MISO (mono-in/stereo-out); stereo from Big Time onward. No clock needed (drone, not rhythmic). **Gain-staging is the whole game:** Clean limits → 108 grinds → Hizumitas saturates → keep Big Time COLOR modest so the limiter doesn't clamp the hot fuzz "to porridge" (per Big Time #9). If the 108 sounds thin even buffer-on, swap to Hizumitas → 108 (Big Time #9 order-swap note). Favor **DRY KILL** on the Big Time if you want a pure wet wall before tape.

## Sound

A thick, saturated, slowly-blooming wall that holds forever on one chord — the rig's defining doom bed. Taste-ref: post-punk-texture-dynamics / doom (Swans-adjacent, Boris/Wata sustaining wall).

## Play

Hold one chord, let the overtone bloom at volume. As a centerpiece move, hold Big Time's RIGHT footswitch to **FREEZE** the bed and solo a banjo line over it. Hold-MODE on Big Time is the panic-reset back to a clean delay if it runs away.
