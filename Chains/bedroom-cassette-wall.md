# Bedroom Cassette Wall

"The demo *is* the song" — Will Toledo's budget-DAW degrade: a quiet source clipped and boosted into crunchy-dry distortion, the part layered "as big as possible," the warts left audible, bounced through a double-cassette print. The most *direct* "recorded-wrong" chain on the rig — push every gain stage past clean and leave the imperfection in. Keep it DRY (no long verb).

This is a **designed integration** — no artist ever used this exact chain. Real method = Audacity/GarageBand/Logic on a laptop, bundled PC mic, a "keeps-breaking" Boss DS-2, a Tele, a Supro, direct-in — none of this rig. ⚠️ no vocal path (screamed-mic = character port); room-bleed approximated by tape glue. 🟣 designed.

## Signal path

banjo/baritone (GK-5 → **VG-800**) → **CB Clean** (always-on) → **Colour Box V2** (crunchy-dry boost-into-clipping) → **Oxford Drive** (DS-2 mid-crunch, relocated front) → **Deco V2** (double-track the part "as big as possible") → *[Board 2 bypassed/dry]* → **Generation Loss** (laptop-bounce cassette) → **PORTA424 → JHS 424** (the double-cassette print) → Apollo.

## Per-unit

- **VG-800** — a bright **E.GUITAR** model (the cheap-Tele-direct-in source) or **#33 iPhone-Intimate Clean Acoustic Bed** for a cleaner source to ruin. Recorded "relatively quietly," boosted downstream. Keep `NORMAL MIX` low.
- **CB Clean** — **#7 Always-On Huge Fuzz Feeder** (Sensitivity high, Dynamics into limiting, Wet hot) so even quiet picking slams the Colour Box consistently = Toledo's "recorded quiet, boosted" move at the front; or #1 *Home Base* for a gentler level.
- **Colour Box V2** — **CBX-LF1 Crunchy-Dry Direct-In** (HI · STEP 3 +28 dB · PRE-VOL 1:00 · MIDDLE +2 with MID SHIFT ~12:00 = the DS-2 mid spike · TREBLE/BASS ~noon — dry, don't fatten · HI-PASS ON ~9–10:00 · MASTER trim to unity). The literal "boost a quiet source into clipping." Keep it DRY downstream.
- **Oxford Drive** — **OXF-LF1 Crunchy-Dry Demo Crunch** (Voice '91 · Clipping Si · Gain 11–12:00 · Bass 8:00 LOW · Contour 9:00 mid-forward = the DS-2 honk · Treble 1:00 bright/dry). **Relocate ahead of the fuzzes** (or run banjo-only into the End-board print) so its fixed-mid filter isn't collapsing a fuzz wall.
- **Deco V2** — **DEC-LF1 Double-Track Jangle Chorus** or **DEC-LF2 Cassette Demo Fatten + Wobble** (Tape Saturation ON light glue · Doubletracker ON, Type sum, Lag ~12:00 = the natural thick chorus · Wobble low–mid = tape movement · optional Wide Stereo). This is the "layer the part as big as possible" doubler. ⚠️ Brock's real movement is a *stereo tremolo* — the Deco wobble + Wide-Stereo doubler is the nearest **approximation**, not the real effect.
- **Generation Loss** — **GL-LF1 Laptop-Bounce Cassette Demo** (Model 7 Dictatron-EX or 10 MS-Walker · Wow/Flutter ~9:00 · Saturate ~11:00 · Failure ~9:00 with **DROP BYP ON** = consistent bedroom warble · Dry Small). Pairs with the PORTA424 print for the double-cassette / laptop-bounce feel. Keep hidden Hiss low + HUM BYP so it doesn't stack a second noise floor into the tape print.
- **PORTA424** — **P424-LF1 Bedroom 4-Track Demo Print** (Input Trim 12–1:00 = the breakup gate · Treble 10:00 shelved down · **9V** for trashier breakup). **JHS 424** — **424-LF1 Demo-Is-The-Song 4-Track Grit** (Volume 11 · Gain 1 12:00 · Gain 2 12:00 *kept under the 3:00 gate* · Treble 12 · XLR → Apollo, mono-commit). The double-cassette finish — chords smear, single notes bloom.

## Routing / sync

**DRY, mono-forward** — this rig prints direct, so the "room-mic'd amp bleed" is *approximated* by tape glue + degrade, not an actual mic'd room (flagged). Board 2 (Microcosm/QI/Dark Star) is **bypassed or barely on** — no long verb; the lo-fi cluster lives entirely in the dirt + degrade arc. Gain-staging order is the rig's console-to-tape arc: **CB Clean → Colour Box (STEP → PRE-VOL → MASTER last, it jumps loud) → Oxford → Deco → Gen Loss → PORTA424 → JHS 424.** Layer multiple passes (re-record the part 2–3×, slightly different) for the dense cheap wall. ⚠️ the blown-out *screamed-vocal* character (CBX-LF2 / P424-LF3) has **no vocal path on this rig** — ported to a string source as *character*, used as a moment, not Toledo's actual chain.

## Sound

A crunchy, dry, mid-forward, double-tracked banjo/baritone part, boosted from a quiet source into clipping, layered big, warbled and dropout-crinkled by cassette, printed through two tape stages — obviously recorded to a four-track, warts proudly audible.

## Play

Record the part quietly, boost it through the Colour Box. Re-record 2–3 layered passes ("as big as possible"). Ride the instrument volume for the clean↔hair dynamic (Brock move) if you relocate Oxford as the ShredMaster base. For a degraded climax moment, swap to **Colour Box CBX-LF2 broken-console max-fuzz** or **Longsword LSW-LF2 8-bit broken slam** into **424-LF2 gated DS-2 spit** — the blown-out-screamed-character crush, as a moment, not a whole song.
