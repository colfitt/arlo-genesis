# Clean Dusty → Hizumitas Stacked Disrepair

Degradation stacked on degradation. Instead of feeding the Hizumitas a clean, well-behaved source, you feed it a source that is *already coming apart* — Clean run in DUSTY mode, cranked into Sag with the Physics spring twitching, so every chord blooms then sputters and falters before the Muff even sees it. The Hizumitas then resolves that crumbling pre-dirt into a sustained doom wall (clipping limiter → Big Muff = two distinct distortion characters in series, not just more gain), and the whole thing prints to cassette through the PORTA424 for tape-ruined weight. A crumbly, sputtering, faltering source eating itself, then aged on tape.

🟣 DOUG-designed integration. No artist played this exact chain — the per-unit patch refs carry their own provenance, and the Taste-ref names the sound it chases (Swans crushing-sustain weight, Boris/Wata fuzz-with-a-distortion-in-front chaos). The Clean-Dusty-as-pre-dirt move and the cassette terminus are the rig's degradation thesis applied to the Hizumitas/Clean spotlight.

## Signal path

guitar / baritone → **CB Clean — "Dusty Pre-Dirt Sputter"** (DUSTY on · Dynamics cranked into Sag · Physics RIGHT twitchy · Mode RIGHT Modulated/drifting EQ) → **EQD Hizumitas — "Doom Wall (max heavy)"** (mono) → **MXNHLT PORTA424 — "Ruined Cassette Wall"** (cassette print, end of chain) → tape.

The only "real-artist signal order" invoked is the general **drive/fuzz-in-front-of-fuzz** standard (Wata's "I often use the Hizumitas with a distortion to create more chaotic feel"), translated here to *clipping-limiter*-in-front rather than a second fuzz. The disrepair is built in *two* distinct stages — Clean's stage-2 Dusty clip + Sag, then the Hizumitas Muff — so the source faltering and the wall saturation read as separate textures, not one mush.

## Per-unit

- **CB Clean — "Dusty Pre-Dirt Sputter"** — the pre-dirt engine. DUSTY dip ON turns Clean into a blooming overdrive that "sputters and breaks when you dig in"; CRANK Dynamics into Sag (~2:00) for the looming, falling-out clip; ROLL BACK Sensitivity (this is the Dusty amount / clip threshold); BOOST Wet (~1:00) to recover the level Sag eats; Dry ~11:00 so the dust bleeds onto the dry signal too. **Physics RIGHT (twitchy)** for the snapping, faltering sputter; **Mode RIGHT (Modulated)** so the EQ drifts under the dirt — that drifting EQ is the "Modulated EQ" twitch that keeps the source unstable. It is finicky: balance Sensitivity/Wet/Dry by ear and by the LED. *Honesty note: the manual publishes the Sag zone as "past 3:00 / last quarter" and the DUSTY walkthrough as a procedure; the exact Sensitivity/Wet/Dry/Attack clock positions here are a dialable recipe, not sourced numbers.*
- **EQD Hizumitas — "Doom Wall (max heavy)"** — the Muff stage. **Volume max · Sustain max · Tone fully CW** (max bass; mids scooped, highs tamed). Full-CW Tone is deliberate: the dark/scooped voicing keeps the wall *controllable* and gives the Hizumitas a thick low-mid source to chew on rather than amplifying the Clean-Dusty fizz into hiss. The Hizumitas does the *smoothing* — it resolves the crumbling, sputtering pre-dirt into a long even sustain. Needs amp/monitor volume for the sustain bloom to arrive so the held chord never decays before the wall builds.
- **MXNHLT PORTA424 — "Ruined Cassette Wall"** — the tape terminus. **Input Trim MAXED · Treble 1:00 · Bass 12:00 · Channel Fader ~90% · Master well down (it's loud) · 9V** for maximum trash/compression. Placed at the end of chain, the whole bus collapses into one blown-out lo-fi bounce — Swans-style brittle density over the sustained doom climax. One-stage-destroys discipline: this *is* the destroy stage, so don't also slam a JHS 424 behind it unless you want total collapse on a final climax. *All clock/fader/voltage values here are designed-to-spec for the ruined-cassette aesthetic, not measured factory positions.*

## Routing

Mono, front to back — guitar/baritone into Clean, Clean into the Hizumitas, Hizumitas into the PORTA424, PORTA424 to tape. No clock needed (this is a held-chord / slow-riff drone arc, not rhythmic).

**Gain-staging is the whole game, and the order of operations is unusual.** Normally you keep the *source* clean so the fuzz behaves; here you deliberately make the source fall apart first. So Clean's Sag/Dusty has to be set just past the point of sputter, *not* into total dropout — if it gates out completely, the Hizumitas has nothing to sustain and the wall stutters instead of blooming. Roll Sensitivity back until digging in trips the sputter but easing off recovers a held note; that touch-sensitive threshold is the performance. The Hizumitas then supplies all the *saturation* — its max-Sustain wall is what carries the faltering source into a continuous bed — so you are not asking the PORTA424 for more dirt, only for tape collapse. At the terminus, bring the PORTA424's Master well down (the Ruined Cassette Wall setting is genuinely loud) and let Input Trim do the trashing. If the low end flubs when the cassette stage clips on a baritone, that is the trade you accept for 9V trash — for a firmer drone bottom, swap the terminus to **MXNHLT PORTA424 — "Baritone Drone Terminus"** (18V, bass shelved down) and lose some of the blown-out crush.

## Sound

A source that is already crumbling — chords bloom, sputter, falter, the EQ drifting underneath — resolved by the Hizumitas into a thick, sustained, low-mid doom wall, then printed to cassette so the whole thing collapses into a blown-out, brittle, tape-ruined bounce. Two distinct distortion characters (sputtering clip-limiter, then Muff saturation) stacked under cassette aging: degradation on degradation on degradation.

**Taste-ref:** Swans crushing-sustained weight with Boris/Wata "fuzz with a distortion in front for a more chaotic feel" energy, finished with the rig's cassette-print disrepair. On-brand recorded-wrong doom.

## Play

1. Set the Hizumitas wall first (Volume/Sustain max, Tone full CW) and confirm a held chord blooms to full sustain at volume.
2. Bring Clean in front in DUSTY mode: crank Dynamics into Sag, Physics RIGHT, Mode RIGHT, then roll Sensitivity back until **digging in makes the source sputter and falter** but easing off lets a note hold. That dynamic threshold is the whole feel — hard hits = stuttering collapse into the wall, soft = a faltering bloom.
3. Ride the instrument volume / pick attack against that threshold while the Hizumitas smooths each gesture into the sustaining wall.
4. Print through the PORTA424 "Ruined Cassette Wall" — Master down, Input Trim maxed — so the doom climax bounces to blown-out tape.
5. To perform a "fall apart" mid-phrase, HOLD Clean's BYPASS footswitch to momentarily slam max Sag (per the Tube Sag gesture) — the source caves, the Hizumitas catches the collapse.
