# Dark Star Frozen Bed + Live Wall Swelling Up From Near-Silence  ⭐

The "Bring the Sun" begin-from-a-drone architecture — a barely-audible frozen bed, then the live wall swells up over it from near-silence into a crescendo. Two layers of sustain at once. Designed integration; Swans layer real instruments over minutes on a loud stage — the freeze + swell manufactures the static "many sustained voices" bed and the rise in the box.

## Signal path

Baritone JM (GK-5 → **VG-800 #5 VIO Bowed Swell Drone**) → **CB Clean #6 Baritone Drone Sustainer** + **Clean #12 Dynamic Volume Swell** (the near-silence build) → Hizumitas #13 (HZ-PP1, the live wall, kicked in over the bed) → (Middle board) → **Walrus QI** parallel → **OBNE Dark Star V3 #32 (DS-PP2) Swans Near-Silence Frozen Drone Bed** (Aux = Hold) → (End board) → Big Time bypassed → tape.

## Per-unit

- **VG-800 #5:** VIO removes the pick attack for an attackless drone.
- **Dark Star DS-PP2 (#32):** **Mix 1 (mostly wet) · Decay 12 · Multiply 12 · Filter 11–12 (slightly dark/warm) · Crush 11 (light bit-crush, "barely there") · Lag 1**, **Aux = Hold** to freeze a baritone/banjo chord into a static barely-audible bed.
- **Clean #6 / #12:** the AUX swell (#12) bows the *live* Hizumitas wall up from silence over it. Stereo, trails on (Dark Star is the Board-2 terminus, #28).

## Routing

Stereo from Dark Star onward. **GOTCHA (DS-PP2):** while Hold is engaged, new notes pass BONE-DRY — that's fine here, because the live wall (Hizumitas) is a separate dry-forward voice swelling in over the frozen bed; don't expect to re-trigger the frozen reverb. Build the bed FIRST (freeze), then swell the wall.

## Sound

A near-silent static drone bed under a slowly-rising saturated wall — the literal Swans crescendo architecture. Taste-ref: Swans "Bring the Sun" — drone-barely-audible → instruments stacked over minutes.

## Play

(1) Strum baritone, freeze it into the Dark Star bed (Aux Hold), barely audible. (2) Engage Clean's swell and bow the Hizumitas wall up from near-silence. (3) Repeat the chord, let it consume. (4) Collapse. This is a *performance arc*, not a static patch.
