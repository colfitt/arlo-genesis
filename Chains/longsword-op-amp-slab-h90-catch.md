# Longsword Op-Amp Slab → H90 Catch (the BC,NR climax)  ⭐

The late, sudden "kick the door in" distortion slab — a dynamics build that drops a Sabbath-class op-amp wall into a reverb that catches the wreckage. Tension/release, not set-and-forget. Designed integration; BC,NR's slab is an Op Amp Big Muff / Maxon, so the rig chases the scooped sustaining result, not the exact pedals.

## Signal path

Baritone JM (GK-5 → **VG-800 #8 FILTER BASS Doom Sub** or #7 −7 STEP) → CB Clean (Home Base, leveling) → **Hizumitas #14 (HZ-PP2) BC,NR Big-Muff Sabbath Slab** (off until the drop) → **Longsword #15 (LS-PP1) BC,NR Op-Amp Sabbath Slab** (Boost footswitch = the door-kick) → Big Time bypassed → **H90 #19 Glitch/Stutter Pre-Mangle → reverb** (Preset B catches it) → tape.

## Per-unit

- **Hizumitas HZ-PP2 (#14):** **Volume max · Sustain max · Tone ~2:00** (the scooped slab voice), parked OFF until the second-half climax.
- **Longsword LS-PP1 (#15):** **DIODE down (silicon squared brick) · Drive ~2–3:00 · Level ~2:00 (rebalance — silicon is loud) · Low ~1:00 · High ~10:00 · SHIFT down (300 Hz Sabbath body) · Boost footswitch ON as the climax stomp** (it is the rig's *most accurate* op-amp-distortion unit).
- **H90 #19:** Glitch/Stutter into a reverb Preset B = the "recorded-wrong" destruction catching the slab.

## Routing

Mono Board 1. The whole chain is a **dynamics arc**: verse = quiet (slab pedals off, just Clean + a dry VG bed), climax = both fuzz/distortion stomps kick in. Watch Longsword output (silicon is the loudest stage — rebalance Level). Pull Low toward noon if the bottom flubs.

## Sound

Restrained → sudden overwhelming scooped slab, then the reverb shreds the tail into glitch. Taste-ref: Black Country, New Road — "Basketball Shoes" / "Sunglasses" late distortion slab.

## Play

Hold the build with only Clean + a VG bed; at the drop, stomp Hizumitas HZ-PP2 then Longsword Boost together for the door-kick; release to collapse. A late, partial, sudden drop — half the rig amps up, not one blow-out.
