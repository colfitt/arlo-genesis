# Baritone Doom Bed, Banjo Lead Cuts Through

A subterranean baritone doom wall with a banjo lead carving over it — the two signature VG-800 voices stacked: baritone weight as the bed, banjo as the line that cuts. The core drone/doom-with-a-lead chain. ⭐ signature-fit.

This is a 🟣 designed integration of owned-gear patches; no artist ever played *this* chain. The Taste-ref names the sound it chases, and the per-unit patch sheets carry their own provenance.

## Signal path

- *Bed (baritone):* Baritone JM (GK-5) → **VG-800 (#7 Doom Baritone Wall, −7 STEP)** → Clean (#6 Baritone Drone Sustainer) → **MXR 108 → Hizumitas → Longsword** (sustained fuzz wall) → [Board 2] → **Dark Star V3 (#26 Octave-down doom pad)** → [Board 3] → **Big Time (#3 Loop Diffuser, Factory #9, FREEZE)** → tape.
- *Lead (banjo):* played live on the same rig via the **61SL #6 split keybed** OR alternated by GK profile — banjo line on **VG-800 (#3 GR-300 Thick-Synth Lead)**, through Clean (#5 Banjo Transient Tamer), riding *over* the frozen baritone bed into **H90 (#27 Harmonized Banjo Lead, Diatonic → Plate)**.

## Per-unit

- VG-800 **#7 Doom Baritone Wall** — `ALT TUNE -7 STEP` (down a 5th), dark amp, `NORMAL MIX ~30%` keeps the Jazzmaster body under the model; recall GK **#2 BARI JM**.
- Clean **#6 Baritone Drone Sustainer** — full-range Envelope Balance (catch the lows), limiting for a sustained ceiling, slow release into a wall. Cut lows on the EQ knob if they over-trigger.
- Fuzz wall — MXR 108 → Hizumitas (Tone 1–2:00 to tame brightness) → Longsword; the sustaining fuzz IS the doom bed's body.
- Dark Star V3 **#26 Octave-down doom pad** — Pitch 1/2 = −1 oct, **Filter LPF (dark)**, light Crush (recorded-wrong), Mix mostly wet. Pairs with the upstream fuzz wall — its home turf.
- Big Time **#3 Loop Diffuser (Factory #9)** — MODE Long, STATE Saturated/#!&%, low COLOR + high FEEDBACK = climbing wall; **hold RIGHT to FREEZE** the bed and solo over it. WET conservative.
- VG-800 **#3 GR-300** + Clean **#5** — the banjo lead voice that plays over the frozen doom bed.
- H90 **#27 Harmonized Banjo Lead** — Diatonic (monophonic): Learn the key, Pitch A = +3rd/+5th in scale, short Plate after. Feed the *cleaner* GR-300 banjo so the tracker locks.

## Routing

Baritone bed is mono front → fuzz (mono) → MISO/stereo into Board 2 → Dark Star stereo terminus → Big Time. **Gain-staging:** the fuzz wall already saturates, so back Big Time COLOR low and keep Dark Star Crush light. Freeze the Big Time bed (hold RIGHT), then the banjo lead rides on top. ⚠️ H90 Diatonic is **monophonic** — single-note banjo leads only; for chordal banjo use Polyphony (Percussive) instead. The **61SL #6 split-keybed** can run the baritone bed in the low octave and the banjo lead up top from one keybed if you're triggering VG models from keys.

## Sound

A crushing, octave-down baritone fuzz wall, frozen into an oceanic bed, with a thick GR-300 banjo lead — harmonized in key — cutting clean over the top. Doom underneath, melody above.

Taste-ref: post-punk-texture-dynamics (Swans crushing sustained crescendo; the drone wall that consumes) + the rig's baritone-weight / banjo-as-lead premise.

## Play

Build the baritone bed first — hold a chord through the fuzz, let the Big Time Loop Diffuser climb, **hold RIGHT to FREEZE**. Then switch to the banjo GK profile / lead voice and play the melody over the static wall. Use Clean's swell on the banjo for bowed-in lead notes. Panic-reset Big Time (hold MODE) if the freeze runs away.
