# Westberg Two-Voice Sustaining Wall (documented signal-order)  ⭐

Rebuild Norman Westberg's *documented* Swans wall architecture on owned gear — two stacked dirt voices + compression + a volume swell + repetition into a "vast sustaining chordal" wall. This is a designed integration honoring a documented signal-ORDER, not a claim that Westberg played this chain (Swans use no fuzz; the two-fuzz stack manufactures the loud-amp overtone result).

## Signal path

Baritone JM / banjo (GK-5 → VG-800, dark E.GUITAR model) → **CB Clean #6 Baritone Drone Sustainer** (the Dyna-Comp role + the **#12 Dynamic Volume Swell** for the build) → **MXR 108 #13 (108-PP1) Westberg Grinding Bite** → **Hizumitas #13 (HZ-PP1) Swans Sustaining Chordal Wall** → Big Time bypassed/clean → **H90 #6 Blackhole Freeze Drone** (lock-a-wall staff move) → tape.

## Per-unit

- **Clean #6 / #12:** tames the boomy lows full-range and extends decay into a sustained ceiling (Dynamics ~noon limiting, Release Slow, full-range Envelope Balance); the **AUX swell (#12)** is the documented volume-pedal "build" tool.
- **108-PP1 (#13):** OUTPUT 12:00 / **FUZZ 12–1:00 (mid-gain grind, not maxed)** / BUFFER ON — the DS-1 "aggressive grinding" role.
- **Hizumitas HZ-PP1 (#13):** **Volume ~1:00 · Sustain ~2:00 (just shy of max so a chord still reads AS a chord) · Tone ~1:00 (bass side, the "warm guitar/bass hum")**.
- **H90 #6:** Blackhole, **Freeze = Feedback fully clockwise** on a Performance-Parameter footswitch.

## Routing

Mono through Board 1; the swell is the live build gesture. No clock. The **order is the point** — this mirrors Westberg's cited chain (dirt → comp → volume → amp): here 108 grind + Hizumitas wall = the two dirt voices, Clean = the Dyna-Comp + the volume swell.

## Sound

A chord (not a single note) that blooms into a sustaining, choir-like wall — "four church choirs singing at once" rendered as 108-grind + Hizumitas-smooth + Clean-extend + Blackhole-freeze. Taste-ref: Swans "The Seer" / "Bring the Sun" — Westberg's sustaining chordal wall.

## Play

Pick a chord with "potential," engage Clean's AUX swell so it bows in, repeat the chord letting overtones stack, then stomp the H90 Blackhole Freeze to lock it and play over the top.
