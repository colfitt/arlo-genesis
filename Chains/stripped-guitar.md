# Stripped Guitar Board

Same artist DNA — Gabriel, Prince, Police, mk.gee — in a leaner rig. No Big Time, Clean, or MOOD. The Deco V2 and H90 do the heavy lifting: Deco covers tape chorus and slapback, H90 runs dual delay + reverb engines simultaneously.

## Signal Path

### 1. TC Electronic Polytune 3
Tuner + buffer.

### 2. JHS 424
The pedal version of mk.gee's Tascam 424 preamp trick. Simpler than the PORTA424 — one knob of lo-fi tape-style gain. Push it for committed grit up front.

### 3. Oxford Drive
British Marshall-voiced distortion. Covers Prince's dirtier rhythm crunch and Gabriel-era Peter Hammill-style aggression. The ShredMaster lineage gives it a focused midrange that cuts without fizz — roll the gain back for funky edge, push it for leads.

### 4. Strymon Deco V2
The Swiss army knife of this board. [Tape saturation side](guide:Strymon%20Deco%20V2/tape-saturation) adds warm compression (replaces Clean's role). [Doubletracker side](guide:Strymon%20Deco%20V2/doubletracker) does:
- **Chorus**: Wide, through-zero flanging — covers The Police territory alongside BF-3
- **Slapback**: Short tape echo for Prince rockabilly-funk moments
- **Flange**: Deep tape-flange sweeps
Stereo output from here forward.

### 5. Boss BF-3
Dedicated flanger for Andy Summers textures that Deco can't cover simultaneously. [Ultra mode](guide:Boss%20BF-3/ultra-mode) for dramatic jet sweeps, [Standard mode](guide:Boss%20BF-3/standard-mode) for subtle movement. Gate/Pan mode does rhythmic stereo effects — useful for Gabriel-style production tricks.

### 6. Eventide H90
Dual engines replace Big Time + reverb duties. Run [delay in Preset A + reverb in Preset B](guide:Eventide%20H90/dual-preset-routing) simultaneously:
- **Delay**: TimeFactor algorithms for 80s rack delay (Gabriel/Police era). TapeEcho for warm repeats, ModDelay for chorus-on-repeats.
- **Reverb**: Blackhole for massive Gabriel pads, [Plate](guide:Eventide%20H90/plate-reverb) for tighter Prince-style room. MangledVerb adds distorted reverb textures (mk.gee territory).
The H90's routing flexibility (series, parallel, dual) means you can run delay-into-reverb or keep them independent per song.

### 7. Strymon Iridium
Same role as Rig A — amp sim at the end. Round for clean, Chime for jangle.

## Routing Notes

- Mono through JHS 424 → Oxford Drive, then stereo from Deco V2 onward
- H90 is center-positive 12V power — do NOT daisy-chain, use its included adapter
- H90 + Deco V2 both have MIDI — two pedals, full preset recall
- Fewer pedals = simpler power, smaller board

## The Vibe

- **Prince funk**: JHS 424 light, Oxford Drive low gain, Deco slapback, H90 tight plate
- **Police shimmer**: Deco chorus + BF-3 flange stacked, H90 ModDelay
- **Gabriel pads**: Deco saturation warm, H90 Blackhole + long delay, expression pedal on decay
- **mk.gee raw**: JHS 424 pushed hard, Oxford Drive stacked, H90 MangledVerb
