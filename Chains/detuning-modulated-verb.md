# Detuning Modulated Verb — the "moves and detunes" wash ⭐

A modulated reverb that drifts and detunes under a sustained source — the queasy, warbling pad (Hammock's "more warble"), not a static hall. The modulated-detuning-verb brief. This is a 🟣 DOUG-designed integration; no artist used this exact pedal chain, the Taste-ref names the sound it chases.

## Signal path

Baritone JM (GK-5) → **VG-800** (VIO bowed swell) → **CB Clean** → [Board 1] → **Dark Star V3** (chorused-detune crushed wash) **and/or** [Board 3] → **Big Sky** (Detune Verb / Falling Shimmer) → tape.

## Per-unit

- **VG-800 — #5 VIO Bowed Swell Drone** (attackless, `NORMAL MIX OFF`) — a smooth bowed source is ideal for a detuning verb to grab; or **#11 Organ Drone** for a thicker pad.
- **CB Clean — #6 Baritone Drone Sustainer** (full-range envelope to tame the boom; slow release to extend into a bed).
- **Dark Star V3 — #27 Crushed ambient wash** (Pitch 1 detune up / Pitch 2 detune down = chorused width, Crush 9 heavy sample-rate, Filter 11) **or #7 Detune / chorus width** (a smidge off the center notch + Lag-wiggle for chorus). This is the on-Board-2 detuning verb.
- **Big Sky (End-board version) — #12 "Detune Verb"** (Shift1 −10 cen / Shift2 +10 cen, INPUT, MOD off = clean detune) **or #11 Falling Shimmer** (both voices −10%, REGEN — a continuously sinking, queasy wall) **or #2 Drone Wall (Cloud, freeze)** with **MOD 1:00** for slow movement.

## Routing

Stereo. Run the detune on **either** Dark Star (Board 2) **or** Big Sky (Board 3), not both — two detuned verbs beat against each other into seasickness (use that on purpose only if you want chaos). Keep MOD speed **slow** (Big Sky #1's "avoid fast/seasick" note).

## Sound

A reverb that visibly *moves* — pitches drifting ±10 cents, chorusing against themselves, the tail sinking or warbling. The "recorded-wrong" detune without bright shimmer.

## Play

Bow-swell the VIO source in (instrument volume or Clean's Dynamic Swell #12) → let the detuning verb bloom → ride Big Sky **MOD** or Dark Star **Pitch 2** by hand for live drift. For the Ful Stop bed specifically, layer **MOOD TB3** (frozen reverb + CLOCK-down drift) underneath.

**Taste-ref:** art-rock-studio-as-instrument — Radiohead "Ful Stop" unison-drift wash; Hammock's detuned-verb "more warble" philosophy (Big Sky #23).
