# Hizumitas Fuzz → Big Time — One-Note Oceanic Wall ⭐

The honest minimal centerpiece: hold ONE note on a sustaining fuzz and let Big Time's feedback + diffusion build an endless oceanic drone bed — the smallest possible signature chain. A single held note swells into a slowly-climbing, defined fuzz wall that never quite resolves; FREEZE turns it into an infinite pad you solo over.

🟣 DOUG-designed integration. No artist used this exact chain — the per-unit patch refs carry their own provenance, and the Taste-ref names the sound it chases.

## Signal path

Baritone JM / banjo (GK-5) → VG-800 → **Clean #7 Always-On Huge Fuzz Feeder** → EQD Hizumitas (mono) → **Generation Loss** → **Big Time IN-L (auto-MISO) #9 Hizumitas Fuzz Wall** → MOOD → Blooper → Chroma → **H90 #2 Blackhole Infinite Ambient Pad** → PORTA424 → tape.

The only "real artist signal-order" invoked is the general **fuzz-before-delay** standard — a documented pedal-order best-practice (EQD / JHS / Reverb signal-chain guides), not an artist-specific path.

## Per-unit

- **Clean #7 Always-On Huge Fuzz Feeder** — but back Dynamics DOWN out of limiting (toward 10:00) per the patch's own note, so the Hizumitas blooms with your attack instead of being slammed flat. Clean stays mono here (front of Board 1).
- **Big Time #9 Hizumitas Fuzz Wall** — MODE Long (not the patch's default Long/varies — use Long so FREEZE holds a non-degrading pad), STATE Compressed (holds the sustaining fuzz, stops runaway), VOICING Focus (shaves highs+lows → keeps the muff wall defined), COLOR LOW ~25–35% (the fuzz already saturates), TILT pushed UP (cut low mud), FEEDBACK ~60%, WET ~45%. SPREAD widen. The Hizumitas Filter knob pre-shapes scooped↔thick before the delay.
- **H90 #2 Blackhole Infinite Ambient Pad** — the downstream hall the wet feeds into for the doom wall.

## Routing

Mono fuzz → **IN-L auto-engages MISO** (mono-in / stereo-out) — the clean way to fan a mono fuzz into a stereo delay field. No clock needed (free-running drone). Favor **DRY KILL ON** (Big Time Options) if you want the wet-only bed to stay out of the dry print. Gain-staging: keep COLOR modest — too much COLOR + a hot fuzz makes the limiter clamp to porridge.

## Sound

A single held note swells into a slowly-climbing, defined fuzz wall that never quite resolves; FREEZE turns it into an infinite pad you solo over.

**Taste-ref:** post-punk/experimental (Swans crushing sustained crescendos); the drone/doom making-aesthetic at its most minimal.

## Play

Hold one note, let the wall build, then **hold Big Time's RIGHT footswitch to FREEZE** the bed (non-degrading infinite pad) and play melody/banjo over the held wall. To collapse, tap RIGHT to unfreeze or **hold MODE to panic-reset** to a clean delay.
