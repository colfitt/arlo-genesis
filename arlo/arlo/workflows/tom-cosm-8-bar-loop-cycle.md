# Workflow: Tom Cosm's 8-Bar Loop Cycle

## The premise

Work only with an 8-bar loop until it is undeniable. If the loop is not exciting at 8 bars, no amount of arrangement will save it at 8 minutes. Arrangement deploys a strong idea — it cannot rescue a weak one. Tom Cosm and Ill Gates both teach versions of this; it is the dominant discipline in modern electronic and bedroom-producer practice.

This workflow handles the **songwriting decision** of when to stay in the loop vs. break out. The DAW mechanics (Session view, scenes, follow actions) live in the general corpus at `corpus/daw/ableton/timeless/eight-bars-first-discipline.md`.

## When ARLO should offer this

Offer this when the user says any of:
- "I have a loop going" / "I have a beat going"
- "I'm working in Session view" / Ableton / FL clips
- "I can't tell if I should keep going or move to arrangement"
- "I keep adding fills and risers and it's not working"
- They are stuck on a track where the loop is the most developed thing and arrangement hasn't started

Do NOT offer this if the user is writing on an acoustic instrument with no DAW loop, or already has a verse/chorus structure sketched out.

## The steps

1. **Define the 8 bars.** Identify the loop. If you don't have an 8-bar boundary, set one now. Anything longer at this stage is hiding.
2. **Apply the undeniable test.** Loop it for two minutes. Would you keep listening if you found this on someone else's track?
3. **If not undeniable: fix the loop, not the arrangement.** Subtract a part, swap a sound, change a chord, change drum velocities. Stay in the 8 bars. Do not add a riser.
4. **Hear three variations before committing.** Duplicate the loop. Try a different lead sound, a different chord voicing, a different drum kit. Don't commit to the first version.
5. **Stay in writing mode.** If you start building a new patch for more than 10 minutes, you've slipped into prep. Stop. Use what's already in the project.
6. **Recognize the breakout signal.** The loop passes undeniable + you can hear the next section + you have a vocal/lead idea waiting. Or: two sessions on this loop without it getting better.
7. **Skeleton the arrangement.** Lay out blank clips for intro / verse / chorus / breakdown / drop / outro before filling content.
8. **Subtract to arrange.** Copy the loop to full length. Remove elements per section. The intro is the loop minus drums and chords. The chorus is the loop full. The breakdown is the loop minus the drums.

## ARLO prompts for each step (verbatim)

**Step 1 — define the 8 bars:**
> "What's the 8-bar boundary right now? If your loop is 16 bars, that's two ideas pretending to be one. Pick the 8."

**Step 2 — undeniable test:**
> "Loop it for two minutes. Honest: if this was on a stranger's track, would you keep listening? Or would you skip? Be brutal."

**Step 3 — fix the loop:**
> "It's not there yet. Don't add a riser. What can you *take away*? What's the laziest element? Swap one sound or remove one part. Loop again."

**Step 4 — three variations:**
> "Before you commit: duplicate the loop. Try a different lead patch. Then a different drum kit. Then a different chord voicing. Listen to all three. Which one are you reaching for?"

**Step 5 — stay in writing mode:**
> "You've been patch-tweaking for 10 minutes. That's prep, not writing. Save the patch idea for later and go back to the loop with what you have."

**Step 6 — recognize breakout:**
> "Three checks: (1) does the loop pass the undeniable test? (2) can you hear the next section, even faintly? (3) do you have a vocal or lead idea waiting? If yes to all three, break out. If no — stay or archive."

**Step 7 — skeleton the arrangement:**
> "Before you fill anything: lay out blank clips for intro, verse, chorus, breakdown, drop, outro. Set the lengths. Now you have a form to fill."

**Step 8 — subtract to arrange:**
> "Copy your loop to every section. Now subtract. Intro: minus drums, minus chords. Verse: minus the lead. Chorus: full. Breakdown: minus drums. The contrast comes from what's missing."

## When to abandon this workflow

- **The loop has been in iteration for 3+ sessions without improving.** It's not getting better; you're just changing it. Archive and seed a new loop.
- **You can't pass the undeniable test on any variation.** The seed isn't strong enough. Strip back to one element and start over, or pick a different seed entirely.
- **You have a vocal/lyric/melody that wants to drive the song.** This isn't your workflow right now. Switch to A11 (Spoon two-demos) or A12 (Joni tuning-first) or A1 (Cave letter method).
- **You've broken out into arrangement and are now stuck.** The arrangement freeze is its own stall — see B5.
