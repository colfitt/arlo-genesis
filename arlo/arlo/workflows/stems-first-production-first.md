# Workflow: Stems-First / Production-First

## The premise

The bedroom-producer school treats production choices as songwriting choices. You build the world first — chords, textures, drum loops, processed samples, atmospheres — and the song reveals itself inside the world. This is how Justin Vernon made *22, A Million*, how Frank Ocean made *Blonde*, how James Blake makes most of his work. The production is not service to the song; production *is* the song.

## When ARLO should offer this

Offer this workflow when the user signals any of:
- "I want to build a track" or "I'm building a beat" (not "I want to write a song")
- "I have a vibe but no song yet"
- "I'm working in [Ableton / Logic / FL / Bitwig] and have a loop going"
- They reference DAW-native production artists (Bon Iver post-2011, Blake, Ocean, Charli, Arca, Caroline Polachek, Yves Tumor)
- They say "the song will come" or "I want to find the song in this"
- Their ARLO.md lists production-first style or names sonic-led influences

Do NOT offer this if the user has a finished lyric in search of music, or is co-writing for someone else, or has a tight deadline.

## The steps

1. **Pick a world-seed.** One element — a sample, a synth patch, a drum loop, a recorded acoustic-room sound. The seed must produce both a *texture* and an *emotional register* within 10 minutes.
2. **Layer atmosphere.** Add pads, drones, reverbs, room. Get the *place* of the track sounding like a place before deciding what happens in the place.
3. **Anchor a rhythm.** Drums or a pulsing sub or a chord-stab pattern — something the body can lock to. This is the track's gravity.
4. **Improvise vocally into the world.** Record yourself singing nonsense, fragments, half-remembered phrases. Capture freely. No editing yet.
5. **Identify the 30-second song.** Listen back. Find the strongest 30 seconds — vocal, melodic, or otherwise. That clip is the song's center.
6. **Build outward from the center.** What leads in? What follows? Now you're arranging. The song may not be verse-chorus — it may be passages and re-entries.
7. **Edit production to serve the song.** Pull elements that fight the vocal; push elements that lift the chorus or center moment. This is where production becomes service.
8. **Check for lock-in.** Stop and ask: is there a song here, or just a finished production? If the latter, bounce the track to audio, mute it, and write against silence.

## ARLO prompts for each step (verbatim)

**Step 1 — world-seed:**
> "What's the first sound? Give me one element — sample, patch, loop — and tell me what *temperature* it has. Cold, warm, glassy, smoky?"

**Step 2 — atmosphere:**
> "Close your eyes and listen to the seed. What room is this in? What's the weather? Now build that. Don't worry about a song yet."

**Step 3 — rhythm anchor:**
> "What's the body of this track doing — swaying, marching, floating, lurching? Build the pulse that does that. Doesn't have to be drums."

**Step 4 — vocal improv:**
> "Hit record. Sing into the world for 15 minutes. Nonsense syllables are fine. One image, one feeling, one person you're addressing. Don't edit."

**Step 5 — find the 30-second song:**
> "Listen back. Where did your voice surprise you? Mark the strongest 30 seconds. That's the center of the song. Everything else exists to lead to or away from it."

**Step 6 — build outward:**
> "What needs to happen before the center? What needs to happen after? Sketch the order. The shape doesn't have to be verse-chorus."

**Step 7 — production as service:**
> "Solo the vocal. What's fighting it? Pull that. What's lifting it? Push that. Production is now in service."

**Step 8 — lock-in check:**
> "Honest question: is there a song here, or a finished production? If you couldn't play this on a piano in any form, the song hasn't arrived yet. Want to bounce-and-mute and try the song against silence?"

## When to abandon this workflow

Abandon and switch when:
- You have a fully arranged track and no song after 6+ weeks. The world has locked you out. Switch to **A6 break-the-demo** or write against silence.
- The vocal improv pass yields nothing usable after 3 attempts. The world is too defined; the song can't enter. Strip back.
- You're tweaking the production instead of finding the song. That's procrastination; switch to **B4 the-demo-trap** recovery.
- The song wants to be acoustic-portable. If it works on a guitar and voice, it doesn't need this workflow. Switch to **A11 spoon-two-demos-a-week** or **A12 joni-mitchell-alternate-tuning-first**.
