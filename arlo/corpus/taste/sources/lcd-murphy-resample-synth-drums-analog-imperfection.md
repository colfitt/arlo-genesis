# LCD / James Murphy — resample-the-synth-for-drums + analog-imperfection method

**The signature DFA move (the one that matters for the rig):** Murphy **synthesizes drum hits on a polysynth, then resamples them** to build the rhythm track. The example everyone cites: **"Get Innocuous!"** — the kick/snare/percussion were **built from the Yamaha CS-60** (an 8-voice poly) and **sampled back in**, then looped. So the LCD groove is often **one synth's voices, resampled into a kit**, not a stock drum-machine kit.

**Drum machines / drum sources used:** Roland **TR-808** (heavily), **TR-909**, **CR-78**; **Simmons SDS-V** (the "thunky" e-drums — the *Losing My Edge* drums); Gretsch acoustic kit for live-feel layers. Behringer RD-808 clone for retracking.

**Synths:** Yamaha **CS-60**, Roland **SH-101** (live/dance — "sweet to noxious in 3 seconds," organic sequencer), Roland **Juno-60**, **EML Electrocomp 100**, **EMS VCS3**, Sequential **Prophet-600** ("gliding leads and pads to warm pulsating drones"), Moog Voyager/Minimoog ("on nearly everything"). Tyler Pope's ARP Odyssey + CR-78 for the "one machine + one synth" EP.

**Philosophy (verbatim spirit):** Murphy wants **"analog and imperfect over synthetic and streamlined."** Tyler Pope: "I prefer the **imperfection of using analogue gear** — the whole signal chain is different." Vintage gear is only a *small component* — the bigger thing is the **method**: build a tight palette, resample, loop, layer additively, leave the human looseness in.

**Compression:** Murphy's studio uses a **DBX 162SL** stereo bus compressor for glue/character (channel/bus compression, not the EDM sidechain pump — LCD's pump is gentler/groovier than peak French house).

**Rig translation:** the resample-the-synth-for-drums method maps **directly** onto the AC50/Digitakt/Octatrack — synthesize a kick/snare/cowbell from the **VG-800 SYNTH** or **OP-1**, resample it into the sampler, tune to key, loop, and **add layers across minutes** (the "All My Friends" additive build). Keep the analog imperfection by printing through the rig's degrade boards (Gen Loss / PORTA424). Cowbell + 4-on-the-floor + analog drum machine = the backbone.
