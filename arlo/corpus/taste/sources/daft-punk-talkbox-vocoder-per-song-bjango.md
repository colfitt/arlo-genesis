# Daft Punk vocal effects — talkbox vs vocoder vs harmonizer (per song)

Daft Punk's 2001 Remix quote: "every one of our vocal tracks uses a different vocoder effect. We have the old Roland one [an **SVC-350**], **Auto-Tune**, and a **DigiTech Vocalist**." So there is NO single Daft Punk vocoder — three families, used differently per song:

**TALKBOX** (a physical tube into the mouth, modulating a synth — the "robot mouth" sound):
- **"Around the World"** = talkbox (likely Heil or Rocktron Banshee) on a **Roland Juno-106** source. This is THE iconic vocal of the cluster's anchor track.
- "Fragments of Time" = Keeley Framptone talkbox.

**VOCODER** (carrier synth + voice modulator, banks of bandpass filters):
- "WDPK 83.7 FM" / "Emotion" = Roland **SVC-350** vocoder.
- "Teachers"/"Oh Yeah"/"Technologic" = Ensoniq **DP/4+**.
- "Harder Better Faster Stronger" / "Robot Rock" / "Technologic"-era / "Human After All" / "Get Lucky" = DigiTech **Talker** (NuVo program). The Talker is a *talkbox-emulating pedal* (no tube) — explains the HBFS chopped-robot vocal.
- RAM-era ("Doin' It Right," "Touch," "Within") = Sennheiser **VSM201** vocoder.

**HARMONIZER / AUTO-TUNE** (pitch-shifted choir / robotic glide):
- "One More Time" = **Auto-Tune** + Mu-Tron Phasor (the over-Auto-Tuned soul vocal).
- "Digital Love" / "Something About Us" = DigiTech **Vocalist** (MIDI-controlled harmony).

**Rig honesty (INDEX-ONLY):** the user's rig has **no vocoder and no talkbox**. The closest approximations are (a) feed a carrier (VG-800 SYNTH saw/GR-300) and modulate its filter rhythmically from a sequenced CC (61SL/Digitakt) — an *envelope/filter* fake, not true vocoder; (b) the VG-800 ALT-TUNE HARMONY / 12STR PITCH blocks for the harmonizer "choir" flavor; (c) granular formant-ish movement on Microcosm/Chroma (not in this cluster's target gear). All vocoder/talkbox patches must be flagged as approximate.
