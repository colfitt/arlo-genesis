# Filter / French house — ingredients & techniques (Daft Punk / Stardust / Modjo lineage)

**Core ingredient:** a **sampled loop (2–4 bars)** from a disco or funk record, repeated through the whole track. Filter-house tracks are "often not much more than one disco loop with drums added on top" — the story is told by **manipulating those few elements**.

**Variation method:** **filtering** (lowpass/highpass cutoff automation) plus related effects — **phasing, flanging** — and **cutting up the loop** (repeating different slices) for variation. The filter opening up over a build is what makes it "hit harder."

**Drums:** "dirty 909-type drums… with plenty of shuffle and swing." Four-on-the-floor kick.

**Sidechain/pump:** classic house technique = the **kick triggers sidechain compression**; applied heavily to the sampled loop it creates the **pumping** that breathes with the beat.

**Vintage-sampler grit:** the signature lo-fi crunch = a vinyl loop recorded into an **SP-1200 at 45 rpm then pitched down** in the sampler → "juicy artifacts." Approximate with **bitcrushers / vintage-sampler emulations**.

**Philosophy:** "lean into minimalism," "embrace imperfections" — keep saturation/distortion rather than EQ-cleaning. (This is exactly the rig's degrade/recorded-wrong aesthetic applied to a groove.)

**Rig translation:** Octatrack/Digitakt loop with a slow LFO or scene-morphed **filter cutoff sweep**; bit-reduction for the SP-1200 grit; the kick-pump faked via an LFO→AMP square/ramp on the loop track or a Transient/comp on the MPC.
