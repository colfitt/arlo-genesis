## Distilled

- **Layered/doubled arpeggios:** Jonny played "cascading arpeggios on his Telecaster through a
  **Fender Deluxe**, **doubled them an octave higher**, then Ed added a **third layer on
  Jazzmaster**." Three stacked arpeggio layers = the jangle wall. (Note: the SongProfile lists a
  '75 Starcaster → AC30 for Jonny — sources conflict on the exact guitar/amp; the *layering
  method* is the consistent, load-bearing fact.)
- **Phil Spector wall of sound:** the guitars were "inspired by Phil Spector's wall of sound,"
  designed to "build like a tidal wave" / "crushing emotional weight." Ed O'Brien's own framing.
- **5/4 against 4/4 (Steve Reich phasing):** Jonny's arpeggio runs a **5-note pattern against
  the 4/4** — "two clocks that refuse to agree," a phasing mismatch (Reich-inspired) that gives
  the song its floaty, groundless momentum.
- **The room:** recorded at **St Catherine's Court**, candle-lit 16th-c. mansion, **in the
  ballroom at 3am because the natural reverb was almost supernatural** — the room IS a reverb.
- **Extra textures:** "a xylophone out of the attic"; Rhodes electric piano + Mellotron layers
  (per SongProfile); **outro = a dying ZX Spectrum that "chirps like a broken modem"** —
  random in-key bleeps, plus the sound of the program saving.

## Translation note (the centerpiece of this wave)
The "Let Down" cascading-arpeggio jangle-delay is the marquee target:
- **Big Time** = the multi-tap cascade engine. Bright, clean-ish repeats (HiFi/Focus, modest
  COLOR), a dotted/16th subdivision, and SPREAD ping-pong = the doubled/tripled arpeggio
  shimmer from one banjo/guitar line. The 5/4-vs-4/4 phasing can be faked by a delay
  subdivision that DOESN'T line up with the song's 4/4 (e.g. dotted-8th or a 5-against-4 tap).
- **Blooper** = literal layering — record the arpeggio, overdub an octave-up copy (Pitcher /
  Stepped Speed octave-up) for the "doubled an octave higher" move, then a third pass.
- **The ballroom reverb** = Big Sky/H90 hall with long pre-delay (index-only gear).
- **ZX Spectrum outro bleeps** = Blooper Scrambler random / MOOD micro-loop glitch.
