# Bon Iver's intimate close-mic + layered-falsetto vocal sound

Distilled concrete info:

- **For Emma, Forever Ago** (the foundational intimacy aesthetic): recorded alone in a cabin with
  two guitars, a Mac + Pro Tools LE + Mbox, and **a single Shure SM57** — used (unconventionally)
  for BOTH guitar and vocals.
- The intimacy came from **mic placement + stacking layer upon layer of falsetto** to compensate
  for the SM57's limits + improvised percussion = the "intimate, rough-hewn" sound. The constraint
  IS the character.
- Later albums (incl. *22, A Million*): Vernon moved to the **Shure SM7** ("super familiar with
  it"); uses **different SM7s and/or different mic distances** for tonal-colour variation between
  layers.
- The whole approach = **close, dry-forward voice; the room/space added later**, the opposite of a
  big-hall capture. Fragile falsetto stacked into a self-choir.

Rig takeaway: the intimacy = dry, close, present source THEN halo/space around it (never a wash
swallowing the voice). In the rig: keep CB Clean + a present dry banjo/VG-800 path, run the QI in
**Parallel** (live playing unaffected), put the cloud/space around it, not over it. The "layered
falsetto self-choir" = the same impulse the Prismizer/QI freeze-pad fakes.
