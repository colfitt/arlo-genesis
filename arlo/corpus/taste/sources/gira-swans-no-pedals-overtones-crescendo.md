# How Swans / Michael Gira actually get the crescendo-wall sound

CONCRETE GEAR (the headline honesty fact):
- **No pedals or effects.** Gira uses "only volume and sustain." The wall is amp + repetition + layered acoustic sources + overtones — NOT a fuzz/reverb pedal chain.
- Electric: Gibson Lucille ES-355, controls gaffer-taped except volume (one unified sound).
- Acoustic: beat-up Guild True American (full of holes, "wolf notes"), amplified via a Fishman pickup; thumbed with the thumb it sits "somewhere between a bass and a guitar."
- Amps (2010–2018): Orange Thunderverb → 4x12 Mesa/Boogie cab, CHAINED with a Gallien-Krueger 1000 bass head + another Mesa 4x12 (i.e. a bass rig carrying the low weight). For *The Beggar*: GK 1000 bass head + Mesa 4x12 with feedback buster.

HOW THE WALL IS BUILT (method, not a knob):
- Find chord voicings with "potential," play them REPEATEDLY, listen into the soundhole for the **ghost notes / overtones** — "letting myself get carried away by the ghost notes I hear… these are overtones." The overtones seed the arrangement.
- For long pieces: LAYER sustained voices UNDERNEATH the chords (keyboards, vibes, piano, horns, human vocals) → shifting textures over a held progression.
- Philosophy: "letting the music play you rather than the reverse"; "My guitar playing in itself is crude and extremely simple, but whole sonic worlds grow out of it."

RIG TRANSLATION TAKEAWAY: every Swans patch on the user's pedalboard is **🟣 designed-to-emulate the RESULT** (sustained overtone wall, bass-weighted low end, slow repetition into crescendo). Gira's actual method is sustain+volume+layering — the rig chases the sound, not the technique. Lean on the Hizumitas/Longsword sustain + baritone (VG-800) low weight + freeze/Multiply for the "many sustained voices" layering. The drone-from-near-silence build is a DYNAMICS performance, not a setting.
