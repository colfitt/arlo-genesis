## Distilled (HOW the jangle is made)

- **The core is a CLEAN amp + pedals, not amp gain.** Documented direction: **Fender Twin Reverb**
  set clean with headroom — Guitar-Chalk reverse-engineered settings: **Volume 5** (clean, slight
  pick-dynamic breakup), **Treble 7** (bright/biting so single-note riffs cut), **Middle 5**,
  **Bass 4** (tight, minimize boom), **Reverb 6** (moderate ambient space), vibrato OFF. The tone
  philosophy: "crisp, articulate single-coil attack with dynamic picking; clean platform with
  slight breakup, pedals do the dirt."
- **Guitars = bright single-coil / P-90.** **Fender Telecaster** (main for years — twangy, biting
  highs), **Fender Mustang/Jazzmaster** (early/offset), **Koll** and **custom Wicks** guitars
  (Wicks w/ P-90s = the "Float On" lead). Bridge / middle single-coil selected for the brittle
  jangle. The brightness + attack of single coils into a clean Twin IS the jangle.
- **Pedals (verified):** Pro Co **RAT** (gritty OD for heavier parts), **Line 6 DL4** (looping +
  experimental delay), **EHX POG** (octave/organ drones), **EarthQuaker Avalanche Run** (delay),
  **EarthQuaker Levitation** (reverb), **DigiTech Whammy** (pitch), **Boomerang chorus/delay**,
  **Seymour Duncan Shape Shifter STEREO TREMOLO** (a Brock-named "must-have" — the stereo-tremolo
  trippiness is central to his recent jangle), **LEM (Mastro Valvola)** stereo tremolo.
- **The jangle recipe (from "Float On"):** very clean amp, HIGH volume for headroom, EQ leaning
  mid/high (Bass 4 / Mid 6 / Treble 7); **delay = ONE clear repeat, feedback 0, mix ~30–40%**;
  the lead is **double-tracked → natural thick chorus**; modulation (tremolo/chorus) used
  SPARINGLY; moderate reverb for space without washing out clarity. Bright, bouncy, articulate.
- **Modulation, not chorus-soup.** Brock's signature movement is increasingly **stereo TREMOLO**
  ("the trippiness of stereo") rather than lush chorus — choppy/throbbing amplitude movement on a
  clean jangle. Double-tracking gives the chorusy thickness; tremolo gives the wobble.
- **Anti-precious recording.** Guitar World (Golden Casket): "I quit trying to find my favorite
  guitar… all the precious pairing of instruments and amps is just time spent not writing." He
  uses whatever's nearest at Dave Sardy's studio. The lo-fi-prolific ethos = capture > perfect.

## Translation note
- The clean jangle is the ONE place the cluster resists the rig's degrade tie-breaker — but the
  tie-breaker still applies: render it as a *brittle, slightly-broken* clean (lo-fi jangle), not a
  pristine one. On the rig: a low-gain edge-of-breakup drive (Kilt Crunch, Oxford low-gain, 424
  Gain-1-low) kept BRIGHT + DRY, banjo-as-lead carrying the jangly single-note line, then the
  **Deco V2 doubletracker** for the double-tracked "natural thick chorus" + tape wobble standing in
  for the stereo tremolo. The brittle top can be made "recorded-wrong" through PORTA424/424 print.
- The cluster has no true stereo-tremolo box in the target list — Deco's wobble + Wide-Stereo
  doubler is the closest *approximation* (flag it).
