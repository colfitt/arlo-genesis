# Thom Yorke's looping on "Daydreaming" — reverse + half-speed

URL: https://thekingofgear.com/post/173197714210/thoms-looping-on-daydreaming
thekingofgear.com · Robert (King of Gear) · ~2018

## Distilled

- Looper: **Electro-Harmonix 45000 4-track Looping Recorder** (Thom's favorite since 2013;
  push-buttons easier than footswitches, 4 independent tracks so he records Jonny's piano,
  Phil's glockenspiel, and his own vocals separately).
- **The reverse trick (glockenspiel):** Phil plays sparse high glockenspiel notes that ring
  out; Thom samples a few, then **reverses them**, so "each note which rang out now gently
  fades in." (Swell-in-from-nothing = the signature reverse-bloom.)
- **The half-speed finale (vocals):** Thom overdubs dense vocal layers into a loop, lets it
  run, then "switches the loop to playback at half speed, **lowering it down an octave** to
  create the very distinctive grinding vocal."
- Tempo kept by a phone metronome app; he conducts with the right hand, works the looper
  with the left.

## Translation note
Two distinct moves, both reproducible on the rig:
1. **Reverse-bloom of a ringing note** → capture a banjo/glock-like pluck, play it reversed so
   it fades IN. MOOD MkII Slip (reverse) or a Blooper reversed-loop layer.
2. **Half-speed octave-down loop** → Blooper **Stepped Speed (B4) octave-down**, or MOOD
   Tape MODIFY at .5× (octave down). The "grinding" quality = the downsample artifact of
   pitching a loop down — lean into CLASSIC / low CLOCK on MOOD for the grind.
