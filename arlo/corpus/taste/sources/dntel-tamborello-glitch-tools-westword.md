# Dntel / Jimmy Tamborello — glitch tools & workflow

**Hardware:** **Roland D50** (with sequencer, from his father's home studio); **MicroKorg** ("really convenient and easy to edit the sounds," used live more than on records).

**Software/DAWs over time:** **Vision** sequencer (earliest; "totally gone now") → **Cubase** (long stretch — this is the *Give Up* era) → **Logic Pro** (switched because "Logic could take advantage of the computer a little bit better") → **Ableton Live** occasionally, "mostly live."

**Glitch aesthetic confirmed:** "**every song I made had some sort of distortion to it or something broken-sounding in it.**" Influences: Autechre, Oval (Warp Records).

**Constraint that shaped the sound:** before upgrading his computer he **couldn't record audio — only sequence MIDI**, so early work is all sequenced/programmed (no live audio until 2001's *Life Is Full of Possibilities*). That's why the beats are tightly programmed beeps/clicks rather than chopped audio.

**Rig translation:** the "something broken-sounding in every track" ethos = the rig's degrade lean applied to a groove; tightly-sequenced MIDI-style beeps → resample a synth blip into the Digitakt/MPC and sequence it cleanly, then add the broken element (bit-crush, conditional glitch retrigs).
