# Daft Punk "Da Funk" lead — patch character & portamento

**Sound:** a **distorted, squelchy, band-passed lead** that reads like an overdriven guitar; originally an **MS-20** (distorted filter envelope into the **high-pass filter**), or a Juno-106/MKS-80 into an Ibanez Fat Cat distortion. Melody moves in **perfect fourths**.

**Patch elements (Moog Grandmother recreation):** sawtooth + **hard sync** + a mixer-feedback trick for the grit; **GLIDE/portamento ~10 o'clock** (the slide between notes is signature); VCA full. Filter env-driven "wah" attack; high-pass character.

**Why it matters:** the lead is a **mono, portamento-glided, filter-envelope synth line with distortion** — exactly the kind of voice the VG-800 SYNTH (GR-300 / SOLO / FILTER BASS) plays, and the banjo-as-lead can carry it (banjo pluck → VG synth model → glide → fuzz wall).

**Rig translation:** VG-800 SYNTH SOLO or GR-300 with PITCH-glide/portamento, filter-env attack, into the rig's distortion = a banjo-driven Da Funk lead. Or OP-1 mono synth with portamento + grid/punch FX.
