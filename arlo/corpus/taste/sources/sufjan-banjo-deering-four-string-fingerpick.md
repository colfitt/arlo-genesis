# Sufjan Stevens' banjo: instrument, tuning, technique

Distilled concrete info:

- Instruments: **Deering Goodtime** (open-back) and possibly a **Gold Tone CC openback**.
- **Plays the banjo like a four-string guitar** — a modified guitar style transposed to banjo;
  **both strums and fingerpicks** (mostly fingerpicked, rolling arpeggios). Does NOT play
  Scruggs/clawhammer bluegrass style — it's a folk fingerpicked voice.
- On *Seven Swans* the banjo is the lead instrument across lush, bare acoustic compositions;
  pared-down (Daniel Smith production) for lucidity.
- On *Carrie & Lowell*, **banjo enters under "Should Have Known Better"** ("banjo and very light
  keys… a high, gentle ostinato"). NOTE: the album opener **"Death with Dignity" is fingerpicked
  GUITAR, not banjo** — keep the attribution honest (the cluster brief listed it under banjo).
- Overall C&L = "circular, fingerpicked chord progressions," whisper vocals + fingerpicked
  guitar/banjo, finger noise and fragility left in.

Rig takeaway: the banjo-as-lead mandate is exactly Sufjan's voice — fingerpicked rolling arpeggios
as the melodic lead, not a novelty. VG-800 EBM5 BANJO profile + fingerpicked rolls; tame the 2–4 kHz
ice-pick with LPF/Tone before any granular/halo stage. "Banjo as a 4-string guitar" = the 5th-string
exclusion strategy already baked into the VG-800 sheet.
