## Distilled

- **Idioteque:** built from a drum-machine pattern Jonny made with a **modular synth**; he felt
  it "needed chaos" and brought in found sounds + sampling.
- **Everything in Its Right Place:** **Ed cut up Thom's Rhodes in real time** and created
  effects on the fly; the album opens with a **sampled Yorke vocal snippet** laid across pulsing
  keys + ambient drum loops. Digitally manipulated vocals, odd time signatures.
- **Kid A method overall:** modular synths, sampling, loops. Aphex Twin / Autechre / Boards of
  Canada as *structural* influences: "granular breaks, cut-up phrasing, rhythmic artifacts left
  uncorrected. Loops are mistreated, stretched, pulled out of phase. **Everything breathes wrong
  on purpose.**"

## Translation note
This line — "loops mistreated, stretched, pulled out of phase… breathes wrong on purpose" — IS
the user's making aesthetic verbatim. Direct rig maps:
- **Cut-up Rhodes / vocal snippet in real time** → MOOD Slip (real-time re-pitch/reverse on a
  micro-loop) + Blooper Scrambler (cut a loop into a grid pattern).
- **Loops stretched / out of phase** → MOOD Stretch (Frozen Grain Wall) + Blooper Stability
  (tape-age wobble = "out of phase / breathes wrong").
- **Granular breaks / rhythmic artifacts** → Blooper Dropper (granular flutter) + Stepped
  Trimmer glitchprint; Big Time CLUSTER + 0.5X crush.
