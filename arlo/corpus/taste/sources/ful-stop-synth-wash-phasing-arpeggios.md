## Distilled (the two halves of Ful Stop)

- **The 2nd-half synth wash** = **multiple synths playing single notes in UNISON**, each voiced
  differently — NOT one patch. Documented stack: **Moog Minimoog Voyager PE** (lead unison
  voice), **Sequential Prophet 5** (MIDI-triggered from the Voyager by Jonny), **Roland Juno-60**
  layer, **DSI Prophet '08** (Thom's hands, shaping params live during the take). Two synths each
  into a channel of a Radial JDI Duplex.
- The defining quality is the **slight pitch + envelope drift between the unison voices** — that
  drift is the wash. Recreate with Prophet-V / Mini V soft synths, layered + chorus/reverb for
  width (Reverb News).
- **The phasing arpeggios** (Jonny singled out Ful Stop for its "good phasing arpeggios," 2018
  Guardian) = **EHX Small Stone V4** on a Telecaster Plus, into **Boss RE-20 Space Echo** + a
  digital reverb, clean Vox AC30. The arpeggios sit in the back half UNDER the synth wash.
- First half = tight dark **motorik** bass+drums (P-bass → SansAmp BDDI → Ampeg SVT), sparse.

## Translation note
- **Synth wash** → VG-800 / OP-1 detuned-unison pad (two voices slightly apart), or layer the
  banjo-via-VG over a sustained sample. The "single notes in unison + drift" is the target.
- **Phasing arpeggios** → no phaser in the rig: the H90 phaser block (index-only) is closest;
  the FAMILY of swirling movement can come from MOOD CLASSIC trails + Tape wobble, or Caroline
  Somersault. Feed the arpeggio into **Big Time** short+dotted (Space Echo voicing) for the RE-20
  role, low WET into a reverb for the ambient bed.
- **Motorik first half** → Digitakt/MPC pulse → Big Time clock-locked Lo-Fi groove (16ths,
  flat velocity), tape glue downstream.
