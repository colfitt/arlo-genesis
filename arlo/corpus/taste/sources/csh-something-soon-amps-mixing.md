## Distilled (HOW the layered crunch + "Something Soon" tone was made)

- **Amps (verified roster):** **Vox AC15** and **Fender '65 Twin Reverb** are the documented CSH amps,
  alongside the Teens-of-Denial-era **vintage Supro**. AC15 = warm chimey grind; Twin = the clean/reverb
  platform. The crunchy overdriven tones are the **Boss DS-2 Turbo Distortion → amp**, layered.
- **"Something Soon" specifics (Teens of Style, 2015 remake of the 2011 bedroom song):** everything was
  **recorded and produced by Will Toledo himself**, with pro mixer **Abe Seiferth brought in only to mix
  the DRUMS and BASS** — the guitars/vocals stayed Toledo's bedroom-DAW work. The track is the cluster's
  template: dense layered crunchy guitars + blown-out push, a *bedroom record cleaned up just enough to
  release*, not a studio tracking date. The original 2011 "Something Soon" (My Back Is Killing Me Baby)
  is the rawer laptop version; the 2015 re-record keeps the spirit while tightening the low end.
- **The signature move stays constant:** Telecaster (bright/biting) → **Boss DS-2** (cheap, mid-spiky,
  "keeps breaking") → amp, recorded relatively QUIETLY and **boosted in the mix** so the clipping shows.
  Result = "crunchy and dry." Density = many DS-2/Tele direct-or-amped passes stacked into a wall.
- **Reverse-engineered amp settings (Guitar Chalk, directional only):** clean-ish Twin with bright
  treble, moderate mids, tight bass, light-to-moderate reverb; the dirt comes from the DS-2, not amp gain.

## Translation note (for THIS rig)
- The user's job is the inverse of Toledo's: pristine VG-800/banjo source that must be MADE crunchy-dry.
  The DS-2 "keeps breaking" character = a spitty, slightly-gated, mid-forward crunch kept DRY (no verb) →
  best matched by **JHS 424** gated grit, **Kilt 10** Distortion/Forbidden, **Longsword** Stormbringer
  (small-crap-amp grind), or **Colour Box** broken-console preamp pushing a quiet source into clip.
- "Recorded quiet, boosted in the mix" = a gain stage that clips on a low-level source then gets level-
  restored — literally the Colour Box STEP-high/PRE-VOL-up move, or any drive's Volume clawed back up.
- "Layer to a wall" = the Deco V2 doubletracker + stacked passes; the AC15/Twin pairing is just the
  clean platform the dirt sits on (the rig's CB Clean + a clean VG-800 model fills that role).
