# Daft Punk *Homework* — synths, samplers, filter-house mechanics

**Hardware roster (Homework, recorded at Daft House, Paris):**
- Synths: Roland **Juno-106**, Roland **MKS-80**, Roland **TB-303**, Sequential **Prophet-VS**.
- Drum machines: **LinnDrum**, Roland **TR-707 / TR-808 / TR-909**.
- Samplers: **E-mu SP1200**, **Ensoniq ASR-10**, Roland **S-760**.

**"Da Funk" lead** = a band-passed synth (likely Juno-106 or MKS-80) run through **distortion** (Ibanez Fat Cat pedal) so it reads like an overdriven guitar. Melody moves in **perfect fourths**. Original lead reportedly an **MS-20** (distorted filter envelope into the high-pass filter). Moog-recreation tip: **GLIDE/portamento ~10 o'clock**, VCA full — the glide between notes is part of the voice.

**"Da Funk" 303 bassline** = a real **TB-303** acid pattern Bangalter had already programmed in 1993; they auditioned old random patterns and picked one that fit. Sequence spans ~F1–F4 (transposed from the 303's native C range via the tuning knob).

**"Around the World" bass** (recreation values): **sawtooth**, filter cutoff **~2.7**, resonance **high but not screeching (~6.89 in TAL U-NO-LX)**, **env-mod ~2**, ADSR = **attack 1, decay 6, no sustain** → a "subtle pluck." Combines TB-303 acid with a fuller Jupiter/Juno round bass for the wide chorus-like movement.

**"Around the World" bouncy lead:** **square wave**, filter **~3**, resonance **~6**, env-mod **~halfway**, **attack ~2** → a "funky wah-wah."

**Effects:** tempo-synced stereo delay — **1/8 left, dotted-1/8 right**, ~25% mix, ~40% feedback.

**Sampled elements:** "Da Funk" brass stab from Zero-G Datafile Three; drum breaks sampled from Vaughn Mason "Bounce, Rock, Skate, Roll" and Barry White.

**Why it matters for the rig:** the filter-house move = a short sampled/looped phrase whose **timbre** is varied (filter sweeps, resonance, sampler grit), not its notes. The 303/Juno acid-pluck bass and the portamento lead map directly onto the VG-800 synth engine and the OP-1/Digitakt.
