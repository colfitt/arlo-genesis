## Distilled

### Ondes Martenot (Jonny's signature lead voice)
- Featured on **"The National Anthem"** and **"How to Disappear Completely"** (Kid A), "Where I
  End and You Begin," etc. On "Motion Picture Soundtrack" a "synthy choir" = **Mellotron +
  ondes Martenot**.
- Playing feel: a **ring controls pitch with a ribbon for seamless glides**; an **intensity key
  responds to finger pressure like a violin bow**, shaping attack/decay in real time. Greenwood:
  "I've always wanted to play an instrument that was like singing, and there's nothing closer."
- Sound in tracks: "swooping between ghostly strings," "howling in parallel" with guitar
  feedback. A vocal/violin-like, gliding, swelling MONOPHONIC lead — slow portamento, expressive
  swell, no fixed pitch grid.

### Polysynth roster (the wash/pad sound)
- **Prophet-series (Sequential/DSI) dominate** — more tracks than any other brand. Prophet 5 =
  "Everything in Its Right Place" + heavy on AMSP & In Rainbows; Prophet 08 = AMSP. Moog Model D
  for bass ("Lotus Flower"). Juno-60, Voyager PE on AMSP.
- KoG's tonal point: "the most significant factor in a subtractive synth's tone is the tone of
  its **filter**" — Curtis-chip Prophet filter = the signature. (Specific detune/unison/mod
  depths not documented at this source.)
- Suggested affordable mono for Radiohead leads: **Korg MS-20 mini** ("traditional synth bass
  and lead… as well as experimental effects").

## Translation note
- **Ondes-style lead → the banjo-as-lead voice via GK-5 → VG-800**, or OP-1, with: slow
  portamento/glide, a swell on attack (CB Clean Dynamic Swell), and vibrato. On the time boards
  the move is a clean-ish sustaining delay (Big Time) under a gliding single-note line.
- **Prophet/Juno wash (Ful Stop)** → VG-800 saw/pad models or OP-1 detuned-unison, layered.
  The "unison-detune drift" is the sound, not a specific synth.
