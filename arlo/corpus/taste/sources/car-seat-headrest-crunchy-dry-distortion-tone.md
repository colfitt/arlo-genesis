Premier Guitar + Equipboard + CityBeat interview · 2018

## Distilled

- **Guitars:** early-'90s **Mexican Fender Telecaster** (main — bright, twangy, biting highs;
  the cluster's brittle-jangle source); **Squier Mini Strat** ($100 practice); Yamaha acoustics.
  Toledo deliberately uses cheap/light guitars — anti-precious, demo-grade instruments.
- **Dirt:** **Boss DS-2 Turbo Distortion** is the primary effect — a cheap, aggressive, mid-spiky
  distortion that he says "keeps breaking." That half-failing quality (a dirt box on its last
  legs) is part of the lo-fi character.
- **Amps:** **vintage Supro** (Teens of Denial studio crunch), **Fender Twin Reverb** (reverb
  source), **Fender Cyber Twin PR393** (live, a modeler).
- **The signature recipe** ("Fill in the Blank" etc.): **Telecaster → Boss DS-2 → Supro,
  recorded relatively QUIETLY and boosted in the mix.** Result = "crunchy and dry." The *dry*
  (little reverb on the dirt) + *boosted-quiet-source* = the clipped, in-your-face, mid-forward,
  slightly-broken-up demo-crunch. NOT a saturated wall — a brittle, dry, overloaded crunch.
- **Other:** Red Panda Particle (granular delay), TC PolyTune, D'Addario .010–.046, Dunlop
  mediums. The layered-crunchy-guitar wall is many DS-2/Tele direct-in passes stacked.

## Translation note
- "Crunchy and dry, recorded quiet and boosted" = a gain stage that clips on a low-level source.
  On the rig: a drive pushed by a near-line VG-800 source (Kilt / Oxford / Longsword) kept DRY
  (no verb), mid-forward, with the *level* boosted after — or the **Colour Box** broken-console
  preamp fuzz (STEP high, PRE-VOL up) which is literally "boost a quiet source into clipping."
- The "keeps breaking" DS-2 character = a slightly gated/spitty crunch; the **JHS 424** gated-fuzz
  edge and the **Kilt Forbidden** spitty fuzz both capture that "half-broken dirt box" feel.
