# AMS digital reverb/delay — the gear behind the Joy Division/Hannett cold gated space

AMS RMX16 (released 1981; Hannett was an early AMS adopter and owned multiple AMS digital units):
- Programs: Ambience, Room, Plate, Halls, Chorus, Echo, **Nonlin**, **Reverse**.
- **Nonlin2** = AMS's GATED reverb program (the Hugh Padgham/Phil Collins gated-reverb sound) — a reverb that abruptly cuts off rather than tailing naturally.
- **Reverse** = the reverse-reverb / backwards swell program.
- Adjustable parameters: **Predelay**, Decay Time, High + Low filters, wet/dry Mix.
- The early AMS digital reverb was clean/cold/HARD-EDGED vs. a warm plate or spring — which is exactly the clinical Joy Division character.

WHY THIS MATTERS FOR THE RIG:
- The defining JD reverb traits, mapped to user gear parameters:
  - **Predelay** (source stays dry/separate, space arrives after a gap) → Dark Star **Lag**.
  - **Gated / non-linear cut-off** → a long verb + a downstream NOISE GATE (BF-3) OR a short-decay setting that stops abruptly; the QI Space pulled short.
  - **Cold / clean digital (not lush hall)** → Dark Star **Filter HPF (above noon)** + **Crush near noon/clean** (the artificial, glassy character), NOT a warm LPF wash.
  - **Reverse** → QI grain Playback REVERSE / Dark Star pitch-reverse-adjacent tricks.
