# Black Country, New Road — actual guitar gear + the build/dynamics approach

GUITARS (DIY/modded):
- Luke Mark: Jazzmaster body + Tele pickups + Strat neck hybrid; a Mexican Strat (built by Isaac) with Mojo Pickups Gold Foil between neck/middle.
- Isaac Wood: homemade Telecaster, now double-cutaway (cut the top horn off "while intoxicated") — "There was some sort of mid frequency kept in there that I really didn't like."

PEDALS / FUZZ / DRIVE (the concrete chain):
- **Isaac:** Z.Vex Box of Rock + Z.Vex Distortion; Ibanez Tone-Lok delay.
- **Luke:** Maxon Distortion Master; Boss EQ (modded by Adrian Thorpe/Thorpy FX); EHX Canyon delay. When the Maxon isn't enough, Luke hits it with a JFET clean-boost pedal Isaac built (a STACKED dirt move).
- **Op Amp Big Muff** = the fuzz used on "Sunglasses" (the opening "blast of fuzz").
- Amps: Orange TH30s.

THE BUILD / DYNAMICS PHILOSOPHY (the cluster's signature):
- Luke on the textural role: "It's more about the DYNAMICS and FILLING OUT another melodic space" rather than prominent riffing.
- Isaac on writing: "You build a small skeleton of a song and everyone pulls together… It's a level playing field." (chamber-rock additive build).
- "Sunglasses" structure: post-rock → post-punk → "explosion of free-jazzy noise"; verses build tension that "shakes as if on the verge of exploding" then half the instrumental amps up (a release, not a full blow-out). The 2021 album cut opens with a minute-long DRONING guitar intro.
- "Basketball Shoes": the 12-minute closer "kicks the door in two thirds of the way through with slabs of distortion and a riff that nods to Black Sabbath."

DISTILLED FOR THE RIG:
- BC,NR = brittle/clean textural guitar that FILLS melodic space, then a late, sudden DISTORTION/FUZZ slab climax (Op Amp Big Muff vibe = the EQD Hizumitas / Longsword wall on the user's rig).
- The "JFET boost into Maxon" stacked move = the rig's "drive-in-front / boost-into-fuzz" stacking (108 → Hizumitas, Longsword Boost).
- The build is a DYNAMICS arc, not a single tone — quiet brittle texture → tension → slab. Patches should provide BOTH poles: a restrained brittle-texture voice and a Sabbath-slab wall.

PROVENANCE: BC,NR use an Op Amp Big Muff, ZVex Box of Rock/Distortion, Maxon — NONE of the user's exact pedals. So rig patches are 🟣 designed-to-emulate the result (Big-Muff-class slab = Hizumitas; op-amp distortion = Longsword; brittle texture = 108 buffer-off / Longsword scoop).
