# Norman Westberg (Swans) — the "vast, sustaining chordal" wall: gear + technique

WESTBERG'S CONCRETE GEAR (the second guitar voice of the Swans wall, alongside Gira):
- Guitar: red **Ibanez AS-200** semi-hollow (since late-80s/early-90s — the body resonance/feedback feeds the sustain).
- Dirt: **Boss DS-1 Distortion** — "helps carve out their aggressive, grinding soundscapes" (note: this is the SAME DS-1 Wata of Boris stacks with the Hizumitas — a recurring doom-wall ingredient).
- Dynamics: **MXR Dyna Comp** compressor (tames/sustains levels) + a **volume pedal** (the swell/build tool) + Boss Chromatic Tuner.
- Time: **TC Electronic ND-1 Nova Delay.**
- Amp/cab: **Orange Thunderverb 200 head + Orange PPC412 cab** (same Orange Thunderverb family as Gira's rig — the band runs LOUD; guitar, bass, AND cassettes through amps on stage).

THE TECHNIQUE / CHARACTER (how the wall is actually built):
- Gira describes Westberg's playing as **"a vast, sustaining chordal sound"** — held chords, not riffs.
- The loudness is functional: Swans want to be INSIDE the sound — "the resonance of the guitars and sustain happening at certain volume levels, creating an effect like FOUR CHURCH CHOIRS singing at once." The sustain/feedback bloom is a volume phenomenon.
- "Bring the Sun" live: **repetition of the same chord and beat repeatedly** for ~6–7 minutes, fading into one of the biggest climaxes before the main song. Repetition is the engine; the volume pedal + Dyna Comp shape the swell from quiet to overwhelming.
- Westberg = "rhythmically slashing AND harmonically sensual" — a wall that's both rhythmic and harmonic at once.

DISTILLED FOR THE RIG:
- TWO sustaining wall voices stacked (Gira + Westberg) = the rig should stack TWO dirt voices into a held-chord wall: e.g. MXR 108 (the DS-1 splat-bite role) → Hizumitas (the long sustain), or Hizumitas + Longsword.
- **Compressor + volume pedal + repetition** = the dynamics performance for the near-silence→overwhelming build. On the rig: CB Clean's dynamic-swell / guitar-volume / a volume pedal feeds the held wall; Dark Star/QI freeze provides the "many sustained voices" layering.
- "Four church choirs" = layered sustain at volume → freeze/Multiply (Dark Star) + QI freeze pads stacked under the live wall.

PROVENANCE: Westberg uses Ibanez AS-200 + Boss DS-1 + MXR Dyna Comp + Orange Thunderverb — NONE of the user's exact pedals (the DS-1 is the only generic-circuit overlap, not the user's unit). All rig patches = 🟣 designed-to-emulate the RESULT (vast sustaining chordal wall built by sustain+compression+volume+repetition).
