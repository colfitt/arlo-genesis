# The Messina / Prismizer vocal-halo chain (Bon Iver, "22, A Million")

Distilled concrete info:

- **The Messina** is a custom real-time vocal-harmony instrument built by engineer Chris
  Messina, inspired by Francis Starlite's **Prismizer** but a different implementation.
- **Signal chain (the actual recipe):**
  1. Voice → **Ableton Live** with **TWO Auto-Tune plug-ins**.
  2. **First Auto-Tune** = tunes the vocal normally (hard correction).
  3. **Second Auto-Tune** = forced to output a SINGLE note = the **tonic** of whatever is sung/played.
  4. That tonic + Justin's **MIDI keyboard** notes feed an **Eventide H8000** running a
     **MIDI-controlled harmony program** → generates up to **4 harmony notes** (somewhat random).
  5. Final Messina sound = **dry + tuned vocal + tonic + the 4 H8000 harmonies** summed = the
     polyphonic "choir of one voice" halo.
- The H8000's **artifacts/randomization ARE the sound** — switching keys produces heavy distortion
  and glitch that's left in. ("One thing sounding like a lot of things.")
- **Core vocal capture:** **Shure SM7** mic; Vernon records many layers, sometimes at **different
  mic distances** for tonal variation. "Almost all these vocal tracks have Auto-Tune — it's part of
  his sound" (adds saturation, not just pitch correction).
- **Mix reverbs:** **Bricasti M7** (set to "Dark Chamber"), **two AMS RMX16**, an **AKG stereo
  spring**, an **EMT 140 plate**. Vocals occasionally through a **Tube-Tech SMC2B** multiband comp
  to shape as they build.

Rig takeaway: the halo is a polyphonic pitch-cloud driven by a MIDI keyboard around one voice, with
deliberate digital artifacts. On hardware the user can only APPROXIMATE the cloud (QI Etherealizer
grain-freeze + chorus; Dark Star dual-pitch; VG-800 ALT-TUNE HARMONY) — none reproduce the
MIDI-played 4-note harmony.
