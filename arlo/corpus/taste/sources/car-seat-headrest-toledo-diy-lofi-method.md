## Distilled (HOW the lo-fi sound was actually made)

- **The chain was a laptop, not a studio.** Toledo started in **Audacity** (free), moved to a Mac
  laptop running **GarageBand**, eventually **Logic Pro**. There was no console, no tape machine,
  no outboard. The degrade is a *budget-DAW* degrade, not a tape-deck degrade.
- **Vocals tracked into the LAPTOP MIC, in the car.** Verbatim: "I was using the laptop
  microphone. I would just be singing or screaming into it." For ~3 years he used the bundled
  cheap computer mic. The band name = recording vocals in his car for privacy at his parents'
  house. The blown-out, clipped, distorted vocal = a cheap mic overloaded + screamed into, NOT a
  designed fuzz.
- **Guitars were DIRECT-IN, amp/effects added after.** "I never really recorded mic-and-amp until
  we were doing this record in the studio; I was always doing it direct input." For the laptop
  era: plug straight in or use a preamp setting, amp-sim the tone afterward. (This is *exactly*
  the user's VG-800 → console/tape → print workflow.)
- **Layer to wall, leave the warts.** "I would record part after part and try to make it all as
  big as possible." Density built from many cheap layers — a bedroom wall-of-sound. He kept the
  production warts: "I just would really screw with stuff until it started to sound interesting."
  Starting with *bad* source forced the creative manipulation — imperfection is identity.
- **"Crunchy and dry," recorded quiet + boosted.** On the remade *Twin Fantasy* / Teens-era
  "Fill in the Blank": **Telecaster → Boss DS-2 Turbo Distortion → vintage Supro amp**, "recorded
  relatively quietly and just kind of boosted it in the mix" = the clipped, dry, mid-forward
  crunch. The DS-2 "keeps breaking" (his words) — a cheap, half-broken dirt box.
- **Live tone = Fender Cyber Twin** (a modeler-amp — "a computer with different settings"), which
  he chose *because it mirrors how he recorded* (direct, recallable, no mic'ing). Other gear:
  early-'90s Mexican **Telecaster** (main), **Red Panda Particle** (granular delay), DS-2.
- **The remake proves the point in reverse.** The 2018 *Twin Fantasy (Face to Face)* re-recorded
  the 2011 bedroom record in a full studio — Toledo: the original was lo-fi "due to lack of
  resources," not aesthetic choice, but the *spirit* (layered, blown-out, direct, demo-is-the-song)
  is what fans love. Both versions are the cluster's signature: bedroom-DAW degrade + crunchy
  direct-in guitars + overloaded screamed vocals + dense cheap layering.

## Translation note (for THIS rig)
- The user has the *opposite* problem from Toledo: pristine modeled sources (VG-800) that need to
  be *made* lo-fi. So the rig RE-CREATES the budget-DAW/cheap-mic degrade with real boxes:
  **Colour Box V2** (the overloaded preamp / "screamed-into-cheap-mic" clipped vocal-character on
  a string source), **Oxford / Kilt / 424 / Longsword** (the DS-2 "keeps breaking" crunchy-dry
  direct-in dirt), **PORTA424 + Generation Loss + JHS 424** (the bedroom-cassette/laptop-bounce
  degrade), **Deco V2** (the doubled-layered "make it all as big as possible" wall + tape warm).
- Direct-in-then-degrade IS the rig's native arc — this cluster is the most literal match.
