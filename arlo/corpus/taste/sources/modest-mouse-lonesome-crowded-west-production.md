## Distilled (HOW the early "recorded-wrong" sound was made)

- **Tracked fast, live, hands-off.** Recorded **May 22 – June 7, 1997** (≈2 weeks) at **Moon
  Studios, Olympia** (+ Avast! and Jon & Stu's, Seattle). Produced by **Calvin Johnson** (K
  Records / Beat Happening) with Brock + Scott Swayze; engineered by Swayze.
- **Calvin Johnson's method = let the tape roll, capture spontaneity.** Verbatim: "the important
  thing is to get a good atmosphere… set an environment where people feel comfortable… it's a
  special place, let's make a special record." Hands-off, anti-polish — capture the room and the
  performance warts, don't fix them. This is the "demo-is-the-song" / production-warts-audible
  ethos in production form.
- **The result:** brittle, dry, room-y, jangly clean guitars next to blown-out distorted bursts;
  Brock's strained/cracking vocals pushed; dynamics and odd-tuning dissonance left raw. Lo-fi by
  capture philosophy more than by extreme degrade gear — a *cheap-room, fast, honest* sound.
- **Brock on it:** Calvin's presence "gave me a confidence I needed" — i.e. permission to leave it
  rough. The fatigue/resentment of constant van touring is *in* the take (Brock's framing).
- **Microtonality / odd tunings:** Brock is documented for microtonality and unusual tunings —
  notes that sit "wrong" against the standard system. ("Dramamine" is standard E-tuning with slide
  guitar by Steve Wold; the microtonal/odd character comes more from bends, slide, and detuned
  passages elsewhere than from one alt tuning.) The slightly-out, brittle dissonance is signature.

## Translation note
- "Let the tape roll, capture the warts" doesn't map to a knob — it maps to a *workflow choice*:
  commit a take through a degrade chain (Colour Box → PORTA424 / 424 print) rather than fixing it.
  The rig's console-to-tape arc IS Calvin Johnson's "let the tape roll" in pedal form.
- The brittle dry room sound = bright drive kept dry + a light cassette/console print, not a long
  verb. The odd-tuning/microtonal dissonance is a *playing* choice (the banjo/VG-800 can detune /
  use the VG's tuning models); the rig's job is to keep that dissonance brittle and audible, not
  smooth it.
