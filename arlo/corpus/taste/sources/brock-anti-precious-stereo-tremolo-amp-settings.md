## Distilled (HOW the jangly brittle clean is made — gear + philosophy)

- **Anti-precious is the method, verbatim (Guitar World):** *"I quit trying to find my favorite fucking
  guitar. Now I find the nearest guitar"*; *"all the precious pairing of fucking instruments and amps… is
  just time spent not writing."* He grabs whatever amp is nearby at Dave Sardy's studio. Favorite
  recordings came from *"shit that couldn't even be tuned."* → capture-over-perfect; the warts and
  tuning-instability ARE the sound. (He used to remove the locking nuts from Floyd Rose guitars, courting
  the detune.)
- **The jangle = bright single-coil into a clean Fender, pedals do the dirt.** Equipboard-listed amp
  direction (reverse-engineered, not spoken): tube Fender, **input volume ~7–8, Treble ~7, Mid ~5.5,
  Bass ~4, Reverb ~3–8, Master ~8** — a loud-clean headroom platform with bright/biting treble and tight
  bass. Brock has used the **middle single-coil pickup live** and a **Filtertron bridge** historically;
  the brightness + attack of single coils/Filtertron into a clean Twin IS the brittle jangle.
- **"Basic pedals for years = a blues driver and a digital delay."** The whole early/core jangle is a
  low-gain blues-driver edge-of-breakup + one clear delay repeat — NOT a wall of effects. Add Pro Co RAT
  for heavier parts.
- **Modulation = STEREO TREMOLO, not chorus-soup.** Brock names the **Seymour Duncan Shape Shifter Stereo
  Tremolo** a "must-have" and a **LEM (Mastro Valvola) stereo tremolo**; he gravitates to stereo configs
  for their *"trippiness."* The signature movement on the clean jangle is choppy/throbbing stereo
  amplitude tremolo + the double-tracked thickness — NOT lush chorus.
- **Recent guitars:** Wicks customs (P-90s), Koll Duo Glide / Spectro Glide 12-string, Fender baritones,
  a rubber-string bass ukulele. Recent rig leans baritone-low and 12-string-jangle.

## Translation note (for THIS rig)
- The clean jangle is the ONE place the cluster resists the degrade tie-breaker — but render it as a
  *brittle, slightly-broken* clean, not pristine: low-gain edge-of-breakup (Kilt 10 Crunch, Oxford
  low-gain shoegaze, 424 Gain-1-low) kept BRIGHT + DRY, banjo-as-lead carrying the single-note line.
- **No true stereo-tremolo box** is in the 8 target units → the **Deco V2 doubletracker** (Wide-Stereo
  + low Wobble) is the nearest APPROXIMATION for the double-tracked "natural thick chorus" + wobble. Flag
  it: Deco gives doubling/warble, not throbbing amplitude tremolo. (The rig's BF-3 / a real tremolo
  elsewhere would be the true match, but those aren't in this cluster's target list.)
- **Odd tunings / "couldn't even be tuned"** = a PLAYING choice (banjo / VG-800 detune / tuning models);
  the pedals keep the dissonance brittle + audible (bright drive, light cassette print), not smoothed.
