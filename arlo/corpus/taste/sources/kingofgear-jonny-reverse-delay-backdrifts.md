# Jonny Greenwood reverse/backwards delay technique

URL: https://thekingofgear.com/post/157322865965/what-pedal-uses-jonny-to-make-the-reverse-effect
thekingofgear.com (the authoritative Radiohead-gear reference) · Robert (King of Gear) · undated post (pre-2017)

## Distilled

- **Backdrifts (Hail to the Thief)** is "the most notable example" of a reverse effect
  on Jonny's *guitar* — applied to the outro solo. It was created with a **custom Max/MSP
  patch programmed by Jonny**, NOT a hardware pedal.
- **Identikit** does NOT have reverse guitar from Jonny — only a Boss RE-20 delay; the
  reverse on that song is Thom's *vocals*, "likely done in a DAW or with a Max/MSP patch."
- **A Moon Shaped Pool** reverse effects are "very precisely placed within songs and are
  often samples of sounds which happened earlier within the same track" — audio recorded to
  tape or to Jonny's Max patch and **subsequently reversed**. Not a real-time pedal swell.
- Correction the post makes: **Jonny has never used a Boss DD-5** (that's Ed O'Brien's).
- Godrich's broader push: Jonny adopted Max/MSP so he could "think in terms of sound and
  maths rather than using someone else's idea of what a delay or reverb should sound like."

## Translation note
The Radiohead reverse sound is **a captured-then-reversed sample**, not a swelling reverse-
delay pedal mode. On the rig this maps best to **MOOD MkII Slip (MODIFY CCW = real-time
reverse)** and **Blooper reverse via Stepped Speed CCW / a captured loop played backwards** —
i.e. capture a phrase, reverse it, layer it back in. Honest: an emulation of the *result*, not
the same method.
