# LCD Soundsystem "All My Friends" — the one-loop long build

**The foundation:** the song opens with **two pianos playing one fast interlocking series of notes**, in A major, and that **double-piano loop runs essentially unchanged through the entire ~7:45 track**. There's an intentional **slight fumble/slur** in the loop (wabi-sabi imperfection) — the human looseness is left in.

**Steve Reich reference:** the interlocking double-piano part directly recalls Reich's *Music for 18 Musicians* — hypnotic, phasing minimalism. James Murphy said he was chasing the feeling of Joy Division's "Transmission" and Arthur Russell/Dinosaur L's "Go Bang" — "the simplest thing I could imagine."

**Additive arrangement:** Murphy's strategy is **additive** — over a long instrumental intro, layers enter one at a time across minutes: electronic percussion, human-played instruments, bass/synths, then layered vocals. The **unchanging tempo + repetitive loop** lets each new layer register distinctly and build emotional weight without the core ever changing.

**Electronic-organic fusion:** the LCD signature = electronic precision (drum-machine loop) married to human-played parts (Murphy's own percussion, the live-feel piano). Written by Pat Mahoney, James Murphy, Tyler Pope; produced by The DFA.

**Rig translation:** lock ONE loop (banjo arpeggio, VG-800 figure, or a sampled piano-ish phrase) on the Octatrack/Digitakt/MPC, set a steady tempo, and **add layers across minutes** — never change the core loop. The "5-against-4 / interlocking" feel = two slightly-offset copies (see also the DT2 Steve-Reich tape-loop phasing pair already on the DT2 sheet).
