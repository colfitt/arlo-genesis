# BC,NR "Sunglasses" — the fuzz-climax / tension-build specifics

- "Sunglasses opens with a blast of fuzz courtesy of an **Op Amp Big Muff**" — the Big-Muff-class fuzz is the song's signature opening texture.
- The 9-minute track "morphs from post-rock to post-punk to an explosion of free-jazzy noise"; incorporates noise-rock + free jazz.
- DYNAMICS: Isaac's "uncomfortable verses" sit over instrumentals "building up tension behind them… they shake as if on the verge of exploding," but they DON'T fully explode — they "release some tension with only half the instrumentals amping up the volume." (Tension/release is the engine, not a single drop.)
- The 2021 album re-recording adds a "minute-long droning guitar intro."

RIG TRANSLATION:
- Op Amp Big Muff = a scooped, sustaining, wall-class fuzz → the user's **EQD Hizumitas** (muff-lineage sustain wall) is the closest unit; the MXR 108 can supply the brighter bite into it.
- The droning intro + tension-build = start with a sustained drone (freeze/sustain), let it shake, then a partial slab. Build a restrained texture voice and a slab voice.
