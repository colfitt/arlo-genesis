# Carrie & Lowell: iPhone-intimate capture, Thomas Bartlett production

Distilled concrete info:

- Several songs **recorded on a stock iPhone Voice Memos app + built-in iPhone mic** (e.g. in a
  hotel room in Klamath Falls, OR; "No Shade in the Shadow of the Cross" has an audible whirring A/C
  unit left in). The lo-fi capture IS the intimacy.
- Produced by **Thomas Bartlett** — took the sparse iPhone sketches and "made sense of it all,"
  kept it deliberately sparse. The studio versions retain the demo intimacy (close, dry, room
  present) rather than gloss it.
- Guest players: Thomas Bartlett, **Sean Carey (S. Carey, Bon Iver's drummer)**, Casey Foubert,
  Ben Lester, Nedelle Torrisi, Laura Veirs. Vocal harmonies are layered/intertwining = "echoes of
  the past."
- The whole record = whisper-close voice + fingerpicked guitar/banjo + faint orchestral/electronic
  washes around the single voice; reverb is a halo, not a hall.

Rig takeaway: the "recorded-wrong / captured-on-the-wrong-machine" lean LANDS here as a documented
artist method (iPhone Voice Memos, A/C hiss left in). The rig analogue = Chroma Console CASSETTE +
Gen Loss for the degraded capture character UNDER an intimate close source, washes (QI/Dark Star/
Microcosm) kept faint around the voice. This is the cluster where the making-aesthetic and the
artists genuinely converge.
