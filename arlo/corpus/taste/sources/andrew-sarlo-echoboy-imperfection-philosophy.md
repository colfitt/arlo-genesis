# Andrew Sarlo's production approach (Big Thief, Bon Iver, Nick Hakim)

Distilled concrete info:

- **EchoBoy on the lead vocal** on Big Thief's *Two Hands* — "we quickly threw the EchoBoy on the
  lead vocal to **mimic my Space Echo** and it stuck!" A vintage-echo slapback as a vocal-glue
  texture.
- Philosophy = **"more people-focused than gear-focused"** — collaboration/emotion over specs.
- **Embraces imperfection / leaves it in:** on Nick Hakim's "Needy Bees" he **kept an accidental
  string fade-in** in the final mix. Captures spontaneous live takes ("first song captured
  appropriately… led the way").
- Room technique example (Show Me the Body): **four mics in the four corners of the room, blown up
  through API preamps** = "the gnarliest thing I've recorded" — i.e. he'll record a room hard and
  distorted when the song wants it.

Rig takeaway: the producer-level lesson reinforces the lens — capture-the-performance, leave the
accidents, add a vintage slapback/space-echo character (rig: Big Time / EchoBoy-style delay) rather
than gridded polish. Saturating a room hard = the rig's degrade lean is legitimate here, not just
the maker's preference.
