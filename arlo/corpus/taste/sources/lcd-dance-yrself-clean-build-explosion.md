# LCD "Dance Yrself Clean" — the quiet-build-then-explosion

**Structure (the famous dynamic):** the track opens as a **two-chord vamp** — a bass tolling over **pattering/tapped percussion** (cowbell + tapped drums) and a **hurdy-gurdy organ line**, with muted, indie-sounding close vocals. It stays subdued and sparse for ~3 minutes, then **erupts at the drop** into a huge fuzzy-synth-pop wall (a big distorted synth bass/arp + full drum-machine groove + shouted vocal). The whole point is the *withholding* — a long quiet additive intro so the explosion lands.

**Mechanics that matter for the rig:**
- The build is **arrangement, not a filter sweep** — layers are added/withheld, then everything slams in at once. (Contrast with Daft Punk's filter-house, where the *timbre* opens over a static loop.)
- The drop's lead = a **fuzzy/distorted synth arp** (Gearspace consensus: a saw arp run hot/saturated, not a clean patch) — the wall IS distorted, which fits the rig's degrade lean perfectly.
- Murphy's additive method (cf. "All My Friends"): lock a tempo + a tiny core (vamp/percussion), add one layer at a time, save the biggest move for one cued boundary.

**Rig translation:** build the quiet half on the Digitakt/Octatrack/MPC as a sparse cowbell-and-tap pattern over a held organ/baritone drone; stage the explosion as a **cued pattern change** (DT2 LAST/NOT-LAST auto-fill, or OT cued Part switch, or 61SL cued Session switch) that drops in a distorted VG-800 GR-300/saw arp + full kick groove on the downbeat. The fuzz on the drop arp = on-aesthetic (run it into the wall).
