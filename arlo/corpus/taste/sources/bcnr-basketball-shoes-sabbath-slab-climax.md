# BC,NR "Basketball Shoes" — the late Sabbath-slab climax + the chamber-rock build

THE STRUCTURE (the cluster's signature build-dynamics in one song):
- "Basketball Shoes" = the 12-minute album closer. It **"kicks the door in two thirds of the way through with slabs of distortion and a riff that nods to Black Sabbath."**
- The whole thing is an additive, restrained build: brittle/clean melodic-space-filling guitar (Luke Mark: "It's more about the DYNAMICS and FILLING OUT another melodic space" rather than riffing) for the first two-thirds → a SUDDEN heavy distortion slab climax at the end.
- "Sunglasses" companion structure: post-rock → post-punk → "explosion of free-jazzy noise"; the verses build tension that "shakes as if on the verge of exploding," then **only HALF the instrumental amps up** — a partial release, not a full blow-out. (Tension/release is the engine.)
- The 2021 re-recording of "Sunglasses" adds a **minute-long droning guitar intro** (a sustained drone bed before the song).

THE GEAR THAT MAKES THE SLAB (from the BCNR DIY-gear source on disk):
- Op Amp Big Muff = the "Sunglasses" opening blast of fuzz; ZVex Box of Rock + ZVex Distortion (Isaac); Maxon Distortion Master + a JFET clean-boost STACKED into it (Luke); Orange TH30 amps.
- The Sabbath slab = a scooped/saturated Big-Muff-class wall arriving LATE and SUDDENLY after a long quiet build.

DISTILLED FOR THE RIG (the "build dynamics" patch pair):
- The cluster needs BOTH POLES as patches: (1) a RESTRAINED brittle/clean texture voice for the long first two-thirds (fills melodic space, doesn't riff), and (2) a LATE SUDDEN SABBATH SLAB wall for the climax (Big-Muff-class = Hizumitas; op-amp distortion = Longsword Full-Bore/Berserker).
- The droning intro = a sustained drone bed (Dark Star / QI freeze) before the slab.
- The "only half amps up" partial release = the slab can be the Longsword Boost footswitch or a second fuzz kicking in, not necessarily a full wall.
