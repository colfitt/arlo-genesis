# Lo-fi / prolific — Car Seat Headrest + Modest Mouse cluster index

> **What this is.** The cross-rig view of every patch derived this wave from the **Lo-fi / prolific**
> lens — anchored on **Car Seat Headrest** (Will Toledo's bedroom-DAW degrade, crunchy-dry direct-in

> Brock's brittle jangly clean single-coil tone, odd tunings/microtonality, and the fast "let the

## How the artists actually get these sounds (research summary)

Captured to `Research/Taste-Profile/sources/`:

- **Will Toledo's lo-fi is a BUDGET-DAW degrade, not a tape degrade.** Audacity → GarageBand →
  Logic on a Mac laptop; vocals "singing or screaming into" the **bundled laptop microphone** (in
  his car, for privacy) — the blown-out distorted vocal = *a cheap mic overloaded by a screamed
  source*, not a fuzz. Guitars were **direct-in, amp-sim added after** ("I never recorded mic-and-amp
  until the studio… always direct input"). He layered "part after part… as big as possible" and
  kept the warts: "I just would really screw with stuff until it started to sound interesting."
  (`car-seat-headrest-toledo-diy-lofi-method.md`)
- **The crunchy-dry guitar tone is a quiet source clipped + boosted.** Early-'90s Mexican
  **Telecaster → Boss DS-2 Turbo Distortion → vintage Supro / Vox AC15 / Fender '65 Twin Reverb**,
  "recorded relatively quietly and boosted in the mix" = "crunchy and dry." The DS-2 "keeps breaking"
  — a cheap, half-failing, mid-spiky, gated-ish dirt box. Bright single-coil + dry (no verb) +
  mid-forward. "Something Soon" (Teens of Style) was **all Toledo's own bedroom work** — a pro mixer
  was brought in *only for drums and bass.*
  (`car-seat-headrest-crunchy-dry-distortion-tone.md`, `csh-something-soon-amps-mixing.md`)
- **Modest Mouse's jangle is a CLEAN amp + bright single-coils + pedals.** Documented direction: a
  **Fender Twin Reverb** clean with headroom (reverse-engineered EQ leans bright/mid: Treble 7 /
  Mid 5–6 / Bass 4 / Reverb 6, vibrato off), **Telecaster / Mustang / Wicks (P-90)** on the bright
  pickup. The lead is **double-tracked → natural thick chorus**; **delay = one clear repeat,
  feedback 0, mix ~30–40%**; modulation (Brock-named **Seymour Duncan stereo TREMOLO**, not lush
  chorus) used sparingly; moderate reverb for space. Bright, bouncy, brittle, articulate.
  (`modest-mouse-brock-jangly-clean-tone-gear.md`)
- **The early "recorded-wrong" sound is a Calvin-Johnson capture philosophy.** *The Lonesome
  Crowded West* (1997) was tracked in ~2 weeks at Moon Studios with Calvin Johnson "letting the
  tape roll" — hands-off, capture spontaneity, leave the warts. Brock's microtonality / odd tunings
  and strained vocals are left raw. Lo-fi by *capture*, not by extreme degrade gear.
  (`modest-mouse-lonesome-crowded-west-production.md`)
- **Anti-precious is the method (Brock, verbatim).** "I quit trying to find my favorite fucking
  guitar. Now I find the nearest guitar… all the precious pairing of instruments and amps is just
  time spent not writing." Favorite recordings came from "shit that couldn't even be tuned" — the
  tuning-instability and warts ARE the identity. His "basic pedals for years = a blues driver and a
  digital delay"; the movement is a **stereo TREMOLO** (Seymour Duncan Shape Shifter, a "must-have"),
  not lush chorus. (`brock-anti-precious-stereo-tremolo-amp-settings.md`)
- **The five signature sounds, pinned to method + flagged for the rig.** (1) bedroom/4-track
  cassette degrade → laptop/fast-capture (rig: PORTA424 + Gen Loss + 424); (2) clipped/overloaded
  preamps → "recorded quiet, boosted" reveals clipping (rig: Colour Box broken-console); (3)
  room-mic'd amps bleeding → left-in room (rig: *approximated* — this is a dry-forward print rig);
  (4) vocals pushed into distortion → cheap mic overloaded (rig: *character only* — no vocal path,
  ported to a string source); (5) jangly brittle clean + odd tunings → bright clean + double-track
  + sparing tremolo (rig: bright edge-of-breakup + Deco doubletracker, tremolo *approximated*).
  (`lofi-prolific-blown-out-vocals-and-warts.md`)
