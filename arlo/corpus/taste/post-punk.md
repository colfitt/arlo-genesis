## How the artists actually get these sounds (research summary)

Captured to `Research/Taste-Profile/sources/`:

- **Swans = no pedals on the wall (volume + sustain + repetition + layering).** Gira uses "only volume
  and sustain" — a Gibson Lucille (controls taped except volume) + a holey Guild acoustic (Fishman
  pickup, thumbed "between a bass and a guitar") into a bass-heavy rig (Orange Thunderverb + a
  **Gallien-Krueger 1000 bass head** + Mesa 4x12s chained). The wall is built by finding chords with
  "potential," playing them **repeatedly**, listening for the **ghost notes / overtones**, then
  **layering sustained voices underneath**. (`gira-swans-no-pedals-overtones-crescendo.md`)
- **Westberg's second wall voice = "a vast, sustaining chordal sound."** Ibanez AS-200 (body
  resonance/feedback) → **Boss DS-1** ("aggressive, grinding") → **MXR Dyna Comp + a volume pedal** (the
  swell/build tools) → Orange Thunderverb. Loud on purpose: the sustain/feedback bloom is a volume
  phenomenon — "like **four church choirs singing at once**." "Bring the Sun" live repeats the same
  chord/beat ~6–7 min into the climax. → the wall is TWO stacked dirt voices + compression + a volume
  swell + repetition. (`westberg-swans-sustaining-chordal-wall-gear.md`)
- **The Swans crescendo is a duration/dynamics event, not a tone.** "Bring the Sun" live: a **drone
  barely audible** (tape loops + gong) → a motorik/kraut **pulse** emerges → instruments stacked one at
  a time over minutes → "warm guitar/bass hum" under "monotonous, **icy stabs** of Gira's guitar" →
  "pulverising, almost kraut-rock crescendo." "The Seer" was tracked then overdubbed for **five months**.
  (`swans-seer-bring-the-sun-crescendo-build.md`)
- **Joy Division's cold/separated space = Hannett's AMS rack + obsessive isolation.** Each drum recorded
  **separately** (zero bleed); kit **deadened**; a **very fast delay on the snare**; the **AMS 15-80S**
  delay "set to some minute period" on the drums; reverb re-amped through an Auratone on a basement
  toilet; **extreme panning** (delay-tails to the opposite side = "cavernous"); **backwards guitar**
  opens "New Dawn Fades"; guitars set **back behind reverb**. Character: cold, clinical, separated, with
  **predelay** so the dry source and the space never blur.
  (`hannett-joy-division-cold-gated-space.md`)
- **The AMS gear names the target parameters.** The AMS RMX16 has **Predelay**, **Nonlin** (the gated,
  abruptly-cut reverb), and **Reverse** programs, and a clean/cold/hard-edged digital character (not a
  warm plate). → aim the Dark Star / QI emulations at **Lag/predelay + cold HPF filter + abrupt/short or
  gated decay + reverse grains**, not a lush warm hall. (`hannett-ams-rmx16-nonlin-gated-reverb.md`)
- **Joy Division's guitar itself is dark, treble-cut, low-gain, rhythmic.** A representative "She's Lost
  Control" Vox AC30 dial = **Gain 3 · Bass 8 · Mid 4 · Treble 2 · Presence 1** — bass up, treble +
  presence cut hard. The documented move: **use an EQ pedal to take away most of the guitar's treble**
  for the "dark, percussive, rhythmic" tone, then the huge cold reverb sits over it.
  (`joy-division-sumner-amp-settings-treble-cut.md`)
- **BC,NR = a chamber-rock build: restrained brittle texture → sudden late slab.** Luke: "It's more
  about the **dynamics** and filling out another melodic space" than riffing. The fuzz is an **Op Amp Big
  Muff** ("Sunglasses" opens with "a blast of fuzz"); stacked dirt = a **JFET clean-boost into the
  Maxon**; "Basketball Shoes" "kicks the door in two-thirds through with **slabs of distortion and a riff
  that nods to Black Sabbath**." Tension/release is the engine — they "shake as if on the verge of
  exploding" then only **half** the instrumental amps up. The 2021 "Sunglasses" adds a "minute-long
  **droning** guitar intro." (`bcnr-diy-guitar-fuzz-dynamics.md`, `bcnr-sunglasses-fuzz-climax.md`,
  `bcnr-basketball-shoes-sabbath-slab-climax.md`)

**The throughline:** all three build from near-silence/restraint to an overwhelming sustained wall via
**repetition + layering + a late dynamic slab**, pairing a **dark low wall** with a **brittle high
voice**, and Joy Division adds a **cold, predelayed, separated** spatial signature. The user's rig
renders this as: a sustained-fuzz wall (Hizumitas/Longsword/108), a brittle/icy texture voice, a
baritone low bed (VG-800), and cold predelayed/reverse space (Dark Star / QI) — performed as a dynamics
arc, never set-and-forget.
