## The Session Story In One Paragraph

*Weeeeee!* is the 2001 album release of songs originally written by Brendan Kilcoyne, working as Threebrain, to accompany the Flash cartoons he posted on his own site threebrain.com ([Discogs, *Weeeeee!*](https://www.discogs.com/release/18229897-Threebrain-Weeeeee); [AllMusic, Brendan Kilcoyne (threebrain)](https://www.allmusic.com/artist/brendan-kilcoyne-threebrain-mn0000898192/credits)). The most visible track, *Weeeeee!* — popularly mis-titled "Gonads and Strife" after its refrain — became one of the defining viral animations of the pre-YouTube era after it was posted to Albino Blacksheep, and the album collects twenty short songs from that animation pipeline ([Know Your Meme, *Gonads and Strife*](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/gonads-and-strife)). No mainstream rock press covered the release at the time; the most-cited contemporary write-up is a short November 2002 *Lollipop Magazine* item that treats Threebrain primarily as "one of my favorite flash video sites" rather than as a recording artist ([Hefflon, *Lollipop Magazine*, 2002](https://lollipopmagazine.com/2002/11/threebrain-review/)). Production personnel, recording location, and gear are not publicly documented in any indexed source, and the corpus value of this record sits in its distribution story rather than in any verifiable studio detail.

## The Album As Cartoon Soundtrack

The structural fact of *Weeeeee!* is that the songs predate the album as a coherent object — they were written, recorded, and released individually as the audio beds for short Flash cartoons on threebrain.com, and the CD pulls them together after the fact ([Discogs, *Weeeeee!*](https://www.discogs.com/release/18229897-Threebrain-Weeeeee)). That inverts the normal album-development pipeline. Rather than tracks earning a place on a record by surviving studio attrition, these songs earned their place by surviving as cartoons — by getting clicked, looped, and emailed around. The reviewer at *Lollipop* picked out the same point in 2002, describing the site's content as a stream of short comedic pieces of variable quality, with the wheelchair-and-squirrel video as the standout ([Hefflon, *Lollipop Magazine*, 2002](https://lollipopmagazine.com/2002/11/threebrain-review/)). The corpus point: the unit of composition is the cartoon, not the song, and the album is a compilation of accepted cartoons. That selection mechanism is closer to a webcomic best-of than to a sequenced record.

## The Distribution Mechanism — Threebrain.com, Newgrounds, Albino Blacksheep

The record's cultural foothold sits in the early-2000s Flash distribution stack rather than in record-store retail. Albino Blacksheep launched in 1999, gained traction as a user-submitted Flash hub through the early 2000s, and peaked around 2006 ([Know Your Meme, *Albino Blacksheep*](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/sites/albino-blacksheep)). Threebrain's *Weeeeee!* / *Gonads and Strife* was uploaded there in 2001 and is the canonical example used to describe the site's role: "notable animations like 'Gonads and Strife' were uploaded to ABS and subsequently became widespread over Newgrounds and Albino Blacksheep" ([Know Your Meme, *Albino Blacksheep*](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/sites/albino-blacksheep)). The cartoon's popularity rose into 2003–2004 before declining, with derivative versions spreading through YTMND and, later, YouTube ([Know Your Meme, *Gonads and Strife*](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/gonads-and-strife)). The corpus point: the album's audience reach was a function of three indie-hosted Flash portals operating before consolidated video platforms existed.

## The Title Confusion — *Weeeeee!* vs. "Gonads and Strife"

A small but important fact for retrieval: the song most listeners know as "Gonads and Strife" is actually titled *Weeeeee!*, the same name as the album that contains it ([Know Your Meme, *Gonads and Strife*](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/gonads-and-strife); [Discogs, *Weeeeee!*](https://www.discogs.com/release/18229897-Threebrain-Weeeeee)). The nickname comes from the chorus refrain. Knowing this prevents the obvious lookup mistake — searching the cartoon's popular name returns meme write-ups, while the album and track title return discography pages. The 2002 *Lollipop* review uses the spelling "Weee!" for the cartoon ([Hefflon, *Lollipop Magazine*, 2002](https://lollipopmagazine.com/2002/11/threebrain-review/)), which is a third orthographic variant in circulation.

## The Tracklist Shape

The album runs twenty tracks in roughly thirty-four minutes, averaging well under two minutes per song ([Discogs, *Weeeeee!*](https://www.discogs.com/release/18229897-Threebrain-Weeeeee)). Track titles surfaced via aggregator listings include *Egyptian Slut*, *Monkey Salad*, *Across the Pond*, *Weeeeee!*, *Robot Hero*, *Hypothermia*, *School Bus!*, *You Could Be A Star*, *Fetus Memories* and *Poopies*. The *Lollipop* review confirms *Hypothermia* as a separately recognised cartoon at the site ([Hefflon, *Lollipop Magazine*, 2002](https://lollipopmagazine.com/2002/11/threebrain-review/)). The short-song format is consistent with the cartoon-soundtrack origin: songs were sized to the length of a Flash loop, not to radio or LP-side conventions. The relevant lesson for the corpus is that an album-as-cartoon-compilation will tend toward sketch-comedy pacing rather than full song forms.

## Cultural Trajectory — Meme Before The Word Was In Common Use

By the time "internet meme" entered general vocabulary, *Weeeeee!* had already cycled through it. The cartoon spread person-to-person through email forwards, instant-messenger links, and early forum embeds, then was replicated through YTMND re-edits, fan AMVs, and slowed-down or sped-up YouTube uploads from roughly 2005 onward ([Know Your Meme, *Gonads and Strife*](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/gonads-and-strife)). The *Lollipop* review captures the contemporary register exactly — the site is recommended on the strength of randomness and the in-jokes of a small enthusiast scene rather than on craft criteria ([Hefflon, *Lollipop Magazine*, 2002](https://lollipopmagazine.com/2002/11/threebrain-review/)). The album therefore sits in the corpus as an early case of music whose distribution mechanism was the animation it accompanied, with the recorded object trailing behind the viral artefact.
