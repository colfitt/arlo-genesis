# EQ/Mid Pedal After — the documented versatility fix

## Concept
The Hizumitas has one documented weakness, and it has a documented fix: community testing found it *"would need some sort of EQ or mid-hump pedal in the mix to help expand its versatility."* The Tone control alone can't reach the upper mids the dense Elk-Muff wall masks. The fix is to place an EQ or mid-hump pedal AFTER the Hizumitas (in this rig, the Chase Bliss Brothers AM run clean-ish) and sculpt the upper mids back in. That turns the wall from a single immovable brick into a shapeable lead/rhythm voice that cuts through the mix. This is the AFTER-the-fuzz EQ stage specifically — distinct from the front-stacking patches.

## How to play it
1. Set the Hizumitas to your wall or lead voice — start with all three knobs near noon (Volume, Sustain, Tone).
2. Place an EQ or mid-hump pedal AFTER it; in this rig, run the Brothers AM clean-ish as the shaper.
3. Boost or sculpt the upper mids the dense wall masks — restore versatility and cut-through that the Tone knob can't reach.
4. Use the downstream pedal as a tone-shaping stage, not as additional gain (the Hizumitas already supplies the saturation).
