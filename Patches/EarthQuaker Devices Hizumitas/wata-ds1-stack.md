# Wata's DS-1 Stack — chaos / aggression

## Concept
Wata of Boris's own, self-documented move: hit the Hizumitas WITH a Boss DS-1 stacked together to create a more chaotic, aggressive feel. The Hizumitas supplies the saturated, sustaining Elk-Muff foundation; the DS-1 adds tighter, brighter distortion edge on top for climaxes and cut. It's a full distortion layered with the fuzz, not a clean boost in front — the dense Boris wall. In this rig the nearest analog is a drive stacked with the Hizumitas.

## How to play it
1. Set the Hizumitas to its core wall voice — Volume unity ~9:00–10:00, Sustain & Tone ~12:00–1:00.
2. Add a Boss DS-1 (Wata's named pedal) stacked in front of or alongside the Hizumitas.
3. Kick the DS-1 in as a second gear above the standalone Hizumitas wall — the two dirt stages pile into a chaotic, aggressive climax.
4. Run into a loud clean amp (Wata uses a Matamp GT-120 clean); add wah, reverb and echo over the top for the full Boris texture.
