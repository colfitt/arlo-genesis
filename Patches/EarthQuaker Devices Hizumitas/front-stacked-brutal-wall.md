# Front-Stacked Brutal Wall

## Concept
Pushing the Hizumitas into brutal/savage high-gain via a drive IN FRONT — Wata's chaos philosophy adapted to this rig. The Hizumitas itself sits near noon across the board; the character comes from the stack. In this rig the MXR 108 already sits front as a gated/splatty pre-stage the Hizumitas smooths into a wall, and the Brothers AM after restores the upper mids the wall masks.

## How to play it
1. **Hizumitas:** Volume 12:00, Sustain 12:00–1:00, Tone 12:00–1:00.
2. **IN FRONT:** the MXR 108 (already front in this rig) set to taste, or a low-gain boost to tighten/slam the Sustain stage.
3. **AFTER:** run Brothers AM clean-ish to restore the upper mids the wall masks; back Longsword gain to 8–10:00 (Hizumitas already supplies the saturation).

## Notes
- Community/artist stacking principle translated to THIS rig.
- **Honest:** Wata's specific stack is Hizumitas + Boss DS-1 chaos (NOT in this rig) — the 108-front + Brothers/Longsword-after chain is the documented-*equivalent* architecture, not a claim Wata uses it.
- Ref: Wata — *"I often use the Hizumitas with a distortion to create more chaotic feel"* (Guitar World); nearest rig analog = front-108 + drive-after architecture.
