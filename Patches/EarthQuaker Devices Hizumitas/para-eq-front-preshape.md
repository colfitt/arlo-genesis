# Para-EQ Front Pre-Shape — sculpt what the fuzz eats

## Concept
SixStringSensei's front-stacking move: put a parametric EQ (Empress Para EQ MkII) IN FRONT of the Hizumitas to pre-shape exactly which frequencies the fuzz saturates. Unlike a clean boost, a para-EQ lets you carve the input spectrum *before* the Sustain stage — taming or emphasizing specific bands so the wall reads the way you want. The Hizumitas' own Tone and Filter knobs shape what comes *out*; a para-EQ in front gives surgical control over what goes *in*, which the Tone knob alone can't reach. For the rig's bright banjo source, that means pulling the high mids before the fuzz so the 2–4 kHz pierce never gets saturated into harshness.

## How to play it
1. Place a parametric EQ (Empress Para EQ MkII) IN FRONT of the Hizumitas.
2. Set the Hizumitas near noon across the board (Volume, Sustain, Tone, Filter) to taste.
3. Use the para-EQ's sweepable mid band to boost or cut the frequencies you want the fuzz to saturate.
4. For the bright banjo source, pull the high mids before the fuzz to tame the 2–4 kHz pierce.
