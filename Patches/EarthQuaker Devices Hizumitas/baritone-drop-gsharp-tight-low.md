# Baritone Drop-G# Tight Low — extended-range riffing

## Concept
Andy's Reverb Tone Report baritone demo: a Reverend baritone tuned to drop G# with P90-ish Railhammer pickups, showing the Hizumitas tracks extreme low tunings with a tight, defined low end. The standout is the treble-bleed volume knob — it cleans the fuzz up off the guitar, which is unusual for a Big Muff-style circuit. Maps onto the rig's baritone for extended-range drone/riffing: tight low-tuned riffs that still clean up to the touch.

## How to play it
1. Tune to a deep drop tuning (the demo is drop G# on a baritone).
2. Set Volume near unity (~10:00), Sustain ~75%.
3. Keep Tone where the low end stays tight/defined; back toward treble to keep clarity on the low strings.
4. Use the guitar volume knob (especially with a treble-bleed mod) to clean up — it responds well, unusually so for a Muff.

## Notes
- The Hizumitas tracks the low end tightly even at drop G# on a baritone — a demonstrated strength in the demo, not inference.
- Treble-bleed-equipped guitars clean up via the volume knob, which is unusual for a Muff-style circuit (called out explicitly in the demo).
- **Mixed sourcing:** Sustain ~75% and unity Volume are quoted/framed in the demo; the exact **Tone** position is *swept* on camera and not published — dial it from the recipe above.
- Distinct from the existing Shoegaze Wall (baritone Jazzmaster), which is a chordal wash; this is tight low-tuned riffing with cleanup.
