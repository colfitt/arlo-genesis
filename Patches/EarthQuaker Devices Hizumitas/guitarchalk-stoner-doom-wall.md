# Stoner/Doom Wall (Guitar Chalk numbers)

## Concept
Thick and seismic — ultra-saturated, feedback-friendly sustain ideal for stoner, sludge, or doom riffs. High Sustain with a fat-leaning Tone builds a crushing wall that still moves, rather than going fully maxed and static. The published numbers come from Guitar Chalk's settings table (Sustain 9, Tone 4, Volume 8 on a 0–10 scale); Tone 4 deliberately keeps a touch more midrange/movement than a full-bass max setting.

## How to play it
1. Set Sustain 9, Tone 4, Volume 8 on a 0–10 dial.
2. Hold power chords or single low notes and let the sustain feed back near a loud amp.
3. Drop or down-tunings reward the heft — mute and ring out for dynamics.
4. Back off Sustain slightly if the wall stops moving; Tone 4 keeps it from going fully dark.

## Notes
- Guitar Chalk's Tone 4 keeps a touch more midrange/movement than full-bass max settings — that's the point of this recipe versus an all-maxed wall.
- Distinct from `doom-wall-max-heavy.md` (all-max, Tone full CW) — this is the PUBLISHED non-max Guitar Chalk recipe; keep both as separate reference points.
- **Honesty — the NUMBERS are sourced (Guitar Chalk 0–10 table), the clock mapping is not.** Guitar Chalk's numbers vs EQD's clock labeling don't always agree (EQD copy: Tone CW = bass, CCW = highs). Tone 4 is on the fat/bass side per the source's labeling, so map the number to a clock position by ear if your knob is unmarked — don't trust an exact clock position.
