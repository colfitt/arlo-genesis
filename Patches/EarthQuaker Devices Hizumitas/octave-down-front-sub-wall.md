# Octave-Down Front Sub-Wall — reliable low octave

## Concept
An octave-DOWN pedal placed IN FRONT of the Hizumitas adds a reliable sub-octave to the wall. Per the dossier, octave-down before the Hizumitas is more reliable than octave-up — the Muff topology doesn't preserve octave-up fundamentals cleanly, so an octave-up in front goes aggressive and ring-modulated. This is a doom/drone patch for adding sub weight before the fuzz: the octave-down feeds clean low-end mass into the Sustain stage and the Hizumitas smooths it into a thick, low wall.

## How to play it
1. Place an octave-DOWN pedal IN FRONT of the Hizumitas.
2. Set the Hizumitas to a wall voice — Sustain up, Tone bass-side for weight.
3. Blend the sub-octave to taste before the fuzz for added low-end mass.
4. Avoid octave-UP in front — the Muff topology ring-modulates it (use that only if you want the chaos).
