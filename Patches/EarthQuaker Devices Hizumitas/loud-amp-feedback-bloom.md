# Loud-Amp Feedback Bloom — voiced for volume

## Concept
Wata's own usage directive: *"I want people to try it in an environment where they can set amps very loud."* The feedback-sustain bloom and the full fuzz wall only arrive at volume — bedroom levels undersell the pedal. At high Sustain into a loud amp, held notes bloom into controlled, singing feedback you steer with proximity and the Tone knob; the compression keeps it predictable rather than runaway.

## How to play it
1. Set the Hizumitas to a sustaining wall voice (Sustain high, Volume hot, Tone to taste).
2. Turn the AMP up loud and stand close — the feedback/sustain bloom only arrives at volume.
3. Hold a note/chord and move toward/away from the speaker to find the feedback node.
4. Use the Tone knob to choose which harmonic feeds back (brighter = squeal, darker = fundamental); the compression keeps it predictable.

## Notes
- Sourced directive from Wata via the making-of interview — the pedal is voiced for loud-amp interaction; Wata runs a Matamp GT-120 clean and loud as part of the recipe.
- Feedback is controllable (low runaway even at high gain) — the compression character keeps it predictable.
- ⚠ **Honesty flag:** this is a TECHNIQUE patch — no published Hizumitas knob values. The variable is amp volume and proximity; dial the pedal from the recipe above, not from a fixed setting.
- Bedroom-level testing undersells the sustain character (gotcha).
