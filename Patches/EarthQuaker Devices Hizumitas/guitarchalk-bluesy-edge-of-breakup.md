# Bluesy Edge-of-Breakup (Guitar Chalk numbers)

## Concept
A dynamic, touch-sensitive fuzz that blends with an overdriven tube amp for blues and roots rock. Low Sustain keeps it articulate so it behaves like a hot, fuzzy overdrive rather than a wall — it cleans up off the guitar volume and picking attack. The published numbers come from Guitar Chalk's settings table (Sustain 3, Tone 4, Volume 6 on a 0–10 scale); the two breakup layers (pedal + amp) interact so the rig responds to how hard you dig in.

## How to play it
1. Set Sustain 3, Tone 4, Volume 6 on a 0–10 dial.
2. Run into an already-overdriven tube amp and let the two breakup layers blend.
3. Ride your guitar volume knob — back it off for grind, dig in for fuzz.
4. Best on neck/middle pickup positions for warmth.

## Notes
- Guitar Chalk uses a 0–10 numbering; translate to clock positions if your dial is unmarked (3 ≈ 9:30, 4 ≈ 10:30, 6 ≈ 1:00).
- **Honesty — the published NUMBER is sourced (Guitar Chalk 0–10 table); the clock mapping is by ear.** Sources disagree on Tone DIRECTION: Guitar Chalk / Premier Guitar read it as CCW = treble, while EQD's own copy labels CW = bass / CCW = highs — same destination, opposite labeling. Dial Tone by ear and treat the clock position as approximate.
- Low-Sustain dynamic blues voice — distinct from `controlled-crunch-low-gain.md` (Sustain minimum + full-CCW Tone factory clip). This one keeps a little more Sustain and a less-bright Tone for a warmer, edge-of-breakup roll.
