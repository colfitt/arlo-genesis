# Shoegaze Wall (baritone Jazzmaster)

## Concept
A crushing shoegaze wall for the baritone Jazzmaster — articulate fuzz that still lets chords *read* inside the wash. Tone is kept near noon (not full CW) on purpose so chords stay articulate: the mid-forward voicing is the Hizumitas's shoegaze advantage over a scooped Big Muff. Strum chords and let them bloom into reverb downstream.

## How to play it
1. Volume 12:00, Sustain 1:00–2:00, Tone 12:00–1:00 (noon-to-slightly-fat).
2. Strum chords on the baritone Jazzmaster.
3. Let the chords bloom into downstream reverb (Big Time / End-board).

## Notes
- **DESIGNED.** Tone near noon (not full CW) keeps chords articulate inside the wash — the mid-forward voicing is the deliberate advantage over a scooped Muff.
- Less Tone compensation than the banjo needs; baritone is home territory for this pedal.
- Ref: Shoegaze cluster (offset-guitar wall) — making-aesthetic lean.
