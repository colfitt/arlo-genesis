# Banjo Infinite-Sustain Drone Feeder

## Concept
Purpose-built feeder for the Lost & Found Sympathetic Resonator: takes a GK-5 banjo's ~1.5s plink and converts it into an effectively infinite, pitched fuzz tone so the downstream resonator has a long, steady note to lock onto. Voiced dark on purpose — bass-side Tone rolls off the banjo's piercing 2–4 kHz (via the 3n3 HPF cap) so the resonator self-selects a clean fundamental and rings as a glassy vibraphone-pipe drone instead of a shrieking upper partial. Distinct from the existing 'Banjo Sustain Manufacturer' (Sustain only 12:00, a lead voice) and from 'Sustain Max, Tone 8–10' (Premier Guitar published seed, but Tone bright for a CUTTING lead) — this one maxes Sustain AND goes bass-side specifically for the drone-feeder role.

## How to play it
1. Volume hot, Sustain MAXED (CW), Tone ~1:00–2:00 (well into the bass-side CW range).
2. Play sustained banjo notes/chords (ideally after a clean/compressor stage that tames the GK-5 attack) — the max Sustain holds the note effectively forever.
3. Feed the long pitched tone into the Lost & Found Sympathetic Resonator — the dark, even input lets the resonator lock a clean fundamental and bloom into a glassy pipe-drone.
4. If the resonator rings shrill, push Tone further bass-side (CW) before touching anything downstream — the shrillness lives in the fuzz feed.
5. Swell in with the instrument volume so the hard fuzz attack never bites the front of the drone.

## Notes
- **DESIGNED** — purpose-built for the Hizumitas → Lost & Found Sympathetic Banjo Drone chain (resonator-feeder role).
- Sustain-maxed gives the resonator a LONG pitched note to lock onto; bass-side Tone keeps it a dark drone so the resonator self-selects a clean fundamental rather than a squealing partial.
- The Hizumitas compression character keeps the long sustained tail predictable rather than runaway even at max Sustain.
- Pairs best after a compressor/limiter (e.g. CB Clean 'Banjo Transient Tamer') that conditions the GK-5's all-attack/no-sustain transient first.
- **Honesty flag:** the knob positions here are a dialable recipe (DOUG's interpretation for the resonator-feeder role), not factory-published numbers. The only published Hizumitas sustain-max seed (Premier Guitar) uses Tone 8–10 for a cutting lead — this patch deliberately voices the opposite.
