# Shimmer/Hall Reverb Drone — textbook drone-doom

## Concept
The textbook drone-doom recipe: the Hizumitas's sustained fuzz wall into a hall/shimmer reverb. The sustained fuzz gives the reverb a stable input to spectrally process; downstream H90 algorithms (CrushedHall, BlackHole, MangledVerb) pair extremely well. The sustain is the load-bearing idea — it makes the reverb sound intentional rather than choppy, because the held wall never drops out from under the wash.

## How to play it
1. Set the Hizumitas to a sustaining wall voice (Sustain up; Tone bass-side for dark, noon for clarity).
2. Feed a hall or shimmer reverb after it (this rig's End Board: H90 CrushedHall / BlackHole / MangledVerb), long decay to taste.
3. Hold chords/notes — the long sustain gives the reverb a stable input to process.
4. Let the wash bloom; the sustain keeps it sounding intentional, not choppy.
