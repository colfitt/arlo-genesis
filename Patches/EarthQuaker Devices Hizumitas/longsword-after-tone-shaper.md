# Longsword After (gain backed off) — tone-shaping stage

## Concept
The rig-specific downstream move: stack the EAE Longsword high-gain distortion AFTER the Hizumitas, with the Longsword's gain backed well off (8–10 o'clock max) so it acts as a tone-shaping stage rather than additional gain. The Hizumitas already supplies the saturation; the Longsword over-saturates if left high, so instead of adding gain it becomes an EQ/voicing stage that reshapes the wall. Stacking a high-gain distortion after a Muff is destructive — but in a good way — provided the second pedal's gain is kept on the floor.

## How to play it
1. Set the Hizumitas to your wall/lead voice — it already supplies the saturation (dial from recipe; Volume/Sustain/Tone to taste).
2. Place the Longsword AFTER it (the rig's signal order).
3. Back the Longsword's gain well off — 8–10 o'clock MAX — so it shapes tone rather than adding gain.
4. Use it as a voicing/tone stage; expect over-saturation and mush if you leave its gain high.

- Complements the EQ-after fix (Brothers AM) — different downstream pedal, different role: the Brothers shapes upper mids clean-ish, the Longsword backed-off shapes the wall's distortion voicing.
