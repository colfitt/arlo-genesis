# Doom Wall (max heavy)

## Concept
Pure drone-doom — a sustained, oceanic single-chord wall with thundering low end. Everything maxed, with the Tone knob fully CW for maximum low-end heaviness (mids scooped, highs tamed). Full bass "immediately turns you into a Doom metal god." This is the tie-breaker patch for the post-punk / Swans crescendo lean, feeding Big Time + the End-board reverb.

## How to play it
1. Volume max, Sustain max, Tone fully CW (max bass).
2. Neck pickup — hit a chord and let it ring.
3. Needs amp volume for the feedback/sustain bloom to arrive.
4. Let the long dense tail blend straight into Big Time.

## Notes
- ⚠ Full-CW Tone here is deliberate — it keeps the wall *controllable* (the scooped/dark voicing tames the chaos).
- Avoid maxing Sustain AND a *CCW* Tone together (clarity loss / hiss / oscillation) — that combination is reserved for the Max Chaos patch where instability is the point.
- The long dense tail blends into Big Time without competing with the dry note — long sustain is what makes downstream granular/reverb sound intentional, not choppy.
- Ref: Post-punk / doom (Swans, Boris-adjacent walls).
