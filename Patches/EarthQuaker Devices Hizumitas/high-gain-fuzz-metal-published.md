# High-Gain Fuzz Metal (Guitar Chalk numbers)

## Concept
Extreme standalone fuzz saturation that still keeps enough clarity for tight metal riffing, with a heavy low-mid focus. Sustain is maxed but Tone is pushed to the bright side for cut and articulation under the gain — no front-pedal needed; the Hizumitas does the distortion work itself. The published numbers come from Guitar Chalk's settings table (Sustain 10, Tone 7, Volume 9 on a 0–10 scale).

## How to play it
1. Set Sustain 10, Tone 7, Volume 9 on a 0–10 dial.
2. Palm-mute chugs — Tone 7 keeps the attack defined under maxed Sustain.
3. Hot Volume (9) is well above unity; use as a boosted lead/rhythm slam.
4. Tighten further with a clean amp so the pedal does the distortion work.

## Notes
- Among the loudest Big Muff variants; Volume 9 will hammer the next stage.
- Reviewers repeatedly note chords keep note separation even near max Sustain.
- **Honesty — the NUMBERS are sourced (Guitar Chalk 0–10 table), the clock mapping is not.** Guitar Chalk/PG describe Tone CCW = treble; EQD's own copy says CW = bass / CCW = highs. Tone 7 is "bright side" per the source's labeling, so dial by ear toward treble if your knob is unmarked — don't trust an exact clock position.
- Distinct from Plumes-Front Metal Stack (a front-pedal recipe) — this is the standalone published-numbers metal voice.
