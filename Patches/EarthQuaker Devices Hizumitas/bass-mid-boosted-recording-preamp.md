# Bass Mid-Boosted Preamp — center-Tone bass voice

## Concept
Mixdown's documented bass voice: with Tone toward CENTER you get a mid-boosted voicing that works great with bass and Bass VI, especially for bass-led music — and it doubles as a studio recording preamp for distorted bass parts. The center Tone zone is the cut-through sweet spot that survives a mix. The Hizumitas source is robust enough to take active or passive pickups, 6/7/8-string guitar, or bass without complaint, and its stronger high-mids than a scooped Muff keep the low strings defined.

## How to play it
1. Set Tone to center (~12:00) for the mid-boosted, cut-through bass voicing.
2. Start Volume and Sustain near noon (Sustain moderate-high for thickness) and adjust to taste.
3. Play bass or Bass VI / VG-800 baritone — the mids cut through a dense mix and the low end stays defined.
4. Use it as a recording preamp for distorted bass parts straight into the interface; blend a clean DI for low-end retention.

## Notes
- Center Tone = the mid-boosted, cut-through zone per Mixdown's three-zone map (CCW = Iommi treble-booster, center = mid-boosted, CW = stupidly fat).
- **Honesty flag:** only the Tone DIRECTION (center) is sourced; Volume/Sustain are a dialable recipe with **no published values** for this specific bass voice (start near noon per the "all on 12" default; Sustain moderate-high for thickness).
- Source-agnostic: "doesn't give a crap" about active/passive pickups, 6/7/8-string, or bass — confirms the ~150kΩ input handles varied sources; community (TalkBass) corroborates rich/thick fuzz with low-end clarity.
- Distinct from Cliff-Burton Bass Fuzz (existing — treble-side Tone ~10:00, Sustain ~3:00); this is the CENTER-Tone mid-boosted preamp voice.
