# Sustain Max, Tone 8–10 — Thick Lows, Cutting Top (Premier Guitar user)

## Concept
A user lead recipe from the Premier Guitar review: Sustain all the way up with Tone in the 8–10 o'clock range. It gives a thick low end while the top end still cuts and individual notes stay clear — a saturated-but-legible lead/riff voice that demonstrates the pedal's articulation even at max gain.

## How to play it
1. Sustain all the way up, Tone between 8:00 and 10:00.
2. Set Volume around unity (~9:00) or higher to slam the chain.
3. The low end stays thick while the 8–10 o'clock Tone keeps the top cutting and notes clear.
4. Great for sustained leads and ringing power chords that need to read in a mix.

## Notes
- Demonstrates the pedal's articulation even at max gain — "notes are clear" even with Sustain maxed.
- Published clock positions: one of the few seeds with sourced Sustain + Tone values (the user's cited recommendation in the review). Volume is "to taste" — the ~9:00 figure is a near-unity starting point, not a published number.
- Distinct from the existing Balanced Mid-Forward Lead/Rhythm (Sustain noon / Tone ~10:00, EQD-quoted) — this maxes Sustain while keeping Tone low.
