# Long Modulated Delay Drone — canonical time pairing

## Concept
The canonical downstream pairing: the Hizumitas's long sustain feeds a long modulated delay, and the slow modulation breathes life into the sustained wall. In this rig that means CE-2W chorus into the Deco tape sim, or a Roland Space Echo (Wata's own foundation). The dense, sustained tail gives the delay something to chew on, so repeats *blend* with the dry note rather than competing with it.

## How to play it
1. Set the Hizumitas to a sustaining wall/lead voice — Sustain up, Tone to taste.
2. Feed a long modulated delay after it (Wata's foundation is a Roland tape echo; this rig: CE-2W → Deco, or Big Time).
3. Use long delay times — the dense tail means repeats blend with the dry signal rather than competing.
4. Let the slow modulation breathe life into the sustained wall.

## Notes
- The load-bearing idea here is the **long-modulated-delay-after architecture**, not specific knob positions.
- **Honesty flag:** no published Hizumitas knob values exist for this pairing — the controls above are a dialable recipe, not sourced settings. The only sourced settings are the *downstream* architecture and Wata's foundation.
- Wata's unchanging foundation is an '86 Les Paul Custom + the fuzz + a Roland tape echo — *"the core of my expression."*
- Long delay works because the Hizumitas's tail is dense enough that repeats don't compete with the dry signal.
