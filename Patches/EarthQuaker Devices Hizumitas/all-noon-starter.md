# All-Noon Starter

## Concept
The neutral anchor for the Hizumitas. All three knobs at noon gives aggressive saturation with smooth sustain and a punchy, mid-forward voice that cuts through a dense chain. Noon on Tone is the flattest spot — the mid-forward cut-through voicing — so this is the position you sweep Tone *away from* to find character.

## How to play it
1. Set Volume, Sustain, and Tone all to 12:00.
2. Engage the footswitch (relay true-bypass needs power to pass signal).
3. Pick a favorite power chord or riff and go.
4. Move OFF noon on Tone to find character — small moves make big shifts because Tone is inverted and touch-sensitive.

## Notes
- Genuinely useful default — punchy mids that survive a dense board.
- Min Sustain is NOT off — it is still a fuzzy overdrive (no clean floor), so this is not your subtle setting.
- Noon ≈ flattest Tone; CW from here = bass-boost/scooped/dark, CCW = treble-boost/near-RAT bright.
