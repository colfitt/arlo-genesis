# Tone Report Creamy Lead — humbucker drop-tuned

## Concept
Andy's Reverb/Tone Report demo voice: humbuckers with Sustain around 75%, then dropping the Sustain lower into the "really creamy" lead zone. Back the Tone off toward treble to let highs through while the low end stays round; raise Volume to fatten and to compensate for low-output pickups.

## How to play it
1. Set Volume near unity (~10:00) — past that is a clean boost.
2. Set Sustain ~75% for the demo voice, or roll it lower for the "creamy" lead zone.
3. Back the Tone off toward the treble (CCW) side to let highs through; low end stays round.
4. With low-output pickups, raise Volume to fatten the low end and compensate.

## Notes
- **Honesty caveat:** unity gain ~10:00 and Sustain ~75% are the demonstrator's spoken figures; the Tone is swept across range in the demo, so its exact position is a dialable recipe, not a published value.
- Demonstrated on humbuckers and drop-tuned riffs.
- Cleanup via guitar volume works unusually well for a Muff — especially on treble-bleed-equipped guitars.
- Absorbs the low-output-pickup fattening trick (raise Volume to make up for low-output pickups) — kept here rather than as a separate seed.
