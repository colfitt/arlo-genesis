# Shoegaze / Low-Gain Ambient

## Concept
TKOG's literal shoegaze patch — a bright, low-gain bloom for shoegaze beds: dynamic arpeggios and chords pushed into delay and reverb. TKOG's own note: "best with dynamic arpeggios or chords and delay/reverb." The shoegaze taste-cluster fit.

## How to play it
1. Play dynamic arpeggios or chords — the low gain lets picking dynamics bloom through.
2. To use this as voiced, relocate the Oxford ahead of the Hizumitas/Longsword so its EQ isn't collapsed by the fuzz wall.
3. Feed it into the End-board verbs and delays for the bed.

## Notes
- Bright + low-gain bloom is the whole point — keep gain low so dynamics survive.
- If left stacked in the 5th slot, the fixed-mid filter collapses the upstream fuzz wall into one honk, defeating the voicing — relocate ahead of the fuzzes to actually USE it as voiced.
- Re-trim Treble after any Contour move.
