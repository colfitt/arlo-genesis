# Banjo Focus (mid-forward, de-iced)

## Concept
Banjo (Gold Tone EBM-5 via GK-5 → VG-800) as a LEAD voice — chasing focus over fizz on an already-bright 2–4 kHz source. It sits IN a wall rather than cutting over it. The '88 voice's input re-filtering keeps it from getting harsh; asym clipping adds touch dynamics for expressive picking.

## How to play it
1. Use '88 voice + asym clipping (Si also works) for warmth and dynamic feel.
2. Pull Treble well down (9:00) to tame the banjo's 2–4 kHz transient spray; Contour mid-focused (8:00).
3. Keep gain low (9–10:00) — focus, not fizz.
4. For VG-800 baritone/modeled pads, use the sibling combo: '88 + LED, Gain 10:00, Bass 8:00, Contour 9:00, Treble 10:00 — the band-limiting eats the synthetic ice-pick where the fuzzes turn it to fizz.

## Notes
- Banjo-as-lead taste-lens fit. Leans darker than a generic modeled-source tame (Treble 9:00, Contour 8:00) for melodic banjo lines.
- The "EBM-5 piercing 2–4 kHz transient spray is the enemy" — the Oxford's inherent filtering eats the ice-pick.
- Manual: the filtering "prevents distortion becoming harsh/buzzy with complex inputs."
