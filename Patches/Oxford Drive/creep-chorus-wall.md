# Creep Chorus Wall

## Concept
The super-crunchy "Creep"-chorus ShredMaster brick, used as a BASE distortion ahead of the fuzzes for a drone/doom/shoegaze wall. Relocate the Oxford ahead of the Hizumitas/Longsword so the fuzzes pile onto it, turning a redundant 5th-slot pedal into a wall foundation. Inferred from the "Creep" (1993) chorus ShredMaster sound — NOT a quoted Greenwood patch.

## How to play it
1. Relocate the Oxford ahead of the Hizumitas/Longsword so it becomes the foundation the fuzzes stack onto.
2. Set Gain near flat-out (3:00–4:00), then back it off slightly per TKOG.
3. Contour at minimum (7:00) for max mids; Treble high at 2:00.
4. Keep Bass LOW (9:00) — do not mirror the "all knobs up" single-amp lore.

## Notes
- INFERRED, not quoted. There is NO published "Creep" Oxford knob setting; TKOG never documented one, and the HarryAndAGuitar 2021 Oxford "Creep" cover published no knobs. The lore is "turn all the knobs up."
- Adapted for THIS rig: Bass kept low (log taper + stacked) and moved to front-of-chain.
- Gain value is "near flat-out, then back off slightly" — keep that nuance; don't pin it.
