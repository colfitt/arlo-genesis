# Classic Overdrive

## Concept
The maker's set-and-forget baseline — the stock ShredMaster push for guitar or baritone at its lowest-gain, pushed-amp crunch. It's the reference patch that proves the low-Bass rule (Bass 8:00) holds even at low gain. Run it as-is into a clean front end.

## How to play it
1. Dial the settings as written: '91 voice, Si clipping, Gain noon, Bass low at 8:00.
2. Run into a clean amp or clean front end — this is the single-pedal reference.
3. Set Volume to taste; unity sits around noon.

## Notes
- The reference baseline. It demonstrates the low-Bass rule even at low gain.
- If left in the current 5th slot (stacked after the fuzzes), drop Bass further toward 7:00 so the downstream BF-3 isn't sweeping mud.
- Bass is logarithmic — keep it low for normal-OD bass cut; high Bass = mud, especially stacked.
- Re-trim Treble after any Contour move (Contour is a treble+mid tilt, not a mid knob).
