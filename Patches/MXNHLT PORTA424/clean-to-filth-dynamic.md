# Clean-to-Filth Dynamic

## Concept
The one written signature recipe for this box: stage the preamp at the edge of breakup so the picking hand modulates clean (soft attack) to gooey grit (dig in). It's the elastic lo-fi move — super responsive to pick attack, going from clean to filth with just your attack. Works on banjo and baritone too (soft plucks warm, hard attacks bloom).

## How to play it
1. Crank Input Trim near max (~3–4 o'clock) and turn the Channel Fader UP to ~85%.
2. Bring Master Volume down to compensate so the output sits roughly at unity.
3. Treble 11, Bass 1 o'clock.
4. Run at 9V for earlier breakup.
5. Ride your pick attack: soft = nearly clean, hard = blooms into grit. The dynamics live in the hand, not a knob.
6. Place at end OR front of chain.
