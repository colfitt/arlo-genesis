# Baritone Drone Terminus

## Concept
Prints sustained baritone (VG-800) drones to cassette so they bloom into grit on harder attacks while the low end stays firm and doesn't flub when the preamp clips. Doom/drone low-end weight at the terminus — sustained baritone blooming into grit on attack is the payoff.

## How to play it
1. Set Input Trim to 12–1 o'clock so sustained baritone blooms into grit on harder attacks.
2. Treble 11; shelve Bass slightly DOWN to 11–12 o'clock to stop the print mudding up.
3. Channel Fader ~80%, Master at unity.
4. Run 18V — essential here to keep the bottom from flubbing when the preamp clips.
5. Mono-sum the bus (a non-issue for low end); place at the end of chain.

## Notes
- 18V is load-bearing here, not optional — the difference between a firm drone and a flubby clipped low end.
- All clock/fader/voltage values are designed-to-spec for the baritone-weight taste lens.
