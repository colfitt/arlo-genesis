# NO DUB — pseudo-live micro-looper effect

## Concept
Turn the always-listening Micro-Looper into a near-live wet/granular effect rather than a sustaining loop. Flicking the **NO DUB (Refresh)** dip drops looper feedback to zero, so the loop refreshes instead of piling up — the channel tracks your current playing instead of holding old material. The higher the **CLOCK**, the more tightly it tracks. While overdubbing you can also move CLOCK to pin notes at the positions you played them but at different speeds, building harmonies. The result is a pseudo-real-time granular/pitch effect instead of a held loop.

## How to play it
1. Flick the **NO DUB (Refresh)** dip switch on.
2. Engage the Micro-Looper and **HOLD the RIGHT footswitch** to keep overdub engaged (set **LATCH** on so you don't have to keep holding it).
3. Turn **CLOCK** up — the higher it is, the more closely the looper tracks your live playing.
4. While overdubbing, freely move **CLOCK**: notes stay where you played them but play back at different speeds, building harmonies.
5. Play — the looper now behaves as a pseudo-live wet/granular effect (feedback at zero, refreshing) instead of a sustaining loop.

## Notes
- ⚠️ Overdub MUST stay engaged for NO DUB to do anything — without it, nothing changes.
- ⚠️ NO DUB and FADE are mutually exclusive: "no dub, no fade."
- In **Classic Mode** + NO DUB + overdub engaged + LATCH off you get the MkI "hold-to-record, release-to-play" behavior (in CLASSIC, NO DUB mutes loop output while overdubbing).
- No published knob values — CLOCK position is a dial-by-feel tradeoff between tracking tightness and grit. Treat the controls above as a recipe, not sourced settings.
