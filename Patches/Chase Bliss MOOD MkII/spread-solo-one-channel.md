# SPREAD SOLO — spread one channel only

## Concept
SPREAD normally scatters/ping-pongs both channels in stereo at once — all or nothing. This hidden option lets you apply SPREAD to **just one channel** while the other stays centered. Keep a mono/centered micro-loop legible but run it through a ping-pong **Delay**, or ping-pong the **Reverb** while **Stretch** stays a normal centered stereo. With the SPREAD dip on, enter hidden options (hold both footswitches until green) and move the **ROUTING** toggle toward the channel you want spread; the **MIDDLE** position spreads both (the default behavior).

## How to play it
1. Engage the **SPREAD** dip.
2. **Hold both footswitches** until the LEDs go green (hidden-options mode).
3. Move the **ROUTING** toggle toward the channel you want spread (MIDDLE = both get spread).
4. Exit; now only the chosen channel ping-pongs/scatters while the other stays centered — e.g. a mono micro-loop passed through a ping-pong delay.

## Notes
- Great for keeping a captured loop legible/centered while the wet effect animates around it.
- MIDDLE position spreads both channels (this is the default).
- Documented example: keep a mono micro-loop but pass it through a ping-pong delay.
- Honesty: this is a routing/toggle recipe — there are no published knob values to dial. The community walkthrough notes the spread side can also be set via the wet **MODE** toggle; flipping ROUTING toward a channel is the manual-documented path. Verify by ear which channel scatters.
