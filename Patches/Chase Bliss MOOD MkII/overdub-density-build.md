# Overdub Density Build — stack the micro-loop

## Concept
Use the MkII's new overdubbing to pile sound after sound onto a captured micro-loop, building massive evolving textures that were impossible on V1. Capture a loop with a tap of the **RIGHT footswitch**, then **HOLD** that footswitch to overdub more material on top — with **LATCH** on it stays engaged hands-free. The key move is that you can switch the looper **MODE** toggle between **Env / Tape / Stretch** *after* capture without erasing the loop, so each overdub pass can be a different engine. Layer a few **Stretch** passes especially and you "turn just about anything into an infinitely interesting texture."

## How to play it
1. Capture a micro-loop with a tap of the **RIGHT footswitch**.
2. **HOLD** the **RIGHT footswitch** (or set **LATCH** on) to engage overdub and play more material onto the loop.
3. Between passes, flip the looper **MODE** toggle to a different engine (**Tape ⇄ Stretch ⇄ Env**) — the loop is not erased.
4. Layer several passes (a few **Stretch** overdubs especially) to build a dense, infinitely evolving bed.

## Notes
- Overdubbing + Stretch is the documented route to "turn just about anything into an infinitely interesting texture."
- Switching loop modes after capture does NOT erase the loop — exploit that for multi-engine layers.
- Add **FADE** (hidden) for overdubs that slowly evolve/replace; remember **FADE** and **NO DUB** are mutually exclusive.
- ⚠️ No published knob values — CLOCK, MODIFY, and MIX positions are dial-by-feel per pass. Treat the controls above as a recipe, not sourced settings.
