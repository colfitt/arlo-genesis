# LATCH — hands-free freeze / overdub

## Concept
Turn the HOLD function of each footswitch into a latch instead of a momentary action. With the **LATCH** dip on, a long-press (HOLD) on the **LEFT** footswitch freezes the wet channel — or a HOLD on the **RIGHT** footswitch engages overdub — and it stays engaged after you lift your foot. Long-press the same footswitch again to deactivate. Without LATCH the hold is momentary: the freeze or overdub stops the moment you release. This is the difference between performing over a sustained frozen pad with both hands free and having to pin a switch down through the entire passage.

## How to play it
1. Flick the **LATCH** dip switch on.
2. HOLD the **LEFT** footswitch to freeze the wet (or **RIGHT** to overdub) — it now stays engaged when you lift your foot.
3. Play freely over the latched freeze, or keep building overdubs hands-free.
4. Long-press the same footswitch again to deactivate the latched state.

## Notes
- Without LATCH the hold is momentary — the freeze/overdub stops "as soon as I let go." LATCH is the practical choice for performance "so you don't have to hold it down the whole time."
- Pairs with **NO DUB** (which requires overdub left engaged — LATCH keeps the RIGHT footswitch on for you) and with overdub-density build patches.
- This is a binary on/off dip toggle, not a continuous knob — there are no numeric settings here, so nothing in this patch is an unsourced dial position.
