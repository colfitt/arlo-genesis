# Spiritbox Texture Wall (MOOD + Microcosm + Gen Loss)

## Concept
Mike Stringer of Spiritbox runs the same MOOD + Hologram Microcosm + Generation Loss texture trio this rig uses — "cleanly arpeggiating lines that flutter over dozens of ambience tracks and reverb tails" stacked into "an otherworldly dense expanse of down-tuned guitar." The takeaway is the **stack/approach** ("Microcosm generates, MOOD warps") more than any single patch: feed MOOD an already-textured, down-tuned source, freeze a heavy reverb smear into a pad, and stretch a micro-loop underneath. Proof the MOOD/Microcosm/Gen-Loss stack scales from ambient to a heavy/doom wall. Dial from the recipe — no published Stringer knob values exist.

## How to play it
1. Feed MOOD a down-tuned, already-textured source — a Microcosm-generated bed and/or Generation Loss tape wobble upstream.
2. Set Wet MODE = **Reverb** with heavy smear; roll **CLOCK** back for degradation.
3. Freeze the wet into a pad (**HOLD the LEFT footswitch**) and capture/stretch a micro-loop underneath (Looper MODE = **Stretch** or **Tape**).
4. Play cleanly arpeggiating lines over the top so they flutter across the dense ambience bed.

## Notes
- 🟣 Designed-to-emulate: Stringer is a documented MkII user running the same texture trio, but **NO published knob settings exist** — every value above is a dialable recipe, not a sourced setting.
- The takeaway is the stack/approach ("Microcosm generates, MOOD warps") more than any single patch.
- Common writeup error: do **not** conflate Microcosm's Micro/Judder/Sustain modes with MOOD's Reverb/Delay/Slip (Wet) + Env/Tape/Stretch (Looper) — they are separate pedals with separate mode sets.
