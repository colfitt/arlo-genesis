# Clock-Decay Character Shift

## Concept
CLOCK is tone, time and quality in one knob. Play a single note and, **while the reverb decays, turn CLOCK down (CCW)** — the character of the tail changes as you sweep. Play another note and it sounds different again. Continue CCW (toward 4k/3k/2k) and "things get real textural." This is the single most impactful gesture on the pedal.

## How to play it
1. Set Wet MODE = **Reverb**, TIME up enough to sustain, MODIFY wherever you like (CW = washed, CCW = distinct taps).
2. Play a single note.
3. While the reverb tail decays, **turn CLOCK down (CCW)** — listen to the character shift.
4. Play another note; continue CCW toward 4k/3k/2k for heavy texture.

## Notes
- Low CLOCK = heavy aliasing/downsampling ("computer noises"); high = hi-fi. CLOCK scale: 2k…3k…4k…6k…(12k noon)…16k…24k…32k…48k…64k.
- Lowering 64k→32k half-speeds both channels together.
- Flick the **SMOOTH** dip for a continuous bend instead of stepped 4ths/5ths.
