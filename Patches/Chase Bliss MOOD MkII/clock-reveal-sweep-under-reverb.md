# Clock Reveal — sweep CLOCK live under a decaying note

## Concept
This is the manual's explicit single-note reveal exercise. Play one note into the **Reverb** and, while the tail decays, slowly rotate **CLOCK** counter-clockwise from a high (hi-fi) setting down through 12k (noon) → 6k → 4k → 3k → 2k. The character morphs dramatically — pure to crunchy, then on into heavy aliasing/downsampling that the manual calls "real textural" and "computer noises." Because CLOCK is "tone, length, and quality all in one," the same sweep also changes loop length/resolution, but with Wet MODE = Reverb the headline effect is hearing the sample rate fall in real time.

## How to play it
1. Set **Wet MODE = Reverb** and start with **CLOCK** high (hi-fi).
2. Play a single sustaining note or chord.
3. While the reverb tail rings out, slowly turn **CLOCK** counter-clockwise.
4. Play another note and notice how much the character has changed; continue CCW for heavy aliasing / "computer noises." (Flick **SMOOTH** for a continuous bend instead of stepped 4ths/5ths.)

## Notes
- The CLOCK scale numbers (2k…64k) and the live-sweep gesture are documented directly in the manual — these are sourced, not invented.
- CLOCK is "tone, length, and quality all in one," so this sweep also changes loop length/resolution, not just brightness.
- Low CLOCK = heavy aliasing/downsampling ("computer noises"); high = pure/hi-fi. Community reports CLOCK moves in perfect 4ths/5ths unless SMOOTH is on, and goes grittily lo-fi without tape-style hiss.
- Differs from the existing clock-decay patch by being the manual's explicit single-note reveal exercise; pairs with SMOOTH for pitch-bend sweeps.
