# "Late Bloomer" — delayed-onset bloom

## Concept
A lush trail that hangs just behind the notes without overpowering the dry. High pre-delay means the reverb arrives well after the note, and a long Length stretches the bloom — so even at a relatively high ~3:00 Mix the dry isn't buried, because the wet shows up late. On-aesthetic for swells that bloom after the note stops.

## How to play it
1. Play sparse parts; the bloom fills the gaps after each note.
2. Raise Feedback and DECAY together for a bigger, more ambient result.

## Notes
- Values are relative (high pre-delay / long length / ~3:00 mix), not all numeric.
