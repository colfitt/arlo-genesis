# Trippy Frozen Drone Wall (Nonlinear Ramp)

## Concept
The single best parameter-level drone-wall workflow on disk. Nonlinear set to the RAMP shape, with Time, Feedback, and Mix all maxed and a very dark Tone, builds a sustained doom/shoegaze bed under baritone, banjo, or the VG-800. The second reverb underneath (Late Decay/Late Level) is kept small so the wall stays focused. Freeze locks the chord so you can play over the top.

## How to play it
1. Swell chords in with the guitar VOLUME knob so the attack disappears into the wall.
2. Freeze the first chord (HOLD = FREEZE).
3. Loop it on a looper placed *after* the Big Sky, then jam over the captured bed.
4. Turn DECAY (= Time) live for morphing motion.
5. Space chords far apart in the harmonization — long Nonlinear tails go dissonant fast.

## Notes
- On Nonlinear the front knobs remap: PRE-DELAY → Feedback, DECAY → Time.
- Dark Tone tames the baritone's metallic long-tail buildup.
- Keep Mod Speed very slow to avoid a fast/seasick wobble.
- Values are verbal-explicit (maxed / dark / slow), not clock positions — the source stated character, not exact dials.
