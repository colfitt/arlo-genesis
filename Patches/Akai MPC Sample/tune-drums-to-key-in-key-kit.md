# Tune-Drums-to-Key — In-Key Kit

## Concept
The most actionable "better drum samples" move: tune the whole kit to one song key so it sounds cohesive and musical, and tie the low end to the rig's baritone sub-bed. The AC50 has no tuner, so pitch is analyzed off-device; only the semitone/cent result lands on the box. ±24 st / ±90 ¢ are real AC50 ranges; the frequency targets are the craft method.

## How to play it
1. Analyze pitch off-device (free Voxengo SPAN High-Res + Hold, or FabFilter Pro-Q).
2. On the box: Tune page → Semi (−24…+24) to the nearest in-key note, Fine (−90…+90 ¢) for cents.
3. Targets: kick fundamental ~50–90 Hz, harmonic thump 150–250 Hz; snare ~E3–B3 (~170–200 Hz). In C minor: kick = C, snare = D♯ (minor 3rd).
4. Keep Warp = Off so length doesn't change.
5. Tune the baritone sub-bed to the SAME root as the kick so they reinforce instead of fight.

## Notes
- ±24 st / ±90 ¢ are real AC50 ranges; the frequency targets are the craft method.
- Only the semitone/cent result goes on the box.
