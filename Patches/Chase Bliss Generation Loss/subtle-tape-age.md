# Subtle Tape Age

## Concept
The manual's own first-demo "nice and wistful" voice — a near-always-on tape patina that sits under banjo-lead and baritone drone. This is the general-purpose degrade you start from. Model 4–5 roll the banjo's piercing top and smear staccato into sustain, and everything downstream (the Big Time repeats) inherits the patina.

## How to play it
1. Recall onboard preset **L** (your home base — also recallable via PC in the cb-stack scene, MIDI ch.2).
2. Leave it on under everything; the degrade is meant to be a constant, low-level patina rather than an effect you toggle.
3. A little goes a long way — resist the urge to push the knobs.

## Notes
- This is the home-base scene; store it to onboard preset **L**.
- Knob positions are the manual's labeled "nice and wistful" starting points distilled to clock-face by the researcher — a faithful reading of intent, not factory-recallable numbers. On recall the pedal restores its real saved state regardless.
- Rig placement: Gen Loss is the Board 3 opener; it breaks the source before Big Time → MOOD → Blooper → Chroma → H90, so the patina propagates through the whole tail.
