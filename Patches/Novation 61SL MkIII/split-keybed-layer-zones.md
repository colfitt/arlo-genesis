# Split-Keybed Layer (Zones) — bass low / lead-banjo up top

## Concept
Play two voices from one keybed at once — a low baritone/bass voice in the low octave and a lead voice (route to a VG-800 banjo model = banjo-as-lead) up top; or overlap the zones for a layered drone. The "one keyboard, whole arrangement" performance patch, mapping the rig's two signature VG voices onto one keybed: baritone weight under a banjo lead.

## How to play it
1. ZONES: Shift+Zones to enable; press Zones to edit — up to 8 zones, 14 params/zone over 2 pages.
2. ZONE1 (low): Dest. Part = Part1 (e.g. VG-800 baritone model on its DIN/Ch), Low Key = C1, High Key = B2 (set from keyboard: select zone → press its soft button twice → play low then high key), Octave Offset to taste.
3. ZONE2 (high): Dest. Part = Part2 (e.g. VG-800 banjo model / a soft-synth lead), Low Key = C3, High Key = C6.
4. PER-ZONE (page 2): enable Pitch/Mod wheels + Channel Pressure per zone (mod wheel + AT ON for the lead zone only).
5. OVERLAP for layering: extend both zones across the same range — lowest-numbered Zone wins on overlap, but both still sound = a stacked drone.
6. SCALES: On + Snap applies to keys AND any sequenced Parts. Each Part routes to its own Channel/Destination so the split feeds two pieces of the rig.
