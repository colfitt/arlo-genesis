# Subtle Always-On Tape Print

## Concept
The default permanent print that sits under everything: rubbery tape-head compression and faint channel grain, with no audible dirt. This is the set-and-leave baseline voice of the whole rig — the terminus where the stereo bus mono-commits into the Apollo. Gain 1 (Trim) where the dirt is born stays low at 9:00; Gain 2 (Channel) sits gently at 10:00, well below the gate; Volume is master-only and adds no drive.

## How to play it
1. Take the XLR out into the Apollo near unity. Volume sets converter level only — it adds no drive.
2. Keep the ground-lift dip switch on hand; engage it if the rack hums.
3. Mono-commit the stereo bus here. Leave it on.
4. Let the upstream MXNHLT PORTA424 carry the tone; this stage just finishes it.

## Notes
- This is the rig-default per the always-on tag.
- Keep Gain 2 well below 3:00 so nothing gates.
- No factory clock-face settings exist for this pedal — these are community/directional positions built from JHS's verbatim cascading-gain concept; treat as starting points, not exact.
