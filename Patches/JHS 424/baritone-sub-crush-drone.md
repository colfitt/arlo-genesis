# Baritone Sub-Crush Drone

## Concept
The doom/post-punk low crush: a baritone Jazzmaster (or VG-800 baritone model) printed through the cassette channel for that near-breakup tape-head texture on the low strings, sustained as a wall. Gain 1 brings the input saturation; Gain 2 stays under the 3:00 gate so the sub-drone sustains instead of chopping. Pairs shockingly well with octave/sub upstream for "Moog-style subs / tape-crushed synth fuzz."

## How to play it
1. Print the baritone through the cassette channel with Gain 1 at 12:00–1:00 for the near-breakup low-string texture.
2. Keep Gain 2 at 1:30, under the gate, so the low drone sustains.
3. Pull Bass back noticeably (10:00) — baritone flubs fast at this gain.
4. Stack octave/sub upstream for Moog-style subs and tape-crushed synth fuzz. Run mono, XLR to the Apollo.

## Notes
- The Bass knob is the trap — at this gain it flubs fast, so it runs LOWER than instinct.
- Gain 2 stays under the gate so the low drone sustains rather than chops.
- Designed/inferred — NOT an artist setting. No factory clocks exist.
