# Banjo-as-Lead Drone Glue

## Concept
Finishing the GK-5 banjo (EBM-5 → VG-800) when it carries the lead inside a sustained bed: rubbery compression and faint grain glue it into the wall without the transients turning spitty or gated. Because the banjo arrives near line level via the VG-800, Gain 1 starts modest; Gain 2 stays well under 3:00 so the lead banjo rings instead of choking. Treble is the make-or-break clarity control here — the banjo's pierce is long gone by this stage.

## How to play it
1. VG-800 is near line level — start Gain 1 lower (10:00) than for a passive guitar; back off if it clips.
2. Keep Gain 2 modest (11:00) so banjo transients don't gate or spit against the wall.
3. Use Treble (1:30) to claw articulation back through the cassette murk; tighten the bottom with Bass (11).
4. Run mono, XLR to the Apollo.

## Notes
- Gain 2 deliberately well under 3:00 so the lead banjo rings instead of choking.
- Treble is the clarity make-or-break here.
