# Gueringer 50s/60s Slap + Saturation

## Concept
Rob Gueringer's published recipe: saturation maxed in cassette voice, with a bounce double kicked into the slapback region — the iconic 50s/60s overdrive-plus-slapback. The only player with fully published knob numbers for the Deco. Off-aesthetic for doom but the clearest artist data point; cassette + slap makes a warmer banjo-lead voice.

## How to play it
1. Engage Tape Saturation and Doubletracker.
2. Saturation maxed, Voice cassette, Volume just under noon, Tone just above noon.
3. Doubletracker: Blend at noon, Wobble very low (~7:00 — nearly off but audibly wobbling on sustained notes), Type bounce.
4. Set Lag Time into the slapback region (~1:00) — he "kicks in slapback."
5. Hold Tape Saturation ON, turn BLEND to set Doubletracker Boost/Cut to +1 to +3 dB so the slap doesn't sound weak.

## Notes
- The exact Lag Time value is a completion of his stated "slapback" (flagged designed/approximate).
- Type bounce is his stated favorite — most pronounced.
- Gain device: Volume sits just under noon to stay near unity given maxed saturation.
