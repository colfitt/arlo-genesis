# Pretty Tape Flange

## Concept
The clean musical 1970s tape flange across a chord — a moving comb-filter sweep, the on-aesthetic shoegaze shimmer. Invert is Strymon's factory choice for its signature tonality. Through-zero flanging gets MORE intense on a saturated/high-bandwidth input, so feed it the dirt wall and it sings on the saturated banjo/baritone source.

## How to play it
1. Engage Tape Saturation (light) and Doubletracker.
2. Voice classic, Type invert, Lag Time ~9–10:00 (flange region), Blend at noon (50/50 = strongest flange), Wobble low-to-mid (~10–11:00).
3. Keep Saturation light (~10:00) — but the more saturated/high-bandwidth the input, the more intense the through-zero.
4. No-dropout flange: keep Blend at noon but nudge Lag Time just PAST zero so the Lag Deck approaches but doesn't cross (avoids full cancellation).

## Notes
- Factory-named preset; clock-faces from the Kaleidoscope walkthrough + Celi's method.
- Through-zero intensity scales with input bandwidth — this is why it shines on the saturated source.
