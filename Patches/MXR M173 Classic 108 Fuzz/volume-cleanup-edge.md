# Volume-cleanup edge (passive guitar)

## Concept
A decongested, glassy edge-of-fuzz that dirties as you turn up — for intimate, dynamic playing. Fuzz low and output at unity; then roll the passive guitar's VOLUME pot down for near-clean, up for dirty. Silicon doesn't go fully clean but "cleans up quite a bit" (Analog Man). BUFFER ON keeps life at low gain (SuperFunAwesome: low gain wants buffer ON).

## How to play it
1. OUTPUT/VOLUME 12 o'clock.
2. FUZZ/INPUT 7–9 o'clock (low).
3. BUFFER ON.
4. Roll the passive guitar's VOLUME pot down for near-clean, up for dirty.

## Notes
- **HARD RIG CAVEAT** — the volume-pot cleanup is **unavailable** from the GK-5/VG-800/Colour Box path (no passive pot in the loop; a buffer decouples the trick — screaminfx confirms cleanup "requires NO buffer between guitar and fuzz").
- Only works with a passive SG / MIJ Jazzmaster plugged into the FRONT of Board 1 ahead of all buffers (or a passive-pickup-simulator pedal in front).
- Low signature-fit (clean-leaning), but it's the essential Fuzz Face party trick — the gateway to the real BC108 character the GK path otherwise mutes. Inferred assembly of a documented mechanism — verify by ear.
