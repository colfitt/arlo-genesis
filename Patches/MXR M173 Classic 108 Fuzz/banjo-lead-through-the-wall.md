# Banjo lead through the wall (EBM-5)

## Concept
The Gold Tone EBM-5 banjo (via GK-5/VG-800) cast as a single-note **lead voice cutting through the doom wall** — banjo-as-lead per the taste lens. The EBM-5's fast, bright, low-mass signal is the hardest case for a tone-control-less fuzz: the 108 **cannot darken on its own**, so the entire EQ rescue lives downstream. Realistically the 108→Hizumitas pair is the unit for banjo.

## How to play it
1. **108:** OUTPUT/VOLUME 12 o'clock, FUZZ/INPUT 12–3 o'clock, BUFFER OFF (darker/fuller to fight the banjo's ice-pick).
2. **Then Hizumitas Tone** 1–2 o'clock (bass side) + a **darker VG-800 cab model**.
3. Optionally add a low-gain drive (Brothers AM) AFTER the 108 with treble rolled off (the guitarTONEoverload taming method).
4. A/B BUFFER ON if it gets too dark/lifeless.

## Notes
- **BUFFER OFF here contradicts the usual rig default** — chosen specifically to darken the bright source (the buffer is the brightness lever; OFF = darker/fuller per Premier Guitar).
- No documentation of a 108 on banjo — inferred, verify by ear.
