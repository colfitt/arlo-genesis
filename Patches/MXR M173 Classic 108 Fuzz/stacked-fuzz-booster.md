# Stacked Fuzz Booster

## Concept
The 108 as a **low-fuzz, high-output PRE-stage** — fuzz down, volume up — to push more gain downstream. This is the closest published analog to its 108→Hizumitas role: a grind-and-level pre-stage that feeds the doom wall, driving the Hizumitas harder for more sustain. The Pedal Picassos independently confirm the move: "FUZZ all the way down and VOLUME all the way up… push the front of your amp… super awesome."

## How to play it
1. OUTPUT/VOLUME high (~3 o'clock, 8/10).
2. FUZZ/INPUT low (~8–9 o'clock, 2/10).
3. BUFFER ON.
4. Feed the Hizumitas (or a dirty amp / the VG-800 model) and let the high output drive it into grit.

## Notes
- Setting derived from the Mini M296 (identical BC108 circuit).
- Low-fuzz/high-output is specifically the grind-and-level pre-stage — use the output as a boost into the next gain stage, not for the 108's own saturation.
