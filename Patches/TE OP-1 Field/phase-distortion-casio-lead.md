# Phase — Casio-CZ phase-distortion lead

## Concept

The Phase engine is a Casio-CZ-style phase-distortion synth — smooth but capable of aggression. Phase distortion was Casio's mid-80s answer to Yamaha FM: a similar idea with a smoother result. Here it's dialed into a focused, glassy 80s lead reminiscent of Casio CZ phase-distortion tones, deliberately distinct from the FM engine's bell character. Moderate phase distortion gives harmonic bite, a band-pass in the mids keeps the lead focused, and modest resonance adds a glassy peak.

## How to play it

1. Select **Phase** via **[Shift]+T1**.
2. Increase phase distortion (Blue) until the tone gains harmonic edge.
3. Place the band-pass band in the mids for a focused lead.
4. Add resonance sparingly for a glassy peak.
5. Use a short envelope, plus a touch of **Spring** or **CWO** for vintage character.

## Notes

- Phase distortion was Casio's mid-80s answer to Yamaha FM — similar idea, smoother result.
- Goes from smooth to aggressive depending on the distortion amount; dial the Blue/Ochre amount to taste.
- **No published values.** The engine description and band-pass-filter behavior come from the magazinmehatronika OP-1 Field review; exact knob positions are not published. Treat all per-knob values above as a dialable recipe, not sourced numbers.
