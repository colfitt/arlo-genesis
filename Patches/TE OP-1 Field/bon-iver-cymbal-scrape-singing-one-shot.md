# Bon Iver — cymbal-scrape singing one-shot

## Concept

Turn a weird percussive gesture into a melodic "singing" voice across the keys — the documented blueprint for the banjo-roll-into-sampler move. On Bon Iver's "33 GOD," Joe Westerlund scraped a stick tip along a cymbal, sampled it, and played it chromatically until it sang. The rig swap: a banjo roll or a fuzz-wall hit instead of the cymbal.

## How to play it

1. Sample ONE percussive one-shot into the **Sampler** engine.
2. Play it chromatically across the keys until the gesture becomes a melodic voice.
3. Try a banjo roll or a fuzz-wall hit as the source for rig-native versions.
