# Bon Iver — sped-up choir reverb-wash

## Concept

A sped-up vocal/choir sample as an ambient reverb wash — serving the sustained-wall / shoegaze aesthetic. On "33 GOD," Bon Iver used a sampled Staves choir played back sped up as an end-of-track reverb wash. Rig swap: a banjo drone or baritone instead of the choir.

## How to play it

1. Sample a choir/vocal into the **Sampler** engine.
2. Play it back **SPED UP** (sampler pitch-up or tape varispeed).
3. Use it as an end-of-track reverb wash / vocal halo.

## Notes

- REAL cited artist technique; method-level. Varispeed direction = UP.
- Bon Iver / Sufjan vocal-halo cluster. Save as `wash-up`.
