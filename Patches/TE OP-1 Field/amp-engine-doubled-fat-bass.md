# Amp engine — doubled fat bass (comp up, tone down)

## Concept

Ollie Loops' bass treatment through the amp engine: record the same bass riff twice through the amp to thicken it, with high compression and lowered tone for a round, dense low end — an on-box bass-double before anything leaves the unit. Density comes from stacking the same performance to one mono tape track rather than width.

## How to play it

1. Open the amp engine with bass into the 3.5mm input (MONO, gain ~60-70%).
2. Set COMPRESSION high and TONE low (CCW) for a round, dense bass.
3. Record the riff to tape using RECORD+NOTE to land on beat one.
4. Overdub the SAME riff again on the same track to thicken.
5. Tweak distortion lightly for grit; watch the master compressor for double-compression.

## Notes

- Dialable recipe (no published values) — exact encoder positions are to-taste; only the method (high compression, lowered tone, doubled pass) is documented (Ollie Loops bass example).
- Overdub stacks rather than overwrites; UNDO (`[Tape]`+LEFT) covers slips.
- Pairs with the wide stereo double if you want width instead of mono density.
