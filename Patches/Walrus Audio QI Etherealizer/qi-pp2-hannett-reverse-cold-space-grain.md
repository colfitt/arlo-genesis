# QI-PP2 — Hannett Reverse / Cold-Space Grain (backwards guitar)

## Concept
The backwards-guitar move that opens 'New Dawn Fades' kept cold and small — reverse grains over a dry-forward source, so it stays separated and the Dark Star DS-PP1 owns the predelayed cold space. This is the LITERAL reverse (granular, not a captured-and-reversed take). Hannett's space is clinical, not warm — keep Tone fairly open (Ref: Joy Division 'New Dawn Fades' + Hannett cold artificial space).

## How to play it
1. Set Flow to **Parallel**, grain to **Grain Cloud**, Playback **REVERSE** (Mix ~1:00, X ~11:00).
2. Chorus off; Delay Mix ~9:00, Feedback low.
3. Keep Space modest (~10–11:00) and COLD — let the Dark Star own the space.
4. Keep Tone fairly OPEN (~1:00) — clinical, not warm.

## Notes
- 🟣 This is the LITERAL reverse (granular, not a captured-and-reversed take).
- Don't stack two big reverbs — let the Dark Star DS-PP1 do the room.
- APPROXIMATION: JD's reverse is a tape-reversed guitar into an AMS rack; the rig chases the result.
