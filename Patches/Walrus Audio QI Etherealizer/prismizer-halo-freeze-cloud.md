# Prismizer Halo Freeze Cloud

## Concept
The hardware approximation of the Bon Iver/Prismizer vocal halo — a pitched harmony cloud around a held banjo/voice note that you play a lead over. The +1-oct x2 grain is the bright prism-up layer; the Tri chorus depth is the slur/glide stand-in for the Prismizer's long glide. The QI is the named hardware approximation of the halo (Ref: Bon Iver '22 (OVER S∞∞N)' / '715 - CRΣΣKS').

## How to play it
1. Set Flow to **Parallel**, grain to **Grain Cloud** (Mix 1–2:00, X ~11:00, Playback x2).
2. Dial the Tri chorus (Mix ~11:00, Rate 9, Depth 11) as the glide stand-in.
3. Play a chord/banjo roll → **short-press Freeze** = grain cloud loops as a pitched cloud around the held note.
4. Play the lead over it.
5. For the "715 hit-it-harder" distortion, push the source hot before the (padded) input.

## Notes
- 🟣⭐ APPROXIMATION FLAG: a fixed pitched grain cloud, NOT the real Prismizer's MIDI-played, formant-shifted 4-note harmony — chases the RESULT not the METHOD.
- RIG-CRITICAL: pad/reamp the VG-800 line level (no level switch — it clips hot).
