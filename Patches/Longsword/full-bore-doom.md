# Full Bore Doom

## Concept
A maxed standalone doom wall in Mastodon/Down/COC territory. Drive high, the Boost footswitch ON, SHIFT up (1 kHz) with the Mid fully clockwise for a "brutal, in-your-face" cut, and silicon clipping for the squared brick. The owner called it "by far the best heavy tone I've achieved with a pedal." The Boost at high gain gets "almost fuzzy" while staying defined.

## How to play it
1. DIODE down (silicon), SHIFT up (1 kHz), Mid maxed (fully clockwise).
2. Drive HIGH; kick the Boost footswitch ON.
3. Pull Low back if the bottom flubs; set High to taste.
4. Rebalance Level by ear after engaging the Boost.

## Notes
- Real owner setting (forum user, not an artist endorser).
- Rig translation: a hot VG-800/baritone source clips earlier than the owner's passive Les Paul, so "high" Drive may sit a touch lower here.
- The Boost adds gain AND a fixed 300 Hz front-end low cut — it tightens as it intensifies.
