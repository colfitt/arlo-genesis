# Digital Rubble (Bit-Crush)

## Concept
Heavy sample-rate-reduction grit — the "recorded-wrong" bit-crushed voice (everyone calls it bit-crush; technically sample-rate reduction). For lo-fi degraded beds and crunchy aliased delay tails. With Dissolve well below noon you get heavy sample-rate crush (further CCW = more aliasing); baritone low fundamentals survive the crush as satisfying grit. Filter LP is the documented antidote to the harsh aliased highs before downstream granular processing.

## How to play it
1. Set global defaults: Routing Stereo, Trails ON.
2. Pull Dissolve down to 8:00–9:00 (well below noon) for heavy crush; turn further CCW for more aliasing.
3. Tame the harsh aliased highs with Filter LP at 1:00.
4. Keep Chance at noon (constant) and Glitch at 11:00 (fixed clock).
5. Keep Mix Standard around noon so the dry baritone fundamental stays intact under the rubble.
6. Save to Preset 3.
