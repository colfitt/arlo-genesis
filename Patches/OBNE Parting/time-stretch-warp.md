# Time-Stretch / Warp

## Concept
Warped blinks in the time-space continuum — Russo Music's (Eric Lapp) "time-stretching" voice. With Glitch above noon the clock is randomly reassigned (half / normal / double) plus random sample-rate halving each LFO cycle — the pedal at its most unpredictably glitchy, octave-jumping and stuttering. Smear at noon smears the pitch-shifting glitch INTO the reverb, which Emily Hopkins notes no other pedal does. Rate sets how often it "rolls the dice."

## How to play it
1. Set global defaults: Routing Stereo, Trails ON.
2. Push Glitch above noon (2:00) so the clock is randomly reassigned and sample-rate randomly halved each cycle (octave jumps).
3. Dissolve at 1:00–2:00 (reverse) for backwards trails under the glitch.
4. Keep Chance noon–1:00 (constant → slight feedback); Rate at noon sets how often it rolls the dice.
5. Set Smear at noon to smear the pitch-shifting glitch into the reverb — Emily's stated unique move.
6. Shape Random Sine for maximum unpredictability.
