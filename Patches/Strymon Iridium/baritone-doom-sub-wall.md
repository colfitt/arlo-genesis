# Baritone Doom Sub-Wall

## Concept
Low, heavy baritone-weight wall — the doom/drone bottom end when you want the REAL instrument rather than a VG-800 model of it. Baritone Jazzmaster MAGNETIC pickups (NOT the GK path) into the Plexi (Punch), whose mids carry the low fundamentals, through the bright 8×12 cab c that deliberately offsets the dark source. A sub-octave foundation under the Punch Wall / Stereo Drone Pad.

## How to play it
1. AMP punch (or round for a cleaner sub-bloom), CAB c; DRIVE 1–2:00, BASS pulled back to ~10:00 (the key move), MIDDLE 10:00, TREBLE 1:00.
2. ROOM 1:00, SIZE LARGE.
3. INPUT LEVEL = Instrument, OUTPUT MODE = Normal, rear = STEREO.
4. Back off DRIVE for a cleaner sub; dig in for Plexi grind without total mush.
5. For the saturated baritone wall, feed a fuzz (Hizumitas / MXR 108) in front.

## Notes
- DESIGNED patch, clock-face values.
- BASS pulled back is the key move — full BASS flubs on a low-tuned baritone.
- The bright 8×12 cab c deliberately offsets the dark baritone source.
- Real magnetic baritone, NOT the GK/VG-800 path.
