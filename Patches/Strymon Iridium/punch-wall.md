# Punch Wall (doom/shoegaze direct)

## Concept
The repo's signature doom recipe — a direct doom/sludge/shoegaze wall, the sustained-crescendo Swans/post-punk translation for any non-GK guitar. Gibson SG humbuckers (or baritone magnetic) into a Marshall Super Lead 1959 Plexi pushed past its real max into Strymon hot-rodded high gain, through the bright 8×12 cab c, stereo out into the End-board reverbs. A sustained chord blooms into a wide ambient wall with no extra pedals — the closest the Iridium gets to the home drone/doom aesthetic.

## How to play it
1. AMP punch, CAB c; DRIVE past 2:00, BASS ~9:00, MIDDLE ~9:00 (dropping the tone stack flattens EQ + raises power-stage level for grind), TREBLE noon.
2. ROOM high ~2:00, SIZE LARGE.
3. INPUT LEVEL = Instrument, OUTPUT MODE = Normal, rear = STEREO (run stereo out if after the CE-2W split).
4. Touch-responsive even here: back off guitar volume for clean, dig in for the wall.
5. For the saturated wall, feed a fuzz (Hizumitas / MXR 108) in front.

## Notes
- Pushes past the conventional doom preset into hot-rodded territory.
- Conservative community cousin = Guitar Chalk "Heavy Drive Doom Stack" on the 0–10 scale: Punch, cab c, Drive 9, BASS 8, MID 7, TREBLE 3, ROOM 2.
- Keep stereo — summing to mono throws away the width.
