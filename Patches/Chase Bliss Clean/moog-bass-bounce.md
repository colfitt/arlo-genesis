# Synth-Bass Bounce (Slow Attack / Fast Release)

## Concept
Devin Belanger's Moog-bass comp: a fairly high ratio on Dynamics with Sensitivity brought in "just to get a nice little bounce", paired with a slow attack and a fast release. The slow attack lets the note's transient pop through before the comp clamps, and the fast release lets the body pump back up quickly — that attack/release combination is exactly what creates the audible bounce. The result is a grooving, bouncing synth-bass compression voice rather than a transparent leveler. Demonstrated on a Moog synth bass; applies directly to the rig's VG-800 synth-bass patches.

## How to play it
1. Set a fairly high ratio on Dynamics.
2. Bring Sensitivity up (watch the left LED) until you feel the bounce.
3. Set Attack SLOW (CW) so the note's transient pops through before the comp clamps.
4. Set Release fast — toggle LEFT (Fast 50 ms) — so the body pumps back up quickly. That's the bounce.
5. Set Wet for level; add Dry for body if desired; EQ to taste.

## Notes
- The slow-attack / fast-release combination is exactly what creates the audible "bounce/pump".
- Demonstrated on a Moog synth bass; applies to the rig's VG-800 synth-bass patches.
- Descriptive values only ("high ratio / slow attack / fast release") — treat as a recipe, not exact numbers. There are no published clock positions; dial by ear and by the left LED.
- All dips OFF.
