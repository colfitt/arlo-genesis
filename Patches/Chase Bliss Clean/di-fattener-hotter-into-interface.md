# DI Fattener — Hotter Into the Interface

## Concept
Devin Belanger's understated guitar trick: use Clean to make the signal hotter and fatter as it hits a DI/interface. A gentle compression base plus the Wet/Dry make-up gain thickens the tone without obvious squash — "very subtle but it works and just fattens the tone." This is a practical level-and-body lift for direct recording, leaning on Clean's quiet, full-bodied character rather than any creative mode.

## How to play it
1. Set a gentle compression base (Dynamics modest, Sensitivity by the left LED).
2. Bring Wet up past noon so the signal hits the DI/interface hotter.
3. Blend Dry for body if you want to keep the uncompressed character.
4. Keep EQ at noon (off); leave the toggles centered.
5. Goal is subtle: a fatter, hotter direct tone, not audible compression.

## Notes
- Leans on Clean's "extremely quiet even with Wet/Dry gained up" character — safe as a recording front-end lift.
- HONEST: the boost is compressor make-up gain, not a pristine clean-boost circuit — plenty to drive a DI, just not transparent.
- DIAL FROM RECIPE — no published clock-face values; the seed describes the move ("subtle... fattens the tone"), not specific knob positions. Set the controls above by ear to the stated targets.
- All dips OFF.
