# Parallel Pop (New York)

## Concept
The maker's quoted use: "Bass, drums, or thickening an instrument or mix." New-York-style parallel compression — push the Wet into extreme/heavy settings, then blend the uncompressed DRY signal back in to restore character and dynamics for a big, punchy sound. Good for bass/baritone, a drum-machine/MPC bus, or thickening a thin source while keeping articulation — a big punchy front-end into the dirt wall.

## How to play it
1. **Set Sensitivity by ear to the left LED first** (run it hot).
2. Push Dynamics HIGH/extreme (noon–2:00) for heavy compression.
3. Bring Wet UP (~1:00) for the compressed level.
4. Blend Dry UP (~1:00) to restore the uncompressed body and articulation.
5. Attack FAST (CCW); Release Mid or LEFT (short); EQ noon.

## Notes
- The DRY knob being labeled DRY (not Mix) makes the NY-comp blend obvious — extreme comp settings restored to life with uncompressed body.
- All dips OFF.
