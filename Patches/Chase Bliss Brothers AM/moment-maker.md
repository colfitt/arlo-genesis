# Moment Maker — specialty channel + always-on

## Concept
The manual's MOMENT MAKER two-channel idea: keep one channel as your reliable always-on sound and reserve the other for an airy, nostalgic, specialty voice you bring in only for a moment. The two channels become a workhorse and a special-occasion tone rather than a stacked pair — Channel 2 in OVERDRIVE as the dependable rhythm/lead voice, Channel 1 set up as a distinct brighter/cleaner specialty sound (e.g. BOOST with the treble booster engaged for sparkle).

## How to play it
1. Set one channel as your dependable always-on voice (main rhythm/lead tone) — e.g. Channel 2 OVERDRIVE at low/medium gain.
2. Set the other channel for a distinct specialty sound — airy/nostalgic, e.g. Channel 1 BOOST with the treble booster engaged for sparkle, or a brighter/cleaner voicing.
3. Use the always-on channel by default; tap both footswitches to swap into the specialty voice for a single moment, then swap back.
4. Balance each channel's VOL so the swap isn't a jarring level change. Start PRESENCE DOWN and raise only if the tone is closed-in.

## Notes
- Manual-named recipe: the concept is quoted, but the specialty voice is open-ended — so all knob values are dial-from-recipe, with no published numbers.
- This is a workflow/role idea, not a fixed tone — treat the specialty channel as a blank slate for whatever "moment" you need.
- DIPs: none required. Consider flipping MASTER on if levels jump between the two voices.
