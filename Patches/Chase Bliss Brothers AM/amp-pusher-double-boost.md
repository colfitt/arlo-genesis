# Amp Pusher — double-boost into the amp

## Concept
The manual's own AMP PUSHER recipe: BOOST mode on BOTH channels, one set louder than the other, so the pedal's job is to make your AMP's own overdrive the focus rather than supplying its own clipping. BOOST is the diode-free, clearest, loudest circuit on the AM — "a clean boost capable of low-gain overdrive… the clearest sound and the loudest overall volume" (manual) — so stacking two gives you clean headroom that tips into edge. Optionally engage the treble booster on Channel 1 and/or push GAIN for a second, richer flavor: one clean transparent boost and one with hair.

## How to play it
1. Set BOTH channels to BOOST with similar settings; keep GAIN low (push the AMP, not the pedal — or nudge up for low-gain OD).
2. Set one channel's VOL louder than the other so you have a smaller always-on lift and a bigger kick.
3. Stack both to push a tube amp into its own natural breakup.
4. For a second, richer flavor, engage the treble booster on Channel 1 and/or push GAIN: now one boost is clean/transparent and the other has edge.

## Notes
- Manual-named recipe + CB's documented "both-Boost amp-pusher" starting point: the modes and the "one louder" intent are quoted; no exact knob numbers are published — dial VOL/GAIN from the recipe.
- Best when your amp does the dirty work; less relevant when the AM sits after a Muff (this one is amp-forward).
- BOOST mode = "a clean boost capable of low-gain overdrive… the clearest sound and the loudest overall volume" (manual).
- Keep the MASTER dip on if you stack both BOOSTs so the louder channel lifts smoothly rather than jumping levels.
