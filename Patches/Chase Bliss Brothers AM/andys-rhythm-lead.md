# Andy's Rhythm/Lead (halfway detailed)

## Concept
AndyDemos' practical two-channel base: both channels in OVERDRIVE (the classic KOT voice), Ch1 (rhythm) at lower gain and Ch2 (lead) at higher gain. Tap both footswitches to swap. The cleanest demonstration that the AM stacks detailed, not mushy, at moderate gain — Andy runs "gain about halfway on both" stacked and reports it stays detailed, not over-compressed.

## How to play it
1. Set Channel 1 (OVERDRIVE) as rhythm with slightly lower gain; Channel 2 (OVERDRIVE) as lead with slightly higher gain.
2. Tap both footswitches at once to swap channels.
3. When stacked, run gain about halfway on both — it stays detailed and uncompressed.
4. On a spanky/bright instrument, hold the footswitch and reduce presence to ~25% to smooth it.

## Notes
- Modes, the ~halfway-gain finding, and the 25% presence smoothing are quoted; precise clocks are inferred.
- The 25% presence-down trick is the inverse of Dan's presence-up move — use it to tame the VG-800 banjo when it gets glassy.
- Slot: intended as a MIDI preset (suggested).
