# Piera's Beano-into-KOT — thick & crunchy / Marshall-ify a Fender

## Concept
Mike Piera's (Analog Man) own stated favorite: the Beano Boost / Rangemaster treble booster running INTO the King of Tone — "it's thick and crunchy." This is the pairing the whole pedal is architected around — the booster is fixed at the front, feeding Channel 1. Piera also notes the treble boost "can turn a Fender amp somewhat into a Marshall" — a midrange-forward grind. Run the Rangemaster into Channel 1 in OVERDRIVE (the KOT voice) for Piera's signature thick, crunchy lead.

## How to play it
1. Set the treble booster DOWN (classic Rangemaster / Beano Boost).
2. Set Channel 1 to OVERDRIVE — the booster feeds the King of Tone circuit, Piera's stated favorite pairing.
3. Dial GAIN 1 for a thick, crunchy lead; keep below max for the touch response. Into a clean Fender turned up, this leans the amp toward a Marshall-like grind.
4. Plug near the front / straight from guitar (low input impedance dislikes buffers ahead); if buffered upstream, back the internal Input-Impedance trim CCW.
5. If the tone feels closed-in, raise PRESENCE (hold the footswitch + turn TONE).

## Notes
- Artist-stated: Piera names the Rangemaster/treble boost INTO the King of Tone as his favorite — "thick and crunchy"; and "adding the treble boost can turn a Fender amp somewhat into a Marshall."
