# Expander — OD opened up by a bright boost

## Concept
The manual's EXPANDER stacking idea: Channel 1 in OVERDRIVE as the foundation voice, Channel 2 in BOOST with its TONE turned up so the second channel enhances the first — amplifying and OPENING it up rather than adding distortion. Kick Channel 2 on when you want more intensity and openness without more grit. The bright BOOST opens and amplifies the OD; it does not add clipping.

## How to play it
1. Set Channel 1 to OVERDRIVE as your always-on foundation voice.
2. Set Channel 2 to BOOST with TONE 2 turned up (brighter).
3. Kick Channel 2 on when you want more intensity and openness WITHOUT adding distortion — it amplifies and opens up Channel 1.
4. Use the MASTER dip so the Channel 2 kick (with VOL pushing) stays a controlled lift and doesn't throw stage level.

## Notes
- Manual-named stacking recipe (EXPANDER): modes and "TONE up" are quoted; no exact knob numbers are published — dial GAIN/VOL from the recipe.
- Distinct from OVERLOADER / 2-IN-1 (which uses BOOST to oversaturate the OD): here the bright BOOST opens the OD up rather than pushing it into more clipping.
- TONE-up on the boost channel is the key differentiator — it's what "opens it out."
