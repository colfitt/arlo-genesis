# Treble-Booster A/B — two booster voices into a boost

## Concept
AndyDemos' bare-bones method for hearing the two treble-booster flavors side by side: put the booster at the front into Channel 1 in BOOST mode and A/B the two voices. The classic Rangemaster voice (DOWN) has an upper-mid emphasis (Marshall-ish bite); the other voice (UP) shaves off even more low end and is brighter, more strident, and adds clean high-end sparkle — great for opening up dark humbucker rigs and for stacking with other effects. A learning/utility patch for picking your booster flavor before committing it to a song.

## How to play it
1. Set Channel 1 to BOOST and leave Channel 2 off — bare-bones so the booster voices come through clearly.
2. Toggle the TREBLE BOOSTER DOWN (classic Rangemaster) and play — note the upper-mid emphasis (Marshall-ish bite).
3. Toggle the TREBLE BOOSTER UP and play — note it shaves more low end and is brighter, more strident, with clean high-end sparkle (good for dark humbuckers).
4. Pick the voice you want; the bright/UP voice stacks especially well with other effects downstream.

## Notes
- AndyDemos + Guitar.com-quoted: the two booster voices and their character (upper-mid Rangemaster vs bright/sparkle) are quoted; knob clocks are NOT published — this is a dialable recipe, so crank GAIN 1 a bit while A/Bing per Andy rather than treating any clock value as sourced.
- MIDDLE = OFF (pure classic KOT, no Rangemaster) — the third toggle position, included for completeness.
- Use this as the A/B utility, then feed the chosen voice into Joel's / Piera's booster patches.
