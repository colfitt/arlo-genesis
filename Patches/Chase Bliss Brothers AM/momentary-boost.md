# Momentary Boost — hold-to-engage lift

## Concept
Use the MOTOBYP ("momentary to bypass") dip so a footswitch engages its channel only while held — a hold-down momentary boost or kill. Set the accent channel as a BOOST (clean lift) or DISTORTION (saturation hit) with MOTOBYP on, and you get an on-the-fly accent: stomp-and-hold for a lead lift or a momentary saturation hit, release to drop back instantly. The other channel runs your normal always-on tone, untouched.

## How to play it
1. Turn the MOTOBYP dip ON for the channel you want as a momentary accent.
2. Set that channel to BOOST (for a clean held lift) or DISTORTION (for a momentary saturation hit). Dial its VOL/GAIN to size the accent.
3. Run your normal tone on the other channel (leave its MOTOBYP off).
4. Stomp and HOLD the accent footswitch for the lift; release to drop back instantly. Keep MASTER on so the held accent stays a controlled lift rather than a level jump. Start PRESENCE DOWN.

## Notes
- Documented feature: MOTOBYP (hold-to-engage momentary bypass) is quoted across sources; the tone you put on the channel is open, so all knob values are dial-from-recipe with no published numbers.
- Great for quick on-the-fly boosts where you don't want a latched state — Guitar.com calls out the momentary mode for "quick on-the-fly boosts."
- Can also be used as a momentary KILL depending on routing.
- MASTER on is recommended here so the held accent is a controlled lift, not a level explosion.
