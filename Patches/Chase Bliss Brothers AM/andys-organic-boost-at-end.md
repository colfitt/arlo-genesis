# Andy's Organic Stack — heavy OD pushed by an end boost

## Concept
AndyDemos' "organic" approach to the Brothers AM: use Channel 1 (right) as a heavier OVERDRIVE foundation and Channel 2 (left) as a BOOST sitting at the END of the chain. Because the BOOST mode clips less than the OVERDRIVE, it stays more dynamic — and rather than piling on the pedal's own saturation, it pushes the amp itself into overdrive. That "let the amp do the overdriving" move is what makes it sound organic, and it's the deliberate contrast to Andy's other 'saturation' DIST→OD stack.

## How to play it
1. Set Channel 1 (right side) to OVERDRIVE as a heavier foundation.
2. Set Channel 2 (left side) to BOOST — because it clips less, it's more dynamic than running another OD.
3. Run them stacked: the end BOOST pushes the amp itself into overdrive for the "organic" sound (utilize the amp, not the pedal's saturation).
4. On a spanky/bright guitar, hold the footswitch and reduce presence to ~25% to smooth it.

## Notes
- AndyDemos-quoted: the modes and the role of each channel are quoted; there are no exact clock values — dial GAIN/VOL from the recipe to taste.
- "Organic" = let the amp do the overdriving, with the BOOST at the end acting as a dynamic pusher (contrasts with Andy's 'saturation' DIST→OD stack, which leans on the pedal's own clipping).
- In Andy's layout: right = Channel 1, left = Channel 2.
- MASTER dip ON is optional but helps keep overall level controlled once the end boost is engaged.
- No published knob values — phrased as a dialable recipe by design.
