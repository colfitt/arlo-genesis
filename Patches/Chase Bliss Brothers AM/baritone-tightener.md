# Baritone Tightener

## Concept
Keep the VG-800 baritone Jazzmaster's low strings from going flabby after the Muff, while adding KOT-grade grit and weight for the drone/doom low end. OVERDRIVE's tightness is the tool; HI GAIN is used sparingly because baritone + Muff + AM distortion compounds compression fast. KOT-style drives are bass-friendly — BOOST/OD add grit without collapsing low end, while DIST + HI GAIN will compress the bottom.

## How to play it
1. Run Channel 1 in OVERDRIVE with GAIN 1 ~10–11; keep TONE 1 ~11 so you don't over-brighten the low strings.
2. Leave the treble booster MIDDLE (off) so the low end stays intact — go UP only if the baritone disappears in the wall.
3. Optionally add Channel 2 (BOOST, GAIN 2 ~9) for a clean low-end push, with VOL 2 as master.
4. Keep HI GAIN 1 OFF by default; engage only for the heaviest passages.
5. MASTER dip ON to keep level sane when the low end compresses.
