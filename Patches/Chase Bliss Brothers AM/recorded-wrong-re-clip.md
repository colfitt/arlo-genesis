# Recorded-Wrong Re-Clip

## Concept
Lean INTO the rig's "recorded-wrong" aesthetic: re-clip the already-modeled, already-saturated VG-800 + Muff signal with hard-clipping DISTORTION + HI GAIN so the heavily-colored compression PG warns about becomes the FEATURE — a squished, gated, mis-biased wall feeding the granular/tape stages downstream. The compression is the point; let the granular/tape stages feed on it.

## How to play it
1. Set the TREBLE BOOSTER UP (brighter/strident — shaves lows, adds the "wrong" edge, stacks well with downstream effects per AndyDemos).
2. Channel 1 = DISTORTION with HI GAIN 1 ON, GAIN 1 ~1–2, VOL 1 ~1 pushing into Channel 2.
3. Channel 2 = DISTORTION or OVERDRIVE, GAIN 2 ~1, VOL 2 as master.
4. Turn PRES LINK on a channel + sweep TONE for the dramatic dark→bright move (CB tech demo demos this in DISTORTION with gain way up).
5. Run PRESENCE high or PRES-LINKed for the brittle, over-bright character; keep MASTER dip ON.

## Notes
- Designed starting points, not found settings.
- Honest flag — this intentionally runs OPPOSITE the rig's usual low-gain-tightener advice. Use it as a deliberate effect (post-punk brittle wall, doom squish), not the default. Embrace the "heavily colored compression at the highest gain levels" as the texture.
- Slot: intended as a MIDI preset (suggested).
