# KOT Recipe Concepts — blues breakup & transparent rhythm crunch

## Concept
Two dial-from-ear voicing CONCEPTS pulled from a published King of Tone settings guide and recast for the AM's controls. (1) BLUES: a warm, vocal, edge-of-breakup voice that cleans up with pick attack and guitar volume, and growls when you dig in. (2) TRANSPARENT RHYTHM CRUNCH: a tight, articulate crunch that firms up the low end and adds bite while staying out of the way of the guitar's natural tone. The honest framing matters here — the guide's actual Volume-A/Volume-B/Drive/Tone numbers are for the ORIGINAL Analogman King of Tone, not the Brothers AM, so this seed keeps only the relative directions (low/modest drive, lower vs higher tone), never the wrong-pedal clock numbers.

## How to play it
1. BLUES: set Channel 1 to OVERDRIVE at low / edge-of-breakup gain with a moderate TONE — back off your picking and it cleans up, dig in and it growls; vocal and dynamic.
2. RHYTHM CRUNCH: run a clean/boost channel into an OVERDRIVE channel at modest gain with a slightly brighter TONE — it tightens the low end and adds bite while staying transparent.
3. Use the TONE knob (not the treble booster) for articulation here so the crunch stays uncolored.
4. Dial entirely by ear: do NOT copy the source's number values — they're for the original KOT, not this pedal.

## Notes
- HONESTY: the Guitar Chalk guide prints specific Volume-A/Volume-B/Drive/Tone numbers for the ORIGINAL Analogman King of Tone — verified, NOT the Brothers AM. The AM has no such numeric scale (GAIN 1/2, VOL 1/2, TONE 1/2 + booster/modes/dips), so the numbers are deliberately omitted; only the relative-direction CONCEPTS are kept.
- Edge-of-breakup territory partly overlaps existing voices — kept as a compact, clearly-labelled "dial-by-ear blues/rhythm" concept reference rather than two separate number-bearing patches.
- If you want the booster-flavored versions, layer the Rangemaster from the treble-booster patches on top.
