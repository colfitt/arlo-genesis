# Presence-Up — open out the closed/chewy tone

## Concept
The headline hidden-control move from the King of Tone world, surfaced on the AM. Presence ships fully down — closed, mid-focused, chewy — and bringing the hidden PRESENCE up "changes the game" and opens the tone right out. On the original KOT this was an internal trim pot; here you hold the channel's footswitch and turn its TONE knob to set it. Some players wrote the KOT off until they found this. Treat it as a general "open it out / add air or tame harshness" patch for any voice that feels boxed-in — the standalone technique, layered on whatever drive you're running.

## How to play it
1. Start with whatever overdrive/boost voice feels closed-in, mid-focused, or chewy.
2. Hold that channel's footswitch and turn the TONE knob — this sets the hidden PRESENCE (ships fully down; was an internal trim on the original KOT).
3. Bring PRESENCE up noticeably until the tone "opens out" and the top end/clarity return; reduce it to soften harsh highs.
4. Don't overdo it (Mick noted Dan went a touch far). Or engage PRES LINK to set presence with TONE on the fly, or drive it via MIDI.

## Notes
- KOT/TPS/CB/PG-quoted: presence ships fully down, was an internal trim on the original KOT, and presence-up "opens it out / changed the game" — but no numeric value is published, so dial it from the recipe rather than a fixed clock.
- Joel's caveat: he prefers presence down and got confused mid-development whenever it was accidentally up — this is a deliberate move, not a default.
- Generalizes the presence reveal; the banjo-specific version is Banjo-Lead Cut Recovery and Dan's Overdrive Boost folds it in — this is the standalone technique.
