# Knobs Chunky-Glue Swell

## Concept
The de-facto signature setting — the Chase Bliss / Knobs flagship sound. A Slow-verb (1A) swell is pushed hard with the hidden GLUE compressor/saturator until it overloads into a thick, compressed wall: *"Very ambient shoegaze reverb that swells in... sprinkled with GLUE — it's like chunky glue."* The GLUE-heavy overload is the whole move. Run as a single channel (or L+R with R parked).

## How to play it
1. Engage the L slot (Slow-verb). Play sustained chords and let the swell bloom in.
2. Set ALT (diffusion) ~3:00 so the tail is fully smeared.
3. **Push GLUE HARD ~2–3:00 until it overloads** — this is the "chunky glue" character.
4. **HOLD the footswitch = INFINITE** to freeze the swell as a sustained pad and play over it.

## Notes
- De-facto signature setting — Knobs (Joel Korte's collaborator) is the closest this pedal has to a named user.
- **Honest:** not a touring-artist credit, it's CB's own launch demo.
- The Slow-verb's diffused tail is louder-than-dry by design, so MIX ~1–2:00 keeps the wet present.
- Nudge EQ slightly CCW to tame mud as GLUE overloads.
