# Gate Slicer (Mono Chop)

## Concept
In a mono slot, the BF-3's Gate/Pan mode is not the L/R rotary — it becomes a hard-cutoff volume slicer / extreme tremolo with swirl. The flanger's rise-and-fall drives 32 on/off cycles, chopping the signal into a rhythmic gate. Good for rhythmic stutter over a sustained wall or pad; tap-sync to land the chop on the beat.

## How to play it
1. Set MODE to Gate/Pan (~9:00), RATE and DEPTH ~9:00, MANUAL and RES way down (~6–7:00).
2. Run a sustained wall or pad into it so the gate has something to chop.
3. Optionally hold the pedal ≥2 s and tap to sync the chop to the song's beat.

## Notes
- Electronic/sliced + post-punk rhythmic-build fit.
- In mono this is a slicer, NOT the L/R rotary — for the rotary you need OUTPUT B / a post-CE-2W stereo repatch (see Stereo Rotary / Leslie Spin).
- Output jumps in Gate/Pan + tap — trim downstream.
