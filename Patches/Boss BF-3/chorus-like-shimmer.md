# Chorus-Like Shimmer

## Concept
A low-RES, shallow, faster setting that reads as a thin chorus or shimmer rather than a jet flange. Gentle thickening for a cleaner banjo/baritone or a clean-ish bed before the wall builds — the notches stay subtle so the source keeps its character.

## How to play it
1. Set MODE to Standard, DEPTH 10:00, RATE ~1:00, RES and MANUAL both low (~7:00).
2. Play an exposed banjo lead or a clean bed through it for a thin chorus shimmer.
3. To add panning, run it in stereo via Gate/Pan with both outputs (Messano demos both mono and stereo).

## Notes
- Best on exposed banjo lead, which otherwise goes thin/metallic through deep flange (the notches hit its 2–4 kHz spike) — keep RES/DEPTH low on bare banjo.
- In stereo (via Gate/Pan) it gains panning.
