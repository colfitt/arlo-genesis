# Banjo-Lead Reactive Grit

## Concept
Designed against the taste lens (banjo-as-lead + Indie-folk cluster — Sufjan / Bon Iver banjo beds). Overdrive mode (G1 up / G2 down) gives modest reactive grit for a banjo via GK-5 → VG-800 banjo model carrying the lead/melody line. The reactive opto-FET gain evens the banjo's attack/decay imbalance and lets it sit INSIDE a wall without ice-pick harshness.

## How to play it
- Set the grid to G1 up / G2 down (Overdrive).
- Keep Gain modest (9:00–10:00) — lean on the pluck for clarity; the reactive gain evens the banjo's attack/decay.
- Roll Tone well **left/darker** (9:00–10:00) to tame banjo ice-pick — go further left than feels natural.
- Low-Cut down/flat to keep what little low end the banjo has.
- Clean up with instrument volume for dynamic phrasing.
- Feed into the Indie-folk room/halo chain (Microcosm / Chroma Console / Dark Star) downstream.

## Notes
- DESIGNED. Banjo-as-lead tie-breaker applied.
- Honest limitation: the Kilt's single Tone tilt is a blunter brightness control than the Hizumitas's filter-like sweep, so it's less surgical at taming banjo ice-pick — roll Tone well left.
