# Bass Reactive Grit

## Concept
Designed recipe synthesized from the bass demo. Subtle low-gain reactive grit for Fender Jazz / P bass (4-string — 5-string low end gets wonky per the bass demo). The opto-FET reactive gain tracks bass dynamics; this fills a drive role none of Brothers/Longsword/Oxford covers, and doubles as sub-bass/baritone-adjacent grit feeding the drone end of the rig.

## How to play it
- Set G1 **up** for grit (or flip G2 up for fuzz to taste).
- Keep Gain subtle in the now-usable first 25% (9:00–10:00) — back off picking to clean up.
- Low-Cut **down/flat** is CRITICAL: flat = the "much beefier" low end. Flip to cut ONLY to sit in a busy mix (or for the drone-end role, cut + parallel blend).
- Run a **parallel clean low-end blend** alongside it (it's a guitar pedal) to keep bass usable.
