# Transparent Edge-of-Breakup

## Concept
A low-gain Crunch-mode (G1 down / G2 down) transparent overdrive — the lowest-gain mode, sitting at edge-of-breakup. It's a dirtier-pedal-into-cleaner-amp move that beefs a clean amp/model without washing out downstream delays/verbs, and doubles as a clean boost. Cleans up exceptionally well with guitar-volume.

## How to play it
- Set the four-mode grid to **Crunch**: G1 down / G2 down (lowest-gain mode).
- Dial Gain into the now-usable first 25% (8:00–9:00) — the headline V2→10 fix made audible.
- Push Volume past noon (1:00–2:00) to compensate for the low gain; the 10's headroom makes this practical.
- Brighten Tone a touch to taste (~12:00). Low-Cut down/flat.
- Roll guitar-volume back to clean up further — this mode cleans up exceptionally well.
