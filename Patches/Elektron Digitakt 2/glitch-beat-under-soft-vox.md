# Glitch Beat under Soft Vox (Postal Service, un-pumped)

## Concept
The Postal Service clattery programmed beat — fast beeps/clicks, dry-forward, with no pump (the un-pumped counterpoint to Daft Punk). Resample a single VG-800/OP-1 tone and chop it into a fast clattery 16th beat, add the broken element with bit-reduction p-locks, conditional/probability trigs and micro-timing nudges, a short retrig, and a slap delay — kept dry-forward so a soft lead sits over the top.

## How to play it
1. Resample a VG-800/OP-1 blip/beep (single tone) and chop it; program a fast clattery 16th beat of beeps + clicks. NO pump.
2. Add the broken element: BIT REDUCTION p-lock on a few steps, conditional/probability trigs (60-80% or 1:2) + micro-timing nudges (Dntel cut-up feel), short RTRG retrig on one step.
3. DELAY slap send (the Line 6 / Alesis MIDIverb role).
4. Keep dry-forward so a soft lead sits on top.

## Notes
- DT2-E3. Tamborello: 'none of that [sidechain] on the album' — keep it un-pumped on purpose.
- Designed-to-emulate — not a found preset. Drum tracks; a soft lead sits over the top.
