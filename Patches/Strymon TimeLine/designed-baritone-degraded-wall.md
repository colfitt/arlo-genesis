# Designed — Baritone degraded wall

## Concept
A from-scratch shoegaze/doom smear dialed for the rig's baritone, given as clock-face starting points. dTape with a dotted-eighth time, repeats pushed to 2–3:00, and tape character (age/bias/wow/crinkle) all dialed in so held chords ring out into a degraded wall — but with a high-pass tuned to keep baritone lows from muddying.

## How to play it
1. Set TIME to a dotted-eighth (or to taste) and REPEATS to 2–3:00.
2. Dial MIX to 12–1:00, then the tape character: Tape Age (FILTER) 1–2:00, Tape Bias (GRIT) 9–10:00, Wow & Flutter (DEPTH) 2:00, Tape Crinkle (SPEED) 1:00.
3. Nudge LO END slightly toward bass and set HP 80–120 Hz for the baritone.
4. Let chords ring.

## Notes
- Inferred starting point (dossier §13a) — not captured from a cited source; treat all knob positions as designed/approximate.
- dBucket Double ("Dark Analog"/"Low Voltage") is the warmer-repeats alternative that won't fight the lows when tape smear gets muddy under baritone.
