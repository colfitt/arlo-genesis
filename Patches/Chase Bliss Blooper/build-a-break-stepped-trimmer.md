# Build-A-Break — Stepped Trimmer intro/build-up

## Concept

Stepped Trimmer shortens the loop in musical subdivisions, down to 1/32 steps — an effect similar to shortening a sequence. As the loop tightens into a shorter, more urgent figure it makes a great intro or build-up: trim it down a step at a time to drive energy before the full loop drops back in.

## How to play it

1. Build a loop, then set MOD A to Stepped Trimmer (A3, default bank).
2. Step the knob off noon to trim the loop down in 1/32 subdivisions — CCW toward 'start 1/32', CW toward 'end 1/32'.
3. Use the progressively shorter loop as an intro/build-up — tighten it to drive energy.
4. Manually moving or ramping the knob lets you rearrange/remix the loop on the fly.

## Notes

- Stepped Trimmer's steps are quantized to musical subdivisions, so the result stays rhythmically coherent.
- When using Blooper as a delay, treat Stepped Trimmer like a tap-tempo subdivision control (see Strange Subdivisions).
- Exact knob positions are not published — this is a dialable recipe; dial in the subdivision you want by ear, and the other knobs are dialed from the recipe rather than from published values.
