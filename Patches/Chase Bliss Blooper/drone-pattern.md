# Drone Pattern

## Concept

Turn a sustained baritone or banjo drone (or any steady source) into a groovy rhythmic pattern. Scrambler chops the loop, with jump points locked to a 16-slice grid so they support the original rhythm. This is Scrambler — the KNOBs co-designer's favorite modifier.

## How to play it

1. Record an ambient or steady source — for example a couple of sustained chords.
2. Set MOD B to Scrambler (B5) and engage to chop into a pattern.
3. Dial the knob: CW for a repeating sequence, CCW for random; higher for more chopping.
4. Toggle on/off to regenerate a new arrangement (jump points re-roll on the 16-slice grid).

## Notes

- Sync the grid feel to the Digitakt clock (see Clock-synced blooping) so the groove feels intentional.
