# Daydreaming Half-Speed Octave-Down Grind

## Concept

Thom Yorke's "very distinctive grinding vocal" finale on Daydreaming — a dense vocal loop switched to half-speed playback, dropping it an octave. Translated to a banjo/baritone/VG loop that drops an octave into a grinding sub-drone. The downsample artifact of pitching the loop down IS the grinding quality. Designed to emulate (Radiohead used an EHX 45000).

## How to play it

1. In ADD mode, build a loop (a vocal-like sustained banjo/VG phrase, or layered overdubs for density like Thom's stacked vocals).
2. Set MOD B to Stepped Speed (B4) octave-down — CCW for reverse, CW for forward; pick the forward octave-down for the grind.
3. Lean into the downsample grind with mild STABILITY (~10 o'clock) for tape-wobble grit.
4. Optionally add Filter (B6) CCW to darken.
5. REPEATS max to hold the dropped loop.

## Notes

- Where Big Time/MOOD do a half-speed move on the wet, Blooper does it to a committed loop you keep playing fresh over — exactly Thom's workflow (let the dense loop run, then drop it an octave while the live part continues).
- Mono-fine; the Chroma Console / H90 re-spread downstream.
