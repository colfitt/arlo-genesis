# All Slow — NORM half-speed bed under live playing

## Concept

Record a loop, engage Stepped Speed at half-speed, then begin overdubbing in NORM — everything in the loop, including your new overdubs, plays back at half-speed while your live playing up front stays full speed. Build a slow, doom-y soundscape over time under a live, full-speed banjo/baritone lead. This is behaviour unique to Blooper: in NORM the half-speed is not committed to the dry signal, so the bed ages and slows while your hands stay in real time.

## How to play it

1. Record a loop in NORM.
2. Set MOD B to Stepped Speed (B4) and engage it at half-speed.
3. Begin overdubbing — the bed AND your new overdubs play back at half-speed, but your live playing stays full speed.
4. Build up a slow, doom-y soundscape under a live, full-speed banjo/baritone lead.

## Notes

- Unique-to-Blooper behavior per the official Recording-with-Modifiers guide.
- Because Stepped Speed is time-based, overdubbed audio still lands in unexpected places (head disconnect) — embrace it, or turn the modifier off before precise overdubs.
- Half-speed is the documented setting; finer values dial from recipe.
- Great doom fit: full-speed live over a half-speed aged bed (relates to Daydreaming, but in NORM rather than ADD).
- Honesty flag: half-speed is the documented step — the other knob positions are not published and dial from this recipe.
