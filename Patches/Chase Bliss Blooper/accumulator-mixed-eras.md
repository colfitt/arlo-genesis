# Accumulator / "mixed eras"

## Concept

A loop that ages like a tape machine wearing out in real time. Vintage layers wobble and decay underneath while clear new playing pops fresh on top — the rig's signature degraded wall. The advantage over Frippertronics: you keep playing fresh notes that appear clean while the bed rots.

## How to play it

1. In ADD mode, record a clean loop with STABILITY off as a reference.
2. Turn STABILITY up — mild (just off minimum) for cumulative aging, moderate for an "analog feel."
3. Keep overdubbing. Each additive pass ages more than the last: older layers wobble, lose highs, and get noisier while new layers pop fresh.
4. Pair with REPEATS pulled back (~11–1 o'clock) so old layers fade, giving a continuous time-shift.
5. For something more adventurous, bring in Scrambler.

## Notes

- Use the BLIP Stability slider (see Setup: Additive Assist + Stability slider) to keep flutter and dial out white noise.
- The honest workflow advantage: fresh notes appear clean while the bed disintegrates.
