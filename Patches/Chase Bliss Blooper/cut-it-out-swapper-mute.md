# Cut It Out — Swapper mutes the loop while overdubbing

## Concept

With the Swapper knob at noon, Swapper mutes your loop whenever overdubbing is engaged — whether you're playing or not. Nice for inserting deliberate silence or getting precise control over what stays in the loop. This is a precision-edit move (punch silence into the loop, carve precise gaps, replace sections) rather than the ambient Rolling Clouds swell.

## How to play it

1. Engage the BANK B dip to access Swapper on MOD B (or load Swapper via BLIP).
2. Set the MOD B (Swapper) knob to noon.
3. Engage overdubbing — the loop mutes whenever you're overdubbing, letting you punch silence into the loop.
4. Play new material into the silence to replace sections, or leave it empty to carve precise gaps.

## Notes

- This is a precision-edit move (insert silence / control parts) rather than the ambient Rolling Clouds swell — more precise than punching record on/off by hand.
- Counter-clockwise of noon sets a threshold for immediate muting; clockwise sets a gradual fade into silence (manual).
- Requires the BANK B dip (or a BLIP load) to put Swapper on MOD B — physical-only setup.
- Knob at noon is the documented position; the surrounding behavior dials from recipe — no published values for the other knobs.
