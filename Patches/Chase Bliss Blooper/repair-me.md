# Repair Me

## Concept

The sound of a tape machine on the edge of death — a loop that is sometimes pure and clean, sometimes wobbly and disheveled, occasionally volatile. This is the strongest "recorded-wrong" degrade fit on the sheet and the doom/decay centerpiece of the rig.

## How to play it

1. In ADD mode, set the ramping dips to STABILITY + BOUNCE + RANDOM.
2. Set the RAMP knob (the VOLUME knob while ramping) fairly SLOW.
3. Loop a wall, then let the slow random Stability ramp ride the line between clean and broken.
4. Layer fresh banjo over the wobble.
5. Expect feedback and self-oscillation — ride VOLUME by holding the RIGHT footswitch.

## Notes

- From the manual (p.45 — "tape machine on the edge of death").
- Dip switches are physical-only, NOT MIDI-addressable, so this ramp setup is a fixed-setup decision, not a per-song recall.
