# Harmonious Fifth — Stepped Speed a fifth up or down

## Concept

Shift the loop a fifth up or down with Stepped Speed and play the shifted version alongside the original key — a harmonious but slightly more complex melodic relationship from one source, with no second instrument. Stepped Speed lands on octaves and fifths by design, so a fifth is the sweet harmonic interval to explore against the original key.

## How to play it

1. Record a phrase, then set MOD B to Stepped Speed (B4).
2. Step the MOD B knob until the loop sits a fifth up (CW) or down (CCW) — Stepped Speed quantizes to octaves and fifths.
3. In NORM the shifted version plays over the clean loop for instant harmony; in ADD it bakes in.
4. Play a fresh live lead over the now-harmonized bed.

## Notes

- KNOBs' explicit tip: a fifth is the sweet harmonic interval to explore against the original key.
- Stepped Speed is time-based — content can be lost or doubled if the speed change implies a different length than the fixed loop container.
- Stepped Speed has one extra octave of range compared to the smooth/chromatic speeds.
- Exact step positions are not published; the MOD B step, STABILITY, and REPEATS values above are a dialable recipe, not sourced settings.
