# Strange Subdivisions — Stepped Trimmer as delay tap subdivisions

## Concept

When you use Blooper as a delay, treat Stepped Trimmer like a unique form of standard tap-tempo subdivisions. Tap a tempo, then step the loop down in 1/32 increments to carve odd, dotted, and triplet-style delay subdivisions out of that one tapped tempo — a subdivision selector that stays musical because every step is quantized.

## How to play it

1. Set Blooper up as a delay: tap-tempo the RECORD footswitch to set the time, pull REPEATS below max (delay/fade behavior), and leave it recording.
2. Set MOD A to Stepped Trimmer (A3, default bank).
3. Step the MOD A knob off noon to shorten the delay window in 1/32 subdivisions — it acts like a subdivision selector on the delay.
4. Combine with Scrambler to speed the pattern up for bursts of jumbled activity.

## Notes

- Stepped Trimmer quantizes to 1/32, so the subdivisions stay musical.
- REPEATS only fades while recording; in playback the loop never fades (it is a manual looper). Backing off REPEATS turns Blooper into a high-quality digital delay.
- Pairs with Scrambler for jumbled / bursting delays (cheat-sheet tip).
- Exact step positions and REPEATS value are not published — this is a dialable recipe; dial the subdivision you want by ear, and the other knobs are dialed from the recipe rather than from published values.
