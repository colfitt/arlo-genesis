# Manual Stretch — scan a time-stretch across the loop

## Concept

With the Stepped Trimmer just right of noon, rotating the knob clockwise introduces a manual time-stretching effect as you scan across the loop — scrubbing the recorded material like dragging tape past the playhead. It's a deliberate coding byproduct the designers kept because they liked it, and it's recordable in Additive.

## How to play it

1. Set MOD A to Stepped Trimmer (slot A3, default bank).
2. Park the knob just right of noon and engage the arcade button.
3. Slowly rotate clockwise to scan a manual time-stretch across the loop.
4. Record the move in ADD (or a one-shot Record) to bake the stretch in, or ramp the knob for hands-free movement.

## Notes

- This is the deliberate "clockwise = time-stretch" quirk KNOBs describes — a byproduct of how Trimmer was coded, kept because they liked it.
- Counterclockwise of noon is the abrasive "crumbs" shortening side instead.
- Distinct from the alt-bank Stretcher (true pitch-independent stretch) — this is the default-bank Trimmer's stretch quirk.
- Cheat-sheet tip: pair Trimmer with Scrambler to speed up Scrambler's pattern for bursts of activity (see the Jumbled Bursts seed).
- No published knob values beyond "right of noon, clockwise" — treat the remaining knobs as a dialable recipe to taste; only the Trimmer slot and clockwise-scan direction are sourced.
