# Down Low — Pitcher slams high material down octaves

## Concept

The deeper extremes of Pitcher are great for sound design: record some high-pitched / bright material, then pitch it way down (toward −3 octaves) for huge, transformed sub textures. Because Pitcher is non-time-based, the loop length never changes and speed is unaffected — this is the ADD-mode "safe zone," so heads stay in sync while you keep overdubbing fresh material on top. Pitching high material down preserves more detail than pitching low material up.

## How to play it

1. Record some high-pitched / bright material (the brighter and busier, the more there is to slam down).
2. Engage the BANK A dip and set MOD A to Pitcher (A2), or load Pitcher via BLIP.
3. Turn the MOD A knob deep counter-clockwise toward −3 octaves to slam the loop down low — speed and loop length stay put.
4. Because Pitcher leaves loop length unchanged, overdub fresh material on top safely in ADD while the dropped loop runs underneath.

## Notes

- Pitcher is the ADD-mode "safe zone" — loop length never changes, so heads stay in sync while overdubbing.
- Pitching high material down preserves more detail than pitching low material up — record bright, end heavy.
- DSP-heavy: mutually exclusive with Stretcher, and it does not combine with the Speed modifiers (reverse excepted).
- Requires the BANK A dip (or a BLIP load) to put Pitcher on MOD A — physical-only setup.
- Exact pitch position dials from recipe — no published values; sources say "deeper extremes / way down," range ±3 octaves.
