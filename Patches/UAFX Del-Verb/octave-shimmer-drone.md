# Octave-Shimmer Drone

## Concept
The closest patch to the home aesthetic without a dedicated shimmer pedal. A Tape EP-III voicing with octave-up (or fifth) "pitched buckets" generates harmonized repeats, which spill into a max-mid-decay 224 reverb for a harmonized wall. Worn-tape Mod plus past-noon Color bake in the degraded, "recorded-wrong" lean.

## How to play it
1. Pre-assign in the app: Reverb toggle "Shimmeron" (or "Fractal Forest") on Hall 224, Delay toggle "EP with octave pitched buckets" (or "...5th...") on Tape EP-III.
2. Swell a long, sustained source — baritone neck pickup, or a VG-800 pad — into the patch and hold.
3. Let the octave-up buckets stack into the reverb wash for a harmonized drone bed.
4. For tape-warp dives, move the Time knob live: **Tape EP-III pitch-shifts as you turn Time**, dropping the drone in pitch.

## Notes
- The pitched-bucket Tape voicing gives octave shimmer for free — no shimmer pedal needed.
- Color "past noon" and Mod "toward worn" are intentional degradation — keep the nuance; back off Color toward noon if the grit is too much.
- "Fractal Forest" adds 150 ms pre-delay vs "Shimmeron's" chamber tail — pick by how much the dry should speak before the bloom.
