# Ghostly Spring Modulated

## Concept
The surprise ambient option on the otherwise-surfy Spring engine. "Spring Ghostly C Vibrato" applies a custom vibrato *after* the reverb for a moving, detuned spring — a sound that fits the art-rock "verb that moves and detunes" character far more than a static spring. A vibrato ping-pong delay reinforces the movement. For the biggest, most cavernous version, swap to "Symphonic Reverb" (pitch-shifted modulation) so it reads as a hall.

## How to play it
1. Pre-assign in the app: Reverb toggle "Spring Ghostly C Vibrato" (or "Spring C Rotato," or "Symphonic Reverb") on Spring '65, Delay toggle "Stereo Ping Pong Vibrato" on Precision.
2. Play single-note lead or sparse chords; the detuned spring smears them into motion.
3. For a rotary feel, use "Spring C Rotato"; for the biggest cavern, use "Symphonic Reverb."

## Notes
- Mod knob stays at noon — the vibrato is baked into the voicing, not the knob.
- "Symphonic Reverb" is the biggest/most cavernous variant if you want it to read as a hall rather than a spring.
- This is the art-rock reading of the Spring engine, not the surf reading (see "Spring Slap" for the surf voice).
