# Present-Plate Drone Bed

## Concept
The biggest *immediate* plate wall the Del-Verb offers, and an alternative drone flavor to the Hall 224 route. "Present Plate" is a ~6-second 140 Plate A decay with no pre-delay — long, immediate, enveloping. A long, low Precision delay sits underneath as support, not as a rhythmic feature. Plate gives a denser, warmer, less grainy wall than Hall 224.

## How to play it
1. Pre-assign in the app: Reverb toggle "Present Plate" on Plate 140, Delay toggle "Default Precision" on Precision.
2. Set the delay long (~600–800 ms) and low in the mix so it underpins rather than rhythms.
3. Play sustained chords / drones and let the ~6-second plate envelop them.
4. If a baritone's low end muddies the tail, switch the reverb voicing to "Plate B Thick Predelay" — its 180 Hz HPF input filter keeps the lows clean under baritone.

## Notes
- The **180 Hz HPF on "Plate B Thick Predelay"** is the pick when low end is muddying the tail; "Present Plate" is the bigger, more immediate option when clarity isn't an issue.
- Delay is deliberately low and long — Feedback at 11:00–noon, Mix ≈9–10. It's a bed, not a feature.
