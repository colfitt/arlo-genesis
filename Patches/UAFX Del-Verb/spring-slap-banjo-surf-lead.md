# Spring Slap (Banjo Surf Lead)

## Concept
A bright, articulate, dry-forward surf/slap voice — the opposite of the drone/doom wall. "Spring Slap Show B" bakes a 100 ms slapback-style pre-delay into the spring tank; a short Precision slap (≈120–150 ms) doubles it for a rhythmic bounce. Trails off keeps it tight with no wash. Kept for **range** (banjo as a clean surf lead), not for the signature aesthetic.

## How to play it
1. Pre-assign in the app: Reverb toggle "Spring Slap Show B" on Spring '65, Delay toggle "Default Precision" on Precision.
2. Keep Feedback at minimum so the Precision adds only 1–2 distinct repeats.
3. Play single-note surf lead — the 100 ms spring pre-delay and the short Precision slap stack into a doubled rhythmic bounce.
4. Use the Delay | Reverb footswitch mode to toggle each engine independently.

## Notes
- Not signature-fit — this is the surf/rockabilly reading of the Spring engine, the opposite of the drone wall.
- Spring's 2–4 kHz splash can ice-pick a bright banjo — back the Reverb level off if it's harsh.
- Trails OFF on purpose: the point is tight and articulate, no tail.
