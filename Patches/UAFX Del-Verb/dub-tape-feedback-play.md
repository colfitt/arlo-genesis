# Dub / Tape Feedback Play

## Concept
The performance patch. Tape EP-III pushed to 2–3 o'clock Feedback sits at the edge of self-oscillation; ride it by hand for dub swells, drop it to minimum to stop. A tube-drive spring ("Spring Tube Drive A") drips behind the repeats for sizzle and compression. Worn-tape Mod and an edge-of-oscillation tail are the degraded, "recorded-wrong" aesthetic.

## How to play it
1. Pre-assign in the app: Reverb toggle "Spring Tube Drive A" on Spring '65, Delay toggle "Default Tape EP-III" (or "Dark Tape n Preamp" for murkier dub) on Tape EP-III.
2. Set Feedback to 2–3 o'clock and **ride it by hand** for dub swells.
3. To stop a runaway, drop Feedback to minimum — bypass will NOT silence it.
4. Use Effects | Tap footswitch mode so the right foot can re-tap tempo on the fly.

## Notes
- **CRITICAL: self-oscillation runs in the BACKGROUND even when the pedal is bypassed** (Trails Off) or keeps ringing (Trails On) — bypass will NOT silence a runaway; **only Feedback-to-min does.** Plan this before a quiet passage.
- *Alternative internal-drive trick:* switch the delay to **Analog DMM** and push its Color past noon to bake overdrive into the repeats.
