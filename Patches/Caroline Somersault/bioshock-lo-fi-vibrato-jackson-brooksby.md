# Bioshock Lo-Fi Vibrato (Jackson Brooksby)

## Concept
Degraded sustained vibrato for guitar or baritone (art-rock / lo-fi). Brooksby's favorite control is the Dark/hi-cut toggle; at 100% wet it yields the darkest "underwater vinyl wobbly" lo-fi voice — "SO lo-fi, so reminiscent of soundtracks from the Bioshock video games... better than anything else I've tried in that category." A prime taste-lens fit.

## How to play it
1. MIX full-CW (5:00) for true 100%-wet vibrato.
2. OFFSET ~10:00 — keep it just above the ~9:00 audibility threshold so the warble stays audible.
3. DEPTH ~12:00, SPEED ~11:00 (slow-ish), triangle WAVE.
4. TONE Dark for the hi-cut on the wet signal. HAVOC optional.

## Notes
- Keep OFFSET just above 9:00 so the warble stays audible.
- Dark hi-cut is the lo-fi maker — it darkens only the wet signal.
