# Halo Shimmer Pad (dual-pitch glide)

## Concept
The dual ±2-oct shifters as a hardware echo of the Prismizer halo's pitched copies — a bright prism-up voice plus a chorused near-unity drop. DOUG-designed starting point to chase the Prismizer-halo / orchestral-electronic-wash-around-one-intimate-voice lane on this banjo/baritone rig. This is the intimate, not the doom, lane.

## How to play it
1. Set Routing Stereo, trails on. Pitch 1 = +1 oct (L), Pitch 2 = detune just off unity (R).
2. Use the "a in Dark" off-noon trick to seat unity, then nudge Pitch 2 a hair for chorused width.
3. Set Crush ~noon (clean; pull to ~11:00 for a light degraded edge).
4. Set Filter ~1:00 (HPF, glassy) so the up-voice floats; Decay mid-high, Multiply low-mid, Mix mostly wet but UNDER the dry voice.

## Notes
- APPROXIMATION FLAG: two FIXED pitched voices, not the played 4-note halo. Maps to Dark Star sheet patches Stacked-octave pad / Dual-pitch shimmer build / Infinite shimmer drone.
- Favor the LPF side of Filter if the up-voice ice-picks over banjo.
- Board-2 terminus recall.
