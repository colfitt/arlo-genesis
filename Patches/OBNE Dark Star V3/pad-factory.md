# Pad Factory (opposed-octave split)

## Concept
A wide stereo split-octave pad: the stacked-octave move with the polarity reversed (Pitch 1 down, Pitch 2 up) so the two octaves land opposed across L/R. Long trail, mostly wet, with a light Crush that tips it onto the degraded aesthetic. A strong Board-2 wide-stereo terminus into Board 3.

## How to play it
1. Set Pitch 1 to -1 octave, Pitch 2 to +1 octave for the opposed L/R split.
2. Set Decay high (long trail) and Mix high (mostly wet).
3. Set Crush just below noon for light degrade — this is what tips it onto-aesthetic over the cleaner stacked-octave pad.
4. Set Spread wide / full CW for normal stereo; leave Filter/Lag/Multiply neutral.

## Notes
- The light Crush below noon is the on-aesthetic differentiator vs the cleaner stacked-octave pad.
- Favor the LPF side of Filter (below noon) if the octave-up channel ice-picks over banjo.
- Strong fit as the Board 2 wide-stereo terminus feeding Board 3.
