# Pitch-tuning trick — "aim for the a in Dark"

## Concept
The single most practical setting on the pedal: making the pitch sit IN a song over chords instead of "horror-movie chaos." Mechanical noon is NOT clean pitch-unity — it is slightly detuned over chords. Nudge Pitch 1 a hair RIGHT of noon, aiming the pointer at the center of the little hole of the "a" in the printed word "Dark." That finds true unity.

## How to play it
1. Do NOT set Pitch 1 exactly at noon — noon is slightly detuned over chords.
2. Nudge Pitch 1 a hair RIGHT of noon, pointing at the center of the hole of the "a" in "Dark."
3. For chorused width, mirror Pitch 2 to Pitch 1, then nudge Pitch 2 a hair off.

## Notes
- This is a dial-in skill, not a stored patch — it applies everywhere.
- Attribution flag: demonstrated on the **original mono** Dark Star; mapped to the V3 (noon ≠ unity holds on the V3 per manual + UsageGuide §2).
- The "a in Dark" visual works because the V3 prints the same wordmark.
