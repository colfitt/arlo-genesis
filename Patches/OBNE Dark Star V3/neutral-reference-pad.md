# Neutral reference pad (all-noon)

## Concept
The most neutral starting point on the pedal — a synthetic pad reverb you dial *away* from rather than a destination. Two reviewers independently name all-noon as *the* neutral pad: "as synthetic as but less sparkly than a shimmer," and "everything at halfway already sounds magical." This is the A/B baseline for the whole device.

## How to play it
1. Set all four detented knobs (Filter, Pitch 1, Pitch 2, Crush) to noon.
2. Set Mix to taste — roughly unity at center.
3. Dial Decay, Lag, and Multiply to taste over a sustained chord.
4. A/B against this voice whenever you want to know how far you've pushed a patch.

## Notes
- Mechanical noon is NOT pitch-unity over chords (see the "a in Dark" tuning trick) — but that doesn't matter here because there's no shift in play.
- The tone reference (Budd / Eno) describes the neutral voice only.
- Use it as the baseline you A/B against; live mode, no slot needed.
