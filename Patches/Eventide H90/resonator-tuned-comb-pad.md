# Resonator — Tuned Comb Pad (bell/string resonances)

## Concept
Feed noisy or percussive playing through a bank of tuned comb filters and the Resonator conjures pitched bell/string-like resonances and rhythmic patterns. Tune the four notes to a chord or open-string voicing and the comb bank rings out those pitches; the Rhythm subdivision and Length set the body and pulse, while Feedback and Resonance decide whether it blooms and sustains or decays into evolving instability. Great for ambient drones and tuned-percussion textures coaxed out of banjo transients or scrapes.

## How to play it
1. Load **Resonator** on Preset A.
2. Tune **Note 1-4** to a chord (e.g. root / 5th / octave / 3rd).
3. Pick a **Rhythm** subdivision and set **Length** for the body.
4. Raise **Resonance** and **FB1** for a sustaining, pitched bloom.
5. Add a little **Reverb** to glue it; use **FB2** to introduce decaying, evolving instability.
6. Pairs well with a percussive input or a noise/scrape source.

## Notes
- No published exact knob values for this recipe — treat the control values above as a dialable starting point, not sourced settings. What IS sourced: the Resonator's Length, Rhythm (an 8-subdivision comb), FB1/FB2, Resonance, Reverb, and Note 1-4 tuning controls are from the Eventide H90 manual.
- FB1 maintains the comb pattern; FB2 degrades it — lean on FB2 for decaying, evolving textures.
- On-aesthetic for tuned banjo/percussion drones: tune the four notes to a triad or open-string voicing and play scrapes/transients into it.
