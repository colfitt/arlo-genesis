# Shimmer — Heaven Octave Stack (manual starting point)

## Concept
The classic celestial shimmer with the manufacturer's recommended dial-in: a reverb tail with pitch shifters set just above and just below an octave so the wash blooms into an angelic choir. Eventide describes Shimmer as "what the guitars sound like in heaven." Distinct from the designed shimmer-pitch-wall scaffold and the low-Mix shimmer-subtle-octave-wash — this one carries the official octave-stack starting point straight from the H90 manual.

## How to play it
1. Load the **Shimmer** reverb algorithm on Preset A.
2. Set **Pitch Shift A** just above 1200 cents and **Pitch Shift B** just below 1200 cents (octave up / octave down).
3. Pull **Delay** to minimum and push **Size**, **Decay** and **Mix** toward maximum.
4. Roll **Pitch Decay** up for a more intense climbing tail; push past 100 for Freeze modes.
5. Use a brighter/bridge-pickup tone to keep clarity in the wash.

## Notes
- The +/-1200c / Delay-min / all-else-maxed values are the **manufacturer manual's recommended starting point** — sourced, not invented. Pitch Shift range is 500c (P4) to 2400c (two octaves).
- Pitch Decay past 100 engages **Pitch Freeze** (locks the pitch shifters but keeps feeding reverb), then **Pitch+verb Freeze** (freezes everything). The Freeze freezes part of the reverb signal, **not** your live input — so you can solo on top.
- Shimmer has complex internal routing unlike the other H90 reverbs; Eventide staff call it "a tricky one." Use a bright/bridge pickup to keep clarity in the wash.
- Factory Shimmer presets to audition: Dark Angels, Quasar, Mountains, Ironworx.
- Distinct from existing shimmer-pitch-wall (designed scaffold) and shimmer-subtle-octave-wash (low-Mix wash) — this is the official octave-stack starting point.
